District Overview

Frisco Independent School District is one of the largest and most academically recognized school districts in Texas, serving a broad swath of North Texas that extends well beyond the city of Frisco itself. The district spans parts of Collin and Denton counties, covering portions of Frisco, northern Plano, and sections of Prosper, McKinney, and Little Elm — making address verification an essential step before any home purchase in the area.

As of the 2024–25 school year, FISD enrolls approximately 66,700 students across 77 campuses: 49 elementary schools, 14 middle schools, and 9 high schools. The district is served by a nine-member elected board of trustees and employs more than 9,000 staff members. Its central offices are located at 5515 Ohio Drive, Frisco, TX 75035.

The district's geographic footprint means that a home address in Frisco, TX does not guarantee Frisco ISD enrollment — and vice versa. Parts of far northern Plano and communities near the Prosper and McKinney borders may be zoned to FISD. Always verify using the TEA School District Locator or FISD's own address lookup tool before making an offer.

Key Cities Served

Frisco (primary), North Plano, portions of Prosper, McKinney, and Little Elm. The district boundary is irregular — verify every address individually.

TEA Accountability Rating

The Texas Education Agency assigns every school district and campus an annual A–F accountability rating based on three domains. Frisco ISD has held a TEA A rating (score: 90 out of 100) for the 2024–25 school year — sustained across multiple consecutive rating cycles.

What the three domains measure:

  • Student Achievement — STAAR test scores, graduation rates, college/career readiness indicators
  • School Progress — year-over-year academic growth compared to similar campuses
  • Closing the Gaps — performance equity across student demographic groups

A score of 90 places FISD firmly in the A range, which begins at 90 points. The district is notable for maintaining this rating at a scale — 66,000+ students — that most A-rated districts never approach. For families tracking campus-level performance, official ratings for all 77 campuses are published at txschools.gov.

FISD has also earned multiple Distinction Designations from TEA in categories including Academic Achievement in Reading/Language Arts, Mathematics, Science, Social Studies, and Comparative Academic Growth — awarded to the top 25% of districts in each category statewide.

Graduation Rate

Frisco ISD's four-year graduation rate is 95.2% (as of October 2025, TEA four-year longitudinal rate). The district consistently outperforms both state and national averages. Nearly all graduating seniors complete at least one college credit — through dual enrollment with Collin College, AP exams, or IB coursework — before leaving high school.

Advanced Placement & International Baccalaureate

Every Frisco ISD high school offers AP courses, and several campuses host dedicated IB programs. The district's AP participation and pass rates rank among the highest in Collin County. Specific IB Diploma Programmes are offered at designated campuses (see Campuses section below). Dual credit enrollment through Collin College is available district-wide, allowing students to earn college credit while in high school at no cost under the state's Texas College Credit Program.

College & Career Readiness

FISD tracks "College, Career, and Military Readiness" (CCMR) as required by TEA. The district's CCMR rate — the percentage of graduates who complete at least one indicator of readiness — is among the highest for large Texas districts. Indicators include AP/IB exam scores of 3 or above, dual enrollment completions, and industry certifications through CTE programs.

STAAR Proficiency

District-wide STAAR proficiency rates (students performing at "Approaches Grade Level" or above) are available by campus at txschools.gov. FISD consistently scores above state averages across all tested subjects. The district reports math and reading proficiency scores publicly through its annual accountability reporting cycle.

Campus Highlights

With 77 campuses, every school in FISD has its own culture and specialty focus. The following are among the most prominent for relocating families — particularly at the high school level, where program choices most significantly affect a student's trajectory.

Frisco High School (FHS)

Grades 9–12~3,500 students

The original district flagship, located in central Frisco. Strong AP program, fine arts, and athletics. Home to the Raccoons — one of FISD's most storied athletic programs.

Lone Star High School

Grades 9–12IB World School

Houses one of FISD's IB Diploma Programmes. Strong college prep culture, academically competitive cohort, and a reputation for producing National Merit Scholars.

Independence High School

Grades 9–12IB World School

Also an IB World School, with robust AP offerings alongside the IB track. Located on the western edge of the district, serving families in parts of Plano and western Frisco.

Heritage High School

Grades 9–12STEM Focus

Known for strong STEM and CTE programs. One of the newer FISD high schools — newer campuses often have updated facilities and smaller initial class sizes as they build enrollment.

Centennial High School

Grades 9–12Fine Arts

Strong fine arts tradition, including orchestra, choir, and visual arts. Houses dedicated arts performance spaces. Academically solid across all departments.

Reedy High School

Grades 9–12Athletics

Located in south Frisco near the Plano border. Competitive athletics program and a strong AP course catalog. Popular with families in newer south Frisco communities.

Elementary and middle school assignments are determined by address. FISD maintains a campus locator at friscoisd.org. For the most current campus information including enrollment caps and specialty programs, contact FISD's enrollment department directly.

Special Programs

Gifted & Talented (GT)

FISD's GT program begins at the elementary level with pull-out enrichment and extends through high school with accelerated coursework. Students are identified through a multi-criteria assessment process. GT services are available at every campus level.

International Baccalaureate (IB)

IB Diploma Programmes are offered at Lone Star High School and Independence High School. Both are authorized IB World Schools. The IB Middle Years Programme (MYP) is offered at select middle schools feeding these campuses.

STEM & Career & Technical Education

FISD operates multiple STEM academies and CTE pathways including technology, engineering, health science, business, and digital media. Students can earn industry-recognized certifications alongside their high school diploma.

Fine Arts

Fine arts programming is among the strongest in North Texas — orchestra, band, choir, visual arts, and theatre are offered across all secondary campuses. FISD fine arts programs regularly advance to UIL State competitions.

Dual Credit (Collin College)

Through FISD's partnership with Collin College, eligible juniors and seniors can enroll in college courses at no cost, earning both high school and college credit simultaneously. Courses are available on-campus and at Collin College campuses.

Special Education

FISD offers a full continuum of special education services per IDEA requirements, including inclusion support, co-taught classrooms, self-contained settings, and life skills programs. A dedicated transition program serves students aging out of eligibility.

Special Education & Support Services

Frisco ISD provides special education and related services to eligible students ages 3–21 under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Services are delivered across the least restrictive environment continuum, from full inclusion in general education classrooms to specialized campus placements depending on the Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Support services include speech-language therapy, occupational therapy, physical therapy, ARD (Admissions, Review, and Dismissal) facilitation, behavioral support, and transition planning for secondary students. Families new to FISD with a student who has an existing IEP from another district should contact the Special Education department promptly — Texas law requires districts to either adopt the current IEP or convene a new ARD meeting within 30 days of enrollment.

For Section 504 accommodations (students who do not qualify for special education but require formal accommodations under the Rehabilitation Act), contact the campus 504 Coordinator. FISD also offers dyslexia testing and intervention services at all campuses.

Athletics & Extracurriculars

Frisco ISD competes in the University Interscholastic League (UIL) at Class 6A — the highest UIL classification — across all nine high schools. The district regularly produces UIL state qualifiers and champions in football, basketball, baseball, softball, soccer, swimming, and cross country.

Beyond the major sports, FISD offers a full range of UIL competitive activities including Academic Decathlon, One Act Play, Debate, Science Olympiad, and Math Olympiad. Student activity options at the high school level typically include 50+ clubs per campus, student government, National Honor Society chapters, and specialty clubs aligned to CTE pathways (DECA, FBLA, HOSA, SkillsUSA).

FISD's fine arts programs participate in UIL competitions separately from athletics — band, orchestra, choir, and theatre all compete at the 6A level, with multiple programs consistently advancing to state. The district's investment in performing arts facilities is notable, with dedicated fine arts complexes at most high school campuses.

School Choice Within the District

Open Enrollment Transfers

Frisco ISD has limited open enrollment capacity for students who reside outside district boundaries. Inter-district transfers (from within FISD boundaries to a different campus than the address-assigned campus) require an application and are subject to campus capacity. Intra-district transfers are not guaranteed and must be renewed annually.

Magnet / Specialty Programs

Access to IB programs requires meeting the host campus's application criteria. GT identification determines access to dedicated GT services. CTE specialty programs at the high school level may involve application or audition processes for certain pathways (e.g., fine arts, health science).

Virtual / Online Options

FISD offers a virtual learning option for eligible students — FISD Online — with synchronous and asynchronous coursework options. Students must maintain enrollment within FISD to access virtual courses. Contact FISD's Curriculum & Instruction department for eligibility requirements and course availability.

Charter School Context

Charter schools operating within or near the Frisco area — including Harmony, KIPP, and Uplift campuses — are state-funded alternatives that accept students regardless of home address. These are separate from FISD and have their own application and lottery processes.

District Finances

Tax Rate

Frisco ISD's 2024 certified total tax rate is $1.03 per $100 of assessed value (Texas Comptroller). This comprises the Maintenance & Operations (M&O) rate and the Interest & Sinking (I&S) rate. The I&S component reflects bond debt service — FISD has passed multiple bond packages over the past two decades to fund new campus construction and facility upgrades to keep pace with enrollment growth.

For a home assessed at $600,000, the FISD tax levy is approximately $6,180 per year at this rate, before any exemptions. Texas homeowners with a homestead exemption receive a $100,000 reduction on their assessed value for school district taxes (increased to $100,000 by HB 3, effective 2023), which meaningfully reduces the effective tax burden.

Per-Pupil Expenditure

Frisco ISD's per-pupil expenditure is publicly reported through the TEA's PEIMS financial data and the federal NCES Common Core of Data. As a large, growing district, FISD receives the state Basic Allotment per student plus various adjustments for bilingual education, special education, and other weighted factors. Current per-pupil expenditure data is available at txschools.gov.

Bond History

FISD has a history of passing voter-approved bond packages to fund the construction of new campuses, stadium upgrades, and technology infrastructure. The district's rapid enrollment growth — adding thousands of students annually at its peak — required continuous capital investment. Families should understand that I&S tax rates in growing districts like Frisco reflect this ongoing construction debt.

Enrollment — New Families

How to Enroll

New student enrollment for Frisco ISD is completed online through the district's enrollment portal at friscoisd.org. The process requires:

  • Proof of residency — lease agreement or deed/closing disclosure (two documents may be required)
  • Birth certificate — for first-time Texas enrollees
  • Immunization records — Texas-required vaccines (Tdap, meningococcal, etc.)
  • Previous school records — transcript, IEP if applicable, standardized test scores
  • Proof of custody — if applicable

Mid-Year Transfers

FISD accepts mid-year enrollments year-round. Families relocating with an offer letter but not yet a signed lease may contact FISD's enrollment department to understand timing options. It is advisable to initiate enrollment within the first week of establishing Texas residency to avoid delays in campus placement.

Pre-K Enrollment

FISD offers Pre-K programs for income-eligible, at-risk, or limited English proficiency students at designated campuses. Private Pre-K is available through some elementary feeder schools. Contact FISD's Early Childhood department for the most current Pre-K eligibility criteria and campus availability.

Gifted Identification

Newly enrolled students are assessed for GT services after initial enrollment. The process typically takes 4–6 weeks. If your child is identified as gifted in another state, bring all available documentation — FISD's GT department will review prior assessment records as part of its multi-criteria identification process.

Frequently Asked Questions

My address says "Frisco, TX" — does that mean I'm in Frisco ISD? ▾
Not necessarily. The FISD boundary does not align perfectly with the city of Frisco's city limits. Some Frisco addresses fall within Prosper ISD, McKinney ISD, or even Little Elm ISD. Conversely, some homes with Plano, McKinney, or Little Elm addresses are zoned to Frisco ISD. Always verify your specific address using the TEA School District Locator before making an offer.
Is the school experience consistent across all 77 FISD campuses? ▾
No — and this is important context for relocating families. While the district maintains consistent curriculum standards and the overall rating is strong, individual campus cultures, test scores, and specialty program availability vary. Newer campuses on the district's edges are still building their culture. Established campuses in central Frisco have more program depth. Research the specific elementary school your home address is assigned to — not just the district overall.
Which Frisco ISD high school has the IB program? ▾
IB Diploma Programmes are offered at Lone Star High School and Independence High School. Access to the IB program is typically determined by which high school your address is zoned to (IB is open to students at those campuses). If your child is highly motivated to pursue IB specifically, verify which high school your address feeds before purchasing a home.
Can I transfer my child to a different FISD campus than our assigned one? ▾
Intra-district transfers are possible but not guaranteed — they depend on available capacity at the receiving campus. The application window typically opens in spring for the following school year. Transfers must be renewed annually and can be revoked if capacity constraints arise.
How does FISD handle students with IEPs transferring from out of state? ▾
Texas law requires FISD to either adopt your child's existing IEP from another state or convene an ARD (Admissions, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment. Bring all prior IEP, evaluation, and eligibility documentation to the enrollment appointment. The Special Education department will contact you after enrollment to initiate the transition process.
What is the property tax impact of living in Frisco ISD? ▾
The 2024 FISD tax rate is $1.03 per $100 of assessed value. On a home assessed at $700,000, the annual school district tax (before exemptions) is approximately $7,210. Texas homeowners with a primary homestead qualify for a $100,000 reduction in assessed value for school taxes, lowering the effective taxable value to $600,000 and the annual levy to approximately $6,180. County and city taxes are assessed separately and vary.
How does Frisco ISD compare to Prosper ISD or Allen ISD? ▾
All three are high-performing Collin County ISDs. Frisco ISD is the largest (66,700 students) and offers the broadest program variety — IB at two high schools, 9 high schools total, and extensive CTE pathways. Prosper ISD is smaller (~28,000), growing rapidly, and notable for every campus earning a TEA A. Allen ISD is mid-sized (~22,000) with a tight community identity and excellent fine arts. Tax rates vary: FISD at $1.03, Allen at $1.13, Prosper at $1.26. Families focused on specific programs should research which district offers that program at the campus level before choosing a home address.
