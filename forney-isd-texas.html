Forney ISD is the school district story most DFW relocatees overlook — and, for the right family, it may be the most compelling value in the entire metroplex. Serving the rapidly growing Gateway corridor in Kaufman County, this is a district that has nearly doubled in enrollment over the past decade and is still accelerating, with more than 18,500 students as of 2024–25 and projections approaching 27,700 by 2029–30. That growth tells a specific story: families are discovering Forney because new construction here runs 30–40% below comparable product in Frisco, McKinney, or Prosper. The honest tradeoff is a B-rated district still building its program depth to match its enrollment, and a 25–30 mile commute east of downtown Dallas on I-20 or US-80. If you're weighing school quality against housing cost and commute, this page is built to help you make that decision clearly.

What makes Forney ISD genuinely distinct isn't just its growth rate — it's how the district has responded to that growth. New campuses are opening regularly, a first-of-its-kind career and college center launched in 2024, and the district earned an A in financial management from TEA even in years when its academic rating sat at B. Families who prioritize affordability, new construction, and a district that is investing aggressively in its own future often find that Forney checks more boxes than they expected — provided their work location makes the commute manageable.

Is Forney ISD Right for Your Family?

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Want new construction at significantly lower price points than Collin County alternatives
  • Are comfortable with a 25–30 mile commute to the Dallas employment core via I-20 or US-80
  • Value a B-rated, financially strong district with clear growth momentum over a pricier A-rated zip code
  • Have children who will benefit from smaller, newer elementary campuses with tight-knit community cultures
  • Want access to CTE and early college pathways — Forney's new Opportunity Central offers industry certifications and associate degree opportunities in high school

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • An A-rated district is a non-negotiable — neighboring Rockwall ISD and some Collin County districts carry higher current ratings
  • Your work location is in Far North Dallas, Plano, or Allen — the commute geometry from Forney to those corridors is difficult
  • You need deep, established AP/IB programming or a long track record of specialized academic offerings
  • Frequent boundary changes would be disruptive — Forney ISD adjusts elementary zones nearly every year as new campuses open
⚠ Boundary Verification — Critical in Forney

Forney ISD is actively rezoning elementary attendance boundaries as new campuses open. A Forney or Kaufman County address does not guarantee assignment to any specific campus. Always verify your exact address at forneyisd.net/about-us/attendance-zones before making an offer. Intermediate, middle, and high school zones are currently stable for 2026–27, but elementary boundaries are being actively adjusted.

TEA A-F Accountability Rating

Texas rates every public school district annually on an A-F scale across three domains. Forney ISD's most recent officially published rating is a B (81) from the 2021–22 school year — the last year before TEA paused or delayed ratings for some districts. TEA has released 2024 and 2025 ratings; verify Forney ISD's current scores at txschools.gov for the most up-to-date accountability data. The 2021–22 ratings remain useful context for understanding the district's baseline trajectory.

The three accountability domains and what they measured in Forney ISD:

Student Achievement

Measures current-year performance on STAAR assessments and graduation. This domain reflects how students are performing right now relative to grade-level expectations — not growth or progress from prior years. In a rapidly growing district like Forney, where many campuses are enrolling students who transferred mid-year from other districts, this number can lag the campus's actual instructional quality.

School Progress

Captures how much students improved academically compared to their own prior scores, and how the district compares to similar-demographic districts statewide. A district adding thousands of students each year — many mid-year — benefits less from this domain than a stable district with the same students enrolled year-over-year. Forney's growth rate means this score can underrepresent instructional gains.

Closing the Gaps

Evaluates whether all student groups — including students with disabilities, English learners, and economically disadvantaged students — are achieving at grade level. Forney ISD scored an 80 in this domain in 2021–22, indicating that closing achievement gaps across its diverse and growing population is an ongoing area of focus. This is common in fast-growth districts where new communities bring varied academic starting points.

What does a B rating mean in practice for a relocating family? A B (80–89 range) indicates a district performing at a solid, above-average level statewide. It does not indicate an underperforming district — the vast majority of Texas ISDs score in the B or C range. The distinction between an A-rated district like Rockwall ISD (89 in 2025) and Forney's B is meaningful but context-dependent: a family choosing a $380,000 new construction home in Forney over a $530,000 home in Rockwall is trading a modest difference in current academic ratings for a significant difference in housing cost, mortgage payment, and long-term financial flexibility.

📊 Financial Accountability

Forney ISD received an A rating in Financial Accountability from TEA — distinct from the academic A-F rating. TEA evaluates districts' financial management separately, and Forney's A indicates strong fiscal stewardship even as the district invests heavily in new campuses to accommodate rapid enrollment growth. This matters for families: a financially well-managed district is better positioned to maintain teacher compensation and services as growth continues.

Academic Performance

Based on state assessment data from the Texas Tribune and Niche, approximately 41% of Forney ISD students score at grade-level proficiency or above in math, and 52% in reading — figures that reflect districtwide averages, not individual campus performance. High school campuses show somewhat stronger results, with roughly 50% proficient in math and 63% in reading at the secondary level. The 2022–23 average SAT score for Forney ISD graduates was 1013, with an average ACT score of 18.2.

What do these numbers mean for a relocating family? Districtwide averages in a fast-growth district are particularly difficult to interpret. Forney ISD is absorbing thousands of new students annually — many transferring mid-year, many arriving from other states with different academic standards — which naturally compresses aggregate proficiency rates. Campus-level data, available at txschools.gov for individual schools, often shows meaningful variation within the district. Families relocating with academically advanced students may find that specific campuses or programs within Forney ISD outperform what the districtwide headline suggests.

The 2023 graduation rate of 91.6% (Class of 2023) is a more stable indicator of district performance. It places Forney ISD above the statewide average and reflects the district's ability to retain and graduate students even as its enrollment base grows rapidly. Advanced Placement courses are offered at both high school campuses, and dual-credit partnerships with local community colleges allow students to earn college hours while still enrolled in high school. The 2024 opening of the Keith Bell Opportunity Central significantly expanded CTE and early college pathways (see Programs section below).

Campuses

Forney ISD operates two comprehensive high schools, three middle schools, three intermediate schools, nine or more elementary schools, one virtual academy, and the Keith Bell Opportunity Central — a total campus count that has grown to 21+ schools and continues expanding. New campus openings have been a regular feature of the district's growth since 2020, with Wilson Elementary opening in 2024 and additional campuses in the planning pipeline.

Forney High School

High School · 6A ~2,400–2,800 students AP Courses

The district's original high school, serving the established Forney community. Forney HS is a 6A campus offering a full slate of AP courses and dual-credit options through partnership with Eastfield College. The campus draws the majority of students from the original Forney city core and surrounding neighborhoods.

North Forney High School

High School · 5A ~2,400–2,700 students AP Courses

North Forney serves primarily the Gateway corridor master-planned communities — Travis Ranch, Devonshire, and similar developments. As the district's newer high school, it is the campus most new construction buyers in the Gateway area will be assigned to. Boundary verification is essential, as campus assignments have shifted as Gateway-area enrollment has grown.

Keith Bell Opportunity Central (OC)

Career & College Center Opened 2024 CTE + Associate Degree

A first-of-its-kind facility for this part of DFW, the Opportunity Central is located near Jackson Middle School and Rhodes Intermediate. High school students can attend the OC for CTE coursework, industry certifications, and early college credit — including pathways toward an associate degree before graduation. This is an unusual offering for a district east of Dallas and a meaningful differentiator for career-focused students.

Jackson Middle School

Middle School Gateway Corridor Location

One of three middle schools in the district, Jackson serves the central Gateway corridor communities. Its proximity to the Opportunity Central makes it part of a growing academic hub in that part of the district. As with all Forney ISD campuses, verify your specific address assignment at the district's attendance zone lookup before making a purchase decision.

Elementary campuses include Griffin Elementary (serving Devonshire, opened 2021), Wilson Elementary (opened 2024), and several others. Elementary boundary changes are the most fluid aspect of the district — new campuses typically trigger redistricting that affects multiple surrounding communities simultaneously. Families relocating with elementary-age children should verify their campus assignment and ask the district whether any boundary reviews are pending for their address area.

Specialty Programs

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

Districtwide, with hub at Keith Bell Opportunity Central. Forney ISD offers CTE pathways across both high schools, with the 2024-opened Opportunity Central serving as a regional hub for advanced coursework. Programs span healthcare, technology, business, and skilled trades. Students may earn industry certifications recognized by employers. No separate application required for standard CTE courses; OC enrollment is coordinated through counselors at both high schools. Parent-provided transportation to the OC may be required depending on schedule.

Dual Credit / Early College

Available at both high schools. Forney ISD partners with Eastfield College and other community colleges to offer dual-credit coursework. Students can earn college credit at no cost while completing high school requirements. The Opportunity Central expands this pathway significantly — students on the early college track can graduate with meaningful college credit hours or a full associate degree. Enrollment is through the high school counseling office; no separate application, though specific courses may have prerequisites.

Advanced Placement (AP)

Available at both high schools. Both Forney High School and North Forney High School offer AP courses across core subjects. The depth of AP offerings continues to expand as enrollment and demand grow — families relocating from districts with a wide AP catalog should verify which specific courses are available at their assigned campus. AP course selection may vary between the two high schools.

Virtual Academy

Districtwide option. Forney ISD operates a virtual academy providing online instruction for students who need flexible scheduling. This program suits students with medical needs, family travel schedules, or learning profiles that benefit from self-paced coursework. Enrollment is through the district's Student Services office. Transportation is not provided for virtual program participants.

Gifted & Talented (GT)

Campus-based, all levels. Forney ISD identifies and serves GT students at the elementary level through differentiated instruction and pullout programming. At the secondary level, GT-identified students are channeled into PreAP and AP coursework. There is no separate GT campus — services are delivered at the student's home campus. Families relocating with a GT-identified child should contact the district's Advanced Academics coordinator to understand how their child's existing identification transfers and what documentation is required.

Special Education & 504

Districtwide, all campuses. Forney ISD provides SpEd and 504 services at every campus. Families transferring with an existing IEP should contact the district's Special Services office before enrollment — Texas law requires an ARD meeting within 30 days of enrollment to review and adopt or modify the existing IEP. Bring all prior documentation. Parent experience with SpEd services varies by campus and individual student needs; contacting Special Services directly (rather than relying on general district reputation) is the most reliable way to assess fit.

Enrollment Steps

Forney ISD enrolls new students on a rolling basis throughout the school year. For the 2025–26 school year, verify current enrollment windows and required documents at forneyisd.net — Student Enrollment, as timelines and document requirements may update annually.

Standard Enrollment Process

1. Confirm your attendance zone. Use the district's address lookup at forneyisd.net/about-us/attendance-zones to identify which campuses your specific address is zoned to. Do this before completing enrollment paperwork — campus assignments drive the rest of the process.

2. Gather residency documents. Typical requirements include a current lease or mortgage/closing disclosure, two utility bills in the parent/guardian's name showing the Forney ISD address, photo ID, and the child's birth certificate and immunization records. Requirements may vary; verify the current list on the district website.

3. Complete online registration. Forney ISD uses an online enrollment portal accessible through the district website. New family accounts are created through the PowerSchool/Skyward portal. Have digital copies of all residency documents ready to upload.

4. Submit records requests. For students transferring from another Texas district, Forney ISD will request records directly. For out-of-state transfers, bring prior report cards, STAAR or equivalent test history, and any IEP or 504 documentation.

Transfer Policy (2024–25)

Intra-district transfers (requesting a campus other than your attendance-zone campus) are considered on a space-available basis. Approval is not guaranteed and typically requires annual reapplication. Transportation is parent-provided for approved intra-district transfers.

Inter-district transfers (students from outside Forney ISD requesting to enroll) are reviewed by the district on a case-by-case basis. Contact Student Services to confirm current policy, as transfer availability varies by campus capacity.

IEP/504 Transfers

Texas law requires Forney ISD to hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment for any student transferring with an existing IEP. Bring all current IEP documents, recent evaluations, and any behavior intervention plans. For 504 accommodations, contact the campus 504 coordinator at your assigned school promptly after enrollment. Families with complex SpEd needs are encouraged to contact Special Services before finalizing a home purchase to understand service availability at specific campuses.

Boundary Verification

Boundary verification is more critical in Forney ISD than in most DFW districts — and that's not an overstatement. The district is opening new campuses every one to two years, and each new campus triggers elementary attendance zone realignments that can affect multiple surrounding communities simultaneously. The Devonshire community, for example, saw its boundary situation shift when Griffin Elementary opened in 2021 — and the district considered further changes to Devonshire zones as recently as 2024–25. Buying based on the current boundary map without confirming your specific address at the district level is a meaningful risk here.

Communities and ZIP Codes Served

Forney ISD serves the city of Forney and the surrounding unincorporated areas of Kaufman County that make up the Gateway corridor. Primary ZIP codes include 75126 (Forney) and portions of adjacent areas. The master-planned communities in the Gateway corridor — Travis Ranch, Devonshire, Gateway Parks, Lawson Farms, and newer developments — are all within Forney ISD boundaries, but their specific campus assignments vary by community and by grade level.

Key Boundary Edge Cases

Not every home with a Forney mailing address is zoned to Forney ISD — some rural Kaufman County addresses near the district perimeter may fall within adjacent districts. Additionally, within Forney ISD, the high school campus assignment for Gateway corridor communities depends on which side of the attendance boundary a specific subdivision falls on. Newer subdivisions in the far eastern reaches of the Gateway corridor should be verified carefully, as build-out of those communities continues and campus assignments may not yet be stable.

New Construction Buyer Alert

The model home sales office at a new construction community will typically tell you which elementary school the community feeds to — but model home staff are not district employees, and they may not have current information if a boundary review has occurred since the community opened. Always use the district's official address lookup tool, and ask specifically: "Are any boundary changes affecting this community being considered for the next 1–2 school years?" A builder representative's answer to that question is not authoritative.

How to verify your address: Use the Forney ISD attendance zone lookup at forneyisd.net/about-us/attendance-zones. You can also use the TEA school locator at tea.texas.gov/apps/school-directory to cross-reference campus assignments.

Making an offer without verifying your specific address can mean your child attends a different school than you planned — and boundary maps do not always match common-sense geography, especially in fast-growing areas where new campuses have recently redrawn lines.

Tax Rate & Finances

The 2024–25 certified total tax rate for Forney ISD is approximately $1.2869 per $100 of assessed value (source: Texas Comptroller, Kaufman County; verify the most current certified rate at comptroller.texas.gov). This rate has declined significantly over the past five years — down roughly 25 cents from peak — due to state-level property tax relief legislation and the district's strong financial management practices.

What does this mean for a monthly payment? On a $375,000 home assessed at market value, a $1.29 rate produces approximately $4,834 in annual school district taxes, or about $403 per month added to a mortgage payment (before the Texas homestead exemption). Texas law provides a $100,000 homestead exemption on school district taxes, reducing the taxable value: on a $375,000 home, the exemption lowers the effective school tax base to $275,000 — approximately $296 per month at the $1.29 rate. This meaningful reduction applies to primary residences; confirm your exemption filing with the Kaufman Central Appraisal District after closing.

How Forney Compares to Neighboring Districts

Forney ISD's $1.29 rate is higher than Rockwall ISD (~$1.15 per $100 in 2024–25) and Royse City ISD (~$1.18 per $100), and notably higher than Mesquite ISD (~$1.10 per $100). However, the property price differential between Forney and Rockwall — where comparable new construction typically runs $120,000–$180,000 more — means the total annual property tax bill is often lower in Forney despite the higher rate. A $380,000 Forney home at $1.29 produces a lower absolute tax bill than a $520,000 Rockwall home at $1.15. The rate comparison alone without the home price context is misleading for relocation decisions.

Per-Pupil Expenditure

Forney ISD's per-pupil expenditure reflects the cost pressures of a rapidly growing district — the district is simultaneously funding new campus construction, staffing growing schools, and maintaining existing facilities. Verify the most current per-pupil figures at txschools.gov under the Finance tab for Forney ISD. TEA's A-rated financial accountability score confirms the district is managing these pressures competently.

Families considering Forney ISD typically cross-shop against Rockwall ISD (just to the north, with higher home prices), Royse City ISD (east of Rockwall, also fast-growing), and Mesquite ISD (west, more urban and lower-priced). Here's how they stack up on key metrics:

Royse City ISD attracts buyers who want an even smaller community feel east of Rockwall at lower price points, though its recent C rating has dampened some interest. Mesquite ISD serves families who need a shorter commute and lower housing cost on the west side of the DFW east corridor, with a more urban school experience. Forney ISD sits in the middle of this landscape as the option where affordability, new construction access, and an improving academic trajectory align — at the cost of a meaningful drive.

Community Feel

The Forney ISD experience varies significantly depending on which part of the district you're in. Families in the established Forney city core describe a close-knit small-town culture with strong booster clubs, active school involvement, and a tight connection between neighborhood identity and school community. Friday night football is genuinely central to social life here in a way that many suburban DFW families from larger districts find refreshing.

The Gateway corridor communities — Travis Ranch, Devonshire, Gateway Parks — have a different character. These are large master-planned communities where the majority of residents are relatively new arrivals, many of them specifically priced out of Collin County. Parent communities on neighborhood forums and Facebook groups for these areas reflect the demographics of families who made a deliberate financial trade: they're more likely to discuss commute strategies, school boundary tracking, and advocacy for expanding programs than families in more established suburban districts. There's an active "we're building this together" energy in the Gateway communities that some families find energizing and others find unsettled compared to districts with longer institutional histories.

Youth sports are strong throughout the district — Forney HS athletics programs in football, baseball, and soccer have grown substantially alongside enrollment. The Opportunity Central's CTE programs are beginning to generate community interest in technical and trades-oriented career pathways that didn't previously exist in the district. One consistent parent sentiment from community forums: the newer elementary campuses in the Gateway corridor have particularly dedicated staff and a tight-knit culture precisely because teachers and administrators know they're building something new — which many families find to be a real advantage for younger children, even as families with older children weigh program depth more heavily at the secondary level.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my Forney address guarantee enrollment at a specific school? ▾
No — and this is more important in Forney ISD than in most DFW districts. The district actively adjusts elementary attendance zones as new campuses open, which happens roughly every one to two years. A specific subdivision may be reassigned to a different elementary school without significant notice to homebuyers. High school and middle school boundaries have been more stable, but elementary zones can change. Always verify your specific address at forneyisd.net/about-us/attendance-zones before making an offer — not just the community name, but your exact street address. Also ask the district whether any boundary reviews are currently underway that might affect your address.
What is the difference between Forney High School and North Forney High School? ▾
Forney High School (6A) is the district's original high school, serving primarily the established Forney city core. North Forney High School (5A) serves primarily the Gateway corridor master-planned communities — Travis Ranch, Devonshire, and similar developments to the north and west. Both campuses offer AP coursework and dual-credit options. Program availability may vary between the two campuses; families with specific course requirements (a particular AP subject, for example) should verify current offerings at their assigned campus before finalizing a home decision. Campus assignments for students in the Gateway corridor have historically been for North Forney HS, but verify your specific address at the district's attendance zone lookup.
How does Forney ISD handle IEP and 504 transfers from another state or district? ▾
For students transferring with an existing IEP, Texas law requires Forney ISD to hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment. At that meeting, the district must either adopt the existing IEP as written or develop a new one. Bring all current documentation — the IEP itself, recent evaluations, progress reports, and any behavior intervention plans. For 504 accommodations, contact the campus 504 coordinator promptly after enrollment; timelines for 504 implementation are not governed by the same statutory deadline as IEPs, but most campuses work quickly. Families with complex or specialized needs are encouraged to contact the district's Special Services office before finalizing a home purchase — not to assess district quality in general, but to understand what services are available at the specific campuses your address would feed to.
Why does Forney ISD have a B from TEA when the Niche grade is also B — are they measuring the same thing? ▾
No — TEA's A-F rating and Niche's grade measure different things using different data. The TEA rating is based on official state accountability data: STAAR performance, graduation rates, progress, and gap-closing — all standardized across Texas districts. A TEA B means the district scored 80–89 on the state's formula. Niche's grade is a composite of multiple factors including TEA data, state test scores, SAT/ACT data, student-to-teacher ratio, and user reviews from parents and students. The methodologies are not directly comparable. For relocation decisions, the TEA rating is generally more standardized and allows for cleaner district-to-district comparisons within Texas; Niche provides a useful supplemental perspective but should be understood as a third-party composite, not an official accountability rating.
How does the tax rate in Forney ISD compare to nearby districts, and how does it affect a real payment? ▾
Forney ISD's 2024–25 rate of approximately $1.29 per $100 of assessed value is higher than Rockwall ISD (~$1.15), Royse City ISD (~$1.18), and Mesquite ISD (~$1.10). However, because new construction home prices in Forney typically run $120,000–$180,000 below comparable product in Rockwall, the total dollar amount of annual school taxes is often lower in Forney despite the higher rate. On a $380,000 Forney home with a $100,000 Texas homestead exemption applied, the taxable base is $280,000 — producing roughly $3,600 in annual school taxes ($300/month). The same exercise on a $520,000 Rockwall home at $1.15 produces a taxable base of $420,000 after exemption — approximately $4,830 in annual school taxes ($402/month). The rate comparison alone, without factoring in home price, can be misleading for families making relocation decisions.
What is the Keith Bell Opportunity Central, and how do students access it? ▾
The Keith Bell Opportunity Central (OC) is a career, college, and community campus that opened in 2024, located near Jackson Middle School and Rhodes Intermediate in the Gateway corridor. It is not a standalone high school but rather a facility where high school students from both Forney HS and North Forney HS can attend CTE coursework, earn industry certifications recognized by employers, and pursue college credit — including pathways toward an associate degree before graduation. Students are enrolled through their home high school's counseling office; no separate application to the district is required, though specific program offerings may have prerequisites or scheduling requirements. Transportation logistics between the home campus and the OC vary; confirm the current arrangement with your assigned high school's counseling department. This is an unusual offering for a district in this part of DFW and represents a meaningful reason to look more closely at Forney ISD for career-oriented students.
What is the commute reality from the Gateway corridor to major DFW employment centers? ▾
Forney and the Gateway corridor sit approximately 25–30 miles east of downtown Dallas via I-20 or US-80. Commute time varies significantly depending on which highway you use, where specifically you're going, and time of day. I-20 provides access to the southern Dallas employment corridor (Mesquite, Grand Prairie, Irving, Las Colinas, DFW Airport) and is generally more predictable than US-80 during peak hours. US-80 provides faster access to downtown Dallas and Mesquite but is a two-lane highway in sections east of Forney, creating bottlenecks. Families commuting to the Plano/Allen/McKinney/Richardson corridor (the northern DFW tech corridor) face a difficult commute geometry from Forney — this route requires driving through or around Dallas and is often 45–60+ minutes in morning rush. The commute works best for families with jobs in the southeast Dallas, Mesquite, or central Dallas corridors. Before committing to a Forney address, map your specific employer location to your prospective Forney home during rush hour using live traffic tools — the difference between a manageable 35-minute drive and a difficult 55-minute drive depends heavily on exactly where in the metro your work is located.
