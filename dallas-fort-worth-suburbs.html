Understanding the DFW Family Score and Affordability Score

⭐ How the Family Score Works

The Family Score is a weighted composite designed specifically for parents relocating to DFW. It pulls from five independent data sources — Texas Education Agency accountability ratings, Niche school grades, FBI crime statistics, Census demographic data, and NTREIS real estate market data — weighted as follows:

  • Schools — 35%. Anchored on the city-level Niche public-schools grade — the schools families in that specific city actually attend — rather than a districtwide average. This matters: a large district can carry an overall "A+" while the campuses serving one city are merely good, and a city inside an "A" district can be served by its strongest schools. Grading at the city level separates the genuinely elite school communities from the merely-good ones far better than a district letter grade, and is refined by TEA A–F accountability ratings and GreatSchools data where available. See our DFW School Districts page for district-level detail.
  • Safety — 30%. Crime data sourced through a three-tier fallback: FBI UCR violent and property crime rates (where available), Niche and CrimeGrade.org city-level crime grades (letter grades converted to a 0–100 scale), and a Niche "families" community grade as a proxy for suburbs too small to appear in national crime databases. Most DFW suburbs land on tier two — Niche and CrimeGrade grades — since the FBI's public API does not currently return offense data for Texas agencies.
  • Family Life & Community — 10%. A lighter, supporting signal — how well a suburb is set up for daily family life. It combines the share of households with children (families cluster where kids thrive), local parks and everyday amenities, and Walk Score. It's weighted modestly on purpose: it refines the ranking between similar suburbs rather than driving it, so a spacious, low-density premium suburb is never penalized for having fewer storefronts per resident than a denser town.
  • Commute — 15%. Census median commute time. Lower is better: 15 minutes scores 100, 45 minutes scores 0.
  • Market Stability — 10%. NTREIS months of supply. A balanced market (2.5–4.5 months) scores highest — both extreme seller's and buyer's markets signal volatility.

The weighted result is then normalized to a 0–100 readability scale so meaningful differences between suburbs are easy to see at a glance. Normalization preserves the ranking order exactly — it only widens the spacing between scores.

💰 How the Affordability Score Works

The Affordability Score answers a different question: how much suburb do you get for the money? It weighs price-to-income ratio (35%), school quality relative to price (30%), home price versus the DFW metro average (20%), and ISD property tax rate (15%). A suburb can score low on Family Score and high on Affordability Score — that's your signal to look closer if budget is a priority. Suburbs like Forney, Wylie, and Rockwall often surface here that families from higher-cost markets overlook.

The 5 Factors That Matter When Choosing a DFW Suburb

School Districts — The Factor Most Families Start With

In DFW, your school district isn't determined by which city you live in — it's determined by which street you're on. A home in Frisco might be zoned to Frisco ISD, Prosper ISD, or McKinney ISD depending on the block. A home in Plano's north side is almost certainly zoned to Frisco ISD, not Plano ISD. This surprises almost every out-of-state family I work with, and it's why I verify district boundaries by address — not city name — before my clients make any offer. Use the TEA School District Locator for any specific address. For a full breakdown of all 36 DFW school districts — including TEA ratings, Niche grades, GreatSchools data, tax rates, enrollment, and notable programs — see our DFW School Districts comparison page.

Neighborhood Safety — What the Data Actually Shows

DFW's family suburbs are among the safest communities in Texas — but safety varies meaningfully across the metro. The Family Score safety dimension pulls FBI Uniform Crime Report data (violent and property crime per 100,000 residents) and Niche's city-level crime grade. As a general pattern: established inner suburbs like Coppell, Southlake, and Colleyville consistently rank among the safest in the region. Fast-growing outer suburbs are adding infrastructure rapidly; crime rates in newer master-planned communities are typically low due to lower density and newer housing stock. Click through to any suburb page to see the raw crime rate compared to the DFW average.

Commute Times — The Factor Families Underestimate

The DFW freeway system is extensive — and during peak hours, the difference between a 20-minute and a 45-minute commute can be a single interchange. The key question: which corridor does your employer sit on? If your office is in the Legacy/Frisco area (Toyota, Liberty Mutual, JPMorgan, PGA of America), Frisco and Prosper are the natural fit. If you're commuting to Las Colinas (Kimberly-Clark, Celanese, Fluor), Coppell and Irving serve you best. If you're going downtown Dallas daily, mid-ring suburbs like Plano and Richardson give you better access than the outer growth suburbs. Sort the table by commute time and filter by county to identify your corridor before shortlisting neighborhoods.

Housing Prices and Market Conditions — Reading the Numbers Right

DFW median home prices range from the low $300s in outer suburbs to well over $1.3M in Southlake — more than a 4x spread across the same metro. Two numbers matter most in the table: median SFR price (what you'll actually pay for a single-family home) and days on market (how competitive each neighborhood is right now). A suburb with 15 days on market means multiple-offer situations are common — you need to be pre-approved and ready to move quickly. A suburb with 50+ days gives you room to negotiate. Texas has no state income tax, but property taxes are significant — typically 1.8–2.5% of assessed value depending on your school district and any MUD district fees layered on top. Your total monthly payment, not just the purchase price, is what matters for long-term affordability.

Lifestyle, Amenities, and Community Feel — The Harder-to-Quantify Factors

Data can tell you the Walk Score, the park count, and the median age of residents — but it can't fully capture why some families feel immediately at home in Flower Mound and others gravitate toward McKinney's historic downtown. As a general guide: established suburbs like Coppell, Plano, and Colleyville have mature trees, walkable town centers, and a settled community feel. Growth suburbs like Prosper, Celina, and northern Frisco offer newer infrastructure, master-planned amenities — lazy rivers, clubhouses, sports complexes — but fewer established gathering places. Argyle and Trophy Club offer a more rural-adjacent character with strong schools at prices below the Frisco peak. The lifestyle data in each suburb page can help orient you, but nothing replaces spending a Saturday morning in two or three neighborhoods before you commit.

Which DFW Suburb Is Right for Your Family? A Quick Guide

Every family's priorities are different. Here's how I'd point you based on the situations I see most often:

  • If schools are your absolute top priority: Start with Carroll ISD (Southlake, Westlake), Lovejoy ISD (Fairview, Lucas), Frisco ISD, or Allen ISD. These are the elite districts in DFW. Southlake and Westlake carry significant price premiums; Allen and select Frisco neighborhoods deliver equivalent school quality at more accessible prices.
  • If budget is your primary constraint (under $450K): Look at McKinney (outer areas), Wylie, Rockwall, Forney, and Royse City. McKinney ISD and Rockwall ISD both rate well. You'll trade some amenity density for significantly more house.
  • If you're commuting to Legacy/Frisco corridor: Frisco, Prosper, Little Elm, and Celina are your natural quadrant — 15 to 25 minutes to most Legacy employers on a typical morning.
  • If you're commuting to Las Colinas: Coppell is the premium answer. Flower Mound and Lewisville give you a few more commute minutes in exchange for more house at a lower price point.
  • If you want the best school quality per dollar: Sort the table by Affordability Score. Allen, McKinney, and Rockwall routinely surface as suburbs where elite school district quality is paired with prices well below Frisco and Southlake peaks.
  • If your family wants space and a quieter pace: Argyle, Lantana, Northlake, and Celina offer larger lots, newer homes, and a more rural character — with strong schools — at prices below the inner Tier 1 suburbs.
