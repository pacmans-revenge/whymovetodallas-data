Getting Your Bearings

Where Is Roanoke, Texas?

Roanoke sits in the northwestern corner of the DFW metroplex, roughly 25 miles from downtown Fort Worth and 35 miles from downtown Dallas. It straddles Denton and Tarrant counties — most of the city falls in Denton County — and is positioned directly along Highway 114, the corridor that connects DFW Airport to the east and the Alliance employment development to the north via Hwy 377. That geographic position is a big part of why so many families end up here: you're close to everything in the northwest quadrant without living in a high-density suburb. The city proper has about 10,000 residents, which means you actually know your neighbors and the Oak Street downtown feels like a neighborhood gathering place rather than a tourist corridor.

The Alliance Corridor is essentially your backyard. Within 8–10 miles, companies like Amazon, FedEx, Bell Textron, BNSF Railway, and American Airlines Maintenance have major operations. DFW Airport is a 22-minute straight shot east on Hwy 114 — a significant perk if anyone in your household travels for work or you're relocating from a city that requires frequent return trips. For families comparing Roanoke to Southlake or Westlake, the tradeoff is real: you'll have less luxury retail and fewer high-end restaurants within city limits, but you'll often spend 20–30% less on the house while being a 12-minute drive from Southlake Town Square.

Transit reality: Roanoke has no DART service, no DCTA commuter rail, and no viable transit alternative. Every trip is by car. The practical upside is that Hwy 114 is a direct, high-speed corridor connecting you east to DFW Airport, Southlake, and Las Colinas — and Hwy 377 connects you north to Alliance and south to Fort Worth — without requiring toll roads or multiple highway transitions. That simplicity is underrated.
Education

Northwest ISD — Schools Deep Dive

Roanoke is served by Northwest Independent School District, one of the consistently strong ISDs in North Texas. The district earned a B (81/100) from TEA for 2024–25 — solid student achievement and graduation outcomes across a district that serves families from Roanoke through Trophy Club, Haslet, and parts of northern Fort Worth. Niche grades it A+ for public schools overall. The standout campus in the system — James M. Steele Early College High School, located right in Roanoke — consistently ranks among the top 30 high schools in Texas. If your high schooler is academically motivated, Steele is a legitimate path to earning college credit tuition-free before graduation. That's a real, tangible benefit that most families moving from out of state don't expect to find in a suburb this size.

Where to Live

Best Neighborhoods in Roanoke for Families

Roanoke's housing stock is a mix of established single-family neighborhoods built in the 1990s and 2000s, newer master-planned communities on the Trophy Club and Northlake borders, and a handful of communities specifically positioned for Alliance corridor workers. The city proper is compact — about 10,000 residents — so "neighborhood" in Roanoke often means knowing your elementary school feeder zone as much as the subdivision name. Here's where families tend to land and why.

Briarwyck

Energy-Efficient Builds Meritage Homes 1,800–4,500 sq ft Feeds Roanoke Elem / Byron Nelson

A Meritage Homes community known for energy-efficient construction and honest value for the area. Homes run 1,800–4,500 sq ft in the ~$450–$620K range. Briarwyck Park is right inside the neighborhood at 1375 Marshall Creek Road. Good fit for families who want Alliance Corridor access at a reasonable entry point without sacrificing school quality. Feeds into Roanoke Elementary, Roanoke MS, and Byron Nelson HS for most addresses.

Fairway Ranch

50+ Acres of Trails Hike & Bike Greenbelts Riverside Park Areas Feeds Cox Elem / Byron Nelson

One of Roanoke's most trail-rich neighborhoods — the master plan dedicates 50+ acres to hike/bike paths, greenbelts, and riverside park areas with a nearby retail node. Homes range ~$500–$680K in a mix of older and newer construction. Neighborhood retail is within walking distance. For outdoor families, this is the Roanoke address worth prioritizing. Historic / Established Roanoke

Walkable to Oak Street Older Character Homes Larger Yards Lower Price Point

The original Roanoke neighborhoods clustered around the Oak Street dining corridor — homes from the 1980s–2000s with more character and bigger yards than newer builds at the same price. If the Oak Street lifestyle is a big draw for your family, start here before looking farther out.

The Numbers

Cost of Living — Roanoke vs. Where You're Coming From

The conversation I have with almost every out-of-state family starts the same way: sticker shock — not in the bad direction. A $593K median home in Roanoke buys a 2,400–3,200 sq ft house with a real yard, a two-car garage, and a school district that doesn't require a private-school backup plan. For families relocating from California, New York, or Illinois, the numbers can feel too good to be true. They're not — Texas just trades differently: you pay more in property taxes, and you pay zero in state income tax. Here's what that looks like side by side.

The California math: A family earning $250,000 in California pays roughly $23,000–$31,000/year in state income tax. In Texas: $0. That alone frequently offsets the higher Texas property tax bill — and then some. Add the home price difference — you'd spend $700K–$900K more for a comparable home in the Bay Area — and the 10-year financial delta typically runs $500K–$900K in favor of Texas. That's not a lifestyle downgrade. That's a different financial life.

No MUD/PID — a meaningful advantage: Unlike many growing North DFW suburbs (Prosper, Frisco new construction, Little Elm), most established Roanoke neighborhoods carry no Municipal Utility District or Public Improvement District surcharge. This keeps effective rates meaningfully lower than comparable new-construction options that can add $0.25–$0.60 per $100 on top of base rates. If you're considering new construction on Roanoke's northern edge, always ask the builder for the full blended tax rate before signing.
