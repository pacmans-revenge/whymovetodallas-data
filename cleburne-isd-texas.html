Cleburne ISD Overview

7,054 Students (2023–24)
12 Campuses
C TEA Rating (2024–25)
B− Niche Grade (3rd party)
95.3% Graduation Rate
$1.17 Tax Rate / $100

Cleburne ISD serves the city of Cleburne — the Johnson County seat, about 30 miles southwest of Fort Worth — and portions of the surrounding rural community. It's a smaller, community-rooted district that doesn't generate the same headlines as fast-growth suburban ISDs to the north, but it offers something families moving to Cleburne for affordability genuinely care about: a 95.3% four-year graduation rate that beats the Texas state average by five full percentage points.

The honest context: Cleburne ISD earned a TEA C rating for 2024–25, with matching C scores across all three accountability domains. College readiness metrics sit below state averages, and the district's student population is majority economically disadvantaged. Families prioritizing elite AP performance or a high TEA score should cross-shop Joshua ISD or Burleson ISD before committing. But for families buying in Cleburne because it delivers real house-per-dollar value while staying within Johnson County, this is a district that keeps kids in school through graduation at a rate that matters.

One standout financial note: Cleburne ISD's $1.17 per $100 tax rate is meaningfully lower than neighboring Joshua ISD ($1.26) and Burleson ISD ($1.26). On a $350,000 home, that difference adds up to roughly $315 per year in property tax savings — worth factoring into any budget comparison alongside the school quality picture.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Are buying in Cleburne primarily for housing affordability and Johnson County lifestyle
  • Value a strong graduation rate and want their child to finish high school without a high dropout risk
  • Have students interested in CTE vocational pathways, hands-on career certification, or dual credit courses
  • Want bilingual or ESL support (Cooke, Irving, and Santa Fe elementaries offer bilingual pre-K)
  • Appreciate a student-teacher ratio (12.9) that outperforms the state average of 14.7

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • You're prioritizing a district with a TEA A or B rating and strong college readiness metrics
  • AP course depth and a high pass rate are central to your child's academic plan
  • You're comparing Cleburne to similarly priced homes in Joshua or Burleson ISD territory where TEA scores are higher
  • Your child has intensive IEP or specialized program needs that benefit from a larger district's resources
Boundary Verification

Not every Cleburne mailing address falls within Cleburne ISD boundaries. Some rural properties in Johnson County are zoned to Joshua ISD or other neighboring districts. Use the district's Find My School Zone tool to verify your specific address before making an offer. New construction on the eastern edges of Cleburne city limits may feed to different campuses than established neighborhoods closer to downtown.

TEA Accountability Rating

Cleburne ISD received an overall C rating from the Texas Education Agency for the 2024–25 school year. All three accountability domains also earned a C: Student Achievement, School Progress, and Closing the Gaps. The prior year (2023–24) rating was a B with a score of 80 — a meaningful shift downward that families should note. The district's accreditation status remains Accredited, and its FIRST financial rating is Superior Achievement.

What the three domains actually measure for families:

Student Achievement (C) — This domain looks at STAAR test performance, graduation rates, and College, Career, and Military Readiness (CCMR). Cleburne's strong graduation rate helps here, but lower STAAR proficiency and college readiness scores pull the domain rating down.

School Progress (C) — This domain measures how much students grow academically year over year, relative to their starting point and relative to schools serving similar populations. A C here means students are growing, but not at the pace Texas considers exemplary.

Closing the Gaps (C) — This domain tracks whether performance gaps between student groups are narrowing. Given Cleburne's diverse population and high economically disadvantaged percentage, this is the domain most directly tied to equity outcomes across the district's campuses.

TEA vs. Graduation Rate — What to Make of It

A C TEA rating and a 95.3% graduation rate can coexist. The graduation rate measures whether students stay enrolled and finish — Cleburne excels at this. The TEA rating captures test performance and college readiness metrics where Cleburne has room to grow. Both numbers tell you something real. The question is which metric matters more for your family's situation.

Academic Performance

The most meaningful academic signal for relocating families: Cleburne ISD's four-year graduation rate was 95.3% for the Class of 2023 — compared to a Texas state average of 90.3%. The dropout rate for grades 9–12 was 1.6% (2022–23), below the state average of 2.0%. These are real outcomes: more than 19 out of 20 students who enter Cleburne High School as freshmen earn a diploma.

AP/IB participation: 26.2% of juniors and seniors took at least one AP exam in 2022–23, slightly above the statewide rate of 24.2%. However, the AP pass rate (score of 3 or higher) was 37% — meaningfully below the statewide rate of 53.3%. Families planning around AP credits toward college admission or placement should note this gap and discuss it directly with Cleburne High School's counseling department.

SAT and ACT: Average SAT scores for 2022–23 graduates were 927 (statewide: 978). Average ACT was 17.5 (statewide: 19.2). College readiness rates — the percentage of graduates meeting TSIA, SAT, or ACT benchmarks — were 30.3% in reading and 16.9% in math, well below state averages of 62.8% and 54.3% respectively.

Putting these numbers in practical terms: if your child is a strong, self-directed student who pursues tutoring and dual credit aggressively, Cleburne ISD gives them the access points to build a competitive college application. If you're counting on the district environment itself to produce elite college prep outcomes at scale, the data says to look at Joshua ISD or Burleson ISD, where metrics sit higher. For families focused on keeping their child safe, engaged, and graduated — Cleburne's performance is genuinely solid.

Campuses

Cleburne ISD operates 12 campuses serving pre-K through 12th grade, including seven elementary schools, one intermediate school, one middle school, two high school options, and a Disciplinary Alternative Education Program campus.

Cleburne High School

High School (9–12) ~1,923 students Home of the Yellow Jackets

The district's sole traditional high school, offering AP courses, a comprehensive CTE program with multiple career pathways, dual credit through Tarleton State and Hill College, fine arts, and UIL athletics. The campus was significantly renovated following the 2016 bond program. CTE pathways include Agriculture, Business, Health Science, and more.

TEAM School

Alternative High School 9–12

An alternative high school pathway for students who benefit from a non-traditional setting. TEAM School provides a personalized, smaller-group environment designed to help students earn a diploma who may not thrive in the larger traditional high school format. It is a distinct campus, not a program within Cleburne High School.

A.D. Wheat Middle School

Middle School (6–8) STEAM · Robotics

The district's main middle school, featuring a STEAM and Robotics program and a College and Career Readiness (CCR) component at the middle school level — an early on-ramp to the AVID college-readiness framework that continues at Cleburne High School.

Lowell Smith Jr. Intermediate School

Intermediate School STEAM

Serves the upper elementary/intermediate grades and runs its own STEAM program. The intermediate configuration (rather than combining with middle school) means Cleburne students transition through a dedicated campus before entering Wheat Middle School — a smaller step-up experience in the sequence.

Elementary Schools (7 Campuses)

Adams · Coleman · Cooke Gerard · Irving · Marti · Santa Fe

All seven elementary campuses offer full-day pre-K. Bilingual pre-K programs are specifically available at Cooke, Irving, and Santa Fe elementaries — not universally across the district. Families seeking bilingual programming should confirm their home feeds to one of these three campuses before purchasing.

Specialty Programs

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

Campus: Cleburne High School. Access: Open to all CHS students; no application or lottery required. CTE at CHS includes programs in Agriculture Science, Business & Finance, Health Science, Law Enforcement, Culinary Arts, and more. Students can earn industry certifications before graduation. This is one of the district's strongest assets for students who prefer hands-on, career-aligned learning. Home-search implication: CTE is centralized at CHS — boundary verification ensures access to the right high school campus.

Dual Credit

Campus: Cleburne High School. Partners: Hill College and Tarleton State University. Access: Open enrollment with GPA and course prerequisites; no lottery. Students can earn college credit while in high school at reduced or no cost. Dual credit is one of the most practical college-readiness pathways at CHS, especially given the district's lower AP pass rates. Families should verify current offerings and prerequisites through the CHS counseling office each school year.

AVID (Advancement Via Individual Determination)

Levels: Middle school (Wheat) and high school (CHS). Access: Application-based; targets students in the academic middle who are first-generation college-bound. AVID teaches college-readiness skills, note-taking, time management, and study habits. Its presence in a smaller, economically diverse district like Cleburne ISD is a meaningful commitment to supporting students who wouldn't otherwise have access to structured college-prep coaching.

Gifted & Talented (GT)

Districtwide. Access: Testing and referral-based identification. Approximately 7.4% of Cleburne students are identified as GT (near the state average of 8.5%). Services are provided at all campus levels but the depth of programming varies by grade — families with highly advanced students should ask specifically about enrichment offerings at the relevant campus rather than assuming districtwide uniformity.

Fine Arts

Districtwide. Cleburne ISD runs a full fine arts department including Band, Choir, Dance, Theatre, and Visual Arts at the secondary level, with elementary arts programs at all campuses. A 2025 bond proposal included a new multi-use stadium to support both athletics and fine arts performance — the district's existing facilities for these programs are among the older infrastructure items being addressed.

Bilingual / ESL

Bilingual Pre-K: Cooke, Irving, and Santa Fe Elementary campuses only. ESL services: Districtwide at all levels. Approximately 24.8% of students are enrolled in bilingual or English language learning programs — matching the state average almost exactly. Families with Spanish-speaking students entering kindergarten should verify that their home address feeds to one of the three bilingual-pre-K campuses if early bilingual instruction is a priority.

Enrollment Steps

Cleburne ISD uses an online enrollment portal through Skyward. Here is the step-by-step process for families relocating to Cleburne:

1. Verify Your School Zone

Before gathering documents, confirm which campus your address is zoned to using the Find My School Zone tool on the district website. Not all Cleburne addresses feed to the same elementary campus — and some Johnson County addresses outside city limits may fall in a different district entirely.

2. Gather Required Documents

Standard residency verification documents required include: proof of residency (utility bill, lease, or mortgage statement with your Cleburne address), student's birth certificate, up-to-date immunization records, prior school records and most recent report card, and any existing IEP, 504, or ARD documentation if applicable.

3. Complete Online Registration

Visit c-isd.com/departments/student-services/student-enrollment to access the Skyward online registration portal. The 2025–26 enrollment window typically opens in spring. Contact the district at (817) 202-1100 for specific dates if you are enrolling mid-year.

4. Intra-District Transfers

Families already within Cleburne ISD who wish to transfer to a different campus within the district may submit a transfer request. Approval is based on available space at the receiving campus. Contact Student Services at (817) 202-1100 for the current transfer policy and timeline. Transfers are not guaranteed and must be renewed annually in some cases.

5. Inter-District Transfers

Families residing in a neighboring district (Joshua ISD, Burleson ISD, Crowley ISD) who wish their child to attend Cleburne ISD may apply for an inter-district transfer. Acceptance is at the discretion of Cleburne ISD administration and depends on campus capacity. Review current policy at c-isd.com/departments/student-services/transfers.

6. IEP / 504 / ARD Transfers

Texas law requires that when a student with an existing IEP transfers to a new district, the receiving district must convene an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days to review and adopt or adjust the IEP. Bring all prior IEP, 504, evaluation, and assessment documentation to enrollment. Contact Cleburne ISD's Special Education department at (817) 202-1100 as early as possible — ideally before closing on your home — to discuss your child's specific needs and timeline.

Boundary Verification

Cleburne ISD's boundaries follow city and county lines that are not always intuitive from a map or a Zillow search. Here is what families need to know before making an offer:

Which Areas Cleburne ISD Serves

The district primarily serves the city of Cleburne and portions of the surrounding Johnson County. The core of Cleburne — established neighborhoods closer to downtown and the high school — are solidly within district boundaries. Growth areas on the eastern and northern edges of the city, as well as rural acreage properties with Cleburne mailing addresses, are more likely to require address verification.

Neighboring Districts — Where Boundaries Blur

Johnson County contains multiple ISDs. Joshua ISD and Burleson ISD both cover nearby communities, and some properties with Cleburne city addresses or ZIP codes may actually be zoned to those districts. Rural route addresses are particularly susceptible to boundary mismatches. Never assume a Cleburne address means Cleburne ISD. This is especially important for new construction developments on the outskirts of the city.

Elementary Campus Boundaries

Within Cleburne ISD itself, the seven elementary campuses have their own attendance zones. Families specifically targeting a bilingual pre-K campus (Cooke, Irving, or Santa Fe) should verify their home address feeds to that campus before purchasing — boundary maps do not always match intuitive neighborhood geography.

How to Verify Your Address

Use the district's official tool: Find My School Zone at c-isd.com. You can also use the TEA School Locator at tea.texas.gov/apps/school-directory for a secondary check. If results are unclear, call the district directly at (817) 202-1100 before closing.

Home-Search Implication

Making an offer without verifying your specific address can mean your child attends a different school — or a different district — than you planned. Boundary maps do not always match common-sense geography, and Zillow's school assignment data is not always current. Always verify with the district directly before signing a contract.

Property Taxes & Finances

2024 Certified Total Tax Rate: $1.17 per $100 Assessed Value

Cleburne ISD's total certified tax rate is among the lower rates in the greater DFW and Johnson County area. To put it in practical terms:

  • $300,000 home: ~$3,510/year in Cleburne ISD taxes ($293/month)
  • $350,000 home: ~$4,095/year ($341/month)
  • $400,000 home: ~$4,680/year ($390/month)

These figures are before the Texas homestead exemption. Texas law provides a $100,000 homestead exemption from school district taxes for your primary residence — meaning the first $100,000 of assessed value is removed from the calculation. On a $350,000 home, you'd pay taxes on $250,000 assessed value, reducing the annual bill to approximately $2,925 ($244/month).

Comparison to Neighboring Districts

Cleburne ISD's $1.17 rate is notably lower than both Joshua ISD ($1.26) and Burleson ISD ($1.26). On a $350,000 home (after homestead exemption, taxed on $250,000), that $0.09 difference saves approximately $225/year compared to Joshua or Burleson. Over 10 years, that's $2,250 in property tax savings — meaningful when you're also making a mortgage payment.

Per-Pupil Expenditure

Cleburne ISD spends approximately $10,989 per student annually — a figure consistent with smaller Texas districts and reflective of the district's operating scale. This figure includes state funding, local tax revenue, and federal contributions.

FIRST Rating

The district holds a Superior Achievement FIRST (Financial Integrity Rating System of Texas) rating — the highest category — indicating sound financial management despite serving a high economically disadvantaged student population.

2025 Bond Proposal

Cleburne ISD put a bond proposal before voters in 2025 to address aging campus infrastructure, including construction of a new multi-use stadium to support athletics and fine arts events. Bond elections, if approved, result in an I&S (Interest & Sinking) component being added to or adjusted within the total tax rate. Families buying now should check the current status of the 2025 bond and its tax rate impact directly with the district.

How Cleburne ISD Compares

Families buying in Johnson County most commonly cross-shop Cleburne ISD against Joshua ISD (smaller, higher academic scores) and Burleson ISD (larger, Fort Worth suburb feel). Here is how the key metrics stack up:

District Size TEA Grade Tax Rate Best For
Cleburne ISD ~7,054 C $1.17 Affordability + strong graduation rate
Burleson ISD ~14,000 B $1.26 Larger suburban district, Fort Worth commute
Joshua ISD ~6,000 B (score: 86) $1.26 Higher TEA score, smaller community, rural feel

Families who prioritize a higher TEA score and are willing to pay a higher tax rate will find Joshua ISD (score 86, grade B) the most direct alternative to Cleburne within Johnson County. Burleson ISD sits to the north, serving a more suburban corridor along US-35W toward Fort Worth, with a B rating and a similar $1.26 tax rate. Cleburne ISD is the right fit if Cleburne's housing values, community character, and lower tax rate are doing most of the work in your decision — and you've made peace with the C TEA rating after reviewing the graduation rate data.

Community Feel

Cleburne is the county seat of Johnson County — an older, established Texas city with a historic downtown, a livestock show tradition, and a strong sense of local identity that differs noticeably from the fast-growth suburbs to the north. The school district reflects that character: this is a community where the same families have sent kids through Cleburne High School for generations, where Yellow Jackets football draws a real hometown crowd, and where parent booster organizations for athletics and fine arts are active and well-established.

Parent sentiment in forums and local Facebook groups tends to be positive about individual teachers and coaches — the district's average teacher experience of 12.6 years (above the state average of 11.1) suggests a more stable teaching workforce than some fast-growth districts where turnover is high. Criticisms tend to focus on aging facility conditions (being addressed by the 2025 bond proposal), the academic performance gap compared to higher-rated neighbors, and the resource constraints that come with serving a majority economically disadvantaged population.

For relocating families, Cleburne ISD occupies a specific niche: it's the district you choose when Cleburne the city is already the right choice for your family's budget and lifestyle, and you want to understand what the schools realistically look like. The graduation rate, the CTE opportunities, and the lower tax rate are genuine positives. The TEA C rating and below-average college readiness metrics are genuine considerations. Families who go in with clear eyes on both sides tend to be satisfied with what they find.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does a Cleburne mailing address guarantee my child is in Cleburne ISD? ▾
No — not automatically. Johnson County contains multiple school districts, and some properties with Cleburne city addresses or ZIP codes may be zoned to Joshua ISD or another neighboring district. Rural acreage and new construction on the city's outskirts are the most common cases where addresses don't match expected district boundaries. Always verify your specific address using Cleburne ISD's official Find My School Zone tool before making an offer.
Why does Cleburne ISD have a C TEA rating but a 95.3% graduation rate? ▾
These two metrics measure different things. The TEA A–F rating is a composite score that weights STAAR test performance, college readiness measures (SAT/ACT benchmarks, dual credit, industry certifications), and year-over-year academic growth. Cleburne scores lower on test performance and college readiness benchmarks than the state average. The graduation rate measures whether students who enter 9th grade earn a diploma by 12th grade — Cleburne excels here, at 95.3% vs. a state average of 90.3%. Both numbers are true simultaneously: the district keeps kids in school through graduation at an above-average rate, while test scores and college readiness metrics trail the state. Neither number tells the full story on its own.
How does Cleburne ISD's tax rate compare to nearby districts? ▾
Cleburne ISD's 2024 certified total tax rate is $1.17 per $100 assessed value — lower than both Joshua ISD ($1.26) and Burleson ISD ($1.26). On a $350,000 home, after applying Texas's $100,000 homestead exemption, Cleburne ISD taxes would be approximately $2,925/year ($244/month), versus approximately $3,150/year for the same home in Joshua or Burleson ISD. That's roughly $225/year in annual savings. Tax rates can change following bond elections — verify the current rate directly with Cleburne ISD before closing, especially given the 2025 bond proposal.
What CTE pathways does Cleburne High School offer? ▾
Cleburne High School has a comprehensive CTE (Career and Technical Education) program with pathways across multiple career clusters including Agriculture Science, Business and Finance, Health Science, Law Enforcement, and Culinary Arts, among others. Students can earn industry certifications before graduation. CTE is available to all CHS students — no application or lottery is required. Dual credit courses with Hill College and Tarleton State University are also available for eligible students. For the full current pathway list, visit chs.c-isd.com/career-technical-education.
My child has an IEP — how does transferring to Cleburne ISD work? ▾
Under Texas law, when a student with an existing IEP transfers to a new district, Cleburne ISD is required to convene an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days to review, adopt, or modify the IEP based on available resources. Bring all prior IEP documents, 504 plans, evaluations, and assessment records to enrollment. Contact Cleburne ISD's Special Education department at (817) 202-1100 as early as possible — ideally before you close on the home — to discuss your child's needs and the district's available services. The quality and fit of special education services can vary significantly by campus and by the nature of your child's needs; a direct conversation is the most reliable way to assess fit.
Is bilingual education available in Cleburne ISD? ▾
Yes, but not at every elementary campus. Bilingual pre-K is specifically available at Cooke Elementary, Irving Elementary, and Santa Fe Elementary — three of the seven elementary campuses. ESL (English as a Second Language) services are available districtwide at all levels for students who qualify. Approximately 24.8% of Cleburne students are enrolled in bilingual or ELL programs — matching the state average. If bilingual pre-K instruction is a priority for your family, verify that your home address feeds to one of the three bilingual campuses before purchasing.
Does Cleburne ISD offer an alternative to the main high school? ▾
Yes — TEAM School is a separate alternative high school campus within Cleburne ISD designed for students who benefit from a smaller, more personalized learning environment than Cleburne High School. It is a distinct campus with its own focus and structure, not simply a program housed inside the main building. Students who are at risk of not graduating in a traditional setting often find TEAM School a better fit. This is one of the reasons Cleburne ISD's graduation rate is as high as it is — the district actively maintains pathways for students who need a different approach to finishing.
