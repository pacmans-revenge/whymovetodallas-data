District Overview

Melissa Independent School District is a fast-growing North Collin County district that has managed something genuinely unusual: it has kept the characteristics families associate with small-town Texas schooling — tight-knit campuses, high visibility for every student — while building a programmatic profile that includes an IB Diploma Programme at the high school, a dedicated alternative education campus, and a college credit completion rate that rivals much larger districts. With approximately 6,700 students across 10 campuses, the district earned a TEA A rating in 2024 and an A− from Niche — results that place it above many of its Collin County peers in the growth corridor.

The district serves the city of Melissa as its primary community, with boundary coverage extending into portions of Anna and Van Alstyne. Families purchasing in the Melissa area — particularly in newer subdivisions north of US-75 — should verify their campus assignment carefully, as growth has required multiple boundary adjustments in recent years. The district central office is located at 1904 Cooper St, Melissa, TX 75454 (972-837-2411).

Melissa ISD's academic performance is anchored by a genuine commitment to college and career readiness: roughly half of each graduating class earns college credit before walking across the stage, a metric the district actively promotes and tracks. That number — roughly 50% of graduates — is the strongest summary of what the district is trying to do academically. The IB Diploma Programme at Melissa High School, rare at a campus of approximately 1,500 students, provides a structured pathway for motivated students that few comparably sized districts can match.

Best fit for families who:

  • Want a smaller, community-rooted district where students are individually known by teachers and staff
  • Value a high school that offers an IB Diploma Programme without requiring a move to a large suburban district
  • Prioritize college readiness — AP, dual credit, and IB together create multiple credit-earning pathways
  • Are drawn to the Melissa area's housing value and relative affordability compared to southern Collin County
  • Prefer a district still in a growth phase, where new facilities and programs are actively being added

May not be the best fit if:

  • Your family needs the breadth of specialized programs (dual language, magnet campuses) that only large districts can sustain across multiple campuses
  • You want an established high school with decades of UIL athletics history and deep sports culture
  • Commute geometry matters — Melissa sits at the northern edge of Collin County, adding meaningful drive time for families working in Plano, Frisco, or Dallas
  • You prefer a district with more stable enrollment projections — rapid growth means ongoing boundary shifts and new campus openings

TEA Accountability Rating

The Texas Education Agency assigns A–F accountability ratings to every Texas public school district and campus annually, based on three performance domains. Melissa ISD earned a TEA A rating in 2024 with a composite score of approximately 90 out of 100 — a result that places it among the higher-performing growth-phase districts in Collin County.

What the three domains measure:

  • Student Achievement — STAAR test scores, graduation rates, college/career readiness indicators
  • School Progress — year-over-year academic growth compared to similar campuses
  • Closing the Gaps — performance equity across student demographic groups

A TEA A in a rapidly growing district is a meaningful outcome. Many fast-growth districts experience temporary rating dips as new campuses open, enrollment spikes mid-year, and teacher turnover increases. Melissa ISD has maintained its A rating through a period of significant growth — a signal that district leadership and campus operations are keeping pace with expansion. Campus-level ratings for all 10 campuses are available at txschools.gov.

Niche, a third-party school review platform, assigns Melissa ISD an overall grade of A−, with strong marks in academics, teachers, and college prep. Third-party grades are directionally useful for comparison purposes; always cross-reference with official TEA data for accountability decisions.

College & Career Readiness

The district's most compelling academic claim is its college credit completion rate: approximately half of each graduating class earns college credit while still in high school, according to district-reported data. That figure represents students earning credit through three distinct pathways — IB courses and exams, AP courses and qualifying exam scores, and dual credit enrollment. For families prioritizing post-secondary preparation, three credit-earning tracks in a single district is a meaningful differentiator at Melissa ISD's enrollment size.

IB Diploma Programme

Melissa High School is an authorized IB World School offering the IB Diploma Programme (DP) — a globally recognized two-year pre-university program for students in grades 11 and 12. IB DP candidates complete six subject courses across academic areas, the Theory of Knowledge seminar, an Extended Essay, and CAS (Creativity, Activity, Service) requirements. Students who complete the full Diploma — and earn qualifying scores on IB external exams — receive internationally recognized transcripts that many universities credit toward freshman year coursework. Having IB DP at a campus of approximately 1,500 students is uncommon in North Texas; the program typically appears in larger suburban districts or as a magnet program at select campuses.

Advanced Placement

Melissa High School offers Advanced Placement courses across core academic disciplines. AP participation and pass rate data are reported annually to TEA as part of the college/career readiness accountability framework. Students who prefer the structure of AP over IB can pursue college credit without enrolling in the full Diploma Programme — and students can combine AP and IB coursework based on their schedule and goals.

Dual Credit

Dual credit enrollment is available for eligible upperclassmen through partnerships with area community colleges. Dual credit courses allow students to earn simultaneously transferable college credit — often at low or no cost — while completing high school requirements. Like AP, dual credit is one of the three pathways contributing to the district's ~50% college credit completion rate.

STAAR & District Rankings

SchoolDigger, a third-party data aggregator, ranks Melissa ISD in the top 50 out of approximately 965 Texas public school districts based on STAAR performance data. That ranking is directionally consistent with the TEA A rating. STAAR proficiency data by campus and subject are available at txschools.gov. At the individual campus level, performance varies — elementary campuses with newer populations and higher mobility typically show more variability than established campuses. Verify your child's specific campus before relying on district averages.

Campus Highlights

Melissa ISD operates 10 campuses — five elementary schools, one standalone Sixth Grade Center, one middle school (grades 7–8), one high school, and two alternative education campuses. The standalone Sixth Grade Center is an unusual campus structure in DFW; most districts send sixth graders to a combined 6–8 middle school. In Melissa, sixth grade is its own separate building and experience — a transitional year between elementary and true middle school that some families find beneficial and others consider an extra move.

Special Programs

Melissa ISD's program depth is notable for a district of its size. The combination of IB Diploma, AP, and dual credit at the secondary level creates multiple structured pathways to college credit. Below are the key programs available district-wide or at specific campuses.

IB Diploma Programme

Two-year pre-university program at Melissa High School for grades 11–12. Students complete six subject courses, Theory of Knowledge, Extended Essay, and CAS. Qualifying IB exam scores may earn college credit at universities worldwide. One of relatively few IB DP offerings at a Texas campus in the 1,000–2,000 student range.

Advanced Placement (AP)

AP courses available at Melissa High School across core academic disciplines — English, math, sciences, and history. AP exam scores of 3 or higher may earn college credit. Students can combine AP and IB depending on schedule and goals. Participation and exam data are reported to TEA annually.

Dual Credit

Eligible upperclassmen can enroll in dual credit courses through area college partnerships, earning simultaneously transferable credit. Often available at low or no out-of-pocket cost. One of the three credit-earning pathways contributing to the ~50% college credit completion rate per graduating class.

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

CTE pathways at Melissa High School align with TEA program requirements and industry certification opportunities. CTE participation contributes to College, Career, and Military Readiness (CCMR) accountability metrics. Specific program offerings vary by year — verify current pathways with the high school counselor directly.

Gifted & Talented (GT)

GT identification begins at the elementary level through a multi-criteria assessment process, as required by Texas Education Code. GT services continue through middle and high school via advanced coursework. Families transferring into the district should note that Texas law allows districts to re-evaluate GT identification — the ARD/GT process typically completes within 30 days of enrollment.

Fine Arts & Athletics

Band, choir, orchestra, and visual arts programs operate at multiple campus levels. UIL fine arts and athletics competition participation begins at middle school. Melissa High School's Cardinals compete in UIL athletics; verify current classification with the district as enrollment growth affects UIL assignment cycles.

Special Education

Special education services are available district-wide as required by Texas law and IDEA. Families transferring students with existing IEPs or 504 plans should contact the Special Services department directly — (972) 837-2411 — prior to enrollment. Texas law requires an ARD meeting within 30 days of enrollment to review and adopt or revise an existing IEP. Parent experiences with SpEd services vary by campus and student need; direct conversation with the district is the most reliable path to evaluating fit for a specific student profile.

Alternative Education

Cardinal Renaissance Academy and Melissa Ridge Education Center provide alternative education settings. Cardinal Renaissance Academy is specifically structured for credit recovery — ensuring students who fall off track academically still have a clear path to graduation. This dedicated infrastructure reflects district investment in completion rates across all student populations.

Enrollment & Transfer Policy

How to Enroll

Melissa ISD enrollment is managed through the district's central office at 1904 Cooper St, Melissa, TX 75454. Families relocating to the area should contact the district at (972) 837-2411 or visit melissaisd.org for current enrollment instructions, required documents, and school-year timelines. Online enrollment is available through the district portal. Required documents typically include proof of residence, immunization records, and prior school records — verify current requirements directly with the district for the 2025–26 school year.

Transfer Policy

Melissa ISD accepts inter-district transfer requests on a space-available basis. Families living outside the district boundaries who wish to enroll should contact the district directly to inquire about transfer availability for the target grade and campus. Transfer approvals are not guaranteed and are typically reviewed each enrollment cycle. Intra-district transfers between campuses are also handled through the district office.

Pre-K Availability

Pre-kindergarten is available for eligible students at select campuses. Texas pre-K eligibility criteria include income thresholds, military family status, and other qualifying factors defined by the Texas Education Code. Contact the district to verify current Pre-K campus locations and eligibility requirements for the upcoming school year.

IEP & 504 Transfers

Texas law requires an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting within 30 days of enrollment for students transferring with an existing IEP. During that meeting, the district team reviews the student's current IEP and determines whether to adopt it as written or propose revisions. Families transferring students with existing IEPs should bring the complete IEP documentation and any recent evaluations to their first district contact. For 504 plan transfers, a similar review process applies — timelines may vary. Contact Melissa ISD Special Services at (972) 837-2411 to coordinate before the first day of attendance.

District Boundaries

Melissa ISD's service area centers on the city of Melissa in northern Collin County, with boundary coverage extending into portions of Anna and Van Alstyne. The district is bordered by Anna ISD to the east and south, Van Alstyne ISD to the north, and the outer edges of McKinney ISD territory to the southwest — a geography that can create ambiguity for families purchasing homes near any of those boundary lines.

Boundary Verification — Required Before Making an Offer

Melissa ISD's rapid residential growth has required repeated boundary adjustments at the elementary campus level. The official interactive zoning tool is available at melissaisd.org/page/interactive-zoning-map. Enter the specific property address — not the subdivision name or general area — to confirm which campus is currently assigned. Contact the district enrollment office at (972) 837-2411 to confirm verbally before closing.

Additionally, homes marketed as "Melissa address" sometimes sit within Anna ISD boundaries or Van Alstyne ISD boundaries — the postal address and the school district do not always match in this part of Collin County. Always verify using the TEA School District Locator (tea.texas.gov) alongside the district's own map.

The district's Bond 2025 program is funding new and expanded facilities to accommodate ongoing enrollment growth. New campus openings may trigger boundary redraws even after you confirm an assignment — if your child is entering a grade that feeds into a new campus opening in the next 1–2 years, ask the district about any anticipated boundary changes before that grade.

Budget & Tax Rate

$1.26 Tax Rate per $100 (2025)
Bond 2025 Active Facilities Program

Melissa ISD's 2025 certified total tax rate is approximately $1.26 per $100 of assessed value. In practical terms: on a $400,000 home, the district portion of the annual property tax bill is roughly $5,040 per year, or approximately $420 per month added to a mortgage payment. On a $500,000 home, that figure rises to approximately $6,300 per year / $525 per month. (These are illustrative estimates — actual tax liability depends on assessed value, homestead exemption, and Collin County appraisal district determinations.)

The $1.26 rate positions Melissa ISD slightly above some of the larger, more resource-rich Collin County districts (McKinney ISD is approximately $1.22), but roughly in line with Anna ISD (~$1.28) given the similar scale and growth pressures both districts face. Smaller, faster-growing districts often carry higher rates than large established peers because their per-pupil property value base is still catching up to their facilities and staffing costs.

The district's Bond 2025 program is actively funding new campus construction and renovation to accommodate enrollment growth. Bond programs are separate from operating tax rates — verify the current total rate with the Collin County Appraisal District or the district's business office before purchasing, as bond elections can affect the I&S (interest and sinking) portion of the rate.

Per-pupil expenditure data are reported annually by TEA through PEIMS and available at txschools.gov.

Community & Culture

Melissa ISD has the feel of a small-town district that is actively working to retain that identity while managing the pressures of rapid suburban expansion. The Cardinal pride culture — shared across all secondary campuses through athletics, fine arts, and UIL competition — creates a cohesive community identity even as enrollment grows. Families who move from larger suburban districts often note that Melissa's school community feels more personally connected: teachers know students by name, and parental involvement is high relative to the district's size.

The city of Melissa is a community still finding its final shape — a mix of long-established neighborhoods near the downtown core and new master-planned subdivisions pushing north and east. That contrast between established Melissa and new-build Melissa creates some cultural variability across elementary attendance zones. Families purchasing in the newer areas should expect a campus community that is itself relatively new, with a higher proportion of relocating families and a school culture still establishing its traditions.

Parent sentiment captured from community discussions is generally positive about academic rigor at the high school level, particularly around the IB and AP programs. Common concerns center on growth management — including boundary changes, portable classrooms at high-enrollment campuses, and the pace of new facility delivery relative to enrollment increases. The Bond 2025 program is specifically intended to address facilities needs, and its progress is worth monitoring during your due diligence.

Unique District Characteristics — Four Worth Knowing

1. IB DP at a small high school. Melissa High School is an IB World School offering the full Diploma Programme at a campus of approximately 1,500 students — an unusual combination in North Texas, where IB programs typically appear in much larger districts or as selective magnet programs.

2. Standalone Sixth Grade Center. Rather than a traditional 6–8 middle school, Melissa ISD routes all sixth graders to a separate campus before East Cardinal MS for grades 7–8. This structural choice creates a distinct transitional year that some families find beneficial — and is a differentiating experience worth understanding before selecting a home in the district.

3. ~50% college credit completion rate. The district actively tracks and promotes the share of graduates who earn college credit before high school graduation. Three concurrent pathways — IB, AP, and dual credit — create multiple routes to that outcome across different student profiles.

4. Cardinal Renaissance Academy. A dedicated alternative education campus with structured credit-recovery programming reflects a deliberate district investment in graduation completion across all student populations — not a temporary measure, but a purpose-built campus with its own identity and staff.
