Azle ISD serves the communities of Azle, Lakeside, Pelican Bay, Reno, and Sanctuary — a stretch of Parker County just northwest of Fort Worth where suburban neighborhoods give way to Texas Hill Country character. With roughly 7,200 students across 12 campuses, Azle is a mid-sized district that offers the tight-knit feel of a smaller school community without being too small to support meaningful extracurricular programming and career pathways. The district's TEA C rating (78/100 in 2024) is an honest tradeoff families should weigh upfront: this is not a top-tier academic district by the metrics, but it earned seven TEA Distinction Designations, meaning specific campuses and programs outperform expectations. Families who prioritize affordability, CTE career pathways, and a genuine small-community feel over prestige rankings consistently rank Azle among their top shortlist options in the Lake Worth/Azle corridor.

The strongest honest tradeoff: STAAR proficiency scores — 55% reading and 42% math district-wide in recent testing — sit below state averages, which means families with students who need strong academic scaffolding may find the experience uneven depending on campus. Azle's value proposition is strongest for families who don't have children in the narrow window where STAAR performance predicts post-secondary outcomes most sharply, or who are drawn to CTE, dual enrollment, and vocational pathways over a traditional AP-heavy academic culture.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Value community character and smaller-district cohesion over brand-name rankings
  • Have students interested in CTE, trades, dual enrollment at Tarleton State, or vocational pathways
  • Are buying in the Azle, Lakeside, or Pelican Bay area where housing costs significantly undercut Keller or Northwest ISD territory
  • Want a 95% graduation rate and strong extracurriculars without the housing premium of higher-rated suburban districts
  • Appreciate the Hornet pride culture — Azle sports programs are deeply community-supported

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • Your family prioritizes top STAAR/TAKS proficiency rates and college-prep academic track density
  • You are buying specifically for a district with an A-rated TEA score as a primary driver
  • You need a large number of AP/IB sections or access to a dedicated International Baccalaureate campus
  • Your child has complex special education needs that require verifying campus-level resources before committing
⚠ Boundary Verification Required

Not every home with an Azle or Reno address falls within Azle ISD boundaries. The Reno community in Parker County is notably split — some addresses route to Eagle Mountain–Saginaw ISD (EMS ISD) instead of Azle ISD. Eagle Heights, though a Fort Worth address, is served by Azle ISD. Always verify your specific address at azleisd.net or through the district's enrollment office before making an offer.

2024 TEA Accountability Rating

Azle ISD received a C rating (78/100) from the Texas Education Agency in 2024 — its most recently published A-F accountability score. TEA rates districts on three domains, each weighted to reflect different aspects of student performance.

79 Student Achievement
Measures how well students meet grade-level standards on STAAR and end-of-course assessments.
78 School Progress
Tracks improvement in student achievement over time and relative to peers with similar demographics.
76 Closing the Gaps
Evaluates how well the district narrows performance gaps between student groups.

All three domain scores cluster tightly in the upper 70s, which tells an important story: Azle ISD's performance is consistent across subgroups, not inflated by a high-performing subset masking gaps elsewhere. The C overall score reflects a district that is functional and improving but has not yet reached the 80-point B threshold that many relocating families use as a benchmark. For context, a district moving from a C (78) to a B (80) on Student Achievement typically means a meaningful lift in STAAR passage rates across core subjects — something Azle ISD's improving trend data suggests is within reach over a 2–3 year window.

7 Distinction Designations

Despite the C overall rating, Azle ISD earned 7 Distinction Designations from TEA — campus- and subject-level awards for outstanding performance. Distinction Designations mean specific campuses or programs are performing well above comparison group averages, even if the district-wide STAAR scores pull the overall rating down. Ask the district which specific campuses and subjects earned designations, as this changes annually.

STAAR proficiency rates — 55% reading and 42% math — sit below the statewide Texas average, which is important context for families accustomed to higher-performing suburban districts. What these numbers mean in practice: roughly half of Azle ISD students are meeting grade-level expectations on state assessments in reading, and fewer in math. For families with students who have historically been in the top academic tier, verify with individual campuses how they differentiate instruction for advanced learners, since district-wide metrics can obscure campus-level variation.

The district's 95% graduation rate, however, is genuinely strong and suggests that Azle ISD does an effective job of keeping students on track through high school completion — even if post-secondary readiness metrics are mixed. The average SAT score of 1,120 and ACT score of 24 are consistent with a mid-tier suburban district focused on broad college and career readiness rather than selective college admissions pipelines. Azle High School offers AP coursework, dual enrollment through Tarleton State University, and CTE programs that align well with students targeting workforce entry, community college, or regional universities.

Campuses

Azle ISD operates 12 campuses serving students from pre-K through grade 12. Below are the key campuses relocating families ask about most.

Azle High School

High School (9–12) ~2,000 students Hornet Stadium

The district's sole high school serves all Azle ISD students grades 9–12. Home to the Hornets, a strong CTE program, AP courses, and dual enrollment through Tarleton State University. Football and fine arts programs are central to campus culture.

Azle Junior High School

Junior High (7–8) Azle ISD

Serves seventh and eighth graders in the district's primary feeder pattern. Students transition here from the district's elementary campuses. Offers core academics, elective rotations, and introductory CTE exploratory courses.

Forte Junior High School

Junior High (6–8) Azle ISD

A second junior high campus serving the district's growing population in northern attendance zones. Verify which junior high your address feeds to with the district — the feeder pattern depends on your specific neighborhood, not just your city.

Cross Timbers Elementary School

Elementary (PK–6) Azle ISD

One of the district's established elementary campuses, serving the Cross Timbers community west of Azle. Pre-K through 6th grade in a neighborhood-focused setting typical of Azle ISD's smaller elementary campuses.

Silver Creek Elementary School

Elementary (PK–6) Azle ISD

Serves families in the Silver Creek area. Consistent with Azle ISD's elementary model — smaller enrollment than many DFW-area elementaries, which contributes to the community feel that Azle families often cite as a primary draw.

Eagle Heights Elementary School

Elementary (PK–6) Fort Worth Address / Azle ISD

Uniquely, Eagle Heights Elementary has a Fort Worth mailing address but is served by Azle ISD — not Fort Worth ISD. This is a boundary nuance that catches families off guard. Always confirm with the district if you're buying in the Eagle Heights area.

Walnut Creek Elementary School

Elementary (PK–6) Azle ISD

Serves the Walnut Creek corridor. Part of the district's core elementary feeder pattern. The smaller campus size (typical for Azle ISD elementaries) allows for a high level of parent engagement and staff familiarity with individual students.

Hornet Academy

Alternative Education Azle ISD District-wide access

Azle ISD's alternative education program provides flexible pathways for students who benefit from a non-traditional learning environment. District-wide enrollment eligibility. Contact the district's counseling department for referral and enrollment process details.

Specialty Programs

Azle ISD's strongest differentiated programs are in Career and Technical Education — a deliberate district investment that reflects the workforce profile of Parker County families.

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

Campus: Azle High School (districtwide access). CTE is Azle ISD's signature strength, offering pathways in health sciences, technology, agriculture, and trades. Students can earn industry certifications while completing their diploma. No application required — all Azle High students have access through course selection. Transportation is standard school-day scheduling.

Dual Enrollment — Tarleton State University

Campus: Azle High School. Eligible juniors and seniors can earn college credit through Tarleton State University courses taught on campus. Students must meet GPA and TSI eligibility requirements. No lottery — enrollment is eligibility-based. A cost-effective path to college hours before graduation.

Gifted & Talented (GT) Program

Campuses: District-wide, K–12. GT identification begins in elementary school and continues through high school. Students are identified through a multi-criteria process. Families should contact the district's GT coordinator to understand how the program is structured at each campus level, as the service delivery model can vary by campus.

Advanced Placement (AP)

Campus: Azle High School. AP courses are available across core subject areas for students seeking college credit through College Board exams. The selection of AP course offerings is consistent with a district of Azle ISD's size — not as extensive as Keller or Northwest ISD, but sufficient for motivated students targeting a broad range of subjects.

Hornet Academy (Alternative Education)

Campus: Dedicated facility, district-wide eligibility. Provides an alternative learning environment for students who benefit from smaller class sizes, flexible scheduling, or non-traditional instructional approaches. Referral process through campus counseling. No application lottery — placement is need-based through district coordination.

How to Enroll in Azle ISD

Azle ISD conducts new student enrollment on an annual cycle. For the 2025–26 school year, contact the district to confirm current enrollment windows.

  1. Confirm your address is in Azle ISD boundaries — use the district locator at azleisd.net or call the enrollment office before beginning any paperwork.
  2. Gather required documents: proof of residency (utility bill, lease, or closing documents dated within 60 days), child's birth certificate, current immunization records (TDap required for 7th grade entry), and most recent school records/report card.
  3. Register online through the Azle ISD enrollment portal at azleisd.net. New student paperwork is typically submitted through the district's online platform, with campus staff completing the process.
  4. Enrollment window: Online registration typically opens in the spring for the following school year. Mid-year and new resident enrollment is accepted on a rolling basis — contact the campus directly to schedule a start date.
  5. Intra-district transfers: Families requesting a campus other than their attendance-zone school must submit a transfer request. Approval is not guaranteed and depends on campus capacity. Contact the district's administrative office for the current transfer request timeline.
IEP / 504 / Special Education Transfers

Under Texas law, when a student with an active IEP or 504 plan transfers into Azle ISD, the district must convene an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee meeting within 30 days of enrollment to review and adapt the existing plan. Bring all prior IEP/504 documentation, evaluation reports, and services summaries to enrollment. Contact Azle ISD's Special Services office directly at (817) 444-3050 before finalizing your move if your child has complex or high-frequency service needs — campus-level resources and staff vary.

Boundary Verification & Address Lookup

Azle ISD's boundary is more complex than its geographic footprint suggests. The district serves communities across Parker County and portions of Tarrant County — and those two counties do not always align neatly with city limits or ZIP codes.

Communities Served

The core Azle ISD service area includes the City of Azle, Lakeside, Pelican Bay, and Sanctuary (all in Tarrant or Parker County). Reno is partially served — the city straddles the Azle ISD and Eagle Mountain–Saginaw ISD (EMS ISD) boundary, and not all Reno addresses fall in Azle ISD. Eagle Heights, despite carrying a Fort Worth mailing address, is served by Azle ISD rather than Fort Worth ISD — a common source of confusion for buyers searching Fort Worth-addressed homes near the city's northwest edge.

Common Edge Cases

  • Reno, TX: Significant portions of Reno are zoned to EMS ISD, not Azle ISD. The dividing line is not obvious from a street map. Verify every Reno address individually.
  • Eagle Heights: Fort Worth mailing address but within Azle ISD boundaries. Buyers who filter by "Azle" city may miss available homes here; buyers who filter by "Fort Worth" may not realize their children would attend Azle ISD.
  • Parker County edge areas: New construction on the western and northern edges of Azle ISD territory may fall in a different district. Always ask your builder which ISD the specific subdivision feeds.
How to Verify Your Address

Contact Azle ISD directly at (817) 444-3050 or visit azleisd.net to confirm your specific address. You can also use the TEA school locator at tea.texas.gov/apps/school-directory as a secondary check. Making an offer without verifying your specific address can mean your child attends a different school than you planned — and boundary maps do not always match common-sense geography.

Property Taxes & School Finances

$1.0979 2024 Total Tax Rate / $100
$0.7487 Maintenance & Operations Rate
$0.3492 Interest & Sinking Rate
$10,772 Per-Pupil Expenditure

Azle ISD's 2024 certified total tax rate of $1.0979 per $100 of assessed value puts it in the mid-range for DFW school districts. On a $350,000 home (a reasonable entry point for Azle ISD territory), the school district portion of your annual tax bill works out to approximately $3,843 — or roughly $320/month added to a mortgage payment. Applying Texas's $100,000 homestead exemption reduces the assessed taxable value to $250,000, bringing annual school taxes to approximately $2,745. Always verify rates with the Parker County Appraisal District for your specific property.

The I&S rate of $0.3492 reflects debt service from recent bond elections — bond-funded improvements are visible on campuses across the district. The per-pupil expenditure of $10,772 is consistent with mid-sized Texas ISDs and slightly above state median, reflecting the higher operational costs of a geographically distributed district with multiple small campuses.

Compared to neighboring districts: Azle ISD's $1.0979 rate sits higher than Keller ISD and Northwest ISD on paper, but Azle ISD territory home prices are meaningfully lower — so the actual dollar amount per home in annual school taxes often ends up comparable or lower than what families pay in pricier districts. The real cost question is total housing + tax burden, not just the rate.

Community Feel & School Culture

Azle ISD has a distinct identity that separates it from the anonymous suburban mega-districts in the DFW corridor. The Hornets brand runs deep — parents at Azle High School describe a Friday night football culture that feels more like small-town Texas than suburban sprawl, with multi-generational families who attended Azle ISD themselves now raising children there. Booster clubs are active, parent volunteerism is high relative to district size, and staff turnover at the elementary level tends to be lower than at larger neighboring districts.

The CTE program has created a specific community around students pursuing trades, health sciences, and technology careers — a counternarrative to the college-prep-only culture that dominates marketing at higher-rated suburban ISDs. Parents on DFW relocation forums consistently note that the district feels "real" in a way that resonates with families who are skeptical of the prestige-district arms race. The tradeoff is that if your child's college plan centers on highly selective admissions, the AP section density and campus resources at Azle High may require supplementing with dual enrollment or outside coursework.

One unique characteristic of Azle ISD: the geographic spread of its territory creates meaningful campus-to-campus variation in student demographics and campus culture. Eagle Heights families may experience a somewhat different campus culture than Walnut Creek families, even within the same district. This is worth exploring by visiting multiple campuses if you have a choice of address within the district. Parent community groups on Facebook (Azle Area Community and Azle ISD Parents groups) offer unfiltered perspectives on individual campuses that district data alone can't provide.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my Azle or Reno address guarantee enrollment in Azle ISD? ▾
No — and this is one of the most common mistakes buyers make in this area. The City of Reno in Parker County is split between Azle ISD and Eagle Mountain–Saginaw ISD (EMS ISD), and the dividing line is not intuitive from a street map. Some Reno addresses also route to Springtown ISD. Additionally, some Fort Worth-addressed homes near the Eagle Heights community fall within Azle ISD rather than Fort Worth ISD. Always verify your specific address with Azle ISD's enrollment office at (817) 444-3050 or at azleisd.net before making an offer. This is not a matter of form — boundaries can differ by individual street or even block.
Does Azle ISD have more than one high school? ▾
No. Azle High School (AHS) is the district's sole high school, serving all students in grades 9–12. This is an important distinction for families coming from multi-high-school districts like Keller ISD or Northwest ISD, where students may have zoned differences in campus resources and programs. Every Azle ISD student will attend the same high school, which creates campus-wide consistency but also means the school serves a wide range of academic interests and learning profiles under one roof. The campus culture is broadly welcoming of this diversity.
How does Azle ISD handle special education and IEP transfers? ▾
Under Texas law (and federal IDEA requirements), when a student with an active IEP transfers to a new district, the receiving district has 30 days to convene an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee meeting to review the existing plan and determine how services will be provided. Azle ISD's Special Services office handles these transitions. Bring all prior IEP documentation, evaluation reports, and any behavioral support plans to your enrollment appointment. For students with intensive or high-frequency service needs, contact the district's Special Services coordinator before your move to understand which campus is best positioned to serve your child — resources vary by campus. Azle ISD's phone number is (817) 444-3050.
Why does Azle ISD have a C TEA rating but a B Niche grade? ▾
TEA's A-F rating is based primarily on STAAR assessment proficiency rates and specific data domains measured by the state of Texas. Niche's grading system incorporates a broader set of factors including teacher quality scores, student-teacher ratios, college readiness data, parent and student reviews, and extracurricular offerings — weighted differently than TEA's framework. Neither is "right" — they measure different things. TEA data is the most legally relevant because it determines accountability interventions; Niche data offers a broader community sentiment picture. Families should look at both and weigh them according to what matters most for their student's specific needs.
What is Azle ISD's transfer policy? ▾
Azle ISD accepts both intra-district transfers (requesting a campus other than your attendance-zone school within Azle ISD) and inter-district transfers (students residing outside Azle ISD boundaries requesting enrollment). Intra-district transfers are processed through the district administrative office and depend on space availability at the requested campus. Inter-district transfers require approval from both your home district (release) and Azle ISD (acceptance) and are subject to space constraints and district policy. Transfer requests are typically processed on an annual cycle — contact the district at (817) 444-3050 for current deadlines and availability for the 2025–26 school year.
How does Azle ISD's tax rate compare to neighboring districts? ▾
Azle ISD's 2024 certified total rate of $1.0979 per $100 of assessed value sits mid-range compared to nearby districts: Keller ISD is lower at approximately $1.0630, Northwest ISD is higher at approximately $1.1075, and EMS ISD is comparable at approximately $1.0748. However, the rate is only part of the equation — Azle ISD territory home prices are meaningfully lower than Keller or Northwest ISD territory, so the actual dollar amount per household in annual school taxes is often comparable or lower even at a similar rate. Texas's $100,000 homestead exemption applies in all districts and reduces the assessed taxable value of your primary residence before the rate is applied.
What makes Azle ISD's CTE program stand out compared to larger neighboring districts? ▾
Azle ISD has invested disproportionately in CTE relative to its overall size. The program at Azle High School offers pathways in health sciences, agriculture (including FFA, which is highly competitive in Parker County), technology, and skilled trades — with the ability to earn industry certifications alongside a diploma. The Tarleton State University dual enrollment partnership adds a college credit layer for eligible students. What distinguishes Azle's CTE from larger districts is not the breadth of offerings (Keller or Northwest have more sections) but the culture: CTE pathways at Azle are central to the school's identity, not supplemental. Students pursuing these pathways tend to have strong peer community and visible career outcomes that make the programming feel purposeful rather than peripheral.
