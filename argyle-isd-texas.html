District Overview

Argyle Independent School District is one of those rare North Texas districts that consistently earns exceptional academic outcomes while remaining small enough that students are known by name, not number. With approximately 8,500 students across 10 campuses, Argyle ISD holds a TEA A rating (score: 92) and an A+ from Niche — performance marks that typically belong to much larger, more resource-rich districts.

The district serves the city of Argyle and the rapidly expanding Northlake area, including major master-planned communities such as Harvest and Pecan Square. Portions of Bartonville and Justin also fall within district boundaries. The service area sits in southern Denton County, roughly between Flower Mound to the southeast and Decatur to the northwest — with easy access to the Alliance Corridor, Fort Worth's North Side, and the DFW Metroplex via I-35W and FM 407.

What makes Argyle ISD stand out is the combination of strong community identity, high graduation rates (99.1% — among the highest in Texas), and a culture of excellence that has persisted even as Northlake's explosive growth has pushed enrollment well beyond what the district looked like a decade ago. The central administrative offices are located at 800 Eagle Drive, Argyle, TX 76226.

Key Cities Served

Argyle (primary), Northlake (Harvest and Pecan Square communities), portions of Bartonville and Justin. The district boundary has shifted as new campuses have opened — verify your specific address before making an offer on any home in the Northlake or Bartonville area.

TEA Accountability Rating

The Texas Education Agency assigns every school district and campus an annual A–F accountability rating based on three domains. Argyle ISD earned a TEA A rating with a score of 92 out of 100 for the 2024–25 school year — a result that places it among the highest-performing smaller districts in the state.

What the three domains measure:

  • Student Achievement — STAAR test scores, graduation rates, college/career readiness indicators
  • School Progress — year-over-year academic growth compared to similar campuses
  • Closing the Gaps — performance equity across student demographic groups

A score of 92 is particularly notable because Argyle ISD is achieving it while absorbing one of the fastest growth rates in Denton County. Districts in rapid-growth phases often see temporary rating dips as new campuses ramp up and new students arrive mid-year — Argyle ISD has so far maintained its rating through that growth curve, a testament to district planning and campus leadership. Campus-level ratings for all 10 campuses are available at txschools.gov.

Argyle ISD has also received TEA Distinction Designations in multiple categories including Academic Achievement in core subjects and Comparative Academic Growth — designations awarded only to the top 25% of similar districts statewide.

Graduation Rate

Argyle ISD's four-year graduation rate of 99.1% is among the highest recorded by any Texas ISD — large or small. Nearly every student who enters Argyle High School as a freshman walks across the stage at graduation. This is not a statistical artifact of a tiny class size; it reflects a culture of expectation, support, and engagement that permeates the district from elementary through high school.

Advanced Placement

Argyle High School offers a robust Advanced Placement (AP) curriculum. Students can earn college credit through AP exam scores, with the district tracking both participation and pass rates as part of its college readiness accountability. AP course availability spans core academic subjects — English, math, sciences, history, and select electives. Dual credit through North Central Texas College (NCTC) is also available for eligible upperclassmen, allowing students to earn simultaneously accredited college credit at reduced or no cost.

College & Career Readiness

Argyle ISD tracks "College, Career, and Military Readiness" (CCMR) as required by TEA. The district's CCMR rate is consistently strong, driven primarily by AP exam performance and dual credit enrollment. Project Lead The Way (PLTW) STEM pathways provide an additional college-readiness track for students interested in engineering, biomedical science, and computer science.

STAAR Proficiency

District-wide STAAR proficiency rates are available by campus at txschools.gov. Argyle ISD campuses consistently perform above state averages across all tested subjects. The district's smaller campus sizes mean teachers know individual students well — a structural advantage that shows up in academic outcomes, particularly in math and reading at the elementary level.

Campus Highlights

Argyle ISD operates 10 campuses — one high school, one middle school, and eight elementary schools, including newer campuses built to accommodate Northlake growth. The district maintains a strong community identity anchored by its single high school, which means the entire secondary community cheers for the same team and graduates from the same building.

Argyle High School

The district's lone high school and its academic and athletic flagship. Multiple state-level UIL athletics championships, strong fine arts, and a college prep curriculum anchored by AP courses and dual credit. The Eagles compete in UIL 5A and are trending toward 6A as enrollment grows.

Argyle Middle School

Grades 6–8

Serves the district's secondary transition years. Rigorous pre-AP coursework prepares students for Argyle High School's college prep track. GT services continue at the middle school level. Strong UIL academic and fine arts competition participation.

Argyle Elementary School

The district's original elementary campus, located in Argyle proper. Strong community roots and experienced faculty. GT identification begins here through a multi-criteria assessment process.

Hilltop Elementary School

Serves portions of the northern district area. Like all Argyle ISD elementaries, Hilltop benefits from the district's consistent curriculum standards and close teacher-student ratios that define the Argyle experience.

Northlake Area Elementary Campuses

Harvest / Pecan SquareNewer construction

The district has opened and continues to plan new elementary campuses to serve the Harvest and Pecan Square master-planned communities. Newer campuses offer updated facilities and a mix of experienced and newer faculty. Verify which campus your specific Northlake address is currently zoned to — assignments have shifted as new buildings opened.

Elementary zone assignments in the Northlake area are subject to change as the district opens additional campuses. Always confirm your current campus assignment using the district's address lookup at argusd.net after making an offer — do not rely on what a neighbor was told a year ago.

Boundary Volatility Alert — Northlake Buyers

Families purchasing homes in the Harvest, Pecan Square, or other Northlake master-planned communities should confirm their campus assignment directly with Argyle ISD before closing. New elementary campuses have opened in recent years and zone boundaries have been redrawn more than once as a result. The district's enrollment office can confirm your exact campus by address.

Additionally, some homes in far northwestern Northlake may be near the geographic boundary with Lewisville ISD or Northwest ISD — always verify with TEA's School District Locator that your address is in fact within Argyle ISD boundaries before purchasing.

Special Programs

Gifted & Talented (GT)

GT identification begins at the elementary level using a multi-criteria assessment process. Services continue through middle school with pre-AP coursework and at the high school level with AP and dual credit tracks. Newly enrolled students from other states should bring all available GT assessment documentation — Argyle ISD will review prior records as part of its identification process.

Advanced Placement (AP)

Argyle High School offers AP courses across core subject areas. Students can earn college credit through qualifying AP exam scores. AP participation and pass rates contribute to the district's strong College, Career, and Military Readiness (CCMR) metrics reported to TEA annually.

Dual Credit — NCTC

Through a partnership with North Central Texas College (NCTC), eligible juniors and seniors can earn college credit while completing high school requirements. Dual credit courses provide a cost-effective path to early college credit, particularly in core academic and career-track subjects.

Project Lead The Way (PLTW)

PLTW STEM courses are integrated across multiple grade levels, providing hands-on engineering, biomedical, and computer science coursework. PLTW pathways lead to industry-recognized certifications and strengthen college and career readiness for students interested in STEM fields.

Fine Arts

Argyle ISD has a strong fine arts tradition at all levels — band, choir, orchestra, and visual arts. The high school fine arts program competes at the UIL level and consistently advances students to regional and area competitions. Fine arts at Argyle is a genuine community pillar, not an afterthought.

UIL Academics

Academic UIL competition is a point of pride for Argyle ISD. Students compete in events ranging from mathematics and science to journalism, debate, and ready writing. The district's small size relative to the quality of competition produces a tight-knit competitive culture where individual student accomplishments are celebrated district-wide.

Special Education & Support Services

Argyle ISD provides special education and related services to eligible students ages 3–21 under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Services span the full continuum of least restrictive environment placements — from full inclusion in general education classrooms with co-teaching support, to resource settings, self-contained classrooms, and specialized program placements for students with more intensive needs.

Related services include speech-language therapy, occupational therapy, physical therapy, behavioral support, and assistive technology. The ARD (Admissions, Review, and Dismissal) process governs all IEP decisions. For families relocating with a child who has an existing IEP from another state, Texas law requires Argyle ISD to either adopt that IEP or convene a new ARD meeting within 30 days of enrollment. Bring all prior IEP, evaluation, and eligibility documentation to the enrollment appointment to avoid delays in service delivery.

Section 504 accommodations (for students who do not qualify for special education but have documented disabilities requiring formal accommodations under the Rehabilitation Act) are managed through each campus's 504 Coordinator. Dyslexia screening and intervention services are available district-wide, consistent with Texas state requirements.

One practical note for families: as the district grows and new campuses open, the physical location of certain specialized programs may shift. If your child requires a specific placement type, confirm with Argyle ISD's Special Education department which campus currently houses that program before finalizing your home purchase decision.

Athletics & Extracurriculars

Argyle ISD competes in the University Interscholastic League (UIL) at Class 5A — and is growing toward 6A as enrollment increases. With a single high school, athletic identity is intensely concentrated: everyone in the community cheers for the Eagles, and the stands at Argyle High School fill up on Friday nights in a way that is rare even among much larger districts.

Eagle football is arguably the district's signature program — Argyle High School has earned multiple state-level recognition and is consistently among the most competitive smaller-classification football programs in Texas. Track and field and cross-country are also perennial strong performers at the regional and state level.

Beyond football, Argyle ISD fields competitive programs in basketball, baseball, softball, soccer, volleyball, swimming, golf, and tennis. The district's athletics culture emphasizes multi-sport participation at the youth level, with feeder programs in the Argyle community contributing to the pipeline for high school programs.

Extracurricular life at Argyle High School mirrors the community's close-knit character. Student government, National Honor Society, and a range of academic and interest-based clubs give students avenues beyond athletics. UIL academic competition teams (see Programs section) are an active part of campus culture and involve a meaningful percentage of the student body.

School Choice Within the District

Attendance Zones

Argyle ISD assigns students to campuses based on home address. There are no magnet schools or lottery programs within the district — attendance is zone-based, and zones are determined by where you live. Given the active campus construction in the Northlake area, attendance zones have been redrawn multiple times and are expected to continue shifting as new campuses open. The district publishes current zone maps at argusd.net.

Intra-District Transfers

Transfer requests between campuses within Argyle ISD are subject to capacity at the receiving campus. Given the rapid growth the district is managing, capacity is a genuine constraint — particularly at newer campuses still building out their enrollment. Transfers are not guaranteed and are evaluated on a case-by-case basis. Contact the district's enrollment office for current transfer availability and application timelines.

Open Enrollment for Out-of-District Students

Argyle ISD may accept a limited number of out-of-district transfer students based on available capacity. Given the district's growth demands, out-of-district transfers are not a reliable option — families seeking Argyle ISD enrollment should live within district boundaries. Contact the district directly for the most current open enrollment policy.

Charter and Private School Context

There are no major charter school networks with campuses directly in Argyle or Northlake at this time, though this may change as the area's population grows. Private school options near the district's service area include several faith-based schools in the Flower Mound, Denton, and Fort Worth corridors. These are independent of Argyle ISD and have their own enrollment processes.

District Finances

Tax Rate

Argyle ISD's total tax rate is $1.21 per $100 of assessed value — higher than some established suburban districts such as Frisco ISD ($1.03) or Coppell ISD. This is not a sign of financial mismanagement; it reflects the district's position as a fast-growing district with significant capital needs. New campuses cost money, and Argyle voters have approved bond packages to fund them. The Interest & Sinking (I&S) component of the rate represents bond debt service from campus construction bonds passed by the community to keep pace with enrollment growth.

For a home assessed at $600,000, the Argyle ISD tax levy is approximately $7,260 per year at this rate, before exemptions. Texas homeowners with a primary homestead exemption receive a $100,000 reduction in assessed value for school taxes, lowering the taxable value to $500,000 and the annual school levy to approximately $6,050. County and city taxes are assessed separately.

Texas FIRST Financial Transparency

Argyle ISD holds a Texas FIRST Superior financial accountability rating — the highest designation from the Texas Comptroller's Financial Integrity Rating System of Texas. This rating confirms that the district manages its resources responsibly and complies with state financial reporting standards. For families concerned about how school bond money is being used, the district's annual financial reports are publicly available.

Bond Program & Capital Investment

The district has an active voter-approved bond program funding new campus construction in the Northlake growth area. These bonds represent a community commitment to building the school infrastructure necessary to serve new residents — the families buying in Harvest, Pecan Square, and adjacent developments. Understanding that the higher-than-average tax rate is financing the facilities those same families' children will attend is important context when evaluating the investment.

Per-Pupil Expenditure

Argyle ISD's per-pupil expenditure is available through TEA's PEIMS financial data and the federal NCES Common Core of Data. As a growth district in a higher-income service area, the district receives the state Basic Allotment per student plus applicable weighted funding adjustments. Current data is available at txschools.gov.

Enrollment — New Families

How to Enroll

New student enrollment for Argyle ISD is completed through the district's enrollment portal at argusd.net. The standard enrollment process requires:

  • Proof of residency — lease agreement or deed/closing disclosure
  • Birth certificate — for first-time Texas enrollees
  • Immunization records — Texas-required vaccines per DSHS requirements
  • Previous school records — transcript, report cards, standardized test scores
  • IEP, 504 Plan, or GT documentation — if applicable, bring all prior records
  • Proof of custody — if applicable

Enrollment Timing

New family enrollment for the following school year typically opens in spring. Given the rapid enrollment growth the district is managing — particularly from new Northlake residents — early enrollment is advisable to ensure campus placement and, where applicable, to initiate GT or special education review processes before the school year begins. Mid-year enrollments are accepted year-round.

Campus Assignment Verification

Because new elementary campuses in the Northlake area have opened and zone boundaries have shifted, do not assume a neighbor's campus assignment applies to your address. Confirm your specific campus directly with the Argyle ISD enrollment office or use the district's address lookup tool. This step is especially important for families in Harvest, Pecan Square, and any new phase of a Northlake master-planned community.

Gifted Identification for Transfer Students

Newly enrolled students with prior GT identification from another state or district should bring all available assessment documentation. Argyle ISD will review prior records as part of its multi-criteria identification process. The timeline for assessment and placement in GT services varies — plan for 4–8 weeks from enrollment to formal identification if a new assessment is required.

Frequently Asked Questions

I'm buying in the Harvest or Pecan Square community in Northlake — am I in Argyle ISD? ▾
Most homes in the Harvest and Pecan Square master-planned communities in Northlake are zoned to Argyle ISD. However, not every address in Northlake falls within Argyle ISD boundaries — some portions may be in Lewisville ISD or Northwest ISD depending on exact location. Always verify your specific address using the TEA School District Locator and confirm with Argyle ISD's enrollment office before closing.
Which elementary school will my child attend if I buy in Northlake? ▾
Elementary campus assignments in the Northlake area have changed as the district has opened new campuses, and they are expected to continue shifting. The most reliable source is Argyle ISD's enrollment office — call or email them with your specific address and they will confirm your current campus assignment. Do not rely on MLS listings, real estate websites, or what neighbors were told previously, as this information is frequently out of date.
How does Argyle ISD's tax rate compare to other high-performing DFW districts? ▾
Argyle ISD's rate of $1.21 per $100 assessed value is higher than some established districts — Frisco ISD is $1.03, Allen ISD is around $1.13, and Coppell ISD is lower still. The difference primarily reflects Argyle ISD's active bond program financing new campus construction in the Northlake growth area. Families buying in new master-planned communities are effectively investing in the school infrastructure their children will use. The higher rate comes alongside a 99.1% graduation rate and a TEA score of 92 — so the academic return on that investment is real.
Is there an IB program in Argyle ISD? ▾
No. Argyle ISD does not currently offer an International Baccalaureate program. The college prep track at Argyle High School centers on Advanced Placement (AP) courses and dual credit through North Central Texas College (NCTC). Families specifically seeking an IB program should consider Frisco ISD (Lone Star and Independence High Schools), Lewisville ISD (Flower Mound High School), or Carroll ISD (Southlake) as nearby options with IB offerings.
How does Argyle ISD handle students with IEPs transferring from out of state? ▾
Texas law requires Argyle ISD to either adopt your child's existing IEP from another state or convene a new ARD (Admissions, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment. Bring all prior IEP, evaluation, eligibility, and related services documentation to the enrollment appointment. Contact the district's Special Education department as early as possible — before moving if you can — to understand the transition process and avoid any gap in services. Given that Argyle ISD is actively opening new campuses, also confirm which campus currently houses the program type your child needs.
How does Argyle ISD compare to Lewisville ISD and Northwest ISD for families? ▾
All three are strong districts in the southern Denton County and northern Tarrant County corridor. Argyle ISD is the smallest and most academically concentrated — one high school, tight community, 99.1% graduation rate, TEA score of 92. Lewisville ISD is much larger (~52,000 students, 10 high schools) with IB programs, broader elective depth, and more program variety but also more variation between campuses. Northwest ISD (~25,000 students, 4 high schools) is also highly rated and serves Haslet, Northlake, and Trophy Club area homes depending on exact address. Tax rates: Argyle at $1.21, Lewisville around $1.10, Northwest around $1.22. For families prioritizing community identity and consistent academic culture over program breadth, Argyle ISD is a compelling choice — but verify your address falls within Argyle ISD before any purchase decision.
