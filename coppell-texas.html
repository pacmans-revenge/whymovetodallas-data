Location & Commute

Where Is Coppell? Commutes & Getting Around

Coppell sits about 20 miles northwest of Downtown Dallas and roughly 25 miles east of Downtown Fort Worth, straddling the Dallas–Denton county line. SH-121 and SH-114 are the city's primary arteries, connecting directly to DFW Airport in about 10 minutes — an advantage very few DFW suburbs share. Loop 9 to the south and I-35E to the east form a tight freeway box that keeps most commutes manageable.

For families with a parent who travels frequently — or works at American Airlines, Nokia, ExxonMobil, or any Las Colinas employer — Coppell's position is hard to beat. You're at the terminal in 10–15 minutes and at the Las Colinas employment corridor in 15–20 minutes. The tradeoff: SH-121 congestion during peak hours is real and unavoidable.

No DART in Coppell. Coppell is not a DART member city — no rail or bus rapid transit. A personal vehicle is essential. SH-121 (Sam Rayburn Tollway) backs up reliably between Coppell and Lewisville during morning and evening peaks. Budget $150–$250/month in tolls depending on your corridor. Belt Line Rd is the free parallel alternative for shorter trips.
Education

Coppell ISD — Schools in Coppell

Coppell ISD is routinely ranked in the top 3–5 school districts in Texas — not just DFW. With a deep college-prep culture, nationally recognized fine arts programs, and a highly involved parent community, the district draws families specifically because of the schools. One high school, two middle schools, and a cohesive feeder pattern keep the community tight.

Family Life

What Life Actually Looks Like in Coppell

ISD Athletics & Booster Culture

Coppell ISD athletics are genuinely competitive across every sport. Friday night football at Coppell High School draws the whole city, and the fine arts programs — band, orchestra, theatre — are nationally recognized. The parent-booster ecosystem is deeply organized and well-funded.

Andrew Brown Park Trail System

Andrew Brown Park spans three segments connected by an internal trail system — one of the best urban green corridors in DFW. Jogging, cycling, splash pad, fishing ponds, sports fields, and a disc golf course. It's the city's outdoor living room and heavily used year-round.

Old Town Shopping & Dining

Old Town Coppell is one of DFW's most genuine small-town commercial districts — local restaurants, boutique retail, a weekly farmers market (Saturdays, spring through fall), and the Coppell Arts Center. It's the community anchor in a way that most DFW suburbs simply don't have.

DFW Airport — 10 Minutes Door to Terminal

No other suburb in DFW puts you at the gate this quickly. For families with a frequent traveler, or anyone in the aviation industry, this is a daily-life advantage that never gets old. American Airlines, FedEx, and hundreds of corporate travel-heavy employers are all a short hop away.

Globally Connected Community

Coppell has one of DFW's most internationally diverse populations — a significant percentage of residents are professionals from India, South Korea, and other countries drawn by the Las Colinas and airport corridor employers. This brings exceptional cultural diversity to the school district and community events.

Easy to Connect

Small city (42K residents) means a tight community. The PTA ecosystem, ISD booster clubs, and Old Town events create natural gathering points. Relocating families consistently say they made friends faster here than in larger neighboring cities — the small-city scale accelerates community integration.

The honest tradeoff: Coppell is essentially complete — which means limited entertainment options within city limits. There's no major mall, no nightlife district, and the restaurant selection, while good in Old Town, is not extensive. For more variety, residents drive to Grapevine, Southlake, or Las Colinas. SH-121 traffic during peak hours is also a real quality-of-life friction that should be priced into your decision.
Getting Outside

Recreation, Walkability & City Amenities

Coppell is car-dependent for most daily errands — no DART rail, no meaningful transit. However, the city compensates with a genuinely excellent park and trail system anchored by Andrew Brown Park, which connects multiple park segments via an internal trail network. For a fully built-out city its size, the outdoor infrastructure is above average for DFW.

Parks, Trails & Green Space

Coppell maintains 35+ parks and green spaces with a connected trail corridor anchored by Andrew Brown Park. The multi-segment park system offers several miles of paved trail, sports complexes, fishing ponds, and a splash pad — an unusually comprehensive system for a city of 42,000.

Andrew Brown Park East

The signature park in Coppell — fishing pond, splash pad, sand volleyball, pavilions, and playground. Connects to the trail system running through the city. This is where the community gathers for weekend events and youth sports.

Andrew Brown Park West

The western segment of the Andrew Brown system — disc golf course, open fields, walking and jogging trail. Less crowded than the East segment, popular with early morning walkers and cyclists.

Old Coppell Greenbelt Trail

A paved trail corridor connecting Old Town Coppell to the broader park system — popular for evening walks and cycling. The closest thing to a walkable trail from the historic district.

Wagon Wheel Park

Youth baseball and softball complex — one of the most heavily used youth sports facilities in Coppell. Multiple fields, concession stand, lighting for evening games. The de facto home of Coppell youth baseball leagues.

Moore Road Park

Large open park with sports fields and a playground in the northern part of the city. Less trafficked than Andrew Brown — good option for families who want field space without crowds on weekday afternoons.

Duck Pond Trail Area

A quiet neighborhood trail loop with a natural pond — popular for morning walks and families with strollers. A smaller-scale green space that gives the nearby neighborhoods a genuine outdoor amenity within walking distance.

Safety & Crime

Is Coppell Safe?

Coppell is one of the safer cities in the DFW area and in Texas overall. Violent crime is exceptionally low — well below both state and national averages. Property crime is also below the national norm, though as with any suburb, standard precautions apply. The Coppell Police Department maintains a strong community policing presence for a city its size.

Violent Crime Rate

<1.0 per 1,000 residents — significantly below the U.S. average of ~4.0/1,000. Coppell consistently ranks among the safest cities in Texas.

Property Crime

~12 per 1,000 residents — below the national average of ~19/1,000. Largely limited to auto theft and package theft; home break-ins are uncommon.

Niche Safety: A

Niche assigns Coppell an A safety grade. The Coppell Police Department runs active community programs and maintains a low response time for a city its size.
