Location & Commutes

Where Forney Sits in DFW

Forney sits 22 miles east of downtown Dallas in Kaufman County, straddling the US-80 and I-20 corridors — the two main arteries connecting East DFW to the metro core. That geography is Forney's defining characteristic: it's genuinely close to Dallas by DFW standards, while still sitting in a county where land is affordable enough to build master-planned communities with 300+ acre footprints, 14-foot ceilings, and community amenity centers that would cost twice as much to replicate in Collin County.

For families relocating from California or the Northeast, the commute context matters. On a good day, you're 25 minutes to downtown Dallas on US-80. On a bad one — think Friday afternoon or school-year morning rush — that can stretch to 45 minutes. The I-20/US-80 connector gives you two routes into the metro, and Mesquite's employment centers are only 15 minutes away. This isn't Frisco's easy highway situation, but it's significantly better than it sounds on a map.

Schools & Education

Forney ISD — What Relocating Parents Need to Know

Forney ISD has one of the most interesting stories in East DFW: it's a district growing fast enough to open new campuses every few years, with a college-prep program that earns an A− from Niche despite sitting in a county most families from California have never heard of. The district's most recent TEA rating was a B (81/100) — note that 2024 ratings have been delayed due to statewide litigation, so that B reflects the 2022 cycle. More meaningful for a relocating parent is the day-to-day campus quality, which varies more than the district average suggests.

The standout is at the elementary level. Johnson Elementary earns a perfect 10/10 from GreatSchools — that's top-tier by any measure. Warren Middle School earns an 8/10. The high schools are where families should look carefully: Forney HS and North Forney HS are both rated at the mid-range by GreatSchools (3–5/10), which reflects the district's rapid growth outpacing facility and staffing build-out. If you're buying near Johnson or one of the newer elementaries and your kids are still years away from high school, the trajectory matters as much as today's rating.

Jobs & Economy

Who's Hiring Near Forney

Forney's own employment base is small relative to its population — most residents commute into Dallas, Mesquite, or the broader I-20 corridor. That said, Kaufman County's industrial and logistics base has grown substantially, and Mesquite (15–20 minutes west) is one of DFW's most active distribution and light-industrial hubs. For remote workers or those with flexible schedules, Forney's cost base is compelling without the commute trade-off.

Family Life

What Life Actually Looks Like in Forney

Forney is a community in active construction — not just of houses, but of identity. The families who've moved here in the last five years are largely from somewhere else: California, New York, Illinois, or other parts of Texas. That shared experience of choosing Forney creates a particular kind of neighborhood culture — one where people are intentionally building community rather than inheriting it. The master-planned developments hold regular events, the youth sports leagues are active, and the school system keeps families connected. It doesn't have the polished infrastructure of a Frisco or Southlake, but the warmth of a community that's figuring it out together is real.

Youth Sports & Athletics

Forney Youth Athletic Association runs baseball, softball, basketball, and flag football leagues that draw hundreds of kids. The Forney ISD athletics program is well-funded — football especially. Club soccer and select baseball are active in the Mesquite/Kaufman corridor for families wanting travel team options.

Outdoor & Lake Life

Forney Community Park's 170-acre footprint includes a fishing pond, trails, and open green space. Lake Tawakoni is 35 minutes east — one of the best bass fishing lakes in Texas, with boat ramps and camping. Lake Ray Hubbard (20 min west via I-30) offers sailboats, kayaking, and the popular Harbor Pointe Marina.

Events & Community Culture

The Spellman Amphitheater at Forney Community Park hosts concerts, movie nights, and seasonal events. The Forney Heritage Museum anchors downtown. Annual events include the Forney Christmas parade and Fourth of July celebration at the park — the kind of small-town tradition that becomes the reason families stay.

Shopping & Dining

Forney's US-80 corridor has expanded significantly — HEB (the beloved Texas grocery chain), Target, and national restaurant chains have all arrived in recent years. For dining variety, Rockwall (15 min) and Rowlett (20 min) add lake-view restaurants and a broader dining scene. This isn't a foodie suburb, but everyday conveniences are solid and improving.

The Growth Story

Forney is one of the fastest-growing cities in Texas by percentage — that means new amenities are arriving steadily. Families who bought in Windmill Farms five years ago have watched the area around them transform. For buyers who don't need everything polished on day one, that trajectory is part of the value story.

Community Feel

Newcomer families find it easier to connect in Forney than in more established suburbs, precisely because so many neighbors are also new. HOA welcome committees, neighborhood Facebook groups, and shared school experiences create natural connection points. If you're coming from out of state and worried about building a social circle, this community's "we all chose this" dynamic is a genuine advantage.

Honest tradeoff: Forney doesn't have the walkable downtown of Rockwall, the arts scene of Plano, or the restaurant density of Frisco. The US-80 corridor is commercial strip development — functional, not charming. Families who value walkability, dining variety, or proximity to cultural amenities will find the comparison to more established suburbs unfavorable. This is a suburb built around community amenity centers, sports fields, and driving distances — own that trade-off before you buy.

Getting Outside

Recreation, Walkability & City Amenities

Forney is car-dependent — there's no DART service, no commuter rail, and no meaningful transit network. Walking to coffee or groceries isn't part of daily life here. What Forney compensates with is land: master-planned communities with built-in trail systems, a 170-acre city park, and easy access to two of the best lake recreation areas in North Texas. For families whose outdoor life centers on parks, trails, sports fields, and water, Forney delivers a lot per dollar.

Parks, Trails & Green Space

Forney has 14 parks covering approximately 242 acres — a strong number for a city its size. The flagship is Forney Community Park, a 170-acre destination with amenities that draw families from across the county.

Forney Community Park

170 acres — the city's crown jewel. Spellman Amphitheater hosts concerts and community events. Fishing pond stocked for kids. Splash pad, multiple playgrounds, open turf fields, picnic shelters. The go-to destination for summer evenings and weekend family time.

Windmill Farms Trail System

Forney's most established community has an internal trail network connecting the neighborhood to parks and green space. Popular for morning runs and family bike rides. Other master-planned communities (Devonshire, Clements Ranch) have their own trail loops.

Lake Tawakoni (35 min)

One of the best bass fishing lakes in Texas — 37,000 acres with boat ramps, camping, and shoreline access. State park on the lake's north shore. Popular weekend destination for Forney families who fish, kayak, or camp. Close enough for Saturday trips without a long drive.

Lake Ray Hubbard (20 min)

Via I-30 west — Rockwall's waterfront gives Forney families lake access without living at lake prices. Harbor Pointe Marina, sailing club, kayak launches, and the Rockwall waterfront dining district. Doable on a weeknight, not just weekends.

Devonshire Amenity Center

Community-level amenities that rival what you'd pay extra for in other markets — resort-style pool, splash pad, playground, gathering pavilion, walking paths. Typical of Forney's master-planned developments, where the HOA amenity center is the neighborhood living room.

Additional Neighborhood Parks

Beyond the flagship park, Forney maintains 13 additional neighborhood parks spread across the city — pocket parks, playground areas, and sports fields. Most master-planned communities also have private green space within the development, making green access a non-issue for families in those neighborhoods.

Safety

Crime & Safety in Forney

Forney's safety profile is the one area where the data deserves honest attention. Niche gives Forney a C crime grade — below average for a family suburb. This doesn't mean Forney is unsafe in an absolute sense; it means the crime rates are measurably higher than the DFW suburbs where most relocating families are comparing. NeighborhoodScout data suggests Forney's violent crime rate is above the national median (~4.0 per 1,000), and property crime rates are in a similar range. The context matters: Forney is a rapidly growing community with the usual dynamics of fast-growth areas — more people, more commercial activity, and some displacement pressure from surrounding urban areas.

Where you live within Forney matters. Master-planned communities (Windmill Farms, Clements Ranch, Devonshire) tend to have lower crime than the US-80 commercial corridor. If safety is a top priority, lean toward the established master-planned communities on the east and north sides of Forney, and check local NextDoor or neighborhood Facebook groups for real-time community signal before buying.

Honest Assessment

Pros & Cons of Living in Forney

The Case for Forney

  • Best value in East DFW: $326K median SFR price with full master-planned community amenities — this combination doesn't exist in Rockwall, Richardson, or anywhere closer to Dallas proper.
  • 25 minutes to downtown Dallas: The US-80 corridor is genuinely fast on a good day — closer than many buyers expect when they first look at the map.
  • No state income tax: For a family earning $150K from California, the annual savings are $12,000–$16,000. In Forney, that's a mortgage payment. More than one.
  • Master-planned community quality: Windmill Farms, Clements Ranch, and Devonshire offer resort-style amenities built in — not extras you have to drive somewhere else to find.
  • Authentic growth trajectory: Forney is one of Texas's fastest-growing cities. Infrastructure, retail, and schools are improving. Families who buy now are positioned for appreciation as the build-out completes.
  • Land and space: Lots are larger than comparable-priced homes in Collin County or closer-in suburbs. If your family needs a yard, Forney delivers it without the premium.

Know Before You Buy

  • High school ratings are a real concern: Forney HS and North Forney HS score in the 3–5/10 range on GreatSchools. The district is growing faster than it can build out programs. If high school years are 2–4 years away, research the specific feeder path before committing.
  • US-80 congestion is real: The 25-minute good-day commute becomes 40–45 minutes during school-year rush hour or on bad weather days. There's no alternate rail option. Families who work fixed downtown hours should do a test commute at 7:30 AM before buying.
  • Crime grade is below DFW norm: Niche rates Forney's crime a C — below average for the metro. The US-80 commercial strip has higher crime activity than the master-planned communities. Neighborhood selection within Forney matters.
  • No transit options: Zero DART service. No bus network. Every trip — work, sports, groceries, kids' activities — is by car. Factor two-car budget and fuel costs into your monthly math.
  • MUD tax risk on new construction: Municipal Utility Districts in new construction areas can add $0.35–$1.00/$100 to your effective tax rate. A $350K home in a MUD could carry $5,000–$8,000+ in annual taxes. Always verify before closing.

FAQ — Forney, Texas

Forney earns a B+ from Niche for families — and the score reflects what's genuinely strong: affordable homes with master-planned amenities, an active youth sports community, and a shared newcomer culture that makes building friendships easier than in more established suburbs. The honest asterisk is that high school ratings are below the top tier, and the crime grade is average rather than excellent. For families with elementary-age kids who value value-per-dollar and community feel, Forney is a strong choice. For families prioritizing top-rated high schools above all else, Rockwall or Frisco may be a better fit.

Forney is approximately 22 miles from downtown Dallas — about 25–30 minutes on US-80 in normal conditions. During peak school-year rush hours (7:30–8:30 AM and 4:30–6:00 PM), that can extend to 40–45 minutes. There is no DART rail service to Forney, so all commuting is by car. Rockwall and Forney are the two most-compared East DFW suburbs — they're 15–20 minutes apart on I-30, and families often choose between them. Rockwall offers a higher overall Niche grade, a scenic lakefront, better school ratings at the high school level, and a polished downtown. Families who prioritize school quality and walkable character lean toward Rockwall; families who prioritize value and space lean toward Forney.

That growth brings new schools, new retail (HEB, Target, and restaurant chains have arrived in recent years), and infrastructure improvements. For buyers, it means buying into a community that's actively building out — which carries some uncertainty (school ratings may lag, amenities still arriving) but also potential appreciation as the suburb matures. The value score of 96/100 reflects how favorably priced Forney is relative to what it offers.
