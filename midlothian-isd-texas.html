Is Midlothian ISD the Right Fit?

Honest answers so you can decide before you tour — not after you close.

Best Fit For

  • Families wanting a smaller-city feel with a suburban DFW price point and more land per dollar
  • Students drawn to hands-on CTE pathways — a standalone center separate from both high schools
  • Buyers seeking acreage-lot options within commute range of Fort Worth or south Dallas employers
  • Families who prefer two mid-size high schools (~2,000–2,500 each) over a single mega-campus
  • Households prioritizing a lower overall tax rate vs. closer-in DFW suburbs
  • Families who see bond passage as a long-term value signal — all three 2025 props passed

Not a Fit For

  • Families needing a sub-20-minute commute to downtown Dallas — budget 35–50 min each way
  • Buyers who expect their address zip code to determine the high school — it doesn't; verify your lot's specific boundary
  • Families seeking dense urban amenities or specialized magnet programs within the district
  • Those wanting all AP/IB offerings concentrated on one flagship campus; they're split across two schools
  • Buyers expecting walkable, amenity-rich neighborhoods immediately surrounding schools

Academic Performance

How Midlothian ISD scores on the measures most families use to evaluate a district.

B TEA Accountability Rating

The Texas Education Agency awarded Midlothian ISD a B (85/100) in its most recent accountability cycle. The district met standards across Student Achievement, School Progress, and Closing the Gaps domains — a consistent outcome for a district managing rapid enrollment growth while sustaining academic results.

A− Niche Overall Grade

Niche.com grades Midlothian ISD an A− overall, with particular strength in academics and teachers. The grade synthesizes state test performance, graduation rates, student-teacher ratios, and survey responses from current parents and students in the district.

📊 Graduation & College Readiness

The district maintains a graduation rate above the Texas state average. Both Midlothian High School and Heritage High School offer Advanced Placement coursework and Dual Credit partnerships with community colleges, giving students multiple pathways to earn college credit before graduation.

🏫 District of Innovation

Midlothian ISD holds District of Innovation designation under Texas law, which allows flexibility in scheduling, staffing, and curriculum beyond standard TEA requirements. This status lets administrators move faster on workforce-relevant programs — especially meaningful given the district's CTE focus.

Campuses & Boundaries

14 facilities across 112 square miles — including two high schools and a standalone CTE Center. Your specific address determines which high school your child attends.

High School

Midlothian High School

The original flagship campus serving the district's western attendance zone. Full AP sequence, UIL athletics across major sports, fine arts, and dual credit. Enrollment approximately 2,000–2,500 students.

High School

Heritage High School

Opened to serve the district's growing northeast residential zones. Mirrors Midlothian HS in AP offerings, UIL participation, and fine arts programs — deliberately sized to avoid mega-campus dynamics.

CTE Center

Midlothian CTE Center

A standalone Career & Technical Education facility shared by both high schools. Students attend for industry-credentialed programs in construction trades, healthcare, welding, automotive, and technology. Bond 2025 Proposition B funds a significant expansion of this campus.

Middle Schools (3)

Walnut Grove · Frank Seale · Mt. Peak

Three middle school campuses serving grades 6–8. Each feeds into the high school serving its geographic attendance zone. Verify your middle school assignment along with the high school boundary.

Elementary (8)

Eight Elementary Campuses

Eight PK–5 campuses spread across the district's 112-square-mile service area. Attendance zones are reviewed periodically as residential growth in Midlothian and surrounding communities continues.

⚠️ High School Boundary Check: Because MISD runs two high schools, your specific lot — not just your zip code — determines which campus your children attend. Confirm your address at midlothianisd.net before closing, or ask your buyer's agent to run the address through the district lookup tool as part of due diligence.

Programs & Pathways

What students actually have access to — and what sets Midlothian ISD apart from neighboring districts.

🔧

Standalone CTE Center

A dedicated Career & Technical Education facility is rare at this enrollment size. Students from both high schools travel to the center for industry-certified programs spanning construction, healthcare, welding, automotive, and technology. Bond 2025 Prop B adds major capacity.

🔬

STEM Programs

Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math coursework is embedded across grade levels. At the secondary level, students can pursue STEM-focused dual credit and AP sequences aligned to four-year university admission benchmarks.

🎓

Dual Credit & AP

College partnerships — including with Navarro College — allow students to earn transferable credit at no cost. Advanced Placement courses cover core academic subjects at both high school campuses.

🏆

UIL Athletics & Fine Arts

Both high schools field UIL athletic teams across major sports and host band, choir, theater, and visual arts programs. Operating two campuses means student-athletes aren't competing for roster spots against a single 5A mega-school population.

🌐

Special Education & Support Services

District-wide services include special education, dyslexia programs, 504 accommodations, and ESL/bilingual support. District of Innovation status provides flexibility in service delivery models.

👶

Early Childhood Education

Pre-K programs are available for qualifying students district-wide based on state eligibility criteria. Early childhood programming anchors the district's academic pipeline from the earliest grades.

Growth, Investment & What's Coming

Midlothian ISD is one of the faster-growing districts in the DFW exurban corridor — and the community voted to fund it.

🗳️ Bond 2025 — All Three Propositions Passed

Midlothian ISD voters approved all three bond propositions in 2025:

  • Prop A — Growth: New campus construction and expansions to manage enrollment surge
  • Prop B — CTE: Major expansion of the dedicated Career & Technical Education Center
  • Prop C — Technology: Infrastructure upgrades and classroom technology refresh across the district

A district where voters pass all three bond measures is a district investing in its own future. That's a meaningful signal for families weighing long-term neighborhood value.

📈 Enrollment Trajectory

Midlothian ISD has grown from fewer than 5,000 students a decade ago to approximately 9,000 today — nearly doubling. This pace mirrors Midlothian city's residential expansion as DFW buyers move south and southwest chasing larger lots and lower prices.

The two-high-school model was built to prevent one campus from becoming overcrowded. Bond Prop A capacity projects will carry the district through the next wave of subdivision development already underway in Ellis County.

🏗️ Community Character & Context

Midlothian earned the title "Cement Capital of North Texas" — three cement plants and a steel mill have anchored the local economy for generations. That industrial base is now supplemented by residential growth and expanding retail and healthcare corridors. The standalone CTE Center's emphasis on trades and industrial credentials reflects both the community's heritage and the real workforce needs of Ellis County employers today.

Key Routes In & Out

US-287 runs northwest directly to Fort Worth and southeast toward Waxahachie. US-67 heads northeast toward Dallas, Cedar Hill, and Duncanville. Both corridors carry substantial commuter traffic, and peak-hour congestion near Cedar Hill and the I-35E interchange can add 10–15 minutes to Dallas commutes. Families working remotely or with Fort Worth-based employers consistently report that more land, lower prices, and a quieter city more than offset the longer drive time.
