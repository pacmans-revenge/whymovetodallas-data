Is White Settlement ISD Right for Your Family?

The Bottom Line

White Settlement ISD earned a TEA C rating (73/100) in 2024 — average by Texas standards. Two specialty campuses (a Fine Arts Academy rated A and an N-STEAM campus rated B) raise the ceiling considerably for families who seek them out. The remaining traditional campuses cluster in the C–D range, and district-wide STAAR pass rates run about 5 points below the state average. This is a reasonable district for budget-conscious buyers, defense-industry families near NAS Fort Worth JRB, and investors — but if your primary criterion is academic rankings, Northwest ISD or HEB ISD will be a better fit at a moderate price premium.

White Settlement is a self-contained municipality of roughly 16,500 residents that sits entirely within the Fort Worth city limits on the west side — its own city hall, its own police department, and its own school district. That small-town identity inside a major metro is the community's most underrated asset. The tradeoff is an academic profile that requires more active navigation than in higher-rated neighboring districts.

The four questions that matter most for this specific district: Is the defense corridor (NAS Fort Worth JRB, Lockheed Martin, Bell) central to your family's location? Does your child's age and interest make the Fine Arts Academy or N-STEAM program relevant? Is home affordability driving the trade-off against school rating? And have you boundary-checked — because a D-rated elementary assignment is a different situation than an A-rated one within the same district?

TEA Accountability Rating: What the C Means

C 73 / 100

The Texas Education Agency rates districts A through F across three domains: Student Achievement, School Progress, and Closing the Gaps. White Settlement ISD earned a C (73/100) for the 2024 accountability year.

A TEA C is not a failing district. It means overall student achievement is close to — but below — state average on most measures, with meaningful variation at the campus level. Approximately one-third of Texas school districts earn a C, placing WSISD squarely in the middle of the state distribution. For a relocating family, the C signals a functional, stable district where the headline number is less useful than the campus-level breakdown below.

Specialty Programs and What Sets This District Apart

Fine Arts Academy (K–5)

The Fine Arts Academy is White Settlement ISD's standout program — a district-wide specialty campus rated A (91/100) by TEA, focused on visual arts, music, theater, and dance. It accepts applications from anywhere in the district rather than serving a geographic zone. The A rating in a C-rated district is a meaningful signal: this campus performs at a level 18 points above the district composite and offers a genuinely different academic experience within WSISD. Families with K–5 students whose interests align with the arts should investigate availability before discounting this district.

N-STEAM Elementary (K–5)

Rated B (80/100), N-STEAM combines a neighborhood assignment with limited open-enrollment seats. The curriculum integrates science, technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics. At 80 points, it outperforms the district average by 7 points and represents the district's answer to the growing STEM-focused demand from defense-industry families in the corridor.

District Police Department and Guardian Program

White Settlement ISD maintains its own independent police department — unusual for a district of approximately 6,800 students — with dedicated school resource officers on each campus. The district also operates a Guardian Program for additional campus security. This level of security infrastructure reflects the community's priorities, which came into sharp national focus in 2019 after a high-profile incident at a local church demonstrated the area's preparedness culture. For families who place safety infrastructure among their school selection criteria, WSISD's investment here is worth noting.

2022 Bond Program: Capital Investment Underway

White Settlement ISD passed a capital bond program in 2022 that funded improvements across all campuses — renovations, technology infrastructure, and facility upgrades. This is an important nuance for buyers: the physical facilities are receiving active investment even as TEA ratings at some campuses remain moderate. Building quality and instructional outcome are separate variables. The bond program indicates the community's long-term commitment to the district, even if academic outcomes are still catching up.

These figures reflect only the ISD levy. Your total Tarrant County tax bill will add approximately $0.45–$0.60 per $100 for county, city, and other special district taxes on top of the ISD rate.

In context: WSISD's rate is higher than Northwest ISD ($1.0879) and lower than Eagle Mountain-Saginaw ISD ($1.2457). The ~$0.08 gap between WSISD and Northwest ISD amounts to roughly $240 per year on a $300,000 home — a relatively modest annual difference compared to the likely price premium for a comparable home inside Northwest ISD's boundary.

Texas Homestead Exemption: Texas homeowners who occupy a property as their primary residence qualify for a $100,000 homestead exemption on school district taxes (as of 2023). This reduces your taxable assessed value before the district rate is applied — a meaningful saving regardless of which ISD you choose.

Boundary Check and School Locator

Not every property in or near White Settlement falls inside White Settlement ISD. Portions of the surrounding area are served by Fort Worth ISD, Eagle Mountain-Saginaw ISD, or other districts — and boundaries do not follow zip code or city lines with any consistency.

The official tool for verifying which campus a specific address is assigned to:

→ WSISD School Site Locator (schoolsitelocator.com)

New students can enroll through the district's online enrollment portal at wsisd.com/students-families/enrollment. The district administration office is at 8224 White Settlement Road, White Settlement, TX 76108.

Two homes, two different campuses: Within WSISD, two properties on the same street can be assigned to different elementary campuses — and the difference between a Fine Arts Academy assignment and a Liberty Elementary assignment is a 24-point TEA rating gap. Always confirm the campus assignment directly with the district before relying on any third-party source, including real estate listings.

Homes and Real Estate in the WSISD Zone

White Settlement ISD covers the city of White Settlement and adjacent portions of western Fort Worth — a compact geography with immediate freeway access to I-820, I-30, and SH-183. The combination of a self-contained municipal identity, defense-industry employment within a short commute, and home valuations below the DFW median makes this one of the more affordable entry points for buyers priced out of the Northwest ISD or Keller ISD zones.

The housing stock runs toward established ranch-style and mid-century homes built between the 1960s and 1990s, with newer construction limited primarily to infill. Buyers focused on price-per-square-foot efficiency rather than top-tier academic ratings will find more purchasing power here than in most neighboring districts. The consistent demand from NAS Fort Worth JRB personnel, Lockheed Martin, and Bell employees creates a rental market with relatively stable, employment-anchored demand — a useful characteristic for investors evaluating long-term hold scenarios.

The trade-off is clear: the academic profile requires more active parental navigation at the elementary level, and the district's overall performance gap relative to Northwest ISD or HEB ISD is real and should factor into any long-term household planning.
