District Overview

Keller Independent School District serves the northwest DFW corridor — one of the region's most active growth markets, stretching from established Keller neighborhoods through the rapidly developing Alliance/Haslet corridor and into portions of Roanoke and North Fort Worth. The district also serves small areas of Southlake and Westlake near its eastern boundary.

As of the 2024–25 school year, KISD enrolls approximately 33,000 students across 42 campuses: four high schools, six middle schools, and more than 27 elementary campuses. District offices are located at 350 Keller Pkwy, Keller, TX 76248. More information is available at kellerisd.net.

Keller ISD's TEA score of 85 (B rating) and Niche A grade reflect a district performing well above state averages — strong enough to draw families specifically for its schools — while managing the real logistical challenge of rapid growth in the Alliance area, where boundary adjustments and campus capacity questions arise regularly. For families considering the Haslet, Roanoke, or North Fort Worth areas, verifying your specific campus assignment before purchasing is essential.

Key Cities Served

Keller (primary), Roanoke, Haslet, portions of North Fort Worth, Alliance corridor, and small areas of Southlake and Westlake. The four high school zones cover distinctly different geographic areas — your address determines which campus your child attends.

TEA Accountability Rating

The Texas Education Agency assigns every school district and campus an annual A–F accountability rating based on three domains. Keller ISD holds a TEA B rating (score: 85 out of 100) for the 2024–25 school year — a score that places it well above the state average and in strong standing among large DFW suburban districts.

What the three domains measure:

  • Student Achievement — STAAR test scores, graduation rates, college/career readiness indicators
  • School Progress — year-over-year academic growth compared to similar campuses
  • Closing the Gaps — performance equity across student demographic groups

A B rating in this context means Keller ISD scores between 80 and 89 — significantly above the state average and comparable to many well-regarded Tarrant County districts. The Niche A grade, which incorporates parent reviews, student-teacher ratios, and college readiness metrics alongside TEA data, reflects consistently positive family experiences across the district. Campus-level ratings for all 42 campuses are published at txschools.gov.

Context for Relocating Families

A TEA B (85) in a large, geographically diverse district is a meaningful achievement. Keller ISD is managing rapid growth in the Alliance corridor while maintaining district-wide averages well above state benchmarks — that is the story behind the score. Individual campus ratings vary; always research the specific elementary school your home address is assigned to.

Graduation Rate

Keller ISD's four-year graduation rate is 94.6% — above both state and national averages. The district tracks college, career, and military readiness (CCMR) as required by TEA, and a high proportion of graduates complete at least one college credit, industry certification, or AP exam before leaving high school.

Advanced Placement

All four Keller ISD high schools offer Advanced Placement (AP) coursework. AP course catalogs vary by campus size and demand, with the larger, more established campuses (Keller High, Central High) typically offering a broader menu of AP titles. Families with students already on an AP track should confirm course availability at the specific high school their address is zoned to.

Dual Credit (Tarrant County College)

Keller ISD's dual credit partner is Tarrant County College (TCC), allowing eligible juniors and seniors to earn college credit at low or no cost while still enrolled in high school. Courses are available both on-campus and at TCC campuses. Dual credit is one of Keller ISD's key college readiness pathways alongside AP and industry certifications through CTE.

College & Career Readiness

The district's Future Ready initiative provides personalized learning pathways that align student interests with academic coursework and post-secondary options. Students work with counselors to build a four-year plan connecting their interests to one of KISD's defined career pathways — from health science to digital media to architecture — creating a more structured route through high school than generic elective selection.

STAAR Performance

District-wide STAAR proficiency rates (students performing at "Approaches Grade Level" or above) are available by campus at txschools.gov. KISD consistently scores above state averages in reading, writing, and mathematics across grade levels. Campus-level data, which varies more meaningfully across the district's geographic range, provides a clearer picture for families evaluating a specific attendance zone.

Campus Highlights

Keller ISD's four comprehensive high schools each serve a distinct geographic area of the district. Which campus your child attends is determined entirely by your home address — this is one of the most important questions to resolve before purchasing a home in the KISD zone.

Keller High School

Grades 9–12IndiansEstablished Flagship

The original district high school, serving central Keller. Strong AP program, storied athletics (particularly football and basketball), and well-developed fine arts. The most established campus culture in the district — families zoned here often cite it as a significant draw.

Central High School

Grades 9–12Cowboys

Serves the central Keller area. Competitive academics with a full AP catalog, active athletics program, and a strong community identity. One of KISD's most competitive campuses for UIL academic and athletic events.

Fossil Ridge High School

Grades 9–12Panthers

Serves the southern portion of the district, including the Watauga area. Known for its fine arts programming and a competitive athletic program. Families in southern KISD neighborhoods — closer to the Loop 820 corridor — are typically zoned here.

Timber Creek High School

Grades 9–12FalconsGrowth Area

Serves the Roanoke and North Fort Worth growth areas, including much of the Alliance corridor. As the fastest-growing part of KISD, Timber Creek has seen significant enrollment increases and boundary adjustments in recent years. Newer campus, still building its program depth — families buying in Alliance-area new construction should verify Timber Creek capacity and any pending boundary changes.

Middle Schools

Grades 6–8

KISD operates six middle schools: Indian Springs, Keller, Fossil Hill, Trinity Springs, Bear Creek, and Vista Ridge. Each feeds into a corresponding high school zone. Middle school assignment follows the same address-based system — confirm your zone at kellerisd.net before enrolling.

Elementary Campuses

Grades PK–527+ campuses

KISD operates more than 27 elementary campuses spread across the district's geographic range. Campus culture, test scores, and program availability vary across the elementary level more than at the secondary level. Research your specific assigned elementary — not just the district — before purchasing.

Alliance Corridor — Important Note for Buyers

Keller ISD serves the rapidly developing Alliance/Haslet corridor. Families buying in new construction near the Alliance area should confirm their specific campus assignment — this area has seen significant boundary adjustments as Timber Creek HS capacity has grown. What is true today may change by your move-in date if a new boundary cycle is pending. Contact KISD's enrollment office directly for the most current zone map for any specific address.

Special Programs

Gifted & Talented (GT)

GT identification and services are available K–12 across all KISD campuses. Elementary GT includes pull-out enrichment; secondary GT is reflected in advanced coursework tracks and AP access. Identification uses a multi-criteria process including assessment data, teacher referrals, and portfolio review.

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

Keller ISD's CTE program is one of the strongest in Tarrant County — a meaningful distinction given the county's large suburban district pool. Pathways include Health Science, Digital Media & Communications, Law & Public Safety, Architecture & Construction, and Advanced Manufacturing. Students can earn industry-recognized certifications alongside their diploma.

Project Lead The Way (PLTW)

KISD offers PLTW STEM programming across multiple grade levels, introducing engineering design, biomedical science, and computer science concepts through hands-on project-based learning. PLTW courses are available at select campuses — confirm availability at your assigned school.

Dual Credit (TCC)

Through the Tarrant County College partnership, eligible KISD juniors and seniors can earn college credit at low or no cost. Courses are available both on campus and at TCC. Strong dual credit participation is a key part of KISD's college readiness strategy alongside AP exams.

Fine Arts

Fine arts programming spans band, choir, visual art, and theatre across all secondary campuses. KISD fine arts programs — particularly band and choir — compete at the UIL 6A level and regularly advance to regional and state competitions. The district has invested in dedicated fine arts facilities at its high school campuses.

Future Ready Initiative

KISD's personalized learning framework helps students map a four-year high school plan aligned to their interests and post-secondary goals. Students select a pathway focus area — from business to health science to engineering — and build their schedule around it, creating coherent academic and career preparation rather than disconnected electives.

Special Education & Support Services

Keller ISD provides special education and related services to eligible students ages 3–21 under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Services are delivered across the full least restrictive environment continuum — from full inclusion in general education classrooms with co-teaching support to self-contained settings and Life Skills programs, depending on each student's Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Dyslexia Services

Keller ISD is recognized for having one of the more developed Dyslexia Services programs in Tarrant County. The district provides dyslexia screening at the elementary level and structured intervention through the state-approved dyslexia program. Families with students who have a diagnosed reading difference or prior dyslexia intervention history should specifically ask about dyslexia services when enrolling.

ARD Process & Out-of-State IEP Transfers

Texas law requires KISD to either adopt your child's existing IEP from another state or convene an ARD (Admissions, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment. Bring all prior IEP, evaluation, and eligibility documentation to the enrollment appointment. The Special Education department will initiate contact after enrollment to begin the transition process. Do not assume a prior IEP transfers automatically — the ARD must occur.

Additional Support Services

Related services include speech-language therapy, occupational therapy, physical therapy, behavioral support, and transition planning for secondary students. Section 504 accommodations for students who do not qualify for special education but require formal supports under the Rehabilitation Act are coordinated by each campus's 504 Coordinator.

Athletics & Extracurriculars

Keller ISD competes in the University Interscholastic League (UIL) at Class 6A — the highest classification — across all four high schools. The district has a strong athletics tradition, with storied programs in football, basketball, cross-country, golf, and swimming. Multiple KISD programs have advanced to UIL regional and state competitions in recent years.

Each of the four high schools carries its own athletic identity and program depth. Keller High School and Central High School have the longest competitive histories. Fossil Ridge and Timber Creek have developed their programs through the same 6A competitive structure, with Timber Creek's program still building alongside its enrollment growth in the Alliance corridor.

Beyond athletics, extracurricular options at the high school level include UIL academic competitions (Academic Decathlon, Debate, Science Olympiad), student government, National Honor Society chapters, and pathway-aligned CTE clubs (HOSA, DECA, SkillsUSA, FBLA). Fine arts extracurriculars — marching band, concert choir, theatre productions — are active at all four campuses and compete at the 6A UIL level.

School Choice Within the District

Attendance Zones

Keller ISD uses a strict attendance-zone system. Your home address determines your assigned elementary, middle school, and high school — all four high schools serve geographically distinct areas of the district. There is no district-wide open enrollment that allows families to choose among the four high schools freely.

Intra-District Transfers

Intra-district transfers (requesting a campus other than your assigned one) are processed by application and are subject to available capacity at the receiving campus. Transfers must be renewed annually and are not guaranteed. Families motivated by a specific high school's program should verify their address zone before purchasing rather than relying on a transfer application.

Roanoke & Haslet Boundary Considerations

Families buying in Roanoke or Haslet may have geographic proximity to campuses in neighboring ISDs. KISD's boundary in this area is well-established, but confirm your specific campus assignment — particularly in new developments near the Roanoke city limits — where boundary lines can run through subdivisions.

Charter & Private Options

Charter schools operating in northwest DFW — including some Harmony campuses and other state-funded alternatives — accept students regardless of home address. These are entirely separate from KISD with their own application and lottery processes. Several private schools also operate in the Keller/Roanoke/Southlake area for families seeking alternatives.

District Finances

Tax Rate

Keller ISD's total tax rate is $1.09 per $100 of assessed value. This comprises the Maintenance & Operations (M&O) rate and the Interest & Sinking (I&S) rate. The I&S component reflects the district's active bond program, which has funded new campus construction and facility upgrades to keep pace with enrollment growth — particularly in the Alliance corridor.

For a home assessed at $550,000, the KISD tax levy is approximately $5,995 per year at this rate, before exemptions. Texas homeowners with a primary homestead exemption receive a $100,000 reduction in assessed value for school district taxes (per HB 3, effective 2023), meaningfully reducing the effective tax burden for owner-occupants.

Texas FIRST Rating

Keller ISD holds a Texas FIRST Superior financial rating — the highest classification issued by the Texas Education Agency's Financial Integrity Rating System of Texas. This reflects sound financial management, appropriate fund balances, and compliance with state financial reporting requirements.

Tarrant County Appraisal

Most Keller ISD properties are appraised by the Tarrant County Appraisal District (TCAD). A small number of properties near the district's northern boundary may fall within Denton County — if your new home is near the county line, confirm which appraisal district applies, as protest procedures and deadlines vary.

Bond Program

KISD has an active bond program serving the Alliance growth corridor — new elementary and middle school capacity has been a recurring need as residential development expands north of Keller into Haslet and into Roanoke. Bond debt is reflected in the I&S rate and is a standard feature of growing suburban Texas ISDs.

Enrollment — New Families

How to Enroll

New student enrollment for Keller ISD is completed through the district's online enrollment portal at kellerisd.net. Required documents typically include:

  • Proof of residency — lease agreement or deed/closing disclosure
  • Birth certificate — for first-time Texas enrollees
  • Immunization records — Texas-required vaccines (Tdap, meningococcal, and others per grade level)
  • Previous school records — transcript, report card, IEP if applicable
  • Proof of custody — if applicable

Which High School Will My Child Attend?

This is the single most important pre-purchase question for families with high school-aged children in KISD. Your specific street address determines your high school zone — Keller, Central, Fossil Ridge, or Timber Creek — and the four campuses have meaningfully different program profiles and community cultures. Use the KISD campus locator at kellerisd.net or call the enrollment office to confirm your address zone before making an offer on a home.

Mid-Year Transfers

KISD accepts mid-year enrollments year-round. Families relocating with an offer letter but not yet a signed lease may contact KISD's enrollment department to understand timing options. Initiating enrollment within the first week of establishing Texas residency helps avoid delays in campus placement and schedule assignment.

Gifted Identification

Newly enrolled students are typically assessed for GT services after initial enrollment. If your child is identified as gifted in another state, bring all available documentation — KISD's GT department will review prior assessment records as part of its multi-criteria identification process. The process typically takes several weeks from initial enrollment to formal identification decision.

Frequently Asked Questions

Keller ISD has four high schools — how do I know which one serves my address? ▾
Your high school assignment is determined entirely by your home address. The four campuses — Keller High (Indians), Central High (Cowboys), Fossil Ridge (Panthers), and Timber Creek (Falcons) — serve distinct geographic areas of the district. Use the campus locator tool at kellerisd.net or call the KISD enrollment office with your specific address before making an offer. Do not assume proximity to a particular campus means you are zoned to it.
How strong is Keller ISD's CTE program compared to other Tarrant County districts? ▾
Keller ISD is recognized as having one of the strongest CTE programs in Tarrant County. Key pathways include Health Science, Digital Media & Communications, Law & Public Safety, Architecture & Construction, and Advanced Manufacturing. Students can earn industry-recognized certifications alongside their diploma — a meaningful credential for students not pursuing a traditional four-year college path. The Future Ready initiative helps students build a coherent four-year plan around their chosen pathway.
I'm buying in a new construction community near the Alliance corridor — which KISD campuses serve that area? ▾
New construction in the Alliance/Haslet area is generally zoned to Timber Creek High School and its feeder elementary and middle schools. However, this area has seen active boundary adjustments as the corridor has grown, and further adjustments are possible as enrollment continues to increase. Before signing a contract on a new build in this area, contact KISD's enrollment office directly with the community address to get the current campus assignment — and ask whether any boundary review is pending for that zone.
What does Keller ISD's TEA B rating (score: 85) mean in practical terms? ▾
A TEA score of 85 places Keller ISD in the B range (80–89) — well above the state average for a large suburban district managing rapid enrollment growth. The Niche A grade reflects strong family satisfaction, favorable student-teacher ratios, and solid college readiness outcomes alongside the TEA academic data. In context: KISD is a high-performing district, not an underperforming one. Families who prioritize a TEA A rating should be aware that the score is 85 (upper B range) rather than 90+ (A range), and should weigh that alongside the district's strong CTE programs, Niche grade, and graduation rate.
My child has a dyslexia diagnosis — how does Keller ISD support students with dyslexia? ▾
Keller ISD has one of the more developed Dyslexia Services programs in Tarrant County. The district provides dyslexia screening at the elementary level and structured intervention through the state-approved dyslexia program. If your child has a prior diagnosis or is currently receiving intervention, bring all documentation (evaluation reports, intervention records, current IEP or 504 if applicable) to the enrollment appointment. Contact the Special Education or Dyslexia Services department after enrollment to ensure services are set up before your child's first day.
How does Keller ISD handle IEP transfers from out of state? ▾
Texas law requires KISD to either adopt your child's existing IEP from another state or convene an ARD (Admissions, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment. Do not assume an out-of-state IEP transfers automatically — the ARD process must occur. Bring all prior IEP, eligibility determination, and evaluation documentation to enrollment. The Special Education department will contact you after enrollment to initiate the transition. If your child requires specific related services (speech, OT, behavioral support), note that at enrollment so the appropriate staff are looped in immediately.
