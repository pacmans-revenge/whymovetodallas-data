Northwest ISD at a Glance

32,000+ Students (2024–25)
35 Campuses
B (81) TEA Grade (2024–25)
A− Niche Grade (third-party)
95.6% Graduation Rate (2023)
$1.0879 Tax Rate per $100 (2024–25)

Northwest ISD serves the fastest-growing corridor in the Dallas–Fort Worth metroplex — a 234-square-mile expanse spanning Northlake, Roanoke, Justin, Trophy Club, and a dozen smaller communities across Denton, Tarrant, and Wise counties. If you're buying in Harvest, Pecan Square, Canyon Falls, or the new construction neighborhoods spreading north and west from Fort Worth, odds are high that Northwest ISD is your district. That growth is both the district's greatest strength and its central management challenge: adding roughly 1,500 students per year, NISD opens new campuses regularly to keep pace.

The district earned a B (81/100) from TEA for the 2024–25 school year — a score that has been stable across multiple years. The honest starting point for any relocating family: that overall B masks a gap between the district's two strongest domains (School Progress and Closing the Gaps, both B-rated) and its Student Achievement domain, which scored a C (76–77) in both 2023–24 and 2024–25. This doesn't mean Northwest ISD is a poor academic environment — it means the district's proficiency outcomes on STAAR testing trail behind some of its neighbors. Niche rates the district A− overall, reflecting above-average performance across its teacher and academic measures. Both ratings tell part of the story; neither tells the whole story.

Three high schools — Byron Nelson (Trophy Club), V.R. Eaton (Fort Worth/Haslet area), and Northwest (Justin) — plus Steele Accelerated High School, an early college pathway, give families meaningful choices as students mature. Career and technical education is a genuine strength, with Project Lead The Way STEM and Biomedical academies earning national recognition at two campuses. For the right family profile, Northwest ISD is a solid choice; it is not the right fit for everyone, and knowing which families it serves best is the point of this page.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Are buying new construction in Northlake, Harvest, Pecan Square, or Roanoke
  • Want a large district with multiple high school options and career academy pathways
  • Value STEM, biomedical, and CTE programs as part of the high school experience
  • Want an Early College High School option that lets students earn college credit tuition-free
  • Need a competitive tax rate — NISD's M&O rate is the lowest of any K-12 district in Texas

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • STAAR proficiency rates are a top-priority benchmark — the C in Student Achievement may concern some families
  • You prefer a smaller, tight-knit campus experience (35 campuses serving 32,000+ students means larger schools)
  • Your Trophy Club address hasn't been verified — nearly half of Trophy Club falls within Keller ISD, not NISD
  • Recent funding pressures and a rejected 2024 VATRE raise concerns about program sustainability
⚠ Boundary Verification — Required Before Offering

Not every home with a Roanoke, Trophy Club, or Northlake address feeds into Northwest ISD. Trophy Club in particular straddles the NISD/Keller ISD boundary street by street. Verify your specific address using the district's school zone locator at nisdtx.org/departments/facilities/attendance-boundaries before signing any contract.

New construction communities in Northlake (Harvest, Pecan Square, Canyon Falls) are generally within NISD boundaries, but specific lot assignments for elementary campuses may vary as new schools open. Always confirm with the district during the home search process — not after closing.

TEA Accountability Rating

Northwest ISD earned an overall B rating from the Texas Education Agency for both the 2023–24 and 2024–25 school years, with scores of 81 and 82 out of 100 respectively. (Note: TEA released ratings for both years simultaneously in August 2025 after accountability data was delayed by court proceedings.) These are the most recent officially published ratings as of May 2026.

TEA evaluates districts across three domains, weighted as follows: Student Achievement and School Progress together account for 70% of the score (using the higher of the two), while Closing the Gaps accounts for 30%.

Domain Breakdown (2024–25)

C (77) Student Achievement
B (82) School Progress
B (80) Closing the Gaps

Student Achievement (C, 77): This domain measures primarily STAAR test results and graduation rates. A C in this domain means proficiency outcomes on standardized tests fall below the threshold for a B-rated district. For relocating families accustomed to A-rated districts in their home state, this is worth understanding: STAAR proficiency benchmarks in Texas are set at the "approaches grade level" standard, and district averages vary significantly by campus. Your child's individual campus may perform quite differently from the district average.

School Progress (B, 82): This domain measures how much students improve over time, and how a district compares to peers with similar economic profiles. NISD's B here indicates students are generally making expected growth, a meaningful signal for families who care about whether kids are being moved forward academically — not just where they started.

Closing the Gaps (B, 80): This domain tracks whether all student groups — by income, race, and learning needs — are progressing toward state standards. NISD's B reflects reasonable consistency across student populations, though families with children who receive special services should verify campus-level support directly with the district.

What the Score Means for Your Family

An overall score of 81–82 places Northwest ISD solidly mid-B-range. It reflects a district managing rapid growth reasonably well, with genuine strengths in how students progress year-over-year, and room to improve on absolute proficiency benchmarks. Families who shortlist NISD typically do so because the district's community, campus options, and location align with where they want to live — and they go in with clear eyes on the academic picture.

Academic Performance

The following data is sourced from official TEA reports and third-party providers where indicated. All figures are labeled by year.

STAAR Proficiency (2023–24, per Niche/state test data)

64% Reading Proficiency
48% Math Proficiency
95.6% Graduation Rate (Class of 2023)
18:1 Student–Teacher Ratio (Niche)

Reading proficiency at 64% and math at 48% reflect STAAR "approaches grade level" results — the state's minimum benchmark. These numbers are lower than those in A-rated peers like Argyle ISD and Carroll ISD, and roughly comparable to Keller ISD and other large B-rated districts. For context: STAAR proficiency rates in Texas districts often appear lower than families from other states expect, because the benchmark reflects basic grade-level attainment, not mastery. A student performing well on a campus may still be counted in a less-than-proficient cohort at the district level. Individual campus data — available through GreatSchools and TEA's School Report Cards — is a more useful lens for families than district averages.

The 95.6% on-time graduation rate for the Class of 2023 is a genuine strength, and one of the highest rates among large DFW districts. It indicates that the vast majority of students who enter NISD high schools are completing their diplomas — a signal of effective student support infrastructure despite rapid enrollment growth.

Advanced Coursework

Northwest ISD offers AP courses across all three high schools. Byron Nelson HS earned an AP Silver Award on the Texas 2024 AP School Honor Roll, and V.R. Eaton HS earned the AP Bronze Award — both recognitions for expanding access to AP coursework and improving student performance in those courses. AP participation and pass rates vary by campus; families prioritizing a specific high school should ask the campus counselor directly about current AP catalog depth.

College, Career, and Military Readiness (CCMR) data — which captures dual credit completion, AP exam scores, workforce certifications, and military enlistment — is reported annually by TEA. Families can access NISD's CCMR breakdown on TEA's txschools.gov district profile.

Campus Highlights

Northwest ISD operates 23 elementary schools, 7 middle schools, 3 traditional high schools, and 1 early college high school (Steele Accelerated). The district opened two new campuses at the start of the 2024–25 school year — Perrin Elementary and a rebuilt Pike Middle School — reflecting the pace of growth in its territory. High school attendance zones are determined by residential address; students attend the high school that serves their home's zone.

Byron Nelson High School

High School (9–12) ~2,970 students GS: 8/10 Trophy Club

Byron Nelson is NISD's flagship campus in terms of community visibility. Rated 8/10 by GreatSchools, it holds an AP Silver Honor Roll recognition (2024) and houses a nationally recognized PLTW Biomedical Sciences Academy — named a Project Lead The Way Distinguished School for two consecutive years. Located in Trophy Club (addresses in that city must be verified to confirm NISD vs. Keller ISD attendance zone).

V.R. Eaton High School

High School (9–12) ~3,260 students GS: 7/10 Fort Worth / Haslet

Eaton HS serves the Haslet and northwest Fort Worth portions of the district and earned the AP Bronze Award on the Texas 2024 AP School Honor Roll, reflecting expanding AP course access for students in the western growth corridor. Enrollment has grown significantly as Haslet and surrounding communities have developed.

Northwest High School

High School (9–12) ~3,000 students Justin

The district's namesake high school serves Justin and surrounding communities. Its STEM Academy was named a 2024–25 Project Lead The Way Distinguished School, earning national recognition for the strength of its STEM curriculum pathway. Families buying in Justin or nearby rural communities are typically zoned to Northwest HS.

Steele Accelerated High School (ECHS)

Early College High School Application Required Associate's Degree Pathway

Steele Accelerated HS is NISD's Early College High School, launched with its first freshman class in the 2021–22 school year. Students can earn an associate's degree alongside their high school diploma — attending dual-enrollment college courses during the school day at no tuition cost to the family. This is a district-wide program (not attendance-zone-based), so families in any part of NISD can apply. Capacity is limited and admission is competitive.

Specialty Programs

Northwest ISD's strongest programmatic identity is in Career and Technical Education (CTE) and STEM pathways. The district does not operate a district-wide IB programme or a dedicated dual-language K-12 pipeline, which distinguishes it from some smaller DFW peers. Families who specifically need those pathways should factor this into their ISD comparison.

PLTW Biomedical Sciences Academy

Campus: Byron Nelson HS | Access: Students zoned to Byron Nelson; no separate application within zone
Project Lead The Way's Biomedical Sciences pathway covers human body systems, medical interventions, and biomedical innovation. Byron Nelson has been named a PLTW Distinguished School for this academy two consecutive years (2023–24, 2024–25). Families specifically targeting this program should confirm their address feeds to Byron Nelson, not Eaton or Northwest HS. Transportation is standard school bus if within zone.

PLTW STEM Academy

Campus: Northwest HS | Access: Students zoned to Northwest HS; no separate application within zone
Northwest HS's STEM Academy achieved PLTW Distinguished School status for the 2024–25 school year. The PLTW curriculum integrates engineering design, computer science, and applied math. Families buying in Justin area who want a structured STEM pathway will find it at Northwest HS without a competitive application process.

Early College High School (Steele)

Campus: Steele Accelerated HS | Access: District-wide application; competitive enrollment; capacity limited
Steele Accelerated HS allows students to earn an associate's degree while completing their high school diploma, at no tuition cost. This is a significant financial advantage for families who want to reduce future college costs. Because it is a district-wide program, home address does not determine access — application and student fit do. Contact the district's College, Career, and Military Readiness office for application timelines.

Advanced Placement (AP)

Campuses: All three traditional high schools | Access: Open enrollment within each campus; teacher/counselor recommendation may apply
AP courses are available across all NISD high schools. Byron Nelson holds a Silver AP Honor Roll designation; Eaton holds Bronze (both Texas 2024). The number of available AP sections and pass rates vary by campus — families who prioritize a wide AP catalog should ask campus counselors for the current course guide during the home search process.

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

Campuses: All high schools | Access: Generally open-enrollment within each campus
Northwest ISD has developed a broad CTE portfolio across areas including health sciences, information technology, construction trades, and business. CTE programs can lead to industry certifications that count toward CCMR in TEA accountability. Specific program offerings vary by campus — visit nisdtx.org/CTE for the full catalog.

Gifted & Talented (GT)

Campuses: District-wide, beginning at elementary | Access: Testing and evaluation required; request through campus counselor
Northwest ISD operates a GT program at the elementary level that continues into middle and high school AP/honors tracks. GT identification involves multiple measures including assessments, teacher input, and parent data. Families relocating mid-year with GT-identified students should contact the district's Special Programs office to initiate re-evaluation; Texas law requires districts to provide GT services to transferring identified students while evaluation is completed.

Enrollment Steps (2025–26)

Online enrollment for the 2025–26 school year is open through the district's registration portal. New families — whether brand new to NISD, returning after a period away, or currently enrolled — complete enrollment online at nisdtx.org/students-families/registerenroll.

What You'll Need

Gather these documents before starting enrollment:

  • Proof of residency (mortgage statement, lease agreement, or utility bill with your NISD-address property)
  • Child's birth certificate
  • Immunization records (Texas-required vaccines)
  • Most recent report card or transcript (from previous school)
  • IEP, 504 Plan, or GT evaluation records, if applicable

Transfer Policy (2025–26)

Intra-district transfers (to a school other than your attendance-zone school) are considered on a case-by-case basis with limited availability. Contact the district's enrollment office to inquire about current intra-district transfer capacity at specific campuses.

Inter-district transfers (from another ISD into NISD) have historically been limited. Beginning with the 2026–27 school year, NISD launched a limited open enrollment program for the first time since 2017 — students from outside NISD can apply for seats at designated campuses with available space and must live within 30 miles. Contact enrollment at (817) 215-0321 for current open enrollment status by campus.

IEP / 504 Transfers

Texas law requires districts to hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of a student with an IEP enrolling. Bring all prior documentation — IEP, evaluation reports, most recent ARD notes — when you enroll. Contact Northwest ISD's Special Services office before your move-in date to begin the coordination process and minimize gaps in service. Families with complex or medically fragile IEPs should request a pre-enrollment meeting with Special Services to assess campus-level fit.

Pre-K Eligibility

Northwest ISD offers Pre-K for eligible 4-year-olds who meet Texas income, language, or other state-defined criteria. Enrollment typically opens in spring for the following school year. Contact your zoned campus or the district enrollment office for current Pre-K availability by location.

Boundary Verification

Northwest ISD covers 234 square miles across 14+ communities in three counties. That geographic complexity creates a real home-search risk: making an offer based on a city name or ZIP code, rather than a verified attendance zone, can put your child in a different school than you planned. The district's zone locator is the only reliable source.

How to Verify Your Address

Use the official NISD school zone locator: nisdtx.org/departments/facilities/attendance-boundaries. Type in your specific address to see your elementary, middle, and high school assignments. Select the correct school year (2025–26 or 2026–27 if you're moving after the school year starts). The tool also provides direct contact links for each campus.

You can also use the TEA's statewide school locator at tea.texas.gov/apps/school-directory to cross-reference district assignment by address.

Trophy Club — Critical Boundary Split

Trophy Club is the most common source of address-assumption errors in NISD's territory. The city straddles the boundary between Northwest ISD and Keller ISD — the dividing line runs street by street through the community, not along a clean geographic marker. Byron Nelson High School, located in Trophy Club, is an NISD campus — but many Trophy Club homes are zoned to Keller ISD, not NISD. Two homes on the same block can feed different districts. If you are buying in Trophy Club, verify the specific address before submitting an offer, not after.

Northlake — New Construction Zone Assignments

Northlake's master-planned communities — Harvest, Pecan Square, Canyon Falls — are predominantly within NISD boundaries. However, as new elementary campuses open to serve rapid growth, attendance zone assignments for specific sections or phases of these communities may shift. The district's zone locator reflects current-year assignments; when buying in a new-construction community where occupancy will occur 6–12 months in the future, confirm the planned zone assignment with the district's Facilities Department directly, as future campus assignments can differ from today's map.

Other Communities with Boundary Nuance

NISD also serves small portions of Flower Mound, Southlake, Westlake, Keller, and northwest Fort Worth — areas where the overwhelming majority of homes feed to other ISDs (Lewisville ISD, Carroll ISD, Keller ISD). If you are buying in any of these cities expecting NISD zoning, confirm before assuming.

Home-Search Implication

Boundary maps do not always match common-sense geography — city names, subdivision names, and even agent representations are not reliable substitutes for address-level verification. Making an offer without confirming your specific address can mean your child attends a different school than you planned. Boundary changes also happen: verify the school year that matches your move-in date, not just the current year.

Tax Rate & District Finances

2024–25 Certified Total Tax Rate: $1.0879 per $100 assessed value

Northwest ISD's 2024–25 total tax rate of $1.0879 per $100 consists of a Maintenance and Operations (M&O) rate of $0.6669 and an Interest and Sinking (I&S) debt service rate of $0.4210. The M&O rate of $0.6669 is the lowest M&O rate of any K-12 school district in the state of Texas — a distinction created largely by the extraordinarily rapid growth in taxable property values across the district's territory, which compresses the effective rate even as the district expands its budget.

What that means in monthly terms: On a $500,000 home in NISD territory, the district tax bill is approximately $5,440 per year before the Texas homestead exemption. After the $100,000 homestead exemption (available to primary-residence owners), the taxable value drops to $400,000, generating a district tax of approximately $4,352 per year — or roughly $363 per month added to your housing cost. Compare that to Argyle ISD ($1.1727 rate, ~$4,691/year on the same home after exemption) or Keller ISD ($1.0852 rate, ~$4,341/year). On a $500,000 home, NISD and Keller ISD tax bills are nearly identical; Argyle ISD runs about $340/year higher.

Per-Pupil Expenditure

Northwest ISD spent approximately $10,052 per student from the general fund in its most recently published financial data (from nisdtx.org financial transparency). Keller ISD's per-pupil expenditure is approximately $11,289. The difference reflects in part how newer, faster-growing districts deploy resources during expansion phases versus more established districts with higher staffing maturity costs.

Recent Funding Context — Worth Knowing

In November 2024, Northwest ISD voters rejected a Voter-Approved Tax Rate Election (VATRE) that would have added roughly $12 million in annual operations funding. The district subsequently pursued a 2025 Proposition A (Operations Funding Election) and is considering a November 2026 election to restore funding for programs. Families evaluating NISD should be aware that this funding uncertainty is real — programs that currently exist may face future reduction if funding elections do not pass. Following district board meetings and local reporting (Community Impact and Fort Worth Report both cover NISD regularly) is a reasonable way to track this situation after a purchase.

Texas Homestead Exemption

All Texas property owners who use a home as their primary residence qualify for a $100,000 homestead exemption on school district taxes, applied automatically once you file with the county appraisal district. File promptly after closing — the exemption does not apply retroactively to prior tax years.

How Northwest ISD Compares

Families shopping in the western DFW growth corridor most commonly cross-shop Northwest ISD against Argyle ISD (smaller, A-rated neighbor) and Keller ISD (similar-size, B-rated neighbor). Denton ISD is occasionally in the mix for families looking at Northlake properties near the Denton County line.

District Enrollment TEA Grade (2024–25) Tax Rate Best For
Northwest ISD ~32,000 B (81–82) $1.0879 New construction buyers; STEM/CTE pathways; early college access
Argyle ISD ~5,000 A (91) $1.1727 Families who prioritize a tight-knit community and high TEA scores; no transfer policy
Keller ISD ~33,250 B (83–85) $1.0852 Similar size district; slightly higher TEA scores; serves Keller, Westlake, Southlake (east)
Denton ISD ~32,440 B (~82) ~$1.16 Families buying in Denton city; university-town culture; diverse programming

Argyle ISD is the district families most often compare to NISD when looking in Northlake — and the comparison is stark in terms of academic ratings (A vs. B) and size (5,000 vs. 32,000 students). Argyle does not accept inter-district transfers, so eligibility depends entirely on where you buy. Families who can afford Argyle territory and want the smaller community feel often choose it; families who want the program breadth and campus variety of a larger district, or who are buying in Harvest or Pecan Square, often land in Northwest ISD and are satisfied.

Keller ISD is the most direct apples-to-apples comparison — similar size, similar tax rate, slightly higher TEA scores, overlapping territory in Trophy Club. The practical difference often comes down to which neighborhood a family wants to be in rather than the ISD. Keller ISD has a slightly higher Student Achievement score but both districts are solidly mid-range B performers.

Community Feel

The Northwest ISD community is not one thing — it's more accurately described as a collection of sub-communities spread across a vast geographic territory. Families in Trophy Club and Byron Nelson's zone experience a different culture than families in the fast-growing new construction communities of Northlake. Trophy Club parents tend to have high involvement with the schools, active booster cultures for athletics and fine arts, and a strong sense of campus identity. Northlake families in Harvest and Pecan Square are often newer to the district, bringing the energy (and the moving truck) from Texas transplant cities — California, Colorado, and Illinois are well-represented buyer pools in those master-plans.

Youth athletics is a meaningful part of life in the NISD footprint. Byron Nelson's programs are active and well-attended; Northwest HS has a strong tradition in its community; Eaton is growing its campus identity as the western growth corridor fills in. Parents on community forums describe Northwest ISD as a district that's doing a reasonable job managing enormous growth — new campuses, new programs, expanding staff — while acknowledging that the pace of expansion sometimes outpaces the infrastructure. The most common themes in parent feedback: teachers are generally dedicated, class sizes are manageable for a large district, and the CTE and career academy programs are a genuine draw for families with students who want vocational or technical pathways alongside traditional academics.

One element worth noting for families from high-performing districts: the culture around STAAR testing can feel less intensive than in some neighboring ISDs. This is not a quality judgment — it's a cultural observation that may suit families who prefer a less test-focused environment, and may concern families who use proficiency rates as a primary accountability signal. As with any large district, campus-level culture varies significantly: visiting your zoned campus before committing to a neighborhood remains the most useful research step available.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my Trophy Club address guarantee enrollment in Northwest ISD? ▾
No — and this is one of the most common address-assumption errors in NISD's territory. Trophy Club straddles the boundary between Northwest ISD and Keller ISD street by street. Byron Nelson High School is located in Trophy Club and is an NISD campus, but many homes in Trophy Club are zoned to Keller ISD. Two homes on the same block can fall in different districts. Always verify your specific address at the NISD school zone locator (nisdtx.org/departments/facilities/attendance-boundaries) before making an offer on any Trophy Club property.
What distinguishes the three Northwest ISD high schools from each other? ▾
Byron Nelson HS (Trophy Club) is the district's most visible campus — it serves the Trophy Club community, holds an AP Silver Honor Roll designation, and houses the nationally recognized PLTW Biomedical Sciences Academy. V.R. Eaton HS (Fort Worth/Haslet area) serves the western growth corridor and earned the AP Bronze Honor Roll in 2024; its enrollment has grown significantly in recent years. Northwest HS (Justin) houses the PLTW STEM Academy and serves the more rural northern portions of the district. All three offer AP coursework and CTE programs, but specific offerings vary. Your attendance zone is determined by your home address, not preference — inter-campus transfers are limited. Steele Accelerated HS is a separate, district-wide early college high school with a competitive application process.
How does Northwest ISD handle special education transfers from another district? ▾
Texas law requires Northwest ISD to hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of a student with an IEP enrolling. The district must provide services comparable to the student's existing IEP during that transition window. Bring all prior documentation — IEP, evaluation reports, most recent ARD meeting notes — when you enroll. Families with complex learning needs or medically fragile situations should contact NISD's Special Services office before their move-in date to assess campus-level support and minimize service gaps. Quality of special education support varies by campus and by the specific learning need; direct conversations with campus staff are the most reliable way to evaluate fit.
Why does Northwest ISD's Niche A− not match its TEA B rating? ▾
These systems measure different things. TEA's A–F ratings are based primarily on STAAR standardized test proficiency rates, year-over-year growth, and gap closure across student groups. Niche's grades incorporate additional factors including teacher quality surveys, college-readiness indicators, extracurriculars, sports, and community reviews. A district can score higher on Niche than TEA when its overall educational environment — facilities, teacher satisfaction, program variety — is stronger than its STAAR proficiency rates suggest, which is a plausible interpretation for a fast-growing district that is adding capacity while managing staff hiring challenges. Use both ratings as data points, not verdicts — and look at campus-level data on GreatSchools and TEA's School Report Cards for a more precise picture of where your child would actually be enrolled.
Can students from outside Northwest ISD transfer in? ▾
Beginning with the 2026–27 school year, Northwest ISD launched a limited open enrollment program for the first time since 2017. Students outside NISD can apply for enrollment at designated campuses that have available space and must live within 30 miles of the requested campus. This is a limited program — not all campuses participate, and space is not guaranteed. Contact the district at (817) 215-0321 or visit nisdtx.org/students-families/registerenroll for current campus availability. Intra-district transfers (between NISD schools) are considered on a case-by-case basis and are also limited by available capacity at receiving campuses.
How does Northwest ISD's tax rate compare to neighboring districts? ▾
Northwest ISD's 2024–25 total certified tax rate is $1.0879 per $100 of assessed value (M&O: $0.6669 + I&S: $0.4210). For comparison, Keller ISD's 2024–25 rate is $1.0852 — nearly identical. Argyle ISD's rate is $1.1727 — roughly $340 per year more expensive on a $500,000 home (after the $100,000 homestead exemption). Denton ISD runs approximately $1.16. Grapevine-Colleyville ISD, further east, has one of the lowest rates in DFW at approximately $0.92. NISD's M&O rate is technically the lowest in Texas, but the total rate (including debt service from significant bond programs) is mid-range among DFW districts. Tax rates are certified annually and can change — always verify the current year rate with your county appraisal district when budgeting for a purchase.
What is the early college pathway at Steele Accelerated High School, and how do students qualify? ▾
Steele Accelerated High School (ECHS) allows students to earn an associate's degree alongside their high school diploma, attending dual-enrollment college courses during the school day at no tuition cost to the family. This pathway launched with its first freshman class in the 2021–22 school year. Enrollment is district-wide — any student in NISD can apply regardless of home attendance zone — but capacity is limited and the program is competitive. Students typically need strong academic standing and motivation to manage college-level coursework in a high school setting. The ECHS pathway can significantly reduce future college costs and accelerate degree completion for students who are prepared. Contact the district's College, Career, and Military Readiness office (nisdtx.org) for current application timelines and eligibility criteria.
