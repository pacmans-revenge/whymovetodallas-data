Getting Your Bearings

Where Is Burleson, Texas?

Burleson sits in Johnson County about 16 miles south of downtown Fort Worth, straddling the I-35W corridor that connects it to one of the most concentrated employment zones in North Texas. That single highway is Burleson's geographic advantage — most residents are at their desk in Fort Worth in under 25 minutes on a normal morning, which is rare for a suburb with homes in the $290K–$500K range. The city proper covers roughly 30 square miles, with older neighborhoods near the historic town center and newer master-planned subdivisions growing south and east.

For families relocating from California or the Northeast, Burleson often registers as an afterthought next to names like Frisco or Southlake — but that's the point. It offers legitimate community character, Burleson ISD schools, 348 acres of parks, and proximity to Fort Worth's hospitals, defense contractors, and aviation industry without the premium price tags of suburbs closer to Dallas. If your job is on the I-35W corridor and your budget is under $450K, Burleson deserves a serious look.

Transit reality: Burleson has no DART rail or commuter rail service. The city operates a small local bus system (The T — Fort Worth), but it's not a practical commute option. A personal vehicle is required for daily life. The silver lining is that I-35W southbound out of Fort Worth clears quickly compared to DFW's eastern corridors, and most residents find the 20-minute Fort Worth run genuinely stress-free outside of peak hours.
Education

Burleson ISD — Schools Deep Dive

Burleson ISD has quietly built a reputation as a solid, community-oriented district — not the flashiest name in DFW, but genuinely above-average and improving. The district earned a TEA B rating in 2024, with standout scores in Student Achievement and College Preparation. For families relocating from states with inconsistent public school quality, the consistency across Burleson ISD campuses — especially at the elementary level — is one of its most underrated selling points. The district's crown jewel is Burleson Collegiate High School, ranked #1 public high school in Johnson County by Niche and earning a TEA score of 99 — one of the highest in the region.

Where to Live

Best Neighborhoods in Burleson for Families

Burleson covers a wide range of price points — from established mid-$200s neighborhoods near the historic town center to newer master-planned communities pushing $500K in the hills south of I-35W. For relocating families, the key variables are which high school your address feeds, distance to I-35W, and whether you want an established tree-lined street or a new-construction community still building out amenities.

$480K–$560K · Established Upscale

Hidden Vistas

One of Burleson's most sought-after addresses, Hidden Vistas features large lots, cul-de-sac streets, mature landscaping, and homes backing to greenbelts. The neighborhood has a quiet, settled feel that's hard to find in newer communities still building out — and it feeds to Burleson High School. Families who've done their suburb shopping consistently put Hidden Vistas on the shortlist.

$370K–$420K · New Construction

Shannon Creek

D.R. Horton's flagship Burleson community, Shannon Creek offers newer construction homes with open floor plans, smart-home features, and HOA-maintained common areas. Priced from the mid-$370s, it's one of the most accessible new-construction options in South Fort Worth. The community has direct trail access to Chisenhall Fields — Burleson's premier youth baseball and softball complex — making it a natural fit for sports families.

$390K–$460K · Semi-Rural Feel

Prairie Timber Estates

For families who want more land without leaving the city, Prairie Timber Estates delivers — half-acre and larger lots in a low-density, semi-rural neighborhood that still has city utilities. Homes here tend to have larger square footage, three-car garages, and a quiet country feel that's only 10 minutes from everything Burleson offers. Listed among Burleson's safest and most desirable neighborhoods.

$290K–$340K · Established Value

Elk Ridge

Elk Ridge is Burleson's entry point for families who want a real neighborhood feel at an accessible price. Homes are mostly 1,800–2,400 sq ft on standard lots, built in the 1990s–2000s with mature trees and walkable streets. The area is well-established, close to Burleson High School, and regularly praised by residents for its quiet, safe character. It's the kind of neighborhood where kids ride bikes and people know each other's names.

$410K–$480K · Lakefront Access

Mountain Valley Lake

A standout for families who want to wake up near water — Mountain Valley Lake wraps around a private lake with fishing access, making it one of the more distinctive neighborhoods in Burleson. Homes are mostly 2,400–3,600 sq ft, the community is mature and well-maintained, and the lakefront lots command a modest premium that still undercuts anything comparable in North Dallas. Great for families who value outdoor access as part of daily life, not just on weekends.

Bent Oaks

Bent Oaks is a classic DFW family neighborhood — established in the 2000s, well-maintained, and consistently producing the kind of place where kids grow up with a backyard and parents actually talk to each other over the fence. The California math: A family earning $250,000 in California pays roughly $23,000+ in state income tax. In Texas, that's $0. Even with Burleson's property tax rate — higher than California's Prop 13 values but consistent with Texas norms — most California families come out $15,000–$25,000 ahead annually after accounting for the income tax savings and the dramatically lower home price. The median Burleson home at $351K buys you what a $1.3M Bay Area home would, on a larger lot, with better highways and shorter commutes.
