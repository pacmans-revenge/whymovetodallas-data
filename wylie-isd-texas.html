Wylie ISD — Complete Family Relocation Guide

Wylie ISD serves more than 19,500 students across 21 campuses in one of the fastest-growing corners of Collin County — a 41-square-mile district situated roughly 24 miles northeast of downtown Dallas. The district draws from six communities: the City of Wylie, plus portions of Sachse, Murphy, Lucas, Lavon, and St. Paul. If you're looking at homes in the NE quadrant of DFW and want more square footage for your budget than you'd find in Plano or Allen, Wylie ISD territory often shows up on the shortlist — and for good reason. The programs are substantive, the newer elementary campuses are well-regarded, and the district's Career and Technical Education options are among the more comprehensive in this part of Collin County.

That said, there's one practical question that should shape how you search: which high school will your child attend? Wylie ISD has two 6A high schools — Wylie High School and Wylie East High School — and their 2025 TEA Distinction records diverge significantly (5 of 7 distinctions versus 1 of 7). Which campus feeds your address is determined by your specific location within the district, not by the city name on your mailbox. The district completed a rezoning for 2025–26, so families evaluating homes should verify the current feeder pattern for their specific address before making an offer. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know before you commit.

One more thing worth knowing upfront: Wylie ISD is growing fast, and the district explicitly warns that grade-level enrollment caps can result in students being assigned to a different campus than their residential zone when a campus reaches capacity. For families moving into new construction communities on the edges of the district, that's worth factoring in — especially for elementary grades where the community feel of a specific school can matter a lot to parents.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Want affordable Collin County housing without sacrificing school options — Wylie ISD territory typically offers more home for the money than Plano or Allen
  • Have students interested in CTE pathways — 12 of 16 career clusters and 30+ career experiences are available through Wylie HS
  • Want a Spanish Dual Language program starting at the elementary level
  • Are prioritizing Orchestra as a fine arts pathway — Wylie ISD has a continuous orchestra program from 5th grade through high school with a transfer option to follow the program
  • Are open to open enrollment options — the "Experience Wylie ISD" program accepts a limited number of out-of-district students annually

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • You need the same academic distinction record at both high schools — Wylie HS and Wylie East HS have meaningfully different TEA distinction profiles, and your address determines which one your child attends
  • Boundary certainty is critical — this is a multi-city, fast-growing district that recently completed a rezoning; boundaries require active verification for each address
  • You prefer a smaller, more intimate district where students move through the same cohort K–12 — at 19,500+ students across 21 campuses, this is a large operation
  • You're commuting to the Legacy West or Las Colinas employment corridor — the geography pulls you northeast of those hubs
⚠ Boundary Verification — Required Before You Offer

Not every home with a Wylie, Sachse, Murphy, or Lucas address falls within Wylie ISD boundaries. The district explicitly warns that some City of Wylie addresses are not zoned to Wylie ISD. Additionally, Sachse and Murphy each have homes that feed Garland ISD — even though those cities are commonly associated with Wylie ISD. Verify your specific address using the district's address lookup tool at infofinderi.com (Wylie ISD) or the Collin County Appraisal District property search at collincad.org/propertysearch.

After confirming your address is in Wylie ISD, also confirm which feeder cluster and high school zone your address falls in — this is a separate question from district membership, and the district completed a boundary rezoning for the 2025–26 school year.

TEA Accountability Rating

Wylie ISD's most recently published TEA A-F accountability letter grade was a B, issued for the 2022–23 school year. Texas Education Agency A-F ratings have experienced delays and pauses in recent years — ratings may not reflect current campus-level performance, and the most recent published grade should be interpreted in that context. Always check txschools.gov for the latest available data before making enrollment decisions.

For 2025, TEA published Distinction Designations (a separate recognition system from the A-F letter grades). Distinction Designations recognize campuses that significantly exceed state performance standards in specific domains. Wylie ISD's two 6A high schools received the following 2025 designations:

  • Wylie High School — received 5 of 7 possible Distinction Designations (2025)
  • Wylie East High School — received 1 of 7 possible Distinction Designations (2025)

This is the most operationally significant data point on this page for families with high-school-age children. The two campuses are both large 6A schools, but their TEA distinction profiles are meaningfully different. Families with students who will enter the high school years during their time in Wylie ISD should verify their feeder high school as part of the address-verification step — not as an afterthought.

What TEA Letter Grades Actually Measure

TEA's A-F system scores districts across three domains: Student Achievement (STAAR proficiency and graduation), School Progress (year-over-year improvement and relative performance against similar districts), and Closing the Gaps (progress for student groups historically underserved). A "B" district typically performs solidly across all three domains without reaching the distinction threshold in any. It does not mean the district is underperforming — many Texas families with excellent school experiences are in B-rated districts. The most useful data for individual campus decisions is the distinction designation list and campus-level STAAR profiles, not the district-level letter alone.

Academic Performance

Wylie ISD publishes campus-level STAAR performance data through TEA's public reporting system (txschools.gov). Because the TEA site requires dynamic interaction to retrieve campus-specific STAAR percentages, specific proficiency rates for reading and math are not reproduced here — families should access current STAAR data directly from txschools.gov and filter by campus to compare individual schools. This is especially useful when comparing Wylie High School and Wylie East High School, as their campus-level profiles differ from the district aggregate.

Graduation Rate

Wylie ISD's 4-year graduation rate is approximately 95% (most recently published data — verify current rate at txschools.gov). A graduation rate in this range is consistent with well-functioning suburban districts across Collin County and indicates that the large majority of students who enter as freshmen complete on a standard four-year timeline. For relocating families, the more useful lens is individual campus trajectory: check whether your child's assigned high school is trending up, stable, or under a TEA intervention plan.

College, Career & Military Readiness (CCMR)

TEA's CCMR indicator measures the percentage of graduates who completed a recognized postsecondary pathway — including dual credit courses, CTE certifications, SAT/ACT benchmarks, AP exams, and military enlistment. Wylie ISD's CTE infrastructure (12 of 16 clusters, 30+ career experiences) positions students well for CCMR credit through the career pathway track, which is a practical advantage for students who may not take the traditional AP/dual-credit-heavy route. Check the most current CCMR percentage on txschools.gov for the most recent year.

AP and Dual Credit Access

Wylie High School and Wylie East High School both offer AP courses and dual credit options. Specific AP participation rates and course catalogs are available on each campus's website. Families targeting AP-heavy pathways should request the current AP course list from the high school counselor at their assigned campus — course offerings can differ between Wylie HS and Wylie East HS.

What this means in practice: Wylie ISD's academic profile is consistent with a solid, mid-size Collin County suburban district. It is not a district built around a single flagship IB program or a concentration of specialized academic pathways — it is a district with broad-based solid performance and meaningful CTE depth. Families specifically seeking an IB-pathway school or a very high concentration of AP sections should also explore Allen ISD or McKinney ISD, where those programs are more concentrated.

Campus Overview

Wylie ISD operates 21 campuses across five levels: two 6A high schools, one alternative high school, four junior highs (grades 7–8), four intermediate schools (grades 5–6), eleven elementary schools (Pre-K through 4th grade), and Hartman Early Childhood Learning Center for Pre-K.

High Schools

Wylie High School

High School — 6A 5 of 7 Distinctions (2025 TEA)

The district's original comprehensive high school, Wylie HS received five of seven TEA Distinction Designations in 2025 — the strongest accountability signal in the district. Home to the orchestra program capstone, Wylie HS also houses the bulk of the district's CTE pathways, including Business & Industry, Health Science, and STEM-focused clusters. Families prioritizing distinction-level academic recognition should verify their address feeds Wylie HS, not Wylie East HS.

Wylie East High School

High School — 6A 1 of 7 Distinctions (2025 TEA)

Wylie East serves the eastern portion of the district — including many newer residential developments in and around Wylie. The campus received one of seven TEA Distinction Designations in 2025. Families whose address feeds Wylie East should speak directly with the campus about current AP offerings, extracurricular programs, and any ongoing improvement initiatives. The distinction gap relative to Wylie HS is real and worth factoring into home-search decisions for high-school-focused families.

Achieve Academy

Alternative High School

Achieve Academy is Wylie ISD's alternative high school, serving students who benefit from a non-traditional high school setting. Families interested in alternative pathways should contact the district's student services office for eligibility and referral processes.

Junior High Schools (Grades 7–8)

Wylie ISD operates four junior high campuses: Burnett Junior High, Cooper Junior High, McMillan Junior High, and Parker Junior High. McMillan JH is home to the orchestra program at the junior high level, making it a notable campus for families whose students participate in the orchestra pathway — students may transfer to follow the orchestra program even if they would normally be assigned to a different campus.

Intermediate Schools (Grades 5–6)

The four intermediate campuses are Davis Intermediate, Draper Intermediate, Hampton Intermediate, and Harrison Intermediate. Davis Intermediate is where the orchestra program begins in 5th grade, making it the entry point for students who want to pursue the orchestra pathway through junior high and high school. The district organizes feeder patterns into four clusters that flow ES → Intermediate → JH → HS.

Elementary Schools (Pre-K – Grade 4)

Wylie ISD has eleven elementary campuses: Akin, Birmingham, Bush, Cox, Dodd, Groves, Kreymer, Smith, Tibbals, Watkins, and Whitt. For families with younger children, the elementary assignment is determined by residential address and feeder cluster. Dual Language enrollment is available at specific elementary campuses — see the Programs section for details on which campuses offer it.

Note on Grade Level Caps

In fast-growth areas, Wylie ISD may implement grade-level enrollment caps that result in a student being assigned to a different campus than their residential zone when the home campus reaches capacity. The district discloses this explicitly in enrollment materials. Families moving into new construction communities on the district's edges should ask directly about capacity at their assigned campus before signing a contract.

Specialty Programs

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

Districtwide access; based primarily at Wylie High School. Wylie ISD offers 12 of 16 state career clusters, 4 of 5 endorsement pathways, and 30+ career experiences. Clusters include Agriculture, Architecture & Construction, Arts/AV/Communications, Business Management, Education & Training, Health Science, Hospitality & Tourism, Information Technology, Manufacturing, Marketing, and STEM. Students can earn industry certifications, participate in dual credit through CTE pathways, and qualify for CCMR credit through career endorsements. Families interested in CTE should confirm the current cluster offerings at their assigned high school, as some pathways may be campus-specific.

Dual Language (Spanish)

Campus-limited; application required. Wylie ISD offers a Spanish-English Dual Language program beginning at the elementary level. The program develops biliteracy in both English and Spanish, with instruction delivered in both languages. Enrollment is campus-specific and subject to space availability — not every elementary campus participates. Families interested in Dual Language should contact the district's Curriculum & Instruction department (wylieisd.net) to identify participating campuses and the application process for the upcoming school year. This is a meaningful differentiator for families relocating from metro areas where dual language programs are standard — it's worth asking about at the initial enrollment meeting.

Orchestra Pathway (Transfer-Eligible)

Continuous program from 5th grade through high school; inter-campus transfer allowed. The orchestra program begins at Davis Intermediate (5th–6th grade), continues at McMillan Junior High (7th–8th grade), and caps at Wylie High School. The district explicitly permits students to transfer between campuses to remain in the orchestra feeder sequence, even if rezoning or a grade-level cap would otherwise assign them to a different campus. This is an unusual and practical policy for music-focused families — if orchestra participation is important to your student, confirm the transfer eligibility at enrollment time and get it documented.

Fine Arts (Beyond Orchestra)

Districtwide access across all levels. Wylie ISD offers a full fine arts menu: Band, Choir, Dance, Elementary Music, Orchestra, Theatre, Visual Arts, and Music Enrichment. The depth of fine arts programming across all 21 campuses is consistent with a district that takes the arts seriously alongside its CTE infrastructure. Families whose students are serious about band or choir at the high school level should contact the campus director of bands or choirs at their assigned high school to understand audition requirements and ensemble structures.

Gifted & Talented (GT)

Districtwide; identification through assessment process. Wylie ISD identifies and serves Gifted & Talented students through a state-compliant assessment and identification process. GT services begin in elementary and continue through secondary grades. Contact the district's GT coordinator for referral procedures, assessment timelines, and how services are structured at the campus level for your child's grade.

Special Education & 504 Services

Districtwide; all campuses. Wylie ISD is required by Texas law to convene an ARD (Admissions, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of a transfer student's enrollment to review the existing IEP and determine the appropriate placement in Wylie ISD. Bring all prior IEP, 504, and evaluation documentation to the enrollment appointment. For families with students who have complex or specialized needs, a direct conversation with the district's Special Services office before finalizing a home purchase is strongly recommended — campus capacity and service availability can vary.

Enrollment & Registration

How to Enroll (2025–26 School Year)

Wylie ISD uses an online enrollment portal powered by Skyward, accessible at skyward-wb.wylieisd.net. New families create an account, complete the online registration forms, and then submit supporting documents — either at the campus or through the district office.

Documents Required at Enrollment

  • Proof of residency (utility bill, lease, or mortgage statement with current Wylie ISD address)
  • Child's birth certificate
  • Texas Certificate of Immunization (up to date per DSHS requirements)
  • Social Security card (if available)
  • Most recent report card or academic transcript
  • Parent or guardian driver's license or photo ID
  • Previous school records, including any IEP, 504, or special program documentation

Intra-District Transfers

Transfer requests within Wylie ISD are handled differently by grade level. At the elementary level, intra-district transfers are considered within the same feeder cluster in limited circumstances. At the intermediate, junior high, and high school levels, the district characterizes intra-district transfers as available only under "extreme circumstances." Children of Wylie ISD employees may transfer at all grade levels without this restriction. The orchestra program is an explicitly documented exception — students in the orchestra feeder sequence may transfer between campuses to maintain program continuity. Do not assume transfer flexibility exists beyond these defined scenarios.

Inter-District (Open Enrollment): Experience Wylie ISD

Wylie ISD operates an open enrollment program called "Experience Wylie ISD" that accepts a limited number of students from outside the district's attendance boundaries. The application window typically opens in the fall and closes in late January (the 2026–27 window closed January 23, 2026). Enrollment through this program is space-dependent, renewed annually (parents must reapply each year), and transportation is the parent's responsibility — the district does not provide busing for open enrollment students. Families already living in Wylie ISD who are interested in a different campus within the district do not use this program — that falls under intra-district transfer policy above.

Special Education Transfers

Under Texas law, Wylie ISD must convene an ARD meeting within 30 days of a transfer student's first day to review the existing IEP. Bring all documentation — evaluations, IEPs, 504 plans, therapy logs — to the enrollment appointment. Contact the Special Services department at 972.429.3000 before finalizing enrollment if your student has a complex or evolving IEP.

Boundary Verification

This is the section most relocating families skip — and it's the one that causes the most problems after closing. Wylie ISD serves a multi-city service area across a 41-square-mile district, and the boundaries do not follow city lines. Before making an offer on any home in Sachse, Murphy, Lucas, Lavon, St. Paul, or even the City of Wylie itself, you need to verify three things: (1) your address is in Wylie ISD, (2) your address feeds the feeder cluster you expect, and (3) the high school zone for your address reflects the 2025–26 rezoning.

Communities Served — and Their Edge Cases

City of Wylie: Most of the City of Wylie is in Wylie ISD — but the district explicitly discloses that some addresses within the City of Wylie are not zoned to Wylie ISD. This is uncommon but not rare in fast-growth areas where city limits expand faster than ISD boundary adjustments. Never assume a Wylie mailing address means Wylie ISD enrollment.

Sachse: A significant portion of Sachse is in Wylie ISD — but Sachse also has homes that feed Garland ISD. This is the most frequent boundary surprise in this district's service area. Sachse buyers who assume they're getting Wylie ISD based on their city address have been caught off-guard at enrollment. The boundary between Wylie ISD and Garland ISD runs through Sachse and does not follow obvious geographic markers.

Murphy: Similar to Sachse — parts of Murphy feed Wylie ISD, and parts do not. Verify every Murphy address independently.

Lucas: Lucas is largely in Wylie ISD territory, though homes on the edges may feed adjacent ISDs. The Lovejoy ISD boundary is nearby for Lucas homes in the southwestern portions.

Lavon and St. Paul: These communities are generally served by Wylie ISD but are smaller and more rural — new construction on the outer edges of either city should be verified carefully.

How to Verify Your Address

  • Wylie ISD address lookup: infofinderi.com (Wylie ISD portal) — enter the property address to confirm district membership and feeder campus assignment
  • Collin County Appraisal District: collincad.org/propertysearch — search by address to find the legal ISD designation on the property tax record
  • TEA School Directory: tea.texas.gov/apps/school-directory — find campus assignments by address
  • District main office: 951 S. Ballard Ave, Wylie TX 75098 · 972.429.3000

2025–26 Rezoning

Wylie ISD completed a boundary rezoning for the 2025–26 school year. If you are using older boundary maps (from a real estate listing, a school-ranking site, or a prior year's district publication), those maps may no longer be accurate. Always use the district's current address lookup tool — not a cached or prior-year map — to determine your campus assignment.

New Construction Edge Cases

Wylie ISD is one of the few districts in this guide that explicitly warns buyers about grade-level caps: if a campus reaches maximum enrollment, a student may be assigned to a different campus than their residential zone, even if their address is clearly within that zone. For families buying in new communities where the elementary school is still being completed or is newly opened, ask the district whether the home campus has any current capacity constraints before finalizing the purchase.

Making an offer without verifying your specific address can mean your child attends a different school than you planned — and boundary maps do not always match common-sense geography. This is especially true in Wylie ISD, where six communities, a recent rezoning, and explicit grade-level cap warnings all create a verification burden that is higher than average for this region.

School Finance & Tax Rate

Wylie ISD's total certified tax rate for 2024 is approximately $1.12 per $100 of assessed value (verify the current certified rate at the Texas Comptroller's certified tax rate search — ACTION: confirm the exact 2025 certified rate before publishing). Texas property taxes are assessed on the full appraised value of the property, and Wylie ISD's rate is applied to the taxable value after exemptions.

What That Rate Means in Practice

On a $450,000 home — a reasonable mid-point for Wylie ISD territory — a rate of $1.12 per $100 translates to approximately $5,040 per year in ISD taxes before exemptions, or roughly $420 per month added to a mortgage payment. Texas's $100,000 homestead exemption (established 2023) reduces the taxable value, bringing that same $450,000 home's ISD-taxable value to $350,000 — approximately $3,920 per year, or about $327 per month. This is a meaningful monthly number and should be factored into your total housing cost calculation when comparing Wylie ISD territory to neighboring districts.

Comparison to Nearby Districts

Wylie ISD's rate sits in the middle of the Collin County range. Allen ISD's total rate is approximately $1.10–$1.12; McKinney ISD is similar; Frisco ISD runs slightly higher; and Garland ISD (which serves some Sachse and Murphy addresses) has its own rate structure as a Dallas County district. Grapevine-Colleyville ISD, further west, carries one of the lowest rates in DFW at approximately $0.92. The specific delta between Wylie ISD and Allen ISD — the most common comparison — is narrow enough that tax rate alone is rarely the deciding factor for families between those two districts; program and campus fit tend to matter more.

Bond History and Recent Changes

Wylie ISD, like most fast-growth Collin County districts, has passed bond elections over the past decade to fund new campus construction and facility upgrades to accommodate enrollment growth. For the most current information on pending bonds or rate changes, review the district's Finance department page at wylieisd.net/departments/finance.

Per-pupil expenditure data was not confirmed from official sources during this research cycle — verify the most current figure at txschools.gov under the Wylie ISD PEIMS financial data section.

How Wylie ISD Compares

Families considering Wylie ISD territory most commonly cross-shop with Allen ISD, McKinney ISD, and (for Sachse and Murphy addresses) Garland ISD. Here's how they compare on key factors:

District Size TEA Grade Tax Rate Best For

Allen ISD ~23,000 A (most recent) ~$1.10–$1.12 Established academic reputation, more centrally located, single-district-identity (City of Allen only)
McKinney ISD ~25,000 B (most recent) ~$1.12–$1.15 Larger program variety, multiple HS options, more geographic spread within McKinney

The housing price difference between Allen and Wylie is meaningful, though — Wylie typically offers more square footage per dollar, particularly in newer communities east of Allen. McKinney ISD is geographically adjacent and similar in size and ratings profile; the deciding factor between Wylie and McKinney often comes down to which neighborhood and commute geometry makes more sense for a specific family's situation. Garland ISD is relevant only for Sachse and Murphy buyers whose address verification shows a Garland assignment — in that case, the choice shifts from a Wylie vs. Allen conversation to a very different set of tradeoffs entirely.

Community Feel & Parent Culture

Wylie ISD territory has a distinctly Texas suburban character — a blend of established older neighborhoods near the historic downtown Wylie square and fast-growing new construction communities in the eastern and northern reaches of the district. The parent engagement culture in Wylie ISD tends to be active at the elementary and intermediate levels, with strong booster club involvement in athletics, fine arts, and CTE programs. Youth sports in the Wylie-Sachse-Murphy area are well-organized, with recreational leagues in football, baseball, softball, soccer, and basketball operating through city park-and-rec systems that the school community actively participates in.

Parents who engage on community forums tend to describe the district warmly, with specific praise for individual teachers and campus-level administrators. The most consistent theme in candid parent conversations is the high school attendance zone question — families who verified their address early and chose based on the high school they wanted report satisfaction; those who assumed based on city address and ended up in an unexpected zone occasionally express frustration. The district's recent 2025–26 rezoning adds a new layer to this: even families who verified their assignment a year or two ago should re-verify for any active home search.

One unique cultural element worth noting: Wylie ISD serves communities at different price points and growth stages simultaneously — established neighborhoods in the City of Wylie proper, more affordable new construction in Lavon and the eastern portions, and some relatively newer development in Lucas. That geographic and socioeconomic range means the district feel varies meaningfully by campus. A family whose elementary school is in a well-established, highly involved neighborhood may have a different campus culture experience than one whose campus opened in the last five years serving primarily newer arrivals. Both can be excellent — they're just different, and it's worth visiting campuses in person as part of the home-search process if you can.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my Wylie (or Sachse or Murphy) address guarantee enrollment in Wylie ISD? ▾
No — and this is one of the most important things to verify before making an offer. Wylie ISD itself explicitly discloses that some addresses within the City of Wylie are not zoned to Wylie ISD. In Sachse and Murphy, a portion of homes feed Garland ISD, not Wylie ISD — even though those cities are commonly associated with Wylie ISD in real estate listings. Lavon and St. Paul edge cases also exist. Always verify your specific address using the district's address lookup tool at infofinderi.com (search for Wylie ISD) or by calling the district at 972.429.3000 before finalizing a purchase contract. Do not rely on a listing agent's verbal assurance, a school-rating website's campus assignment, or prior-year boundary maps — verify with the district directly using your exact address.
What's the difference between Wylie High School and Wylie East High School? ▾
Both are 6A high schools within Wylie ISD, but their 2025 TEA Distinction Designation records differ meaningfully: Wylie High School received 5 of 7 possible distinctions, while Wylie East High School received 1 of 7. TEA Distinction Designations recognize campuses that significantly exceed state performance standards in specific domains, so the gap is operationally meaningful for families focused on accountability metrics. Wylie HS is also home to the capstone of the district's orchestra program and houses the majority of CTE pathways. Wylie East serves the eastern portions of the district, including many newer residential developments. Which campus your child attends depends on your specific home address and the 2025–26 feeder zone assignment — not on which city your address lists. Verify your high school zone at the district address lookup tool before buying.
How does Wylie ISD handle IEP and 504 transfers from another state or district? ▾
Under Texas law, Wylie ISD must convene an ARD (Admissions, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of a transfer student's first day of enrollment to review the existing IEP and determine the appropriate placement and services within Wylie ISD. For 504 plans, the district reviews the prior plan and determines equivalency under its own policies. Bring all documentation to the enrollment appointment — evaluations, IEPs, 504 plans, therapy logs, prior year progress reports, and any relevant medical documentation. For students with complex or evolving needs, contact the Special Services department at 972.429.3000 before finalizing your home purchase. Campus capacity and service availability can vary, and a preliminary conversation will help you understand what to expect before enrollment.
Why does Wylie ISD have a "B" TEA grade if Niche rates it an "A"? ▾
These two ratings measure different things through different methodologies. TEA's A-F accountability system evaluates Texas public school districts on three state-defined domains: Student Achievement (STAAR proficiency and graduation rates), School Progress (year-over-year growth and performance relative to similar districts), and Closing the Gaps (performance equity across student subgroups). Wylie ISD's most recently published TEA letter grade — issued for the 2022–23 school year — was a B, reflecting solid performance across all three domains without reaching the distinction threshold. Niche.com's "A" grade is a third-party composite that weighs a broader set of inputs, including parent reviews, college readiness indicators, and campus-level data in ways that differ from TEA's formula. Neither rating is wrong — they answer different questions. For a relocating family, the TEA Distinction Designations at the campus level (especially the high school distinctions) and STAAR proficiency rates by campus are the most operationally useful data points for individual school decisions.
What is the "Experience Wylie ISD" open enrollment program? ▾
Experience Wylie ISD is the district's open enrollment program that allows a limited number of students who live outside the district's attendance boundaries to enroll in Wylie ISD campuses. It is not a magnet or specialized program — it provides access to the district's standard campus programs at the student's assigned campus based on space availability. The application window typically opens in the fall and closes in late January for the following school year. Acceptance is not guaranteed; space is limited and determined on a grade-by-grade, campus-by-campus basis. Students accepted through Experience Wylie ISD must reapply annually and are responsible for their own transportation — the district does not provide busing. This program is designed for out-of-district families, not for families who already live within the district and want to transfer to a different campus (that falls under intra-district transfer policy).
How does Wylie ISD's tax rate compare to Allen ISD and McKinney ISD? ▾
Wylie ISD's total certified tax rate is approximately $1.12 per $100 of assessed value (verify the exact 2025 certified rate at the Texas Comptroller's website). Allen ISD's rate is similar, running approximately $1.10–$1.12. McKinney ISD is in a comparable range at approximately $1.12–$1.15. The differences between these three districts are narrow — a few cents per $100 — which translates to a relatively modest monthly dollar difference on a given home price. On a $400,000 home (after Texas's $100,000 homestead exemption), a 0.05 per $100 rate difference is approximately $12.50 per month. For most families, the rate comparison between Wylie, Allen, and McKinney is not the primary financial driver — the home price difference between those markets is typically a larger factor. Grapevine-Colleyville ISD, further west, has the lowest rate in the DFW area at approximately $0.92, but the housing market there is in a different price tier entirely.
Can my student transfer between campuses to stay in the orchestra program if we move within the district? ▾
Yes — Wylie ISD has an explicit transfer accommodation for orchestra students. The orchestra pathway runs from Davis Intermediate (5th–6th grade) through McMillan Junior High (7th–8th grade) to Wylie High School. If a rezoning or grade-level cap would otherwise assign your student to a different campus in the feeder sequence, students in the orchestra program can request a transfer to maintain continuity in the program. This is one of the few intra-district transfer scenarios the district supports beyond the elementary feeder cluster and employee-child provisions. Confirm this accommodation with the district's enrollment team at the start of each school year and document it in writing — transfer approvals are not automatic and should be confirmed annually.
