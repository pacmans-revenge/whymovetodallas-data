District Overview

Prosper Independent School District is one of the most remarkable growth stories in Texas public education. The district served approximately 8,000 students in 2015 and enrolls nearly 28,400 students today — a nearly fourfold increase in a decade. Thirty-five campuses and counting have opened to keep pace, with new construction underway as the communities of Prosper and surrounding areas continue to grow.

The district's primary service area includes the City of Prosper (Collin County), portions of Celina, and — critically — portions of the City of Frisco that fall within PISD's boundaries rather than Frisco ISD's. It also encompasses border areas of McKinney. This boundary complexity is one of the most important things any family buying a home in this corridor needs to understand before selecting an address.

Despite its explosive growth, Prosper ISD has maintained exceptional academic performance, earning a TEA A rating (score: 91) and a Niche A for 2024–25. That combination — elite outcomes alongside a district still actively building its infrastructure — is what defines the PISD experience for families arriving today.

District administrative offices are located at 605 E. 7th Street, Prosper, TX 75078. The district website is prosperisd.net.

Key Communities Served

Prosper (primary), Celina (portions), City of Frisco (boundary areas), McKinney (border areas). Growth is ongoing — new campuses open annually and attendance zones shift accordingly.

TEA Accountability Rating

Prosper ISD holds a TEA A rating with a score of 91 out of 100 for the 2024–25 school year. This places PISD among the top-performing large school districts in Texas — a meaningful achievement for a district that has added tens of thousands of students over the past decade while maintaining academic excellence.

The A rating reflects strong performance across TEA's three accountability domains: Student Achievement (STAAR proficiency rates), School Progress (year-over-year growth), and Closing the Gaps (equity across student subgroups). For a fast-growing district serving predominantly new construction communities, maintaining this level of performance requires deliberate investment in curriculum alignment and teacher retention — both areas PISD has prioritized.

Campus-level TEA scores for individual Prosper ISD schools are available at txschools.gov. Because the district is growing so rapidly and new campuses open with smaller initial cohorts, campus-level scores can fluctuate more year-to-year than in a stable-enrollment district. The district's overall A score is the most reliable performance signal for families evaluating PISD as a whole.

STAAR Performance

Prosper ISD's STAAR proficiency rates are among the highest in Collin County, consistent with its TEA A rating. The district's student population — predominantly families who relocated specifically to access high-performing schools — produces strong aggregate academic outcomes across grade levels and subjects. Campus-level results are available at txschools.gov by campus name or PISD district ID.

Advanced Placement

All three Prosper ISD high schools offer AP course catalogs spanning core academic subjects: English Language, English Literature, Calculus AB/BC, Statistics, Chemistry, Biology, Physics, US History, World History, Government, Psychology, Spanish, and more. AP participation and pass rates at Prosper High School are well-established; Rock Hill and Walnut Grove high schools are newer campuses whose AP programs continue to expand as grade cohorts mature.

Dual Credit

PISD partners with Collin College and other accredited Texas institutions to offer dual enrollment opportunities for qualifying juniors and seniors. Students can earn college credit tuition-free while completing high school graduation requirements. Dual credit course availability continues to expand across all three high school campuses as PISD grows its college readiness infrastructure.

Graduation Rate

Prosper ISD's graduation rate of 99.1% is exceptional by any measure — among the highest of any major Collin County district. It reflects a student population with high college aspirations and a culture of academic expectation that families relocating here are actively choosing.

Boundary Change Warning

New campus openings in Prosper ISD routinely trigger attendance zone redraws. A neighborhood that fed one elementary school in 2023 may feed a different campus by 2025. If you are buying in a new construction development in PISD, verify the current campus assignment — not just the one listed in marketing materials — by calling the district directly at the time of your offer.

Special Programs

Gifted & Talented (GT)

Prosper ISD provides a multi-criteria GT identification process beginning in elementary school. GT services include differentiated curriculum, pull-out enrichment opportunities, and accelerated coursework pathways through middle and high school. The GT program continues to expand alongside district growth.

Advanced Placement (AP)

All three PISD high schools offer AP courses across core academic disciplines. AP participation rates are high, reflecting the academically motivated student population that characterizes new-construction Collin County communities. Prosper HS has the most established AP catalog; Rock Hill and Walnut Grove are building theirs.

Project Lead The Way (PLTW)

PISD has implemented Project Lead The Way STEM curriculum at multiple campuses, providing hands-on engineering design, biomedical science, and computer science pathways from elementary through high school. PLTW programs connect to CTE certification pathways at the high school level.

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

PISD's CTE pathways cover technology, healthcare, business, digital media, and construction trades. Students can pursue industry-recognized certifications alongside their core academic coursework. CTE programs are growing rapidly alongside the district itself, with new pathways added each year.

Fine Arts

Band, choir, orchestra, visual art, and theater are offered at all secondary campuses. Prosper HS fine arts programs are well-established; Rock Hill and Walnut Grove are building strong traditions of their own. Athletics facilities and fine arts performance spaces at newer campuses are purpose-built — among the best-equipped in Collin County.

Dual Credit

Partnerships with Collin College allow juniors and seniors to earn college credit tuition-free. Dual credit availability is expanding across all three high schools. Eligible students can enter college with a meaningful credit head start while still in high school.

Special Education & Support Services

Prosper ISD is required under IDEA to provide a full continuum of special education services. The district's rapid growth means that specialized programs are actively expanding — a fact that is both an asset and a caution for families with children who have complex needs. PISD has invested in autism support services and structured learning environments as enrollment has scaled, and the district continues to develop its SpEd infrastructure in step with its overall growth.

Services available through PISD include: speech-language therapy, occupational therapy, physical therapy, applied behavior analysis (ABA) support, resource and co-taught inclusion models, self-contained classroom options, life skills programming, and transition services for secondary students. The ARD process in PISD follows Texas law — an Admission, Review, and Dismissal meeting must occur within 30 days of enrollment for any student transferring with an existing IEP.

For families relocating with a child who has an IEP or 504 plan, contact PISD's Special Education Department directly before enrollment. Bringing complete documentation — most recent evaluation reports, current IEP, prior written notice, and placement records — will significantly expedite the transition. With a district growing this quickly, the SpEd team is managing a high volume of incoming transfers; early contact and thorough paperwork are your best tools for a smooth start.

PISD also provides dyslexia testing and intervention per the Texas Dyslexia Handbook, Section 504 accommodations, and English Language Learning (ELL) services. Given the district's growing student population, ELL services are an expanding priority.

Athletics & Extracurriculars

Prosper ISD competes in UIL Class 6A — the highest classification — at all three high schools. Prosper High School Eagles have established a strong athletics tradition across football, cross-country, swimming, track, and multiple other sports. The Eagles have been regionally competitive across classifications, and the campus culture has a deep pride in athletics that reflects the engaged community that built it.

Rock Hill High School Hawks are building their own athletic identity since opening in 2021. Cross-country and track programs at Rock Hill have been regionally competitive early in the school's history. Football and other established sports continue to develop as the school's upper-class population grows.

Athletics and fine arts facilities at all PISD campuses benefit from the district's bond-funded construction program — purpose-built stadiums, gymnasiums, and performance spaces are among the best in Collin County for campuses of their age.

Student organizations, academic competition teams (UIL Academic, DECA, FBLA, Science Olympiad), and a wide range of clubs are available at the high school level. Given the district's size and growth trajectory, extracurricular offerings continue to expand each year.

School Choice Within the District

Attendance Zone Assignment

Campus assignment in Prosper ISD is based entirely on home address, and assignments are updated regularly as new campuses open. This is the single most important operational fact for families buying in PISD. A home purchased in a development marketed with a specific campus assignment may be rezoned before your child's first day — particularly in new construction areas where entire neighborhoods are created faster than the district can finalize zones. Always call PISD to confirm your current campus assignment at the time of your offer.

Inter-District Transfers

Transfer requests from students residing within PISD boundaries (intra-district) are considered on a space-available basis, with applications typically opening in spring. Given the rapid growth of all campuses, available transfer spots are limited. Inter-district transfers from outside PISD boundaries follow the same process and face similar capacity constraints.

No Magnet or Choice Programs

Unlike some larger DFW districts, Prosper ISD does not currently operate magnet school programs or district-wide specialized choice programs (such as IB or STEM academies requiring a separate application). All specialty programming — GT, AP, CTE, PLTW — is available at neighborhood campuses through the normal assignment process.

Frisco Address, PISD Boundary — Confirm Before Purchase

This bears repeating: portions of the City of Frisco are served by Prosper ISD, not Frisco ISD. For families relocating specifically to access Frisco ISD — one of the most consistently rated districts in Collin County — a Frisco mailing address is not a guarantee of Frisco ISD enrollment. Use the TEA School District Locator (available at txschools.gov) with the specific property address before any purchase decision.

District Finances

Tax Rate

Prosper ISD's total tax rate of $1.26 per $100 of assessed value is the highest among the major profiled DFW school districts. This rate reflects the significant bond program PISD has undertaken to build and equip 35+ campuses over the past decade — new schools, gymnasiums, athletic facilities, technology infrastructure, and student transportation to serve a district that has quadrupled in size. Bond-funded growth is the direct driver of the rate premium over neighboring districts.

For a home assessed at $600,000, the PISD school district tax levy is approximately $7,560 per year before exemptions. The Texas homestead exemption reduces the taxable assessed value by $100,000 for school district purposes, lowering the effective levy to approximately $6,300 on a $600,000 home. Buyers in new construction should also confirm the current Collin County appraisal district assessed value — newly built homes are often assessed at or near purchase price in the first year, which can create a tax bill higher than the prior year's estimate suggests.

Compared to neighboring districts: Frisco ISD is approximately $1.03, Allen ISD approximately $1.07, and McKinney ISD approximately $1.17. The PISD premium reflects growth investment rather than operational inefficiency — but it is a real cost that families should factor into their relocation budget.

FIRST Rating

Prosper ISD participates in the Texas Financial Integrity Rating System (FIRST). The district has earned a Superior Achievement FIRST designation, indicating sound fiscal management and transparent financial reporting despite the extraordinary complexity of managing a rapidly expanding bond program. Current FIRST status and financial reports are available at prosperisd.net.

Bond Program Context

PISD has passed multiple bond packages to fund its growth — and more are likely in the coming years as the district continues to build. Bonds approved by Collin County voters fund capital construction without directly raising the maintenance and operations tax rate, but they do increase the debt service portion of the total tax rate. The $1.26 total rate includes both components. As bonds are retired and growth stabilizes, the rate may decline — but families buying now should plan for the current rate.

Enrollment — New Families

How to Enroll

New student enrollment for Prosper ISD is managed through the district's online enrollment portal at prosperisd.net. The enrollment portal typically opens in spring for the following school year. Mid-year enrollments are accepted on a rolling basis. Required documentation includes:

  • Proof of residency — signed lease, deed, or closing disclosure. A secondary document (utility bill) may be required.
  • Birth certificate — required for initial Texas enrollment
  • Immunization records — Texas DSHS-required vaccine schedule
  • Prior academic records — transcript, report card, or standardized test results
  • IEP/504 documentation — if applicable; bring complete evaluation and placement records

Contact the district enrollment office at (469) 219-2000 or visit prosperisd.net for current enrollment procedures and campus assignment verification. Given high enrollment volume, reaching out early in the process — before your move date if possible — is strongly recommended.

Campus Assignment — The Most Important Step

Before finalizing any home purchase or lease in PISD, call the district to confirm your assigned campus at that specific address. Do not rely on campus assignments listed in builder marketing materials, real estate listings, or neighborhood websites — these may be outdated. New campus openings frequently trigger zone redraws that change assignments for existing addresses. This step takes five minutes and can prevent significant frustration.

Special Education Enrollment

Families with a child who has an existing IEP should contact PISD's Special Education Department before enrollment day. Texas law requires PISD to either adopt the existing IEP or convene an ARD meeting within 30 days of enrollment. Bringing complete documentation — most recent evaluation report, current IEP, prior written notice, and any related service records — will significantly accelerate the transition process. Early contact is especially important in a high-growth district managing large incoming transfer volumes.

New Construction Buyers — A Note

If you are purchasing a home currently under construction in a Prosper ISD development, verify the campus assignment once the home reaches substantial completion — not at the time of contract. New campuses sometimes open between your purchase agreement and your move-in date, changing which school your address feeds. Confirm again shortly before enrollment.

Frequently Asked Questions

My new construction home has a Frisco address — does that mean I'm in Frisco ISD? ▾
Not necessarily. Portions of the City of Frisco fall within Prosper ISD's attendance boundaries, not Frisco ISD's. A Frisco mailing address reflects the city limits designation, not the school district boundary — those are drawn independently. The only way to confirm your district is to verify the specific address using the TEA School District Locator at txschools.gov, or by calling Prosper ISD directly. This is one of the most common and consequential errors families make in this corridor. Both Prosper ISD and Frisco ISD are excellent districts — but if you are specifically choosing for one over the other, verify before you sign.
Can my child's campus assignment change after we buy our home? ▾
Yes — and in Prosper ISD, this has happened with regularity. As new campuses open, the district redraws attendance zones across all grade levels. A neighborhood that fed one elementary school in one year may be reassigned to a newly opened campus the following year. Prosper ISD provides advance notice of boundary changes, but the timing does not always align with new construction purchase timelines. Families should build this possibility into their expectations: the specific campus your child attends may differ from what was in place when you purchased. The district's overall academic quality remains strong across all campuses.
Why is Prosper ISD's tax rate the highest in this area? ▾
Prosper ISD's $1.26 per $100 tax rate reflects the extraordinary bond program required to build 35+ campuses over the past decade — a pace of construction almost unmatched in Texas. Each new school requires bond financing, which is repaid through the debt service portion of the tax rate. The district has earned a Superior Achievement FIRST rating, meaning the money is being managed transparently and effectively. As bonds are repaid and enrollment growth eventually stabilizes, the rate may moderate. For families buying now, plan for the current rate. A homestead exemption of $100,000 off your assessed value applies to school district taxes, which provides meaningful relief.
Does Prosper ISD have strong special education services? ▾
Prosper ISD provides the full IDEA-required continuum of special education services and has invested in expanding autism support and structured learning environments as the district has grown. Honest assessment: this is a district in the middle of rapid expansion, which means SpEd programs are also expanding and not yet as mature as those in longer-established districts with decades of SpEd infrastructure. For students with complex or specialized needs, families should schedule a direct conversation with PISD's Special Education Department before enrollment to understand exactly what services are available and how their child's IEP will be implemented. For students with milder support needs, the district's resources are solid and growing.
How does Prosper ISD's academic quality compare to Frisco ISD and Allen ISD? ▾
All three districts are strong performers in Collin County. Frisco ISD and Allen ISD are well-established with decades of academic infrastructure; both carry TEA A ratings and Niche A grades. Prosper ISD also holds a TEA A (score: 91) and Niche A — remarkable for a district that has quadrupled in size in a decade. The honest difference: Frisco and Allen have stable enrollment and campus-level consistency built over many years; Prosper has equivalent outcomes today but more variability as new campuses open and mature. For families prioritizing a brand-new campus experience in a fast-growth community, PISD is a genuinely excellent choice. For families prioritizing long-established campus cultures, Frisco and Allen offer that stability.
What is the enrollment process and when should I start? ▾
The PISD enrollment portal opens each spring for the following school year. Mid-year enrollment is accepted on a rolling basis. Start as early as possible — the district's high incoming enrollment volume means early contact gives you more time to resolve any questions, particularly around campus assignments and SpEd transitions. Before enrolling, call or email the district to confirm your home's current campus assignment for each grade level. Required documents include proof of residency, birth certificate, immunization records, prior academic records, and IEP/504 documentation if applicable. The PISD enrollment office can be reached through prosperisd.net.
