District Overview

Coppell Independent School District is one of the most academically distinguished small districts in Texas, serving approximately 13,361 students across 16 campuses. Its compact scale — compared to mega-districts like Frisco or Plano — creates a focused, community-driven school environment where resources are concentrated and families remain closely connected to district leadership.

The district's primary service area is the city of Coppell, but its boundary extends into portions of Irving — specifically the Valley Ranch neighborhood. This is an important detail for families searching homes in Las Colinas or Valley Ranch: some Irving addresses fall within Coppell ISD. Address verification is essential before any offer, particularly near city boundary lines.

Coppell ISD's high per-pupil expenditure of $15,984 reflects the strength of the local commercial and industrial tax base — anchored by proximity to DFW International Airport and major logistics and distribution operations nearby. This translates into exceptional program investment for a district of its size. The district's central offices are located at 200 S. Denton Tap Road, Coppell, TX 75019.

Key Cities Served

Coppell (primary), portions of Irving (Valley Ranch neighborhood), and select addresses in the Las Colinas area. City of address does not determine ISD — always verify your specific address using the TEA School District Locator before making an offer.

TEA Accountability Rating

The Texas Education Agency assigns every school district and campus an annual A–F accountability rating based on three domains. Coppell ISD holds a TEA A rating (score: 93 out of 100) for the 2024–25 school year — placing it among the highest-scoring districts in the Dallas–Fort Worth area regardless of size.

What the three domains measure:

  • Student Achievement — STAAR test scores, graduation rates, college/career readiness indicators
  • School Progress — year-over-year academic growth compared to similar campuses
  • Closing the Gaps — performance equity across student demographic groups

A score of 93 is notably high even within the A range, which begins at 90 points. Coppell ISD's score reflects consistent excellence across all three domains — not simply a strong performance in one area masking weakness in another. Individual campus ratings are published at txschools.gov; virtually every Coppell ISD campus carries an A or B rating.

The district has also earned multiple TEA Distinction Designations — awarded to the top 25% of similar districts statewide — in categories including Academic Achievement in Reading/Language Arts, Mathematics, Science, and Comparative Academic Growth. These distinctions are competitive at any district size; earning them at Coppell's smaller enrollment underscores the depth of academic performance across the student body.

Graduation Rate

Coppell ISD's four-year graduation rate is 98% — among the highest in DFW and the state. This figure reflects not only the socioeconomic profile of the community but also the district's genuine investment in student support services, academic advising, and intervention programs that keep students on track from freshman year forward.

Advanced Placement & International Baccalaureate

Coppell High School offers one of the most extensive AP catalogs in North Texas. For families seeking the IB pathway, the district operates a full IB Diploma Programme at Coppell High School — making it an IB World School. This means students who live within Coppell ISD have direct access to the IB curriculum at their zoned high school, without requiring a transfer to a specialty campus across town. IB and AP are complementary pathways at the same campus; students self-select based on interest, rigor preference, and counselor guidance.

New Tech @ Coppell

Within Coppell High School, the district operates New Tech @ Coppell — a project-based learning magnet that uses real-world collaborative challenges rather than traditional lecture-based instruction. New Tech is nationally recognized and available to CISD students by application. It represents a meaningful differentiator: a district of Coppell's size offering not just IB and AP, but a third rigorous academic pathway anchored in applied learning.

College & Career Readiness

CISD tracks College, Career, and Military Readiness (CCMR) as required by TEA. The district's CCMR rate — the percentage of graduates who complete at least one qualifying indicator — is among the highest in DFW. Indicators include AP exam scores of 3 or above, IB exam scores, dual enrollment completions, and industry certifications earned through CTE pathways.

STAAR Proficiency

District-wide STAAR proficiency rates consistently exceed state averages across all tested subjects and grade levels. Campus-level data is available at txschools.gov. Coppell's smaller class sizes relative to larger DFW districts contribute to more individualized instruction — a factor that shows clearly in student outcome data at the elementary and middle school levels.

Special Programs

IB Diploma Programme

Coppell High School is an authorized IB World School offering the full IB Diploma Programme for grades 11–12, with IB coursework beginning in grade 9. Students in the IB track pursue a rigorous international curriculum alongside a Theory of Knowledge course, extended essay, and CAS (Creativity, Activity, Service) requirements. This pathway is available to all CISD students zoned to CHS — no cross-district transfer required.

New Tech @ Coppell

A nationally recognized project-based learning magnet within Coppell High School. Enrollment is by application within CISD. Students develop real-world problem-solving, communication, and collaboration skills through extended projects rather than traditional lecture and test cycles. Available to incoming freshmen who apply and are accepted.

Advanced Placement (AP)

Coppell High School offers an extensive AP catalog covering virtually all College Board AP subjects. AP participation and passing rates are among the highest in DFW. Most college-bound students take at least 3–5 AP courses over their high school career. AP and IB courses can be blended based on counselor guidance and student load preferences.

Dual Language Immersion (DLI)

Coppell ISD offers a Spanish/English Dual Language Immersion program at the elementary level. DLI is available by application within the district and typically begins in Kindergarten. The program develops bilingual proficiency alongside grade-level academic content. Enrollment is on a first-come, first-served / application basis with earlier deadlines than general enrollment — check the district calendar each spring.

Gifted & Talented (GT)

GT services are available K–12 across all CISD campuses. Identification uses a multi-criteria process including academic performance, ability assessments, and teacher/parent input. GT services at the elementary level include pull-out enrichment; at the secondary level, acceleration, honors coursework, and IB/AP pathways serve identified students.

Fine Arts & CTE

Coppell ISD is known for strong fine arts programming — visual art, orchestra, choir, and theatre are offered across all secondary campuses and advance regularly to UIL State competition. Career and Technical Education (CTE) pathways at the high school level include technology, business, health science, and digital media, with industry certifications available alongside the diploma.

Special Education & Support Services

Coppell ISD provides a full continuum of special education services for eligible students ages 3–21 under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). The district is known for a strong inclusion model — particularly at the elementary level — where students with IEPs are supported within general education classrooms alongside typically developing peers to the maximum extent appropriate.

The district's smaller size is an advantage for families of students with disabilities: caseloads per specialist tend to be lower than in large DFW districts, and individualized planning through the ARD (Admissions, Review, and Dismissal) process receives more focused attention. Related services including speech-language therapy, occupational therapy, physical therapy, and behavioral support are provided across all campuses.

Families relocating with a student who has an existing IEP from another state should contact CISD's Special Education department immediately upon establishing Texas residency. Texas law requires the district to either adopt the current IEP or convene a new ARD meeting within 30 days of enrollment. Bring all prior IEP documents, evaluation reports, and eligibility determinations to the enrollment appointment.

For students who require 504 accommodations (but do not qualify for special education under IDEA), contact the campus 504 Coordinator. CISD also offers dyslexia testing and intervention services at all campuses per Texas state requirements.

Athletics & Extracurriculars

Coppell High School competes in the University Interscholastic League (UIL) at Class 5A/6A — the upper tiers of UIL competition — as the Cowboys. The single-high-school structure means every student at Coppell High School represents the same team, which generates an exceptionally unified school culture and community following around athletics.

Coppell ISD athletic programs are competitive across multiple sports, with particular strength in swimming and tennis, which have produced UIL State qualifiers and champions. Football, basketball, baseball, softball, soccer, cross country, and volleyball all field competitive programs. The Cowboys athletic identity is a defining feature of the community — Friday night football at Coppell ISD stadium draws the broader Coppell community regardless of whether families have students on the field.

Beyond athletics, Coppell High School offers a wide range of UIL academic competitions (Academic Decathlon, Debate, Science Olympiad, One Act Play) and student organizations aligned to CTE pathways including DECA, FBLA, HOSA, and SkillsUSA. Fine arts UIL competition is separate and equally strong — the orchestra, choir, band, and theatre programs at CHS regularly advance to state.

School Choice Within the District

Out-of-District Open Enrollment

Coppell ISD accepts a limited number of out-of-district transfer students on a space-available basis. This is not a guaranteed option — capacity is assessed annually, and seats are typically limited at the elementary level where class sizes are already managed closely. Families residing outside CISD boundaries who wish to enroll should contact the CISD Enrollment Office early in the spring for the following school year.

New Tech @ Coppell Magnet

New Tech @ Coppell accepts CISD-enrolled students by application for the incoming freshman class each year. There is no additional tuition — it is a district program, not a private program. Application typically opens in January or February for the following fall. Students who are not admitted participate in the standard Coppell High School academic program, which is itself rigorous and IB-accessible.

Dual Language Immersion (DLI) Enrollment

The Spanish/English DLI program at the elementary level has its own application window, which typically closes earlier than general enrollment. DLI enrollment for Kindergarten applicants is by application within CISD. Families interested in DLI should contact CISD enrollment in January or February of the year the child will enter Kindergarten — waiting until general enrollment opens in spring may mean DLI spots are already filled.

IB Program Access

Unlike some large DFW districts where IB is concentrated at a single specialty campus that requires a cross-town transfer, Coppell ISD's IB Diploma Programme is offered at the district's only high school — Coppell High School. All CISD students are zoned to CHS, so every family in the district has IB access without any additional application or transfer process. IB participation at the course level is open to any student who meets prerequisite requirements; the full Diploma Programme involves a formal track selection, typically in 9th or 10th grade.

Charter School Context

Charter schools in or near the Coppell/Irving area — including select Harmony campuses — are state-funded alternatives that accept students regardless of home address and have their own application processes. These are separate from CISD and do not affect CISD enrollment eligibility.

District Finances

Tax Rate — Among the Lowest for an A-Rated DFW District

Coppell ISD's total tax rate is $1.00 per $100 of assessed value — one of the most favorable rates for an A-rated district in the entire Dallas–Fort Worth metro. To put that in context: neighboring Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD runs at $1.11 and Lewisville ISD at $1.04, while districts with comparable academic profiles in Collin County often exceed $1.10. Coppell's $1.00 rate reflects the strength of its commercial and industrial tax base, particularly from DFW Airport-area properties, logistics facilities, and major distribution centers that share the tax burden with residential owners.

For a home assessed at $600,000 in Coppell, the annual school district levy (before exemptions) is $6,000. With Texas's homestead exemption ($100,000 reduction in assessed value for school taxes), the effective taxable base drops to $500,000 — reducing the annual school levy to approximately $5,000. County and city taxes are assessed separately and vary by the specific appraisal district (Dallas or Denton County — see note below).

Appraisal District Note

Most Coppell addresses are under Dallas County Appraisal District. However, some portions of the district's service area — particularly near the northern edges of Coppell — may fall under Denton County Appraisal District. Confirm your specific appraisal district assignment before closing; it affects where you file your homestead exemption and how property values are assessed.

Per-Pupil Expenditure

Coppell ISD's per-pupil expenditure of $15,984 is among the highest for any DFW district of its size. This figure reflects the district's strong local tax base combined with its relatively small enrollment — producing more dollars per student than larger districts with comparable or higher tax rates. The result is exceptional program investment: fully funded IB and New Tech pathways, robust fine arts facilities, and support staff ratios that are favorable compared to districts with two to five times as many students.

Financial Rating

Coppell ISD holds a Texas FIRST Superior financial integrity rating — the highest designation awarded by TEA's Financial Integrity Rating System of Texas. This rating reflects sound financial management, balanced budgets, and appropriate reserve levels. For families making a long-term real estate investment, a district's financial health is a meaningful indicator of program sustainability.

Enrollment — New Families

How to Enroll

New student enrollment for Coppell ISD is completed through the district's online enrollment portal at coppellisd.com. Standard required documents include:

  • Proof of residency — utility bill or executed lease/purchase agreement showing the Coppell ISD service address
  • Birth certificate — required for first-time Texas school enrollees
  • Immunization records — Texas-required vaccines per DSHS schedule (Tdap, meningococcal, varicella, etc.)
  • Previous academic records — transcripts, report cards, IEP if applicable, standardized test scores
  • Proof of custody — if applicable, legal documentation of custody arrangement

Mid-Year Transfers

CISD accepts mid-year enrollments year-round. Families under contract on a home but not yet moved in should contact the Enrollment Office to understand timing options. It is advisable to initiate contact with CISD within the first week of establishing Texas residency to avoid delays in campus placement and program access.

DLI Program — Earlier Deadlines Apply

Families interested in the Spanish/English Dual Language Immersion program must apply separately and earlier than general enrollment. The DLI program begins in Kindergarten and is not available for mid-year entry at most grade levels. Contact the CISD Enrollment Office in January or early February to confirm current DLI deadlines for the upcoming school year — missing the window typically means waiting until the following fall.

GT Identification for Transferring Students

Students previously identified as gifted in another state or district should bring all available assessment records and GT identification documentation to the enrollment appointment. CISD's GT department will review prior records as part of its multi-criteria identification process. Initial GT determination typically takes 4–6 weeks from the date of enrollment.

IEP Transfers from Out of State

Students with an existing IEP from another state should alert the CISD Special Education department at the time of enrollment. Texas law requires Coppell ISD to either adopt the existing IEP or convene an ARD meeting within 30 days of enrollment. Having all prior IEP, evaluation, and eligibility records available at enrollment accelerates the process and reduces gaps in service delivery.

Frequently Asked Questions

My address says "Irving, TX" — can I still be in Coppell ISD? ▾
Yes — this is one of the most important things to understand about Coppell ISD. The district boundary extends into the city of Irving, specifically the Valley Ranch neighborhood and some adjacent areas in Las Colinas. If you are searching for homes in Valley Ranch and prioritizing Coppell ISD, your Irving address may very well be within CISD. The inverse is also true: not all Irving addresses are in Coppell ISD. Always verify your specific address using the TEA School District Locator before making an offer — do not rely on city-of-address or ZIP code.
How do I access the IB program — is there a separate application? ▾
No separate application is required to attend Coppell High School — every CISD student is automatically zoned there. Access to IB coursework at CHS is open to all students who meet subject prerequisites; the full IB Diploma track involves a counselor-guided pathway selection typically in 9th or 10th grade. The New Tech @ Coppell magnet (also within CHS) does require a separate application for incoming freshmen. If your priority is IB access, Coppell ISD's model is one of the most straightforward in DFW: live in the district, attend CHS, and pursue IB.
How does the Dual Language Immersion (DLI) program work, and when do I need to apply? ▾
Coppell ISD's Spanish/English DLI program begins in Kindergarten and runs through elementary school. It is a two-way immersion model where instruction is delivered in both English and Spanish — students develop academic proficiency in both languages simultaneously. The program is available by application within the district; enrollment does not happen automatically at general registration. DLI applications for the following school year typically open in January or February and close before general enrollment — contact the CISD Enrollment Office early in the calendar year for current deadlines. Mid-year DLI entry is not available at most grade levels.
How does Coppell ISD's $1.00 tax rate compare to other top DFW districts? ▾
Coppell ISD's $1.00 per $100 rate is among the lowest for any A-rated district in DFW. For comparison: Frisco ISD sits at $1.03, Lewisville ISD at $1.04, Allen ISD at $1.13, Prosper ISD at $1.26. Coppell's lower rate is sustained by the commercial and industrial tax base near DFW Airport — office parks, logistics facilities, and distribution centers contribute significantly to total assessed value, reducing the burden on residential properties. For a family buying a $700,000 home, the difference between Coppell's rate and a district at $1.25 is roughly $1,750 per year in school taxes — a meaningful long-term difference.
What does the $15,984 per-pupil expenditure actually mean for my child? ▾
Per-pupil expenditure is the total district spending divided by enrollment. Coppell's $15,984 figure is high because the district has a strong commercial tax base and relatively small enrollment — more dollars chase fewer students. In practice, this means the district can fully fund the IB programme, the New Tech magnet, a robust fine arts program, dual language immersion, and competitive special education staffing without the budget pressures that constrain larger districts. It does not mean individual student classroom budgets are $16,000 — the figure includes all district operations, facilities, administration, and transportation. But it is a meaningful indicator of programmatic depth and financial health.
Can families from outside Coppell ISD enroll their children in the district? ▾
Coppell ISD does accept out-of-district transfer students on a space-available basis — but availability is limited and not guaranteed. The district is not a large open-enrollment destination; seats fill based on address-zoned students first. If you are specifically targeting Coppell ISD for its programs (IB, New Tech, DLI) and do not live within the district, the most reliable path is purchasing or leasing a home within the CISD boundary. Contact the CISD Enrollment Office for current transfer availability before planning a commute-based enrollment strategy.
