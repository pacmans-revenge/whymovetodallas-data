DeSoto ISD is a midsize suburban district serving approximately 9,500 students in southern Dallas County, primarily in the cities of DeSoto and the Dallas County portion of Glenn Heights, with smaller sections of Ovilla and Cedar Hill. The district is built around a single comprehensive high school — DeSoto High School — with multiple magnet program pathways housed on one campus, plus 11 feeder campuses. That single-HS structure is the first thing families should understand: it shapes everything from program access to athletic culture to how your child's entire K–12 peer group is assembled.

Families who take the time to look past the TEA C rating often find a district with genuinely exceptional strengths. DeSoto's athletics program has won more than 20 state championships, including three football titles in four years (2022, 2023, and 2025). The fine arts department has been recognized as a Best Community for Music Education for six consecutive years, led by a two-time Grammy-nominated choir director. The Career and Technical Education department offers more than 35 certification pathways, and the Early College High School program allows students to earn an associate's degree at no cost while completing their diploma. The honest tradeoff is that the district's overall academic accountability rating — a TEA C score of 73 — reflects below-average STAAR proficiency rates, and families prioritizing test-score benchmarks above all else may find a better fit in neighboring districts to the north or west.

Southern Dallas County offers substantially more affordable housing than the north DFW suburbs this site typically features. Families relocating from high-cost metros often find that DeSoto and Glenn Heights deliver more home for the dollar, with shorter commute access to downtown Dallas job corridors. The tradeoff is a district that performs at the state's C-level on academic measures — meaningful context for a family's decision, not a dismissal of the district's real strengths.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Have a student-athlete who wants to compete at a nationally recognized program (football, basketball, track, and more)
  • Value exceptional fine arts — band, choir, dance, theater — with a high-performance culture
  • Want access to an Early College pathway or 35+ CTE certification programs at no extra cost
  • Are stretching a budget and need more home for the money in southern Dallas County
  • Are relocating to serve communities south of downtown Dallas and want their child at a school with strong extracurricular identity

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • Your primary priority is high STAAR proficiency rates or a district with a TEA A or B rating
  • You are in Glenn Heights and have not yet verified which county your address falls in — Ellis County residents attend Red Oak ISD, not DeSoto ISD
  • You prefer a district with multiple high school campuses, where a student can find a smaller-school feel within a larger system
  • You are looking at new construction in nearby areas — boundaries in this part of Dallas County are less straightforward than in the northern suburbs
⚠ Boundary Verification — Read Before Making an Offer

Not every DeSoto or Glenn Heights address falls within DeSoto ISD. Glenn Heights is split between two counties: the Dallas County portion is served by DeSoto ISD, while the Ellis County portion is served by Red Oak ISD. A home one street over can mean a completely different district. Always verify your specific address using DeSoto ISD's attendance zone maps at desotoisd.org/attendance_zones or the TEA school locator before making an offer.

TEA Accountability Rating

DeSoto ISD earned an overall TEA score of 73 (C) on its 2024 A–F Accountability Rating. Texas rates districts and campuses across three domains, each measuring a different dimension of student success. A score of 73 places DeSoto ISD below the state average — an important data point, but one that requires context to interpret correctly for a relocating family.

The Three Accountability Domains

Student Achievement — This domain measures how well students perform on STAAR exams and other assessments in a single year. It rewards high mastery rates across the student population. Districts with high proportions of students performing at grade level score well here.

School Progress — This domain looks at two things: how much growth individual students make year over year (regardless of where they start), and how a district compares to other districts with similar economic demographics. A district can earn a strong Progress score even if its raw achievement numbers are below average, if students are growing at an above-peer rate.

Closing the Gaps — This domain examines whether outcomes are improving for historically underserved student groups. It rewards districts that are actively narrowing performance gaps rather than simply maintaining averages.

What a C (73) means for families: A district-level TEA C rating reflects an average across many campuses — some campuses within DeSoto ISD may perform meaningfully above or below the district score. When evaluating any district with a C rating, families should look at individual campus scores rather than treating the district score as the definitive measure of every school in the system. A C rating also tells you nothing about a student's experience in athletics, fine arts, CTE, or the Early College program — those programs are genuinely exceptional, and the TEA score does not reflect them.

TEA Data Note

The 2024 accountability ratings are the most recently released as of this writing. Visit txschools.gov for current domain scores and campus-level breakdowns for DeSoto ISD campuses.

Academic Performance

DeSoto ISD's graduation rate is approximately 88%, slightly below the Texas state average. The district's overall STAAR proficiency rates in reading and math are below the state average — a pattern reflected in its TEA C rating. For families making a relocation decision primarily on academic test-score benchmarks, that context matters and should be weighed honestly.

The more complete academic picture includes programs that go well beyond STAAR performance. The Early College High School program produces graduates who enter college with an associate's degree already in hand at no cost to their family — a concrete economic and academic head start. The College, Career, and Military Readiness (CCMR) metric, which TEA uses to measure students meeting defined post-secondary readiness indicators, reflects the district's emphasis on CTE certifications and dual-credit pathways as parallel measures of college and career preparation. The Class of 2022 earned more than 480 career certifications, and graduates collectively received more than $4M in college scholarships and more than 400 college acceptances.

What this means for families: If your student is on a traditional college-prep STAAR trajectory, review individual campus performance at txschools.gov — campus scores vary. If your student is drawn to athletics, performing arts, a technical career certification, or the Early College pathway, DeSoto ISD's programs in those areas are among the strongest in southern DFW and compete with districts that carry higher academic ratings.

Key Academic Data Points

  • Graduation rate: approximately 88% (state average approximately 90–91%)
  • Early College High School: students can earn a full associate's degree at no cost while completing their diploma
  • CTE certifications: 35+ programs; Class of 2022 earned 480+ career certifications
  • Dual credit: available through district partnerships; expands college credit options beyond Early College program
  • STAAR proficiency: below state average in reading and math; visit txschools.gov for the most current campus-specific data

Campuses

DeSoto ISD operates 12 campuses: 7 elementary schools, 3 middle schools, 1 freshman campus (DeSoto Ninth Grade Center), and 1 comprehensive high school. Every student in the district — regardless of which elementary or middle they attend — feeds to DeSoto High School. The single-HS structure means athletics, fine arts, and magnet programs are concentrated at one building, which can create an extraordinarily tight school community and a very high-energy extracurricular culture.

DeSoto High School & iSTEAM3D

High School (Grades 10–12) ~3,000 students Multiple Magnet Pathways

DeSoto's comprehensive high school houses multiple magnet pathways under one roof — iSTEAM3D (design, technology, arts), Early College (associate's degree), and the International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme. It is also the home of one of Texas's premier athletics programs, with 20+ state championships across multiple sports and a nationally recognized fine arts department.

DeSoto Ninth Grade Center

Freshman Campus (Grade 9) Dedicated Transition Campus

DeSoto operates a dedicated freshman campus that houses all 9th graders separately before they join the main high school in 10th grade — a model that research suggests eases the transition from middle school and can reduce early dropout risk. Families should understand this means their 9th grader will not be on the main high school campus initially.

McCowan Middle School

Middle School iSTEAM3D Magnet

One of three DeSoto ISD middle schools, McCowan offers the iSTEAM3D magnet program — a design and technology-focused curriculum using project-based learning. All three middle schools in the district carry the iSTEAM3D magnet designation, making the program accessible to students across attendance zones rather than concentrating it at a single campus.

Cockrell Hill & Curtistene S. McCowan Elementary

Elementary Schools

DeSoto ISD's 7 elementary campuses serve students in Grades PK–5 across DeSoto, Glenn Heights, and the district's boundary areas. Campus-level performance varies; families should review individual campus TEA and GreatSchools data at GreatSchools.org for the campuses that would serve their specific address.

Specialty Programs

DeSoto ISD's program lineup is one of its clearest differentiators from neighboring districts. Understanding how each program is structured — and what access actually looks like — matters enormously for families targeting a specific pathway.

iSTEAM3D Magnet Academy

Districtwide — all three middle schools and the high school. iSTEAM3D (Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, Math, and Design) uses project-based learning centered on creativity, innovation, and design thinking. Because the program is offered at all middle school campuses, access is based on attendance zone rather than a competitive application. At the high school level, the iSTEAM3D pathway continues as a specialized curriculum strand. Families do not need to strategically place themselves near one specific campus to access this program.

Early College High School

Campus-based at DeSoto High School. Students in this pathway earn both a high school diploma and a full associate's degree at no cost to their family, through a partnership with a community college. The Class of 2022 collectively received more than $4M in college scholarships. Enrollment in this pathway typically involves an application process and academic preparation requirements — contact the high school for current 2025–26 enrollment procedures. This is a significant financial and academic advantage for families whose students are college-bound and can meet program expectations.

International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma Programme

Campus-based at DeSoto High School. The IB Diploma Programme is an internationally recognized college-prep curriculum for Grades 11–12. Access typically requires maintaining strong academic standing and meeting IB-specific prerequisite courses in 9th and 10th grade. Families specifically targeting the IB pathway should contact the high school's IB coordinator to understand the preparatory course sequence starting in Grade 9 and what academic performance is expected. Transportation is student/family responsibility to the high school campus.

Athletics

Districtwide — all campuses feed to the DeSoto Eagles. DeSoto's athletics program is one of the most decorated in Texas high school sports. The Eagles have won more than 20 state championships across multiple sports, including four football state titles (2016, 2022, 2023, 2025) and multiple girls basketball state titles. The Class of 2022 included more than 40 scholar-athletes who earned over $3.2M in college athletic scholarships. For families with a student who plays at a high level and wants to be recruited, DeSoto's coaching staff and program visibility are genuine assets.

Fine Arts — Band, Choir, Dance, Theater, Visual Arts

Districtwide, with flagship recognition at DeSoto High School. DeSoto ISD has been recognized as a Best Community for Music Education for six consecutive years. The district's choir program is led by a two-time Grammy-nominated director and has traveled nationally and internationally. Band, dance, visual arts, and theater are all active programs. For performing arts families, DeSoto's fine arts culture is distinctive — not incidental.

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

Districtwide — 35+ programs at DeSoto High School. DeSoto ISD's CTE department offers more than 35 career certification pathways — from healthcare to technology to skilled trades — allowing students to earn industry-recognized certifications before graduation. The Class of 2022 earned more than 480 career certifications. These credentials carry real labor market value and are a practical alternative or complement to a traditional four-year college path. Dual credit courses are also available to expand college credit options.

Enrollment Guide

If you're relocating to DeSoto ISD territory, here's how enrollment typically works. All time-sensitive details below are labeled with the applicable school year — verify current-year details at desotoisd.org/student_enrollment.

Step-by-Step Enrollment

  1. Verify your address falls within DeSoto ISD — use the attendance zone maps at desotoisd.org/attendance_zones before any other step. Glenn Heights residents in Ellis County are NOT in DeSoto ISD.
  2. Gather residency documents — typically: current lease or mortgage statement, two utility bills, and government-issued ID. Exact requirements are on the enrollment page.
  3. Complete online registration — DeSoto ISD uses an online registration portal. New student registration and re-enrollment are managed through the district website.
  4. Enrollment window — Re-enrollment for returning students typically opens in spring for the following school year. New student enrollment is accepted year-round for in-boundary families. Contact the district for 2025–26 window dates.
  5. Intra-district transfers — Families within DeSoto ISD boundaries may request a transfer to a different campus within the district. Transfer approval depends on available capacity and is not guaranteed. Contact the district's Student Services office for the current 2025–26 transfer application window.
  6. Inter-district transfers — DeSoto ISD families wishing to transfer to a neighboring district must apply through the receiving district. Families from outside DeSoto ISD wishing to attend may apply for an inter-district transfer subject to available capacity and board approval.
  7. IEP/504 transfers — Texas law requires that an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting be held within 30 days of a student with an IEP transferring to a new Texas district. Bring all prior IEP, evaluation, and 504 documentation to enrollment. Contact DeSoto ISD's Special Education department directly before finalizing your home purchase to understand current service capacity for your child's specific needs.

Magnet Program Enrollment

The iSTEAM3D program at middle school is attendance-zone based — no application required. The Early College and IB pathways at the high school level typically have academic requirements and application timelines. Contact DeSoto High School's counseling office at desotohs.desotoisd.org to understand current program entry requirements before the 2025–26 school year.

Boundary Verification

DeSoto ISD's boundary situation is more nuanced than most north DFW districts, and it is where families most commonly make preventable mistakes. Understanding the geography before you make an offer is essential.

Cities and Areas Served

  • DeSoto, TX — The district's primary city. Most addresses within the city limits of DeSoto fall within DeSoto ISD, but boundary maps should still be confirmed for properties near the city edge.
  • Glenn Heights, TX (Dallas County portion only) — This is the most important boundary nuance in the district. Glenn Heights straddles the Dallas/Ellis county line. Homes in the Dallas County portion of Glenn Heights attend DeSoto ISD. Homes in the Ellis County portion of Glenn Heights attend Red Oak ISD — a completely separate district. A home's address will look like "Glenn Heights" in both cases. Only the county determines which district you are in.
  • Ovilla, TX (portions) — Small portions of Ovilla fall within DeSoto ISD boundaries. Not all Ovilla addresses are in DeSoto ISD.
  • Cedar Hill, TX (small portions) — A minor area of Cedar Hill is within DeSoto ISD boundaries. The majority of Cedar Hill is served by Cedar Hill ISD.

New Construction Edge Cases

Southern Dallas County has seen some new development activity in recent years. Any new construction in Glenn Heights, Ovilla, or near the Cedar Hill border should be treated as boundary-uncertain until you have confirmed the specific lot address in the district's boundary tool. Builder sales representatives do not always have current or accurate ISD information — verify independently.

How to Verify Your Specific Address

  • DeSoto ISD Attendance Zones: desotoisd.org/attendance_zones
  • TEA School Locator: tea.texas.gov — school district locator
  • Call the district: DeSoto ISD Student Services can confirm your specific address — (972) 223-6666
Home Search Implication

Making an offer on a Glenn Heights home without verifying the county can mean your child ends up in Red Oak ISD instead of DeSoto ISD — or vice versa if that is your preference. Boundary maps do not always match common-sense geography. Verify the specific address with the district before your offer is finalized. This is especially true for any new construction or recently annexed properties.

School Finance & Tax Rate

DeSoto ISD's 2024 certified total tax rate is approximately $1.17 per $100 of assessed value. For a home assessed at $350,000, that equals approximately $4,095/year in school district property taxes, or roughly $341/month added to a mortgage payment. After the Texas $100,000 homestead exemption, the taxable value drops to $250,000, making the annual school tax approximately $2,925.

Comparison context: A tax rate of $1.17 is in the mid-range for southern Dallas County suburban districts. It is slightly above the DFW-wide average, which has trended lower in recent years as Robin Hood recapture requirements and the Texas school finance formula have evolved. Always check the most current certified rate at the Texas Comptroller's website before closing — rates change annually.

Texas law provides a $100,000 homestead exemption off the appraised value for school district taxes on a primary residence. Homeowners over 65 receive an additional exemption and a freeze on the school district portion of their tax bill. Veterans with a 100% disability rating may be fully exempt from school district property taxes — an important consideration for veteran families.

Per-Pupil Expenditure

For current per-pupil expenditure data, visit txschools.gov and search for DeSoto ISD. Per-pupil spending figures are updated annually by TEA and reflect total district spending divided by average daily attendance.

Recent Bond Activity

Check the DeSoto ISD newsroom at desotoisd.org/newsroom for any recent bond election activity or pending rate changes. Bond elections can affect the I&S (Interest & Sinking) portion of the tax rate in future years.

Families focused on academic accountability ratings will find that all four southern Dallas County districts cluster near a TEA C, reflecting similar demographics and regional economic context. If academic ratings are the primary driver, families typically look further north — to McKinney ISD, Frisco ISD, Allen ISD, or Plano ISD — accepting that housing costs rise significantly in those territories.

Community Feel

DeSoto ISD families often describe the district's culture through two lenses: sports and family pride. Friday night football games at DeSoto are a genuine community event — the Eagles' program is well-known statewide, and the school draws alumni and community members who are deeply invested in its success. For families where sports are central to their child's identity and aspirations, that culture is genuinely motivating. Parents of student-athletes consistently note the coaching quality and the visibility the program offers for college recruitment.

The fine arts community is similarly tight-knit. The district's choir, band, and performing arts programs attract students who are serious about their craft, and the Grammy-nominated choir director brings a level of national recognition that is unusual for a district of this size. Parents in the fine arts community describe a culture of high expectations, travel opportunities, and deep investment from the department's leadership. Performing arts families who have researched the district carefully often say the program punches well above its weight relative to the district's overall rating.

The practical community context for relocating families includes understanding that DeSoto and Glenn Heights are affordable relative to north DFW suburbs, which means families stretching a budget can often access more home and more land than they could in Frisco or Flower Mound. The commute to downtown Dallas and Uptown is shorter from southern Dallas County than from many of the northern suburbs — a real quality-of-life consideration for families with urban employment. The community is diverse and has strong civic institutions. Families who have lived in northern DFW suburbs and are used to brand-new infrastructure throughout a school campus should note that DeSoto ISD's facilities vary by campus age; some campuses are newer while others reflect an older build vintage. Visiting individual campuses before enrollment is always worthwhile.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my Glenn Heights address guarantee enrollment in DeSoto ISD? ▾
No — and this is the most important boundary question in DeSoto ISD territory. Glenn Heights straddles the Dallas/Ellis county line. If your Glenn Heights address is in Dallas County, you are in DeSoto ISD. If your address is in Ellis County, you are in Red Oak ISD — a completely different district. The city name "Glenn Heights" appears on both sides of the county line. Do not assume. Verify your specific address at desotoisd.org/attendance_zones or call DeSoto ISD Student Services at (972) 223-6666 before making any offer on a home.
What are the different program pathways at DeSoto High School? ▾
DeSoto High School operates as one campus with three distinct program pathways: the iSTEAM3D magnet (design, technology, arts, and innovation — open to all enrolled students), the Early College High School (students earn an associate's degree at no cost while completing their diploma), and the International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma Programme (internationally recognized college-prep curriculum for Grades 11–12). All three pathways share the same campus building and extracurricular access, including athletics and fine arts. Entry into the Early College and IB pathways typically requires meeting academic prerequisites and completing an application; contact the high school's counseling office for current requirements. Note that 9th graders start at the separate DeSoto Ninth Grade Center before joining the main high school campus in 10th grade.
How does DeSoto ISD handle special education transfers from out of state? ▾
Texas law requires DeSoto ISD to hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of a student with an active IEP enrolling in the district. At that meeting, the team reviews the prior IEP and determines how to implement or adapt it within the Texas education system. Bring all existing IEP documents, recent evaluations, eligibility documentation, and any 504 plans to enrollment. For students with significant or complex needs, it is advisable to contact DeSoto ISD's Special Education department directly before finalizing your home purchase — call the district at (972) 223-6666 and ask to be connected with Special Education Services. Parent experience with special education varies by campus and by student need, and a direct conversation before you commit to a specific address is always the most reliable path.
Why does DeSoto ISD have a TEA C rating if the athletics and fine arts programs are so strong? ▾
The TEA A–F system measures STAAR test proficiency and related academic metrics. It does not measure athletic achievement, fine arts recognition, CTE certification rates, or the value of the Early College program. A district can have nationally recognized athletics and a Grammy-nominated music program and still earn a TEA C if STAAR proficiency rates are below the state average across its student population. Both things are true simultaneously. For families making a relocation decision, it is important to understand what the TEA score measures and what it does not measure, and to evaluate the district based on the criteria that matter most for their specific child. Niche.com's third-party grade (also a C) reflects similar academic performance signals. For campus-level data within DeSoto ISD — some campuses perform above the district average — visit txschools.gov and GreatSchools.org.
What is DeSoto ISD's transfer policy? ▾
For intra-district transfers (within DeSoto ISD, from one campus to another), families must apply through the district's Student Services office. Transfers are approved based on available capacity and are not guaranteed. For inter-district transfers from outside DeSoto ISD, families apply through the district and approval is subject to available capacity and board policy. For families currently in DeSoto ISD who wish to transfer a child to a neighboring district, that application goes to the receiving district. Contact DeSoto ISD's Student Services at (972) 223-6666 for current 2025–26 transfer procedures and deadlines, as policies and timelines change annually.
How does DeSoto ISD's tax rate compare to neighboring districts? ▾
DeSoto ISD's 2024 certified total tax rate is approximately $1.17 per $100 of assessed value — mid-range for southern Dallas County. For comparison, Cedar Hill ISD is approximately $1.25, Duncanville ISD approximately $1.20, and Lancaster ISD approximately $1.15. On a $350,000 home (after the $100,000 homestead exemption, taxable value is $250,000), DeSoto ISD's school district tax is approximately $2,925/year. Always verify the current certified rate at the Texas Comptroller's website before closing, as rates are adjusted annually. Note that total property tax includes city, county, and other taxing unit rates in addition to the school district rate.
What is the iSTEAM3D program and how do students access it? ▾
iSTEAM3D (Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, Math, and 3-Dimensional Design) is DeSoto ISD's signature project-based learning magnet approach, focused on creativity, innovation, and design thinking. A key distinction: iSTEAM3D is offered at all three DeSoto ISD middle schools — not just one selective campus. This means access is determined by your attendance zone rather than a competitive application process at the middle school level. Students in any DeSoto ISD middle school participate in the iSTEAM3D curriculum. The program continues as a pathway strand at DeSoto High School. This districtwide availability is a notable difference from magnet programs in other districts that concentrate specialized curriculum at a single campus, which can force families to either live near that campus or apply for a transfer.
