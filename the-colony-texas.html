Location & Commute

Where Is The Colony? Commutes & Getting Around

The Colony sits about 28 miles north of Downtown Dallas and roughly 45 miles northeast of Downtown Fort Worth, in Denton County. The city is bordered by Frisco to the north (literally adjacent — crossing Sam Rayburn Tollway puts you in Frisco), Lewisville to the south, and Lake Lewisville to the west. The Dallas North Tollway (DNT) and Sam Rayburn Tollway (SH-121) are the two primary commute corridors, giving residents fast access to both the Plano/Legacy West job hub and the Frisco employment cluster.

The Colony's greatest location advantage is its proximity to Frisco. Companies clustered around The Star and the DNT corridor — T-Mobile HQ, Dallas Cowboys HQ, Toyota regional offices, and Baylor Scott & White at The Star — are 12–20 minutes north. Families who want Frisco's employment access without Frisco's home prices have found The Colony to be the practical answer.

Toll roads are the reality here. The Colony's fastest commute routes — DNT south toward Dallas and Plano, and Sam Rayburn Tollway east toward Frisco — are toll roads. Budget $150–$300/month depending on usage if you're commuting daily on these corridors. Non-toll alternatives exist via I-35E and SH-195 but add 10–20 minutes each way. No rail service currently serves The Colony — a personal vehicle is essential for all daily errands and commutes.
Education

Lewisville ISD — Schools in The Colony

The Colony is served by Lewisville ISD — one of the largest and most highly rated districts in DFW, earning an A from Niche overall and an A+ for public schools. The key detail families must understand: The Colony addresses feed The Colony High School — not Marcus HS or Flower Mound HS, which are the district's highest-rated campuses. LISD as a whole is excellent; your campus is determined by your specific home address within the district.

Family Life

What Life Actually Looks Like in The Colony

Nebraska Furniture Mart / Grandscape

One of the largest retail and entertainment complexes in Texas — Nebraska Furniture Mart alone is over 560,000 square feet of home furnishings. Grandscape adds restaurants, entertainment venues, hotels, and outdoor plaza space. Having this as a neighborhood amenity is genuinely unusual in DFW. Families use it for dining, weekend activities, and browsing without driving 30+ minutes.

Lake Lewisville & Stewart Creek

Stewart Creek Park borders the western edge of The Colony with direct lake access to Lake Lewisville — boating, kayaking, fishing, and lakeside trails. The lake is one of DFW's largest recreational reservoirs, and Colony residents have some of the most direct access to it. Families report this is a genuine weekend lifestyle draw, not just a scenic backdrop.

Frisco Spillover — Without Frisco Prices

The Star (Cowboys HQ, T-Mobile, restaurants), Frisco Square, Toyota Stadium (FC Dallas), and the entire Frisco entertainment corridor are 12–20 minutes north. Colony residents access everything Frisco offers while paying $150K–$200K less for their home. This is the core value proposition that attracts many buyers from other states.

LISD School Community

The Colony HS, Lakeview MS, and the elementary campuses have active school communities — PTA involvement, booster clubs, and neighborhood connections built around school athletics. The city's 30% households-with-children stat is lower than Frisco or Flower Mound, but the school-oriented community culture is still genuine and welcoming for relocating families.

Affordable Frisco-Adjacent Lifestyle

The Colony's median home value is roughly $190K below Frisco's. For families prioritizing school quality, employment access, and suburban amenities over the Frisco address itself, The Colony represents a meaningful financial advantage — lower mortgage, lower property taxes, and access to the same corridor employers on the same roads.

Young, Growing Community

The Colony attracts young professional families and remote workers drawn to the Frisco-adjacent lifestyle at a lower price point. The city has an active young family demographic — median age is 37, and the 22% work-from-home rate suggests a significant remote worker population that values the lifestyle location over a specific employer proximity.

The honest tradeoff: The Colony High School campus ratings are noticeably lower than Marcus HS or Flower Mound HS within LISD — if your high school campus rating is the top priority, verify addresses before purchasing. The city's older housing stock in some neighborhoods may require more renovation budget than newer Frisco builds. Toll road costs are a real monthly expense for daily commuters. In-city dining and retail outside Grandscape is thinner than Frisco proper — most upscale dining requires driving north or south.
Getting Outside

Recreation, Walkability & City Amenities

The Colony is car-dependent for daily errands — no DART rail, no meaningful public transit, and most commercial areas require driving. A personal vehicle is essential. What the city compensates with is significant: Stewart Creek Park and Lake Lewisville shoreline access on the western edge, The Colony Recreation Center as a full-service facility, and Grandscape as an outdoor plaza / entertainment destination that most DFW suburbs can't match.

Parks, Trails & Green Space

The Colony maintains approximately 45 parks and green spaces, with trail corridors connecting neighborhoods to the lakefront. Stewart Creek Park is the crown jewel — direct access to Lake Lewisville shoreline for fishing, kayaking, and lakeside walking. The DNT trail corridor also provides a paved bike/run route with a Frisco connection to the north.

Stewart Creek Park — Lake Access

The Colony's most distinctive outdoor asset — a waterfront park on Lake Lewisville with fishing piers, boat launch access, picnic areas, and walking trails along the shoreline. For families who prioritize lake access in their lifestyle, this puts The Colony in a different category from inland DFW suburbs.

Colony Park Trail System

Connected neighborhood trails run through Colony Park and link to the broader city trail system. Good for morning runs, family bike rides, and walking without driving to a trailhead. The DNT greenway extending north toward Frisco is accessible to cyclists comfortable riding alongside a major toll road corridor.

Lake Lewisville — Recreational Access

One of DFW's largest freshwater lakes — 29,000 acres of recreational water with multiple marinas, camping, fishing, and boating within reach of The Colony. Families with boats, kayaks, or paddleboards use the lake heavily in spring and summer. Multiple access points around the lake allow for variety in activities.

Grandscape Outdoor Plaza

The outdoor plaza and event spaces at Grandscape function as a genuine community gathering area — outdoor concerts, seasonal events, and open green space within the complex. It's not a traditional park, but for families looking for walkable entertainment and outdoor space in a mixed-use environment, it delivers a lifestyle experience most DFW suburbs lack.

Community Neighborhood Parks

Scattered throughout Colony neighborhoods are community parks with playgrounds, athletic fields, and splash pads — the type of parks that become regulars for families with young children. Not destination parks, but practical daily-use green spaces that make a difference in day-to-day family life.

Frisco's Parks — 12 Minutes North

When Colony's own parks don't cover a specific need, Frisco's extensive park system (consistently ranked among the best in Texas) is 12–20 minutes north. Frisco has over 60 parks, a velodrome, hockey rinks, and an extensive trail system — Colony residents access all of it without paying Frisco home prices.

Safety & Crime

Is The Colony Safe?

The Colony earns a Niche Safety grade of B+ — safe by DFW standards for a city of its size and character. The violent crime rate runs below the national average. Property crime is present, as in any mixed-use suburban city with significant commercial activity (the Grandscape/Nebraska Furniture Mart corridor generates some retail-associated property crime), but residential neighborhoods are considered safe by residents and law enforcement metrics alike.

Violent Crime Rate

~2.0 per 1,000 residents — below the national average of ~4.0/1,000. Violent crime in The Colony is consistent with comparable Denton County suburban communities.

Property Crime

~20 per 1,000 residents — slightly elevated compared to lower-activity suburban neighbors, partly driven by high-traffic commercial areas. Residential neighborhoods have lower incident rates than the commercial corridors.

The Colony PD

The Colony Police Department is active in community policing and maintains good response times across residential neighborhoods. Niche Safety Grade: B+. Source: Niche 2025, NeighborhoodScout, FBI UCR data.

New Construction

New Homes in The Colony — 1 Active Community

New construction in The Colony is limited — just 1 active community currently listed. This is a suburb where most buyers are focused on resale, which means the new builds that do exist often attract serious attention quickly. The upside: a new home here comes with a builder warranty, modern energy standards, and floor plans designed for how families actually live today — harder to find in an established resale market. One thing many families don't realize: you can bring your own Realtor to any new construction purchase at no cost to you — the builder pays the commission.
