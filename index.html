Your DFW Relocation Questions, Answered

It depends entirely on your priorities. Frisco and Allen lead on school rankings. Flower Mound offers the best balance of schools, community feel, and value. Southlake is unmatched for prestige but commands a premium price. Coppell is ideal if DFW airport access matters. The right suburb for your family depends on commute, budget, school priorities, and lifestyle — which is exactly what Kristen helps you figure out before you ever look at a house.
North DFW is home to some of the best public school districts in Texas. Frisco ISD, Carroll ISD (Southlake), Coppell ISD, and Lewisville ISD (Flower Mound) are all consistently rated among the state's top performers. Allen ISD and McKinney ISD are strong and improving year over year. The full comparison guide on this site covers every major ISD in detail with ratings, programs, and family feedback.
Significantly lower than most out-of-state markets. Texas has no state income tax. DFW median home prices are a fraction of California, New York, or Illinois equivalents for the same square footage and school quality. The tradeoff is property taxes, which are higher than many states — but most families relocating from high-income-tax states still come out well ahead financially.
Corporate transferees often work on 30–60 day timelines and it's very doable with the right specialist. Out-of-state families doing research remotely often take 3–6 months from first contact to close. Kristen works with both timelines and has a process designed to move as fast or as methodically as your situation requires.
Plano and Allen are closest to Toyota (Legacy West), JPMorgan Chase, and Capital One. Flower Mound and Coppell offer easy access to DFW Airport and American Airlines HQ in Fort Worth. Westlake and Southlake are closest to Charles Schwab. Frisco serves the expanding Legacy West corridor well. Kristen factors your exact commute destination into every suburb recommendation.
