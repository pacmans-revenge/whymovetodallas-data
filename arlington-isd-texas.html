Arlington ISD at a Glance

53,309 Students (2024–25)
82 Campuses
C / 76 TEA Grade 2025
B Niche Grade (3rd party)
~87% Graduation Rate
$1.09 Tax Rate per $100 (2025)

Arlington ISD is one of the largest school districts in Texas, serving the entire city of Arlington and the small city of Pantego — a combined population of roughly 400,000 people in central Tarrant County. With 53,309 students across 82 campuses and six high schools, it is a genuinely diverse, urban district with a wide range of campus quality and a surprisingly deep program menu. The honest starting point for any relocating family: not all Arlington ISD campuses are equal. The same district that houses an internationally recognized IB programme and a university-partnered STEM Academy also has a handful of campuses that struggle on state accountability measures. Where your specific address places your child matters enormously here.

That said, Arlington ISD has shown consistent improvement over the past three accountability cycles — from a score of 72 in 2022–23 to 74 in 2023–24 to 76 in 2025 — and the number of A-rated campuses nearly doubled in the most recent cycle while F-rated campuses dropped from 14 to 8. Families who take the time to understand the program pathways available and verify their specific campus assignments can access opportunities — free university credit, IB coursework starting in elementary school, four P-TECH career pathways — that many smaller suburban districts simply do not offer.

Arlington ISD is most often shortlisted by families relocating for work in the Mid-Cities corridor (AT&T, General Motors, American Airlines), by families seeking IB or STEM-specific programs, and by buyers looking for value relative to the more expensive North DFW suburbs. The tradeoffs are real — this is not the district for families who want a reliably uniform campus experience across the board — but for families who do their homework, it can be a compelling fit.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Work in central Tarrant County (AT&T, American Airlines, GM, UTA) and want to reduce commute
  • Want access to IB, STEM Academy, or P-TECH pathways and are willing to verify address-to-campus routing
  • Are prioritizing housing value — Arlington offers significantly more square footage per dollar than Frisco or Southlake
  • Have a student entering middle or high school who can target a specific program campus
  • Prefer urban district scale: more electives, more sports, more diverse peer networks

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • Consistency across all campuses is the top priority — quality varies significantly by school
  • You're looking for a district where any address in the city reliably feeds a high-performing elementary
  • Your commute destination is North DFW (Frisco, Plano, Allen) — the geography makes this district a longer haul
  • Your child has specialized needs that require intensive SpEd infrastructure — verify directly with Special Services before deciding
⚠ Boundary Verification

Not every home with an Arlington city address falls within Arlington ISD boundaries. Some western and southern portions of the city are served by Mansfield ISD. Additionally, Pantego — a separate municipality — is fully within Arlington ISD boundaries even though it is its own city. Always verify your specific address using the Arlington ISD school locator at aisd.net enrollment page before making an offer.

Within Arlington ISD, the six high schools serve distinct geographic zones — and many specialty programs (IB, STEM Academy, P-TECH) require a separate application. Knowing both your attendance-zone school and which programs your child is eligible for are two different research steps.

TEA Accountability Rating

Arlington ISD received a C (score: 76) from the Texas Education Agency in 2025 — its third consecutive year of improvement. This is the most recent official accountability rating as of the 2025 release cycle, which covers the 2024–25 school year. TEA A-F ratings are based on three domain scores, each measuring a different aspect of student performance.

The Three Domains

Student Achievement — measures how well students are performing at or above grade level on state assessments (STAAR), plus graduation rates and college/career/military readiness. This is a snapshot of where students are today.

School Progress — measures whether students are growing academically over time, and whether that growth is happening relative to schools with similar student populations. A school serving more students with additional challenges can still score well on Progress if those students are growing.

Closing the Gaps — measures whether the district is narrowing achievement gaps between different student groups. This domain is most sensitive to equity outcomes across economic, racial, and disability status lines.

For detailed domain scores, visit Arlington ISD's profile on txschools.gov.

What the C Rating Means in Practice

A district-level C from TEA tells you something about the average — but Arlington ISD's campus distribution is extremely wide. In the 2025 ratings, 7 campuses earned an A and 22 earned a B, meaning roughly 40% of campuses are performing at an above-average or excellent level. At the same time, 8 campuses received an F (all elementary). This spread is much wider than you'd find in a uniformly suburban district. The practical implication: your child's experience in Arlington ISD will be shaped almost entirely by their specific campus, not the district average. A family whose address zones to one of the A-rated or strong B-rated schools may have a very different experience than the district C suggests.

The consistent three-year improvement trend (72 → 74 → 76) is a meaningful signal. The district actively appealed its 2025 scores and corrected STAAR reporting errors that affected six campuses, each of which moved up a letter grade as a result.

Academic Performance

Academic performance in Arlington ISD is best understood at the campus level, not the district level. For 2024 STAAR results, the district falls roughly in line with a C-rated urban district of its size and student composition — reading proficiency rates are generally in the mid-60s percentile range, while math proficiency tends to run in the low-to-mid 40s. Full STAAR data by campus is available at txschools.gov.

Graduation Rate

Arlington ISD's four-year graduation rate is approximately 87% based on the most recent TEA-published cohort data. This is in line with the statewide average and reflects the district's improving trajectory over recent years.

AP, IB, and College Credit

AP programs are available at all six high schools. Seguin High School has reported an AP participation rate of approximately 57%, and Martin High School approximately 49%, per US News rankings data. The IB Diploma Programme at Bowie High School is the district's most rigorous college-preparatory curriculum and is available to students districtwide through an application process. The STEM Academy at Martin High School allows students to earn up to 60 hours of free university credit in partnership with the University of Texas at Arlington — beginning in ninth grade. P-TECH programs at four campuses also embed dual enrollment throughout the high school experience.

College, Career, and Military Readiness (CCMR)

Arlington ISD's CCMR rate — which measures the percentage of graduating seniors who are college-, career-, or military-ready — is a key TEA accountability metric. Current CCMR data is available on the district's TEA accountability reports at txschools.gov. The district's four P-TECH campuses, Early College High Schools, and CTE pathways are specifically designed to improve CCMR rates by embedding credentials and credit hours into the high school experience.

Practical Interpretation for Relocating Families

State test scores in a district this size describe an average across campuses that serve very different student populations. The family whose address zones to a strong B or A campus, or whose student enters a targeted program (IB, STEM Academy, P-TECH), is not living in the district average. Before dismissing Arlington ISD on headline scores, look up your specific address's assigned campus and compare that campus's individual accountability rating. The district's program access policies — particularly the STEM Academy, which is free and open to students from outside the district — are an unusual strength worth understanding before you decide.

High School Campuses

Arlington ISD operates six comprehensive high schools, each serving a distinct geographic zone of the city. Importantly, every high school also hosts at least one specialized program (IB, P-TECH, STEM, Fine Arts) — so your attendance-zone assignment and your program access are two separate questions worth investigating simultaneously.

Martin High School

High School ~1,900 students TX Rank: #342 (US News)

Home of the STEM Academy — a free, districtwide program for all AISD students (and outside applicants) in partnership with the University of Texas at Arlington. Students can earn up to 60 hours of college credit in engineering, biomedical science, computer science, or math/science pathways beginning in 9th grade. Martin consistently ranks as the highest-performing of the six high schools on state and national metrics.

Bowie High School

High School ~2,200 students IB Diploma + P-TECH

Hosts the IB Diploma Programme — the only campus in Arlington ISD offering the full IB curriculum at the high school level, open to students districtwide via application. Also hosts P-TECH in the healthcare pathway, giving students embedded college credit and work-based learning in medical fields. The IB programme at Bowie combines rigorous coursework with an internationally recognized credential.

Lamar High School

High School ~2,479 students P-TECH: Business/Cybersecurity

One of Arlington's larger high schools, Lamar hosts P-TECH pathways in business and cybersecurity, with embedded dual enrollment and work-based learning tied to industry partners in those sectors. Ranked #898 in Texas by US News for college readiness metrics.

Arlington High School

High School ~2,501 students Fine Arts & Dual Language

The largest of the six high schools and home of the Fine Arts and Dual Language Academy (FADLA), which provides pathways in visual and performing arts alongside Spanish language immersion. Arlington High serves the central and western portions of the district. Families with students strong in fine arts or languages who are zoned here can access specialized instruction without leaving their attendance-zone school.

Sam Houston High School

High School ~1,800 students P-TECH: Logistics/Mechatronics

Hosts P-TECH pathways in logistics and mechatronics — career-technical fields aligned with the manufacturing and distribution sector employers in the Arlington/Grand Prairie area. The school-within-a-school P-TECH model allows students to pursue the career pathway while still participating in traditional extracurriculars and athletics.

Seguin High School

High School ~2,000 students P-TECH: STEM/CADD

Hosts P-TECH in STEM and Computer Aided Drafting and Design (CADD), with one of the higher AP participation rates among the six campuses at approximately 57%. Ranked #492 in Texas by US News. Serves the eastern portion of the Arlington ISD footprint.

Notable Elementary Campus

Crow Leadership Academy

K–6 Magnet IB Primary Years Programme

One of only a small number of elementary campuses in DFW offering the International Baccalaureate Primary Years Programme (PYP), which emphasizes inquiry-based learning and international-mindedness from kindergarten. Students who begin the IB continuum here can transition into the Diploma Programme at Bowie High School, creating a K–12 IB pathway across Arlington ISD. Access requires an application and is open to students districtwide.

Specialty Programs

Arlington ISD operates 14 specialized programs across the district. Each has different access rules — some are open to all AISD students districtwide via application, some are attendance-zone based, and one (the STEM Academy) is open to students from entirely outside the district. Before committing to a home based on a specific program, confirm both the program's current enrollment process and whether your specific address routes to the relevant campus.

STEM Academy at Martin High School

Access: Open to ALL Arlington ISD students and students from outside the district — no attendance-zone requirement. Free of charge. Application required. Transportation: Parent-provided for out-of-zone students.

Students partner with the University of Texas at Arlington to earn up to 60 hours of college credit in engineering, biomedical science, computer science, or math/science. Pathways begin in 9th grade. This program is unusually accessible for a university-partnership STEM program — most comparable programs require district residency.

IB Diploma Programme — Bowie High School

Access: Open to all AISD students districtwide via application. Not attendance-zone restricted. Transportation: Parent-provided for out-of-zone students.

The full IB Diploma Programme includes Theory of Knowledge, Extended Essay, and six subject groups, culminating in a globally recognized credential. Students who complete the IB Primary Years Programme at Crow Leadership Academy have the benefit of continuity through the IB curriculum.

IB Primary Years Programme — Crow Leadership Academy

Access: Districtwide magnet, application required. Grades K–6. Transportation: Parent-provided for out-of-zone students.

One of the few IB PYP elementary programs in DFW. Creates a K–12 IB pathway when combined with enrollment at Bowie High School. Home-search implication: families specifically targeting the IB pathway should confirm their address and apply to Crow; living near the school does not guarantee enrollment — capacity is application-based.

P-TECH Programs (4 Campuses)

Access: Application required. Campus-specific pathways: Bowie (healthcare), Lamar (business/cybersecurity), Sam Houston (logistics/mechatronics), Seguin (STEM/CADD). Students attend their assigned high school and participate in P-TECH as a school-within-a-school.

P-TECH embeds work-based learning, internship opportunities, and dual enrollment beginning in 9th grade. Students graduate with both a high school diploma and college credit or an industry credential. The four-pathway structure means families can factor employer proximity (healthcare, manufacturing, tech) into their high school choice.

Fine Arts and Dual Language Academy (FADLA)

Access: Attendance-zone based at Arlington High School, with some open-enrollment capacity. Spanish dual language immersion component. Fine arts pathways include visual arts, music, theatre, and dance.

Elementary students districtwide receive Spanish and visual arts instruction through the "Arts for All" framework. FADLA at Arlington High School is the secondary continuation for students with strong fine arts interests who want a dedicated arts track alongside standard curriculum.

Career and Technical Education (CTE)

Access: Available at all six high schools — attendance-zone based, no separate application required for standard CTE courses. Scope: Districtwide.

CTE covers a broad range of industry pathways: health science, business, information technology, architecture/construction, arts/AV, and more. Students can earn industry certifications alongside their diploma. The four P-TECH programs are the most intensive CTE pathway but represent only one entry point — students at all campuses have access to foundational CTE courses.

Enrollment Process

The following reflects Arlington ISD's enrollment process as of the 2025–26 school year. Time-sensitive details such as enrollment windows and portal links should be confirmed directly with the district at aisd.net before you move.

Step 1 — Verify Your Address and School Assignment

Before enrolling, confirm that your specific address falls within Arlington ISD boundaries and identify your assigned campus at each level (elementary, middle, high school). Use the school locator at aisd.net.

Step 2 — Gather Required Documents

Arlington ISD requires proof of residency (utility bill, lease agreement, or mortgage statement), the child's birth certificate, immunization records, prior school records/report cards, and any IEP or 504 documentation from the previous district.

Step 3 — Complete Online Registration

New student registration is completed online through the Arlington ISD enrollment portal at aisd.net. The district typically opens enrollment windows in the spring for the upcoming school year. Mid-year enrollment for new residents is accepted on a rolling basis.

Step 4 — Apply for Specialized Programs (if applicable)

Programs including the STEM Academy, IB Diploma Programme, IB Primary Years Programme at Crow, and P-TECH each have separate application processes and their own timelines. These applications are typically submitted during specific windows — confirm current deadlines directly with the relevant campus or the district's Academic Services department.

Intra-District Transfers

Arlington ISD allows students who live within the district to apply to attend a school other than their assigned campus. Transfer applications are processed through the PEIMS and Transfers department (transfers@aisd.net; 682-867-7217) and are considered on a space-available basis.

Inter-District Transfers (from outside Arlington ISD)

Arlington ISD accepts inter-district transfer students from outside its boundaries during designated transfer windows. The 2025–26 window included an open enrollment period with applications processed first-come, first-served. Check aisd.net for the current academic year's transfer window dates.

IEP / 504 Transfers

Under Texas law, Arlington ISD must schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of a student's enrollment if they are transferring with an active IEP. Bring all prior IEP and 504 documentation — evaluations, service plans, and most recent progress reports. For complex needs, contact the district's Special Education and Student Services office before finalizing your home purchase to understand service availability at your campus of assignment.

Boundary Verification

Boundary verification is more critical in Arlington ISD than in most DFW suburban districts — and for several reasons that are specific to this district's geography.

Which Areas Arlington ISD Serves

Arlington ISD serves the city of Arlington in its entirety and the city of Pantego (a small city of approximately 2,400 residents fully surrounded by Arlington). However, not all Arlington city addresses are within Arlington ISD. Some areas of Arlington — primarily in the southern and southwestern portions of the city — fall within Mansfield ISD boundaries. This is a common source of boundary confusion for relocating families who assume "Arlington address = Arlington ISD."

Common Boundary Edge Cases

Southern Arlington / Mansfield ISD overlap: Neighborhoods in south Arlington, particularly in ZIP codes around 76001 and parts of 76017, may fall within Mansfield ISD boundaries. Mansfield ISD is separately rated (TEA A) and serves a different catchment — families who specifically want Mansfield ISD should filter property searches by ISD boundary, not by city name.

Pantego: Despite being an independent municipality with its own city government, Pantego is fully within Arlington ISD for school purposes. Students in Pantego are AISD students.

Grand Prairie overlap: The western boundary of Arlington ISD is adjacent to Grand Prairie ISD. Addresses near the city border of Arlington and Grand Prairie should be verified individually.

High School Attendance Zones Within the District

Within Arlington ISD, each address is assigned to one of the six high school zones. The six zones are not equal in the programs they host at the zoned campus — though all specialized programs (IB, STEM Academy, P-TECH) are technically open to students districtwide via application. A family buying a home to be in the "STEM Academy zone" is making a logical but imprecise decision, since any AISD student can apply regardless of zone. What matters more is which high school campus your child would attend as the default, and whether the application-based program they want has available capacity.

New Construction Considerations

New construction activity in southern and southwestern Arlington may cross ISD boundary lines. Always confirm your specific subdivision's ISD assignment — particularly in areas that abut the Mansfield ISD boundary — with the builder and verify using the Arlington ISD locator tool before going under contract.

How to Verify Your Address

Use the Arlington ISD online school locator at aisd.net enrollment page or the TEA school locator at tea.texas.gov/apps/school-directory. Enter the exact property address — not the street name or zip code alone.

Home-Search Implication

Making an offer on a home in Arlington without verifying your specific address can mean your child attends a different school — or even a different district — than you planned. Boundary maps do not always match common-sense geography in a large urban district like this one. Verify both the ISD assignment and the specific campus before you go under contract.

Finances & Tax Rate

Arlington ISD's 2025 certified total tax rate is $1.0929 per $100 of assessed value — reduced from $1.17 in the prior year, continuing a multi-year trend of rate reductions as property values have risen and state funding formulas have adjusted.

What the Rate Means for Your Mortgage

On a $400,000 home in Arlington ISD territory (after the Texas $100,000 homestead exemption, which reduces your taxable value to $300,000), the school district portion of your property tax would be approximately $3,279 per year — or roughly $273 per month added to your housing costs. On a $500,000 home, the same calculation yields approximately $4,372/year ($364/month). This is in line with most mid-range Tarrant County ISDs and is meaningfully lower than some of the North DFW ISDs that have posted rates above $1.20 or $1.30.

Tax Rate Comparison (2025)

Mansfield ISD's 2025 certified total rate is approximately $1.17 per $100. Grand Prairie ISD's rate is comparable to Arlington's. The Grapevine-Colleyville ISD, which serves much of the Mid-Cities corridor, operates at one of the lower rates in Tarrant County at approximately $0.92 per $100 — though home prices there are considerably higher. In terms of tax rate alone, Arlington ISD is reasonably priced relative to its peer group.

Texas $100,000 Homestead Exemption

All Texas homestead properties receive a $100,000 exemption from the school district's assessed value, meaning the first $100,000 of your home's appraised value is excluded from the school tax calculation. This benefit applies only to your primary residence.

Per-Pupil Expenditure

Arlington ISD's per-pupil expenditure is approximately $11,500–$12,000 per student based on the district's 2024–25 budget and enrollment. Exact figures are available in the district's official financial reports at aisd.net financial transparency and through TEA's PEIMS data system.

Bond Activity

Arlington ISD has passed bond programs over recent years supporting facility improvements and technology upgrades across its 82-campus footprint. Families considering the district should check the district's bond page for current bond project status and any pending rate impacts.

How Arlington ISD Compares

Families considering Arlington ISD most often cross-shop with Mansfield ISD (south Arlington overlap), HEB ISD (mid-cities), and Grand Prairie ISD (adjacent to the west). Each serves a different geography and has a distinct academic profile.

District Size TEA Grade Tax Rate (2025) Best For
Arlington ISD ~53,309 C / 76 $1.09/$100 Program access, urban scale, value housing
Mansfield ISD ~35,000 A ~$1.17/$100 Consistent suburban performance, south Tarrant County
HEB ISD ~22,000 B ~$1.09/$100 Mid-cities geography, mid-size district cohesion
Grand Prairie ISD ~28,000 C ~$1.12/$100 Adjacent to Arlington, similar profile, west Dallas County

Families who prioritize consistent campus quality and a proven suburban track record typically prefer Mansfield ISD — it carries a TEA A, serves a somewhat smaller area, and has a more uniform performance profile across campuses. The tradeoff is that Mansfield home prices have climbed and the tax rate is slightly higher.

HEB ISD is a strong mid-cities option for families who work in Hurst, Euless, or Bedford and don't need to be in Arlington proper. Its smaller size creates more of a cohesive district feel, and its B rating reflects broadly solid campus performance without the wide variance Arlington shows.

Grand Prairie ISD is demographically comparable to Arlington ISD and shares a similar TEA rating. It serves western Tarrant / eastern Dallas County geography. Families in the Grand Prairie / Arlington border area sometimes have flexibility between the two; verify which district your specific address falls in.

Community Feel

Arlington ISD is a genuinely urban school district — which means the community experience varies more by campus and neighborhood than in a geographically tight suburban district. At the elementary level, parent engagement culture differs substantially between campuses: schools in established neighborhoods and those with active PTA networks tend to have strong booster involvement, while campuses in higher-need areas operate in a more resource-constrained environment. Families who want to understand what their specific campus is like should look at the individual campus's accountability report and, if possible, attend a campus open house or speak with current families in the zone.

Arlington's extracurricular and athletics culture is notably strong at the high school level — the city's size supports robust UIL competition across all six high schools in football, basketball, band, choir, debate, and academic competitions. Six high schools means six athletic programs competing in the same district, which keeps competition levels high. The Mansfield, Bowie, Lamar, and Arlington High rivalry games draw real community attention. For a student who wants a big-school athletics experience without moving to Frisco or McKinney, Arlington ISD delivers.

Parent sentiment in community forums consistently highlights the district's program quality as a genuine differentiator — the STEM Academy partnership with UTA comes up regularly as a legitimate college-access pipeline that doesn't exist in most suburban districts. The concern that surfaces equally often is campus-to-campus inconsistency and the sense that a family's experience depends heavily on which elementary zone their address falls into. Parents who have done the research on their specific campus are generally satisfied; those who assumed "C district" without digging tend to be more frustrated. For a relocating family, this suggests the due diligence investment is well worth it before choosing a neighborhood.

4 Things Specific to Arlington ISD You Won't Find Elsewhere

1. STEM Academy open to non-AISD students: Most university-partnership STEM programs require district residency. The STEM Academy at Martin is explicitly open to outside applicants, which is a rare access policy in DFW.

2. A K–12 IB continuum: The IB PYP at Crow Leadership Academy and the IB Diploma at Bowie together create one of the few K–12 IB pathways available at a public school in Tarrant County. Both are application-based, not attendance-zone based.

3. Four distinct P-TECH industry pathways at four different high schools: Healthcare, business/cybersecurity, logistics/mechatronics, and STEM/CADD — students can choose a pathway partly based on their career interest and partly on which high school serves their address zone. This depth of P-TECH infrastructure is uncommon in a single district.

4. Pantego is its own city but an AISD school community: Pantego (pop. ~2,400) maintains its own city government, police department, and zip code — but its residents are fully within Arlington ISD. Buyers interested in Pantego's neighborhood character get the small-city feel with AISD school access.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my Arlington city address guarantee enrollment in Arlington ISD? ▾
No — not all Arlington city addresses are zoned to Arlington ISD. Some areas in southern and southwestern Arlington fall within Mansfield ISD boundaries. Additionally, a small number of addresses near the city's western border may fall in Grand Prairie ISD. The safest approach is to look up your specific address using the Arlington ISD school locator (aisd.net) before making an offer, not after. ZIP code or city name alone is not sufficient for verification.
What's the difference between the six high schools — and does it matter which one my child is zoned to? ▾
Yes, it matters — but perhaps not in the way you'd expect. All six high schools offer AP courses and CTE programs. The differences come down to the specialized program hosted at each campus: Martin has the STEM Academy (UTA partnership), Bowie has the IB Diploma Programme and P-TECH healthcare, Lamar has P-TECH business/cybersecurity, Arlington High has Fine Arts and Dual Language, Sam Houston has P-TECH logistics/mechatronics, and Seguin has P-TECH STEM/CADD. Crucially, the IB programme at Bowie and the STEM Academy at Martin are both open to students districtwide via application — so your attendance-zone assignment and your program eligibility are separate questions. Families targeting a specific program should apply regardless of their home address zone.
How does Arlington ISD handle special education transfers for families moving in? ▾
Under Texas law, Arlington ISD must schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of a student's enrollment if they are transferring with an active IEP. Bring all prior IEP documentation — evaluations, service plans, and the most recent progress report — at the time of enrollment. For 504 plans, the district must review and recognize the existing plan. For families with children who have complex or significant special education needs, I recommend contacting the Arlington ISD Special Education and Student Services office before finalizing your housing decision to understand what services are available at your campus of assignment. Parent experience with SpEd can vary by campus, and direct outreach will give you the most accurate picture of what to expect.
Why does Arlington ISD have a B on Niche but a C from TEA? ▾
TEA's A-F rating is based almost entirely on STAAR assessment outcomes, graduation rates, and college/career readiness measures from state accountability data. Niche combines test scores with parent reviews, teacher quality ratings, diversity metrics, and extracurricular offerings — giving more weight to the community experience alongside raw academic scores. For a district like Arlington ISD, which has genuine program depth, strong extracurriculars, and improving (but still developing) test scores, the gap between the two ratings reflects exactly this difference in methodology. Neither is wrong; they measure different things. TEA measures academic outcomes on state tests; Niche aggregates broader quality signals including parent satisfaction. For relocation decisions, I recommend using both together: TEA for accountability accountability, Niche for a community feel signal, and campus-level TEA reports for the specific school your child would attend.
What are the transfer policies if we move into the district mid-year? ▾
New residents moving into Arlington ISD boundaries can enroll at any time during the school year — mid-year enrollment is accepted on a rolling basis through the district's online portal. For families already within Arlington ISD who want their child to attend a different campus than the one their address is zoned to, intra-district transfer applications are processed through the PEIMS and Transfers department (transfers@aisd.net; 682-867-7217) on a space-available basis. For families coming from outside Arlington ISD who want to enroll without moving, inter-district transfers are accepted during designated windows each year — typically a window opens in spring for the following school year and a summer window for remaining seats. Check aisd.net for current window dates.
How does Arlington ISD's 2025 tax rate compare to nearby districts? ▾
Arlington ISD's 2025 certified total rate is $1.0929 per $100 of assessed value — among the lower rates for Tarrant County school districts of comparable size. For comparison: Mansfield ISD runs approximately $1.17 per $100, and HEB ISD is comparable to Arlington's rate. The Grapevine-Colleyville ISD, one of the lowest-rate districts in DFW, is at approximately $0.92 — but home prices there run significantly higher than Arlington. On a $400,000 home with the Texas $100,000 homestead exemption (taxable value $300,000), Arlington ISD's rate translates to roughly $273/month in school property tax. The rate has trended downward for several years as property values have risen, and the district adopted a 1-cent reduction in 2025.
Is the STEM Academy at Martin High School really open to students from outside Arlington ISD? ▾
Yes — this is one of the more unusual program access policies in DFW. The STEM Academy at Martin High School is explicitly open to students from outside Arlington ISD, not just to AISD residents. It is free of charge, operates in partnership with the University of Texas at Arlington, and allows students to earn up to 60 hours of college credit in engineering, biomedical science, computer science, or math/science pathways beginning in 9th grade. Transportation is parent-provided for out-of-zone and out-of-district students. Application and enrollment are through Arlington ISD's academic programs office. For families who live just outside AISD boundaries but are drawn to the UTA partnership, this program is worth investigating directly with the district.
