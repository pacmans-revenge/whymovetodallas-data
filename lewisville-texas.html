Location & Commute

Where Is Lewisville? Commutes & Getting Around

Lewisville sits about 30 miles north of Downtown Dallas and roughly 35 miles east of Downtown Fort Worth, in Denton County. The city runs along I-35E, with SH-121 providing the key east-west connector to Frisco, Plano, and the entire Legacy West employer corridor. Lewisville Lake — one of DFW's largest reservoirs — sits directly to the north, framing the city's outdoor character.

Lewisville's central position in the Metroplex means reasonable commutes in multiple directions — 30–35 minutes to Dallas, 22–35 minutes to Plano/Legacy West, 25–35 minutes to Frisco. It's not as close to any one employment hub as Irving is to Las Colinas, but it covers a wider range of commute patterns without being too far from anything.

I-35E is the primary corridor to Dallas and backs up meaningfully during peak hours — heading south between 7–9 AM and 4:30–7 PM can add 15–20 minutes to quoted times. Budget toll costs if you use the Dallas North Tollway or SH-121 regularly; $50–$100/month is typical for commuters.
Education

Lewisville ISD — Schools in Lewisville, Texas

Lewisville ISD is an A-rated district serving not just Lewisville but also Flower Mound, Highland Village, and parts of Coppell — making it one of Denton County's most significant school districts. The critical nuance: LISD is large, and school quality varies meaningfully by campus. Lewisville city students typically attend Lewisville HS or Killough MS — which perform below the district's flagship schools like Marcus HS (Flower Mound) and Flower Mound HS. Research your specific address carefully.

Family Life

What Life Actually Looks Like in Lewisville

Lake Lewisville — The Defining Amenity

One of DFW's largest reservoirs, Lake Lewisville offers 48 miles of shoreline, sandy public beaches, marinas, boat ramps, and camping areas. Families here boat, fish, paddleboard, and swim in a way that most DFW suburbs simply can't offer. In summer, it's genuinely exceptional.

Old Town Lewisville Revival

The historic downtown has seen genuine reinvestment — local restaurants, coffee shops, craft breweries, and Wayne Ferguson Plaza amphitheater hosting concerts and events. It's not The Star, but it's a real neighborhood dining and entertainment district that gives Lewisville personality many similarly-priced suburbs lack.

LISD School Community Culture

Lewisville ISD is A-rated and deeply woven into the community fabric — athletics, performing arts, and academic competitions are major community events. Even within Lewisville city (rather than Flower Mound), the LISD culture creates school-community engagement that families relocating from California or New York often find refreshing.

Affordable Entry to Denton County

Lewisville provides access to Denton County's lifestyle — lower taxes than Dallas County, the lake, established community feel — at a ~$400K median that's $100K+ below Flower Mound. For families who stretch their budget to get into the LISD district, Lewisville is the practical answer.

Diverse, Established Community

Lewisville has Niche's A+ for diversity — the city is genuinely multicultural, with a mix of long-established residents alongside newer arrivals. If you're coming from a diverse urban background and want your kids to grow up in a similarly mixed environment, Lewisville delivers this more naturally than most north DFW suburbs.

Central Location = Flexibility

Lewisville's I-35E/SH-121 position means reasonable commutes in multiple directions — Dallas, Frisco, Plano, Fort Worth, and the Alliance/Denton corridor are all under 40 minutes in normal traffic. For dual-income households with jobs in different parts of DFW, this geographic flexibility is a real advantage.

The honest tradeoff: Lewisville High School doesn't match Marcus or Flower Mound HS on rankings — the district's top schools are in Flower Mound, not Lewisville city. Families who move to Lewisville specifically for LISD and don't research their specific campus assignment sometimes feel that gap acutely once their kids start school. I-35E traffic is also a real constraint — the south-bound commute to Dallas during rush hour is one of DFW's most consistently congested routes. Know both of these realities before you buy.
Getting Outside

Recreation, Walkability & City Amenities

Lewisville is car-dependent for daily errands — there is no DART rail, no DCTA rail, and no meaningful transit option. A personal vehicle is essential. The city more than compensates with Lake Lewisville as a defining outdoor amenity — 48 miles of shoreline, multiple public beaches, marinas, and camping areas that no other DFW suburb at this price point can match.

Parks, Trails & Green Space

Lewisville maintains over 50 parks with more than 1,200 acres of parkland, anchored by the Lake Lewisville shoreline. The lake's multiple public access points — including Lewisville Lake Park with a beach, marinas, and camping — make it the standout recreational asset in this price range anywhere in DFW.

Lake Lewisville (Lewisville Lake Park)

The defining outdoor amenity — 48 miles of shoreline with public beaches, boat ramps, marinas, fishing, camping, and kayaking. Sandy beaches along the southern shore are packed with families in summer. Lewisville Lake Park encompasses hundreds of acres of public access land.

Lewisville Lake Environmental Learning Area (LLELA)

A 2,000-acre nature preserve and environmental education center on the lake's western shore. Native prairie, wooded trails, and wildlife observation areas — genuinely unusual for a suburban DFW city. Used by LISD schools and families for nature education and hiking.

East Lake Park

A community park on the eastern lake shore with fishing, picnic areas, playgrounds, and trail access. Popular with families for weekend outings. More accessible for east Lewisville and Castle Hills residents than the main Lewisville Lake Park entrance.

Dick Powell Trail Extension

A multi-use trail corridor connecting Lewisville neighborhoods to the lake and Old Town. Popular for running, cycling, and dog walking. Connects to the broader Denton County trail network and provides a practical north-south trail spine through the city.

Orr Park & Sports Complex

A multi-field sports complex hosting youth baseball, softball, and soccer leagues across the city. Heavily used on weekends for Lewisville Youth Association and select league games. The primary organized youth sports venue for Lewisville families.

Wayne Ferguson Plaza

Lewisville's public gathering space and outdoor amphitheater in Old Town — concerts, community events, and farmers markets throughout the year. Anchors the Old Town revival and gives Lewisville a downtown outdoor space that most similarly-priced suburbs lack entirely.

Safety & Crime

Is Lewisville, Texas Safe?

Lewisville is a generally safe city with a B+ safety grade from Niche. Violent crime runs approximately 2.5 per 1,000 residents — above the Denton County premium-suburb average (Flower Mound, Highland Village are lower) but well below the national average of ~4.0/1,000. Crime is not evenly distributed; Castle Hills and the newer northeast neighborhoods consistently track lower than the Old Town and commercial corridor areas. Old Town's revitalization has brought more foot traffic, which correlates with lower property crime in that district.

Violent Crime Rate

~2.5 per 1,000 residents — well below the national average of ~4.0/1,000. Higher than premium Denton County suburbs like Flower Mound (~1.2/1,000) but within normal range for a city of 115K.

Property Crime

~22 per 1,000 residents — moderately above the Denton County suburban norm but below state and national averages. Higher rates in industrial and commercial zones; lower in residential neighborhoods, especially Castle Hills and Lakepointe.

Lewisville PD

Lewisville Police Department operates community-oriented policing with neighborhood programs and proactive patrols. Crime statistics are published regularly. The department has been recognized for community engagement initiatives in the LISD corridor.
