Honest Assessment

Pros & Cons of Living in Aubrey

I won't sell you on Aubrey. Here's the real picture so you can decide if it's right for your family.

✓ What Aubrey Does Well

  • Exceptional value — $350K buys a 2,400–3,000 sq ft home here. The same money gets you half that in Frisco or Prosper. For families who need space, this is the strongest value proposition in North DFW.
  • New construction inventory — Multiple active builder communities mean you can customize, get builder incentives (rate buydowns, closing cost help, upgrades), and move in quickly without bidding wars.
  • Lake Ray Roberts proximity — A 29,000-acre lake with camping, hiking, fishing, and equestrian trails. Less than 25 minutes away. No comparable suburb in DFW has this.
  • Master-planned community amenities — Paloma Creek, Sandbrock Ranch, and Providence Village deliver resort-style pools, trails, and fitness facilities included in HOA fees.
  • Room to breathe — Bigger lots, less density, genuine rural character at the edges. If your family needs space to spread out, Aubrey delivers it.
  • Buyer's market conditions — 6 months of supply and 48 days on market means you can negotiate. That's rare in DFW right now.
  • No state income tax — Significant savings for high earners, especially those relocating from California, New York, or Illinois.

— What to Weigh Carefully

  • Dual ISD split — address-by-address verification required — The 76227 zip code spans both Aubrey ISD (TEA C/79) and Denton ISD (B). The difference is significant for families with school-age kids. One street can change the district. Verify every address before you fall in love with a house.
  • Aubrey ISD rating — If you land in Aubrey ISD, the district carries a TEA C rating — the weakest academic rating in this area. Some families are fine with it; others want a B or A ISD regardless of price.
  • MUD/PID fees in new communities — Sandbrock Ranch, Paloma Creek, and parts of Providence Village carry special district fees that add $1,750–$5,250/year indefinitely. These don't go away. Model the full tax picture before buying.
  • Car-dependent — all of it — Walk Score of 7. No transit. Two cars required. Every errand, every activity, every school pickup involves driving. This is a meaningful quality-of-life factor, especially if you have a spouse returning from a walkable city environment.
  • Limited local retail and dining — The dining and shopping options in Aubrey proper are thin. HEB is close, but variety means driving 15–25 minutes to Little Elm, Denton, or Frisco. Not a dealbreaker, but plan for it.
  • US-380 congestion is growing — The corridor is developing fast and the road hasn't kept up. Peak-hour driving on US-380 is increasingly slow. It won't get better before it gets addressed with infrastructure.
  • City services and infrastructure still maturing — Aubrey proper is a small city. Roads, municipal services, and emergency response infrastructure are not at the level of Frisco or Prosper. The master-planned communities compensate somewhat, but it's worth understanding.

Common Questions

Aubrey, Texas — Frequently Asked Questions

Aubrey is a strong fit for families who prioritize space, value, and outdoor access over walkability and school district prestige. The master-planned communities (Paloma Creek, Sandbrock Ranch, Providence Village) offer resort-style amenities at a fraction of what you'd pay in Frisco or Prosper. Lake Ray Roberts State Park adds a quality-of-life dimension that most suburbs simply can't offer. The key tradeoff: the ISD split means school quality varies significantly by address — verify before you buy.

This is the most important question in Aubrey — and the answer is: it depends on your specific address. The 76227 zip code spans two school districts. The northern and central portions of Aubrey (including Aubrey proper and much of Providence Village) are in Aubrey ISD — a TEA C-rated district. The southern portions, including Sandbrock Ranch, Paloma Creek, and parts of southern Providence Village, are in Denton ISD — a TEA B-rated district with stronger academic outcomes. One street can be the difference. I verify this by address for every client before we make an offer — it's non-negotiable.

The base combined rate for Aubrey ISD properties in Denton County is approximately $2.21 per $100 of assessed value — covering Aubrey ISD (~$1.46), City of Aubrey (~$0.46), Denton County (~$0.19), and NCTC (~$0.10). On a $350,000 home, that's roughly $7,735/year before any special district fees.

Important: new communities like Sandbrock Ranch, Paloma Creek, and some Providence Village sections carry MUD (Municipal Utility District) or PID (Public Improvement District) fees, adding $0.50–$1.50 per $100 — an extra $1,750–$5,250/year. These fees fund development infrastructure and don't go away. Always confirm the full tax picture by specific address before committing to purchase. Denton ISD area rates may differ slightly.

Aubrey is positioned well for the Frisco and Denton employment corridors. Frisco (T-Mobile HQ, PGA of America, The Star) is approximately 20 minutes east on US-380 — the best daily commute from Aubrey. Denton (UNT, Texas Woman's University, Denton Regional Medical) is about 15 minutes west. Legacy West/Plano (Toyota NA, JPMorgan Chase, FedEx) is 35 minutes. Downtown Dallas is 45–50 minutes — that's a significant daily commitment. For remote workers and hybrid employees, Aubrey's location is genuinely excellent — far enough out for the space and price, close enough to the corridor for 2–3 day office schedules.

The neighborhoods I recommend most for relocating families, in order: Sandbrock Ranch (Denton ISD, new construction, resort amenity center — best overall package for families prioritizing schools), Paloma Creek (Denton ISD for southern portions, three amenity centers, established community with mature trees and trails), Providence Village (mix of Aubrey ISD and Denton ISD depending on your specific street — ask your agent to verify), and Aubrey Proper (Aubrey ISD, small-town character, lower price points, older homes with larger lots). Each suits a different priority — I'll match you to the right one based on your ISD requirement, budget, and community style.

Yes — Aubrey is a safe community. Niche grades the area a B for Crime & Safety. Property crime rates are below Texas state averages. Some national crime databases (NeighborhoodScout) show higher per-capita violent crime for Aubrey — this is a base rate artifact of a very small city population (~8,500) where one or two incidents in a year can spike the per-capita rate significantly. The master-planned communities (Sandbrock Ranch, Paloma Creek, Providence Village) have HOA governance and a neighborhood character that is very family-safe. As with any suburb, I recommend checking specific neighborhood data and talking to residents, which I do as part of every relocation consultation.

For families relocating from California, Aubrey represents one of the most dramatic financial improvements in all of DFW. A home priced at $350,000 here would cost $1.2M–$1.5M in the Bay Area or $900K–$1.1M in greater LA. Texas has no state income tax — a family earning $250,000 saves roughly $20,000–$30,000/year compared to California rates. Even accounting for Aubrey's property tax rate (~2.2% base, higher with MUD/PID), the annual tax savings from eliminating state income tax far exceed the property tax difference for most families. The total package — larger home, lower purchase price, no income tax, and strong outdoor lifestyle — is what drives California families specifically to Aubrey over closer-in suburbs.

New construction in Aubrey can be a great value — but there are important caveats. First, verify the school district by specific address, not just neighborhood name. Second, model the full property tax picture including any MUD/PID fees before comparing monthly payments to a resale home. Third, understand builder contracts and incentive structures — builders typically offer rate buydowns and closing cost assistance that meaningfully reduce your effective cost, but these incentives have timing windows and conditions. I've closed many new construction deals in the Aubrey/Denton County area and can walk you through which builders, which communities, and which lots make the strongest case for your family. Don't sign a builder contract without an independent buyer's agent — it costs you nothing extra (the builder pays my fee) and protects you significantly.
