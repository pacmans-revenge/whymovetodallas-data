Location & Commute

Where Is Celina? Commutes & Getting Around

Celina sits about 45 miles north of Downtown Dallas and roughly 22 miles north of Frisco, in Collin County. The city straddles US-380 (University Drive) and has easy access to the Dallas North Tollway via Preston Road — the primary artery connecting Celina to the Frisco employment corridor. The Tollway is the fastest route south but adds toll costs.

Celina is genuinely far from the traditional Dallas employment core — most residents are commuting to Frisco, McKinney, or Prosper for work. Families whose employer is in the Frisco/The Star area or who work remotely are the best fit. If your employer is Downtown Dallas or Las Colinas, the daily commute is a 45–65 minute drive that requires honest consideration.

The Dallas North Tollway is the practical lifeline south; budget $80–120/month for toll costs if you commute to Frisco or beyond daily. US-380 (no toll) connects east to McKinney and west to Prosper as a free alternative for those corridors.
Education

Celina ISD — Schools in Celina

Critical ISD zoning note: Not all homes in the Celina area are zoned to Celina ISD. Light Farms — one of the largest and most popular master-planned communities in the area — is zoned to Prosper ISD (Niche A), not Celina ISD. This is a significant distinction since Prosper ISD is rated higher. Always verify the specific ISD for any property address before purchasing. Do not assume based on the community name or ZIP code.

Celina ISD holds a Niche B+ overall grade with an A- academics grade and a B+ college prep rating. Niche rates the district A+ for families — reflecting the young, engaged parent community that has moved into Celina's master-planned neighborhoods. The ISD is growing rapidly alongside the city and has invested in new school construction to keep pace.

Celina offers new construction at prices generally $50–100K below comparable Frisco and Prosper neighborhoods — which is the main financial argument for the area. The important caveat: MUD districts in many of Celina's master-planned communities add $0.25–$0.50 per $100 to your effective tax rate, which should be factored into your monthly payment calculation.

Family Life

What Life Actually Looks Like in Celina

Celina Bobcats Football

Celina High School football is a genuine cultural institution — the Bobcats are one of the most storied programs in Texas 5A, drawing the whole community to Friday night games. If Friday Night Lights culture matters to your family, Celina delivers it authentically.

Resort-Style HOA Amenities

Light Farms and Mustang Lakes offer amenity packages that rival small resorts — on-site restaurants, multiple pools, fitness centers, miles of trails, and social programming. For families who want neighborhood infrastructure built in from day one, Celina's master-planned communities deliver.

Outdoor Recreation

Celina's lower density and larger lots give families more outdoor space than comparable-priced Frisco neighborhoods. The master-planned communities include trail systems and open green space. Nearby Collin County natural areas add options for hiking and outdoor recreation beyond the HOA amenities.

Small-Town Celina Downtown

Historic downtown Celina offers a rodeo grounds, local events, and genuine small-town Texas character that contrasts with the master-planned communities surrounding it. The annual Celina PRCA Rodeo is a community staple. It's not a polished retail district, but it's authentic.

Young Community — Easy to Connect

Celina's master-planned communities are filled with families in the same life stage — most moved in within the past few years, kids are the same ages, and HOA social programming actively creates community. Relocating families consistently report meeting neighbors quickly in this environment.

Emerging Retail & Dining

Celina's retail and dining scene is actively growing along US-380 and near the master-planned communities. Light Farms' on-site restaurant is a genuine neighborhood hub. For more variety, Frisco and McKinney are 20–30 minutes away — most families do a mix of local and corridor errands.

The honest tradeoff: Celina is a long commute to Downtown Dallas — 45–65 minutes on a good day, longer in traffic. The retail and dining infrastructure is still catching up to the population, so most non-HOA errands mean a drive to Frisco or McKinney. MUD taxes in many communities add meaningfully to the property tax bill beyond what headline rates suggest. Families whose employer is in Frisco or who work from home will find Celina makes the most sense; Dallas-core commuters should model the daily drive before committing.
Getting Outside

Recreation, Walkability & City Amenities

Celina is extremely car-dependent — there is no DART, no DCTA, and no transit of any kind. A personal vehicle is essential for every errand, commute, and activity. Celina compensates through its HOA-run amenity infrastructure: the master-planned communities offer trails, pools, fitness centers, and open space that keep families active without leaving their neighborhoods.

Parks, Trails & Green Space

Celina's city park system is still developing alongside the rapid residential growth. Most outdoor recreation infrastructure is currently HOA-managed within the master-planned communities. The city has green space and is building out its parks program, but families looking for extensive city-managed trail networks comparable to Frisco or Flower Mound should set expectations accordingly.

Celina City Park

Celina's primary city-operated park includes sports fields, playground equipment, and open green space used by community leagues. Growing infrastructure as the city expands.

Light Farms Amenity Parks

Light Farms' HOA-managed open spaces include multiple waterfeature parks, playground areas, and the trail corridor connecting neighborhoods. Exclusively for residents — one of the best HOA park systems in North DFW.

Mustang Lakes Amenity Center

Mustang Lakes features a resort-style pool, fitness center, tennis and pickleball courts, and lakeside green space — exclusively for community residents. One of the premium HOA amenity packages in Collin County.

Collin County Nature Preserve (Nearby)

Collin County's natural areas — including portions northeast of Celina — offer hiking and wildlife observation. A short drive away for families who want to get into nature beyond the HOA amenities.

Community Trails (In-Progress)

Celina is actively building its multi-use trail network connecting neighborhoods and parks. Several trail segments are open; more are planned as new communities come online. Check with the city for current completed routes.

Future Parks (Planned)

Celina's parks master plan includes multiple additional parks and green corridors to serve the growing population. Families buying now should consider proximity to planned future park sites when evaluating lots.

Safety & Crime

Is Celina Safe?

Celina is a very safe, small, fast-growing city. With a population still under 60,000, it has not yet experienced the property crime volumes seen in larger adjacent cities. Violent crime is extremely low — well under 1.0 per 1,000 residents. Niche grades Celina's safety A-. The Celina Police Department is a smaller department that has been growing its capacity alongside the city's rapid expansion.

Violent Crime Rate

Very low — under 1.0 per 1,000 residents. Well below the U.S. average of ~4.0/1,000. Celina's size and demographics contribute to a very safe environment.

Property Crime

Low — below state and national averages. As the city continues rapid growth, property crime rates may change; current rates are among the lowest in Collin County.

Celina Police Dept

Actively expanding capacity alongside rapid population growth. HOA communities maintain their own security patrols supplementing city coverage in master-planned neighborhoods.
