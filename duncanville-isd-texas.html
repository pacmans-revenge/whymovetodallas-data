Duncanville ISD serves approximately 12,000 students across 18 campuses in southwest Dallas County, covering the entire city of Duncanville and portions of Dallas, Cedar Hill, and DeSoto. For families relocating to southwest DFW, this district is worth understanding clearly — the TEA letter grade tells one story, and the 94.4% graduation rate (well above the national average of 86.5%) tells another. The honest picture is a district that is actively improving, has strong CTE and Fine Arts pathways, and sits in a housing market where your dollar goes meaningfully farther than in many higher-rated DFW districts.

The district runs one traditional high school — Duncanville High School — along with four middle schools, nine elementary schools, and an early learning academy. Families considering Duncanville ISD should verify their specific address carefully: because the district serves parts of four different cities, it is one of the more boundary-complex districts in Dallas County. The ongoing 2023 Bond reconfiguration has also shifted some attendance zones, so post-bond maps are the ones to check before making an offer.

Duncanville ISD is commonly shortlisted by families prioritizing affordability in southwest DFW, by parents whose students are drawn to athletics or the performing arts, and by buyers who recognize that a C TEA rating does not automatically predict a poor campus experience for their individual child.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Are relocating specifically to Duncanville city and want to be zoned to their neighborhood school
  • Prioritize affordability — Duncanville offers some of the most accessible price points in Dallas County
  • Have students who thrive in athletics, fine arts, or CTE career pathways
  • Value a strong graduation rate and pathways to post-secondary readiness over letter-grade rankings
  • Want a district with active investment — the 2023 Bond is funding significant campus improvements

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • You are prioritizing top-quartile standardized test scores and STAAR proficiency rates
  • Your family wants a choice of high schools within the same district
  • You are buying in Cedar Hill or DeSoto and assume you will be zoned to Duncanville ISD — verify your address first
  • Highly specialized GT or IB programming is a deciding factor in your home search
⚠ Boundary Verification

Not every home with a Duncanville, Cedar Hill, or DeSoto mailing address falls within Duncanville ISD boundaries. The district serves portions of four different cities, and some neighborhoods within Duncanville itself are served by adjacent districts. The 2023 Bond has also resulted in updated attendance zones. Always verify your specific address at duncanvilleisd.org before making an offer — do not rely on zip code or city name alone.

TEA Accountability Rating

Duncanville ISD received an overall C rating (score: 74) in the 2023 TEA A–F accountability cycle, which is the most recently officially published rating. Note: TEA ratings often lag one year behind — if more recent ratings have been released, verify at txschools.gov.

The TEA A–F system evaluates districts on three primary domains:

  • Student Achievement — Measures overall performance on STAAR assessments, graduation, and college/career readiness across all student groups
  • School Progress — Evaluates year-over-year academic growth and how the district performs relative to similar-income districts
  • Closing the Gaps — Assesses performance equity across student subgroups, including economically disadvantaged students and English learners

For relocating families, the important context behind Duncanville ISD's C rating is this: a score of 74 means the district is performing in the lower-middle tier of Texas accountability, but the gap between a C (70–79) and a B (80–89) is often smaller in practice than the letter grades suggest. More meaningfully for families, the 94.4% graduation rate outperforms districts with higher TEA letter grades — which tells you that students are completing high school at a very strong rate, even as campus-level STAAR proficiency rates have room to grow.

What the Grade Means in Practice

A TEA C does not mean every campus in the district is underperforming — it reflects a district average. Some individual campuses within Duncanville ISD have ratings that differ from the district overall. Your child's experience will be shaped by their specific campus, their teacher relationships, and the programs they access — not solely by the district's composite letter grade.

The district has reported that 40% of its campuses eligible for TEA ratings improved by at least one letter grade in the most recent cycle, indicating measurable upward movement at the campus level.

Academic Programs & Outcomes

Duncanville ISD's academic profile presents a mixed but evolving picture. The district's graduation rate of 94.4% is above the national average of approximately 86.5% — a meaningful indicator that students are completing their education successfully. For families focused on ensuring their child finishes high school with a diploma and some post-secondary pathway, this figure deserves more weight than it typically gets in district comparisons focused on letter grades alone.

Key Academic Indicators

  • Graduation rate: 94.4% (source: Texas Tribune/TEA data)
  • Per-pupil expenditure: $13,055 per student — below the national average of approximately $14,347, which reflects the district's cost structure in Dallas County (source: publicly available PEIMS/district finance data)
  • Dual Credit: Available at Duncanville High School, allowing students to earn college credit while still in high school
  • STAAR proficiency: Verify current reading and math proficiency rates by campus at txschools.gov — rates change annually and the most accurate figures come directly from TEA's published campus reports

For families whose students are college-bound, Duncanville High School offers dual credit coursework and AP courses, though the breadth of the AP catalog is smaller than what you would find at a larger ISD like Plano or Frisco. Families prioritizing a wide menu of AP or IB courses should factor this into their district comparison. For students interested in career and technical pathways, the CTE offerings at the high school level are a genuine strength — discussed further in the Programs section.

Campus Highlights

Duncanville ISD operates 18 campuses: one early learning academy serving pre-K, nine elementary schools (Pre-K through 5th grade), four middle schools (6th–8th grade), one high school (9th–12th grade), and two alternative campuses. All traditional high school students in the district attend Duncanville High School — there is no second comprehensive high school option within the district.

Duncanville High School

High School Grades 9–12 GS: 3/10 Niche: B−

The district's sole comprehensive high school, serving all Duncanville ISD 9th–12th graders. Home to the Panthers — one of the most storied football programs in southwest DFW. Offers Dual Credit, AP courses, CTE pathways, and a robust Fine Arts program including orchestra, band, and theater.

Duncanville Early Learning Academy

Early Childhood Pre-K

Dedicated pre-K campus providing foundational early childhood education for the district. Families interested in pre-K enrollment should contact the district for eligibility requirements (income-based and/or special needs criteria typically apply for public pre-K access).

One of four Duncanville ISD middle schools. Middle school attendance is zoned by address — confirm your specific campus assignment through the district's boundary lookup tool, particularly if your home is in a border area of Duncanville, Cedar Hill, or DeSoto.

Hastings, Reed & Bilhartz Elementary Schools

Elementary Pre-K – 5th Grade

Among the nine elementary campuses in the district. The 2023 Bond is funding renovations across several elementary campuses. Note that elementary boundaries were subject to reconfiguration as part of the bond — verify your specific zoned campus before purchasing.

One High School — What That Means for Your Family

Having a single comprehensive high school is a meaningful feature of this district. On one hand, it means the entire community rallies around one school identity — which creates a strong sense of shared culture, especially in athletics. On the other hand, families who want to choose between high schools based on programs, enrollment size, or campus culture do not have that option within Duncanville ISD. If high school choice is a priority, comparing districts with multiple high schools (like Grand Prairie ISD or Lancaster ISD) is worth the conversation.

Specialty Programs

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

Campus: Duncanville High School (district-level program).
Access: Open to all enrolled high school students — no application or lottery required.
CTE pathways span industry certifications across health sciences, business, technology, and trades. Students can earn industry-recognized credentials before graduation. For families with students who know they want a technical or vocational pathway, this is one of the district's strongest offerings. Transportation from home to campus is standard as part of regular enrollment.

Dual Credit

Campus: Duncanville High School (in partnership with area community colleges).
Access: Open to qualifying high school juniors and seniors — typically requires a minimum GPA and placement scores.
Dual credit courses allow students to earn college credit at no cost while still enrolled in high school. Families targeting this pathway should confirm current partner institutions and course offerings directly with the Duncanville High School counseling office each year, as availability can shift.

Fine Arts

Campus: District-wide, with the full program at Duncanville High School.
Access: Open enrollment — students choose fine arts electives through the standard course selection process.
Duncanville ISD has a strong Fine Arts culture at the high school level, including orchestra, band, choir, theater, and visual arts. The program has deep roots in the school community. For families with students focused on performing or visual arts, this is worth factoring into your decision.

Athletics

Campus: All campuses; most prominent at Duncanville High School.
Access: Open to all enrolled students — tryouts required for competitive teams.
Duncanville ISD athletics — particularly Panthers football — are a defining feature of the school community. The program has a serious competitive history in the Dallas area. For families with student athletes, the athletic culture here is genuine and deeply supported. Note: youth sports culture in Duncanville tends to be community-wide, not just school-level.

Gifted & Talented (GT)

Campus: Available across the district at the elementary and middle school level.
Access: Referral and assessment process — contact the district's GT coordinator for the current identification timeline and criteria.
Duncanville ISD provides GT services as required by Texas law. Families relocating with a student who has been identified as gifted in another state or district should contact the Special Programs office promptly — Texas GT identification is campus-level and may require a new assessment, but the district is required to provide services during the transition period.

Advanced Placement (AP)

Campus: Duncanville High School.
Access: Course enrollment through the standard scheduling process — prerequisites may apply.
AP courses are available at Duncanville High School, though the catalog is more limited than what you'll find at larger districts like Plano ISD or Frisco ISD. Families for whom a broad AP menu is a priority should verify the current course list directly with DHS counselors each year, as offerings expand as enrollment and staffing allow.

Enrollment & Registration

Duncanville ISD enrollment is address-based for all traditional campuses. Here is the general process for families relocating to the district's attendance zone (verify current-year specifics at duncanvilleisd.org):

  1. Verify your address. Confirm your home address is within Duncanville ISD boundaries and identify your zoned campus before doing anything else. Use the district's online address lookup or call the main registration office.
  2. Gather required documents. Typical requirements include: proof of residency (signed lease or utility bill in your name at the Duncanville address), government-issued photo ID, student's birth certificate, up-to-date immunization records, and prior school records/transcripts.
  3. Complete online registration. Duncanville ISD uses an online enrollment portal. Access it through the district website. Families without reliable internet access should contact the district for in-person enrollment support.
  4. Enrollment window. New student enrollment is accepted year-round for families who have established residency in the district. For the 2025–26 school year, verify open enrollment dates and any registration deadlines at the district website — timelines can shift, particularly for kindergarten pre-registration drives in spring.
  5. Intra-district transfers. If you live within Duncanville ISD but wish to request a different campus than your zoned school, contact the district's registration office for the transfer request process. Approval is not guaranteed and is typically limited by campus capacity.
  6. Inter-district transfers (from another Texas ISD). Duncanville ISD may accept transfer students from outside the district on a space-available basis. Contact the registration office directly — policies and available campuses change year to year.
  7. IEP and 504 transfers. Under Texas law, if your child has an active IEP or 504 plan from a previous district, Duncanville ISD must convene an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee meeting within 30 days of enrollment to review and adopt or modify the plan. Bring all prior documentation — IEP, 504 plan, evaluation reports, and progress notes. Contact the district's Special Education department at the time of enrollment to initiate this process. Do not wait until the first day of school.

Boundary Verification — Critical for Southwest DFW Buyers

Duncanville ISD is one of the more boundary-complex districts in Dallas County. It serves the entire city of Duncanville and portions of three adjacent cities: Dallas, Cedar Hill, and DeSoto. This means that city name and mailing address are not reliable predictors of your school district.

Common Edge Cases

  • Cedar Hill addresses: Parts of Cedar Hill are zoned to Duncanville ISD, while the majority falls within Cedar Hill ISD. Do not assume a Cedar Hill mailing address means Cedar Hill ISD — verify the specific parcel.
  • DeSoto addresses: Similar boundary split exists near the DeSoto/Duncanville border. Some DeSoto addresses fall within Duncanville ISD's service area.
  • Dallas addresses near the boundary: The northwest portions of Duncanville ISD territory include addresses with Dallas mailing addresses. These are particularly easy to misread without a boundary map.
  • Post-2023 Bond reconfiguration: The 2023 Bond included campus renovations paired with attendance zone adjustments. Families purchasing in 2024 or 2025 should use the most current boundary maps from the district — not maps published before the bond was approved.
  • New construction communities: Any new development on the edges of the district's boundary should be verified individually — new streets are not always assigned to the "expected" district.

How to Verify

Use the district's official address lookup at duncanvilleisd.org. You can also cross-check using the TEA school locator at tea.texas.gov/apps/school-directory. If you are working with a real estate agent, ask them to run the address through the district locator before you write an offer — not after closing.

Home-Search Implication

Making an offer without verifying your specific address can mean your child attends a different school — or a different district — than you planned. Boundary maps do not always match common-sense geography in southwest DFW, and the 2023 Bond reconfiguration adds an extra layer to confirm. I routinely run address verifications for my clients before they write an offer. If you'd like help with this, reach out before you fall in love with a home.

Finances & Tax Rate

Duncanville ISD's 2025 total certified tax rate is approximately $1.17 per $100 of assessed value (verify the most current certified rate at comptroller.texas.gov or through Dallas CAD). The district has publicly committed to fiscal responsibility, noting that its total tax rate has decreased every year for the past five years — including maintaining the rate within the same range when the 2023 Bond was approved.

What $1.17 Means for Your Mortgage

On a $400,000 home in Duncanville ISD territory — after applying Texas's $100,000 standard homestead exemption, which reduces your assessed value to $300,000 — the annual school tax comes to approximately $3,510, or roughly $293/month added to your mortgage payment for school taxes alone. This is a meaningful number to factor into your monthly budget when comparing neighborhoods across districts.

Comparison to Nearby Districts

Cedar Hill ISD and DeSoto ISD both carry approximately the same $1.17 rate as Duncanville ISD, making them tax-equivalent comparisons. Grand Prairie ISD also sits at the same rate. For families considering a move to a higher-rated district for purely tax-efficiency reasons, there is no significant savings opportunity in the immediate southwest DFW peer group — the differentiator is the school experience, not the tax bill. Midlothian ISD (farther south, TEA B) and other suburban districts may have different rate structures.

Per-Pupil Expenditure

Duncanville ISD spends approximately $13,055 per student, which is below the national average. This figure reflects the district's cost structure and enrollment composition — it should be read alongside outcomes data (graduation rate, program offerings) rather than in isolation.

2023 Bond Election

Duncanville ISD voters approved a bond package in 2023 to fund campus renovations and improvements. The district's commitment during the bond campaign was to maintain the total tax rate at or below the pre-bond level. Families interested in the bond's specific projects and campus improvement timelines can find details on the district website under "Building the Future."

Texas Homestead Exemption

Texas provides a $100,000 homestead exemption on school district taxes for your primary residence. If you are a homeowner age 65 or older or are disabled, additional exemptions apply. Apply through the Dallas Central Appraisal District after you close — the filing deadline is typically April 30 of the tax year in which you qualify.

Community Feel

Duncanville has a well-established suburban identity with deep roots — it's not a master-planned newcomer community, and the school culture reflects that. Panther Pride is real: Friday night football at Duncanville High School is a community-wide event, and the athletics booster culture is active and well-organized. Families who have never lived in a Texas football town sometimes underestimate how central that shared identity can be to the school year.

Beyond athletics, the Fine Arts community at Duncanville High School has a similarly dedicated parent-supporter culture. The orchestra, band, and theater programs have long-standing traditions, and the boosters for these programs are organized and visible. Families with students interested in performing arts often find the community more supportive than they expected given the district's academic ranking.

Parent conversations on community forums about Duncanville ISD tend to cluster around a few consistent themes: appreciation for the graduation rate and the fact that students are cared for at the human level; frustration with STAAR-level academic performance; and genuine enthusiasm for the athletic and fine arts programs. The honest synthesis is that this is a district where relationships — between students, teachers, and community — are a strength, and where the headline accountability metrics don't fully capture what it's like to be a family here day-to-day. As with any district, campus-level experience varies, and the best predictor for your child is the specific campus and teachers they encounter — not the district composite grade.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my Duncanville or Cedar Hill address guarantee enrollment in Duncanville ISD? ▾
No. A Duncanville mailing address does not automatically mean Duncanville ISD, and a Cedar Hill mailing address could mean either Cedar Hill ISD or Duncanville ISD depending on the exact parcel. The district serves the entire city of Duncanville and portions of Cedar Hill, DeSoto, and Dallas — so you must verify your specific street address using the district's lookup tool at duncanvilleisd.org before purchasing. Do not rely on zip code, city name, or what a neighbor says — verify the parcel directly.
Is there more than one high school in Duncanville ISD? ▾
No. Duncanville ISD has one comprehensive high school: Duncanville High School. All 9th–12th grade students in the district attend the same campus. There are alternative program options (and two alternative campuses in the district), but for traditional enrollment there is no second comprehensive high school. If your family values having multiple high school campuses to choose from within the same district, you may want to consider Grand Prairie ISD (three high schools) or another neighboring district with multiple campuses.
How does Duncanville ISD handle IEP and 504 transfers from another state or district? ▾
Under Texas law, Duncanville ISD must convene an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee meeting within 30 days of your child's enrollment to review any existing IEP from a previous district. For 504 plans, the district must review and adopt or modify the plan as part of enrollment. Bring all prior documentation to enrollment — the IEP or 504 plan itself, recent evaluation reports, progress notes, and any current accommodations. Contact the district's Special Education or Special Services office at the same time you complete registration paperwork; do not wait until school starts. The quality of the transition experience varies by campus, so I recommend asking specifically which campus staff member will be the primary IEP contact for your child's grade level.
Why does Duncanville ISD have a TEA C but a 94% graduation rate? ▾
The TEA A–F system weights STAAR standardized test proficiency heavily in its scoring. A district can have students who graduate at high rates while still having room to improve on the specific STAAR benchmarks TEA uses to compute the composite score. Duncanville ISD's 94.4% graduation rate indicates that students are completing high school — but the underlying STAAR proficiency rates on reading and math tests, which drive much of the A–F score, are lower than the state's top-rated districts. The gap between the two numbers is real and worth understanding: it suggests a district that is keeping students engaged and on track to graduate, even while working to improve standardized academic benchmarks. Neither number alone tells the full story — both matter for making a well-informed decision.
What is Duncanville ISD's transfer policy? ▾
For intra-district transfers (requesting a campus different from your zoned school), Duncanville ISD considers requests on a space-available basis. Contact the registration office to submit a request — approval is not guaranteed and typically depends on the receiving campus having available capacity. For inter-district transfers from another Texas school district, Duncanville ISD may accept students from outside its boundaries depending on available space. Policies change year to year; verify the current-year process directly with the district's registration office. Texas law generally allows families to request inter-district transfers, but receiving districts are not required to accept all requests.
How does the $1.17 tax rate compare to neighboring districts? ▾
Duncanville ISD's approximately $1.17 tax rate (verified 2025 certified rate — confirm current year at the Texas Comptroller's website) is shared by Cedar Hill ISD, DeSoto ISD, and Grand Prairie ISD — the three districts most commonly compared against Duncanville in southwest DFW. In other words, you will not save on school taxes by choosing one of these peer districts over another. For context, higher-rated districts elsewhere in DFW can carry rates ranging from $0.92 (Grapevine-Colleyville ISD) up to $1.30 or more in some fast-growth districts. On a $400,000 home after the $100,000 Texas homestead exemption, $1.17/$100 translates to approximately $3,510 per year in school taxes, or roughly $293/month.
What is the 2023 Bond and how does it affect attendance zones? ▾
Duncanville ISD voters approved a bond package in 2023 to fund campus renovations, improvements, and some campus reconfiguration. As part of the bond implementation, the district has updated attendance zones at several campuses — which means families should verify their zoned campus using current (post-bond) boundary maps rather than older ones. If you purchased a home in Duncanville ISD before 2023 or used an older map during your home search, confirm your zoned elementary and middle school has not changed. The district posts updated boundary information and a "Building the Future" bond progress tracker at duncanvilleisd.org.
