Getting Your Bearings

Where Is Westlake, Texas?

Westlake sits in northeastern Tarrant County, tucked between Southlake to the east, Keller to the west, and Trophy Club to the northwest. It covers roughly 6.5 square miles and shares borders with some of the most sought-after communities in the Metroplex. Texas Highway 114 runs through the southern edge, providing quick, direct access to DFW International Airport to the east and downtown Fort Worth to the west — making Westlake one of the rare DFW suburbs that's genuinely equidistant to both city cores.

What sets Westlake apart geographically isn't just its location — it's what's inside those 6.5 square miles. Circle T Ranch, a 2,500-acre mixed-use master-planned development anchored by Hillwood, houses the Fidelity Investments campus (5,000+ employees), the Charles Schwab corporate headquarters, and Deloitte University, one of the most notable executive training facilities in the country. Residents of Westlake live, quite literally, next to Fortune 500 campuses surrounded by open landscape, longhorn cattle, and manicured estate neighborhoods. That's an unusual combination anywhere in DFW.

The trade-off is the airport proximity: if your life involves frequent air travel — corporate, military, or otherwise — Westlake's 14-minute run to DFW Terminal D is legitimately one of the best commute advantages in North Texas.
Education

Westlake's Three School Options

Westlake is one of the few communities in Texas where families choose between three genuinely elite school options — and which one their kids attend depends entirely on where in Westlake they purchase. The eastern portion of town (Glenwyck Farms, Terra Bella, and most of the core residential areas) falls within Carroll ISD, consistently ranked the #1 school district in Texas. The western portion, including the Vaquero community, sits within Keller ISD, a well-regarded district in its own right. And every Westlake resident is eligible to apply to Westlake Academy — a public, tuition-free K–12 school offering the full International Baccalaureate curriculum, operated directly by the Town of Westlake.

Carroll ISD — #1 in Texas

All 11 campuses earned A ratings from TEA in both 2023–24 and 2024–25. District score of 95/100 — highest in Region 11. 88% of students meet or exceed grade level (state avg: 50%). Graduation rate 99.7%. Serves Glenwyck Farms, Terra Bella, and eastern Westlake.

Westlake Academy — Public IB K–12

One of the only public, tuition-free K–12 schools in Texas offering the full IB curriculum (PYP, MYP, Diploma). 888 students. Niche A+, GreatSchools 10/10. Average ACT 30, SAT 1340. 95% graduation rate. Lottery admission — apply early, waitlist is competitive.

ISD Zoning Is Address-Specific

Carroll ISD serves eastern Westlake; Keller ISD serves western Westlake (including Vaquero). All residents may apply to Westlake Academy via lottery. Verify your specific address at southlakecarroll.edu or kellerisd.net before making any offer.

Where to Live

Westlake has no apartment complexes, no starter-home subdivisions, and no "affordable" entry point — the town is almost entirely composed of gated estate communities. Every neighborhood here is luxury by definition. The meaningful distinctions come down to community feel (golf-and-club lifestyle vs. wooded privacy), lot size (1–5+ acres vs. standard estate), ISD zone (Carroll vs. Keller), and price tier ($2M entry vs. $5M+ custom). Here's the honest breakdown of the town's main residential communities.

Market & Money

Westlake is not a value-per-square-foot play — it's an elite-schools, ultimate-privacy, tax-efficiency play for a specific buyer profile. The median home price of ~$4.9M exceeds even San Francisco Bay Area medians, so the "cost of living" comparison works differently here than it does for most DFW suburbs. The financial argument for Westlake is almost entirely about income tax savings for high earners and the school premium that would otherwise be paid for private education.

Family Life

What Life Actually Looks Like in Westlake

Vaquero Club

Westlake's private membership community features the Vaquero Club — a gated golf and social enclave with a PGA-caliber course, equestrian facilities, tennis, resort-style pool, and club dining. A luxury lifestyle without ever leaving your neighborhood.

Southlake Town Square — 5 min

Westlake itself has no commercial district — and doesn't need one. Southlake Town Square is a 5-minute drive: walkable open-air retail, Whole Foods, dozens of restaurants, a luxury movie theater, weekly farmers market, and year-round event programming.

Bob Jones & Bear Creek Trails

Bob Jones Nature Center (Southlake, 5 min) offers 738 acres of forested trails and wildlife habitat. Keller's Bear Creek Park, Southlake's Bicentennial Park, and Trophy Club Park are all within 10 minutes. Genuine outdoor access from an estate setting.

Westlake Academy Arts & IB Culture

Westlake Academy's full K–12 IB curriculum creates an intellectual community throughout the town. Fine arts, music, theater, and inquiry-based learning define student life here — and that energy shapes the broader neighborhood character in a way that's uncommon in DFW suburbs.

14 Minutes to DFW Airport

Westlake has DFW's best residential runway to the airport. For C-suite executives, consultants, and military families managing frequent travel, the ability to leave home at 7:45am for an 8:55am flight is genuinely rare in suburban DFW.

Small-Town Intimacy

With ~1,500 residents and no commercial development inside the town proper, Westlake functions more like a private estate community. Neighbors know each other. HOA culture is active. Town events are intimate. This is what people who've outgrown "big suburb energy" actually want.

The honest tradeoff: Westlake's lifestyle is exceptional — but it's outsourced. You'll drive to Southlake or Keller for groceries, dinner, coffee, and kids' activities. The town itself is quiet by design. Families who thrive here want the private, estate-like feel and are comfortable car-centric. If walkable neighborhood energy matters to your family's daily rhythm, Southlake is a better fit for the main residence.

Parks, Trails & Green Space

Westlake has limited public park infrastructure — the town's character is intentionally low-density and undeveloped. Circle T Ranch preserves open space with natural landscape, longhorn cattle, and ranch-style land woven between corporate campuses. For structured parks and trails, residents rely on adjacent communities.

Bob Jones Nature Center (Southlake)

738-acre nature preserve with 4+ miles of forested hiking and mountain biking trails, a nature education center, and meadow habitats. One of the finest preserved natural areas in Northeast Tarrant County — 5 minutes from central Westlake.

Bear Creek Park (Keller)

Keller's flagship park — athletic fields, splash pad, miles of connected trails, and picnic areas. Connected to the Bear Creek Greenbelt corridor. About 8 minutes from Westlake via Ottinger Road.

Southlake Bicentennial Park

9 acres with playgrounds, tennis courts, sand volleyball, a pavilion, and trail connections. Hosts a Saturday farmers market during spring and fall. One of Southlake's most-used community parks, 5 minutes from Westlake.

Trophy Club Park & Lake Grapevine

Trophy Club Park sits on Lake Grapevine's shoreline — fishing, kayaking, and boating access for families with waterfront interests. Roanoke's developing trail network adds additional options within 10–15 minutes.

Crime & Safety

How Safe Is Westlake?

Westlake is a very safe community. A town of ~1,500 people with gated enclaves, private security in some neighborhoods, and essentially no commercial traffic through the residential core means absolute crime numbers are extremely low. Niche rates it B for crime and safety — lower than neighboring Southlake and Carroll ISD communities — primarily because of small sample size, not any meaningful crime concern. A single incident in a 1,500-person town can move per-capita statistics significantly.

Honest Assessment

Pros & Cons of Living in Westlake

I won't oversell Westlake. Very few families belong here — and those who do know exactly why they're paying for it.

✓ What Westlake Does Well

  • #1 schools in Texas — Carroll ISD (95/100 TEA) and Westlake Academy (public K–12 IB) give Westlake the strongest school combination in DFW.
  • Privacy and exclusivity — Gated enclaves, estate-sized lots, no commercial traffic, no drive-through development. Genuinely private.
  • Corporate campuses next door — Fidelity and Schwab employees live 5 minutes from work, with DFW Airport 14 minutes the other direction.
  • No state income tax — For C-suite and executive earners from CA, NY, or IL, Texas's zero income tax is a six-figure annual financial advantage.
  • Access without isolation — Southlake, Keller, Trophy Club, and Fort Worth are 10–30 minutes. Seclusion without sacrifice.
  • DFW Airport 14 min — Best residential runway to DFW in all of North Texas. Daily-life advantage for frequent travelers.
  • Long-term prestige — Westlake's cachet as an ultra-luxury address has grown consistently. Adjacent Fortune 500 campus investment continues.

— What to Weigh Carefully

  • Ultra-thin resale market — ~24 sales/year means fragile comparables, price volatility, and longer listing windows (96 days avg). Selling is a deliberate process.
  • Off-market dependency — Many transactions never touch the MLS. Without an agent with off-market relationships, you'll miss inventory.
  • No walkability, no amenities in town — No grocery store, coffee shop, or restaurant inside Westlake. You are always driving.
  • Split ISD complexity — Carroll ISD (east) vs. Keller ISD (west) depends on exact address. Westlake Academy is lottery admission — not guaranteed by residency.
  • Property tax at $4M+ — Carroll ISD zone (~$1.30/$100): ~$52,000/yr. Keller ISD zone (~$1.46/$100): ~$58,000/yr on a $4M home.
  • No public recreation — No city parks system, no community rec center. Non-Vaquero families rely on Southlake/Keller facilities.
  • HOA standards are rigorous — Gated communities take architectural and maintenance standards seriously. Review CC&Rs carefully before offer.

Nearby: Sabre Corporation HQ (Southlake, 5 min), American Airlines Group HQ (Fort Worth, 27 min · 13,000+ employees), Nokia (Southlake), Bell Textron (Fort Worth, 30 min), and Alcon Laboratories (Fort Worth, 25 min).

Westlake has four primary residential communities. Vaquero is the most prestigious — guard-gated golf and equestrian community in the Keller ISD zone, largest lots, highest price points, most amenities. Glenwyck Farms is in Carroll ISD — semi-custom and custom estates at $2–5M+, known for tree-lined streets and strong neighborhood cohesion. Terra Bella offers newer custom construction in Carroll ISD at slightly more accessible price points. Paigebrooke Farms / Aspen Lane is smaller and somewhat more approachable. All four have very limited inventory — off-market relationships matter here.

Southlake and Westlake are two of DFW's top family destinations, but they serve different priorities. Southlake is a full-service town — walkable Town Square, Carroll ISD (same district for east-zone Westlake), city parks, recreation centers, and a broader price range ($900K–$5M). Westlake is more exclusive and private — smaller, no commercial development, Westlake Academy option, Fidelity/Schwab employment, but no walkable amenities of its own. Southlake is the better choice if neighborhood energy and daily walkability matter. Westlake is the better choice if you're paying specifically for schools, privacy, and employer proximity.

Westlake's market is among the thinnest in DFW — roughly 24 single-family sales per year (about two per month). Median sale price is approximately $4.9M; days on market average 96 days. With 16.5 months of supply it's technically a buyer's market, but that reflects a small qualified buyer pool — not distress or overpricing. Many transactions happen off-market. If you're considering a purchase here, working with an agent who has established off-market relationships inside the town's agent community is not optional — it's how inventory gets found.
