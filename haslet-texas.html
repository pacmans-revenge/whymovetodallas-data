Getting Your Bearings

Where Is Haslet, Texas?

Haslet sits at the northern end of Tarrant County, just west of US-287 and north of Fort Worth — roughly 18 miles from Downtown Fort Worth and 6 miles from the Alliance Corridor, one of the most productive logistics and light industrial hubs in the country. The city borders Rhome and Newark to the north, Justin and Northlake to the east, and the broader Fort Worth metro to the south and west.

What makes Haslet interesting for relocating families is its position: you're close enough to Fort Worth's employment base for a reasonable daily commute, but living in a quieter, more rural-feeling environment than you'd get in Keller, Roanoke, or North Richland Hills. The Alliance Corridor — home to over 600 businesses and 73,000+ jobs — is practically in your backyard. For families with a parent at FedEx, Amazon, BNSF, or the dozens of companies that have planted their logistics operations north of Fort Worth, the commute math is simply hard to beat.

Education

Northwest ISD — Schools in Haslet

The vast majority of Haslet's residential developments — Northstar, LeTara, Avondale, Van Zandt Farms, and most newer construction — are zoned to Northwest ISD. Some older portions of the city near the eastern boundary are served by Eagle Mountain-Saginaw ISD. Always verify your specific address before making an offer — both districts are strong, but your school assignment depends entirely on where the parcel lands.

Family Life

What Life Actually Looks Like in Haslet

Youth Sports Culture

Haslet Sports Association has organized youth baseball, softball, basketball, flag football, and volleyball here since 1994. Eaton youth athletics programs are strong. Travel leagues operate out of Alliance-area complexes. The activity level is high — in the best way.

Trails & Outdoor Space

Northstar and Sendera Ranch have miles of internal trail networks. Lake Bridgeport is 35 minutes west for weekend boating and fishing. Haslet rewards families who want outdoor space and breathing room without the cost of true rural living.

Alliance Town Center Shopping

Alliance Town Center — 10–12 minutes away — is the closest major retail hub, anchored by Target, Best Buy, and a solid dining lineup. Keller Crossing (20 min) and Golden Triangle Mall (20 min) fill out the shopping options. Not walking distance, but a quick drive.

Community Events & Western Roots

Haslet Western Days is the town's signature annual event — rodeo, parade, and a community feel that feels genuinely Texan, not manufactured. NW ISD school events are well-attended. The community is tightly knit in the way smaller towns often are.

The Alliance Edge

Living next to one of the nation's most productive logistics corridors isn't just about the commute — it also means infrastructure investment, road maintenance, and long-term growth pressure that tends to push residential values up. Haslet is positioned for continued appreciation as Alliance expands northward.

Easy to Connect

With 92% owner-occupancy and a high concentration of families in the same life stage — working parents, school-age kids — it's genuinely easy to meet people in Haslet. Master-planned HOAs facilitate block events. New neighbors are the norm, not the exception.

Honest tradeoff: Haslet trades urban energy for space and quiet. You are not walking to coffee shops or restaurants — that requires a 15-minute drive, minimum. If you're coming from a city neighborhood with walkable amenities, that adjustment is real. Families who thrive here are those who want their yard, their trails, and their neighborhood to be the center of life — with Fort Worth 20 minutes away when they want it.
Getting Outside

Recreation, Walkability & City Amenities

Haslet is car-dependent — that's the starting point for any honest conversation about this suburb. With a Walk Score of 26, daily errands require a vehicle, and there is no public transit. What Haslet offers instead is something different: master-planned community trails, generous lot sizes, and quick highway access to Fort Worth's full recreation infrastructure.

Parks, Trails & Green Space

Haslet has a small municipal parks system, but the master-planned communities more than compensate. Northstar and Sendera Ranch each have extensive internal trail networks, pocket parks, and green corridors that residents use constantly. Fort Worth's broader parks infrastructure is 20 minutes south and considerably larger.

Northstar Trail Network

Miles of paved hike-and-bike paths winding through the Northstar community, connecting pocket parks, playgrounds, and green spaces. Well-maintained and used heavily on evenings and weekends by residents of all ages.

Sendera Ranch Amenity Center

The community amenity center at Sendera Ranch includes pools, tennis courts, walking trails, playgrounds, and organized resident events throughout the year. One of the best community facility sets in northwest Tarrant County.

Lake Bridgeport Access

About 35 minutes west, Lake Bridgeport offers fishing, boating, camping, and swimming. Walnut Creek Park on the lake provides public boat ramp access. A popular weekend destination for Haslet families who want water recreation.

Avondale Greenbelt Corridors

Avondale's community plan included greenbelts along its borders — walking paths and open lawn areas used by residents daily. Not a structured park, but a genuine outdoor amenity built into the neighborhood fabric.

LeTara & Van Zandt Farms Open Space

Newer luxury communities in Haslet were developed with intentional open space between home sites. LeTara and Van Zandt Farms have walking paths and larger private lot setbacks, giving residents more breathing room than typical suburban development.

Fort Worth Parks (20 min)

The Fort Worth Nature Center & Refuge, Trinity Trails (40+ miles), Botanic Garden, and Benbrook Lake are all accessible in 20–30 minutes. Haslet residents effectively have a much larger parks system just south of them.

The Honest Assessment

Pros & Cons of Living in Haslet, Texas

✓ What Haslet Does Well

  • Alliance Corridor access — If you work at FedEx, Amazon, BNSF, or one of 600+ Alliance employers, your commute is 8–12 minutes. That's extremely rare in DFW.
  • Northwest ISD schools — Solid A- from Niche, TEA B (81/100), and strong CTE pathways. Not the flashiest name in DFW, but a genuinely good district.
  • Very low city tax rate — At ~$0.1461/$100, Haslet's city rate is among the lowest in Tarrant County. Your combined tax bill is meaningfully lower than in Keller, Southlake, or Colleyville.
  • Master-planned communities — Northstar, Sendera Ranch, and others have trails, pools, and green space built in — you don't have to drive to find amenities.
  • Very safe — 2.4 violent crimes per 1,000 — well below the national average of 4.0. High owner-occupancy, stable community.
  • $506K median opens real value — At this price point in Haslet, you're getting 2,400–3,600 sq ft, a yard, a garage, and community amenities — not a townhome in a compromised location.
  • Growing infrastructure — Alliance's continued northward expansion means roads, retail, and services around Haslet are improving steadily.

— What to Weigh

  • Completely car-dependent — Walk Score 26. Every errand, every meal out, every school pickup — driving is not optional. Budget for two vehicles and fuel.
  • Limited in-town dining and shopping — There is no meaningful restaurant or retail district in Haslet proper. Alliance Town Center (10–12 min) and Keller (20 min) are your real options.
  • Small municipal parks system — Haslet ranks near the bottom in DFW for parkland per capita. The community trails compensate, but a city parks system this size won't satisfy families who want diverse public open space.
  • NW Tarrant commute premium to East Dallas/Plano employers — If your office is at Legacy West or in East Dallas, expect 45–60 minutes each way. This suburb rewards the right commute direction.
  • Limited nightlife and urban energy — Downtown Fort Worth is 20–25 minutes, but Haslet itself has none. If you want walkable bars, live music, or city street life, you'll need to leave.
  • Smaller city services — A city of ~3,280 incorporated residents has limited public programming. For larger city services — major hospital, big public library, aquatic center — you're driving to Fort Worth or Keller.
