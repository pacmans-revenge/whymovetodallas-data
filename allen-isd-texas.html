Allen ISD serves the city of Allen, Texas — a mature, family-centric suburb in Collin County roughly 25 miles north of downtown Dallas. With approximately 22,000 students across 26 campuses, the district has earned a TEA rating of A (score: 91) and a Niche grade of A+ for 2024–25, placing it consistently among the strongest school systems in the Dallas–Fort Worth metro.

The district is anchored by Allen High School — one of the largest single-campus high schools in the state of Texas, with enrollment around 5,800 students. Far from being lost in the crowd, Allen HS carries a national reputation built on academic rigor (IB Diploma, 30+ AP courses), arts excellence (the Allen Eagle Escadrille Band), and championship-level athletics. For families who want the full range of a big-school environment paired with legitimate academic depth, Allen ISD delivers both.

Relocation Snapshot

Allen ISD is a strong choice for families who prioritize academic breadth and extracurricular depth in a stable, well-funded suburban district. The primary service area is the city of Allen. Portions of Plano, Lucas, and Fairview may fall within Allen ISD boundaries at the edges — always verify your specific address with the TEA School District Locator before purchasing.

TEA Rating & Academic Performance

Texas assigns each school district an A–F accountability rating each year through the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Allen ISD earned an A rating with a score of 91 for the 2024–25 school year — a consistent result that reflects strong performance across all three state accountability domains.

How the TEA Rating Is Calculated

TEA ratings are built on three domains:

  • Student Achievement — STAAR test scores, graduation rates, college/career readiness
  • School Progress — year-over-year growth for all students
  • Closing the Gaps — performance equity across student subgroups

Allen ISD's score of 91 puts it near the top of the Collin County ISD peer group. For context: a score of 90–100 earns an A; 80–89 earns a B. Very few large suburban ISDs in Texas sustain a 90+ score year over year.

Niche Grade: A+

Niche.com grades Allen ISD an A+ overall, combining academic data with parent and student survey results. Among Niche's category grades, Allen ISD earns high marks for academics, teachers, college preparation, and extracurricular activities. Niche and TEA ratings typically align for Allen ISD — a good sign that the data and the community experience point in the same direction.

Per-pupil expenditure is approximately $13,200 annually — above the Texas state average, reflecting the district's investment in staffing, facilities, and program depth. The district carries a TEA FIRST (Financial Integrity Rating System of Texas) rating of Superior, the highest financial tier, meaning state funds are managed responsibly and transparently.

Academics & College Preparation

Allen ISD consistently produces college-ready graduates. The district's graduation rate of approximately 97% is above both Texas and national averages. More importantly, the percentage of graduates who are college or career ready — based on SAT/ACT performance, AP exam scores, CTE certification, and dual-credit completion — reflects a district that takes post-secondary outcomes seriously at scale.

Advanced Placement

Allen High School offers more than 30 AP courses, one of the largest AP catalogs among single-campus high schools in Texas. AP enrollment and exam participation rates are high — students are expected to challenge themselves, not just enroll in AP classes as a checkbox. The district's AP pass rates (scores of 3 or higher) are consistently above state averages.

IB Diploma Programme

Allen High School is an authorized IB World School offering the full IB Diploma Programme for students in grades 11–12. The IB Diploma is an internationally recognized rigorous two-year curriculum that includes extended essays, theory of knowledge, and community service alongside six higher-level academic subjects. IB students in Allen ISD gain access to a cohort-based academic community within the larger high school — a meaningful distinction at a campus of nearly 6,000 students.

Allen Collegiate High School (ACHHS)

Allen ISD operates the Allen Collegiate High School, an early college high school in partnership with Collin College. ACHHS students earn both a high school diploma and significant college credit — in some cases, an associate degree — at no tuition cost to families. This is one of the most compelling academic offerings in the district for students who know they want to accelerate into college coursework.

Dual Credit — Collin College Partnership

Beyond ACHHS, Allen ISD students across the district can access dual credit courses through Collin College. Eligible courses count simultaneously toward high school graduation requirements and college transcript credit — a cost-effective way to enter college with hours already completed.

Key Campuses

Allen ISD operates 26 campuses: 15 elementary schools, 5 middle schools, 1 high school, the Lowery Freshman Center, and several specialized campuses including the Allen Collegiate High School and the Billy Bolin Early Childhood Center. Most campuses carry TEA ratings of A or B.

Allen High School

Grades 10–12 ~5,800 Students IB World School UIL 6A

One of the largest single-campus high schools in Texas. Home to the IB Diploma Programme, 30+ AP courses, the nationally recognized Allen Eagle Escadrille Band, and Eagle Stadium (18,000-seat capacity). Multiple state championship programs in football, swimming, tennis, and track. A full-scale university-like environment with deep academic and extracurricular infrastructure.

Lowery Freshman Center

Grade 9 Dedicated Freshman Campus

A dedicated campus for ninth-graders, designed to ease the transition from middle school to the larger high school environment. Ninth-grade students attend Lowery rather than the main Allen HS campus, giving them a structured, smaller setting before stepping into the full high school experience as sophomores.

Allen Collegiate High School (ACHHS)

Grades 9–12 Early College Collin College Partnership

An early college high school where students earn both a diploma and substantial college credit — potentially an associate degree — at no cost to families. Separate admissions process; not automatic based on address. Designed for academically motivated students who want to accelerate their path to college completion.

Curtis Middle School

Grades 6–8 TEA Rated

One of five middle schools in Allen ISD. Like all Allen ISD middle schools, Curtis offers GT (Gifted and Talented) instruction, pre-AP coursework, athletics, fine arts, and CTE introductory pathways. Middle school in Allen ISD is designed to build academic readiness for the rigorous high school course catalog.

Elementary Schools (15 Campuses)

Grades PK–5 Majority TEA A-Rated

Allen ISD's 15 elementary schools are distributed across the city's neighborhoods. The majority carry TEA A ratings. Elementary campuses offer GT identification and services, fine arts integration, STEM programming through Project Lead The Way, and campus-level family engagement programs. Attendance zone is determined by home address.

Campus Assignment

Elementary and middle school campus assignments in Allen ISD are determined by attendance zone, which is tied to your home address. As Allen is a built-out city with relatively stable enrollment, boundary redraws are less common than in fast-growing ISDs like Prosper or Celina. Verify your specific campus assignment at allenisd.org.

Signature Programs & Pathways

Allen ISD's program breadth is one of its distinguishing characteristics. Where many districts are strong in academics alone, Allen ISD has intentionally built depth across academic acceleration, fine arts, STEM, and career and technical education.

IB Diploma Programme

Authorized IB World School at Allen High School. Full two-year Diploma Programme for grades 11–12, including extended essay, Theory of Knowledge, and CAS (Creativity, Activity, Service). Internationally recognized credential with significant college credit potential at IB-friendly universities.

Advanced Placement (AP)

30+ AP courses offered at Allen High School — one of the largest AP catalogs in North Texas. High participation rates and above-average pass rates. Strong College Board institutional relationships, with AP vertical alignment starting in middle school through pre-AP courses.

Gifted and Talented (GT)

GT identification and services at every level: elementary pull-out enrichment, middle school GT classes and pre-AP placement, and high school IB/AP pathways. GT testing typically occurs in second grade, with retesting available each year through district request. Out-of-state GT documentation is evaluated by the district's GT coordinator upon enrollment.

Project Lead The Way (PLTW)

STEM and engineering pathways through PLTW at both the elementary and secondary level. Elementary students engage in hands-on Design and Modeling; secondary students can pursue Pathway to Engineering, Biomedical Science, or Computer Science sequences. Strong feeder into engineering and STEM programs at Texas universities.

Allen Eagle Escadrille Band

The Allen Eagle Escadrille Marching Band is one of the most decorated marching bands in the United States. National championship titles, BOA (Bands of America) recognition, and a rehearsal and performance culture that rivals college programs. Fine arts at Allen ISD — orchestra, choir, theater, visual arts — are built on a district-wide culture of excellence that starts in elementary school.

Career and Technical Education (CTE)

30+ CTE pathways across 16 career clusters, including Health Science, Business, Architecture and Construction, Information Technology, and more. Students can earn industry certifications, dual credit, and workforce credentials alongside their diploma. ACHHS students can layer CTE with college coursework for maximum post-graduation flexibility.

Dual Credit — Collin College

Allen ISD's formal partnership with Collin College gives qualifying students access to dual credit courses that count toward both the high school transcript and the Collin College transcript. Tuition is typically reduced or covered through district agreements. Separate from ACHHS — available to eligible students across the district.

Fine Arts (District-Wide)

Band, orchestra, choir, theater, visual art, and dance programs at every level. Allen ISD's commitment to fine arts is institutional — not a budget afterthought. Award-winning ensembles at multiple campuses. The Escadrille sets a cultural expectation of excellence that permeates fine arts across the district.

Special Education & Support Services

Allen ISD provides a full continuum of special education services as required under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). The district operates Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committees that determine each student's Individualized Education Program (IEP) based on evaluation results and parent input.

Service Continuum

Allen ISD offers the full IDEA service continuum, including:

  • Inclusion / co-teach — SpEd students in general education classrooms with support
  • Resource room — small-group instruction in specific skill areas
  • Self-contained settings — for students requiring more structured environments
  • Life Skills programs — functional academic and daily living skills curriculum
  • Transition services — post-secondary planning beginning at age 16 (or earlier per IEP)

Related services include speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, physical therapy, orientation and mobility, assistive technology, and counseling, depending on individual student needs as determined by the ARD committee.

Out-of-State IEP Transfers

If your child has an active IEP from another state, Texas law requires Allen ISD to convene an ARD meeting within 30 days of enrollment to review the existing IEP and determine comparability. The district must provide services during this review period. Texas uses different eligibility categories than some states — the ARD may result in a new evaluation or an amended IEP. Bring all prior IEP documentation, evaluation reports, and progress notes to the enrollment appointment.

For SpEd Families

Contact Allen ISD's Special Education Department directly before enrollment if your child has an IEP, 504 plan, or pending evaluation: allenisd.org. Request a pre-enrollment meeting with the campus special education staff. The 30-day ARD window begins at the date of enrollment, so the sooner you engage, the sooner services are in place.

Section 504

Students who do not qualify for special education services but have a documented disability that substantially limits a major life activity may be eligible for a 504 Accommodation Plan. 504 plans are managed at the campus level. Out-of-state 504 plans are reviewed and re-evaluated by the campus 504 coordinator upon enrollment.

Allen ISD Athletics

Allen ISD competes at the UIL 6A level — the highest classification in Texas high school athletics. For student-athletes, Allen ISD offers one of the most competitive and well-resourced athletic environments in the state.

Allen Eagles Football

Allen Eagle football is one of the marquee programs in Texas prep sports. The Eagles have won multiple UIL 6A Division II state championships and consistently compete at the highest level of Texas high school football. Home games are played at Eagle Stadium, a purpose-built facility with a seating capacity of approximately 18,000 — larger than many small-college stadiums. The culture around Allen football is community-wide; home game attendance is a significant part of Allen family life, especially in the fall.

Other Championship Programs

Football is far from the only story at Allen. The Eagles field competitive programs across virtually every UIL sport, with state-level recognition in:

  • Swimming and Diving — consistently one of the top programs in 6A
  • Tennis — team and individual state competitors
  • Track and Field — state qualifiers across multiple events
  • Basketball, Baseball, Soccer, Volleyball — deep playoff runs in most years

Athletic Facilities

Eagle Stadium anchors the athletic facilities picture, but Allen ISD also maintains high-quality competition facilities for baseball, softball, tennis, aquatics, and soccer. Student-athletes have access to strength and conditioning programs, athletic trainers, and coaching staffs with significant resources behind them.

For Student-Athletes

UIL eligibility rules apply: students must be enrolled at Allen ISD schools and meet academic standards to compete. Transfer students may face UIL transfer eligibility waiting periods depending on circumstances. If your student-athlete is relocating mid-season, contact the Allen ISD Athletic Department early to understand eligibility timelines. The UIL official transfer and eligibility rules are at uiltexas.org.

School Choice & Open Enrollment

Allen ISD is an attendance-zone based district. Your child's campus assignment for elementary and middle school is determined by the boundaries of your home address — not by preference or application.

Intra-District Transfers

Allen ISD does offer intra-district transfer requests on a limited basis. Transfers are approved at the district's discretion and depend on available campus capacity. They are not guaranteed. Reasons for approval may include documented hardship, childcare arrangements, or sibling placement. Contact Allen ISD's Student Services department for current intra-district transfer policies and application windows, which typically open in spring for the following school year.

Out-of-District Open Enrollment

Open enrollment for students who live outside Allen ISD boundaries is limited and not guaranteed. Allen ISD is a high-demand district — capacity is typically consumed by in-boundary students. Families who want their children to attend Allen ISD should purchase a home within the district's boundaries rather than relying on open-enrollment approval.

Allen Collegiate High School

ACHHS has a separate admissions process and is not assigned by attendance zone. Interested students within Allen ISD apply through a competitive admissions process. Seats are limited. The ACHHS application timeline and requirements are available at allenisd.org.

Verifying Your Attendance Zone

Before purchasing a home you believe is in Allen ISD, verify the address using two methods: (1) the TEA School District Locator, and (2) the Allen ISD campus finder at allenisd.org. Boundary lines near Plano, Lucas, and Fairview can be counterintuitive — always check the specific parcel, not the city name.

District Finances & Tax Rate

Understanding Allen ISD's tax rate is important for any family buying a home in Allen — it is the largest single component of your annual Texas property tax bill.

Current Tax Rate

Allen ISD's total tax rate is $1.13 per $100 of assessed value for 2024–25. For a home assessed at $550,000, that means approximately $6,215 in annual school district taxes — before the homestead exemption. Texas homeowners who claim a homestead exemption receive a $100,000 reduction in the assessed value for school district tax purposes, which brings that figure down meaningfully.

How Allen ISD Compares

Allen ISD's $1.13 rate is above some neighboring Collin County ISDs but below others. For context:

  • Frisco ISD: $1.03 per $100
  • Coppell ISD: $1.00 per $100
  • Allen ISD: $1.13 per $100
  • McKinney ISD: approximately $1.17 per $100
  • Prosper ISD: $1.26 per $100

A higher tax rate does not automatically mean worse value — it reflects bond investments in facilities and the cost structure of the district. Allen ISD's FIRST Superior financial rating means that higher rate translates into well-managed, high-outcome programs rather than administrative inefficiency.

TEA FIRST Rating: Superior

The Texas FIRST (Financial Integrity Rating System of Texas) program evaluates school district financial management annually. Allen ISD holds a Superior rating — the highest tier — indicating that the district is managing public education funds with integrity, transparency, and effectiveness. Families paying Allen ISD taxes can be confident that those dollars are being stewarded responsibly.

Bond History

Allen ISD has passed multiple bond packages over the past two decades, funding facilities including Eagle Stadium, campus renovations, and technology upgrades. Allen is a largely built-out city at this point — unlike Prosper or Celina, which are building entirely new campuses each year — so bond needs are more focused on maintenance and upgrades than on rapid expansion. This contributes to the district's financial stability relative to fast-growth ISDs.

Tax Calculation Example

$600,000 home assessed value
Minus $100,000 homestead exemption = $500,000 taxable value
x $1.13 per $100 = $5,650 / year in Allen ISD school taxes
Additional county, city, MUD, and other taxing entity rates apply on top of this figure. Always verify current rates with the Collin County Appraisal District (CCAD) at collincad.org.

Enrolling in Allen ISD

Allen ISD uses an online enrollment portal accessible through allenisd.org. Enrollment windows for the upcoming school year typically open in the spring. Mid-year enrollment is accepted on a rolling basis and is common for relocating families.

Required Documents

  • Proof of residency — lease agreement, closing disclosure, or two recent utility bills in a parent/guardian's name showing the Allen address
  • Immunization records — Texas-required vaccines per DSHS schedule (your child does not need Texas-specific records if immunizations are current; a prior-state record is accepted)
  • Birth certificate — certified copy
  • Prior school records — most recent report card, transcript, standardized test scores
  • IEP / 504 documentation — if applicable; bring all evaluation reports, not just the summary document
  • GT identification records — if your child has been identified as GT in another state, bring documentation for district review

Timeline for Relocating Families

If you are closing on a home in Allen between April and August, enroll as soon as closing documents are available. High-demand elementary campuses can fill GT clusters and certain program offerings early. For families moving mid-year, enrollment can typically be completed within a few business days once all documents are submitted.

Campus Assignment

Your elementary and middle school campus is assigned automatically based on your home address. Allen High School (or Lowery Freshman Center for 9th grade) serves all in-boundary students regardless of neighborhood. If you are interested in ACHHS, note that it has a separate and selective admissions process — enrollment in Allen ISD does not automatically include ACHHS placement.

Enrollment Contact

Allen ISD Student Services: allenisd.org | 612 E. Bethany Drive, Allen, TX 75002. For special education, IEP transfers, or GT testing inquiries, request the appropriate department coordinator at the main enrollment contact.

Allen ISD — Frequently Asked Questions

Is Allen ISD really one of the best school districts in Texas? ⌄
Yes, by most objective measures. Allen ISD holds a TEA rating of A (score: 91) and a Niche grade of A+ for 2024–25. Its graduation rate of ~97% and its college/career readiness metrics place it in the top tier of Texas suburban ISDs. The IB Diploma Programme, 30+ AP courses, and ACHHS early college program provide academic depth that most districts — including larger ones — cannot match at the same breadth. For families who value both academic rigor and extracurricular excellence, Allen ISD consistently delivers.
Does every Allen student go to Allen High School? ⌄
Essentially yes — Allen ISD has a single comprehensive high school, Allen High School (grades 10–12), plus the Lowery Freshman Center for ninth-graders. The only alternative is the Allen Collegiate High School (ACHHS), which is a separate early college campus with selective admissions. If your child is not admitted to ACHHS, they will attend Lowery (grade 9) and then Allen HS (grades 10–12) like all other in-boundary students. The size of Allen HS (roughly 5,800 students) is a real consideration — some families love the breadth and opportunity; others prefer a smaller school environment. Allen ISD is not the right fit if a small high school is a non-negotiable.
How do I verify that a specific home address is in Allen ISD? ⌄
Use two methods: (1) The TEA School District Locator at tea.texas.gov — plug in the full address and it returns the ISD. (2) The Collin County Appraisal District parcel search at collincad.org — every property record lists all taxing jurisdictions, including the school district. Never assume a home in the city of Allen is automatically in Allen ISD — boundary lines near Plano, Fairview, and Lucas can cross city lines. Always verify the specific parcel before making a purchase decision based on school district assignment.
My child has an IEP from another state. What happens when we enroll? ⌄
Texas law requires Allen ISD to convene an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of your child's enrollment to review the out-of-state IEP. During that 30-day window, the district must provide comparable services. After the ARD, the district may adopt the existing IEP, amend it, or initiate a new evaluation if they believe the current data does not support the existing services. Bring all evaluation reports, not just the IEP summary document — the more documentation you provide upfront, the smoother the ARD process. Contact Allen ISD's Special Education Department before your enrollment date to give them advance notice and begin the process proactively.
How does Allen ISD's tax rate affect my home purchase budget? ⌄
Allen ISD's tax rate of $1.13 per $100 of assessed value is a meaningful line item in your annual cost of living. On a $600,000 home with the homestead exemption applied, you would owe approximately $5,650 per year in Allen ISD school taxes alone — roughly $471 per month in your effective housing cost. That figure comes on top of your mortgage payment, homeowners insurance, city and county taxes, and any HOA or MUD fees. When comparing Allen ISD to Frisco ISD ($1.03) or Coppell ISD ($1.00), the difference on a $600,000 home is roughly $600–$780 per year. Not the deciding factor for most families, but worth building into your budget comparison. I always run this math with relocation clients before we narrow a home search to a specific ISD.
