Grand Prairie ISD Overview

25,500+ Students Enrolled
37 Campuses
C TEA Grade (2023)
B Niche Grade (3rd party)
93.9% Graduation Rate
$1.06 Tax Rate / $100 (2025)

Grand Prairie ISD is a mid-size urban district serving approximately 25,500 students across 37 campuses in the Dallas County portion of Grand Prairie. If you are evaluating GPISD as a relocating family, the most important thing to understand upfront is this: the district's overall TEA grade of C (2023) does not define every student's experience here. The district operates a genuinely unusual portfolio of specialty and choice schools — including a high school ranked 6th in all of Texas by U.S. News — alongside its neighborhood campuses. Whether GPISD is the right fit depends heavily on which programs your child can access, not just the district average.

GPISD covers 58 square miles entirely within Dallas County. It serves the Dallas County portion of Grand Prairie — a distinction that matters a great deal at the home-purchase stage, because a Grand Prairie mailing address does not guarantee enrollment. Homes in the Tarrant County portion of Grand Prairie feed to Arlington ISD, not GPISD. We cover this in full in the Boundary section below, but it belongs in the first paragraph of any honest overview.

The district's strongest differentiators are its choice and specialty program ecosystem: a dedicated career high school (Dubiski), a 6-12 Collegiate Institute ranked among the state's best, a fine arts academy, early college pathways at two traditional high schools, and a School for the Highly Gifted at the elementary level. Families who can identify and access the right program for their child will find genuine opportunity here. Families who need a strong districtwide baseline across all neighborhood campuses should weigh the TEA data carefully alongside neighboring options like Mansfield ISD.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Have a student who qualifies for or can apply to a specialty program (Collegiate Institute, Fine Arts Academy, School for the Highly Gifted)
  • Are prioritizing value — Grand Prairie offers lower housing costs than Mansfield or Arlington for families who want DFW's mid-cities area
  • Have a student with strong CTE or career-pathway interests (30+ pathways at Dubiski and the traditional high schools)
  • Are comfortable verifying their specific address feeds to a particular campus before buying
  • Want early college credit options — both Grand Prairie HS and South Grand Prairie HS offer Early College HS programs

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • Your primary criterion is a districtwide TEA A or B rating across all campuses
  • You need consistent high performance at any neighborhood campus you might be assigned to, without applying to a choice program
  • You are planning to purchase in Tarrant County Grand Prairie (those homes feed to Arlington ISD, not GPISD)
  • You prefer a smaller district where students move through one elementary, one middle, and one high school together
⚠ Boundary Verification — Read Before You Make an Offer

Grand Prairie ISD serves only the Dallas County portion of Grand Prairie. A Grand Prairie address in Tarrant County zoned to Arlington ISD. Always verify your specific property at gpisd.org or via the TEA school locator before making a purchase decision. This line runs through the middle of Grand Prairie and does not follow obvious geographic boundaries.

TEA Accountability Rating

Grand Prairie ISD received a C grade with a score of 74 in the most recently published TEA A-F accountability system. Note: TEA ratings were delayed for the 2024 cycle due to legal proceedings; check txschools.gov for the most current published rating at time of your search.

Texas measures districts across three accountability domains. Here is what each means in practice for Grand Prairie ISD:

Student Achievement — Measures STAAR passing rates, graduation, and College, Career, and Military Readiness (CCMR). This is the headline metric most families reference. A district-level C here typically reflects uneven performance across campuses — some well above the state average, some below. It does not mean every campus in GPISD performs the same way.

School Progress — Measures whether students are growing year over year relative to academic peers. A school can score higher on Progress than Achievement, reflecting that students are gaining ground even if they haven't yet crossed proficiency thresholds. This domain matters especially when evaluating schools serving populations with higher concentrations of students catching up academically.

Closing the Gaps — Measures whether performance gaps between student groups are narrowing. Urban districts with diverse populations are often measured most critically on this domain.

Practical Takeaway for Families

A district-level C means the average across all 37 campuses is C-range — but the range within that average is wide. Grand Prairie Collegiate Institute, ranked 6th in Texas by U.S. News, sits in the same district as neighborhood schools with much lower individual ratings. Evaluating GPISD means understanding which campus your child would actually attend — not just what the district headline says.

Academics & Outcomes

Grand Prairie ISD's graduation rate is 93.9% — above the national average of 86.5%, which is a meaningful data point in a district often dismissed on the basis of its TEA grade alone. High school completion matters as much as any test score for a family focused on long-term student outcomes.

STAAR & Proficiency: Districtwide STAAR passing rates are below the state average on reading and math, consistent with the C accountability rating. However, this district average masks significant campus-level variation. Families should review individual campus performance on txschools.gov for the specific campuses serving their address, rather than relying on the district aggregate.

College & Career Readiness: Grand Prairie ISD has invested heavily in pathways beyond standard coursework. Early College High School programs at Grand Prairie High School and South Grand Prairie High School allow students to earn an associate degree alongside their diploma — a genuine differentiator that can offset tuition costs for families. CTE offerings span 30+ career pathways across 12 of the 14 state-defined career clusters, with dedicated industry certifications at Dubiski Career High School. Grand Prairie Collegiate Institute reports a high AP and dual credit participation rate and has been recognized nationally for college-readiness outcomes despite its relatively small size.

Interpreting these numbers: A family moving to Grand Prairie and enrolling a high-achieving student who qualifies for the Collegiate Institute or the Early College HS pathway is stepping into a very different academic environment than a student assigned to a neighborhood campus. The district's program choice architecture is genuinely designed to surface opportunity — but it requires active navigation. Families should contact GPISD's Schools and Programs of Choice office to understand application windows and eligibility requirements before settling on a home address.

Key Campuses

GPISD operates 37 campuses: 22 elementary schools, 7 middle schools, 5 high schools, and 3 multi-level (6-12) schools. Below are the campuses most likely to matter to a relocating family's decision-making process.

Grand Prairie Collegiate Institute

Grades 6–12 ~448 students #6 in Texas — US News

GPISD's flagship academic choice school. Ranked 6th in all of Texas by U.S. News & World Report's Best High Schools. Focuses on accelerated instruction with Pre-AP, AP, and dual credit coursework. Admission is competitive — students apply districtwide and must meet academic criteria. This is the single strongest argument for GPISD as a destination district for academically driven families.

South Grand Prairie High School

High School ~3,150 students Early College HS

The district's largest high school by enrollment. Offers an Early College High School program allowing students to earn college credit — including an associate degree pathway — alongside their high school diploma. Houses robust CTE and athletics programs. Students in Tarrant-adjacent areas of Grand Prairie often discover they feed here rather than to Arlington ISD — worth confirming at the address-lookup stage.

Grand Prairie High School

High School ~2,815 students Early College HS

The district's original comprehensive high school, also offering an Early College High School program with college credit and associate degree pathways. Competes in UIL 6A athletics. Located near the historic core of Grand Prairie, it serves the western portions of the district's attendance zones.

John A. Dubiski Career High School

High School (Application) ~1,560 students Career-Focused

A rare district-level dedicated career high school — students apply and choose a specific career track rather than attending as a neighborhood assignment. Offers extensive CTE pathways with industry certifications. The fact that GPISD maintains a fully separate career high school — not just a CTE wing — is an unusual structural investment for a district of this size. Students interested in trades, health sciences, technology, or business should explore Dubiski specifically.

Grand Prairie Fine Arts Academy

Grades 6–12 ~880 students Audition Required

A dedicated fine arts magnet school spanning middle and high school. Students audition for admission. Disciplines typically include visual art, theatre, dance, band, orchestra, and vocal music. Families with students serious about performing or visual arts should research current audition requirements directly through the GPISD Schools of Choice office.

School for the Highly Gifted

Elementary ~158 students Testing Required

A district elementary school specifically for students who test into the gifted range. Enrollment is by identification and testing — not attendance zone. One of very few dedicated gifted schools at the elementary level in DFW. Families with students who have been GT-identified in other districts should verify the identification and transfer process with GPISD Special Programs before purchasing.

Campus Research Tip

For neighborhood campuses not listed above, review individual ratings and STAAR data on GreatSchools and txschools.gov. Campus quality within GPISD varies meaningfully — your child's actual experience is shaped by their specific campus, not the district aggregate.

Specialty Programs

Grand Prairie ISD's Schools and Programs of Choice system is one of the most developed choice architectures in DFW's mid-size urban districts. Most specialty programs require an application; some require testing or audition. Transportation policies vary by program — confirm directly with GPISD whether district transportation is provided to your address for your child's chosen program.

Grand Prairie Collegiate Institute (GPCI)

Grades 6–12 · Application required · Districtwide eligibility
Accelerated academics with Pre-AP, AP, and dual credit coursework. Ranked 6th in Texas by US News. Students apply to GPCI regardless of home address — families anywhere in GPISD can apply. Admission criteria include academic record. Home-search implication: GPCI eligibility is not tied to a specific neighborhood, so families can live anywhere in the district and still apply.

Early College High School

Grades 9–12 · Available at GPHS & South GPH · Districtwide
Two traditional high schools — Grand Prairie HS and South Grand Prairie HS — offer Early College programs where students can earn college credit toward an associate degree at no cost. This is a significant financial benefit for families managing college costs. The program is campus-based, so attendance-zone assignment matters here more than for GPCI.

Dubiski Career High School

Grades 9–12 · Application required · Districtwide
Students apply to attend Dubiski and select a specific career track. Over 30 career pathways spanning health sciences, information technology, business, construction trades, culinary arts, and more. Industry-recognized certifications are a primary outcome. Families specifically targeting CTE depth — not just CTE electives — should consider Dubiski over a comprehensive high school assignment.

Grand Prairie Fine Arts Academy

Grades 6–12 · Audition required · Districtwide
Dedicated fine arts school for students with serious performing or visual arts focus. Audition-based admission. Disciplines include visual art, theatre, dance, instrumental music, and vocal music. Families should verify current audition windows and whether district transportation is provided from their address.

Dual Language Program

Elementary · Attendance-zone dependent · Spanish/English
GPISD offers a Two-Way Dual Language program at select elementary campuses, providing instruction in both Spanish and English. This program is not available districtwide — enrollment is tied to specific campuses. Families whose children are entering dual language or are currently enrolled in a dual language program from another district should identify which campuses offer it and verify their address feeds to one of those schools.

Specialty Elementary Programs

Elementary · Various · Application or attendance-zone
GPISD operates an unusually rich set of themed elementary campuses: Environmental Science Academies (two campuses), an STEM Academy, a Montessori Academy, a World Language Academy, a Fine Arts Academy, and a School for the Highly Gifted. Each has its own admission process. Families with elementary-age children should identify which program fits their child and confirm enrollment eligibility before choosing a neighborhood.

Enrollment Guide

Grand Prairie ISD is an open enrollment district. The steps below reflect the 2025–26 enrollment process — always confirm current-year windows and requirements at gpisd.org/registration.

1. Establish Residency in GPISD Boundaries

You must reside within the Dallas County portion of Grand Prairie served by GPISD. See the Boundary section for the Tarrant County distinction. Required residency documents typically include two proofs of address (lease, utility bill, mortgage statement) plus photo ID.

2. Gather Required Documents

Standard enrollment documents include: proof of residency (two documents), birth certificate, immunization records (Texas-required vaccinations), and previous school records including report cards, transcripts, and any IEP/504 documentation.

3. Complete Online Registration

New student registration is handled through the district's online portal at gpisd.org/registration. For Schools of Choice (GPCI, Dubiski, Fine Arts Academy), a separate application process applies with its own deadlines — contact the Programs of Choice office at gpisd.org before assuming your child can enroll in a choice program mid-year.

4. Intra-District Transfer Policy

Families within GPISD can apply for intra-district transfers to campuses outside their attendance zone, subject to campus capacity. Transfer requests are typically reviewed on an annual basis. Contact the district's Student Services office for current procedures.

5. Inter-District Transfer Policy

Students from outside GPISD may apply for inter-district transfers (Controlled Interdistrict Transfer, or CIT), subject to space availability and board approval. GPISD's current transfer policy is governed by Texas Education Code §25.036. Verify the most current policy at gpisd.org, as policies can change each school year.

6. IEP / 504 Transfers

Under Texas law, GPISD must hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of a student's enrollment to review and adopt or modify a transferred IEP. Bring all prior documentation: the current IEP or 504 plan, evaluation reports, and any private assessments. Contact GPISD's Special Education department directly at gpisd.org to initiate the process before your move if possible. Parent experience with SpEd services varies by campus — direct conversations with the campus SpEd team are strongly recommended for families with high-need students.

7. Schools and Programs of Choice Application Timeline

Choice programs (GPCI, Dubiski, Fine Arts Academy) typically open applications in January–February for the following school year. Mid-year applications may be accepted on a space-available basis. Families considering a choice program should plan their home purchase timeline around the application window to avoid missing enrollment by a year.

Boundary Verification

This section covers the most important practical issue for any family buying in the Grand Prairie area: the county line runs through the middle of the city, and it determines which school district you're in.

The Dallas County / Tarrant County Split

Grand Prairie is a large city that straddles both Dallas County and Tarrant County. Grand Prairie ISD serves exclusively the Dallas County portion of Grand Prairie. If a home's address is in the Tarrant County portion of Grand Prairie — which includes significant portions of the city's western and southwestern neighborhoods — the students at that address attend Arlington ISD, not Grand Prairie ISD.

This is not a minor edge case. A meaningful share of Grand Prairie home listings on major real estate portals will be in Arlington ISD territory. Buyers who search "Grand Prairie" and assume GPISD without verifying can find themselves zoned to an entirely different district. The district boundary does not correspond neatly to neighborhoods, ZIP codes, or street names — it follows the county line, which is not visible in a typical home search.

What to Verify Before Making an Offer

Use GPISD's address lookup tool at gpisd.org to confirm which district serves a specific address. You can also use the TEA School Locator (tea.texas.gov) to identify the assigned district and campus for any Texas address.

ZIP Codes and Campus Assignments

Grand Prairie ZIP codes include 75050, 75051, 75052, and 75054 (Dallas County, typically GPISD) and 75052 partial / 75061 partial and others in Tarrant County (typically Arlington ISD). ZIP codes cross the county line — a ZIP code alone does not confirm district assignment. Always use the address lookup tool.

New Construction and Edge Boundaries

New construction in and around Grand Prairie — particularly in the far western and southern growth areas — may sit in either county. Builders do not always make district assignments prominent in their marketing materials. If you are buying new construction in the Grand Prairie area, confirm the district at the contract stage, not after closing.

Campus Attendance Zones Within GPISD

Once inside the GPISD boundary, your address is assigned to a specific elementary, middle, and high school attendance zone. These zones are separate from the county boundary question. Use the same GPISD address lookup tool to identify your specific assigned campus. If a choice program is your target, remember that choice program eligibility is generally districtwide — your assigned neighborhood campus is not the only option.

Home-Search Implication

Making an offer without verifying your specific address can mean your child attends an entirely different school district than you planned — not just a different campus. Grand Prairie's county split is one of DFW's most commonly missed boundary issues. Confirm the district at gpisd.org and the TEA locator before your offer goes in.

Finances & Tax Rate

Grand Prairie ISD's 2025 certified total tax rate is $1.0627 per $100 of assessed value (per the Board of Trustees resolution adopted September 22, 2025). This rate is the combined Maintenance & Operations (M&O) and Interest & Sinking (I&S) rate.

What this means monthly: On a $350,000 home (a common price point in Grand Prairie's established neighborhoods), the 2025 rate produces approximately $3,719 per year in school district property taxes — or about $310/month added to your mortgage payment. On a $450,000 home, that rises to roughly $478/month. Texas offers a $100,000 homestead exemption on school district taxes, which reduces the taxable value for owner-occupied primary residences and lowers the effective annual bill accordingly.

How this compares to neighbors: GPISD's $1.06 rate sits below Arlington ISD ($1.17), Mansfield ISD ($1.15), and Irving ISD ($1.17). For a family choosing between Grand Prairie and Mansfield, the approximately $0.09 per-$100 difference adds up meaningfully over time — particularly at higher home price points. This rate advantage is one of GPISD's genuine financial benefits for value-focused buyers.

Per-pupil expenditure: GPISD spends approximately $12,948 per student annually (Texas Tribune, based on PEIMS data). This figure is consistent with similarly sized urban districts serving higher concentrations of economically disadvantaged students, as Texas's funding formula provides additional funding for students qualifying for compensatory education programs.

Bond history: GPISD has passed facility bonds in recent cycles to address aging infrastructure. Families should review the district's current bond status and any pending rate changes at the Texas Comptroller's website (comptroller.texas.gov) for the most current year's certified rate at time of purchase.

How Grand Prairie ISD Compares

Families evaluating GPISD most commonly cross-shop against Arlington ISD (which serves much of the same geography), Mansfield ISD (a higher-rated district to the south), and Irving ISD (a similarly-sized urban district to the north).

District Enrollment TEA Grade Tax Rate (2025) Best For
Grand Prairie ISD ~25,500 C / 74 $1.06 Choice program access, CTE depth, lower housing costs
Arlington ISD ~57,000 C / 75 $1.17 Large district breadth, UT Arlington partnership, Tarrant County GP access
Mansfield ISD ~34,000 B / 88 $1.15 Higher district-wide ratings, strong athletics, suburban growth corridor
Irving ISD ~33,000 C / 72 $1.17 Similar scale and demographics, DFW airport corridor access

Families who place the highest weight on districtwide TEA ratings typically find Mansfield ISD more competitive — it is the only B-rated option among these four and offers newer suburban development along FM 157 and US 287. The tradeoff is a higher tax rate and generally higher home prices for equivalent square footage.

Families who are flexible on the district-average metric and willing to target a specific choice program often find GPISD's value proposition compelling: lower housing costs than Mansfield, a tax rate $0.09–$0.11 lower than Arlington or Irving, and genuine access to one of the state's top-ranked high schools if their child qualifies for the Collegiate Institute. The key variable is whether your child is likely to access a choice program versus attending a neighborhood campus.

Arlington ISD is the natural comparison when the home falls in the Tarrant County portion of Grand Prairie — at that point GPISD is simply not available, and the comparison becomes Arlington vs. Mansfield (if the buyer is willing to move further south).

Community Feel

Grand Prairie ISD reflects the character of the city it serves: culturally diverse, heavily invested in athletics and fine arts, and deeply community-oriented at the campus level in ways that may not show up in aggregate ratings. The district is approximately 69% Hispanic and has a significant proportion of multilingual families, which shapes the culture of its dual language programs, community events, and parent engagement in distinctive ways. Booster clubs at the major high schools are active and well-attended — South Grand Prairie and Grand Prairie High in particular have strong UIL competition traditions in football, band, and mariachi.

Parents who move to Grand Prairie from higher-rated districts sometimes report a learning curve in navigating the choice program system — identifying the right application, meeting the window, and understanding transportation logistics. Those who do navigate it successfully often describe a strong community within their child's specific program, particularly at the Collegiate Institute and Fine Arts Academy. What you find in GPISD depends significantly on which door you open.

Grand Prairie as a city is one of DFW's more affordable and underrated areas. It sits between Dallas and Fort Worth with reasonable access to both urban cores, and housing costs that are noticeably lower than Mansfield or the North Dallas suburbs. For families who are focused on getting the most home for their budget while keeping a mid-cities location, Grand Prairie is worth serious consideration — provided they go in eyes open about the district's structure and do the homework on campus-level fit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my Grand Prairie address guarantee enrollment in GPISD? ▾
No — and this is the single most important thing to verify before buying. Grand Prairie is split between Dallas County (served by GPISD) and Tarrant County (served by Arlington ISD). A Grand Prairie mailing address tells you the city but not the county. Verify your specific address at gpisd.org's enrollment page or using the TEA School Locator at tea.texas.gov before making any purchase decision. A real estate agent or escrow company cannot substitute for this verification — only the district address lookup is authoritative.
What makes Grand Prairie Collegiate Institute different from the other high schools? ▾
The Grand Prairie Collegiate Institute (GPCI) is a 6-12 choice school focused on accelerated academics with Pre-AP, Advanced Placement, and dual credit coursework. It is ranked 6th in Texas by U.S. News & World Report — an extraordinary standing for a school in a C-rated district overall. Admission is competitive and application-based, open to any GPISD student regardless of home address. Unlike the traditional comprehensive high schools, GPCI is smaller (~448 students), which many families find creates a more focused, cohesive environment. If your child is academically motivated and would thrive in a rigorous, college-preparatory setting, GPCI is the strongest academic argument for choosing GPISD.
How does the TEA C grade affect my child's day-to-day experience? ▾
The TEA C reflects the district average across all 37 campuses — it does not predict your child's experience at a specific campus or choice program. GPISD's range runs from a nationally recognized high school (GPCI, ranked 6th in Texas) to neighborhood elementary schools with lower individual ratings. For families whose child attends a choice program or a stronger neighborhood campus, the district-level C will be largely irrelevant to their daily experience. For families whose child is assigned to a lower-performing neighborhood campus without a specialty program, it will matter more. The right question is not "what is the district grade?" but "what is the grade and performance data for the specific campus my child will attend?"
Why does Niche give GPISD a B when TEA gives it a C? ▾
These are two different measurement systems. TEA's A-F system is Texas-specific and based on STAAR achievement, academic growth, and closing-gaps metrics across all state schools. Niche incorporates a broader set of signals including college readiness, teacher quality survey data, diversity, and parent/student reviews — and compares schools nationally rather than only within Texas. A district that scores lower on TEA's accountability domains can still score higher on Niche's broader composite, particularly if its specialty programs and graduation outcomes perform strongly. Neither grade is "wrong" — they measure different things. When two sources diverge, it signals that the district performs unevenly across different metrics, which is consistent with what we observe in GPISD: strong outcomes at choice programs, more uneven results across neighborhood campuses.
How does the special education transfer process work when moving to GPISD? ▾
Under Texas law, GPISD must hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of your child's enrollment to review and adopt or modify a transferred IEP. Bring your child's current IEP, most recent evaluation reports, any private assessments, and prior year progress data. Contact GPISD's Special Education department before your move to understand their process and, if possible, initiate the transfer conversation early. Parent experience with SpEd services varies by campus — families with high-support-needs students should speak directly with the SpEd coordinator at the specific campus their child will attend, not just the district-level office.
How does GPISD's tax rate compare to neighboring districts? ▾
GPISD's 2025 certified total tax rate is $1.0627 per $100 of assessed value — lower than Arlington ISD ($1.17), Mansfield ISD ($1.15), and Irving ISD ($1.17). On a $400,000 home with the Texas $100,000 homestead exemption applied (taxable value: $300,000), GPISD produces approximately $3,188 per year in school district taxes. Arlington ISD at the same home produces $3,510 — a difference of about $322 per year, or $27/month. The difference compounds at higher price points. For families comparing Grand Prairie to Mansfield or Arlington on value, the tax rate is a genuine advantage in GPISD's favor.
Can my child attend Dubiski Career High School even if our address is zoned to a different high school? ▾
Yes. Dubiski Career High School is a Schools of Choice campus — students apply to attend regardless of their attendance-zone assignment. If your child is interested in a specific career pathway (health sciences, IT, construction, culinary, business, etc.) and wants a dedicated career high school environment rather than just elective CTE courses, they should apply to Dubiski through the GPISD Programs of Choice process. Confirm current application windows and any capacity constraints with the Programs of Choice office at gpisd.org, as seats are limited and application timing matters.
