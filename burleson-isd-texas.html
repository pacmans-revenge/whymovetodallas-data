Burleson ISD serves the Johnson County city of Burleson, about 15 miles south of Fort Worth on the I-35W corridor. It earns solid marks — a TEA B (82) and Niche B+ — without the premium price that comes with Mansfield or Keller to the north. For families whose job puts them in south Fort Worth, or who need to stretch their housing budget without giving up a functional school system, Burleson belongs on the shortlist. The honest tradeoff: this is not a destination district that families move to DFW specifically for. Families prioritizing elite academic credentials or programs like IB will find stronger options nearby — but families who land here because of geography and cost tend to find the schools workable and, at the campus level, often genuinely good.

The district serves approximately 14,000 students across 20 campuses, including two traditional high schools — Burleson High School (home of the Elks) and Burleson Centennial High School (home of the Bulldogs) — plus Burleson Collegiate High School, an early college campus with a TEA A (99) rating that most relocating families have never heard of. BISD has invested in CTE programming and dual credit pathways through Hill College and UT Austin, and the district's growth along US-174 and I-35W is reshaping attendance zones. Families buying new construction in this area should verify boundary assignments at the address level, not the subdivision level.

One nuance that matters for home search: Burleson ISD territory spans both Johnson and Tarrant Counties, and its boundaries do not map cleanly onto the city of Burleson's limits. Some homes with a Burleson mailing address feed to Mansfield ISD or Crowley ISD instead. Always run your specific address through the BISD enrollment portal before making an offer.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Commute to south Fort Worth, downtown Fort Worth, or the I-35W employment corridor
  • Want solid B-rated schools without paying Mansfield ISD or Keller ISD home premiums
  • Value athletics culture, school spirit, and a close-knit suburban community
  • Have a student interested in early college, CTE, or dual credit pathways
  • Are comfortable verifying boundary assignments before finalizing a neighborhood

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • Academic performance ranking is your primary criterion and a higher-rated district is accessible
  • You need a robust IB programme, advanced dual-language track, or a specialized magnet
  • Your primary employer is in North Dallas, Plano, or Frisco — the commute runs 45–65+ minutes
  • You need a district with well-established complex-learning-profile support — verify directly with Special Services before choosing an address
⚠ Boundary Verification

Not every Burleson-area home falls within BISD boundaries. The district spans Johnson and Tarrant Counties, and new construction along US-174 and I-35W may feed to Crowley ISD or Mansfield ISD instead. Always verify your specific address at enroll.burlesonisd.net before making an offer.

TEA Accountability Rating

Burleson ISD earned a B with an overall score of 82 in the 2024 Texas Education Agency A–F accountability ratings — the most recently published district-level score available at the time this page was written. TEA also released 2025 ratings in May 2025; the current year's domain breakdown is available at txschools.gov.

The TEA system scores districts across three domains:

  • Student Achievement — STAAR performance across grades and subjects, graduation rates, and College, Career, and Military Readiness (CCMR) indicators.
  • School Progress — year-over-year academic growth, and how the district performs compared to schools with similar economic profiles.
  • Closing the Gaps — consistency of outcomes across all student populations, including economically disadvantaged students, English learners, and students receiving special education services.

What does an 82 (B) mean for a relocating family? It means the district is performing solidly without systemic failures, but hasn't crossed the threshold of consistently high-performing A districts like Carroll or Argyle. For a growing suburban district serving a socioeconomically mixed community, an 82 is a genuine result. Individual campus outcomes within BISD vary meaningfully: Burleson Collegiate High School earned a TEA A (99) in 2025, which is an elite result — but that campus serves a self-selected early college student body. The two traditional high schools post more typical outcomes consistent with the district average.

TEA vs. Third-Party Ratings

Niche's B+ reflects parent reviews, college readiness data, and test scores weighted by Niche's own methodology. GreatSchools ratings use a different formula still. TEA's A–F score is state-standardized and comparable across all Texas districts. For making apples-to-apples DFW comparisons, TEA ratings are the most consistent baseline — but all three sources together give a fuller picture than any one alone.

Academic Performance

Burleson ISD's academic outcomes are consistent with a solid mid-B district. On 2024 STAAR assessments, the district showed variation by grade: sixth-grade reading proficiency improved to approximately 56% at grade level (up from 52% the prior year), while sixth-grade math declined to approximately 44% at grade level — a pattern seen across many Fort Worth-area districts that year. The district generally outperformed state math averages in elementary grades but tracked near or slightly below the state average in eighth-grade math.

The graduation rate of 95.3% (per Texas Tribune / TEA data) is notably strong — above the national average of approximately 86.5%. A high graduation rate in a B-rated district tells you something important: the district moves students successfully through 12 years of school regardless of starting point. For families who want their children to graduate college-eligible without needing a top-decile district, that's a meaningful data point.

College, Career, and Military Readiness (CCMR): Burleson ISD participates in TEA's CCMR framework, which credits students who pass an AP or dual credit course, earn an industry certification, or enlist. The district's Hill College and UT Austin OnRamps partnerships make CCMR contributions real — families with students interested in CTE rather than a traditional college-prep track should look closely at what Burleson offers, because the dual credit and certification pathways are stronger than the district's average academic profile suggests.

For college-bound students with strong academic profiles, Burleson Collegiate High School — with its TEA A (99) and early college model — is the district's standout academic pathway. That option is open to all BISD residents but requires deliberate application. It will not happen automatically by virtue of living in the district.

Notable Campuses

Burleson High School

High School ~1,735 students Grades 9–12

The district's original high school, home of the Elks. Serves the southern and western attendance zones. Offers AP coursework, Hill College dual credit, and a broad CTE program. Approximately 93% graduation rate and a student-teacher ratio around 15:1. The Elks-Bulldogs rivalry with Centennial is a defining element of community culture and drives strong booster club engagement.

Burleson Centennial High School

High School ~2,100 students Grades 9–12

Opened to serve Burleson's northern growth along I-35W. Home of the Bulldogs. Larger campus than BHS; serves northern and eastern zones. Offers AP courses, OnRamps dual credit through UT Austin, and access to BISD's district-wide choice programs. Both HS campuses draw from the same specialty school pool — your home address determines which traditional campus your child attends.

Burleson Collegiate High School

Early College TEA A (99) — 2025

BISD's hidden gem. An early college campus where students earn up to 60 college credit hours or an associate degree from Hill College — tuition-free — while completing their diploma. Earned a TEA A (99) in 2025 ratings, an exceptional result. Open to all BISD residents by application; not assigned by address. Students attend a separate campus and do not participate in traditional HS athletics or extracurriculars. The right fit for academically motivated, self-directed students.

BISD Choice Campuses

STEAM Middle School Game Dev & Design 6–12 Application Required

BISD operates several application-based choice campuses including a STEAM Middle School and a Game Development & Design School (grades 6–12). These are district-wide — not assigned by address — and require separate applications through BISD's Choice Schools office. Transportation to choice campuses is parent-provided. Families interested should contact the district well in advance of the application window.

Specialty Programs

Early College — Burleson Collegiate HS

Districtwide, application required. BISD's flagship academic pathway. Students earn up to 60 college hours or an associate degree from Hill College, tuition-free, while completing their high school diploma. Requires application and meeting academic criteria. Transportation is parent-provided to the separate campus. This is the most compelling offering in the district for academically motivated students — families should research it as soon as they identify Burleson ISD as a candidate district.

Dual Credit — Hill College Partnership

Available at Burleson HS and Centennial HS. College-level courses taught during regular school hours by qualified instructors, earning simultaneous high school and college credit at no cost. Eligibility based on academic standing. A meaningful pathway for students who want college credit without committing to the early college campus model. Check the district's Advanced Academics page for current course offerings by campus.

OnRamps — UT Austin Partnership

Available at Centennial HS. University of Texas at Austin OnRamps courses are taught by certified BISD teachers and offer college credit upon passing UT's final assessment. Provides a rigorous academic option within the traditional high school setting. Families specifically targeting OnRamps should confirm current course availability at Centennial and verify any enrollment prerequisites.

Gifted & Talented (TAG)

Districtwide K–12, identification required. BISD's TAG program places a gifted specialist on each elementary campus. In K–2, identified students receive dedicated services; in upper grades, identified students are clustered in regular classrooms with GT-trained teachers. Focuses on language arts, math, science, and social studies enrichment. Identification requires formal assessment through the district's Advanced Academics office — contact them for current testing windows.

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

Districtwide at both traditional high schools, no application required. BISD's CTE pathways include Business Management, Engineering, Culinary Arts, Marketing, and Teaching & Training. Students completing designated sequences can earn industry certifications that count toward CCMR metrics. These pathways are available through the regular course catalog — no separate application — and are a genuine strength of the district for students who want workforce-readiness credentials alongside a diploma.

Game Development & Design School

Districtwide choice campus, application required, grades 6–12. An unusual specialty option for a district Burleson's size, focused on game design, programming, and digital media. One of BISD's more distinctive offerings. Transportation is parent-provided. Contact the district's Choice Schools office for application timelines and current seat availability — enrollment is not guaranteed.

Enrollment Guide

All Burleson ISD enrollment is managed online through enroll.burlesonisd.net. No in-person visit is required to initiate the process.

Step-by-Step Enrollment (2025–26 School Year)

  1. Verify your address first. Before signing a lease or closing on a home, look up your specific address at enroll.burlesonisd.net to confirm it falls within BISD and to see which campuses serve that address.
  2. Gather residency documents. You will need a current utility bill or signed lease/deed showing your BISD address. A purchase contract is generally not sufficient — the district requires evidence of actual residency.
  3. Complete online registration. Use the enrollment portal to submit the registration form. You will need your child's birth certificate, current immunization records, and prior school records or transcripts.
  4. Enrollment window timing. BISD typically opens new and returning student registration in summer (historically mid-to-late July for fall enrollment). Check the district enrollment page for the current-year window dates.
  5. Campus assignment. Your home address determines your zoned campus for traditional schools. Choice school placements (Collegiate, STEAM, Game Dev) require a separate application and are not assigned by address.

Transfer Policy

Intra-district transfers (between BISD campuses): The district accepts intra-district requests based on available capacity. Students must meet academic, attendance, and behavior criteria. Applications are reviewed annually and approvals are not guaranteed. See BISD Student Transfers for current-year details.

Inter-district transfers (from outside BISD): Non-resident students may apply for interdistrict admission, subject to available seats and the same academic and behavioral standards. Contact BISD Student Services for current-year seat availability before making relocation decisions based on a transfer assumption.

IEP / 504 Transfers

Under Texas Education Code, if your child has an existing IEP or 504 plan, BISD must schedule an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment to review and adapt the plan. Bring all prior evaluations, the current IEP, and any supplementary documentation from previous providers. Contact BISD's Special Services office at the start of your enrollment process — not after — to ensure continuity of services. Do not assume services will resume automatically without the ARD meeting.

Boundary Verification

Burleson ISD's attendance boundaries do not map cleanly onto city limits or county lines. The district is actively growing, and attendance zone assignments for specific campuses can shift between school years as new campuses open and enrollment pressure redistributes. For families relocating from out of state, assuming a "Burleson" address equals BISD enrollment is one of the most common — and costly — mistakes.

Key Boundary Facts

  • Johnson and Tarrant Counties: BISD territory spans both counties. Homes in the northern Burleson area may sit in Tarrant County for appraisal purposes while being zoned to BISD — or they may fall outside BISD boundaries entirely and feed to Mansfield ISD or Crowley ISD instead. County line does not equal district boundary.
  • Northern boundary with Mansfield ISD: The BISD–Mansfield ISD line runs through active residential development. Some subdivisions straddling this boundary have homes in different districts depending on the specific lot. Verify at the address level — not the subdivision or zip code level.
  • Western boundary with Crowley ISD: Homes along the US-287 corridor west of Burleson may feed to Crowley ISD. Physical proximity to a BISD campus does not guarantee BISD zoning.
  • New construction on US-174 and I-35W: Rapid residential development in southern and southeastern corridors is adding significant enrollment pressure. Campus assignments for newer neighborhoods may update as BISD opens additional campuses to manage growth.
  • High school attendance zone split: Even within confirmed BISD territory, your address determines whether your child attends Burleson HS or Centennial HS. If one campus matters to your family, verify the specific HS zone — not just the district.

How to Verify

Use BISD's official enrollment portal at enroll.burlesonisd.net to look up any specific address. The TEA school locator at tea.texas.gov provides an independent second check. Both tools accept a full street address and return the assigned campus.

Home-Search Implication

Making an offer without verifying your specific address can mean your child attends a different school — or a different district — than you planned. Boundary maps and neighborhood names do not always match the actual legal attendance zone. This is especially true in new construction communities where zone assignments may have changed since the most recent publicly available map was published.

School District Finances

Burleson ISD's 2024–25 certified total tax rate is $1.2552 per $100 of assessed property value, as adopted by the BISD Board of Trustees in September 2025. The rate is composed of a Maintenance and Operations (M&O) component and an Interest and Sinking (I&S, or debt service) component; the breakdown is available on the BISD Property Tax page.

What this rate means for your monthly payment: On a $330,000 home — a representative entry-level price for the Burleson area — after applying the Texas $100,000 homestead exemption, the taxable value is $230,000. At $1.2552 per $100, that's approximately $2,887 per year in school district taxes, or about $241/month. County, city, and special district taxes are additional line items on top of this figure.

Comparing to neighboring districts: Mansfield ISD's 2024–25 rate is $1.1469/$100 — nominally lower, but median home prices in Mansfield territory typically run $60,000–$80,000 higher for comparable homes. On a $400,000 Mansfield home after the homestead exemption, you'd pay approximately $3,441/year ($287/month) in school taxes. The difference in actual dollar terms narrows or reverses, depending on the specific homes compared. Midlothian ISD, south of Burleson in Ellis County, has a lower rate (~$1.08/$100) but its territory is farther from Fort Worth employment centers, which affects the total cost-of-living calculation.

Texas $100,000 homestead exemption: All Texas homestead property owners receive a mandatory $100,000 reduction in school district taxable value. If your Burleson home is in Johnson County, apply through the Johnson County Appraisal District; if it's in the Tarrant County portion of the district, apply through the Tarrant Appraisal District. Verify which county your specific property is in at closing.

Per-pupil expenditure and detailed financial reports are published annually via TEA's PEIMS Financial Standard Reports. Current figures are available at Texas Tribune Schools Explorer — Burleson ISD Spending.

Bond programs: BISD has used bond financing to fund campus construction as enrollment has grown along the I-35W corridor. Check the district's Financial Information page for current bond status and any upcoming elections that could affect the I&S component of the tax rate.

How Burleson ISD Compares

Families considering Burleson ISD most often cross-shop against three nearby districts. Here's a side-by-side snapshot:

Mansfield ISD ~37,000 B $1.15 Larger program catalog; stronger academic brand; higher home prices

Crowley ISD ~17,000 C (75) $1.26 Budget buyers; west Fort Worth proximity; academics secondary

Burleson vs. Mansfield: Mansfield ISD is 10–15 miles north on I-35W, with a larger AP and CTE catalog, more specialized program options, and a more established academic brand — but median home prices in Mansfield territory run $60,000–$80,000 higher for comparable square footage. The TEA ratings are both B, and families who've lived in both districts generally report the day-to-day school experience is more similar than the reputations suggest. Families who specifically need a broader program portfolio or a stronger college counseling infrastructure may find Mansfield worth the premium. For everyone else, the Burleson pricing advantage is real.

Burleson vs. Midlothian: Midlothian ISD is a smaller Ellis County district south of Burleson. In 2025, 85% of Midlothian campuses earned A or B ratings — a strong showing — and the tax rate at approximately $1.08/$100 is meaningfully lower than Burleson's. The tradeoff is geography: Midlothian adds 10–20 minutes to a Fort Worth commute and sits farther from the I-35W/US-287 employment and retail corridor. Families open to a more rural community feel with a slightly lower cost structure find Midlothian a compelling Burleson alternative.

Burleson vs. Crowley: Crowley ISD and Burleson ISD carry similar tax rates, but Crowley's TEA C (~75) represents a meaningful gap in accountability outcomes. For families who have geographic flexibility and academics as a priority, Burleson is the stronger choice. Crowley's lower home prices may appeal to buyers for whom cost is the primary driver.

What It's Like to Be in the Burleson ISD Community

Burleson ISD has a more pronounced athletics-first community culture than you'd expect from a B-rated district of its size. The Elks (Burleson HS) vs. Bulldogs (Centennial HS) rivalry is genuine and deeply felt — families report it drives booster club engagement, parent volunteerism, and student identity at levels that rival much larger DFW districts. If you have a student-athlete, Burleson's community investment in athletics is a real draw. If you don't, the culture still manifests in high parent engagement and a strong sense of campus belonging. It's the kind of district where families tend to develop real roots.

The community skews toward working and middle-class families who prioritize affordability, community character, and practical school outcomes over elite credentials. Parent engagement at BISD is visible but not the pressure-cooker dynamic you find in the district's wealthier northern neighbors. The district is mid-transformation — Burleson is one of the faster-growing cities in Johnson County — and the community conversation is frequently about new campuses, growth management, and keeping academic quality in step with enrollment increases. Families moving in arrive in a community that's figuring out its next chapter, which brings both opportunity and some uncertainty around boundaries and school assignments.

Burleson Collegiate High School is genuinely different from everything else in the district. Parents who have placed students there describe an environment that feels more like a small college than a high school — rigorous, focused, and self-selecting. The absence of traditional extracurriculars is the most common tradeoff mentioned, but families who made that choice for the right student consistently say it was worthwhile. For families who discover this campus exists only after arriving in the district, the regret is usually about not having researched it sooner. That's a correctable mistake if you start early.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does a Burleson mailing address guarantee my child will enroll in Burleson ISD? ▾
No — not automatically. Burleson ISD boundaries do not perfectly match the city limits of Burleson. Some addresses with a "Burleson" mailing designation fall within Crowley ISD, Mansfield ISD, or Joshua ISD territory. The district also spans both Johnson and Tarrant Counties, and boundary definitions follow the district's official map rather than any county line or city limit. Always verify your specific address at enroll.burlesonisd.net before signing a purchase contract or lease. Use the full street address — a zip code lookup is not sufficient.
What is the difference between Burleson High School and Burleson Centennial High School? ▾
Both are traditional college-prep high schools within BISD offering AP coursework, dual credit, and CTE programs. Burleson HS (Elks, approximately 1,735 students) is the older campus and serves the southern and western attendance zones. Centennial HS (Bulldogs, approximately 2,100 students) is newer, larger, and serves the northern and eastern zones. Centennial additionally offers UT Austin OnRamps courses; Burleson HS features Hill College dual credit. Both campuses feed into BISD's district-wide choice programs. Which traditional campus your child attends is determined by your home address — verify the specific HS zone through the enrollment portal if this matters to your family.
What is Burleson Collegiate High School, and how do students get in? ▾
Burleson Collegiate High School is BISD's early college campus, available to all district residents by application. Students can earn up to 60 college credit hours or an associate degree from Hill College — tuition-free — while completing their high school diploma. The campus earned a TEA A (99) in the 2025 accountability ratings, which is an exceptional result. Collegiate operates on a separate campus; students who attend do not participate in traditional HS football, basketball, or other campus extracurriculars. The application is competitive and requires meeting academic and commitment criteria. For families with academically motivated students, this is the most compelling option the district offers — and it's worth researching before you finalize your address.
How does Burleson ISD handle IEP and 504 plan transfers? ▾
Under Texas Education Code, when a student with an existing IEP transfers into Burleson ISD, the district is required to hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment to review and adopt or adapt the plan. Services do not simply continue automatically — the ARD meeting is the legal mechanism. Bring all prior evaluation reports, the current IEP, and any supplementary documentation from previous providers. Contact BISD's Special Services office at the start of your enrollment process, not afterward. For families with students who have complex learning profiles or significant service needs, Kristen recommends speaking directly with BISD's Special Services team about your child's specific situation before finalizing a home address — service quality and availability can vary by campus.
Why is Burleson ISD's tax rate higher than Mansfield ISD's? ▾
Burleson ISD's 2024–25 certified rate of $1.2552/$100 is higher than Mansfield ISD's $1.1469/$100 primarily because Burleson has a lower average property value base. Texas school funding formulas require districts to levy a rate that generates sufficient per-pupil revenue; districts with lower average property values must set a higher rate to produce the same dollars per student. In practical terms, a $330,000 Burleson home generates roughly $2,887/year in school taxes after the homestead exemption, while a $400,000 Mansfield home generates approximately $3,441/year. The higher rate on the lower-priced home often produces a lower total annual bill. Always run the math on the actual home price you're considering — rates alone can be misleading.
Does Burleson ISD offer a dual language or Spanish immersion program? ▾
Based on current district information, Burleson ISD does not offer a formal K–12 dual language immersion program. The district concentrates its specialty programming on early college, CTE, STEAM, and game development pathways. Families for whom dual language is a primary decision factor should contact BISD's Advanced Academics office directly to confirm current offerings, then evaluate Mansfield ISD, Fort Worth ISD, or other DFW districts with established dual language tracks as alternatives. Program availability can change, so verify current status directly rather than relying on this summary.
What does the daily commute from Burleson to north DFW actually look like? ▾
I-35W runs directly north from Burleson to Fort Worth, putting downtown Fort Worth roughly 20–25 minutes away without traffic and 35–45 minutes during typical morning rush. For mid-cities employers in Hurst, Euless, or Bedford, budget 30–45 minutes. For Plano, Frisco, or North Dallas employers, expect 45–65+ minutes each way during peak hours — and that's on a good day. Burleson is a strong commuter choice for south Fort Worth, Tarrant County industrial and logistics employers, and the I-35W/I-20 corridor. It becomes a harder sell once your daily one-way commute exceeds 50 minutes. If you work in north DFW and Burleson is on your shortlist, drive the actual route at rush hour before you commit to a neighborhood.
