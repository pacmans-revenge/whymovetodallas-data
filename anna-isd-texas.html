Anna ISD is a fast-growing outer-ring Collin County district serving the city of Anna and portions of surrounding communities across more than 64 square miles. With approximately 6,150 students spread across five elementary schools, two middle schools, and one high school, it is a midsize district in a phase of rapid growth — enrollment has climbed steadily as Anna's new-construction neighborhoods have drawn families priced out of McKinney, Frisco, and Prosper.

The district presents a genuine split picture that matters a great deal to families with school-age children. At the district level, Anna ISD holds a TEA "C" rating (~75 points) as of 2024–25, which places it below many Collin County peers. That number reflects system-wide challenges in STAAR math proficiency and the ongoing strain of absorbing rapid population growth. But at the same time, Anna High School earned a TEA "A" rating in 2024–25 — an unusual and important distinction. If your children are already in or approaching high school, the campus-level story is more encouraging than the district number alone suggests. For elementary-age children, the picture is more mixed, and families who rank academic ratings highly will want to compare carefully with neighboring Melissa ISD before committing to an Anna address.

What Anna ISD does offer is genuine affordability, a strong community spirit, a dual language program at the elementary level that is uncommon for a district this size, and a Teacher Incentive Allotment designation that signals the district is actively competing for and retaining quality teachers — even on a smaller tax base than its neighbors. The community is working hard, and the trajectory is upward. That context matters for families thinking long-term.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Are prioritizing housing affordability and newer construction in Collin County
  • Have children at or approaching high school age (Anna High School earned TEA "A")
  • Value a tight-knit, small-district community feel over program breadth
  • Want dual language elementary instruction and multilingual community exposure
  • Are buying in Anna and comfortable with a district on an upward trajectory

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • Academic ratings are a primary driver — neighboring Melissa ISD consistently outperforms at the same approximate price point
  • You need consistent campus quality from elementary through high school (campus ratings vary significantly)
  • You need a deep menu of specialty programs available at all grade levels
  • Your timeline is short and you don't want to wait for the district to mature further
⚠ Boundary Verification Required

Anna ISD spans 64+ square miles and serves more than just the city of Anna. Not every home with an Anna mailing address falls within Anna ISD boundaries, and some edge communities near Melissa and Van Alstyne may be in a different district entirely. Always verify your specific address using the Anna ISD Attendance Zones page before making an offer. New construction communities at the fringes are especially likely to straddle district lines.

TEA Accountability Rating

The Texas Education Agency assigns A–F ratings to every district and campus in Texas, measuring three primary domains. Anna ISD's most recently published ratings:

C District — 2024–25
A Anna High School — 2024–25
~75 District Score (est.)

The Three TEA Domains

TEA evaluates each district across three domains:

  • Student Achievement — Performance on STAAR tests, graduation rates, and College, Career & Military Readiness (CCMR) indicators. For Anna ISD, STAAR math proficiency (approximately 37% at grade level in 2024–25 per state data) is the primary drag on this domain.
  • School Progress / Academic Growth — Whether students are growing at least one year academically each year, and how the district performs relative to peers with similar economically disadvantaged populations. This is where Anna ISD shows more strength — the district can demonstrate growth even when absolute proficiency numbers are below state averages.
  • Closing the Gaps — Achievement equity across racial/ethnic groups and student subgroups. With approximately 52.7% of students qualifying for free or reduced lunch, this domain is closely watched and contributes to the overall C rating.

What the C Rating Means in Practice

A district TEA "C" score in the 70–79 range signals that overall academic performance is below the state average on key measures, but the district is not in accountability intervention. For families, the most practical implication is this: your child's experience will depend heavily on which campus they attend and which grade level they enter. Anna High School (TEA "A") offers meaningfully better outcomes per state metrics than the district's elementary average suggests. Families entering the district at the elementary level should verify campus-level ratings, as scores vary across the five elementary schools.

An Important Distinction

7 out of 8 rated Anna ISD campuses either improved or maintained their rating from the prior year in 2024–25, according to district communications. This trajectory matters: a district moving upward, even from a C base, is a different situation than one that is stagnant or declining. Families buying with a 5–10 year horizon may find the current rating underrepresents what the district will look like by the time their children reach high school.

Academics

51% Reading Proficiency (2024–25)
37% Math Proficiency (2024–25)
97% Graduation Rate
~1,100 Avg SAT (user-reported)

STAAR proficiency rates — the percentage of students scoring at or above grade level — are the most direct measure of academic outcomes in Texas. In 2024–25, approximately 51% of Anna ISD students met or exceeded grade-level standards in reading, and 37% did so in math, per state assessment data. These figures are notably below the state averages and well below high-performing Collin County peers such as Melissa ISD, McKinney ISD, and Prosper ISD. Reading proficiency is more encouraging than math, but neither approaches the levels families find in the district's nearest academic competitors. For families whose primary relocation criterion is academic performance, this gap is real and worth weighing explicitly.

The graduation rate tells a more optimistic story: at 97% per the district's published figures, Anna ISD retains and graduates virtually all of its students — a genuine strength that reflects a supportive school environment even where standardized test proficiency numbers are lower. Niche reports a 95% graduation rate using slightly different methodology; both figures confirm that students who start at Anna High School finish at rates that rival much higher-rated districts.

Advanced Coursework

Anna High School offers Advanced Placement (AP) courses across core subjects, including AP English, AP United States History, AP Government, AP Environmental Science, and AP Statistics, among others. Students may also earn dual credit with Collin College starting as early as sophomore year, covering courses that count simultaneously toward high school graduation and a college transcript. The district's Career & Technical Education department offers industry certifications in pathways including healthcare, technology, agriculture, and business — credentials students can earn before graduating. The Niche College Prep grade of B+ reflects meaningful advanced coursework availability at the high school level, even as overall proficiency scores remain below peers. Average SAT scores of approximately 1,100 and ACT scores near 23 are reported by Niche users from the district, though these are self-reported and should be treated as directional rather than authoritative.

Practical Interpretation

A gap between graduation rate (97%) and STAAR math proficiency (37%) is common in fast-growing, demographically mixed districts. It means the district is good at retaining and graduating students but has room to grow in foundational academic mastery. For families with younger children, this context is important: strong classroom relationships and community support may coexist with academic gaps that take years of district investment to close.

Campus Highlights

Anna ISD operates five elementary schools (PreK–5), two middle schools (6–8), one high school (9–12), and an alternative education center. Campus-level ratings vary — families should check txschools.gov and GreatSchools for the most current campus-specific information.

Anna High School

High School (9–12) TEA: A (2024–25)

The district's flagship campus is its academic standout. Anna High School earned a TEA "A" rating in 2024–25 — the only campus in the district at that level — reflecting strong academic growth and College, Career & Military Readiness metrics. Offers AP courses, dual credit with Collin College, CTE pathways, fine arts, athletics, and an active extracurricular culture including UIL competition teams. For families with children in or approaching high school, this campus-level story is meaningfully different from the district average.

Clemons Creek Middle School

Middle School (6–8)

One of two middle schools in the district, Clemons Creek serves a portion of the Anna ISD student population. Feeder campus for Anna High School. The district's commitment to the Teacher Incentive Allotment program applies district-wide, meaning secondary campuses benefit from the same teacher compensation strategy as the high school. Families should verify current attendance boundaries as the district manages growth across both middle school campuses.

Slayter Creek Middle School

Middle School (6–8)

The district's second middle school, also feeding into Anna High School. Both middle schools compete in UIL academic and athletic programs. Families moving into Anna should confirm which middle school their home address is zoned to, as boundary assignments may shift as the district's new construction communities continue to grow.

Judith Lindsey Harlow Elementary

Elementary (PreK–5) Niche: B

Harlow Elementary carries the highest Niche grade among Anna ISD's elementary campuses at a B. For families comparing elementary options within the district, Harlow's third-party rating is a useful signal — though families should verify which campus their address is zoned to before using campus grades to inform their home search.

Elementary (PreK–5) Niche: B−

Rattan Elementary is one of the district's established elementary campuses. Like all Anna ISD elementaries, it offers the district's G&T identification process and access to dual language programming (at designated campuses — verify at enrollment). Contact the district registrar to confirm which school your home address feeds.

Specialty Programs

Anna ISD offers several programs that are notable for a district of its size. For each program, the critical question for relocating families is whether the program is available at their assigned campus — not just district-wide. Verify access at enrollment.

Gifted & Talented (G&T)

Districtwide, referral-based. Students are identified through a structured referral and assessment process (CogAT and performance criteria). G&T services are available at all campuses, though the depth of services may vary by campus and grade level. Families who relocate mid-year with a child already identified as G&T should contact the district's Advanced Academics office early — identification is re-evaluated in Texas when students transfer. The district also participates in Destination Imagination, a competitive creativity and problem-solving program for G&T students. Access: application/referral required. Transportation: standard school bus routes apply.

Dual Language Program

Elementary level — campus-specific. Anna ISD offers a Dual Language program as part of its English Learner Support department. This is a Spanish-English two-way immersion model offered at the elementary level. Enrollment is open to both English-dominant and Spanish-dominant students — it is not exclusively for English learners. For families relocating from bilingual households or who want multilingual education for their children, this is a genuine differentiator: a district of Anna ISD's size rarely offers structured dual language at this level. Contact the English Learner Support office to confirm which campus(es) host the Dual Language strand before buying in a specific neighborhood. Access: open enrollment; transportation provided per standard routes.

Advanced Placement (AP)

Anna High School only. AP coursework is available across core disciplines including English Language & Composition, US History, Government & Politics, Environmental Science, Statistics, and others. Enrollment is open to qualifying students — no lottery or separate application required. This is a standard Texas high school AP pathway. College Board AP exam scores can earn college credit, depending on the receiving institution's policies. Families targeting the AP pathway should confirm current course offerings with Anna High School directly, as AP course menus evolve. Access: teacher recommendation and prerequisite coursework for most courses.

Dual Credit with Collin College

Anna High School, starting sophomore year. Students can earn simultaneous high school and college credit through Collin College's dual credit partnership. This program is one of the most practical college-prep pathways available at smaller Texas high schools — students can graduate with a meaningful head start on their college transcripts at no college tuition cost. Course availability changes year to year; contact Anna High School's counseling department for current offerings. Access: minimum GPA and TSI (college readiness assessment) requirements apply. No lottery.

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

Anna High School, districtwide pathway. Anna ISD's CTE program is one of the stronger elements of the high school curriculum, offering multiple Programs of Study that lead to industry certifications, licensure, or workforce readiness. Pathways include healthcare sciences, agricultural sciences, business, technology, and skilled trades. A Makerspace at the high school supports project-based learning and technical skill development. Students can earn certifications and licenses before graduating — credentials with real labor market value. The district's CTE Advisory Committee includes local employers, giving the program a connection to Anna's growing commercial sector. Access: open to all high school students; some pathways have sequence prerequisites.

Teacher Incentive Allotment (TIA)

Districtwide strategic designation. Anna ISD holds a Teacher Incentive Allotment designation — a TEA program that allows districts to pay effective teachers meaningfully higher salaries, funded through a combination of state money and local budget. For families, this matters because teacher quality is the single most powerful predictor of student outcomes. In a district with a C overall rating, the presence of an active TIA program signals that leadership is making deliberate investments in classroom talent. It does not guarantee uniformly strong teaching at every campus, but it changes the long-term trajectory in ways that raw TEA scores don't yet reflect.

Enrollment & Registration

All enrollment for Anna ISD is managed through the district's online system. For 2025–26, the process below applies; confirm current-year details at annaisd.org/enrollment-registration.

New Student Enrollment Steps

  1. Verify your attendance zone — Confirm your home address is within Anna ISD at annaisd.org/attendance-zones before beginning enrollment. Also confirm your campus assignment — the district assigns students to specific campuses based on their home address.
  2. Gather required documents — Proof of residency (utility bill, lease, or mortgage statement); child's birth certificate; immunization records meeting Texas K–12 requirements; previous school records and withdrawal documentation; parent/guardian ID.
  3. Complete online enrollment — New student enrollment is processed through the Ascender Parent Portal at the Anna ISD parent portal. Contact your campus registrar if you need assistance.
  4. Enrollment window — Anna ISD opens enrollment windows for the upcoming school year typically in spring. Contact the district at (972) 924-1000 for current 2025–26 enrollment dates.
  5. Pre-K enrollment — Anna ISD offers Pre-K for qualifying students. Eligibility requirements follow state guidelines. Contact the district for current Pre-K enrollment dates ("Round-Up") — these typically occur in April for the following fall.

Transfer Policy (2025–26)

Intra-district transfers (requesting a campus other than your assigned school within Anna ISD) are considered on a space-available basis. Submit requests to the Student Services department. Transfers are not guaranteed and are reviewed annually.

Inter-district transfers (enrolling in Anna ISD from a different home district) are also considered on a space-available basis, with priority given to children of district employees. Contact the Student Services office at (972) 924-1000 for current availability and the application process.

IEP / 504 Transfers

Under Texas law, Anna ISD must convene an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of a student with an active IEP enrolling in the district. Bring all prior documentation — your child's most recent IEP, evaluation reports, and any related service records. Contact the Special Education department before or immediately upon enrollment to initiate this process. For 504 plans, contact the campus 504 coordinator promptly — 504 accommodations can typically be implemented more quickly than IEP services, but do not assume continuity without explicit confirmation.

Boundary Verification

Making an offer without verifying your specific address against the district boundary map can mean your child attends a different school — or even a different district — than you planned. In fast-growing outer Collin County, this is not a theoretical risk. It happens regularly.

Who Anna ISD Serves

Anna ISD serves the city of Anna (primary) and portions of surrounding unincorporated Collin County communities. The district spans more than 64 square miles. Some addresses with Anna mailing addresses may fall outside district boundaries; some properties in adjacent zip codes may actually be within Anna ISD territory. Do not rely on the city name, zip code, or builder's marketing materials to determine which district your home feeds to.

Edge Cases and Common Boundary Risks

  • Near Melissa: The Anna–Melissa boundary runs through active residential development. Homes on one side of a street may feed Melissa ISD; homes on the other side feed Anna ISD. This matters because these two districts have meaningfully different TEA ratings. Verify at both districts before selecting a home based on school assignment.
  • Near Van Alstyne: Anna ISD's eastern boundary abuts Van Alstyne ISD territory. Some outer rural parcels have addresses that may appear to belong to Anna but are in Van Alstyne ISD.
  • New construction communities: Subdivisions at the edge of Anna's growth zone may begin in one district and expand into another as new sections plat. Always ask your builder which ISD the specific lot is assigned to and request the answer in writing.
  • Within-district campus assignment: Even once confirmed in Anna ISD, your elementary and middle school campus assignment is address-specific. With five elementary schools and two middle schools, boundaries within the district matter too — especially if you're targeting the Dual Language program (offered at specific campuses).

How to Verify

  • Anna ISD Attendance Zones: annaisd.org/attendance-zones — view current boundary maps by campus level.
  • TEA School Locator: tea.texas.gov/apps/school-directory — enter a specific address to confirm district and campus assignment.
  • Call the district directly: Anna ISD main office at (972) 924-1000. Campus registrars can also confirm address-specific zoning.
Home-Search Implication

If school assignment is a deciding factor in your home purchase, do not complete a contract until you have verified the district and campus in writing — either through an official district confirmation or the TEA school locator. Boundary maps published on third-party real estate sites are not always current and should not be the basis for a purchase decision.

Finances & Tax Rate

$1.26 Tax Rate / $100 (2024)
$9,763 Per-Pupil Expenditure
$100K TX Homestead Exemption

Tax Rate

Anna ISD's 2024 certified total tax rate is approximately $1.26 per $100 of assessed value, covering both Maintenance & Operations (M&O) and Interest & Sinking (I&S) components. Confirm the current certified rate at the Anna ISD Business Office or through the Texas Comptroller's property tax rate database.

What This Means for Your Monthly Payment

At a $1.26 rate, a home assessed at the Collin County median of approximately $352,000 (after the $100,000 Texas homestead exemption reduces the taxable value to $252,000) generates an annual school tax bill of approximately $3,175, or roughly $265/month added to your mortgage payment. On a $450,000 home (taxable value $350,000 after exemption), the annual school tax is approximately $4,410, or about $368/month. These are estimates — your actual bill depends on your property's certified appraised value from the Collin Central Appraisal District.

Comparison to Neighboring Districts

Anna ISD's $1.26 rate is at the higher end of nearby Collin County districts. Melissa ISD's rate is also approximately $1.26 — the same tax burden for a meaningfully higher-rated academic product, which is the core trade-off families face when comparing these two adjacent districts. McKinney ISD's rate is approximately $1.13, reflecting a larger, more established tax base. Celina ISD sits around $1.24. For families comparing districts primarily on cost, the differences are modest in absolute dollar terms — a few hundred dollars per year between Anna ISD and McKinney ISD on a comparable home.

Per-Pupil Expenditure

Anna ISD spends approximately $9,763 per student (Niche, based on available PEIMS data), which is below the national average of roughly $17,834 and reflects the tax base constraints of a fast-growing outer-ring district that has not yet fully benefited from its expanding property tax base. For context, per-pupil spending is not a direct predictor of outcomes — but the gap is worth noting as families evaluate the district's capacity to fund programs and staffing at scale.

Texas Homestead Exemption Note

Texas homeowners who use a property as their primary residence qualify for a $100,000 homestead exemption that reduces the taxable value for school district taxes. File with the Collin Central Appraisal District once you close on your home. This exemption is not automatic — it must be applied for — and it meaningfully reduces your school tax bill.

Community Feel

Anna is one of the fastest-growing small cities in North Texas, and that growth shapes the school district's character. Families who have settled in Anna consistently describe a strong sense of belonging — not despite the district's smaller scale, but because of it. In a district of roughly 6,150 students across nine campuses, children are not anonymous. Teachers know families; class sizes feel manageable; and the high school, with one campus serving all students grades 9–12, creates a shared identity that larger multi-HS districts can't replicate. Anna Coyotes athletics — particularly football — generate genuine community turnout on Friday nights.

Parent engagement is a visible part of Anna ISD's culture. The Anna Education Foundation supports district programs through fundraising and community grants. The "I ♥ AISD" campaign and Silver Coyote Club (senior citizen program) reflect a community that views the schools as the center of civic life, not just a service. The district's ParentSquare communication platform and active social media presence make it easier for new families to feel connected quickly — which matters a great deal to relocating parents who are building community from scratch.

The honest tradeoff is program breadth. Compared to Prosper, Frisco, or McKinney ISD, Anna ISD simply has fewer specialized programs — fewer AP sections, no IB programme, fewer fine arts elective tracks — because it has a smaller budget and a smaller student body per campus. Families with a child who has identified a specific competitive pathway (varsity orchestra, debate, a specific AP sequence, IB Diploma) should verify that pathway exists and is accessible before committing to an Anna address. What the district does well — community connection, a supportive school environment, an improving high school, and a Dual Language strand that most comparable-size districts don't offer — is genuine and worth weighing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my Anna, TX address guarantee enrollment in Anna ISD? ▾
No — and this is one of the most important things for buyers to verify before going under contract. Anna ISD spans more than 64 square miles and serves communities beyond the city limits of Anna proper. Not every home with an Anna mailing address falls within Anna ISD territory, and some addresses near Melissa or Van Alstyne may be in a different district. Always verify your specific property address using the Anna ISD Attendance Zones page or the TEA School Locator at tea.texas.gov/apps/school-directory before making a purchase decision. Ask your builder or seller for written confirmation of district assignment.
Why does Anna High School have a TEA "A" if the district is rated "C"? ▾
The TEA rates each campus independently as well as the district overall. Anna High School's "A" rating in 2024–25 reflects strong performance on the metrics evaluated at the campus level — particularly academic growth, College Career & Military Readiness indicators, and graduation outcomes. The district's "C" rating is a weighted composite across all campuses, and the elementary-level campuses bring down the overall average, particularly on STAAR math proficiency. This split matters practically: families with children in or approaching high school have a meaningfully different data picture than families with young children. Both the campus-level and district-level ratings are real — they just measure different things.
How does Anna ISD compare to Melissa ISD, and when should I choose one over the other? ▾
These are the two districts families most often compare when looking at outer north Collin County. Melissa ISD holds a TEA "A" rating at the district level, offers an IB Diploma Programme at Melissa High School, and shows stronger STAAR proficiency — at approximately the same tax rate ($1.26/$100) as Anna ISD. For families whose primary criterion is academic ratings, Melissa ISD is the stronger choice at the same price. Anna ISD tends to appeal to families who prioritize a specific neighborhood or community, those buying homes in Anna's new construction corridors where a Melissa address isn't available, or those who are buying primarily on price and community feel and are comfortable with a district on an upward trajectory. Both districts serve similar Collin County geography; Melissa is not significantly farther from major employment centers. The decision most often comes down to housing availability and whether a specific home is in one district or the other.
What does the TEA "C" rating actually mean for my child's daily experience? ▾
A TEA "C" rating reflects below-average aggregate test scores on the STAAR assessment — it does not mean schools are unsafe, dysfunctional, or failing. In Anna ISD's case, the C reflects a combination of STAAR math proficiency (approximately 37% at grade level) that is below state averages, and a student population where about 52.7% qualify for free or reduced lunch — a demographic profile that correlates with academic challenges that take sustained investment to address. What it means day-to-day depends heavily on your child's specific campus, teacher, and grade level. The graduation rate of 97% tells you the district is retaining and supporting students. 7 of 8 campuses improved or maintained their rating in 2024–25. The district has a Teacher Incentive Allotment designation. These are all indicators of a district investing in improvement — just not yet at the point where test scores reflect that investment fully.
What is the difference between Niche's "B−" and TEA's "C" for Anna ISD? ▾
Niche and TEA measure different things using different methodologies. The TEA "C" is based primarily on student STAAR test performance, academic growth, and closing-the-gaps metrics — all anchored in state standardized assessments. Niche's "B−" is a composite of academic performance, diversity, teacher quality, college prep, clubs and activities, and other factors, weighted by their proprietary formula. Niche gives Anna ISD an "A" in Diversity (the district is ranked among the top 100 most diverse districts in Texas) and a "B+" in College Prep — categories that improve the Niche grade above what the raw test data would suggest. When comparing districts, treat both scores as useful but different lenses: TEA is the authoritative measure of academic performance under Texas standards; Niche captures a broader picture of school experience.
How does Anna ISD's tax rate compare to nearby districts? ▾
Anna ISD's 2024 certified tax rate is approximately $1.26 per $100 of assessed value. For comparison: Melissa ISD is approximately $1.26 (same rate, higher-rated district), Celina ISD is approximately $1.24, and McKinney ISD is approximately $1.13. On a $400,000 home (taxable value $300,000 after homestead exemption), the difference between Anna ISD ($1.26) and McKinney ISD ($1.13) is about $390 per year — meaningful but not dramatic. Verify current rates at the Texas Comptroller's office, as rates can change annually based on district budget adoption.
My child has an IEP — what happens when we enroll in Anna ISD? ▾
Under Texas law, Anna ISD must convene an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) committee meeting within 30 days of your child enrolling with an active IEP. Bring all prior documentation: the most recent IEP, most recent evaluation reports, related service records, and any prior progress data. Contact the Anna ISD Special Education department before or immediately upon enrollment to initiate this process. Services cannot be assumed to transfer automatically — the ARD committee will review prior documentation and determine how services will be provided in the new placement. For families with children who have significant or complex special education needs, I strongly recommend a conversation with the Special Services office as part of your relocation research, before you finalize a district choice.
