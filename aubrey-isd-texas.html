Aubrey ISD — Complete Family Guide

Aubrey ISD at a Glance

~4,500 Students (2024–25)
8 Campuses
C (79) TEA Rating 2024–25
B Niche Grade
94.9% Grad Rate (2023)
$1.22 Tax Rate / $100 (2024)

Aubrey ISD is a small, fast-growing Denton County district that appeals most to families who want manageable class sizes, a tight-knit school community, and more house for their money than the more expensive north DFW suburbs offer. With roughly 4,500 students across eight campuses — including a new middle school opening for 2025–26 — it is expanding quickly to keep pace with the residential development spreading north from Frisco and Little Elm toward rural Denton County.

The honest starting point for any family considering Aubrey ISD is the 2024–25 TEA rating: the district dropped from a B (81) in 2023–24 to a C (79) in 2024–25. That single-year dip does not necessarily signal a failing system — rapid enrollment growth and new campus boundary reshuffles commonly create temporary score fluctuations as student populations settle — but it is a real data point that families prioritizing top academic ratings should weigh carefully. The district's graduation rate remains strong at 94.9% (Class of 2023), and Niche rates it a B overall as of 2025. Dual credit and AP courses give motivated high schoolers meaningful pathways, even without an IB programme.

The other essential thing to know before buying a home here: not every address in Aubrey, Providence Village, or Cross Roads falls within Aubrey ISD boundaries. The district serves these communities in specific zones — and some streets in Providence Village and Cross Roads belong to Denton ISD instead. Boundary verification is not optional. It should happen before any offer is made.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Prioritize small class sizes and a community-scale school feel over program breadth
  • Are working remotely or are employed near Denton, where the commute geometry makes north Denton County practical
  • Want significantly more house for the dollar compared to Frisco, Prosper, or Southlake
  • Come from smaller markets and prefer a district where kids know their teachers and each other across multiple grade levels
  • Are comfortable doing thorough boundary verification and monitoring a growing district's trajectory

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • Broad AP course selection, IB programme access, or competitive fine arts pathways are the top academic priority
  • A commute to the Frisco/Plano/Legacy Corridor employment hub needs to stay under 30 minutes — north Denton County is typically 35–50 minutes from those centers
  • The 2024–25 TEA C rating is a dealbreaker without waiting to see if the rating rebounds in 2025–26
  • The family needs the district to be stable and established rather than actively growing through new campus openings
⚠ Boundary Verification — Critical

Not every home with an Aubrey, Providence Village, or Cross Roads mailing address falls within Aubrey ISD. Providence Village is divided at Fish Trap Road — homes north of Fish Trap Road are typically Aubrey ISD; homes south are typically Denton ISD. Cross Roads is similarly split. New construction communities near these boundary lines carry real risk of assignment to an unexpected campus.

Always verify your specific address using the Aubrey ISD enrollment office at aubreyisd.net or the TEA school locator at tea.texas.gov/apps/school-directory before making an offer.

TEA Accountability Rating

Texas A-F accountability ratings measure district and campus performance across three domains: Student Achievement, School Progress, and Closing the Gaps. The Texas Education Agency released both 2024 and 2025 ratings in 2025.

2024–25 rating (most recent): Aubrey ISD received a C (score: 79). This is the official TEA letter grade for the 2024–25 school year.
2023–24 rating: Aubrey ISD received a B (score: 81).

The three accountability domains and what they measure:

  • Student Achievement — STAAR proficiency rates, graduation rate, CCMR (college, career, and military readiness). This is the snapshot of how students perform at a single point in time.
  • School Progress — Year-over-year growth for all students, and whether lower-performing students are catching up. A district can earn a good progress score even without top raw proficiency rates.
  • Closing the Gaps — Whether performance gaps between student groups are narrowing. Fast-growth districts enrolling a rapidly changing student population sometimes see volatility here.
What the C Rating Means in Practice

A one-point drop from 81 to 79 — crossing the B/C threshold — sounds alarming, but context matters. Aubrey ISD has added hundreds of new students and opened new campus boundaries over the past two school years as residential development in Providence Village and Cross Roads expanded. Boundary reassignments shift student populations between campuses mid-growth, which can temporarily depress domain scores. Families should track the 2025–26 rating (expected fall 2026) to see whether this is a growth-phase fluctuation or a longer-term trend. For the most current domain-level detail, visit txschools.gov and search "Aubrey ISD."

Academic Performance

Below are the key academic indicators for Aubrey ISD, sourced from TEA, Niche, and district-reported data. All figures are labeled by school year or class year.

  • Graduation rate: 94.9% (Class of 2023, Niche; consistent with TEA reported 95% over five-year trend)
  • Student-teacher ratio: 17:1 (Niche, 2024–25 data) — smaller than most large DFW districts, which typically run 18–22:1
  • Average GPA: 3.39 (Aubrey High School, Niche 2024–25)
  • SAT average: ~1,170 (Niche 2024–25, third-party aggregate)
  • ACT average: ~25 (Niche 2024–25, third-party aggregate)
  • AP courses: Available at Aubrey High School — course catalog accessible at aubreyisd.net
  • Dual Credit: Available — allows high schoolers to earn college credit at partnering institutions while still enrolled in AHS
  • GreatSchools district rating: Average (per GreatSchools.org, 2024–25 data)
What These Numbers Mean for Your Family

A 94.9% graduation rate in a small district is a meaningful signal: most students who enter Aubrey ISD are completing high school on schedule. The 17:1 student-teacher ratio is one of Aubrey's most concrete advantages — in practice, smaller classes mean teachers can identify a struggling student earlier and respond faster than in a campus with 25+ students per teacher. The SAT average of ~1,170 is below the state's selective-district leaders (Carroll, Frisco, Lovejoy), but it reflects a full-range student population, not a selective enrollment model. Families with high-achieving students should ask specifically about AP section availability and dual credit options at the high school, since course breadth is narrower than in a district with 20,000+ students.

Campuses

Aubrey ISD operates eight campuses for the 2024–25 and 2025–26 school years, including a new middle school that opened for 2025–26. Enrollment numbers are approximate based on district and third-party reporting.

The district's sole high school, serving all Aubrey ISD students in grades 9–12. Offers AP courses, dual credit, and CTE pathways. GreatSchools rates it 6/10 as of 2024–25 (third-party). SAT average ~1,170; graduation rate ~95%.

Why Campus Boundaries Matter More Here Than Elsewhere

Aubrey ISD is actively redistricting as new campuses open. The 2024–25 year brought two new elementary schools and updated boundaries districtwide. The 2025–26 year brought Owens Middle School and another middle-school redistricting. Families moving into new construction communities in Providence Village, Cross Roads, or outer Aubrey should confirm not just their district assignment, but their specific campus assignment — both may differ from a neighbor's experience just one year prior.

Specialty Programs

Aubrey ISD does not offer an International Baccalaureate programme or a dedicated fine arts academy. Its specialty programming is practical and growth-appropriate for a small district — most relevant for high schoolers who want college credit or career-readiness pathways before graduation.

Dual Credit

Districtwide (High School only). Aubrey High School offers dual credit courses allowing students to earn transferable college credit while enrolled in grades 9–12. Enrollment is typically based on academic eligibility; check aubreyisd.net or the AHS counseling office for current course offerings and partnering college(s). No lottery — eligibility-based enrollment. Parent-provided transportation.

Home-search implication: Families specifically interested in maximizing dual credit hours should ask the AHS counselor which courses are offered each year — availability varies by faculty and term.

Advanced Placement (AP)

Districtwide (High School only). AP courses are available at Aubrey High School. The catalog is smaller than in large comprehensive high schools — families expecting 30+ AP sections should verify current availability at aubreyisd.net. Students in smaller districts often have more access per course (fewer students competing for each seat) even with a narrower menu.

Gifted and Talented (GT)

Districtwide. Aubrey ISD operates a GT program beginning in elementary school. Referrals can come from teachers, parents, or self-referral. GT identification follows the Texas state model: a multi-criteria assessment process. Contact the district's GT coordinator via aubreyisd.net for referral timelines and assessment procedures. Available at all grade levels.

Career and Technical Education (CTE)

High School. CTE pathways at Aubrey High School cover a range of career clusters typical of small-to-midsize Texas districts. Pathways include business, health science, agriculture (common in rural Denton County communities), and technology. CTE credits can count toward graduation and are available without application or lottery. Contact AHS for the current pathway catalog.

Enrollment — How to Register

Aubrey ISD enrollment is managed through the district's online registration portal. The steps below reflect the 2025–26 enrollment process — always verify current dates and requirements at aubreyisd.net before enrolling.

  1. Verify your address. Before registering, confirm your specific home address falls within Aubrey ISD boundaries — and identifies the correct campus. Use the district office or TEA school locator (addresses in Providence Village and Cross Roads require extra verification).
  2. Gather residency documents. Typically required: current lease or mortgage statement, two proof-of-residency documents (utility bills, bank statement), birth certificate, immunization records, and prior school records/transcripts.
  3. Complete online registration. Visit aubreyisd.net and follow the enrollment portal link. New student registration is typically available beginning in spring for the following school year.
  4. Enrollment window (2025–26). Open enrollment for new students typically begins in spring 2025. Mid-year enrollment is accepted on a space-available basis. Contact the district registrar for exact windows.
  5. Intra-district transfers. Transfers between Aubrey ISD campuses are allowed on a space-available basis. Families should submit requests through the district's transfer form available at aubreyisd.net. Transportation is typically parent-provided for transfer students.
  6. Inter-district transfers. Families outside Aubrey ISD may apply for an inter-district transfer. Approval is not guaranteed and is subject to space availability and district discretion. Contact the district administration office for current inter-district transfer procedures.
  7. IEP/504 transfers. Texas law requires that a receiving school district schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting within 30 days of a student's enrollment to review and adopt, modify, or reject the incoming IEP. Bring all prior IEP/504 documentation, evaluation reports, and related services records on enrollment day. For Special Education inquiries, contact the Aubrey ISD Special Services office at (940) 668-0060 or visit the Special Education section at aubreyisd.net.

Boundary Verification

Aubrey ISD serves the city of Aubrey and portions of several surrounding communities. The boundary is not coextensive with any single city limit — and two of the communities it partly serves are also partly served by Denton ISD. This creates meaningful risk for buyers who assume that a city or ZIP code determines their district.

Communities Served (and the Nuances)

  • Aubrey: The city of Aubrey is primarily served by Aubrey ISD. Most homes with an Aubrey city address are within the district, but always verify — city limit lines and ISD boundary lines do not always match.
  • Providence Village — split at Fish Trap Road: Homes north of Fish Trap Road in Providence Village are typically zoned to Aubrey ISD. Homes south of Fish Trap Road are typically zoned to Denton ISD. Because Fish Trap Road runs through an actively developing area with multiple new subdivisions on both sides, this is the single highest-risk boundary in the district for buyers to verify.
  • Cross Roads — partially served: Northern portions of Cross Roads fall within Aubrey ISD; the remainder is Denton ISD. If you are buying in Cross Roads, treat boundary verification as mandatory regardless of what listing portals or neighborhood marketing say.
  • Krugerville: Served by Aubrey ISD. A small, established community with less boundary ambiguity than Providence Village or Cross Roads.
  • Portions of Little Elm: A small portion of the Little Elm ZIP code area is served by Aubrey ISD. Buyers in outer Little Elm should verify — most of Little Elm is served by Little Elm ISD.

New Construction Risk

The residential boom north of Frisco has brought dozens of new subdivisions to Aubrey ISD's territory over the past five years. New communities built near the Providence Village or Cross Roads boundary lines may or may not feed to Aubrey ISD — and within Aubrey ISD, they may feed to newly opened campuses (Monaco ES, West ES, Owens MS) rather than legacy campuses. Making an offer without verifying your specific address can mean your child attends a different school — or a different district — than you planned, and boundary maps do not always match common-sense geography.

How to Verify

  • Aubrey ISD district office: Call (940) 668-0060 or visit aubreyisd.net — the registrar can confirm district assignment and campus for a specific address.
  • TEA school locator: tea.texas.gov/apps/school-directory — enter any Texas address to identify the assigned district and campus.

Property Taxes & District Finances

2024 certified Aubrey ISD total rate: approximately $1.22 per $100 assessed value (Aubrey ISD M&O + I&S combined; source: Denton County Appraisal District / TX Comptroller data for 2024). Verify the exact certified rate for the current tax year at comptroller.texas.gov.

What that rate means for a typical home: On a $400,000 home with the Texas $100,000 homestead exemption applied ($300,000 taxable value), the Aubrey ISD portion of property taxes is approximately $3,660/year (~$305/month) — before adding county and city tax. This is in line with comparable small north Denton County districts.

Texas homestead exemption: Texas offers a $100,000 homestead exemption on school district taxes for a primary residence. This applies to Aubrey ISD. File with the Denton Central Appraisal District (dentoncad.com) in the year you purchase your home.

Comparison to neighboring districts (2024 rates):

  • Aubrey ISD: ~$1.22/100
  • Little Elm ISD: ~$1.23/100
  • Celina ISD: ~$1.24/100
  • Denton ISD: ~$1.16/100 (lower rate, larger district)

Aubrey ISD's rate is competitive within its peer group of north Denton County small districts. The district has been investing heavily in new facilities — Owens Middle School and two new elementary campuses required significant bond expenditures — which accounts for the I&S (debt service) portion of the rate remaining solid rather than declining.

What It Feels Like to Be a Family in Aubrey ISD

Aubrey ISD has the cultural DNA of a small-town Texas school district — Friday night football matters, booster club involvement is high, and most parents recognize each other at events because there is only one high school and one set of community sports programs. That cohesion is real. Families relocating from larger metro districts often describe it as refreshing after years of anonymity in a 50-campus system. The downside is equally real: the smaller talent pool for competitive academic teams, fine arts programs, and athletics means some paths that would be open in a Frisco or Allen ISD simply aren't available here.

The growth of Providence Village and Cross Roads has brought a more diverse wave of newcomers into Aubrey ISD over the past five years — remote workers, price-conscious DFW commuters, and families priced out of Frisco or Celina. Parent sentiment on community forums reflects a district in transition: pride in the community feel, concern about whether new campuses and redistricting will preserve it, and consistent praise for individual teachers and campus-level staff. The Chaparrals (Chaps) athletic program draws strong family support at all levels.

One detail that sets Aubrey apart from most north DFW districts: the rural character is not purely aesthetic. Agriculture is integrated into the school community through FFA and ag-science programs — a genuine part of the school's identity, not a nostalgic vestige. Families coming from suburban or urban backgrounds sometimes find this surprising; others find it exactly what they were looking for when they chose to move north of the sprawl.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my Aubrey or Providence Village address guarantee enrollment in Aubrey ISD? ▾
No — not automatically. While most Aubrey city addresses are within Aubrey ISD, the district boundaries do not perfectly match city limit lines. Providence Village is split along Fish Trap Road: homes north of Fish Trap are typically Aubrey ISD, while homes south are typically Denton ISD. Cross Roads is similarly divided. Before making an offer on any home in these communities, verify your specific address with the Aubrey ISD registrar at (940) 668-0060 or via aubreyisd.net, or use the TEA school locator at tea.texas.gov/apps/school-directory.
What happened to Aubrey ISD's TEA rating — it was a B and now it's a C? ▾
Aubrey ISD received a B (81) in 2023–24 and a C (79) in 2024–25 — a two-point drop that crossed the letter-grade boundary. In a fast-growth district that opened two new elementary campuses and one new middle school during this period, with associated boundary redistricting, short-term rating fluctuations are common. New campuses start without established cohorts; boundary reshuffles move student populations between schools mid-growth; and new enrollment from outer communities can introduce temporary volatility in the Closing the Gaps domain. Whether this is a one-year dip or the beginning of a longer trend will become clearer when the 2025–26 rating is released in fall 2026. Families should monitor txschools.gov for updates and ask the district about its accountability improvement plan if the C rating is a concern.
Which middle school will my child attend — Aubrey MS or the new Owens Middle School? ▾
Aubrey ISD redistricted middle school boundaries for the 2025–26 school year when Owens Middle School opened. The assignment for your child's specific address depends on where you live — the boundary line may not follow an obvious geographic marker. Families must verify their campus assignment directly with Aubrey ISD at aubreyisd.net or by calling the district office. Do not assume based on proximity to either campus — new construction neighborhoods near the boundary line are especially likely to be assigned to the less obvious campus.
How does Aubrey ISD handle special education transfers from another district? ▾
Texas law requires Aubrey ISD to schedule an Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) meeting within 30 days of a student's enrollment to review the incoming IEP and determine whether to adopt it, modify it, or develop a new one. Bring all prior IEP documents, evaluation reports, related services records, and any 504 plans on your first enrollment day. Contact Aubrey ISD's Special Services office at (940) 668-0060 ext. 1810 or at the Special Education section of aubreyisd.net. Parent experience with special education services varies by campus and student needs — if your child's program is complex, we recommend visiting the campus and meeting the special services coordinator before finalizing your home purchase.
Does Aubrey ISD have an IB programme or a fine arts academy? ▾
No. Aubrey ISD does not offer an International Baccalaureate programme or a dedicated fine arts magnet or academy. The district is a small single-high-school district — the program breadth that comes with a 3,000-student high school (Carroll ISD, Frisco ISD) is not replicated here. For families whose child is pursuing an IB Diploma pathway or a competitive fine arts or theatre program, districts like Carroll ISD (Southlake), Frisco ISD, or Allen ISD would offer more options. Aubrey ISD does offer AP courses, dual credit, GT services, and CTE pathways — but families expecting an IB or specialized fine arts environment should investigate other districts.
How does Aubrey ISD's tax rate compare to nearby districts? ▾
Aubrey ISD's 2024 certified rate is approximately $1.22 per $100 of assessed value. Comparable districts: Little Elm ISD (~$1.23/100), Celina ISD (~$1.24/100), and Denton ISD (~$1.16/100). The difference between Aubrey and Denton ISD — roughly $0.06/100 — translates to about $180/year on a $300,000 taxable home value. That is a modest difference for most families. The Texas $100,000 homestead exemption applies to all four districts and reduces the taxable base for primary residences. Always verify the current certified rate at the Texas Comptroller's website (comptroller.texas.gov) for the tax year in which you close.
Is Aubrey ISD a good fit for a family relocating from a high-performing suburban district? ▾
It depends on what "high-performing" means to your family. If that means: top TEA ratings, IB access, 30+ AP courses, and a competitive fine arts program — Aubrey ISD is probably not the match. If it means: a school where your child knows their teachers personally, the student-teacher ratio is 17:1, the graduation rate is ~95%, and the community shows up for each other — Aubrey ISD can deliver that in a way that many larger, higher-rated districts cannot. Families coming from tight-knit suburban or exurban districts (rather than large metro districts) often find Aubrey ISD a natural fit. Families coming from Carroll ISD, Frisco ISD, or Allen ISD should have an honest conversation about program expectations before committing.
