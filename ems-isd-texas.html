Eagle Mountain-Saginaw ISD serves the northwest corner of Tarrant County — one of the fastest-growing pockets of the DFW Metroplex. With 32 campuses spanning Saginaw, Blue Mound, northwest Fort Worth, Haslet, and Newark, EMS ISD has crossed 24,000 students and continues to add enrollment each year. That growth is a signal: families are choosing this area, and the district is investing to keep up.

The district’s TEA “B” rating (87/100 in 2024) puts it in solid middle ground among Tarrant County ISDs — above average, with room to grow. The honest picture: EMS ISD’s five high schools and dedicated Hollenstein Career and Technology Center give high schoolers strong options in AP, dual credit, and industry-certified CTE pathways. At the elementary level, the story is more variable — campus-to-campus quality is real, and your neighborhood assignment matters more here than in a more uniformly rated district.

For families relocating from California, Colorado, or the Pacific Northwest, EMS ISD tends to surprise in two ways: the scale of its career and technology programs, and the community warmth. The district deliberately cultivates what it calls a “family culture” — a small-district identity that it works to maintain despite doubling in size over the past decade.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Want a fast-growing suburb with new construction and a strong community identity
  • Have a student interested in CTE, career pathways, or dual enrollment with Tarrant County College
  • Prioritize five high school options including a dedicated career tech campus
  • Value an active fine arts culture — all five programs have dedicated tracks
  • Want TEA B-range ratings without paying Keller or Carroll price premiums

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • Your highest priority is top-decile academic rankings or a district-wide TEA “A”
  • You’re comparing to Southlake Carroll or Keller ISD and expect similar test score profiles
  • Elementary campus quality is critical and you can’t verify your specific campus assignment
  • You need to be close to employment hubs in Plano, Frisco, or Allen — EMS is northwest of Fort Worth
⚠ Boundary Verification

Not every home with a Saginaw, Haslet, or Fort Worth address falls within EMS ISD boundaries. Always verify your specific address at emsisd.com before making an offer. New construction communities in northwest Fort Worth and Haslet straddle multiple ISD zones — ask your builder to confirm in writing.

TEA Accountability Rating

Texas’s A-F accountability system rates districts on a 100-point index. Eagle Mountain-Saginaw ISD earned a B (87) in the 2024 rating cycle — the first full accountability cycle since COVID-related waivers ended. The district described it as a “High B,” noting that 4 campuses earned an A and 16 earned a B. Eight campuses rated C, which is where families should pay closest attention when researching their specific home’s assigned campus.

Student Achievement — how well students perform on STAAR assessments districtwide. This domain pulled EMS ISD’s composite score in 2024: math proficiency came in at 39%, slightly below the state average of 41%. Reading proficiency was 54%, modestly above the state average of 51%. The district acknowledged declines across most grade levels and has publicly committed to targeted intervention strategies.

School Progress — measures year-over-year academic growth, particularly for students who started below grade level. A district can earn strong progress scores even with modest absolute proficiency numbers, which is relevant for a high-growth district absorbing many mid-year transfers from other states.

Closing the Gaps — tracks performance for economically disadvantaged students, English learners, and special education populations relative to state targets. EMS ISD’s demographic diversity (approximately 70% minority enrollment, 38% economically disadvantaged) means this domain is especially important to review at the campus level.

What the “B” Means for Your Family

An 87 positions EMS ISD solidly above average among Tarrant County’s larger districts. It is not a top-10 DFW district academically, but it offers genuine program breadth and a functioning improvement plan. The bigger question for any specific family is the campus-level rating — 4 A-rated campuses exist within this district, and your attendance zone determines which one your child attends. Always research campus ratings by your specific address, not just the district composite.

Academic Performance

STAAR Proficiency (2024)

Districtwide STAAR proficiency for the 2023–24 school year:

  • Mathematics: 39% at or above grade level (Texas average: 41%)
  • Reading/ELA: 54% at or above grade level (Texas average: 51%)

These district composites mask significant campus-to-campus variation. The four A-rated campuses perform well above these averages; the eight C-rated campuses pull the composite down. Research your specific assigned campus’s STAAR data at txschools.gov, not just the district average.

Graduation Rate

EMS ISD’s 4-year longitudinal graduation rate is approximately 94%, above the Texas state average. The district has sustained high graduation rates even as enrollment has grown rapidly — a meaningful indicator across five high school pathways.

Advanced Coursework

All five EMS ISD high schools offer Advanced Placement (AP) courses, with approximately 28 AP course titles available across the district. Pre-AP coursework begins at the middle school level. Dual credit is available through a formal MOU with Tarrant County College (TCCD). The district also participates in UT Austin’s OnRamps dual enrollment program and a third pathway, Premier Pathway (Tarleton Today), in partnership with Tarleton State University.

College, Career & Military Readiness (CCMR)

EMS ISD’s CTE infrastructure — particularly Hollenstein Career and Technology Center — contributes to its CCMR performance. Students earning industry-recognized certifications through CTE pathways count toward CCMR, one reason a district with mid-range STAAR scores can still post solid overall accountability scores. The district also touts 2 international winners and 23 national winners in the NASA Space Settlement Design Contest over 7 years.

Gifted & Talented — PACE Program

The district’s GT program uses a PACE (Plan of Action for Curriculum Enrichment) model at the elementary level — pull-out enrichment with trained GT specialists. Middle school GT students are served through GEMSS (Gifted Education Middle School Seminar). At the high school level, GT students advance into Pre-AP and AP sequences rather than a separate dedicated program track.

Notable Campuses

EMS ISD operates 32 campuses: 18 elementary, 6 middle, 5 high schools, and 3 specialty campuses. Here are the highest-profile campuses for relocating families:

Saginaw High School

High School ~2,683 students Niche: A

The district’s largest high school and one of its highest-rated. Niche grades it an A overall, with strong marks for athletics and extracurriculars. Serves the core Saginaw community with established neighborhood cohesion.

Chisholm Trail High School

High School ~2,202 students Niche: A−

U.S. News ranks Chisholm Trail #536 in Texas. Opened in 2010 to serve the district’s southwest growth. Strong AP participation and modern facilities make it a popular choice for families in newer developments.

Boswell High School

High School Northwest Fort Worth U.S. News: #324 TX

EMS ISD’s highest-ranked high school by U.S. News — #324 in Texas. Serves northwest Fort Worth and Eagle Mountain Lake areas. Strong dual credit and AP participation rates.

Eagle Mountain High School

High School Eagle Mountain Lake Area

Serves the Eagle Mountain Lake corridor and surrounding neighborhoods. Smaller enrollment than Saginaw or Chisholm Trail, which many families prefer for a more personal campus feel and tighter athletics community.

Eagle Mountain Elementary

Elementary GreatSchools: 7/10

The highest-rated elementary school in the district on GreatSchools (7/10). Serves families near Eagle Mountain Lake. A useful benchmark: if your assigned elementary scores similarly, you’re in solid shape academically.

Hollenstein Career & Technology Center

Specialty Campus Grades 10–12 CTE Focus

EMS ISD’s dedicated career and technology campus. High schoolers from across the district attend for industry-certified CTE pathways. Students can earn 20+ industry certifications before graduation — a genuine differentiator in a credential-driven job market.

All campus ratings are third-party assessments and change annually. Verify your assigned campus at txschools.gov before purchasing.

Specialty Programs

EMS ISD offers several programs that affect home-search strategy — some are districtwide; others require applications, testing, or a specific campus assignment.

Career & Technology Education (CTE)

Districtwide, grades 9–12. Anchored by Hollenstein Career & Technology Center, EMS ISD’s CTE spans healthcare, business, technology, agriculture, and the trades. Students can earn 20+ industry-recognized certifications before graduation — many transferable directly into employment or college credit. All EMS ISD high school students can apply to Hollenstein CTE pathways; transportation available from home campuses.

Dual Credit — Tarrant County College

Available at all high schools, grades 11–12. A formal MOU with TCCD allows EMS students to earn college credit simultaneously with their high school diploma. Students typically need to meet TCCD TSI assessment requirements. Home-search implication: any home in the district gives access to this pathway — it is not campus-limited.

OnRamps Dual Enrollment (UT Austin)

Available at select high schools. UT Austin’s OnRamps program offers college-level courses taught by EMS teachers trained and credentialed by UT. Students earn UT Austin college credit. Availability varies by campus — check your assigned high school’s current OnRamps catalog before purchasing in that zone.

Advanced Placement (AP)

Districtwide, grades 9–12. Approximately 28 AP courses offered across EMS ISD high schools. Pre-AP coursework begins in middle school to build readiness. AP exam fees may be subsidized for eligible students. All five high schools offer AP; specific course selection and depth varies by campus.

Gifted & Talented — PACE / GEMSS

Elementary: pull-out PACE enrichment. Middle: GEMSS seminar. GT identification requires a formal referral and evaluation. Elementary students receive dedicated time with GT-certified teachers. GEMSS at the middle school level is a seminar format. High school GT students advance into Pre-AP/AP sequences. Home-search implication: PACE and GEMSS are campus-based; your assigned school’s specific GT resources matter.

Fine Arts — 5 Dedicated Program Tracks

Districtwide, all grade levels. EMS ISD maintains separate program tracks for Band, Choir, Dance, Theatre, and Visual Arts, each with its own directors and curriculum sequence from elementary through high school. The district competes in UIL Marching Band, Orchestra, and Academic contests. Specialized programs like Dance and Theatre are strongest at select high schools.

How to Enroll in EMS ISD

EMS ISD uses Skyward for online enrollment and records management. Here is the step-by-step process for families relocating from out of state:

Step 1 — Verify Your Attendance Zone

Before gathering documents, confirm which campus your home address is assigned to. Use the district’s school locator at emsisd.com or call (817) 232-0880. Attendance zones are updated each year — new construction neighborhoods may have changed zone assignments since your agent last checked.

Step 2 — Gather Required Documents

  • Two proofs of residency (utility bill, lease agreement, or mortgage statement)
  • Child’s official birth certificate
  • Texas immunization records (Form IMM-70 or equivalent from your prior state)
  • Most recent report card or unofficial transcripts
  • Special education documentation (IEP or 504 Plan if applicable)
  • Social Security card (optional but often requested)

Step 3 — Complete Online Registration

EMS ISD’s primary enrollment window for the 2025–26 school year typically opens in late January through March. New families moving mid-year can enroll at any time. Visit emsisd.com → Enrollment to access the Skyward registration portal.

Step 4 — Transfer Policies

Intra-district transfers (to a campus other than your assigned campus within EMS ISD) are reviewed annually and subject to campus capacity. Requests typically open in spring for the following school year. Approval is not guaranteed.

Inter-district transfers (students living outside EMS ISD who wish to attend an EMS campus) require approval from both your home district and EMS ISD. Space availability is the primary factor.

Step 5 — IEP / 504 Transfers

Texas law requires EMS ISD to convene an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of a transfer student with an active IEP. The district must honor the existing IEP until a new one is developed. 504 Plans transfer immediately. Bring all current documentation and contact the Special Education department directly at enrollment to expedite.

EMS ISD Realtor Toolkit

The district maintains a dedicated Realtor Toolkit page for real estate professionals and relocating families at emsisd.com — boundary maps, enrollment information, and district contacts all in one place.

Boundary Verification — A Critical Step Before You Buy

EMS ISD covers 73 square miles in northwest Tarrant County, but the boundary is not a clean city-limit line.

Cities and Areas Served

  • Saginaw — the district’s core city; nearly all Saginaw addresses fall within EMS ISD
  • Blue Mound — fully within EMS ISD
  • Northwest Fort Worth — large portions, particularly near Eagle Mountain Lake; some Fort Worth addresses feed into Fort Worth ISD instead
  • Haslet — portions; Haslet also has homes in Northwest ISD and other districts
  • Newark — portions
  • Unincorporated northwest Tarrant County
Important: Azle Is Not in EMS ISD

Despite appearing alongside EMS ISD in relocation searches, the city of Azle is served by Azle ISD — a separate school district entirely. If you are looking at homes in Azle, verify with Azle ISD directly.

New Construction Communities

Northwest Fort Worth and Haslet are adding thousands of new homes in master-planned communities. These developments sometimes straddle ISD attendance zones — a home on one side of a road may be in EMS ISD while the next street feeds into Northwest ISD. Ask your builder to confirm the ISD assignment in writing before signing a contract.

How to Verify Your Address

  • EMS ISD school locator: emsisd.com
  • TEA school locator: tea.texas.gov
  • Call (817) 232-0880 and provide the exact street address
  • Request confirmation in writing — a screenshot of the school locator is good; a letter from the district is better

Taxes & Finances

EMS ISD Tax Rate (2024–25)

The certified EMS ISD tax rate for 2024–25 is $1.2457 per $100 of assessed valuation:

  • Maintenance & Operations (M&O): $0.7469
  • Debt Service (I&S): $0.4988

This is a slight decrease from the prior year’s $1.2472 rate, part of a broader trend as property values rise. The rate is set annually by the EMS ISD Board and certified by the Tarrant County Appraisal District.

What This Means for Your Monthly Payment

On a $400,000 home with the Texas $100,000 homestead exemption applied (taxable value: $300,000):

  • Annual EMS ISD tax: $300,000 ÷ 100 × $1.2457 = $3,737/year
  • Monthly add-on: approximately $311/month (EMS ISD portion only)

On a $500,000 home (taxable value $400,000): approximately $4,983/year or $415/month. Your total tax bill also includes Tarrant County, city, and other taxing entities.

Per-Pupil Expenditure

EMS ISD spends approximately $14,784 per student per year — modestly above the Texas state median of $14,148. The district allocates meaningfully to CTE infrastructure, fine arts, and GT programming, which shows up in the breadth of pathways offered at the high school level.

Comparison to Neighboring Districts

EMS ISD’s $1.2457 rate is on the lower end among Tarrant County ISDs. Keller ISD and Northwest ISD both run approximately $1.31/$100. For families prioritizing a lower ISD tax rate alongside a solid B-rated district, EMS ISD is competitive.

Community Feel & What Parents Say

EMS ISD leans hard into a “family culture” identity — a term the district itself uses deliberately — and it shows in the parent engagement you’ll find at Friday night football in Saginaw or a Saturday band competition at Chisholm Trail. For a district of 24,000 students, there is a notable level of community cohesion that relocating families often mention as a pleasant surprise.

The northwest Tarrant County area has historically had a more working-class identity than the Collin County suburbs, and EMS ISD’s culture reflects that: less academically pressurized, more invested in vocational pathways and community athletics. Youth sports — football especially — are genuinely central to life here in a way that extends well beyond high school into weekend park leagues and youth organizations. Families relocating from Frisco or Allen sometimes miss the intensity of the college prep culture; families coming from communities with similar identities feel immediately at home.

The honest caution: because the district is growing so rapidly, parent experience varies significantly depending on where you land within EMS ISD. Established Saginaw neighborhoods have a different texture than newer Haslet subdivisions, where the school community is still forming. Parents in newer areas sometimes report that it takes a year or two for a cohesive parent network to develop around a new campus. If community connection on day one matters, targeting an established neighborhood with a longer-standing campus is a meaningful strategic choice when you search.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Eagle Mountain-Saginaw ISD a good school district? ▾
EMS ISD earned a B (87/100) from the Texas Education Agency in 2024 — a solid rating above average among Tarrant County districts. It offers strong CTE pathways, five high school options, a dedicated career tech campus (Hollenstein), dual credit access through Tarrant County College, and an active community culture. The best evaluation is at the campus level: four campuses rated A in 2024, and your attendance zone determines which one your child attends.
What cities does Eagle Mountain-Saginaw ISD serve? ▾
EMS ISD serves Saginaw (its core city), Blue Mound, portions of northwest Fort Worth near Eagle Mountain Lake, portions of Haslet and Newark, and unincorporated northwest Tarrant County. The district covers 73 square miles. Azle is NOT part of EMS ISD — Azle has its own independent school district. Not every address in Haslet or Fort Worth falls within EMS ISD boundaries, so always verify your specific address before purchasing.
What are the best high schools in EMS ISD? ▾
EMS ISD has five high schools. Boswell High School is ranked highest by U.S. News (#324 in Texas). Saginaw High School earns an A on Niche with the district’s largest enrollment (2,683 students). Chisholm Trail High School earns an A− on Niche and is ranked #536 in Texas by U.S. News. Eagle Mountain and Watson High Schools round out the five options. Hollenstein Career & Technology Center serves grades 10–12 across the district as a dedicated CTE campus.
How does EMS ISD compare to Keller ISD? ▾
Keller ISD generally posts higher STAAR proficiency rates and has historically had a higher Niche district grade. Keller ISD homes typically carry a price premium and a higher tax rate (~$1.31 vs EMS ISD’s $1.2457). EMS ISD’s advantages include stronger CTE infrastructure with a dedicated career tech campus and — for many families — a community culture that feels less pressurized. Both districts earn a B from TEA. If your student’s primary interest is academic rankings, Keller ISD edges EMS. If your student is interested in CTE pathways, dual credit, or fine arts, EMS ISD is genuinely competitive.
What is the EMS ISD tax rate? ▾
The certified EMS ISD tax rate for 2024–25 is $1.2457 per $100 of assessed valuation ($0.7469 M&O + $0.4988 I&S). On a $400,000 home with the Texas $100,000 homestead exemption, EMS ISD taxes run approximately $3,737 per year or $311 per month. This covers your school district tax only — your total property tax bill also includes Tarrant County, city, and other taxing entities. Verify the current rate with the Tarrant County Appraisal District before closing.
Does my address guarantee enrollment at a specific EMS ISD campus? ▾
Yes — your home’s attendance zone determines your assigned campus. However, campus assignments are not permanent: EMS ISD has rezoned attendance areas as growth required new campuses to open. If you are buying in a new construction neighborhood, ask explicitly which campus the address is currently zoned for — and verify through the district school locator, not just the builder’s sales team. Intra-district transfers are possible but require annual approval and are subject to capacity.
What happened to EMS ISD’s STAAR scores in 2024? ▾
EMS ISD’s 2024 STAAR results showed declines across most grade levels compared to pre-COVID baselines — a pattern shared by many Texas districts. Districtwide math proficiency came in at 39% (slightly below the Texas average of 41%); reading was 54% (slightly above the 51% state average). The district has publicly acknowledged the challenge and outlined targeted intervention plans. Families with students who need academic acceleration should research campus-level STAAR data at txschools.gov for their specific assigned school.
How do I enroll my child in EMS ISD? ▾
EMS ISD enrolls through an online Skyward portal at emsisd.com. Required documents include two proofs of residency, your child’s birth certificate, Texas immunization records, most recent transcripts, and any IEP or 504 documentation. New families can enroll mid-year at any time. The district’s primary enrollment window for the following school year typically opens in late winter. For students with IEPs, Texas law requires EMS ISD to convene an ARD meeting within 30 days of transfer. Call (817) 232-0880 for enrollment assistance.
Does EMS ISD offer dual credit for high school students? ▾
Yes. EMS ISD has three dual credit pathways: (1) Tarrant County College (TCCD) dual credit through a formal MOU — courses earn both high school and college credit; (2) OnRamps dual enrollment through UT Austin — college-level courses taught by EMS teachers with UT credit awarded; and (3) Premier Pathway (Tarleton Today) through Tarleton State University. All five high schools have access to at least the TCCD pathway. OnRamps and Premier Pathway availability varies by campus. Students typically need to meet TSI assessment scores for TCCD dual credit.
Is EMS ISD growing, and will that affect my child’s school? ▾
Yes — EMS ISD is one of the fastest-growing districts in Tarrant County, crossing 24,000 students in 2024–25. Growth has two effects on families: new campuses (meaning possible rezoning) and capacity pressure at high-demand schools. Before buying, ask whether your assigned campus has had recent rezoning or has significant growth planned nearby. Rezoning is more common in newer northwest Fort Worth and Haslet developments than in established Saginaw neighborhoods where boundaries are stable.
