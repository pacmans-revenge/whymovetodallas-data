Getting Your Bearings

Where Is Heath, Texas?

Heath is a small, affluent city in Rockwall County sitting on the eastern shore of Lake Ray Hubbard, approximately 25 miles east of downtown Dallas. With a population of just over 10,000, it's one of the more intentionally compact suburbs in DFW — there's no sprawling commercial strip, no dense development push, just quiet residential streets, large lots, and waterfront access that most suburbs would envy. Interstate 30 is the main artery connecting Heath westward through Garland and into downtown Dallas, while US-80 offers an alternate corridor for certain trips. Hwy 740 provides internal access across the city and connects south toward Forney and Kaufman County.

What makes Heath's location unique in DFW is the combination of Lake Ray Hubbard access and manageable Dallas proximity. You're close enough to the metro for a daily commute, but the city itself functions more like a lakeside retreat — 95% homeownership, zero high-density apartment complexes, and a Niche "Families" grade of A+. The tradeoff is that Niche grades Heath's commute a C: I-30 westbound into Dallas during the morning rush can stack up quickly, and there are no toll-road shortcuts available in the same way as western suburbs like Frisco or Plano enjoy.

Transit reality: Heath has no DART rail service, no bus lines, and no meaningful public transportation. This is true of most Rockwall County suburbs. Every household needs at least one car, and most families with working adults need two. If you're relocating from a city where public transit was part of your daily routine, this is a real adjustment — plan for it in your budget.
Education

Rockwall ISD — Schools Deep Dive

Every home in Heath is zoned to Rockwall ISD — and that's one of the most underrated selling points of this suburb. Rockwall ISD earned a TEA score of 89/100 (B letter grade) for the 2024–2025 school year, up two points from the prior year, and holds an A overall from Niche with an A+ in both college preparedness and its grade for families. That combination — top school ratings inside a small, quiet suburb at the eastern edge of the metro — is what draws families who have done their research. The flagship campus, Rockwall-Heath High School, was ranked #1 among 40 comparable Texas high schools for academic growth by the TEA. For families moving from California or New York where school quality varies wildly by zip code, Rockwall ISD is the kind of consistent, high-performing district you can trust across multiple campuses.

Work & Economy

Major Employers — Heath & Nearby

Heath itself is a predominantly residential city — its economy runs on high-earning professionals who commute rather than work locally. Most residents make the 25–40 minute drive to Dallas, Garland, or Rockwall for work. In-city employers are anchored by municipal services, the school district, and a growing hospitality sector along Lake Ray Hubbard. The nearest major employment corridor is Rockwall (10 minutes east) followed by the broader Garland-Dallas arc to the west.

Family Life

What Life Actually Looks Like in Heath

Competitive Athletics & Youth Sports

Rockwall-Heath High School is one of the most athletically competitive campuses in Rockwall ISD, with strong UIL programs across football, basketball, swimming, and track. The Rockwall Youth Association runs baseball, softball, and soccer leagues serving Heath families, and private club swim teams through neighborhood pools are a fixture for elementary-age kids.

Lake Ray Hubbard — Right Outside Your Door

Very few DFW suburbs offer genuine lakefront living at this scale. Lake Ray Hubbard stretches along Heath's western edge and residents here don't visit the lake — they live on it. Private boat docks, kayaking at sunrise, family fishing trips after school, and watching the sun set over open water are a real part of daily life here in a way that's hard to replicate in any other East Dallas suburb.

The Harbor & Rockwall Dining Scene

Rockwall's Harbor development — just 10 minutes from Heath — is the social centerpiece for most Heath families. Waterfront restaurants including Soulman's Bar-B-Que, Pelican's Landing, and Chandler's Landing anchor the dining scene. For weekday errands, FM 740 has the essentials; for bigger shopping or a wider restaurant range, Garland or East Dallas are 25–30 minutes out.

Community Events & Small-Town Feel

For a city of roughly 10,000, Heath punches above its weight on community events. The annual Heath Easter Egg Hunt draws hundreds of families, the 4th of July celebration is a neighborhood tradition, and the city's HOA-anchored neighborhoods organize regular block parties and social gatherings. It feels more like a small town than a suburb — residents frequently note they know their neighbors in a way that's rare this close to Dallas.

Acreage Lots & Custom Home Living

What distinguishes Heath from most DFW suburbs isn't just the price point — it's what you get for it. Large lots (half-acre to multiple acres are common), custom and semi-custom construction, gated community options, and genuine privacy. If you're coming from California where a standard suburban lot is 6,000 square feet, the sheer scale of a Heath property can be genuinely surprising. This is the DFW suburb that most closely delivers what Californians mean when they say "room to breathe."

Tight-Knit, High-Achieving Community

With a 95% homeownership rate and median household income of $216,000, Heath's community profile is distinctly cohesive. PTAs are active, school volunteer rates are high, and neighborhoods organized around HOAs tend to have engaged residents who stick around. The tradeoff is less diversity than denser DFW suburbs — but families who value a stable, established community with deeply involved parents consistently name this as one of Heath's defining strengths.

Honest tradeoff: Heath is not for families who want walkable urban amenities, a vibrant nightlife, or a quick drive to dozens of restaurants. Niche gives it a C+ for nightlife and a C for commute for good reason — this is a car-dependent suburb where most dining and entertainment involves driving to Rockwall or further. The payoff is space, safety, water, and quiet. That trade is exactly right for some families and a dealbreaker for others. Know which one you are before you fall in love with the lot size.
Getting Outside

Recreation, Walkability & City Amenities

Heath is a car-dependent suburb — that's the honest starting point for any relocating family. With a Walk Score of 16, daily errands require a vehicle and there is no meaningful public transit serving the area. What the city compensates with is genuinely exceptional: 19 parks and green spaces, a growing P.A.T.H. Hike & Bike Trail network, direct access to Lake Ray Hubbard's 22,745 acres of water recreation, and two golf courses. For families who measure outdoor quality by lakes, trails, and open space rather than walkable blocks, Heath competes with the best of DFW.

Parks, Trails & Green Space

Heath ranks among the top DFW suburbs for parks per capita — with 19 mapped parks and green spaces anchored by Lake Ray Hubbard and the expanding P.A.T.H. trail network. The city's park coverage is especially strong for a community of 10,000; most neighborhoods have trail access within a short drive if not a short walk.

Towne Center Park

Heath's main community park, featuring a playground, open green space, pavilion with covered seating, and direct access to the P.A.T.H. trail system. The park anchors the city's central area and hosts community events including the annual Easter Egg Hunt and 4th of July festivities.

P.A.T.H. Hike & Bike Trail System

The Pedestrian And Trail Heritage (P.A.T.H.) network is Heath's commitment to connected outdoor recreation. The multi-use trail system links parks, neighborhoods, and green corridors throughout the city, creating a framework for cycling, jogging, and walking that extends well beyond individual parks. New segments are added regularly as the city grows.

Lake Ray Hubbard Shoreline Access

The 22,745-acre Lake Ray Hubbard defines Heath's western edge and is the suburb's most significant outdoor amenity by far. Public boat ramps, fishing areas, and shoreline access points give residents easy access to boating, kayaking, wakeboarding, and fishing. Private waterfront homes along the lake shore often include dedicated boat docks — a genuinely rare perk in DFW.

Buffalo Creek Golf Club

An 18-hole semi-private golf course woven into the Buffalo Creek master-planned community. The course is open to the public and provides residents with a convenient option without the drive to Rockwall or further east. The surrounding neighborhood benefits from its well-maintained green space and mature tree canopy even for non-golfers.

Neighborhood Pocket Parks & Green Corridors

Heath's 19 parks include numerous pocket parks and green corridors distributed throughout HOA communities. These smaller spaces — playgrounds, open turf, shaded benches, and trail connections — are designed to put outdoor access within walking distance of every neighborhood. The distribution is notably better than in many DFW suburbs of comparable size.

Heath Golf & Yacht Club

A private lakeside club offering golf, a marina, dining, and social events for members. Not a public amenity, but representative of the lifestyle infrastructure available in Heath for families who choose to invest in club membership. The marina provides covered boat slip storage for members — a key draw for serious boating families who want their vessel minutes from home.

Crime & Safety

How Safe Is Heath?

Heath is one of the safest cities in Rockwall County and consistently ranks among the lower-crime communities in the broader DFW metro. With a population of roughly 10,000 and a 95% homeownership rate, the demographics here correlate strongly with low crime. Niche assigns Heath a B crime and safety grade — notably strong for a Texas suburb — and residents on local Facebook groups and Nextdoor consistently describe feeling comfortable letting children play outside and walking at night. The most common safety concern raised by residents involves package theft and car break-ins in unlocked vehicles, which is true across essentially all of DFW's upscale suburbs.

Heath's market tells a buyer-friendly story in 2026 that most DFW suburbs can't match. At 9.3 months of supply, this is solidly a buyer's market — meaning more homes are available than buyers can absorb at current demand levels. The 70-day average median days on market confirms it: sellers are typically waiting more than two months for offers, and homes that are priced correctly and show well are moving, but overpriced inventory is sitting. Honest Assessment

Pros & Cons of Living in Heath

I won't sell you on Heath. I'll give you the real picture so you can decide if it's right for your family.

✓ What Heath Does Well

  • True lakefront living — Lake Ray Hubbard access is the defining lifestyle advantage. Waterfront homes with private docks, kayaking, boating, and sunset views are a real part of daily life here in a way that no other East Dallas suburb can match.
  • Top-tier Rockwall ISD schools — TEA B-rated, Niche A overall, with Rockwall-Heath High School serving as one of the district's strongest academic and athletic campuses. For families prioritizing education, RISD consistently delivers.
  • Exceptional safety — Violent crime at roughly 1.4 per 1,000 residents is far below both the Texas and U.S. averages. Residents consistently describe a community where children are visible outside and the neighborhood feels secure.
  • Space, privacy, and large lots — Half-acre to multi-acre lots are standard, not the exception. For families leaving California's 6,000-square-foot suburban lots, the transition to Heath is genuinely striking. Gated community options, custom homes, and quiet streets define the standard experience.
  • Buyer's market leverage — 9.3 months of supply gives buyers negotiating power on price, closing costs, and timing that most Texas suburbs haven't offered since 2019. If you're relocating and want time to make a careful decision, Heath rewards patience.
  • Tight-knit, engaged community — 95% homeownership, high median income, active PTAs, and strong HOA community structures create a neighborhood culture that many families moving from denser urban areas describe as refreshing.
  • No state income tax — The standard Texas advantage applies fully here. For high earners relocating from California, New York, or Illinois, this is a meaningful annual savings that helps offset Heath's property tax rate.

— What to Weigh Carefully

  • Dallas commute is genuinely long — 35–45 minutes to core Dallas employment in best conditions; 45–60 minutes during rush hour on I-30 westbound. If your office is in Uptown, the Medical District, or Downtown, this adds up quickly. Work-from-home families absorb it much better than daily office commuters.
  • Virtually no walkable amenities — Walk Score of 16. There is nothing to walk to. Every errand, every coffee run, every dinner out requires getting in the car. Families who walked to their coffee shop in their last neighborhood will feel this immediately.
  • High price floor — $751K median means the entry level for a well-located home is closer to $500K–$600K. Heath is not a budget move within DFW. Families on tighter budgets will find better value in Rockwall, Rowlett, or Forney.
  • Slower market means slower equity growth — A buyer's market with 70 days on market is great for buying in, but if you need to sell in 2–3 years, you may face similar conditions. Heath rewards long-term residents more than short-term flippers.
  • Car-dependent — No meaningful public transit. A personal vehicle is essential for every member of the household who needs to get anywhere. Budget for two cars if you have a two-adult household with separate work or activity schedules.
  • Distance from Frisco/Legacy West corridor — If your employer is in Frisco, Allen, McKinney, or along the DNT, the commute from Heath runs 45–65 minutes. Heath is oriented toward Dallas and inner East suburbs, not North DFW employment centers.

Heath, Texas — Frequently Asked Questions

Heath is consistently ranked among the best places to live in Rockwall County and one of the top small cities in the Dallas–Fort Worth metro. Niche gives it an A overall grade for families, an A+ for public schools, and an A for housing. The combination of exceptional safety, top-rated schools, lakefront access to Lake Ray Hubbard, and a highly engaged community makes it particularly well-suited for families relocating from high-cost states who want space, quality schools, and a stable neighborhood — without paying California prices.

Heath is zoned to Rockwall ISD, which holds a TEA B rating with a score of 89/100 for 2025 — one of the stronger district scores on the east side of DFW. Niche gives the district an A overall with an A+ for college prep. Rockwall-Heath High School, which serves most of Heath, is rated 8/10 by GreatSchools and is known for strong athletics and academic programs including the Burton College & Career Academy for specialized pathways. Amy Parks-Heath Elementary rates 9/10 on GreatSchools. Always verify your specific address against RISD's attendance zone maps before making an offer — small areas of Heath may feed into different campuses than you expect.

Heath is approximately 27–30 miles from Downtown Dallas, a drive of about 35–45 minutes under normal conditions via I-30 West or US-80. During rush hour (7–9 am westbound, 4–6:30 pm eastbound), that commute can extend to 50–60 minutes. Heath is positioned better for East Dallas employment centers — Deep Ellum, the Medical District along Harry Hines, and near East Dallas — than for Uptown or West Dallas. For most Dallas-area remote workers, the commute is manageable a few days per week; for daily downtown commuters, it requires an honest look at the time cost.

Yes — Heath is one of the safest cities in Rockwall County and consistently ranks in the lower-crime tier of DFW suburbs. Violent crime runs at approximately 1.4 per 1,000 residents, compared to the U.S. average of about 4.0. Property crime at roughly 4.5 per 1,000 is dramatically below the Texas average of 22.4. Niche grades Heath B for crime and safety. The most common resident complaints involve opportunistic car break-ins and package theft, which is consistent with affluent suburbs across all of DFW. Overall, Heath families regularly cite safety as one of the community's strongest qualities.

The combined property tax rate in Heath runs approximately $1.6748 per $100 of assessed value, broken down as: Rockwall ISD (~$1.0669), City of Heath (~$0.2757), Rockwall County (~$0.2510), and Collin College (~$0.0812). On the current $751K median home, that works out to roughly $12,578 per year before the homestead exemption. Some newer master-planned communities may carry additional MUD/PID assessments — always verify the full tax load for any specific address before making an offer.

For California buyers: the effective tax rate is higher than Prop 13, but Texas has no state income tax. A family earning $250,000 avoids $20,000–$25,000+ in California income tax annually. For most California households, the net financial position in Heath is meaningfully better than staying in the Bay Area or LA — even accounting for the higher property tax rate.

Yes — waterfront and water-view properties are one of Heath's most distinctive features. Lake Ray Hubbard forms the city's western border, and a meaningful portion of Heath's premium housing stock sits directly on or near the shoreline. Many waterfront homes include private boat docks. The Heath Golf & Yacht Club offers a marina with covered boat slip storage for members. Water-view and waterfront lots command significant premiums above Heath's already-elevated median, but they represent a lifestyle option that almost no other DFW suburb can offer at any price point.

Heath is best known within the DFW metro for three things: its lakefront location on Lake Ray Hubbard, its highly rated Rockwall ISD schools, and its premium residential character. The city is deliberately low-density — large lots, custom homes, quiet streets — which has made it a destination for high-income families seeking a private, suburban acreage lifestyle within commuting distance of Dallas. Niche ranks Heath among the top communities in Texas for families. For families moving from California or the Pacific Northwest specifically, the lakefront lifestyle and large-lot standard often resonate immediately.

As of early 2026, Heath is firmly in buyer's market territory with 9.3 months of inventory supply — well above the 6-month threshold that defines a balanced market. Homes are averaging 70 days on market before selling. For relocating families, this is favorable: you have time to be thorough with inspections, sellers are more willing to negotiate on price and terms, and there is less competition from other buyers than in the hot suburban markets of 2021–2022. The tradeoff is that buyer's market conditions historically correlate with slower appreciation — don't expect rapid equity growth in the near term.

Heath and Rockwall share the same school district (Rockwall ISD) and the same county, but they feel quite different on the ground. Rockwall has a more developed commercial core — The Harbor, established retail, a historic downtown square, and more dining options — with a broader price range from ~$350K to $1M+. Heath skews more residential, more private, more expensive, and more oriented toward large-lot and waterfront living specifically. Families who want walkable amenities within city limits tend to prefer Rockwall. Families who want maximum space, privacy, and direct lake access often choose Heath. Both are excellent for schools and safety — the decision usually comes down to lifestyle and budget.

The primary commute route from Heath to Dallas is via I-30 West toward Downtown Dallas — approximately 27–30 miles and 35–45 minutes off-peak. Rush hour adds 15–20 minutes in the worst conditions. Access is via Horizon Road or FM 740 to I-30 west or US-80. I-30 is Heath's commuter artery, and it can back up significantly between Mesquite and downtown during peak hours. For commuters to East Dallas, the University of Texas Southwestern, or Garland, the route is relatively manageable. For Uptown, Oak Lawn, or the North Dallas employment corridor, the daily round-trip time commitment is meaningful and worth modeling against the lifestyle trade-off.
