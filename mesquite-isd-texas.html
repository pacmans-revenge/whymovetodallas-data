Mesquite ISD Overview

~38,000 Students Enrolled
52 Campuses
B TEA Grade (2025)
B Niche Grade (3rd party)
~88% 4-Year Grad Rate
$1.097 Tax Rate (2025)

Mesquite ISD serves roughly 38,000 students across 52 campuses in Mesquite, Balch Springs, and parts of Sunnyvale — making it one of the larger suburban districts in Dallas County. If you're buying in Mesquite primarily for housing affordability, the school district deserves a careful look before you make an offer. The honest starting point: Mesquite ISD is not the academic powerhouse of Frisco, Allen, or Carroll, but it is also not the underperforming district some families assume. In 2025, the Texas Education Agency upgraded the district's rating from a C to a B — a full letter grade jump that reflects real, documented improvement in student outcomes across all four traditional high schools.

What makes Mesquite ISD stand out in a practical sense is its program breadth. The CTE department offers 35 programs of study across 12 career clusters, with dual-credit pathways through Dallas College — more workforce-ready depth than many higher-rated suburban districts. The fine arts program is unusually strong for this size district, including an active mariachi program that is genuinely rare among North Texas schools. For families whose children are interested in career-technical education, performing arts, or athletics, Mesquite ISD can be a solid fit. For families whose primary priority is AP and IB college-prep intensity comparable to Frisco or Allen, the comparison will look different.

The district serves a large, diverse student population with approximately 64% of students identifying as Hispanic and roughly 82% qualifying for free or reduced-price lunch — among the highest economically disadvantaged percentages of any Dallas County suburban district. This context matters for understanding both how district resources are allocated (significant federal Title I funding) and the broad range of programming available. A district of this scale and demographic profile invests heavily in intervention and support services alongside its enrichment programs.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Are purchasing a home in Mesquite, Balch Springs, or western Sunnyvale and want to understand the ISD before closing
  • Have students interested in CTE career pathways, dual credit, or fine arts (including mariachi and performing arts)
  • Value a large district's program variety — broad athletics, multiple high schools, robust extracurriculars
  • Are relocating from states or districts where Mesquite ISD's B rating represents an upgrade in quality
  • Are open to campus choice within the district (some feeder patterns are stronger than others)

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • Your priority is the highest AP/IB participation rates in DFW — nearby Richardson ISD or Garland ISD have stronger college-prep reputations
  • Your address is in Sunnyvale (verify carefully — parts of Sunnyvale fall in Sunnyvale ISD, a much smaller district with different character)
  • You're comparing Mesquite ISD to Carroll, Frisco, or Allen as a primary decision factor and those districts are within your budget
  • Campus-level variation concerns you and you need guarantees about which feeder pattern applies to your address
⚠ Boundary Verification

Not every home with a Mesquite or Sunnyvale address falls within Mesquite ISD boundaries. Parts of Sunnyvale are served by Sunnyvale ISD, and the boundary line does not follow obvious geographic markers. Always verify your specific street address at mesquiteisd.org/attendance-zones before making an offer. The district also has a small overlap with Garland ISD on the northern edge.

TEA Accountability Rating

Mesquite ISD received a B rating from the Texas Education Agency in 2025, improving from a C rating in 2024 (overall score: 76). This is a meaningful, documented improvement — not marketing language. All four of the district's traditional high schools moved from C to B in the same 2025 rating cycle, and 40% of the district's 50+ eligible campuses improved by at least one full letter grade. The number of campuses rated "needs improvement" or below dropped from 11 to four, with zero campuses receiving an F.

The TEA A–F system evaluates districts across three performance domains:

  • Student Achievement — STAAR proficiency rates, college/career/military readiness, graduation, and ELL progress. This domain captures how many students meet grade-level standards in a given year.
  • School Progress — Academic growth (how much students improve year over year) and relative performance compared to similar-population districts. A district can score well here even while improving from a lower baseline.
  • Closing the Gaps — Performance equity across student groups, including economically disadvantaged students, English language learners, and students with disabilities. For a district with Mesquite's demographics, performance on this domain is particularly significant.

What the 2025 B rating means practically for relocating families: the district is on an upward trajectory that is showing up at every level of the system. That said, a B-rated district still means roughly 10–20% below the performance threshold of an A — and campus-level variation within a 52-campus district is real. Your child's day-to-day experience will be shaped by their individual campus, not the district average. Verify which campus your specific address feeds and check that campus's individual TEA rating before finalizing a home purchase.

Data Note

TEA publishes A–F ratings annually, but the timeline can lag one to two years. The 2025 ratings (reflecting 2024–25 school year data) represent the most current publicly available ratings as of the publishing date of this page. For the most up-to-date status, visit txschools.gov — Mesquite ISD profile.

Academic Performance

Mesquite ISD's academic profile reflects a district serving a high proportion of economically disadvantaged students while steadily building college and career readiness outcomes.

Key Academic Indicators

  • Four-year graduation rate: approximately 88% (2023–24, per TEA) — above the Texas state average of roughly 85%
  • AP/Dual Credit: Advanced Placement coursework is available at all four traditional high schools. Horn High School reported approximately 36% AP participation rate (U.S. News data). Dual credit through Dallas College is available districtwide and is one of the district's stronger academic offerings — allowing students to earn college hours at no cost during high school.
  • CCMR (College, Career, and Military Readiness): The district's 2025 TEA improvement was partly driven by gains in CCMR — students who graduate having met college-readiness benchmarks or completed career certifications. The CTE program's 35 pathways contribute meaningfully to CCMR rates.
  • STAAR performance: District-level STAAR proficiency rates in reading and math are below the state average, reflecting the demographics of the student population served. Campus-level results vary significantly — some campuses perform near or above state averages, while others are in improvement status.

For families whose children need academic support or intervention services, Mesquite ISD's Title I-funded campuses have meaningful resources in place. For students seeking the most rigorous college-prep track in a Dallas County context, the dual-credit pathway through Dallas College is the district's strongest offering — and it's available at no tuition cost to students. What Mesquite ISD does not currently offer is a districtwide IB (International Baccalaureate) programme. Families specifically seeking IB should evaluate Garland ISD, Richardson ISD, or districts further north.

High School Campuses

Mesquite ISD operates four traditional high schools plus Poteet High School and the Vroonland/Vanguard alternative program. All four traditional high schools improved from a C to a B in the 2025 TEA accountability ratings. Enrollment figures below are approximate based on district-reported data.

Horn High School

High School ~2,700 students TEA: B (2025)

The largest campus in the district, Horn serves families in northern Mesquite and has the highest AP participation rate of the four traditional high schools at approximately 36% (U.S. News). Feeds a mix of established neighborhoods and newer residential areas.

West Mesquite High School

High School ~2,000 students TEA: B (2025)

Serves western Mesquite and has experienced some enrollment growth with new residential construction in the Iron Horse area. Ranked 642nd in Texas by U.S. News. Strong athletic programs.

North Mesquite High School

High School ~1,950 students TEA: B (2025)

Serves northern feeder patterns. Ranked 579th in Texas by U.S. News — the highest-ranked of the four traditional Mesquite high schools on that measure. Enrollment has trended slightly downward in recent years on this feeder pattern.

Mesquite High School

High School ~2,050 students TEA: B (2025)

The district's original campus, serving central and southern Mesquite neighborhoods. Strong fine arts tradition including the district's mariachi and performing arts programs. Feeds Balch Springs communities.

Poteet High School

High School ~1,485 students

Serves southeastern Mesquite and some western Sunnyvale address areas that fall within Mesquite ISD boundaries. Smaller than the traditional high schools — some families specifically seek it for a more contained campus experience.

Vroonland / Vanguard High School

Alternative / Magnet ~1,590 students

Alternative and Vanguard programming for students seeking a different educational structure. Includes credit recovery options and non-traditional scheduling. Not the typical choice for relocating families but available districtwide.

The five-campus structure means Mesquite ISD does not offer the single-identity high school experience of smaller districts. Campus quality and culture varies — neighborhood, feeder elementary, and specific campus programs all shape the experience more than the district rating alone. Verify which high school your specific address feeds using the district's attendance zone tool.

Specialty Programs

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

Districtwide — available at all high schools. Mesquite ISD's CTE department offers 35 programs of study across 12 career clusters including Health Science, Engineering, IT, Business, Architecture, and more. Students can earn state and nationally recognized certifications and college credit through dual enrollment with Dallas College at no tuition cost. Transportation to Dallas College campuses may vary — verify with the district. This is the program most likely to differentiate Mesquite ISD for a relocating family with career-focused students.

Fine Arts (including Mariachi)

Districtwide — no application required for standard enrollment. Mesquite ISD offers band, choir, orchestra, theater, visual arts, and a mariachi program — the last being genuinely unusual among DFW suburban districts of this size. The mariachi program has a strong competitive history. Fine arts participation is open to all students at their attendance-zone campus; advanced performance ensembles may have audition requirements at the campus level.

Gifted & Talented (GT)

Districtwide — requires GT identification through district assessment. GT services are provided at all grade levels. Elementary and middle school GT services are delivered through pull-out programs and advanced coursework. High school GT students access Pre-AP and AP coursework as their primary advanced pathway. Students relocating from another Texas district with GT identification should schedule an ARD/review meeting within 30 days — Texas law requires transfer services to continue during review. Contact the district's Student Services office to initiate the process.

Dual Credit with Dallas College

Available to eligible 11th–12th grade students districtwide. Students can earn college credit hours at Dallas College (formerly Dallas County Community College District) while completing high school. Qualifying criteria include GPA thresholds and TSI (Texas Success Initiative) placement scores. Tuition is covered; students may be responsible for fees. This pathway is Mesquite ISD's strongest college-readiness offering and contributes to CCMR accountability metrics.

Mesquite Virtual Learning Experience

Available to eligible rising 11th–12th grade students. A hybrid learning option that blends in-person and remote coursework. Available to qualifying upperclassmen with demonstrated academic standing. Not a full-time virtual school — students maintain a campus enrollment and connection. Details and application through campus counselors.

Dual Language

Elementary level — specific campuses only. Mesquite ISD offers a dual language (Spanish/English) program at select elementary campuses serving communities with high Spanish-speaking populations. Enrollment is campus-based and tied to attendance zones. Families specifically seeking dual language should verify which campuses offer the program and whether their address feeds to one of those campuses. Contact the district's Academic Services department for current campus listings.

No IB (International Baccalaureate) programme is currently offered in Mesquite ISD. Families specifically seeking IB should look at Garland ISD or Richardson ISD for the nearest campus-level IB options in the eastern Dallas County area.

Enrollment Steps

All time-sensitive enrollment details below reflect 2025–26 district guidance. Verify current timelines at mesquiteisd.org/enrollment before scheduling.

  1. Verify your attendance zone: Use the Mesquite ISD attendance zone lookup with your specific street address. Do not assume based on ZIP code or neighborhood name — boundary lines do not follow zip codes.
  2. Gather residency documents: Required documents typically include two proofs of residency (utility bill, lease, or mortgage statement), a photo ID, the student's birth certificate, immunization records, and prior school records or unofficial transcript. New residents renting or staying temporarily may need a notarized residency affidavit.
  3. Complete online registration: Enrollment begins through the district portal at mesquiteisd.org/enrollment. New students must initiate online registration before the in-person enrollment appointment.
  4. Enrollment windows: The district typically opens online registration for returning students in spring (April–May) and for new students in spring/summer ahead of the fall semester. Mid-year enrollment for families relocating is accepted on a rolling basis — contact the campus registrar directly.
  5. Intra-district transfers: Transfers between Mesquite ISD campuses (outside your attendance zone) are available on a space-available basis and require a completed transfer application. Transfers are not guaranteed and may be rescinded if capacity changes. Apply through the district's Student Services office.
  6. Inter-district transfers: Families wishing to transfer into Mesquite ISD from another district must apply through the Student Services office. Acceptance depends on campus capacity and program availability. Similarly, Mesquite ISD students seeking to transfer out to another district must apply to and be accepted by the receiving district.
  7. IEP / 504 transfers (Texas law): If your child has an active IEP or 504 Plan from a prior district, Texas law requires Mesquite ISD to convene an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment to review and honor or modify the plan. Bring all prior documentation — the IEP, evaluation reports, and any accommodation plans. Contact the district's Special Education Services office as soon as enrollment begins: mesquiteisd.org/departments.

Boundary Verification

Mesquite ISD boundary verification is particularly important for families buying in two specific areas: Sunnyvale and the northern fringe of Mesquite near Garland.

The Sunnyvale Situation

The city of Sunnyvale is split between two school districts: Mesquite ISD (western portions) and Sunnyvale ISD (the remainder). These are fundamentally different districts — Sunnyvale ISD has approximately 3,000 students and a different academic profile than Mesquite ISD's 38,000-student operation. A home with a "Sunnyvale, TX" mailing address or 75182 ZIP code may fall in either district. The boundary line does not follow obvious geographic markers like major roads — it cuts through residential neighborhoods. Making an offer without verifying your specific address against both district boundary maps can mean your child attends a different district than you planned, and this cannot be easily corrected post-closing through a transfer request.

Northern Mesquite / Garland ISD Overlap

A small number of addresses in northern Mesquite and Rowlett-adjacent areas fall within Garland ISD rather than Mesquite ISD, despite carrying Mesquite addresses. This is less common than the Sunnyvale split, but worth checking if you are buying near the Mesquite–Garland city boundary.

New Construction Areas

Newer residential developments in western Sunnyvale and along the eastern Mesquite perimeter may feed to unexpected campuses even within Mesquite ISD. The district's attendance zone tool is updated as growth occurs, but a campus assignment confirmed during construction can shift before move-in if boundary revisions are implemented. Confirm again at closing or just before school registration.

How to Verify

  • Mesquite ISD attendance zone lookup: mesquiteisd.org/attendance-zones
  • TEA school locator: tea.texas.gov/apps/school-directory
  • Sunnyvale ISD boundary check: sunnyvilleisd.net

Making an offer without verifying your specific address can mean your child attends a different school than you planned — and boundary maps do not always match common-sense geography. This is especially true in areas where city limits, ZIP codes, and district boundaries are all different lines.

District Finances & Tax Rate

2025 Certified Total Tax Rate: $1.097 per $100 assessed value

Based on Texas Comptroller data, Mesquite ISD's 2025 certified total tax rate is approximately $1.097 per $100 assessed valuation. On a $350,000 home (representative of Mesquite's median), that equates to roughly $3,840 per year in ISD property taxes, or about $320/month — before the Texas homestead exemption. Texas law provides a $100,000 homestead exemption on the school district portion of your tax bill, effectively removing the first $100,000 of appraised value from the taxable calculation. On that same $350,000 home with a homestead exemption, the taxable base is $250,000, making the ISD tax approximately $2,743/year (~$229/month).

This is a meaningful number for buyers comparing Mesquite to higher-priced suburbs. The tax rate is slightly lower than Garland ISD ($1.17) and Richardson ISD ($1.17), reflecting Mesquite ISD's current tax structure. That said, the lower assessed home values in Mesquite mean the absolute dollar amount of school taxes tends to be lower than in higher-value suburbs regardless of the rate comparison.

Per-Pupil Expenditure

Mesquite ISD invests approximately $11,000–$12,000 per student annually (estimated from TEA PEIMS data, 2023–24). A significant portion of this is driven by Title I federal funding allocated to the district's high proportion of economically disadvantaged students. For families, this means the district has real resources for intervention and support programming — which is funded separately from general education budgets.

Bond Elections

Mesquite ISD has periodically pursued bond elections for facility improvements. Buyers should check the district's financial transparency page for the most current bond status and any approved tax rate adjustments: mesquiteisd.org/financial-transparency. Approved bonds can incrementally adjust the I&S (interest and sinking) portion of the total tax rate.

How Mesquite ISD Compares

Families buying in eastern Dallas County most commonly cross-shop Mesquite ISD against Garland ISD and Richardson ISD. Here's how the key metrics compare:

District Size TEA Grade Tax Rate (2025) Niche Grade Best For
Mesquite ISD ~38,000 B (improved 2025) $1.097 B Affordable Mesquite housing; CTE, fine arts, mariachi
Garland ISD ~55,000 B (82) $1.17 A- Larger district, Rowlett/Sachse; broader AP access
Richardson ISD ~38,000 B (85) $1.17 A- North Dallas/Lake Highlands families; stronger college-prep reputation

Families prioritizing college-prep academics and a stronger third-party reputation will generally prefer Richardson ISD, though home prices in RISD territory (Richardson, North Dallas/Lake Highlands) run considerably higher than Mesquite. Garland ISD is a mid-point — larger than both, slightly better Niche rating than Mesquite ISD, serving communities like Rowlett and Sachse that have their own character. Mesquite ISD's advantage is typically housing value: comparable square footage costs less in Mesquite, and the 2025 B rating has narrowed the academic gap that was more visible when the district was C-rated.

None of these three districts offers the high-end college-prep profile of Frisco, Allen, or Carroll ISD. If that is the primary decision factor and budget allows, it may be worth expanding the housing search to the northern suburbs. If Mesquite is the right housing market for your family's situation, Mesquite ISD's trajectory — and its CTE and fine arts offerings — make it a more defensible choice than it was three to five years ago.

Community Feel

Mesquite has a genuine hometown character that shows up in the school system. Parents who choose Mesquite ISD and stay often cite strong booster club cultures at the high school level — athletics in particular draws committed family involvement, and the Friday night football culture is real. The district's five-campus structure means high school identity is tied to individual campuses rather than a single district brand, and those campus communities (Horn families, West Mesquite families, etc.) tend to be tight.

The fine arts community is a meaningful part of what keeps families engaged with Mesquite ISD. The mariachi program, in particular, draws students from across the district and has a competitive performance circuit that parents describe as both demanding and rewarding. For families with musically inclined kids, it's a concrete reason to choose a Mesquite ISD campus — not many districts this size have maintained a mariachi program at this level.

On the honest tradeoff side: parent discussions on Reddit and local community groups show that Mesquite ISD families are often more aware of campus-level variation than district-level reputation. The general sentiment is that the "best" Mesquite ISD experience depends heavily on which elementary and middle school your child attends and which high school they feed to. Families who research feeder patterns carefully and stay engaged at the campus level tend to report better experiences than those who evaluate the district from the top down. The 2025 TEA improvement cycle is recent enough that community perception hasn't fully caught up to the rating change — which may actually represent a buying opportunity for families who do the homework.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my Mesquite or Sunnyvale address guarantee enrollment in Mesquite ISD? ▾
No — and this is one of the most important things to verify before buying. Not all Mesquite city addresses fall within Mesquite ISD, and the Sunnyvale/Mesquite ISD boundary is particularly confusing. Parts of Sunnyvale are served by Sunnyvale ISD (a much smaller district with approximately 3,000 students), while the western portions are in Mesquite ISD. There is also a small northern Mesquite area that falls in Garland ISD. Always verify your specific street address at the Mesquite ISD attendance zone tool (mesquiteisd.org/attendance-zones) and confirm via the TEA school locator. Do not rely on ZIP codes or neighborhood names to determine district boundaries.
What is the difference between Mesquite ISD's four traditional high schools? ▾
All four traditional high schools — Horn, West Mesquite, North Mesquite, and Mesquite High School — received a B rating from the TEA in 2025, up from C the prior year. Horn High School is the largest (approximately 2,700 students) and has the highest reported AP participation rate at around 36%. North Mesquite ranks highest among the four on U.S. News at 579th in Texas. West Mesquite has seen enrollment growth with new residential construction nearby. Mesquite High School has a strong fine arts tradition including the district's mariachi and performing arts programs. In practice, the high school your child attends is determined by your address's feeder pattern, not campus choice — verify which high school your address feeds before buying.
How does Mesquite ISD handle special education and IEP transfers? ▾
Under Texas law, when a student with an active IEP or 504 Plan transfers to Mesquite ISD, the district must convene an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days to review and either honor or appropriately modify the existing plan. Services continue during this review period — there should be no gap in service. Bring all prior documentation: the full IEP, evaluation reports, and any accommodation plans. Contact the district's Special Education Services office as early as possible in the enrollment process — ideally before your child's first day. Do not wait for the campus to initiate contact. The quality of special education services varies by campus and by student need; the district's Special Services office is the right resource for assessing fit for your child's specific situation.
Mesquite ISD has a B TEA rating but some sites show a B or C on third-party rankings. Why do they differ? ▾
TEA A–F ratings are published by the state government and reflect a specific formula evaluating student achievement, academic growth, and equity across student groups — released annually, typically in summer. Third-party systems like Niche and GreatSchools use their own methodologies, which may weight different factors and update on different timelines. As of the date of this page, Mesquite ISD holds a TEA B rating (2025) and a Niche B grade. When these sources show different grades, the TEA rating is the official state accountability measure. Third-party ratings provide useful supplementary context but should not be treated as equal in authority to the TEA system.
What is Mesquite ISD's transfer policy for families moving within the district? ▾
Intra-district transfers (within Mesquite ISD, attending a campus other than your attendance-zone campus) are available on a space-available basis. They require a completed transfer application submitted to the district's Student Services office. Transfers are not guaranteed — capacity and grade-level availability determine approval. Approved transfers may also be rescinded in future years if enrollment at the requested campus exceeds capacity. Transfers for special programs, GT, fine arts, or CTE pathways follow the same process. For inter-district transfers (enrolling in Mesquite ISD from another district without living in the boundary), contact Student Services — acceptance is at the district's discretion based on capacity.
How does Mesquite ISD's tax rate compare to neighboring districts? ▾
Mesquite ISD's 2025 certified total tax rate is approximately $1.097 per $100 assessed valuation. For comparison: Garland ISD is approximately $1.17 and Richardson ISD is approximately $1.17. Mesquite ISD's rate is slightly lower than both neighbors. That said, the bigger financial variable for most buyers is home price — Mesquite generally has more affordable housing than Richardson or north Garland/Rowlett, so the total tax bill (rate × assessed value) ends up lower in Mesquite even before the rate advantage. All Texas homeowners who qualify for a homestead exemption receive a $100,000 exemption on the school district portion of their taxes, which meaningfully lowers the taxable base.
What makes Mesquite ISD's fine arts program distinctive? ▾
Mesquite ISD maintains a mariachi program — genuinely unusual for a DFW suburban district of this size. The program has a competitive history and is embedded in the district's fine arts culture, which also includes band, choir, orchestra, theater, visual arts, and other performing arts pathways. For families with musically inclined children, particularly those with cultural ties to mariachi music, the program is a real differentiator. Fine arts programs are available at all grade levels districtwide; advanced performance ensembles at the high school level may have audition or placement requirements at the campus level.
