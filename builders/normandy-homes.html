Who Is Normandy Homes? Score 97 A+

Normandy Homes has been building in Dallas–Fort Worth since 2012, headquartered in Plano, Texas. The company operates as a subsidiary of Green Brick Partners, Inc. (NYSE: GRBK), a publicly traded homebuilder and land developer — which gives Normandy access to strong financial backing while keeping all construction and design decisions local. Green Brick's model is intentionally decentralized: Normandy runs its own sales, design, and construction operations without corporate interference from the parent.

The builder has earned more than 20 homebuilding awards since founding and focuses exclusively on the DFW market. Normandy's construction managers average 17 years of industry experience, and its community sales managers average over 12 years. Unlike national-volume builders that rotate staff frequently, this consistency in field leadership is notable — and buyer reviews reflect it. The company's sales territory is concentrated in the high-demand north Collin County corridor (Frisco, Prosper, Celina, McKinney) plus Mansfield in Tarrant County.

What Sets Normandy Apart

Normandy occupies a specific lane in the DFW market: move-up and upper-move-up homes with architectural detail that stands out from standard production builders — without crossing into true semi-custom or luxury territory. Here's what distinguishes them:

European-Inspired Architecture as Standard

Normandy's exterior design language — brick detailing, varied rooflines, stone accents, and elevated façades — is built into every plan rather than treated as an upgrade. This is distinct from most production builders in DFW, where curb appeal upgrades are sold through the design center. Buyers consistently cite exterior appearance as a primary reason for choosing Normandy over competitors at the same price point.

Green Brick Financial Backing

As a Green Brick Partners company, Normandy has direct access to land acquisition capital and construction financing that independent regional builders typically lack. This matters for relocating families: it means Normandy can open communities in the most competitive north DFW land markets (Frisco, Prosper, Celina) without the financial constraints that limit smaller builders' lot pipelines.

Experienced Field Leadership

Community sales managers averaging 12+ years and construction managers averaging 17+ years of experience creates meaningful consistency in how Normandy delivers homes. Buyers working from out of state — which describes a large share of DFW relocating families — benefit from that stability: fewer staff changes mid-build, better communication continuity, and construction managers who have solved most problems before.

Normandy Homes Warranty — Builder-Backed 1-2-6 Coverage

Normandy Homes provides a builder-backed limited warranty: 1 year on workmanship, 2 years on distribution systems, and 6 years on structural elements. The structural component is administered by 2-10 Home Buyers Warranty (2-10 HBW), a third-party structural warranty company. Texas law does not mandate a specific structural warranty duration — the 10-year structural coverage offered by some DFW builders (Perry Homes, David Weekley) is an industry standard choice, not a legal requirement. Normandy's 6-year structural coverage is below that voluntary industry benchmark; buyers should factor this into their builder comparison. Per the official Normandy FAQ, the standard coverage is 1-2-6.

How Normandy Handles Warranty Repairs

Normandy services its own warranty requests for workmanship and systems issues through a dedicated warranty team rather than routing all requests through the sales office. Buyers submit warranty requests through the online portal at normandyhomes.com/warranty-request/. Normandy has publicly stated that it employs in-house warranty staff, which buyers in reviews have noted as generally responsive in the early months post-close. The 6-year structural component is administered by 2-10 Home Buyers Warranty, a national third-party company with its own claims process separate from Normandy.

What the warranty doesn't cover

Standard exclusions apply: buyer-caused damage, normal wear and tear, landscaping and sod, appliances (covered by manufacturer warranty), settlement cracks that don't affect structural performance, and cosmetic issues that appear after the 30-day post-close orientation window. The structural warranty through 2-10 HBW covers major structural defects — it does not cover general maintenance items, exterior caulking, or cosmetic concrete cracks in flatwork (a common BBB complaint theme for Normandy). Always read the 2-10 HBW warranty booklet carefully before close; the definitions of "structural defect" are specific and narrower than buyers often expect.

Honest Assessment — What Families Love and What to Know

Here's what actually drives the choice for or against Normandy for relocating families.

Strengths

  • Distinctive exterior design — European-inspired façades with brick, stone, and varied rooflines are built into every plan — not sold as upgrades. Neighbors frequently remark on curb appeal in buyer reviews.
  • Excellent BBB standing — A+ grade, BBB-accredited since 2015, only 6 complaints filed in the last 3 years for a builder with 13+ years in a competitive market. That complaint volume is low relative to peers of similar size.
  • Prime north DFW suburb access — Communities in Frisco, Prosper, Celina, and McKinney put families inside Frisco ISD, Prosper ISD, Celina ISD, and McKinney ISD — some of the most consistently high-performing districts in North Texas.
  • Experienced field teams — 12-year average sales manager tenure and 17-year construction manager average is genuinely unusual in production homebuilding, where turnover is common. Consistent personnel reduces mid-build surprises for out-of-state buyers.
  • Green Brick financial stability — As a publicly traded parent company (GRBK), Green Brick Partners' balance sheet provides land pipeline security. Normandy is unlikely to pause or cancel communities mid-construction due to financing issues the way some smaller regional builders have.

Things to Know

  • Structural warranty is 6 years, not 10 — Many DFW builders voluntarily offer 10-year structural coverage (Perry Homes, David Weekley, Highland Homes). Normandy's 6-year structural through 2-10 HBW falls below that voluntary benchmark. Texas law does not require a specific structural warranty period, but 10 years is the industry norm buyers should expect. Confirm current terms in writing before signing.
  • Design center costs can escalate significantly — Base prices are attractive, but Normandy's Design Studio upgrades — flooring, countertops, cabinetry — add up fast. BBB complaint data includes pricing disputes tied to design center expectations vs. actual cost. Budget conservatively for design selections.
  • Limited structural customization — Normandy is a production builder; floor plan layouts are fixed before the foundation is poured. Structural changes (moving walls, adding rooms) are generally not available. Buyers who need true flexibility should look at David Weekley or Drees Custom Homes instead.
  • Eliant community scores are moderate — Community-level Eliant data for Bretton Woods (Frisco) shows construction experience and installation/workmanship scores around 3.5 out of 5 — solid but not exceptional. Finish quality has been a recurring theme in both Eliant reviews and the occasional BBB complaint.

Who Normandy is the right fit for — and when to look elsewhere

Normandy is a strong fit for families relocating to Frisco, Celina, Prosper, or McKinney who want move-up design quality ($450k–$800k range) and care strongly about exterior curb appeal and street presence. If your priority is interior customization flexibility, a longer structural warranty, or a lower price point, Normandy is not the right builder. Consider Highland Homes for more structural options at similar prices, or First Texas Homes for better value at the lower end of the DFW move-up market. If design detail is your top priority and budget is flexible, David Weekley and Drees Custom offer more customization at comparable or higher price points.

Normandy Homes Incentives & Current Promotions

Incentives Change — Here's How to Find Them

Normandy has historically offered closing cost assistance, rate buydown programs, and design credit packages tied to their preferred lender, Green Brick Mortgage. Their website's "Select Savings" page (normandyhomes.com/select-savings/) publishes current promotions — as of research date, Normandy has advertised bonus savings on select move-in-ready homes. Incentive availability varies by community and lot phase; end-of-phase deals are typically the most aggressive.

Closing Cost Assistance

Closing cost credits toward title, lender fees, and prepaids — typically tied to using Green Brick Mortgage as the lender. Confirm exact dollar amount and terms in your purchase agreement.

Rate Buydown Programs

Normandy has offered 2-1 buydown programs in rate-sensitive markets. A 2-1 buydown reduces your first-year rate by 2 points and second-year by 1 point — check the current terms on their promotions page.

Design Credit Packages

Select communities may offer design studio credits — dollars toward flooring, countertops, or fixtures at the Design Studio. Read the fine print; credits often apply to a limited selection of items rather than any upgrade.

Important: Builder incentives are almost always tied to using their preferred lender. That's not inherently bad — sometimes the rate is genuinely competitive — but you should always get a competing quote from an independent lender first. Green Brick Mortgage is Normandy's in-house lender; compare their offer against a local bank or mortgage broker before committing. A buyer's agent can help you compare the true cost of the deal across lenders.

Normandy Homes Floor Plans & Design Process

Normandy's DFW floor plans range from approximately 1,800 to 4,000+ square feet, priced from roughly $370k to $800k depending on community, lot size, and design selections. Plans emphasize open-concept living with elevated ceilings, large owner suites, gourmet kitchen configurations, and flexible study or media room options — functional layouts for modern family living rather than architectural novelty. The architectural detail that sets Normandy apart is expressed primarily on the exterior and in interior standard features, not through a wide menu of structural options.

Production Floor Plans with Design Center Flexibility

Normandy is a production builder: floor plan structural layouts are fixed before construction begins. Buyers choose from Normandy's plan portfolio for each community — they cannot move walls, add rooms, or change the footprint. What buyers do control is a wide range of interior finish selections made at the Design Studio: flooring, cabinetry, countertops, lighting packages, plumbing fixtures, tile, and similar finish elements. This is meaningful customization within set structural boundaries — it's the right model for buyers who want a well-designed home without the project management complexity of true custom construction.

Design Studio

Normandy operates its own Design Studio where buyers work with design consultants to select interior finishes. The studio is staffed by Normandy's in-house team rather than outsourced to a third-party design center. For relocating families who are moving virtually or managing the process from out of state, Normandy's team offers virtual appointments — an important practical consideration. Budget carefully: Design Studio selections are where base-priced homes often increase by $40,000–$80,000 or more depending on choices. Families who set a hard budget ceiling should establish it before the Design Studio appointment, not after.

Standard features included at base price

Normandy includes several features standard that other DFW builders sell as upgrades: elevated ceilings (10-foot on main level in most plans), Lennox HVAC systems with a 10-year parts warranty, stainless steel kitchen appliances, architectural exterior brick and stone detailing, and energy-efficient construction including spray foam insulation on exterior walls (verify at contract — standards vary by community). The Lennox HVAC coverage is builder-backed and separate from the structural warranty. Confirm the specific standard features list for your community and plan at time of contract, as they can vary.

Normandy Homes DFW — Frequently Asked Questions

Is Normandy Homes a good builder?

Normandy Homes has a strong track record in DFW for families prioritizing architectural design and north Collin County locations. They hold an A+ BBB grade (accredited since 2015) with only 6 complaints in 3 years — a low complaint rate for a builder that has been active in the DFW market for 13+ years. Community-level Eliant review data shows construction experience scores around 3.5 out of 5, which is solid without being exceptional. How much do Normandy Homes homes cost in DFW?

Normandy's base prices in DFW typically start around $370,000 and extend to approximately $800,000, with the majority of active communities concentrated in the $450,000–$650,000 range as of the most recent data. Base prices reflect the plan and lot only — Design Studio selections for flooring, countertops, cabinetry, and fixture upgrades add cost on top of base. Buyers who fully customize through the design center commonly add $40,000–$100,000 or more above base price. Always establish a total budget that includes design selections, lot premiums, and closing costs — not just the base plan price shown in marketing materials.

Where does Normandy Homes build in DFW?

Normandy's active DFW communities are in Frisco, Prosper, Celina, McKinney, and Mansfield as of the most recent data. The north Collin County concentration (Frisco, Prosper, Celina, McKinney) puts buyers inside four of the most consistently high-performing school districts in North Texas — Frisco ISD, Prosper ISD, Celina ISD, and McKinney ISD. Community availability changes as phases sell out — verify current active communities at normandyhomes.com or through Kristen's ShowingNew portal.

What is Normandy Homes' warranty?

Normandy provides a 1-2-6 warranty: 1 year on workmanship (finish defects, trim, caulking, tile), 2 years on distribution systems (plumbing, electrical wiring, HVAC ducting), and 6 years on structural elements through 2-10 Home Buyers Warranty. Texas law does not mandate a specific structural warranty period — the 10-year coverage offered by some competitors is a voluntary industry standard, not a legal floor. Normandy's 6-year structural is below that benchmark; compare carefully when evaluating builders. HVAC equipment is covered by the Lennox manufacturer warranty (10 years on parts), separate from the builder warranty. Confirm current warranty terms in your purchase contract before signing.

How long does Normandy Homes take to build a house in DFW?

Normandy does not publicly specify build timelines on their website. Yes. Normandy Homes is a subsidiary of Green Brick Partners, Inc. (NYSE: GRBK), a publicly traded homebuilder and land developer based in Plano, Texas.
