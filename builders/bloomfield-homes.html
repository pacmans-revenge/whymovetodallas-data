Who Is Bloomfield Homes? Score 84 A

Bloomfield Homes was founded in 2004 in Mansfield, Texas, starting with just seven homes built from new floor plans developed by the Harder Organization. Over the next two decades, the company grew organically into DFW's 5th largest homebuilder by volume, delivering more than 20,000 homes for local families — while remaining privately held and family-oriented throughout. In 2021, the Greater Fort Worth Builders Association recognized Bloomfield as Homebuilder of the Year. The company is now headquartered in Southlake, TX.

Bloomfield builds exclusively in Texas — and almost entirely in DFW. That singular focus means their production systems, subcontractor relationships, and design center are all calibrated to the North Texas market. Their signature approach is an all-included features model: instead of stripping the base price and upselling buyers on every finish, Bloomfield bundles a wide range of features as standard. That distinction matters most at the contract table, when you're comparing true apples-to-apples cost with other production builders.

What Sets Bloomfield Apart

Two things distinguish Bloomfield from other production builders in the same price tier: what comes standard, and where they build.

All-Included Features at Base Price

Bloomfield includes stone exterior elevations, rotunda entryways, granite countertops, custom-built cabinetry, stainless steel appliances (range, microwave, dishwasher), architectural windows, a full sprinkler system, upgraded landscaping, and large covered front and rear porches — all at the base contract price. These are upgrade-line items at most competing production builders. For relocating families comparing true move-in costs, this bundled approach often means Bloomfield's effective price is closer to market reality than a lower-looking base price from a builder that charges for each finish separately.

DFW-Exclusive Focus

Bloomfield builds only in DFW. Unlike national volume builders that divide their attention and supply chains across dozens of markets, Bloomfield's entire operation — sales, design, construction, and warranty — is focused on North Texas. Their trades know the soil conditions, their lot selection reflects local drainage realities, and their sales teams have genuine community-level knowledge. That's a practical advantage for relocating families who are making high-stakes decisions quickly.

Value-Corridor Market Positioning

Bloomfield concentrates in DFW's high-growth value corridors — Midlothian and the southwest suburbs, Aubrey and Denton in the north, Royse City in the east, and Melissa in north Collin County. These markets consistently deliver more square footage, larger lots, and newer infrastructure per dollar than established premium suburbs. For families relocating from high-cost markets where $600K felt modest, Bloomfield's DFW communities often represent a genuine lifestyle upgrade.

Bloomfield Homes Warranty — One Key Number to Know

Texas law (RCLA) sets minimum warranty floors for new construction: 1 year for workmanship defects, 2 years for mechanical systems, and 10 years for structural. Bloomfield's administered warranty, managed through third-party provider American eWarranty, meets the 1-year and 2-year minimums. However, Bloomfield's written structural warranty runs 6 years — shorter than the 10-year structural coverage offered by most competing DFW builders including Highland Homes, Perry Homes, Meritage, and David Weekley. That gap is real and worth factoring into your decision, especially if you are buying in a market with expansive clay soils or drainage variability. Source: bloomfieldhomes.com/warranty.

The 6-year structural warranty — what it means for buyers

Most major DFW builders (Highland Homes, Perry Homes, Meritage, David Weekley, D.R. Horton) provide 10 years of administered structural coverage. Bloomfield's American eWarranty policy runs 6 years. Texas RCLA implied warranties provide some independent protection, but they require litigation to enforce — the administered warranty is what buyers actually use day-to-day. Ask your buyer's agent to explain the practical difference before you sign. If structural warranty coverage is a priority for your family, compare Bloomfield's written warranty terms against the alternatives before deciding.

How Bloomfield Handles Warranty Repairs

Bloomfield processes warranty service requests through American eWarranty — homeowners submit claims online or by phone. The warranty is transferable to a new owner if the home is sold during the coverage period, but written notice must be submitted to American eWarranty within 30 days of the resale closing date, or the warranty becomes void. Per BBB complaint data, warranty responsiveness has been a recurring concern for some buyers — particularly around electrical defects and roofing issues. Bloomfield's standard process includes a scheduled repair appointment, but escalation may be necessary for complex or recurring defects.

What the warranty doesn't cover

Normal wear and tear, cosmetic cracks that develop after the first-year period, landscaping beyond the initial installation, homeowner-caused damage, and items covered under manufacturer warranties (appliances, HVAC units, water heaters) once those product warranties expire. Read the American eWarranty documentation carefully at closing — exclusions are specific and matter for Texas soil and climate conditions.

Bloomfield Homes Incentives & Current Promotions

Incentives Change — Here's How to Find Them

Bloomfield Homes runs year-round incentive programs across their communities, primarily structured as closing cost credits and interest rate buydowns. Documented offers have included seller contributions in the range of 4% of the purchase price, applicable toward closing costs or a rate buydown. Incentives vary by community and by where a phase is in its sell-down — end-of-phase lots often carry enhanced packages. The amounts change frequently and are not always advertised publicly; asking the sales agent directly (with your Realtor present) is how you find out what's actually available that day.

Rate Buydown

Bloomfield can purchase points on your loan to reduce the interest rate — either temporarily for years 1–2 (2-1 buydown) or permanently. With a typical DFW mortgage, a 1-point rate reduction saves hundreds per month. This is usually available when using Bloomfield's preferred lender.

Closing Cost Credits

At select communities, Bloomfield contributes directly toward your closing costs. This effectively lowers the cash needed at closing and is most useful for buyers who have stretched to maximize purchase price in a community they love.

Phase-Close Deals

As Bloomfield's lot sections fill out, they often offer enhanced packages on remaining lots — sometimes combining price adjustments, design center credits, or extra closing cost help. Kristen tracks active phase timelines across Bloomfield communities.

Important: Builder incentives are almost always tied to using their preferred lender. That's not inherently bad — sometimes the rate is genuinely competitive — but you should always get a competing quote from an independent lender first. A buyer's agent can help you compare the true cost of the deal across lenders.

Bloomfield Homes Floor Plans & Design Process

Bloomfield offers two main plan collections in DFW. Their Classic collection spans approximately 1,800 to over 4,400 square feet, targeting the move-up buyer who wants more bedrooms, a media room, or a larger kitchen footprint. The Elements collection ranges from about 1,500 to 3,000 square feet and focuses on efficient modern layouts at a lower entry price. Both collections carry Bloomfield's full standard features package — Elements is not a stripped-down version, just a smaller footprint at a lower price.

Production Floor Plans with Design Selections

Bloomfield operates as a production builder — the floor plans and structural layouts are fixed at the community level. Buyers choose from the plan set available in their specific community. Structural changes (moving walls, adding rooms, changing the floor plan layout) are not available after signing. This is standard for production builders in DFW; it is how Bloomfield controls build timeline and cost. What buyers do control is the design center selections from the plans available: exterior elevation choice, interior finishes (cabinetry style, countertop material and color, flooring, tile, fixture package), and lot position within the community.

Design Center

Bloomfield operates an in-house design studio where buyers finalize their selections after going under contract. Virtual and remote appointments are available — relevant for families relocating from out of state who cannot travel to DFW mid-build for a design meeting. Kristen can connect clients with the right Bloomfield sales contact and help prioritize which selections have the most impact on resale value in the specific market.

Bloomfield Homes DFW — Frequently Asked Questions

Bloomfield Homes scores 84/A on our 100-point builder index, driven by a strong all-included features package, 20+ years of DFW-exclusive experience, and solid TrustBuilder reviews from 1,038 verified buyers (4.3 stars). The biggest honest flag is their 6-year administered structural warranty, which is below the 10-year coverage most competing DFW builders provide. For the price point and value corridors they build in, Bloomfield is a solid choice — with that warranty caveat clearly understood before signing.
