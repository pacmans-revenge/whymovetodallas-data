Who Is David Weekley Homes? BBB A+

David Weekley Homes was founded in Houston, Texas in 1976 by David Weekley, then 23 years old, and his brother Dick. David had left a previous builder after a dispute over a bonus he had earned, and started his company with an explicit goal: build a culture where people are treated differently. That founding principle — and the family of unusually loyal Team Members it produced — is what distinguishes David Weekley from the publicly traded national builders that now dominate new construction volume. The company is still privately held, still headquartered in Houston, and still led by the founding family. David Weekley serves as Chairman; John Johnson, who joined decades ago, serves as President and CEO.

David Weekley expanded into Dallas–Fort Worth in 1985 during the Houston oil bust, and DFW has been a core market for over 40 years. Today the company operates in 22 markets across 13 states, has served more than 125,000 homebuyers, and has appeared on Fortune magazine's "100 Best Companies to Work For" list 19 times — an indicator of low turnover among construction managers and sales staff that matters more than most buyers realize. The company is the only builder to win the Triple Crown of American Home Building, which includes "America's Best Builder," "National Housing Quality Award," and "National Builder of the Year." In DFW, David Weekley builds across the Denton County and Collin County growth corridors — from entry-level garden-series homes in Fate to estate-series homes exceeding 5,000 sq ft in Flower Mound.

What Sets David Weekley Apart

David Weekley competes in the semi-custom tier — above high-volume production builders like D.R. Horton but below true custom builders. The differentiators that justify the price premium are specific and worth understanding before you visit a model home.

FlexSpace℠ — Structural Customization Before the Pour

David Weekley's FlexSpace program lets buyers choose how bonus rooms function structurally — not just cosmetically. A room can be designed from the start as a media room with soundproofing prep, a true fifth bedroom with full egress, a dedicated study, a playroom, or a hobby room. These are not cosmetic finishes applied at the design center; they are structural layout choices made before the foundation is poured. For families relocating from California who are accustomed to flex space in newer construction, this is the most relevant differentiator David Weekley offers over lower-tier builders in the same price band.

In-House Design Center with Remote Access

David Weekley operates its own DFW Design Center staffed by in-house Design Consultants — not a third-party showroom. Buyers select from curated palettes for flooring, cabinets, countertops, lighting, hardware, and exterior finishes (brick, stone, Outdoor Living configurations). For relocating families who are buying remotely, David Weekley offers virtual design center appointments — a meaningful logistics advantage when you're comparing finishes from out of state. The interior and exterior selection process is more structured than what you get at lower-tier production builders, and less open-ended than true custom.

Dedicated Warranty Service Representative

Each new David Weekley buyer is assigned a personal Warranty Service Representative who stays with them after closing. That rep conducts a walk-through approximately 11 months after closing — just before the 1-year workmanship warranty expires — to identify items needing attention. This is a documented, consistent process, not a call center system. It doesn't change the warranty terms (1-2-10, which matches Texas minimum), but it does change the experience of using the warranty. BBB complaints about David Weekley tend to cluster around warranty response timing — not about the warranty being denied — which suggests the process works but the timeline for repairs is a recurring friction point for buyers.

Privately Held — Culture and Incentive Alignment

Because David Weekley is not publicly traded, the company is not under quarterly earnings pressure to cut subcontractor costs or reduce build specifications. This matters in practice: David Weekley's construction managers tend to have longer tenures than at comparable volume builders, which translates to more consistent quality control over time. The 19-year streak on Fortune's Best Companies list is a proxy for low turnover — and in homebuilding, consistent site management is a more reliable quality signal than marketing claims.

David Weekley Homes Warranty — Meets Texas Legal Minimum

David Weekley's warranty is a standard 1-2-10 structure: one year on workmanship and materials, two years on mechanical systems, and ten years on structural elements. Texas law (the Residential Construction Liability Act, or RCLA) requires exactly these minimums — so David Weekley's warranty matches what the law requires, without exceeding it. That is not a criticism; most regional and national builders use the same 1-2-10 baseline. It is worth knowing when you're comparing to builders like Highland Homes, which covers workmanship for two years (double the minimum). What distinguishes David Weekley's warranty program is not the term length but the delivery model: every buyer gets a named Warranty Service Representative and a proactive 11-month walk-through before the workmanship period expires.

How David Weekley Handles Warranty Repairs

Each buyer is assigned a personal Warranty Service Representative at closing. Warranty service requests are submitted through an online portal at davidweekleyhomes.com/contact/warrantyrequest. The assigned rep is the point of contact for all repairs — not a call center. Approximately 11 months after closing, the rep conducts a proactive walk-through to capture any outstanding workmanship items before the 1-year window closes. David Weekley employs in-house warranty staff rather than fully outsourcing repairs, which generally means more control over scheduling — though BBB complaint data shows that response timelines are the most common friction point for buyers, particularly in busy markets.

What the warranty doesn't cover

Normal wear and tear, cosmetic items identified but not noted at the final pre-closing walk-through, damage caused by homeowner modifications or negligence, and items covered by separate manufacturer or appliance warranties. Grading and drainage around the home — a recurring theme in BBB complaints — is covered only if a defect prevents water from flowing away from the foundation; minor ponding in the yard after heavy rain typically falls outside the workmanship scope. Bring a third-party inspector to the pre-close walk-through and document everything in writing before you sign.

⚠ Things to Know

  • Warranty meets minimum — not above it — the 1-year workmanship coverage matches Texas RCLA requirements. If a warranty term that beats the legal minimum matters to your family (it should), compare to Highland Homes' 2-year workmanship coverage before signing with David Weekley.

Get a competing quote from an independent lender before committing — the incentive math sometimes works out, and sometimes the savings on rate or credits are offset by less competitive mortgage terms.

David Weekley Homes Floor Plans & Design Process

David Weekley DFW offers multiple product series spanning 1,662 sq ft (Garden Series, Denton) to 5,282 sq ft (Estate Series, Flower Mound). Plans range from 3 to 5 bedrooms across the DFW portfolio, with prices from the mid-$300s in eastern corridors (Fate, Rockwall) to over $1.2M in Flower Mound's Whyburn development. Unlike production builders with locked floor plans, David Weekley allows structural modifications before the pour — which makes the floor plan process meaningfully more involved and more personalized.

Semi-Custom Structural Options

Within each plan series, buyers can modify room function, opt for extended outdoor living areas, add or remove garages, change owner's retreat placement (some plans offer a first-floor primary suite option), and select from multiple elevation styles for the exterior. These structural changes are locked in at contract — after the foundation is poured, the layout is fixed. The design center process for finishes comes afterward. For families who have specific functional requirements (separate guest suite, dedicated home office on the main floor, extended garage for a boat or RV), asking David Weekley's sales consultant about pre-pour structural options early in the conversation is the right first step.

Design Center

David Weekley's DFW Design Center is an in-house facility staffed by Design Consultants employed by David Weekley — not an outsourced showroom. Buyers work through curated selections for flooring (hard surface, carpet, tile), cabinetry (door style, finish, hardware), countertops (granite, quartz, other stone), lighting packages, plumbing fixtures, and exterior finishes including brick, stone, and Outdoor Living configuration. Virtual appointments are available for remote buyers — Kristen has worked with relocating families who completed design center selections entirely over video call without needing to fly to DFW until they were ready to finalize.

Standard features included at base price

Base David Weekley homes include granite or quartz countertops in the kitchen, stainless appliances (range, dishwasher, microwave), hard-surface flooring in common areas, a structured wiring package, and Energy-efficient windows and insulation. Appliance packages, smart home technology, and upgraded flooring are design center add-ons priced separately. Unlike some builders (Perry Homes includes a smart thermostat and Wi-Fi switch as standard), smart home technology is an upgrade at David Weekley — factor this into your true base-price comparison when cross-shopping.

David Weekley Homes DFW — Frequently Asked Questions

Is David Weekley Homes a good builder?

David Weekley Homes holds an A+ BBB rating with 17 complaints in the past three years in the DFW market — a relatively low complaint volume for a builder of their production size. Founded in 1976, the company is one of America's largest privately held builders with 50 years of Texas experience and 40+ years specifically in DFW. The primary strengths are design flexibility (FlexSpace structural options), a dedicated Warranty Service Representative assigned to each buyer, and a corporate culture that has produced 19 consecutive appearances on Fortune's "100 Best Companies to Work For" list — which translates to lower turnover among site managers and more consistent build quality. The honest caveat: their warranty matches Texas legal minimums (1-2-10) but doesn't exceed them, and BBB complaints follow a consistent pattern of warranty repair response time rather than outright denials. For families who want a semi-custom experience with a proven company, David Weekley is one of the more reliable choices in DFW's mid-to-upper price range.

How much do David Weekley Homes cost in DFW?

David Weekley DFW homes range from approximately $362,000 for entry-level Garden Series plans in eastern DFW (Fate) to over $1.2 million for Estate Series homes in Flower Mound. Budget 10–20% above the base plan price for a fully selected home, and ask for an itemized design center estimate before comparing David Weekley's price to a competitor's quote.

What is David Weekley Homes' warranty?

David Weekley offers a standard 1-2-10 warranty: one year on workmanship and materials, two years on mechanical systems (plumbing, electrical, central HVAC), and ten years on structural elements including the foundation. This matches Texas RCLA legal minimums exactly — it is the same baseline most large builders use. What differentiates David Weekley's warranty program is the delivery: each buyer receives a named Warranty Service Representative at closing and a proactive walk-through approximately 11 months into ownership, before the workmanship period expires. Warranty service requests are submitted online and managed by the assigned rep. For comparison, Highland Homes offers a 2-year workmanship warranty (double the minimum), which is a meaningful advantage if near-term cosmetic and workmanship coverage matters to your family.

How long does David Weekley take to build a house in DFW?

Typical David Weekley build times in DFW range from approximately 8 to 14 months from contract signing to closing, depending on plan complexity, current permit and inspection scheduling in the municipality, and the availability of design center selections.
