Who Is Ashton Woods? Score 75 B+

Ashton Woods was founded in 1989 and is headquartered in Alpharetta, Georgia. Operating across 12 US metro areas — including DFW, Austin, San Antonio, Houston, Atlanta, Phoenix, Tampa, Denver, Charlotte, Raleigh, and Jacksonville — the company has built its reputation as a design-forward national builder focused on the move-up and semi-custom buyer. Ken Balogh serves as CEO and President. In 2023, Builder Magazine named them Builder of the Year. They appear on Newsweek's Most Trustworthy Companies in America list for 2025, and Lifestory Research named them the Most Trusted Builder in America for two consecutive years in independent consumer surveys.

Unlike purely production-focused national builders, Ashton Woods runs "The Studio" — an in-house design center with professional consultants who guide buyers through personalized finish selections. This is a meaningful differentiator for relocating families who are doing much of their home search remotely and want more than a basic finish package. Their DFW communities are concentrated in East and North Collin County — Princeton, Melissa, Wylie, and Plano — plus an urban infill location in Dallas proper at Buckner Terrace. The company is privately held and does not publish annual home-delivery volume publicly.

Ashton Woods Warranty — Meets Texas Minimum

Texas law (RCLA) sets a floor for new home warranties: 1 year on workmanship, 2 years on mechanical systems, and 10 years on structural defects. Ashton Woods' standard 1-2-10 program matches this floor exactly — it does not exceed it on workmanship or systems. The 10-year structural warranty is administered through 2-10 HBW, a national third-party warranty company. This matters practically: workmanship and systems claims go to Ashton Woods' own warranty team, while structural claims go to 2-10 HBW for evaluation and resolution — two separate processes with different timelines and contacts.

How Ashton Woods Handles Warranty Repairs

Warranty requests are submitted through the online portal at ashtonwoods.com/warranty-ticket. Ashton Woods' own warranty team handles workmanship and systems claims during the first two years. Structural claims in years 1–10 are routed to 2-10 HBW for evaluation. The warranty is transferable to the next owner during the active coverage period. Based on the Carrollton, TX BBB record (8 complaints in 3 years as of Q2 2026), response time on warranty requests is a documented area of concern — multiple complaints cite slow acknowledgment or no follow-up. Families should submit all warranty requests through the online portal in writing, retain confirmation and timestamps, and follow up in writing at regular intervals if a response does not come within the builder's published timeframe.

What the warranty doesn't cover

Normal settling, shrinkage, and expansion of materials are typically excluded from workmanship coverage after a short initial period — relevant in DFW given the region's clay-heavy soils and temperature swings. Cosmetic issues that fall within the builder's published tolerances are also excluded. Yard grading problems — a recurring theme in Ashton Woods' BBB complaint record — may be covered under workmanship, but coverage can be contested. Damage caused by the homeowner's own modifications, landscaping additions, or irrigation systems is excluded. Always request the written warranty document at contract signing, not at closing — review it before you commit, not after.

Ashton Woods Floor Plans & Design Process

In DFW, Ashton Woods offers floor plans ranging from approximately 1,520 to 4,107 square feet across 3–5 bedrooms, with base pricing from $315,000 to $899,000 depending on the community, collection, and lot. Monticello Park in Princeton serves the entry-to-move-up buyer at the lower end of that range; Inspiration in Wylie — a master-planned community — extends into the high $800Ks with their largest plans and broadest collection selection.

Semi-Custom Design Process

Ashton Woods describes their approach as semi-custom. Before construction begins, buyers can select from pre-approved structural options — adding a bedroom, extending a living area, modifying a garage layout — and then make all finish selections through The Studio. This is more flexibility than a pure production builder where the plan is fixed, but it is not fully custom construction. Structural options must be locked in early in the contract phase, before framing. If you purchase a completed spec home (quick move-in), all finishes are already selected and structural modifications are no longer available.

The Studio Design Center

The Studio by Ashton Woods operates in the Dallas area with professional design consultants on staff. Buyers select flooring, cabinetry, countertops, backsplash, hardware, plumbing fixtures, lighting, and exterior finishes from curated options organized by design aesthetic. Virtual appointment formats are available for buyers relocating from out of state who cannot travel for an in-person Studio session — a practical advantage for the families Kristen works with most. The curated catalog approach keeps selections manageable and design-coherent, which most families find less overwhelming than fully open-spec custom choices.

Standard features and what's typically an upgrade

Ashton Woods' standard inclusions vary by community and collection. Core structural and mechanical components (framing, roofing, HVAC, insulation, plumbing rough-in) are standard across all plans. Finish-level standards at base price cover entry-level package options — the finishes shown in the model home are almost always upgraded selections. Premium countertops, LVP flooring throughout, enhanced cabinetry, and extended tile work are typically Studio upgrades. Families should ask the sales agent for a written base-price specification list before visiting The Studio. Budget separately for Studio upgrades — buyers commonly spend $30,000–$60,000+ at the design center depending on plan size and desired finish level.

How long does Ashton Woods take to build a house in DFW?

Ashton Woods typically takes 6–9 months from contract to close on a new build in DFW, though timelines vary based on floor plan size, Studio customization decisions, community scheduling, and local permit processing. Completed spec homes (quick move-ins) can close in 30–60 days and are a good option for families on a firm relocation deadline. If building from scratch with Studio customization, plan for 8–10 months in your relocation timeline as a conservative buffer. Ashton Woods does not publish guaranteed build-time commitments publicly — ask the community sales agent for the current projected schedule for the specific plan and lot you're considering, and get the projected timeline in writing.

Do I have to use Velocio Mortgage to buy an Ashton Woods home?

No, you are not required to use Velocio Mortgage. However, Ashton Woods' incentive programs — rate buydowns and closing cost credits — are typically conditional on using Velocio, their affiliated lender. This means bringing your own lender may cost you the incentive. The right approach is to get a full loan quote from Velocio and a competing quote from an independent lender, then compare the total cost of the deal: interest rate, loan origination fees, discount points, monthly payment, and the cash value of the incentive. In some cases the buydown makes Velocio the better overall deal; in others, an independent lender's base terms are competitive enough that the incentive doesn't change the math meaningfully. A buyer's agent can help you run this comparison before you go under contract.
