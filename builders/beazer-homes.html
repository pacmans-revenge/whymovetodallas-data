Who Is Beazer Homes? Score 90 A+

Beazer Homes USA, Inc. is a publicly traded national homebuilder (NYSE: BZH) headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia. The company traces its roots to 1985 when the British Beazer construction group acquired Cohn Communities, an Atlanta-based builder, and expanded aggressively into U.S. markets throughout the late 1980s and 1990s. Beazer Homes USA was incorporated and went public on the NYSE in 1994. Today it operates in roughly 13 states and has closed hundreds of thousands of homes over its history, consistently ranking among the top 10 national builders by volume.

In the Dallas–Fort Worth market, Beazer operates primarily in the fast-growing outer suburbs of Collin and Rockwall counties — Anna, Princeton, Royse City, and Little Elm — as well as the premium lakefront community at Heath Golf and Yacht Club in Heath, and an active-adult community at Gatherings at Twin Creeks in Allen. Their DFW position is entry-to-move-up production: competitively priced homes in communities where land is still affordable and school districts are still building out. Beazer's DFW design center is located at 1750 Valley View Lane, Farmers Branch, TX 75234. The Texas division office is at 1501 LBJ Freeway, Suite 300, Farmers Branch, TX 75234.

What Sets Beazer Apart

Beazer differentiates on energy performance, financing flexibility, and in-house structural warranty administration — three specific, verifiable commitments that matter for families buying production homes in DFW's outer growth corridors.

ENERGY STAR Certified & HERS Rating Guarantee

All Beazer DFW homes are ENERGY STAR certified, meaning they meet EPA energy performance standards verified by a third-party rater. Beyond the certification, Beazer backs each home's projected HERS (Home Energy Rating System) index score with a written guarantee: if the home doesn't perform within defined thresholds during the first two years, Beazer corrects it at no cost to the homeowner. For DFW families coming from high-utility markets in California or the Northeast, this is a meaningful, verifiable promise — not just marketing language.

Mortgage Choice Program — Competing Lenders

Rather than locking buyers into a single preferred lender, Beazer's Mortgage Choice program requires all participating lenders to maintain a customer satisfaction rating of 90% or higher to stay in the program. Lenders compete for Beazer buyers' business. Participating lenders have included Benchmark Mortgage (NMLS #2143) and Guild Mortgage (NMLS #3274), among others. Beazer does not require buyers to use a Mortgage Choice lender — you can bring your own financing — but incentives (closing cost credits, rate buydowns) are typically tied to using a program lender. Always compare the all-in cost with an independent lender quote before committing.

10-Year Structural Warranty — In-House Administration

Beazer administers its 10-year structural warranty directly rather than outsourcing it to a third-party warranty company (e.g., 2-10 HBW). This means structural defect claims go directly to Beazer — not to a separate warranty company with its own claims process and exclusion language. For buyers, this simplifies the path to resolution on major structural issues, though it also means Beazer is the sole arbiter of what qualifies as a covered structural defect. Review the warranty document carefully at beazer.com/warranty-information before closing.

Beazer Homes Warranty — Meets Texas Minimums with HERS Bonus

Texas law (RCLA) sets the floor for new home warranties: 1 year for workmanship, 2 years for systems, 10 years for structural. Beazer matches these minimums exactly — 1-2-10. What pushes it above minimum is the HERS rating guarantee on energy performance, which adds a separate layer of protection during years 1–2 that other builders at this price tier don't offer. The full warranty is published at beazer.com/warranty-information — read it before signing a purchase agreement.

How Beazer Handles Warranty Repairs

Beazer uses an online warranty service request portal — homeowners submit claims through their homeowner account at beazer.com. Beazer's local DFW warranty team reviews and schedules repairs. The 10-year structural warranty is administered directly by Beazer (not a third-party company like 2-10 HBW), which means all structural claims are evaluated by Beazer's own staff. Response time for non-emergency warranty work is typically scheduled in batches; urgent issues (e.g., plumbing failures, HVAC in summer) should be documented in the portal and followed up by phone with the local warranty office. Beazer's DFW warranty office is reached through the national customer service line on the warranty page.

What the warranty doesn't cover

Normal wear and tear, consumer products with manufacturer warranties (appliances, fixtures), damage from homeowner modifications, landscaping beyond the initial grading period, and cosmetic settling cracks that don't affect structural integrity. The workmanship warranty excludes items that were accepted on the pre-closing punch list walkthrough — everything you sign off on at walk-through is considered accepted. Never skip the pre-closing walkthrough, and never sign off on items that aren't actually fixed. This is one area where having a buyer's agent present pays for itself.

Honest Assessment — What Families Love and What to Know

Beazer Homes scores a 90 (A+) on our 100-point builder index. That score reflects strong TrustBuilder ratings (4.6/5.0 from 911 DFW reviews), A+ BBB standing, and consistent ENERGY STAR certification across all homes. Here's what drives the score — and where families relocating to DFW should pay close attention.

Strengths

  • Strong TrustBuilder score — 4.6/5.0 from 911 verified DFW homeowner reviews on NewHomeSource, indicating consistent buyer satisfaction at the transaction and early ownership stage.
  • ENERGY STAR certified standard — Every Beazer home is ENERGY STAR certified with a HERS score guarantee through year 2. For families from California or the Northeast, DFW utility bills already feel low — Beazer makes them lower.
  • Competitive entry pricing — Churchill communities in Anna start under $300k for 3-bed plans, placing Beazer among the most accessible new-construction options in Collin County school districts.
  • Mortgage Choice lender competition — Competing lenders bid for Beazer buyers' business rather than one captive preferred lender, which creates more genuine rate transparency than the single-lender model most builders use.
  • Active adult option in Allen — The Gatherings at Twin Creeks community in Allen offers a 55+ option in an established, well-connected suburb — useful for families relocating with aging parents who want their own community.

Things to Know

  • Finish quality is the persistent complaint — Across multiple review platforms, Beazer buyers consistently report paint, trim, drywall, and flooring finishes at closing that fall below expectations for the price. This theme appears in BBB complaints, ConsumerAffairs (1.6/5 satisfaction average), and buyer forums. A thorough pre-closing walkthrough with a checklist — and an independent inspector — is not optional with Beazer; it's essential.
  • HVAC reliability concerns in DFW heat — Multiple homeowners report repeated HVAC service calls within the first 3–5 years beyond normal maintenance. In DFW summers, a poorly specified or installed HVAC system is not a minor inconvenience. Ask specifically about HVAC brand, sizing report (Manual J), and filter access before signing.
  • Warranty response time varies — Review evidence indicates warranty service scheduling can be slow, particularly for non-urgent items. The online portal system helps document issues but doesn't always speed resolution. Keep written records of every warranty request and response.
  • Outer suburb locations = longer commutes — Anna and Princeton are 45–55 miles from downtown Dallas and 35–50 miles from the major employment corridors around Legacy Drive in Plano/Frisco. If your family's commute tolerance is limited, confirm drive times to your actual workplace before choosing an outer Collin County community.

Beazer Homes Incentives & Current Promotions

Incentives Change — Here's How to Find Them

Beazer runs active promotions across most DFW communities — as of the most recent ShowingNew data, 10 of 11 active communities show current promotional offers. Specific amounts (rate buydown points, closing cost credit dollars, upgrade packages) are not publicly listed and change with each phase close and market cycle. Beazer's promotions page at homes.beazer.com/Texas-Promo lists current Texas-specific offers — verify directly on the day you're making a decision.

Rate Buydowns

Beazer periodically offers temporary or permanent rate buydowns through Mortgage Choice lenders. Terms change with each rate environment — ask the sales agent for the current buydown offer and get the corresponding APR from an independent lender to compare.

Closing Cost Credits

Closing cost assistance is typically available when using a Mortgage Choice lender. Credit amounts vary by community and home price. Do not assume any specific amount — get it in writing in the purchase agreement.

Design Center Packages

Beazer occasionally bundles upgrade packages for spec and inventory homes — flooring, cabinetry, or appliance upgrades at a flat credit. These are most common near phase closes when Beazer wants to move remaining lots quickly.

Important: Builder incentives are almost always tied to using their preferred lender. That's not inherently bad — sometimes the rate is genuinely competitive — but you should always get a competing quote from an independent lender first. A buyer's agent can help you compare the true cost of the deal across lenders.

Beazer Homes Floor Plans & Design Process

Beazer's DFW portfolio spans 1,427–3,894 sq ft across 6 active suburbs. Entry-level plans in Anna start with 3-bedroom, 2-bath layouts designed for first-time buyers and young families. Move-up plans in Little Elm (Valencia on the Lake, Lake Pointe) and Heath (Golf and Yacht Club) reach into the $450k–$750k range with 4–5 bedrooms and more generous lot sizes. Floor plan availability varies significantly by community — plans offered in Anna may not be available in Royse City.

Production Plans with Structural Options

Beazer builds production-style floor plans — the basic square footage, bedroom count, and structural layout are set before you sign. Within a given plan, Beazer typically offers optional structural modifications (bonus rooms, extended garage, covered patio extensions) that must be selected before the foundation is poured. Design center selections (flooring, counters, cabinets, fixtures) come after contract but before framing. There is no true custom or semi-custom path — once structural options are locked, the plan is fixed.

Design Center

Beazer's DFW design center is located at 1750 Valley View Lane, Farmers Branch, TX 75234. Most DFW buyers attend one or two design center appointments after contract to make their selections. For relocating families buying from out of state, Beazer offers virtual design appointments — the process is done remotely with a design consultant via video call and digital catalogs. This is one area where Beazer does a reasonable job accommodating remote buyers, though the selection experience is more limited than an in-person visit to the physical showroom.

Standard features included at base price

All Beazer DFW homes include ENERGY STAR certification and HERS-rated insulation, tankless water heaters (in most DFW plans), smart home features including programmable thermostat and keyless entry, and granite or quartz countertops in kitchen (varies by plan and community). Stainless appliance package, 2-car garage, and full sod and irrigation are standard in most DFW communities. Specific inclusions vary by floor plan and community — always confirm the exact inclusion list for the specific plan you are considering before comparing to comparable homes from other builders.

Kristen's Agent Perspective

Kristen Carpentier DFW Family Relocation Specialist · TREC #760457 · eXp Realty
"Beazer makes the most sense for families who are stretching to get into a new-construction home in a good Collin County school district without the premium of a semi-custom builder. The ENERGY STAR certification and the TrustBuilder score are real — but so are the finish complaints. I tell clients: go in with eyes open, bring an independent inspector, and don't skip the pre-closing walkthrough."

For families relocating to DFW from higher-cost markets, Beazer's price point in Anna, Princeton, and Royse City is genuinely attractive — you can get 1,500–2,800 sq ft in a growing Collin County community for $290k–$465k. That range is increasingly hard to find with other builders as land costs have risen. The HERS energy guarantee is a legitimate differentiator for families coming from California or the Mid-Atlantic who are used to paying close attention to utility costs.

Where I counsel caution is at the pre-closing stage. The pattern in buyer reviews is consistent enough that I consider it a structural characteristic of Beazer's production model rather than an outlier: paint, trim, and drywall finishes at closing often need attention. This doesn't mean Beazer won't fix them under warranty — it means you need to catch them before you sign off. I always recommend hiring a third-party inspector for new construction, and with Beazer I treat that as non-negotiable, not optional. Who is Beazer best for? Budget-conscious families who want new construction in a growing Collin or Rockwall County ISD and are willing to be proactive about the pre-closing walkthrough. If you want move-in-ready with minimal post-closing repairs, consider a semi-custom builder like Highland Homes or Perry Homes in the same school zones. When another builder fits better: if your family needs to be within 30 miles of Dallas or close to the Legacy Drive tech corridor, Beazer's current DFW footprint — concentrated in the outer suburbs — may not be the right match regardless of price.

Bottom line: Beazer is a solid value play in DFW's outer growth corridors for families who understand that production-tier finish quality requires proactive oversight — not passive trust. Beazer Homes DFW — Frequently Asked Questions

Is Beazer Homes a good builder?

Beazer Homes earns a 90/100 (A+) on our DFW builder index, driven by a 4.6/5.0 TrustBuilder score from 911 verified DFW homeowner reviews and A+ BBB standing. That puts them in strong territory by national production-builder standards. The consistent caveat is finish quality at closing — paint, trim, and drywall issues appear often enough in buyer reviews that they represent a known characteristic of Beazer's production model, not an anomaly. Whether Beazer is a good fit for your family depends on your priorities: if competitive pricing in a new Collin County community and ENERGY STAR certification matter more than polish at handoff, Beazer delivers real value. If move-in-ready perfection is the baseline expectation, a semi-custom builder will serve you better at a higher price.

How much do Beazer Homes homes cost in DFW?

Beazer's DFW price range spans $289,990 to $746,990 based on current ShowingNew community data. Entry-level plans in Anna start under $300,000 for 3-bedroom homes (1,427–1,668 sq ft) in the Churchill and Villages of Hurricane Creek communities. Mid-range plans in Princeton and Royse City run $325k–$485k for 3–5 bedrooms (1,536–3,317 sq ft). Move-up options at Valencia on the Lake and Lake Pointe in Little Elm range from $346k to $680k. The premium tier is the Heath Golf and Yacht Club community, where plans range from $569k to $747k on larger lakeside lots. Base prices are before design center upgrades, which can add $20,000–$60,000+ depending on selections.

Where does Beazer Homes build in DFW?

As of the most recent data, Beazer is active in six DFW suburbs: Anna (4 communities — Churchill Fields 40', Churchill Meadows 50', Villages of Hurricane Creek Overlook 70', Villages of Hurricane Creek Meadows 50'), Princeton (Whitewing Trails), Royse City (Verandah Crossings 60' and Verandah Meadows 50'), Heath (Heath Golf and Yacht Club), Little Elm (Valencia on the Lake and Lake Pointe), and Allen (Gatherings at Twin Creeks, 55+). Beazer does not currently build in the inner-ring established suburbs like Frisco, McKinney, Plano, Coppell, Southlake, or Flower Mound, where land costs make production-tier pricing difficult. Their DFW strategy is outer-corridor growth markets in Collin and Rockwall counties.

What is Beazer Homes' warranty?

Beazer offers a standard 1-2-10 warranty: 1 year for workmanship (paint, trim, drywall, flooring, visible construction elements), 2 years for systems (HVAC, plumbing, electrical), and 10 years for structural defects affecting the foundation and load-bearing components. Beazer administers the structural warranty in-house rather than through a third-party warranty company. An additional feature is the HERS energy performance guarantee during years 1–2: if the home doesn't meet its projected energy performance score, Beazer corrects it at no cost. The full warranty document is published at beazer.com/warranty-information. Workmanship claims must be submitted during year 1; items accepted on the pre-closing punch list are not covered, so the pre-closing walkthrough is critical.

Do I need a Realtor to buy a Beazer Homes new construction?

You don't need one, but you should have one — and it costs you nothing extra. Beazer pays the buyer's agent commission; the builder price is the same whether you bring a Realtor or not. Beazer's sales agent works for Beazer, not for you. A buyer's agent reviews the contract, explains upgrade pricing relative to market value, advises on lot selection, recommends independent inspectors, and represents your interests at the pre-closing walkthrough — which, with Beazer specifically, is one of the most important steps in the purchase process given the documented finish quality concerns. How long does Beazer Homes take to build a house in DFW?

A typical Beazer production build in DFW runs 7–10 months from contract execution to closing, assuming no significant supply chain disruptions. Spec (inventory) homes that are already under construction close faster — sometimes within 60–90 days if the home is near or past framing stage when you contract. Beazer typically has some level of spec inventory in active communities, which can be advantageous for families with firm relocation deadlines. Build time varies by community, plan size, and current production scheduling — ask the sales agent for the specific current build time estimate for the plan and lot you're considering, and get a projected closing date range in writing in the purchase agreement.

Does Beazer Homes require you to use their lender?

No — Beazer does not require buyers to use a specific lender and does not guarantee the products or services of any lender. However, promotional incentives like closing cost credits and rate buydowns are typically tied to using a participating Mortgage Choice lender (Benchmark Mortgage, Guild Mortgage, and others). If you bring outside financing, you may not qualify for the full incentive package. The practical question is whether the incentive value outweighs any difference in loan terms between a Mortgage Choice lender and your own bank or mortgage broker. Get a competing quote from an independent lender on the same loan terms and compare the total cost before deciding.
