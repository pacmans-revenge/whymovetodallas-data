Who Is K. Hovnanian Homes? Score 89 A

K. Hovnanian® Homes is the marketing brand of Hovnanian Enterprises, Inc. (NYSE: HOV), one of the largest publicly traded homebuilders in the United States. The company was founded in 1959 in Toms River, New Jersey, by Armenian-American immigrant Kevork S. Hovnanian and his three brothers, who pooled their savings and a $20,000 loan to start building affordable homes. The company expanded into Texas in 1973 and has been building there continuously ever since — making it one of the longest-tenured national builders in the state. In fiscal year 2025, Hovnanian Enterprises delivered 6,430 homes nationally with total revenues of $2.98 billion.

In the Dallas–Fort Worth market, K. Hovnanian operates as a production builder targeting move-up buyers in the $330k–$699k range. Their DFW communities are concentrated along the east I-30 and Rockwall County corridors — Forney, Fate, Wylie, and Heath — rather than the higher-priced northern Collin County suburbs where many competing national builders are most active. This east-DFW focus means families who are priced out of Frisco or Prosper often find K. Hovnanian's communities offer comparable plan sizes at meaningfully lower entry points, particularly in Forney's Gateway Parks community.

What Sets K. Hovnanian Apart

For relocating families comparing national volume builders, K. Hovnanian offers a distinct combination of east-DFW price accessibility, a publicly traded parent company's accountability infrastructure, and a dedicated online warranty management system. Here's what that means in practice:

Publicly Traded Accountability

As a NYSE-listed company (HOV), Hovnanian Enterprises publishes quarterly earnings, maintains formal investor relations, and operates under public company financial disclosure requirements. That doesn't guarantee construction quality, but it does mean the parent company has reputational and legal exposure beyond what a local or regional builder faces. For relocating buyers who can't visit communities in person during due diligence, the paper trail on a public company is more accessible than for a privately held builder.

Dedicated Online Warranty Portal

K. Hovnanian operates a dedicated homeowner warranty portal at warranty.khov.com where buyers can submit service requests, upload photos, and track repair progress in real time — 24 hours a day, without waiting for a sales office to open. For families relocating from out of state who close and then return home before their warranty year is up, the ability to document and track issues remotely is a practical advantage over builders who manage warranty calls by phone or email only.

East DFW Value Corridor

K. Hovnanian's current DFW communities are positioned in the east I-30 and Rockwall County growth corridor — specifically Forney (Kaufman County), Fate and Heath (Rockwall County), and Wylie (Collin/Rockwall county line). This corridor offers strong school districts at lower price points than north Collin County: Forney ISD, Rockwall ISD, and Wylie ISD all serve growing suburban families. Commutes to Downtown Dallas run 30–50 minutes depending on location and time of day.

How K. Hovnanian Handles Warranty Repairs

Warranty claims are submitted through the online portal at warranty.khov.com. Homeowners create an account, submit a service request with photos and descriptions, and track repair status in real time. The company routes claims to their DFW warranty team, who schedule contractor visits. Based on BBB complaints filed against the DFW LLC, repair timelines have run 45 days or longer in some cases — a gap between online claim submission and actual repair completion that families should factor into their expectations, particularly for issues discovered late in year one.

What the warranty doesn't cover

K. Hovnanian's published warranty documentation explicitly excludes gale force winds and acts of nature from structural coverage — meaning storm damage, even if it reveals an underlying structural issue, may not be covered under the 10-year structural warranty. Standard exclusions also apply: normal wear and tear, cosmetic issues that arise after the first year, damage caused by the homeowner or their contractors, and settling that falls within accepted industry tolerances. Always read the full warranty document before closing and document any defects discovered during your pre-closing walkthrough in writing.

Honest Assessment — What Families Love and What to Know

K. Hovnanian Homes scores a 89 (A) on our 100-point builder index. Here's what drives that score — and where relocating families should pay attention.

Strengths

  • A+ BBB Rating — The K. Hovnanian Homes DFW LLC holds an A+ BBB rating with 13 complaints closed in 3 years — a manageable number for a national builder of their volume. Not BBB accredited, but the grade and complaint ratio reflect basic accountability.
  • 65+ Years of National Track Record — Publicly traded since 1983, Hovnanian Enterprises has operated through multiple market cycles. There's a corporate complaint and escalation structure that many smaller regional and local builders simply don't have.
  • East DFW Entry Point — Gateway Parks in Forney starts in the $330s for plans up to 3,164 sq ft — a combination that's increasingly hard to find in north DFW. Families who need the space but can't reach Frisco or Allen price points should look east.
  • Online Warranty Portal — Claim submission, photo uploads, and status tracking at warranty.khov.com, 24/7. For out-of-state buyers who close and travel back, this is a real operational advantage over phone-only warranty processes.
  • TrustBuilder 4.3 from 366 Verified Reviews — Dallas-area buyers on NewHomeSource rate K. Hovnanian 4.3/5.0 across quality (4.1), trustworthiness (4.3), value (4.2), and responsiveness (4.3). That's a broad verified sample, not a small handful of reviews.

Things to Know

  • Warranty Repair Timelines — BBB complaints against the DFW LLC document repair timelines of 45 days or more and difficulties obtaining insurance information from the builder for certain claim types. The online portal makes it easier to submit claims; getting them resolved on a reasonable timeline is the documented friction point.
  • Finish Quality Variability — Consumer review sources document issues including nail pops, crooked fixtures, and at least one documented case of ceiling insulation that was not installed before move-in. These are not unique to K. Hovnanian among production builders, but families should budget for a thorough pre-close inspection by an independent third-party inspector.
  • Production Floor Plans — K. Hovnanian is a production builder: floor plans are set, structural changes are very limited, and the design center handles finish selections rather than layout modifications. Buyers who want semi-custom structural flexibility should look at Highland Homes, Drees, or David Weekley instead.
  • KHAM Incentive Lock-in — K. Hovnanian American Mortgage (KHAM) is the company's in-house preferred lender. The largest incentive packages require using KHAM; using an outside lender can reduce incentives by an amount equivalent to 5% of the purchase price. Always get an independent quote and compare the true net cost — sometimes KHAM is competitive, sometimes it isn't.

K. Hovnanian Homes Incentives & Current Promotions

Incentives Change — Here's How to Find Them

K. Hovnanian typically offers rate buydowns, closing cost credits, and flex cash — but the specific terms vary by community, change at phase closes, and are almost always tied to using their in-house lender K. Hovnanian American Mortgage (KHAM). No specific 2026 promotions were publicly listed on khov.com at time of research. Verify current offers directly at khov.com/new-construction-homes/texas/greater-dallas-tx before your visit.

Rate Buydowns

K. Hovnanian has historically offered permanent or temporary interest rate reductions through KHAM. Builder Forward Commitments (BFCs) — where the builder buys down your rate using their preferred lender — are a standard tool in their incentive toolkit. Confirm the buydown structure and actual rate with KHAM before signing.

Closing Cost Credits

Cash contributions toward buyer closing costs are typically tied to KHAM financing. The credit amount varies by community and phase. If you use an outside lender, published guidance indicates incentives may be reduced by an amount equivalent to 5% of the purchase price — compare the net cost carefully.

Design Center / Flex Cash

Some K. Hovnanian communities offer upgrade allowances or "flex cash" that can be applied toward design center selections (flooring, counters, cabinets, fixtures). These are most common at phase closes and end-of-quarter pushes when sales teams have inventory to move.

Important: Builder incentives are almost always tied to using their preferred lender. That's not inherently bad — sometimes the rate is genuinely competitive — but you should always get a competing quote from an independent lender first. A buyer's agent can help you compare the true cost of the deal across lenders.

K. Hovnanian Homes Floor Plans & Design Process

Across their current DFW communities, K. Hovnanian offers plans ranging from approximately 1,616 sq ft (Heath Golf & Yacht Villas entry-level) to 3,796 sq ft in larger move-up plans in Fate and Wylie. Bedroom configurations run 3–5 bedrooms; most DFW plans include 2–4 bathrooms. DFW base pricing (per ShowingNew data as of the most recent monthly update) ranges from $327,900 in Forney to $699,000 in Heath. Specific plan availability varies by community — check the community page at khov.com for current plan releases.

Production Floor Plans

K. Hovnanian builds from a set catalog of floor plans — buyers choose from available plans within each community rather than designing from scratch. Structural customization (moving walls, changing room counts, altering the footprint) is generally not available once a plan is selected. Buyers can personalize their home through finish and upgrade selections made at the design studio, but the plan's layout is fixed at the time of contract. This is standard for national production builders and means K. Hovnanian homes are ready to move into on a more predictable timeline — but it is a material difference from semi-custom builders like Highland Homes, Drees Custom Homes, or David Weekley, where structural options are available before the foundation is poured.

Design Center

K. Hovnanian operates a design studio for finish selections — flooring, countertops, cabinetry, tile, plumbing fixtures, and lighting. For relocating buyers who can't travel to DFW for in-person design appointments, the company has offered virtual design center options in some markets. Confirm with the community's sales team whether virtual appointments are available for the specific community you're considering — this is particularly important for buyers who plan to select finishes before closing remotely. The design center is typically scheduled early in the construction process, so buyers should plan accordingly once under contract.

Standard features included at base price

K. Hovnanian's base specifications vary by community and plan series. Generally included at the base price level in DFW communities: hard-surface flooring in main living areas, granite or quartz countertops in kitchens, stainless steel appliances (range, dishwasher, microwave), and a standard landscaping package. Smart home technology packages have been part of K. Hovnanian's standard offering in recent years, though the specific included devices vary by community. Buyers should request the written specifications sheet for their plan and community — not just the model home finishes, which are typically upgraded well beyond base level and can create inaccurate expectations about what comes standard.

What is K. Hovnanian Homes' warranty?

K. Hovnanian offers a 1-2-10 warranty: a 1-year "bumper-to-bumper" workmanship period covering construction and installation defects, 2-year coverage on mechanical systems (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, water heater), and 10-year structural coverage on the foundation and load-bearing elements. The structural warranty excludes gale force winds and acts of nature. Warranty claims are managed through K. Hovnanian's own online portal at warranty.khov.com — not through a third-party warranty company. This warranty meets but does not exceed the Texas Residential Construction Liability Act (RCLA) minimums of 1/2/10. Full documentation is available at khov.com/why-khov/k-hovnanian-warranty.

How long does K. Hovnanian take to build a house in DFW?

As a production builder, K. Hovnanian's DFW construction timelines typically run 8–12 months from contract signing to closing. Build time varies based on plan size, community lot availability, and current subcontractor scheduling in that corridor. Spec (inventory) homes — units already under construction or complete — are available in most K. Hovnanian DFW communities and can close in 30–90 days. As of the most recent ShowingNew data, Gateway Parks (Forney), Monterra (Fate), Kreymer at the Park (Wylie), and the Heath Golf & Yacht communities all had spec inventory available. Confirm with the community's sales team which homes are spec versus to-be-built and what the estimated completion date is before signing.

What is KHAM and should I use K. Hovnanian's preferred lender?

KHAM stands for K. Hovnanian American Mortgage, L.L.C. — the company's in-house affiliated mortgage lender. K. Hovnanian ties its largest incentive packages (rate buydowns, closing cost credits, flex cash) to using KHAM. Published guidance indicates that if a buyer uses an outside lender instead of KHAM, incentives may be reduced by an amount equivalent to 5% of the purchase price. This is a meaningful number on a $500k home. That said, "tied to the preferred lender" doesn't automatically mean KHAM offers the best deal — sometimes the rate and fees are competitive, sometimes an independent lender beats the net cost even after the incentive reduction. The right approach: get a full loan estimate from KHAM, get a competing quote from an independent lender, and compare the total cost of borrowing side by side. A buyer's agent can help you read the incentive math correctly before you decide.
