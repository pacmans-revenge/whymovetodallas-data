Who Is Coventry Homes? Score 90 A+

Coventry Homes is a Texas-only regional builder founded in 1988 under the umbrella of McGuyer Homebuilders, Inc. (MHI), based in Houston. Over 35+ years, Coventry has built more than 55,000 homes across the four major Texas markets — Houston, Dallas–Fort Worth, Austin, and San Antonio — making it one of the most tenured regional builders operating exclusively in Texas. In 2021, Dream Finders Homes (NASDAQ: DFH) acquired MHI's assets for $471 million; Coventry continues to operate under the same brand with the same team and communities.

In DFW, Coventry is active across 17+ suburbs — concentrated in the growth corridors of Denton County (Northlake, Highland Village, Flower Mound, Aubrey), Collin County (Celina, Prosper, McKinney, Anna), and the eastern and southern metro (Rockwall, Wylie, Mesquite, Mansfield, Waxahachie). Their product spans a broad price band from family-accessible move-up homes in the mid-$300s to larger semi-custom builds approaching $1 million, with a Build-On-Your-Lot program layered on top for buyers who own land.

What Sets Coventry Apart

Coventry occupies a specific niche in the DFW builder landscape: a Texas-only semi-custom builder with the reach of a large regional company and the flexibility of a design center that lets families personalize finishes before breaking ground. Here are the differentiators that matter most for relocating families.

Texas-Only, 35+ Years of DFW Experience

Unlike national builders whose DFW divisions are managed from out-of-state headquarters, Coventry has operated exclusively in Texas since 1988. Every community, every sales team, and every trade partner relationship is rooted here. For relocating families who want a builder that truly understands DFW land conditions, school district zoning nuances, and the North Texas market — Coventry's depth of local tenure is a genuine differentiator.

Semi-Custom Design Center with Broad Selection

Coventry's Design Studios (in-person and with virtual options for remote buyers) offer flooring, countertops, cabinets, appliances, lighting, faucets, window treatments, and home automation selections. Buyers can meaningfully personalize a home before breaking ground, which matters particularly for families relocating from California or the Northeast where high-finish standards are the expectation. The tradeoff — discussed in the pros/cons section — is that the standard selections are frequently described as dated, meaning budget for upgrades accordingly.

Build-On-Your-Lot Program (DFW)

Coventry offers a Build-On-Your-Lot program in DFW — a significant differentiator among regional builders. Families who already own land, inherited a lot, or want to build in an established neighborhood where no new communities exist can still access Coventry's floor plan library and construction process. Two DFW product lines are available: the Premium Series and the Elite Series, with plan sizing from approximately 1,800 to 4,200+ square feet. This program is especially relevant for buyers looking at established suburbs like Flower Mound or Southlake where land is available but new community lots are scarce.

Coventry Homes Warranty — Below Texas Minimum on Systems

Texas law (Residential Construction Liability Act, RCLA) sets a minimum warranty floor: 1 year on workmanship, 2 years on mechanical systems (HVAC, plumbing, electrical), and 10 years on major structural components. Coventry's published warranty covers workmanship and mechanical systems under a single 1-year term — which meets the workmanship minimum but falls short of the 2-year systems minimum required by Texas law. The 10-year structural warranty matches the legal floor. Families should understand this gap before signing: if a plumbing or HVAC issue surfaces in month 14 or 20, Coventry's standard warranty does not cover it.

How Coventry Handles Warranty Repairs

Warranty claims are submitted through Coventry's online portal (homefiniti.com). Coventry's in-house warranty team handles initial review and dispatches trade partners for approved repairs. The process is builder-managed rather than third-party warranted for the first year. Based on publicly available reviews and complaints, response consistency is an area where buyer experience varies: some homeowners report prompt resolution while others document repeated follow-up needed before work is completed. Submitting warranty requests in writing through the portal (rather than phone calls) creates a paper trail that is useful if escalation is needed.

What the warranty doesn't cover

Normal wear and tear, caulking and drywall cracking from settlement (distinguished from structural cracking), defects in materials or work supplied by anyone other than Coventry, damage from acts of God, flooding or weather events, homeowner modifications, and items not meeting the published performance standards. Coventry's warranty book also specifically excludes "cosmetic variations" in materials — meaning minor color or texture inconsistencies in flooring, tile, or countertops. Warranty documentation (DFH Form Rev. 4/6/2023) is publicly available at homefiniti.com for review before signing a contract.

Honest Assessment — What Families Love and What to Know

Coventry Homes scores a 90 (A+) on our 100-point builder index — though that score reflects partial data given the absence of a verified DFW BBB profile and TrustBuilder rating. Here's what drives that score and where relocating families should pay careful attention.

Strengths

  • Broadest DFW suburb coverage of any regional builder — Coventry is active in 17+ DFW suburbs from Aubrey in the northwest to Waxahachie in the south, giving relocating families real flexibility on suburb choice without switching builders.
  • Texas-only with 35 years of DFW tenure — Coventry has built in every major DFW growth corridor since 1988. Their trade base, land positions, and community relationships are deeply local in a way national builders can't replicate.
  • Presence in top family school districts — Active communities in Celina ISD (consistently A-rated), McKinney ISD, Northlake (Northwest ISD), and Prosper ISD give families school-zone choices that compete directly with the top-tier suburbs.
  • Semi-custom Design Center flexibility — Full in-person and virtual design center options let buyers personalize floor plans, finishes, and technology packages before breaking ground — an important feature for out-of-state families who may be making decisions remotely.
  • Quick move-in inventory available in DFW — Coventry regularly carries spec inventory at various completion stages, which matters for families on corporate relocation timelines who can't wait 9–12 months for a build to complete.
  • Build-On-Your-Lot program — One of the few regional builders offering a BTY program in DFW, relevant for buyers with land in established suburbs where new community lots aren't available.

Things to Know

  • Systems warranty is only 1 year — below Texas' 2-year minimum — This is the most important contractual fact to understand before signing. Texas law requires 2 years on mechanical systems; Coventry's published warranty covers systems at 1 year. Read the warranty document at homefiniti.com before signing, and ask the sales agent to clarify in writing.
  • Standard selections are frequently described as dated — Multiple buyer reviews describe base-level finishes (flooring, cabinets, countertops) as entry-grade and out of step with 2024–2026 design expectations. In the $500k–$975k range, budget an additional $30k–$75k in Design Center upgrades to reach a finish level consistent with comparable resale homes in the same price band.
  • Warranty follow-through is inconsistent — The most recurring complaint theme across BBB records (San Antonio), PissedConsumer, and Google reviews is warranty service response: initial claims submitted but not resolved, repeated follow-up required, and in some cases builders declining warranty coverage that homeowners expected to be covered. Document every communication in writing through the portal.
  • Incentives are tied to Jet HomeLoans — Coventry's preferred lender is Jet HomeLoans (Dream Finders Homes' in-house lending arm). Advertised incentives — rate buydowns, closing cost credits — typically require using Jet. Get an independent quote before committing; sometimes Jet is genuinely competitive, sometimes the incentive math favors a different lender.
  • Score is based on partial data — The 90/A+ score reflects strong Google ratings and Texas tenure but does not include a verified DFW BBB grade or TrustBuilder score, both of which are currently unavailable. The actual independent-review score may differ once those data sources are confirmed.

Coventry Homes Floor Plans & Design Process

Coventry's DFW plan library covers a wide range: single-story and two-story layouts from approximately 1,400 to 4,200+ square feet, priced from the mid-$300s to approaching $1 million depending on community and upgrades. Named plans include the Plano (4BR/3.5BA, single-story), Fayetteville (3BR/2.5BA, single-story), and Crockett (4BR/3BA, single-story), alongside two-story plans suited to families needing 5+ bedrooms. Community-specific plan offerings vary — the plan available at Celina's Cambridge Crossing may not be available at Mansfield's South Pointe.

Semi-Custom Options Available Pre-Pour

Coventry operates as a semi-custom builder. Floor plan modifications — structural changes, room additions, garage expansions — are available on select plans before the concrete slab is poured. After that point, customization is limited to interior finishes and upgrades through the Design Center. Buyers who want deeper structural customization beyond what's available in the plan library should ask the community's sales agent specifically which structural options are available on that homesite before signing.

Design Center

Coventry's Design Studios are available in-person at regional locations and virtually for out-of-state buyers. The selection covers flooring (hardwood, tile, carpet), countertops (granite, quartz), cabinetry (door style, finish, hardware), appliances (brands and finishes), lighting packages, plumbing fixtures, window treatments, and home automation/smart home technology. Virtual appointments are available with an assigned design consultant — a practical feature for families relocating from California, New York, or other distant markets who may be making all design choices remotely. Virtual appointments typically happen 30–60 days after contract signing.

Standard features included at base price

Coventry's base homes include energy-efficient construction, open-concept living layouts, covered back patio, two-car garage (minimum), and basic smart home technology integration. However, buyers consistently report that the standard finish tier — base flooring, base cabinets, laminate or entry-grade countertops — reflects an earlier design era and is not competitive with what's standard at comparable price points from Perry, Highland, or Meritage. If you're paying $500k+ for a Coventry home, plan to use the Design Center to bring the finishes to a level consistent with current DFW market expectations. That upgrade budget needs to be factored into your total purchase price calculation.

Coventry Homes DFW — Frequently Asked Questions

Is Coventry Homes a good builder?

Coventry Homes scores 90/A+ on our DFW builder index, driven by their 35+ year Texas tenure, active presence in top family school districts, and Google rating of 4.6 stars. That said, the score is based on partial data — no verified DFW BBB profile or TrustBuilder rating are currently available — so independent review sources should be weighted carefully. On third-party review platforms outside Google (Trustpilot, PissedConsumer), Coventry's ratings are significantly lower, with recurring complaints focused on warranty response follow-through and outdated standard finish selections. For families prioritizing proven DFW experience and suburb coverage breadth, Coventry is a legitimate option. For families who prioritize warranty depth or premium standard finishes, Perry Homes or Highland Homes offer stronger comparisons on those specific dimensions.

How much do Coventry Homes cost in DFW?

Coventry's DFW price range runs from approximately $350,000 to $975,000 depending on suburb, community, plan size, and Design Center selections. Entry-level communities in markets like Anna, Waxahachie, and Mesquite tend to start in the $350k–$450k range. Move-up communities in Celina, McKinney, and Northlake typically run $450k–$700k at base. Larger semi-custom plans in premium Denton and Collin County communities can approach $975k with upgrades. One critical planning note: Coventry's base finish selections are entry-grade, and most buyers in the $500k+ range spend $30k–$80k in Design Center upgrades. Your effective purchase price is base price plus Design Center — make sure both numbers are in your budget before contract signing.

Where does Coventry Homes build in DFW?

Coventry is one of the most geographically broad regional builders in DFW. As of mid-2026, active communities include: Denton County (Northlake's Pecan Square and Oak Point, Highland Village, Flower Mound's Heartland and Furst Ranch, Aubrey's Sandbrock Ranch, Denton's Landmark); Collin County (Celina's Cambridge Crossing, Mustang Lakes, Hillside Village, and Ramble; McKinney's Painted Tree; Anna's Meadow Vista); and additional communities in Prosper, Arlington, Fort Worth (Hulen Trails, Wildflower Ranch), Mansfield, Rockwall, Waxahachie, Wylie, and Mesquite. Community availability changes monthly as phases open and sell out — verify current availability at coventryhomes.com/dallas-ft-worth or through Kristen's ShowingNew portal.

What is Coventry Homes' warranty?

Coventry's published warranty (DFH Form Rev. 4/6/2023, updated January 2026) covers: 1 year on workmanship and materials (meets Texas 1-year minimum); 1 year on mechanical delivery systems including HVAC, plumbing, and electrical (below Texas' 2-year legal minimum — this is the most important fact to understand before signing); and 10 years on major structural components including the foundation and load-bearing walls (meets Texas minimum). The systems gap — 1 year vs. the 2-year Texas RCLA requirement — means a mechanical system failure discovered in months 13–24 falls outside Coventry's standard warranty coverage. Review the full warranty document at homefiniti.com before signing your purchase agreement.

How long does Coventry Homes take to build a house in DFW?

A standard Coventry build in DFW typically takes 9–12 months from contract signing to closing, depending on the plan size, community location, and current trade labor availability. Larger semi-custom plans or those with significant structural options can run 12–14 months. Coventry also maintains spec inventory (homes in various stages of completion that buyers can purchase for faster closings) across multiple DFW communities — a relevant option for families on corporate relocation timelines who can't wait for a full build. Ask the community sales agent for the current spec inventory list and stage of completion when you visit.

Who owns Coventry Homes?

Coventry Homes is owned by Dream Finders Homes (NASDAQ: DFH), a publicly traded homebuilder headquartered in Jacksonville, Florida. Dream Finders acquired Coventry from McGuyer Homebuilders, Inc. (MHI) in October 2021 for $471 million. Coventry continues to operate under the same brand name with the same leadership team and communities. MHI had operated Coventry as a Texas-only builder since 1988. Being part of a publicly traded company adds financial transparency and stability, though it also means some operational decisions are influenced by quarterly earnings pressures common to public homebuilders.
