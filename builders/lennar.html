About Lennar

Lennar was founded in Miami in 1954 by Leonard Miller and Arnold Rosen — the name is a portmanteau of their first names. Today it's the largest homebuilder in the United States by closings, operating in 26 states. Lennar entered the Dallas–Fort Worth market in the early 1990s and now builds across 17 DFW suburbs, from established corridors like Plano and Frisco to fast-growing communities in Celina, Northlake, and Anna.

The company's corporate headquarters for Texas operations is in Irving, TX. Unlike regional builders who concentrate on a handful of zip codes, Lennar's scale gives it access to land positions across the full DFW metro — from legacy suburbs closer to Dallas to the high-growth Collin County and Denton County corridors where most new construction activity is concentrated.

What Sets Lennar Apart in DFW

Everything's Included®

Lennar bundles features that most builders charge extra for — quartz or granite countertops, stainless appliances, smart home technology (Ring doorbell, smart thermostat, Wi-Fi certified home package), and designer tile/flooring. What you see in the model home is essentially what you get at base price. This makes budget planning more predictable.

NextGen® Homes

Lennar's multigenerational floor plan concept — a private attached suite with its own entrance, living area, bedroom, bathroom, and kitchenette. Popular with DFW families bringing parents from out of state or housing adult children. Not every DFW community offers NextGen, but it's available in select Collin County communities.

Lennar Mortgage & Title

Lennar operates its own in-house mortgage company (Lennar Mortgage LLC) and title company (CalAtlantic Title). Most Lennar incentives — rate buydowns, closing cost credits — are contingent on using Lennar Mortgage. The rates are competitive, but families should still get an outside quote for comparison before committing.

Lennar Warranty Coverage

Lennar offers a standard limited warranty on all new homes. Warranty coverage periods are fixed and do not vary by community or price point.

Coverage Type Duration What's Covered
Workmanship & Materials 1 Year ✓ Defects in workmanship and materials in all components of the home
Systems 2 Years ✓ Mechanical, electrical, and plumbing systems (distribution only — fixtures excluded)
Structural 10 Years ✓ Major structural defects per Texas statute

How Warranty Claims Work

Warranty service requests are submitted through myLennar, Lennar's online homeowner portal. This is the only accepted channel — phone calls to the sales office do not constitute a warranty claim. New homeowners should register on myLennar within the first 30 days of closing. Response times vary by market and season; the Dallas division's TrustBuilder responsiveness score is 3.5/5, which is below average for the DFW new construction market.

Drainage and Grading

Lennar's warranty explicitly excludes damage caused by improper drainage or grading issues that develop after the first 90 days. DFW clay soil can shift significantly — have a third-party inspector evaluate drainage and foundation during the 11-month warranty review and before the structural warranty's first major milestone.

Pros & Cons for DFW Families

✓ Strengths

  • Everything's Included® pricing — No surprise upgrade bills; model-quality finishes at base price makes budget planning easier
  • Broadest DFW footprint — 17 suburbs means you can stay with the same builder if your top suburb doesn't have availability
  • Streamlined process — National-scale systems, digital design selections via myLennar, and a defined timeline make the buying process predictable for relocating families
  • NextGen® multigenerational homes — A genuine differentiator for extended-family households; rare in the DFW market at this price point
  • QMI (Quick Move-In) inventory — Lennar consistently maintains spec inventory, which matters for families with firm relocation timelines who can't wait 8–12 months

✗ Watch-Outs

  • Below-average warranty responsiveness — 3.5/5 TrustBuilder score for responsiveness; post-close service is the most common complaint on Reddit and Trustpilot
  • Production-builder finish quality — Everything's Included® is genuine, but fit-and-finish (trim alignment, caulking, touch-up paint) tends to be production-grade; budget for a few fixes in year one
  • Incentives tied to Lennar Mortgage — Rate buydowns and closing cost credits require using the in-house lender; always get a competing quote before accepting this tradeoff
  • Limited customization — Structural changes (adding a bedroom, moving walls) are typically not available after a certain build stage; you're selecting from pre-set floor plans and design options

Best fit for: Families relocating on a corporate timeline who need a reliable build schedule and predictable pricing; multigenerational households; buyers in the $300s–$500s who want model-quality features without the premium of a semi-custom builder.

Current Lennar Incentives & Programs

Lennar's incentive programs vary by community and change frequently. The examples below reflect confirmed programs as of May 2026 — verify current availability with your Lennar community rep or with Kristen.

Interest Rate Buydowns

Lennar Mortgage has offered 30-year FHA rates as low as 3.99% on select communities (confirmed May 2026). Buydown programs vary by community; some use temporary 2-1 buydowns while others are permanent rate reductions. Requires using Lennar Mortgage to qualify.

Closing Cost Credits

Up to $6,000 in closing cost assistance has been available on select DFW communities (confirmed May 2026). Credits are structured as a contribution toward closing costs when using Lennar Mortgage and CalAtlantic Title. This effectively reduces your out-of-pocket cash at closing.

Quick Move-In Pricing

Lennar regularly offers price reductions on completed spec homes (Quick Move-In or QMI). These homes are finished and ready for occupancy within 30–45 days — ideal for families with a hard move-in deadline. QMI discounts are negotiated case-by-case; bring a buyer's agent (like Kristen) to the table.

Using a Buyer's Agent with Lennar

Lennar works with buyer's agents and does not reduce incentives if you bring representation. Your agent is paid by Lennar, not by you. Having a buyer's agent review the purchase contract, negotiate on QMI homes, and advocate during the warranty period costs you nothing and protects your interests significantly.

Floor Plans & Design Options

Lennar offers approximately 500+ floor plans nationally, with a curated selection of DFW-specific plans available in each community. Plans range from 1,400 sq ft townhomes in the $170s (typically in Forney and Mesquite) to 3,500+ sq ft single-family homes in the $600s–$700s in Prosper and Frisco.

Design selections are made through Lennar's myLennar online portal and, in some markets, through a Design Studio appointment. Structural changes (adding rooms, bay windows, extended covered patios) are available early in the build cycle. Finish selections (flooring, cabinet color, countertop edge) are made after contract. The Everything's Included® model means fewer upgrade decisions to make — most finishes are already specified at a level that doesn't need upgrading.

Popular DFW plan families include the Magnolia series (1-story, 2,000–2,600 sq ft, strong demand from Texas buyers preferring single-story), the Walton series (2-story, 2,400–3,200 sq ft, common in Collin County master-planned communities), and NextGen-enabled plans in select communities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Lennar a good builder in DFW?

Lennar earns a Builder Score of 83/100 (A) on this site — solid, but not the highest in DFW. Their BBB rating is A+ and they've been building in the market for 35+ years. The main area where they fall below average is post-close warranty responsiveness (3.5/5 on TrustBuilder). For families prioritizing a smooth buying process and predictable pricing over boutique custom service, Lennar is a strong choice.

How much does a new Lennar home cost in DFW?

Pricing ranges from the $170s (townhomes in Forney and Mesquite) to the $700s (larger single-family homes in Prosper and Frisco). The most common family home — 3–4 beds, 2-car garage, 2,000–2,800 sq ft — falls in the $380,000–$550,000 range depending on suburb and community. Everything's Included® means base price is close to move-in price for most buyers.

Where does Lennar build in the Dallas–Fort Worth area?

As of May 2026, Lennar builds in Allen, Anna, Argyle, Celina, Coppell, Flower Mound, Forney, Fort Worth, Frisco, McKinney, Mesquite, Northlake, Plano, Princeton, Prosper, Royse City, and Wylie. The highest concentration of active communities is in the Collin County growth corridor (Celina, Anna, Princeton, Wylie) and Denton County (Northlake, Argyle).

What does Lennar's warranty cover?

Lennar offers a 1-2-10 limited warranty: 1 year on workmanship and materials, 2 years on mechanical/electrical/plumbing systems, and 10 years on structural defects. Claims must be submitted through the myLennar online portal — not via phone or email to the sales office. Response times vary; budget time for follow-up on warranty items in the first year.

Do I need a real estate agent to buy a Lennar home?

You're not required to use a buyer's agent, but it costs you nothing to have one and provides significant protection. Lennar pays the buyer's agent commission — it does not come out of your purchase price or incentives. Your agent can review the purchase contract, negotiate on quick move-in homes, and advocate on your behalf during the construction and warranty period.

How long does a Lennar home take to build in DFW?

Build times typically run 5–8 months for a new-construction home from contract to close, though this varies by plan and how busy the Dallas division is. Quick Move-In (QMI) homes — already completed spec homes — are ready in 30–45 days and are a strong option for families with a fixed relocation date. Always ask about QMI availability first if your timeline is tight.

What is Lennar's Everything's Included® program?

Everything's Included® is Lennar's signature pricing model: features that other builders charge as upgrades — quartz or granite countertops, stainless steel appliances, designer tile, smart home technology (Ring doorbell, smart thermostat, Wi-Fi certified package) — are bundled into the base price. This makes the model home price the actual purchase price for most buyers, with no surprise upgrade invoices at the design studio.
