Who Is Grand Homes? Score 96 A+

Grand Homes was founded in 1986 by Stephen H. Brooks, who identified a gap in the Dallas housing market for a locally-rooted builder focused on architectural design and customer experience. Incorporated in July 1986 and headquartered in Addison, Texas, the company has operated exclusively in the Dallas–Fort Worth metroplex for its entire 40-year history — making it one of the few major DFW builders that has never expanded outside the market. That DFW-only focus means Grand's sales teams, construction managers, and warranty staff are all locally based and specifically calibrated to North Texas land, HOA requirements, and school zone dynamics.

Today the company remains family-owned under Stephen Brooks as CEO, with Beau Brooks serving as President and COO — a rare continuity of family ownership in an industry dominated by national public builders. Grand builds over 400 homes annually across 14+ DFW suburbs, with an active presence stretching from the East DFW corridor (Rockwall, Heath, Fate, Wylie) to the established North DFW suburbs (Frisco, Prosper, McKinney, Celina). In 2011 the company received the "America's Best Builder" designation from Builder Magazine. Grand was named a Homebuyers' Choice Award finalist by Zonda (formerly Hanley Wood) based on verified buyer surveys.

What Sets Grand Apart

Grand Homes occupies an interesting position in DFW new construction — priced above entry-level production builders like D.R. Horton or LGI, but below true luxury custom builders like Shaddock or Toll Brothers. That middle lane — move-up and semi-custom — is where the company has built its 40-year identity.

DFW-Only Builder — Deep Local Roots

Unlike national builders that operate in dozens of states with regional managers rotating in and out, Grand Homes has built exclusively in DFW for 40 years. Their construction managers know the soil conditions in Rockwall County, the HOA requirements in Prosper developments, and the school attendance zone lines that separate one subdivision from another. For relocating families making a once-in-a-decade move, that depth of local knowledge is genuinely harder to find than the marketing suggests.

Semi-Custom Options at Production Prices

Grand operates as a semi-custom builder — floor plans are established, but buyers can select from structural options (room configurations, ceiling heights, additional windows) before the slab is poured, and finish selections are made through Grand's in-house design center. This gives buyers more personalization than a true production builder's "pick from three packages" model, without the price premium of a fully custom home. For relocating families who want the home to feel like theirs rather than the model, this matters.

Broad Suburban Footprint Across Multiple Corridors

Grand is active in both the East DFW corridor — Rockwall, Heath, Fate, Wylie — and in established North DFW growth suburbs including Frisco, Prosper, McKinney, Celina, and Corinth. They also have communities in Irving, Grand Prairie, and Midlothian. That breadth means a relocating family can compare Grand communities across multiple school districts and commute patterns without switching builders — which simplifies the process considerably when you're shopping from out of state.

Grand Homes Warranty — Meets Texas Legal Minimum

Grand Homes offers a standard 1-2-10 limited express warranty, which is the most common warranty structure in DFW new construction. Texas law (RCLA) sets the floor at 1 year for workmanship, 2 years for mechanical systems, and 10 years for structural defects — Grand's warranty meets these minimums exactly. The workmanship coverage period (1 year) is the Texas floor; it does not exceed it. Warranty administration is handled in-house by Grand's own warranty team, not a third-party warranty company. This means your service requests go directly to Grand staff, which can be an advantage when issues are resolved promptly — and a friction point when responsiveness lags, which is the most consistent complaint theme across BBB and Eliant reviews.

How Grand Handles Warranty Repairs

Grand Homes administers its warranty in-house through a dedicated warranty department. After closing, homeowners submit service requests directly to the Grand warranty team. Grand typically schedules a warranty inspection to assess items reported, then coordinates its trade contractors to complete repairs. The in-house structure means there is no third-party warranty company intermediary — all communication goes through Grand's own staff. Based on verified Eliant reviews (997 completed through May 2026), most buyers rate their Grand builder/project manager experience positively during construction, but post-closing warranty follow-through — particularly on warranty period items approaching expiration — is the area where satisfaction drops most noticeably. Buyers should document every warranty request in writing and keep records of all scheduled repair dates.

What the warranty doesn't cover

Standard exclusions apply: normal wear and tear, cosmetic shrinkage and settling (minor cracks, nail pops) after the first year, damage from homeowner modifications or deferred maintenance, landscaping and irrigation systems, fence and retaining walls beyond structural requirements, and appliance manufacturer defects (those are covered by separate manufacturer warranties). HVAC component defects have appeared in BBB complaint records — if an HVAC issue manifests after the systems warranty window, repairs become the homeowner's cost. Document HVAC performance thoroughly during the first two years and request any issues be addressed in writing before warranty periods expire.

Grand Homes Incentives & Current Promotions

Incentives Change — Here's How to Find Them

No specific public promotions were confirmed on Grand Homes' website at the time this page was researched (May 2026). Grand Homes' incentives change at the community level — phase closes, lot releases, and quarter-end pushes all produce different offers by community. The three incentive types Grand has historically offered in DFW are described below, but current availability and amounts must be confirmed directly with the sales team or through a buyer's agent who is actively tracking the communities you're interested in.

Rate Buydowns

Temporary or permanent mortgage rate reductions funded by Grand Homes, typically available only through Grand Home Loans. When offered, these can meaningfully reduce monthly payment in the first one to three years. Confirm the rate, points, and APR against an independent lender quote before evaluating the deal.

Closing Cost Credits

Dollar credits applied at closing, typically ranging from a few thousand dollars to a percentage of purchase price. Usually require Grand Home Loans financing. Useful for reducing cash-to-close, but confirm whether the credit is structured as an origination offset or a price concession — the tax and appraisal implications differ.

Design Center Packages

At phase opens or to move spec inventory, Grand sometimes offers design center upgrade packages (flooring, countertops, cabinet hardware) at a discounted bundle price. For relocating buyers who value move-in readiness over customization, these can be a good value — confirm exactly what's included before signing.

Important: Builder incentives are almost always tied to using their preferred lender. That's not inherently bad — sometimes the rate is genuinely competitive — but you should always get a competing quote from an independent lender first. A buyer's agent can help you compare the true cost of the deal across lenders.

Grand Homes Floor Plans & Design Process

Grand Homes' DFW floor plan portfolio spans approximately 1,900–5,000 square feet, with four- and five-bedroom plans making up the majority of their active inventory. Community-specific plan availability varies significantly — East DFW communities like Heath Golf & Yacht Club offer larger luxury plans ($735K–$1.3M), while communities in Fate and Corinth focus on move-up families in the $415K–$860K range. For relocating families comparing multiple suburbs, the plan selection available in each community is one of the first things worth verifying before visiting model homes.

Semi-Custom Options

Grand Homes positions itself as a semi-custom builder, meaning buyers can select from structural options before construction begins. These typically include room additions (study, game room, media room), ceiling height upgrades, bay window extensions, and additional garage options. Structural changes must be selected before the slab is poured — once framing begins, the floor plan is fixed. The level of structural flexibility varies by community and plan; communities with active sales have the most options available, while communities selling spec inventory have fixed floor plans. For buyers relocating from a distance who can't visit frequently, Grand's construction managers have historically been noted (in verified Eliant reviews) for providing regular photo updates and communication throughout the build process.

Design Center

Grand Homes operates an in-house design center where buyers select interior finishes: flooring, countertops, cabinet styles and hardware, tile, paint, lighting fixtures, and appliance packages. The design center appointment typically occurs early in the process, often before the buyer has walked through their community lot with the builder — a sequencing that some buyers have flagged as counterproductive in Eliant reviews, noting they would have made different selections after better understanding the home's layout and natural light. Ask about scheduling a builder walkthrough before your design center appointment if this concerns you.

Standard features included at base price

Grand Homes includes ENERGY STAR certification as a standard feature across their plans, which includes upgraded insulation (R-38 in attic), low-E windows, and programmable thermostats. Architectural shingles with a 30-year manufacturer warranty and A/C compressors with a 5-year manufacturer warranty are standard inclusions. Wood flooring in main living areas, granite or quartz countertops, and stainless appliances are included in base plans — verify the specific package in the community you're considering, as standard inclusions can vary by price tier. Grand's homes are built to exceed the Texas Energy Code as a baseline; confirm the current ENERGY STAR status for any community before purchase, as program participation can change.

Grand Homes DFW — Frequently Asked Questions

Is Grand Homes a good builder?

Grand Homes holds an A+ BBB rating and earns a 4.3/5 score from 997 independently verified Eliant homeowner reviews (November 2024–May 2026), with a 4.4 willingness-to-refer rating — both are solid indicators for a builder of their volume. The company has built exclusively in DFW for 40 years under family ownership, which provides a level of local market continuity you won't find at most national builders. Where Grand receives the most critical feedback is post-closing warranty responsiveness — specifically, warranty items approaching expiration not being fully resolved, and HVAC concerns requiring multiple service trips. Grand is a good builder for families who stay engaged during the warranty period and document issues in writing. If "set it and forget it" post-closing is important to you, compare Grand's warranty process carefully against builders that offer third-party structural warranty administration (like 2-10 HBW).

How much do Grand Homes homes cost in DFW?

Grand Homes' active DFW communities range from approximately $415,000 on the entry side (move-up communities in Fate and Corinth) to $1.3 million or more in luxury communities like Heath Golf & Yacht Club. The sweet spot of Grand's portfolio — where most of their community count concentrates — is roughly $500,000–$900,000. Floor plan sizes range from around 1,900 square feet to over 5,000 square feet. Pricing varies significantly by community, lot premium, structural options, and design center selections. Base prices are set by the builder; design center upgrades can add $30,000–$100,000+ depending on choices. Always ask the sales team for a clear breakdown of base price, lot premium, and standard vs. upgrade items before evaluating value.

Where does Grand Homes build in DFW?

Grand Homes is currently active in 14+ DFW suburbs, including Fate, Wylie, Rockwall, Heath, Corinth, Frisco, McKinney, Prosper, Celina, Anna, Forney, Midlothian, Irving, and Grand Prairie. Their footprint covers both the East DFW corridor (Rockwall County and surrounding areas) and the established North DFW growth corridor. Grand notably does not build in highly developed, land-constrained suburbs like Coppell, Southlake, or Flower Mound — those markets have minimal raw land for new construction at Grand's scale. The specific communities open at any given time change as phases sell out, so always verify current availability through the builder or through Kristen's ShowingNew portal.

What is Grand Homes' warranty?

Grand Homes provides a standard 1-2-10 limited express warranty: 1 year for workmanship (defects in materials and construction quality), 2 years for mechanical systems (plumbing, electrical, HVAC distribution), and 10 years for structural defects (foundation, load-bearing walls, structural systems). These warranty periods match the Texas RCLA legal minimums — Grand's workmanship warranty does not exceed the 1-year floor. Warranty administration is handled in-house by Grand's own warranty team, not a third-party company. Based on verified buyer reviews, warranty responsiveness — particularly in the months approaching warranty expiration — is the area that receives the most critical feedback. Keep written records of all warranty requests, including dates submitted and responses received.

Do I need a Realtor to buy a Grand Homes new construction?

You don't need one, but you should have one — and it costs you nothing extra. Grand Homes pays the buyer's agent commission; the builder price is the same whether you bring a Realtor or not. Grand's sales agent works for Grand Homes, not for you. A buyer's agent reviews the purchase contract, explains upgrade pricing relative to market value, advises on lot selection, recommends independent inspectors, and represents your interests at the pre-closing walkthrough. If you visit a Grand Homes model home without registering a Realtor on your first visit, you may lose the ability to have that Realtor represent you for that community — this is why calling Kristen before your visit (not after) matters.

How long does Grand Homes take to build a house in DFW?

Grand Homes' typical build timeline in DFW runs approximately 8–14 months from contract to closing for a to-be-built home with structural options selected. Move-in ready or spec inventory homes can close in 30–60 days. Timeline can vary based on community demand, permit timing, subcontractor availability, and how many structural options are involved. Supply chain disruptions have extended timelines industry-wide in recent years. Relocating buyers with firm lease-end dates should discuss expected timelines with the sales team early and build in buffer when planning school enrollment and corporate relocation deadlines.

Is Grand Homes' in-house lender (Grand Home Loans) a good deal?

Grand Home Loans is Grand Homes' in-house preferred lender, and incentives (rate buydowns, closing cost credits) are typically available only if you finance through them. Whether it's a good deal depends entirely on the current rate and terms compared to outside lenders — sometimes the incentive structure makes Grand Home Loans genuinely the better financial choice; other times, the rate isn't competitive enough to offset the incentive value. The only way to know is to get a competing quote from an independent lender before committing. A buyer's agent (like Kristen) can help you run the numbers across both scenarios so you're making a fully informed decision, not just taking the builder's word for it.
