Who Is Meritage Homes? Score 73 B+

Meritage Homes was founded in 1985 as Monterey Homes in Scottsdale, Arizona, by Steve Hilton and William "Bill" Cleverly. The company rebranded as Meritage Homes Corporation in 1997 and began trading on the New York Stock Exchange under ticker MTH. In 2025, Meritage celebrated its 40th anniversary by delivering its 200,000th home, a milestone that places it among the most experienced production builders in the country. Today it is the fifth-largest public homebuilder in the United States, operating in 12 states across more than 300 communities.

In Dallas–Fort Worth, Meritage builds as part of its Central region, concentrating in the Collin and Denton County growth corridors. Their DFW communities — including McKinney, Anna, Princeton, Denton, and Corinth — sit firmly in the entry-level to move-up production segment, where price points remain accessible relative to DFW's premium established suburbs. The builder's defining competitive claim is one no other top-10 national builder can match: every single Meritage home, in every market, is built to ENERGY STAR certification standards.

What Sets Meritage Apart

Three features distinguish Meritage from other production builders in the same DFW price bracket — one is a verifiable EPA program, one is a structural product decision, and one is a financing integration that delivers real short-term savings when conditions align.

100% ENERGY STAR Certified — Every Home, No Exceptions

Meritage is the only top-10 national homebuilder that builds 100% of its homes to ENERGY STAR certification — not as an upgrade, not in select communities, but as the standard for every plan in every market. The company has earned eleven consecutive EPA Partner of the Year awards for Sustained Excellence and has delivered more than 99,000 ENERGY STAR-certified homes since 2001. In DFW's climate, where summer utility bills can run $300–$500/month on older construction, the spray foam insulation, low-E windows, and third-party-tested air sealing in a Meritage home typically reduce energy consumption by 20–30% compared to a comparable code-built resale home. For families budgeting a new mortgage, that $150–$250/month utility savings is real money every month.

Spring / Signature / Premier Series — Three Tiers, One Community

Meritage organizes its DFW floor plans into three distinct product tiers within the same community: Spring Series (entry-level, approximately 1,318–2,357 sq ft), Signature Series (mid-range, approximately 1,833–3,100 sq ft), and Premier Series (larger move-up, approximately 1,874–3,429 sq ft). This structure means a relocating family can enter at the Spring level and see exactly what the Premier plans look like before committing — important for families who plan to upsize or want to understand the full neighborhood range before signing a contract.

MTH Mortgage — In-House Financing With Real Rate Buydown Capacity

Meritage holds a 65% ownership stake in MTH Mortgage, its affiliated lender. This structure allows Meritage to offer rate buydown promotions tied to closing timelines — in active promotion periods, stepped rates as low as 2.99% in year one have been published (confirm current terms at the community level). The trade-off: incentive programs require financing through MTH Mortgage and using a preferred title company, which limits your ability to independently shop lenders while still receiving the advertised discount. Always get a competing quote from an independent lender and compare the full cost of each option before deciding.

Meritage Homes Warranty — Meets Texas Minimum on Workmanship

Texas law (the Residential Construction Liability Act, RCLA) sets minimum warranty standards for all new home builders: 1 year for workmanship and materials, 2 years for mechanical systems, and 10 years for structural defects. Meritage Homes meets these minimums exactly — their 1-year workmanship warranty is the legal floor in Texas, not an enhancement above it. Cosmetic defects (paint, drywall finishing, trim) are addressed one time only, at or near the 1-year anniversary closing date. For relocating families accustomed to builders in states that offer 2-year workmanship coverage as standard, this is a meaningful distinction that's worth understanding before signing.

How Meritage Handles Warranty Repairs

Meritage uses an online portal for all warranty claims. Homeowners log into their account, submit a work order describing the issue, and are contacted by the Meritage warranty team to schedule a repair appointment. One important operational limitation: the system allows only one active work order at a time. If a submitted ticket is not officially closed by the warranty team — even after a visit was made — you cannot open a new one. This has been a recurring complaint in buyer reviews: technicians visit, leave the ticket open, and the homeowner is blocked from reporting the next issue. Document every interaction in writing, confirm ticket status after each appointment, and follow up promptly in writing if closure is not recorded.

What the warranty doesn't cover

Standard exclusions include cosmetic wear and tear, normal settling cracks below ASTM measurement standards, landscaping and drainage unless directly caused by a structural defect, damage from homeowner modifications or deferred maintenance, and items covered under separate manufacturer warranties (appliances, HVAC equipment). Foundation movement is covered structurally for 10 years — but buyers in specific communities with documented soil movement history (Roanoke/Briarwyck had known foundation issues affecting multiple homes) should request site-specific soil reports and ask about the community's soil investigation history before signing. Known subdivision-level issues are not always disclosed proactively.

  • Foundation concerns in specific communities — The Roanoke/Briarwyck subdivision had multiple documented foundation movement cases. This is community-specific, not company-wide — but ask for soil reports and community history before signing in any Meritage development.

Meritage Homes Incentives & Current Promotions

Incentives Change — Here's How to Find Them

Meritage runs active promotions on a monthly cadence, typically tied to phase closes and inventory clearance goals. As of May 2026, Meritage had promotions running on homes closing by June 29, 2026, through MTH Mortgage. Offers are community-specific and lot-specific — two homes in the same community can have different incentive packages based on phase and inventory status. Rate Buydown

Meritage offers temporary or permanent rate buydowns when using MTH Mortgage. Published promotions in May 2026 included stepped rates as low as 2.99% in year one. Confirm whether any buydown offered is temporary (rate adjusts upward in year 2 or 3) or permanent before evaluating against an independent lender's standard rate.

Closing Cost Credits

On select inventory homes and end-of-phase lots, Meritage has offered closing cost credits or sales price reductions up to $30,000. These credits are typically tied to specific quick-move-in homes. Verify current availability by community — not all communities run the same credit at the same time.

Move-In Ready Packages

Some Meritage spec homes include bundled finish packages (flooring, cabinetry, countertops) priced into the home at model-level selections. These packages can be valuable for families who can't make multiple design center trips, but compare the package's per-item cost against what you'd pay selecting independently if customization matters to you.

Important: Builder incentives are almost always tied to using their preferred lender. That's not inherently bad — sometimes the rate is genuinely competitive — but you should always get a competing quote from an independent lender first. A buyer's agent can help you compare the true cost of the deal across lenders.

Meritage Homes Floor Plans & Design Process

In DFW, Meritage offers three plan series spanning approximately 1,318 to 3,429 square feet. As of May 2026, base prices in active DFW communities range from approximately $268,000 (Spring Series, McKinney) to just under $480,000 (Premier Series, Corinth and Princeton). Lot premiums, elevation selections, and design center upgrades add to base pricing — always ask for the full out-of-pocket number, not just base price.

Production Floor Plans

Meritage is a production builder. Floor plan layouts are determined before construction begins and cannot be structurally modified — there is no option to move walls, change room configurations, or add structural elements after the plan is selected. What buyers control are design center finishes: flooring, cabinetry, countertop materials, fixtures, and sometimes exterior elevation. Move-in ready (spec) homes have these selections already made and locked in. If a specific finish combination is important to your family, confirm whether the plan you want is still available as a to-be-built option or only as a spec home with pre-selected finishes.

Design Center

Meritage maintains a Design Studio process where buyers select interior finishes from curated packages. For relocating families who cannot be in DFW regularly during the build process, Meritage offers virtual design consultations — confirmed available on their buyer resources pages. Design selections are made during a scheduled appointment after contract signing; the window for decisions is fixed, so remote buyers should plan their virtual consultation scheduling carefully.

Standard features included at base price

Every Meritage home includes ENERGY STAR-certified spray foam insulation, low-E windows, and high-efficiency HVAC as standard — not as upgrades. Smart home pre-wiring (for smart home devices and systems) is included on all plans. Finish levels vary by series: Spring plans carry entry-level standard cabinet, countertop, and flooring packages; Premier plans ship with an elevated standard package that includes upgraded countertop and cabinetry options. Ask the on-site team for the specific standard selections on the plan you're evaluating — the difference between series packages can be meaningful in terms of what you'd otherwise pay as upgrades.

What is Meritage Homes' warranty?

Meritage offers a standard 1-2-10 warranty: 1 year for workmanship and materials, 2 years for mechanical systems (plumbing, electrical, HVAC), and 10 years for structural defects. The 1-year workmanship coverage matches the Texas legal minimum under the RCLA — it is not an improvement above it. How long does Meritage Homes take to build a house in DFW?

Build times in DFW vary by community, plan series, and current pipeline capacity. Move-in ready (spec) homes are available immediately or within 30–90 days. To-be-built homes — selecting a plan from available lots — typically run 8–14 months from contract to close in current DFW market conditions. What does ENERGY STAR certification actually mean on a Meritage home?

ENERGY STAR is a U.S. Environmental Protection Agency certification program that requires homes to meet specific standards for insulation, air sealing, windows, HVAC efficiency, and duct performance — verified by third-party testing before certification is granted. Meritage builds 100% of its homes to ENERGY STAR standards, making it the only top-10 national builder to do so across its entire production. In DFW's climate — where triple-digit summer temperatures are routine — an ENERGY STAR certified home typically consumes 20–30% less energy than a comparable code-built home. For a family relocating from a milder climate, that can mean $150–$250/month in lower utility costs compared to a non-certified resale home of similar size, which is a real offset against the new construction price premium.
