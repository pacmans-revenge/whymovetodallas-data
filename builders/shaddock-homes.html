Who Is Shaddock Homes? Score 86 A

Shaddock Homes is the longest-running family-owned builder in DFW. Founded in 1966 by Peter H. Shaddock in Plano, Texas, the company has built homes in North Texas for more than 60 years without being acquired, merged, or taken public. That independence matters: purchasing decisions, design standards, and warranty commitments flow through the same Shaddock family that has put its name on homes since the Johnson administration. Today the company is led by Peter Shaddock Jr. and Beth Shaddock Cesare, Peter Sr.'s son and daughter, with Todd Shaddock also active in leadership.

Shaddock builds in the move-up and luxury segments — priced from the mid-$400s in Celina to over $1.4M in master-planned communities like Light Farms in Celina and Custer Ridge Estates in Allen. They are not a volume production builder; their DFW footprint at any given time is concentrated in a handful of carefully selected communities across North Collin County, the Rockwall corridor, and Denton County. For 2026, active communities span Allen, Argyle, Celina, Frisco, Rockwall, and Wylie. The company has also expanded to the Austin market (Leander), though that product is separate from their DFW operations.

What Sets Shaddock Apart

In a DFW new construction market dominated by national volume builders, Shaddock occupies a distinct position: a locally-rooted family company building semi-custom homes at move-up and luxury prices. A few specific things differentiate their product from both production builders and fully custom firms.

60-Year Family Ownership — No Acquisition Risk

National builders regularly acquire regional brands and then gradually standardize product lines and downgrade specifications post-merger. Shaddock has resisted that path for six decades. The Shaddock name on your contract is the same family that will answer your warranty call two years later. For relocating buyers who've watched out-of-state builders change their standards after acquisition, that continuity has real value.

Name-Brand Fixtures and Materials as Standard

Shaddock specifies name-brand fixtures and appliances — Moen, Kohler, and GE Appliances — at the base price, rather than generic builder-grade hardware that buyers often replace within a few years. Their brick and stone elevation work is consistently cited in reviews as more detailed than what you get at the same price point from a high-volume national builder. These aren't upgrade line items; they're the standard product.

In-House Design Studio in The Colony

Shaddock operates their own dedicated design studio at 6601 Cascades Court in The Colony — a fixed showroom staffed by design professionals who guide buyers through interior selections, exterior treatments, and structural option decisions. This is not outsourced to a regional design center shared with other builders. For families relocating from California or the Northeast who are accustomed to semi-custom or custom products, Shaddock's studio process will feel familiar; for buyers new to new construction, having a dedicated team (rather than a crowded shared design center) tends to reduce the overwhelm.

Shaddock Homes Warranty — Meets Texas Minimum on Workmanship

Shaddock uses the industry-standard 1-2-10 structure administered through 2-10 Home Buyers Warranty (2-10 HBW), a nationally recognized third-party warranty company. Texas law sets the floor at 1 year workmanship / 2 years systems / 10 years structural under the Residential Construction Liability Act (RCLA). Shaddock matches that floor exactly for workmanship — they do not extend workmanship coverage beyond the one-year Texas minimum, which is worth noting because some regional competitors (Highland Homes, Perry Homes) offer 2-year workmanship coverage. The warranty is transferable to subsequent owners, which can add value at resale.

How Shaddock Handles Warranty Repairs

Warranty service requests are submitted through Shaddock's online warranty portal at shaddockhomes.com/warranty-request. Once received, the team will contact you to schedule an inspection appointment; appointments are available Monday through Friday, 8 a.m. to 4 p.m. The 2-10 HBW third-party backing means that for structural claims in years 2–10, you have recourse through an insured program rather than solely through the builder's financial ability to pay — a meaningful protection on a six-figure asset. Shaddock's Google review rating of 4.7 stars (222 reviews) suggests their warranty process performs reasonably well in practice, though individual experiences vary.

What the warranty doesn't cover — and what to do before year one ends

Standard new-construction exclusions apply: normal shrinkage and settling, damage from buyer modifications, landscaping and irrigation (unless tied to a covered system), cosmetic blemishes that fall within builder tolerances, and appliance warranties (covered by the appliance manufacturer, not Shaddock). More practically: your workmanship warranty expires at the one-year mark. That means you need to submit a comprehensive punch list of all visible defects — nail pops, paint inconsistencies, grout issues, cabinet adjustments, door alignment — before your one-year anniversary. Do not wait to be contacted. Commission an independent home inspection at months 10–11 to document everything before the window closes.

Honest Assessment — What Families Love and What to Know

Shaddock Homes scores an 86 (A) on our 100-point builder index. Here's what drives that score — and where relocating families should pay attention.

Strengths

  • 60-year family ownership — The Shaddock family has built in DFW for six decades without being acquired or going public. Leadership continuity means the standards established by the founder are still intact in current product lines.
  • Detailed exterior elevations — Brick, stone, and masonry work on Shaddock homes is consistently cited as more elaborate than what national production builders deliver at the same price point. Curb appeal is a genuine differentiator, not a marketing claim.
  • Name-brand fixtures at base price — Moen, Kohler, and GE Appliances are standard, not upgrades. Buyers don't get stuck with anonymous hardware they'll want to replace.
  • Strong structural reputation — Foundation and structural issues are rarely flagged in Shaddock buyer reviews. In a market where foundation performance is a legitimate concern, this track record is worth noting.
  • In-house design studio — A dedicated Shaddock design facility in The Colony, staffed by Shaddock employees, offers a more curated semi-custom selection experience than shared regional design centers used by larger national builders.
  • High customer satisfaction ratings — 4.7 stars across 222 Google reviews reflects a sustained pattern of positive buyer experiences, not an outlier.

Things to Know

  • 1-year workmanship warranty only — Shaddock meets the Texas minimum but does not extend beyond it. Highland Homes and Perry Homes both offer 2-year workmanship coverage. On a $700k+ home, that extra year matters — schedule your independent inspection at months 10–11.
  • Quality can vary by framing crew — Buyer reviews note that build quality is generally strong but not perfectly consistent across crews. Requesting independent third-party inspections at slab, frame, and pre-drywall stages is worth the cost at Shaddock's price points.
  • BBB grade is B- — With only 2 complaints in 3 years, the volume is low. But complaints include an unresolved drainage issue and a reported instance of sales pressure via threatening communications. Not disqualifying, but worth awareness.
  • Narrow DFW footprint — If your target suburb is Plano, Southlake, Coppell, Flower Mound, McKinney, or Prosper, Shaddock may not have an active community there at the time you're buying. Their concentrated footprint means fewer options than national builders with 30+ DFW communities.

Incentives Change — Here's How to Find Them

Rate Buydowns

Shaddock has offered rate buydown incentives tied to their preferred lender in the past. Specific rates and points vary by community and change when phases sell out. Verify before your visit — never assume a rate you saw online is still available.

Closing Cost Credits

Closing cost assistance has been offered in some communities, typically tied to preferred lender use. Dollar amounts and eligibility vary by community and contract date. Ask the sales agent for the current written offer, not a verbal summary.

Design Center Upgrades

Upgrade credit packages toward the design studio are periodically offered on spec homes or at phase closes. These are often the best value for move-up buyers who want premium finishes without paying full upgrade pricing.

Important: Builder incentives are almost always tied to using their preferred lender. A buyer's agent can help you compare the true cost of the deal across lenders.

Shaddock Homes Floor Plans & Design Process

Shaddock offers a broad range of floor plans across their active DFW communities, from compact 1,500 sq ft cottages in Celina (Cottages of Celina) to estate-scale plans exceeding 4,800 sq ft in communities like Light Farms Graham/Reagan and Custer Ridge Estates in Allen. Most plans fall in the 2,500–4,000 sq ft range, oriented toward families with 3–5 bedrooms and multiple living areas. Pricing in DFW ranges from the high-$300s in Celina entry communities to above $1.4M in premium master-planned communities.

Semi-Custom Options

Shaddock is a semi-custom builder, which sits between production and fully custom on the flexibility spectrum. Their base floor plans are fixed — you're choosing from Shaddock's established designs, not drawing from scratch. However, many plans offer structural options that can be selected before the slab pour: room additions, covered patio extensions, media rooms, additional bedrooms, and alternative primary suite configurations. Once framing begins, structural changes are no longer possible. Interior finish selections — flooring, cabinetry, countertops, tile, fixtures — are made through the design studio after contract and before construction milestones. Buyers who've worked with national production builders typically find Shaddock's semi-custom process gives them meaningfully more personalization; buyers coming from full custom backgrounds should understand this is still a builder-defined plan, not a blank canvas.

Design Studio

Shaddock's design studio is located at 6601 Cascades Court, The Colony, TX 75056. It is a dedicated Shaddock facility, not a shared center — buyers work with Shaddock design professionals who know the product line and can provide real context on which finishes perform well in DFW's climate. For families relocating from out of state who will be making most selections remotely, Shaddock's design team has experience accommodating virtual appointments and managing decisions via digital samples and video walkthroughs. Ask your sales contact specifically about their remote buyer process when you make contact.

Standard features included at base price

Standard specifications at base price include Moen and Kohler plumbing fixtures, GE kitchen appliances, detailed brick and stone exterior elevations (elevation-dependent), interior trim package, and integrated outdoor-indoor living configurations on many plans. Specific standard specs vary by community and plan — always request the community's specification sheet from the sales office before making assumptions about what's included versus what's an upgrade.

Shaddock Homes DFW — Frequently Asked Questions

Is Shaddock Homes a good builder?

Shaddock Homes scores an 86 out of 100 (Grade A) on our 100-point builder index and holds a 4.7-star Google rating across 222 reviews — both strong indicators of sustained performance. They are the longest-running family-owned builder in DFW (60+ years), which means the ownership and standards that established the brand are still intact today. Their structural reputation is excellent, and buyers consistently praise the exterior elevation work and name-brand fixtures. The main tradeoff is a 1-year workmanship warranty (Texas minimum), whereas some regional competitors offer 2-year workmanship coverage. For move-up and luxury buyers in the six suburbs where Shaddock is currently active, they are one of the stronger semi-custom options in DFW.

How much do Shaddock Homes homes cost in DFW?

In Dallas–Fort Worth, Shaddock Homes currently ranges from approximately $399,000 (entry-level plans in Celina's Cottages of Celina community) to over $1.4 million (estate plans in Light Farms Graham/Reagan in Celina and Custer Ridge Estates in Allen). The majority of their active inventory falls in the $500,000–$1.1 million range, oriented toward move-up and luxury family buyers. Pricing varies significantly by community, lot size, plan size, and design studio selections — the base price is the starting point, not the all-in number. Always confirm current pricing directly with the community's sales office, as prices change as phases sell out.

Where does Shaddock Homes build in DFW?

As of 2026, Shaddock's active DFW communities are in Allen, Argyle, Celina, Frisco, Rockwall, and Wylie. Their footprint is deliberately concentrated — they build in fewer communities than national volume builders and maintain them longer. Historically they have also built in Plano, McKinney, Prosper, Forney, Fate, Lewisville, and other North Texas suburbs. If your target suburb isn't in their current portfolio, check with Kristen about the next planned community opening or consider parallel conversations with semi-custom builders like Highland Homes or David Weekley who have broader coverage.

What is Shaddock Homes' warranty?

Shaddock provides a standard 1-2-10 warranty administered through 2-10 Home Buyers Warranty (2-10 HBW), a nationally recognized third-party warranty company. This covers: 1 year for workmanship defects (visible fit-and-finish issues — nail pops, drywall, trim, tile, doors), 2 years for mechanical systems (HVAC, plumbing, electrical), and 10 years for structural defects (foundation and load-bearing elements). The warranty is transferable to subsequent owners. It matches the Texas RCLA minimum for workmanship but does not extend beyond it. Important: you must submit all workmanship claims before your one-year anniversary or the builder can decline them. Commission an independent inspector at months 10–11 to document everything before the window closes.

Do I need a Realtor to buy a Shaddock Homes new construction?

You don't need one, but you should have one — and it costs you nothing extra. Shaddock pays the buyer's agent commission; the builder's price is the same whether you bring a Realtor or not. Shaddock's sales agent works for Shaddock, not for you. A buyer's agent reviews the contract, explains upgrade pricing relative to resale market value, advises on lot selection, recommends independent inspectors, and represents your interests at the pre-closing walkthrough. How long does Shaddock Homes take to build a house in DFW?

Typical build times for a Shaddock semi-custom home in DFW range from approximately 8 to 14 months from contract to close, depending on plan size, community, structural options selected, and supply chain conditions. Spec (inventory) homes that are already under construction can close in 60–120 days depending on how far along they are. Shaddock's semi-custom process adds some time compared to pure production builders because design studio selections must be finalized early in the timeline — delays in design decisions can push the schedule. For families with a hard move date, ask the sales team for a written timeline milestone schedule at contract and build in a 30-day buffer when coordinating your lease end or current home sale.

How does Shaddock Homes compare to Highland Homes or David Weekley in DFW?

All three are regional or semi-custom builders operating in DFW's move-up and luxury segments. Highland Homes offers a 2-year workmanship warranty (versus Shaddock's 1-year) and a broader DFW footprint — if your target suburb is outside Shaddock's active communities, Highland is worth a close look. David Weekley competes at similar price points with a national brand behind them and a strong emphasis on energy efficiency (their BuildSmart program). Shaddock's edge is local family ownership and longevity — 60 years in DFW without acquisition — plus an in-house design studio experience. The right choice depends on your target suburb, price range, and how much you value local ownership versus broader coverage or warranty length.
