Who Is Megatel Homes? Score 76 B+

Megatel Homes was founded in 2006 by brothers Aaron and Zach Ipour in the Dallas–Fort Worth area. Starting from a DFW base, Megatel has grown into one of the most recognized builder brands in Texas — now active in DFW, Houston, Austin, San Antonio, and Oklahoma City — with more than 100 developments and several thousand homes completed since founding. The Megatel Group includes both the homebuilding operation and MCI Mortgage, Inc., the company's in-house lending arm.

Megatel's signature concept is the lagoon community — master-planned neighborhoods built around Crystal Lagoon technology. Sicily Lagoon in Princeton and Venetian in McKinney are the company's current DFW flagships, featuring manmade bodies of water with white sand beaches, resort amenities, paddleboarding access, and event programming. This lifestyle differentiator is what draws most Megatel buyers in — it delivers a resort-style community experience at production-builder prices that few competitors can match in the $309k–$579k range. Megatel concentrates its DFW footprint in the fastest-growing Collin County corridors and the Kaufman/Rockwall eastern tier.

Megatel Homes Warranty — Meets Texas Legal Minimum

Texas law (the Residential Construction Liability Act, RCLA) sets the floor for new home warranties: 1 year workmanship, 2 years systems, 10 years structural. Megatel Homes meets this floor on all three tiers through the 2-10 Home Buyers Warranty (2-10 HBW) program — a well-established third-party structural warranty administrator. Megatel holds Platinum Builder status with 2-10 HBW for four consecutive years, reflecting consistent program enrollment. Note: meeting the legal minimum means Megatel does not provide extended workmanship coverage the way some top-rated builders do (Perry Homes, for example, offers 2-year workmanship); buyers should factor this into their due diligence plan.

How Megatel Handles Warranty Repairs

All warranty service requests must be submitted in writing through the 2-10 HBW homeowner portal at secure.2-10.com/Homeowner/. Megatel processes workmanship and systems claims in-house for the first two years; structural defect claims in years 2–10 are handled by 2-10 HBW directly. Important caveat: documented BBB complaints specifically name warranty non-response as a recurring issue — multiple buyers report difficulty reaching anyone with authority after closing. Maintain complete written records of every submission, including confirmation emails and timestamps. If a claim goes unanswered, escalate through 2-10 HBW's dispute process.

What the warranty doesn't cover

Normal wear and tear, cosmetic issues (minor paint touch-ups, small drywall scuffs), buyer-caused damage, deferred maintenance, HOA-related infrastructure, items excluded by the 2-10 HBW policy terms, and any condition not specifically documented in writing before closing. Review your 2-10 HBW certificate carefully at closing — exclusions are defined by the policy, not by marketing language.

Honest Assessment — What Families Love and What to Know

Megatel Homes scores a 76 (B+) on our 100-point builder index. Here's what drives that score — and where relocating families should pay close attention before signing.

Strengths

  • Crystal Lagoon lifestyle — Sicily Lagoon (Princeton) and Venetian (McKinney) deliver white sand beaches, open-water swimming, and resort amenities at a price point no other DFW production builder matches.
  • 24-suburb DFW footprint — One of the widest active coverage maps of any regional Texas builder, concentrated in the Collin County growth belt where families are targeting top ISDs (McKinney, Princeton, Celina, Anna, Frisco).
  • Entry-accessible pricing — At $309k–$579k, Megatel delivers community amenity levels typically associated with higher price tiers, making resort-style living reachable for move-up buyers.
  • 2-10 HBW Platinum Builder (4 years) — Four consecutive years of this designation confirm consistent participation in the full 2-10 structural warranty program.
  • In-house lender can simplify closings — MCI Mortgage is owned by the same founders, enabling direct coordination on builder-incentive financing; always compare with an independent lender before committing.

Things to Know

  • B- BBB rating with 29 documented complaints — Below average for DFW regional builders. Complaint volume and grade are meaningful signals; 29 complaints over three years is the highest documented count among builders at this price point on this site.
  • Construction delays are specific and recurrent — Verified BBB complaints document timelines extended by 12–38+ months, including one case where infrastructure was unbuilt at 38 months post-contract. If you have a hard close deadline, the delay risk is real.
  • Price-increase demands at contract stage — At least one BBB complaint documents a $30,000 price-increase demand delivered days before scheduled closing. Have your Realtor review all contract terms and price-change provisions before signing.
  • Warranty non-response is documented — Multiple BBB complaints specifically name failure to respond to warranty service requests after closing. Post-close service is Megatel's most consistently cited weakness.
  • Incentives tied to in-house lender — MCI Mortgage is part of the Megatel Group; rate-buydown and closing-cost offers require using MCI. Always get an independent competing mortgage quote before deciding.

Megatel Homes Floor Plans & Design Process

Megatel's DFW floor plans range from townhome-style configurations (approximately 1,900 sq ft) to larger single-family homes, covering 2–5 bedroom layouts across multiple communities. In the $309k–$579k DFW price range, plans typically run 1,800–3,500 sq ft. Community-specific availability varies — the plans live page at megatelhomes.com/plans filters by community and price range.

Production Floor Plans with Design Center Selections

Megatel operates as a production builder, meaning the structural floor plan you select is fixed before pouring. Structural changes to the plan are not available after contract execution. Within the plan, design center selections — flooring, cabinet finishes, countertop materials, fixtures, and appliances — are made at Megatel's in-house design center after contract signing. Design appointments typically occur within the first 30 days of contract; coordinate your schedule early.

Design Center

Megatel operates an in-house design center for selections. For relocating buyers who cannot visit in person, Megatel's sales team has facilitated virtual selection appointments — confirm the virtual option with the specific community's sales agent before contracting if this applies to your situation. In-person visits to the design center are generally preferred for finishes decisions that are difficult to evaluate on screen.

Standard features included at base price

Megatel markets its homes as eco-friendly and energy-efficient construction as a baseline standard. Community-specific inclusions vary by plan and community — review the published standards list for the specific floor plan you're considering with the sales agent before contracting. Do not assume standard features listed in one community apply to another; product lines and inclusion packages differ across Megatel's wide DFW footprint.

Megatel Homes DFW — Frequently Asked Questions

Is Megatel Homes a good builder?

Megatel Homes is a regional Texas builder with a BBB rating of B- and 29 documented complaints over three years — below the standard of top-rated DFW builders like Perry Homes (A+) or Highland Homes (A+). That said, the B- rating doesn't tell the whole story. Megatel's Crystal Lagoon communities (Sicily Lagoon in Princeton, Venetian in McKinney) offer a genuine lifestyle differentiator not found at this price point with other builders. The most accurate answer: Megatel is a compelling option for the right buyer — families who prioritize the lagoon community lifestyle, are buying in their growth-corridor sweet spot, and have flexibility in their move timeline. It's a weaker option for buyers who need tight timeline certainty, have a hard school-enrollment deadline, or place a premium on post-close service responsiveness. Come in with independent buyer's representation and a thorough inspection plan.

How much do Megatel Homes homes cost in DFW?

In the Dallas–Fort Worth area, Megatel homes range from approximately $309,000 to $579,000. The lower end of that range covers townhome-style or smaller single-family plans in emerging growth markets like Princeton and Forney. The upper range reaches larger single-family homes in more established growth communities. Pricing varies significantly by community, lot premium (lagoon-view and corner lots typically carry premiums), and design center selections — the published base price is the starting point, not the final cost. Megatel's community-specific pricing is published at megatelhomes.com/communities/dallas-fort-worth-tx.

Where does Megatel Homes build in DFW?

Megatel builds across 24 DFW suburbs, with the heaviest concentration in Collin County's northern growth corridor: Princeton, McKinney, Anna, Celina, Frisco, Prosper, Allen, Plano, and Little Elm are among their most active markets. They also build in the Kaufman and Rockwall county corridor (Forney, Royse City, Rockwall), Denton County (Flower Mound, Roanoke, Lewisville, Denton), and several established mid-tier suburbs (Irving, Mansfield, Fort Worth, Waxahachie, Midlothian, Lucas, Sachse, Wylie). Community availability changes monthly as phases sell out — always verify current availability directly at megatelhomes.com or through Kristen's ShowingNew portal.

What is Megatel Homes' warranty?

Megatel provides a 1-year workmanship warranty, 2-year systems warranty, and 10-year structural warranty through the 2-10 Home Buyers Warranty (2-10 HBW) program — meeting Texas's legal minimum on all three tiers. Megatel holds the 2-10 HBW Platinum Builder designation for four consecutive years, reflecting consistent program participation. Warranty service requests must be submitted in writing through the 2-10 HBW portal at secure.2-10.com/Homeowner. One important note: documented BBB complaints specifically name warranty non-response as a recurring issue — maintain written records of all submissions with timestamps, and escalate through 2-10 HBW's dispute process if a claim goes unanswered.

Do I need a Realtor to buy a Megatel Homes new construction?

You don't need one, but you should have one — and it costs you nothing extra. Megatel pays the buyer's agent commission; the builder price is the same whether you bring a Realtor or not. Megatel's sales agent works for Megatel, not for you. A buyer's agent reviews the contract, explains upgrade pricing relative to market value, advises on lot selection, recommends independent inspectors, and represents your interests at the pre-closing walkthrough. If you visit a model home without registering a Realtor on your first visit, you may lose the ability to have that Realtor represent you for that community. Given Megatel's documented complaint history around contract terms and warranty non-response, having independent representation is especially important.

How long does Megatel Homes take to build a house in DFW?

For spec/inventory homes with Megatel, closes can happen within 30–90 days of contract. For build-to-order homes, typical timelines run 8–14 months from contract to close under normal conditions. However, documented BBB complaints indicate that timeline extensions at Megatel can be significant — complaints specifically reference original close dates extended by 12 months or more, and at least one buyer documented a process exceeding 38 months from contract for a home still incomplete at the time of the complaint. If you're working with a hard-deadline relocation (job start, school enrollment cutoff), discuss timeline contingencies explicitly with the sales agent, ask about the specific community's current build pace, and have your Realtor review the contract's timeline and delay provisions before signing.

What is the lagoon community experience at Megatel?

Megatel's lagoon communities are built around Crystal Lagoon technology — engineered bodies of water maintained at swimming quality using a fraction of traditional pool chemicals. Sicily Lagoon in Princeton and Venetian in McKinney are Megatel's current DFW flagship communities. Residents enjoy access to white sand beaches, open-water swimming, paddleboarding, kayaking, cabanas, and resort-style event programming. Lagoon access is an HOA-managed amenity — confirm the monthly HOA dues and any separate lagoon-use fees with the sales agent before contracting, as lagoon-community HOA dues are typically higher than standard non-amenity neighborhoods. The lagoon and amenity package is a real differentiator; just go in with full eyes on the carrying costs.
