About LGI Homes

LGI Homes was founded in 2003 by Thomas Lipar in Conroe, Texas, with a singular focus: make homeownership accessible to renters and first-time buyers who thought it was out of reach. The company went public on NASDAQ (LGIH) in 2013 and has grown into one of the fastest-moving entry-level builders in the country. Eric Lipar — Thomas's son — has served as CEO since 2009 and steered the company through its aggressive national expansion across more than 20 states.

In Dallas–Fort Worth, LGI targets the affordability tier with spec homes in outer suburbs like Princeton, Forney, Fort Worth, and Midlothian. Their signature CompleteHome™ package means every home ships move-in ready: stainless steel appliances, granite countertops, full landscaping, and window blinds included in the base price — no design center, no upsell pressure. That transparency is a genuine differentiator at this price point. However, their post-close service record carries real risk that families should understand before signing.

LGI Homes — Honest Pros & Cons

BBB Rating: F (Not Accredited) — LGI Homes holds an F rating with the Better Business Bureau based on a pattern of failure to respond to and resolve customer complaints. This is the most significant risk factor for families considering LGI. The F rating does not mean LGI is fraudulent — it reflects post-close customer service performance, particularly warranty claim response. Families who buy with LGI should document everything in writing and work with a buyer's agent who can advocate for them after closing.

Pros

  • All-inclusive pricing via CompleteHome™ — appliances, granite, landscaping, blinds included. No upgrade ambush at the design center.
  • Entry-level price point — DFW starting prices from $242k make LGI accessible for families who haven't been able to break out of renting.
  • Fast close on spec inventory — move-in-ready homes can close in 30–45 days, ideal for families on a tight corporate transfer timeline.
  • Texas roots — founded in Conroe, headquartered in The Woodlands; LGI understands the Texas market and builds to Texas conditions.
  • No upsell pressure — what you see is what you get. Families on a fixed budget won't be nudged into costly upgrades during the build process.

Cons

  • BBB F rating — post-close risk — LGI's customer service after closing is the most-cited complaint. Warranty claims can go unanswered. This is not a minor issue.
  • Zero customization — LGI builds spec only. You can't choose finishes, layouts, or lot positions. If personalization matters, look elsewhere.
  • Systems warranty gap — no explicit 2-year mechanical systems warranty (HVAC, plumbing, electrical). Standard at other builders of this caliber.
  • Outer suburbs only — LGI's DFW footprint is limited to affordability-tier locations farther from employment cores. Commute times to major employers can be 45–60 minutes.

Kristen's Take on LGI Homes

LGI does one thing genuinely well: they make the first step into homeownership feel possible for families who've been stuck renting. The CompleteHome™ package is real — you're not walking into a builder's design center and watching $40,000 evaporate before your foundation is poured. At $242k–$390k with appliances and finishes included, LGI offers something few DFW builders can match at that price point.

That said, I have to be honest with the families I work with: the BBB F rating matters. It's not a paperwork technicality — it reflects a documented pattern of complaints going unresolved after closing. For first-time buyers who have never navigated a warranty claim, or families relocating from out of state who can't easily drive back for service appointments, that post-close risk is significant. I've seen it play out, and it's stressful.

Who LGI is the right fit for: a buyer whose top priority is price and speed — someone who needs to stop renting now, understands they're buying a production home without customization options, and is prepared to advocate assertively for warranty service if issues arise. Having a buyer's agent who will document everything and push on your behalf makes a real difference with LGI.

Who should look elsewhere: if your family wants to personalize your home, if you're prioritizing school districts in Frisco ISD or Allen ISD, or if you need post-close peace of mind, I'd point you toward Highland Homes, Bloomfield Homes, or Meritage Homes — all of whom have stronger post-close service reputations and more robust warranty programs.

Bottom line: LGI can be the right move for the right buyer. Let's talk through whether Princeton Heights or LGI's other DFW communities fit your family's timeline and priorities — before you walk into their sales office without representation.

LGI Homes DFW — Frequently Asked Questions

Is LGI Homes a good builder in DFW?

LGI Homes is a legitimate, publicly traded builder with a strong track record on the construction side — they close homes efficiently and the CompleteHome™ package delivers real value at an entry-level price. The major caveat is post-close service: LGI holds an F rating with the Better Business Bureau based on a documented pattern of unresolved customer complaints, primarily around warranty response. For buyers who prioritize affordability and speed and are prepared to advocate for post-close service, LGI can be a solid choice. For families who want strong after-sale support, other DFW builders have better reputations at a slightly higher price point.

How much do LGI Homes cost in Dallas–Fort Worth?

LGI Homes in DFW currently range from approximately $242,000 to $390,000 (Q2 2026). Princeton Heights — LGI's active Collin County community — shows pricing from $289,900 to $389,900 with homes from 1,242 to 2,523 square feet. All prices include the CompleteHome™ package (appliances, granite, landscaping, blinds), so what you see is what you pay — no upgrade surprises at a design center. Pricing fluctuates with inventory and available promotions.

What does LGI Homes' CompleteHome™ package include?

The CompleteHome™ package includes: stainless steel kitchen appliances (range, dishwasher, and microwave), granite countertops throughout the home, full front and back landscaping (sod, plants, irrigation), and window blinds on all windows. These items are included in the base price of every LGI home — there is no design center where you'd add them as upgrades. The estimated value of these inclusions vs. competitor base packages is $30,000–$50,000. Specific inclusions may vary slightly by plan and community — confirm with the LGI sales representative.

What is LGI Homes' warranty in DFW?

LGI Homes offers a 1-year workmanship warranty on the entire home and a 10-year structural warranty on the foundation and load-bearing components. Unlike many other builders, LGI does not publish an explicit 2-year mechanical systems warranty (covering HVAC, plumbing, and electrical) — those systems rely on manufacturer warranties after Year 1. Always request the full written warranty document from the LGI sales team and review it with your buyer's agent before signing. The BBB F rating reflects that warranty claims have historically been difficult to resolve post-close — documentation and persistence are key.

Where does LGI Homes build in DFW?

LGI Homes is currently active in four DFW suburbs: Princeton (Collin County — Princeton Heights community), Forney (Kaufman County), Fort Worth (Tarrant County), and Midlothian (Ellis County). These communities are concentrated in the affordability corridor — outer suburbs with longer commutes to major employment centers but significantly lower entry prices. LGI does not build in the core northern suburbs like Frisco, Allen, or Flower Mound.

Do I need a Realtor to buy an LGI Homes new construction?

Yes — and it costs you nothing. LGI Homes' on-site sales agents represent LGI, not you. Having a buyer's agent means you have someone reviewing the purchase contract, advocating for your interests on inspection items, and following up on warranty claims after closing. With LGI's BBB F rating, having documented representation and a paper trail from the start is especially important. As a buyer, you don't pay the agent's commission — the builder does. Call Kristen at (602) 405-4115 before your first community visit so she can register as your agent.

How long does LGI Homes take to build in DFW?

LGI builds on spec — homes are already under construction or move-in ready when you buy them. That means closings can happen in as few as 30–45 days, significantly faster than custom or semi-custom builders that require 6–12 months. If you're on a tight corporate transfer timeline or need to be in a home quickly, LGI's inventory model can be a real advantage. The trade-off is that you select from what's available, not what you design — but for buyers who prioritize speed over customization, this is a feature, not a limitation.
