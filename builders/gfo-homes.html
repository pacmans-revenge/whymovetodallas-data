Who Is GFO Home? Score 75 B+

GFO Home is a Texas-based semi-custom builder founded in 2018 by Glenn Gehan, a third-generation homebuilder. Although Glenn's family has deep roots in Texas homebuilding — his grandfather was a co-founder of the company that became Gehan Homes — GFO Home LLC is an entirely separate and independently-owned builder. (Gehan Homes rebranded to Brightland Homes in 2023; GFO Home has no affiliation with that company.) Operating across Austin, Dallas–Fort Worth, and Houston, GFO Home entered the DFW market around 2022 with a boutique approach: fewer communities than large national builders, with a focus on semi-custom finish quality and buyer experience.

In DFW, GFO Home currently builds in six suburbs — Mansfield, Celina, Forney, Royse City, Haslet, and the Fort Worth–Aledo corridor — with eight active communities. Prices start in the $400s in Mansfield and Forney and climb to the $700s+ in Celina. The builder positions itself as a semi-custom alternative for families who want more personalization than a large production builder offers without the full premium of a true custom build. Corporate headquarters are at 1717 McKinney Ave, Suite 110, Dallas, TX 75202.

What Sets GFO Home Apart

GFO Home occupies a specific niche between large national volume builders and true custom homes. For families relocating to DFW who want more influence over their home's layout and finishes than D.R. Horton or Meritage typically allow — but don't have the timeline or budget for a fully custom build — GFO Home is worth a serious look. The semi-custom approach, family ownership, and focused DFW footprint are the three factors that distinguish it.

Third-Generation Family Builder

Glenn Gehan brings generational Texas homebuilding expertise to GFO Home. A family-owned structure means executive accountability is real — the owner is not insulated from buyer outcomes by layers of corporate management. This translates, at its best, into higher personal investment in reputation and quality. It also means the company's reputation rises and falls on each community it builds.

Semi-Custom Flexibility with In-House Design Studio

GFO Home offers structural customization options that most production builders don't — buyers can modify floor plans beyond standard finish selections. The in-house design center is located at 2801 E. State Hwy 121 in Lewisville, TX. Virtual design consultations are available for relocating families who can't easily visit in person. This level of personalization is a meaningful differentiator for families with specific layout priorities.

GFO Home Warranty — Meets Minimums, Structural Falls Short

GFO Home provides a Texas-compliant warranty on workmanship (1 year) and systems (2 years), meeting the legal minimums for both. The structural warranty at 6 years is the significant exception: most major DFW builders offer 10-year structural coverage. For families planning to own their GFO Home long-term, this is a material difference worth understanding before signing.

Structural warranty is 4 years below the DFW industry standard

Most major DFW builders — Highland Homes, Meritage, David Weekley, Perry Homes, Toll Brothers — provide 10-year structural warranties. GFO Home's 6-year coverage is legally compliant but meaningfully below what the market offers at comparable price points. If you're planning to stay in your home through year 7–10 (when foundation and structural issues are most likely to surface in North Texas clay soils), this gap matters. Discuss it explicitly with GFO Home before signing, and consider a third-party home warranty product to supplement coverage in years 7–10.

How GFO Home Handles Warranty Repairs

GFO Home manages warranty claims through their in-house service team. Submit warranty requests through the GFO Home customer portal or by contacting your community's customer care contact directly. The builder conducts a standard 11-month walkthrough to address workmanship issues before the 1-year warranty expires — schedule this proactively and document every issue in writing with photos. Based on BBB complaint data (4 complaints in 3 years, themes including warranty response delays and HVAC issues post-close), it is important to keep a written record of all communications and request specific response timelines in writing.

What the warranty doesn't cover

Normal maintenance items (caulking shrinkage, nail pops, minor settlement cracking consistent with North Texas soils), appliance warranties (covered separately by manufacturer), any modifications or repairs performed by the homeowner, and cosmetic issues that don't affect structural integrity or system function. GFO Home's HVAC complaint history suggests buyers should pay particular attention to HVAC performance during the systems warranty period (year 1–2) and document any issues immediately.

GFO Home Floor Plans & Design Process

GFO Home offers multiple floor plan series across their DFW communities, with plans generally ranging from approximately 1,800 to 4,500+ square feet and 3–6 bedroom configurations. The semi-custom positioning means buyers have access to structural modifications — not just finish selections — giving more flexibility than standard production-builder plans.

Semi-Custom Flexibility

GFO Home's semi-custom model allows structural changes such as extended living areas, added bedrooms, media rooms, and modified room configurations — options that most production builders don't offer at this price point. The degree of structural flexibility varies by plan and by how early in the construction phase you are at contract signing. Ask specifically about the cut-off date for structural changes when reviewing your contract, and get all approved modifications documented in writing before build begins.

Design Center

GFO Home's in-house design center is located at 2801 E. State Hwy 121, Lewisville, TX. Virtual design consultations are available — a meaningful benefit for families relocating from outside Texas who can't easily make in-person visits. Buyers work with a GFO Home design consultant to select interior finishes, cabinetry, flooring, countertop packages, fixtures, and exterior options. Allow 1–2 full design appointments before your cut-off date.

Standard features included at base price

GFO Home includes features as standard that some production builders charge for — engineered hardwood options, quartz countertop packages, and energy-efficient construction standards are typically included or available at low upgrade cost. Verify the current standard-vs.-upgrade list for your specific plan at contract signing, and compare it against competing builders' base specs before assuming any feature is "standard." What's standard changes by community and promotion period.

GFO Home DFW — Frequently Asked Questions

Is GFO Home a good builder?

GFO Home scores a 75/B+ on our 100-point builder index — a decent score that reflects solid Google reviews for a newer DFW builder, good suburb placements, and acceptable warranty coverage. The score is significantly pulled down by the C− BBB rating and the below-standard structural warranty (6 years vs. 10-year offered by most major DFW builders). GFO Home is a legitimate option for families seeking semi-custom flexibility at production builder prices, but it's a builder where having experienced buyer representation is especially important — not just nice to have.

How much do GFO Home homes cost in DFW?

GFO Home's DFW price range is approximately $400s to $700s+ depending on the suburb and community. Mansfield and Forney communities typically start in the $400s. Fort Worth–Aledo area communities (Eagle's Estates, Sweetgrass, Walsh corridor) start in the $500s. Celina communities including Highland Crossing start in the $700s. Prices change based on lot premiums, structural options, and design center selections — the base price is the starting point, not the final number. Budget a realistic 15–25% above base for options and upgrades.

Where does GFO Home build in DFW?

As of 2026, GFO Home has 8 active communities across 6 DFW suburbs: Mansfield, Celina, Forney, Royse City, Haslet, and Fort Worth (Aledo area). Their primary DFW concentration is in Mansfield (southwest), Celina (north Collin County), and the Fort Worth–Aledo corridor (west). Verify current community availability directly — communities sell out and new ones open on an ongoing basis. Browse current listings at Kristen's ShowingNew portal or at gfohome.com/division/dallas.

What is GFO Home's warranty?

GFO Home provides a 1-year workmanship warranty, 2-year systems warranty (HVAC, plumbing, electrical), and 6-year structural warranty. The workmanship and systems coverage meets Texas legal minimums. The structural warranty at 6 years falls short of the 10-year structural coverage that most major DFW builders offer. For families planning to own their home long-term, this is a meaningful gap — North Texas expansive clay soils put structural systems under ongoing stress. Consider a third-party home warranty product to supplement coverage in years 7–10, and have that conversation with GFO Home before signing your contract.

Do I need a Realtor to buy a GFO Home new construction?

You don't need one, but you should have one — and it costs you nothing extra. GFO Home pays the buyer's agent commission; the builder price is the same whether you bring a Realtor or not. GFO Home's sales agent works for GFO Home, not for you. A buyer's agent reviews the contract, explains upgrade pricing relative to market value, advises on lot selection, recommends independent inspectors at construction milestones, and represents your interests at the pre-closing walkthrough. Given GFO Home's BBB history and below-standard structural warranty, having independent representation is particularly important. If you visit a GFO Home model home without registering a Realtor on your first visit, you may permanently lose the ability to have that Realtor represent you for that community.

How long does GFO Home take to build a house in DFW?

GFO Home's typical build timeline in DFW ranges from 6–10 months for more standard configurations. Semi-custom structural modifications can extend the timeline to 9–12 months or longer. Actual timelines vary by community, current labor and materials conditions, and the complexity of your selections. Ask the GFO Home sales team for a community-specific build timeline at contract signing, and request milestone dates (framing, rough-in, drywall, pre-closing walkthrough) in writing so you can schedule independent inspections at each stage.

Is GFO Home related to Gehan Homes or Brightland Homes?

No — GFO Home and Gehan Homes/Brightland Homes are completely separate companies with separate BBB profiles, warranties, ownership structures, and track records. GFO Home LLC was founded in 2018 by Glenn Gehan, whose family has multi-generational homebuilding history in Texas. Gehan Homes (a separate company founded in 1994) rebranded to Brightland Homes in 2023. The two companies share no ownership or operations. This distinction matters practically: if you've researched "Gehan Homes" or "Brightland Homes" reviews and ratings, that information does not apply to GFO Home. Treat GFO Home as an independent builder on its own merits.

What does GFO Home's BBB C− rating mean for buyers?

GFO Home LLC holds a C− rating with the Better Business Bureau — the company is not accredited. The BBB profile shows 4 complaints in 3 years, with complaint themes including warranty response delays, warranty claim denials, HVAC issues post-close, and poor communication. One complaint received no response from the builder. A C− rating is not automatically disqualifying, but it warrants due diligence: research reviews on Google and community-specific forums, speak with residents in current GFO Home communities before you buy, and use an independent buyer's agent who can help you ask the right questions and review your contract carefully. Document all builder communications in writing throughout the process.
