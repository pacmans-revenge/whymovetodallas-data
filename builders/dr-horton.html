Who Is D.R. Horton? Score 84 A

D.R. Horton was founded in November 1978 by Donald R. Horton in Fort Worth, Texas. Starting with a single spec home in a Fort Worth subdivision, Horton built the company into the nation's largest homebuilder by volume — a position it has held every year since 2002. The company went public in 1992, completed more than 14 acquisitions between 1994 and 1999, and has now built over one million homes across the United States. Founder Donald R. Horton passed away in May 2024 at age 74; the company's operations continue under professional leadership at its Arlington, Texas headquarters.

In the Dallas–Fort Worth market, D.R. Horton operates across multiple product lines: the Express Series targets first-time buyers in the sub-$350k range, the core D.R. Horton brand serves move-up buyers in the $350k–$500k range, and the Emerald Series offers elevated floor plans and finishes for buyers above $500k. This product ladder makes D.R. Horton one of the only national builders with inventory in DFW's highest-growth affordability corridors — Celina, Anna, Princeton, Fate, and Aubrey — where land costs are lower and lot sizes can still accommodate four-bedroom plans at accessible price points.

What Sets D.R. Horton Apart

D.R. Horton's national scale creates tangible purchasing advantages at the community level — and the multi-brand product ladder means buyers at nearly any budget can find a community that fits their school zone and commute requirements.

National buying power = lower base prices in competitive suburbs

As the highest-volume builder in the U.S., D.R. Horton negotiates material and trade costs that most regional builders cannot match. That purchasing power flows through to base prices: in North Collin and Denton County growth corridors where land is still affordable, D.R. Horton consistently offers the lowest entry price among the national builders. For a family moving from California or Illinois where $350,000 feels like a budget constraint, that starting point opens suburbs — and school districts — that other builders price out of reach.

Three product lines serving different budgets in the same suburb

The Express Series, the core D.R. Horton line, and the Emerald Series are three distinct products — different floor plans, finish packages, and price points — that often coexist within the same master-planned community or adjacent neighborhoods. A relocating family who starts at Express and grows into D.R. Horton's move-up plans can stay within the same school attendance zone. This matters specifically in suburbs like Celina (Prosper ISD) and Anna (Anna ISD), where the grade of school district matters more than the specific community name.

Spec inventory for buyers on tight relocation timelines

D.R. Horton builds a significant percentage of its communities as spec homes — completed or near-complete homes that can close in 30–60 days. For corporate transferees with a six-week start date or military families with PCS-style urgency, that quick-close inventory is often the deciding factor. The tradeoff is limited customization: a spec home's selections are already fixed, so buyers choose from what's available rather than building from scratch.

D.R. Horton Warranty — Meets Texas Legal Minimum

Texas law (the Residential Construction Liability Act) sets the floor for new home warranty coverage: 1 year on workmanship, 2 years on systems, 10 years on structural. D.R. Horton's published warranty matches that floor exactly — no extended workmanship coverage, no additional systems provisions beyond what the law requires. That is a meaningful distinction from builders like Perry Homes (2-year workmanship) or Highland Homes (2-year workmanship), both of which exceed the legal minimum. For a production builder at this price point, a 1-2-10 structure is common — but families should understand what they are getting before they sign.

Structural 10 years 10 years ✓ Foundation and load-bearing structural elements — failures that affect the structural integrity of the home

How D.R. Horton Handles Warranty Repairs

D.R. Horton uses an in-house warranty team accessed through their online customer portal at drhorton.com/resources/warranty. Homeowners submit warranty requests through the portal; a D.R. Horton representative reviews and schedules repairs. The warranty clock starts on the closing date, not the move-in date — if there is any gap between closing and when you take possession, that time counts against your warranty period. Multiple reviews note that initial warranty response times vary significantly by community and division; the first 90 days after move-in are the best window to document and report issues before the 1-year workmanship period runs down.

What the warranty doesn't cover

Normal settling (hairline drywall cracks, minor nail pops) is excluded from workmanship coverage unless it exceeds published tolerances. Cosmetic issues that were visible at the pre-closing walk-through but not documented are generally excluded after closing — always get issues in writing at the walkthrough. HVAC maintenance, filters, and consumables are owner responsibility from day one. Landscaping and exterior grading is excluded. Appliances (typically Whirlpool) carry manufacturer warranties directly, not the builder warranty. Any modifications made by the homeowner after closing void coverage for the affected areas.

Honest Assessment — What Families Love and What to Know

D.R. Horton scores a 84 (A) on our 100-point builder index. The score reflects a strong BBB grade and broad DFW coverage — tempered by warranty coverage that only meets the legal minimum and a documented pattern of entry-level finish quality. Here's what drives that score, and where relocating families should pay close attention.

Strengths

  • Most accessible entry price in DFW's growth corridors — D.R. Horton consistently offers the lowest base prices among national builders in North Collin and Denton County suburbs. In Celina, Anna, and Princeton, Express Series homes start in the upper-$200k to low-$300k range — the only national production builder with inventory at that price point in those school zones.
  • Widest suburban footprint in DFW — With 60+ active communities, D.R. Horton builds in more DFW zip codes than any other national builder. For a relocating family unsure which suburb fits their commute and school preference, D.R. Horton likely has inventory in several shortlist options simultaneously.
  • Spec inventory for fast-close timelines — D.R. Horton builds a significant share of its communities as standing inventory. For corporate transferees with a defined start date, spec homes that can close in 30–60 days are a genuine differentiator that few regional builders can match at scale.
  • A+ BBB grade despite high complaint volume — 788 complaints in three years reflects national scale, not proportionally higher complaint rates. D.R. Horton has maintained an A+ BBB grade, meaning the vast majority of complaints were resolved to the complainant's satisfaction.
  • DHI Mortgage can streamline new-construction financing — D.R. Horton's in-house lender (DHI Mortgage) is experienced with new-construction timelines, construction-to-permanent loan structures, and builder-specific contract terms. When the rate and fees are genuinely competitive, it can simplify the process — though buyers should always get a competing quote first.

Things to Know

  • 1-year workmanship warranty = Texas legal minimum only — D.R. Horton does not extend beyond the state-required floor. Regional builders like Perry Homes and Highland Homes offer 2-year workmanship coverage. If finish quality is a priority and you are comparing communities between builders, this difference matters — especially in a Texas climate that stresses caulking, flashing, and HVAC systems hard in the first two years.
  • Entry-level finishes at the base price — Multiple verified buyer accounts document production-grade cabinets, fixtures, and flooring as standard inclusions on Express and lower-tier D.R. Horton plans. The materials are functional but reflect the price point — peeling countertops, cheap cabinet hardware, and base-grade carpet are consistently cited in BBB complaints. Upgrade packages exist and are meaningful for buyers who can budget for them.
  • HVAC sizing and moisture complaints are recurrent — Among 788 BBB complaints in the last three years, a documented pattern involves HVAC systems that run continuously in Texas summer heat without effectively controlling humidity, leading to condensation issues and, in some cases, mold. This appears most frequently in DFW communities built 2022–2024. Buyers should request a Manual J load calculation for their specific home and have the system independently verified before closing.
  • DHI Mortgage class action (2025 disclosure) — A class action lawsuit filed in 2025 alleged that DHI Mortgage systematically underestimated property tax figures when presenting monthly payment estimates to buyers, making payments appear lower than what buyers actually paid. No final judgment has been issued as of this writing. Buyers using DHI Mortgage should independently verify property tax estimates using the county appraisal district's taxable value for the property — not the builder's estimate.

D.R. Horton Incentives & Current Promotions

Incentives Change — Here's How to Find Them

D.R. Horton adjusts incentives community by community, often changing them at phase closings, quarter-ends, or when interest rate market conditions shift. No specific current promotions were published on D.R. Horton's official site at the time this page was researched — that does not mean incentives are unavailable, only that they are not publicly listed. The three categories below reflect the incentive types D.R. Horton has historically offered in DFW. Always verify current offers directly with the sales agent and through Kristen before your first visit.

Rate Buydowns

D.R. Horton has offered interest rate buydowns (temporary or permanent) through DHI Mortgage on select communities. Buydowns are typically tied to using DHI Mortgage as the lender. Verify the specific rate and points structure — not just the marketed monthly payment — before comparing to an independent lender offer.

Closing Cost Credits

Closing cost assistance (varying amounts by community and phase) has been offered, generally tied to DHI Mortgage use. A flex cash credit toward closing costs, options, or price reduction has also appeared on select inventory homes. Confirm the current amount and any lender requirement before signing.

Design Center & Upgrade Packages

On to-be-built contracts, D.R. Horton has offered design center credit packages (specific dollar amounts vary) that allow buyers to upgrade cabinets, countertops, or flooring above the base package. Spec homes come with pre-selected upgrades already included in the list price.

Important: Builder incentives are almost always tied to using their preferred lender. That's not inherently bad — sometimes the rate is genuinely competitive — but you should always get a competing quote from an independent lender first. A buyer's agent can help you compare the true cost of the deal across lenders.

D.R. Horton Floor Plans & Design Process

D.R. Horton offers dozens of floor plans across its three DFW product lines, ranging from approximately 1,200 sq ft starter plans in the Express Series to 3,500+ sq ft move-up plans in the core D.R. Horton line and Emerald Series. In DFW communities, most plans are 3–5 bedrooms with 2–4 bathrooms; the Express Series focuses on efficient single-story and two-story plans while the core brand and Emerald Series offer more bedroom flex rooms, larger primary suites, and open-concept family spaces. Specific floor plans vary by community — not every plan is available in every location.

Production Floor Plans — Limited Structural Changes

D.R. Horton is a production builder: floor plans are fixed and not structurally customizable after the slab is poured. On to-be-built homes, some communities offer structural option selections before concrete pour — bay windows, bedroom conversions, media room additions — but these are pre-defined options, not true custom modifications. What you see in the plan is what you get in terms of room layout. Interior selections (flooring, countertops, cabinets, fixtures) are made at the design center on to-be-built homes; spec homes come with pre-selected finishes already installed.

Design Center

D.R. Horton operates in-house design studios where buyers of to-be-built homes select interior finishes. Appointments are typically scheduled after contract signing. D.R. Horton does offer virtual design consultations for remote buyers — relevant for families relocating from out of state who cannot easily travel to DFW during the selection window. Design center selections are guided by a D.R. Horton design consultant; upgrades above the base package are priced separately and can add $20,000–$60,000+ to the total depending on the level of selections. Budget for upgrades separately from the base price when modeling your total cost.

Standard features included at base price

Base inclusions vary by series. In the Express Series, standard features typically include laminate countertops, base-grade carpet in bedrooms, vinyl or wood-look LVP in living areas, stainless steel appliances (Whirlpool brand), brushed nickel fixtures, and a covered patio on select plans. The core D.R. Horton line adds granite or quartz countertops in the kitchen and upgraded cabinet door styles as standard in many DFW communities. Smart home features (video doorbell, smart thermostat, keyless entry) are marketed as standard on many D.R. Horton plans — confirm what is actually included (vs. demonstrated in model homes) before closing. Always ask for the specific inclusion sheet for your contract community — the model home is furnished with upgrades that are not standard.

D.R. Horton DFW — Frequently Asked Questions

Is D.R. Horton a good builder?

D.R. Horton scores 84 out of 100 on our DFW builder index (Grade A), driven by an A+ BBB rating and the widest community footprint in the metro. For what it is — a high-volume production builder targeting the entry and move-up segments — D.R. Horton delivers consistent value at an accessible price. The relevant question for a relocating family isn't whether D.R. Horton is "good" in the abstract, but whether it's the right match for your expectations. If you're looking for the most affordable path into a specific DFW suburb with a strong school district, D.R. Horton often has the most inventory at the lowest base prices. If you're expecting semi-custom finishes, extended warranty coverage, or lower complaint rates per home built, regional builders like Perry Homes, Highland Homes, or David Weekley are worth comparing side by side.

How much do D.R. Horton homes cost in DFW?

In the Dallas–Fort Worth market, D.R. Horton homes range from roughly $250,000 to $500,000 depending on the product line, community, and suburb. The Express Series — aimed at first-time buyers — typically starts in the upper-$200k to low-$300k range in North Collin County growth suburbs like Anna, Princeton, and Aubrey. The core D.R. Horton brand runs $300k–$500k in similar suburbs, and higher in more established or expensive communities. The Emerald Series (elevated finishes and plans) exceeds $500k in select DFW locations. These ranges shift with market conditions; always verify current base prices directly with the sales agent or at drhorton.com. Budget separately for design center upgrades, lot premiums, and closing costs — the base price is a floor, not the total cost.

Where does D.R. Horton build in DFW?

D.R. Horton has the broadest geographic footprint of any national builder in DFW, with approximately 60+ active communities across the metro as of mid-2026. Their concentration is heaviest in North Collin County (Celina, Anna, Princeton), Denton County growth corridors (Aubrey, Little Elm), East DFW (Fate, Royse City, Forney, Mesquite), and Fort Worth's northwest and southwest growth areas. They are generally not present in established, land-constrained suburbs like Coppell, Southlake, Colleyville, or Grapevine — these markets have little available land for production building. For the most current community list, visit drhorton.com/texas/dallas-fort-worth, as communities open and close as lots sell out.

What is D.R. Horton's warranty?

D.R. Horton provides a 1-year workmanship warranty, 2-year systems warranty (HVAC, plumbing, electrical), and 10-year structural warranty — exactly matching Texas law's minimum requirements. The warranty is administered through D.R. Horton's in-house team via their online portal at drhorton.com/resources/warranty. Important timing note: the warranty clock starts on your closing date, not your move-in date. If you close but don't take possession for several weeks, that time still counts against the 1-year workmanship period. Document all visible issues at your pre-closing walkthrough in writing, and submit any warranty requests early — don't wait until month 11 to report issues that have been present since move-in. For comparison, builders like Perry Homes and Highland Homes offer 2-year workmanship coverage; D.R. Horton does not exceed the state floor.

Do I need a Realtor to buy a D.R. Horton new construction?

You don't need one, but you should have one — and it costs you nothing extra. D.R. Horton pays the buyer's agent commission; the builder price is the same whether you bring a Realtor or not. D.R. Horton's sales agent works for D.R. Horton, not for you. A buyer's agent reviews the contract, explains upgrade pricing relative to market value, advises on lot selection, recommends independent inspectors (D.R. Horton does not require them but buyers absolutely should get one), and represents your interests at the pre-closing walkthrough. If you visit a D.R. How long does D.R. Horton take to build a house in DFW?

D.R. Horton's typical DFW build time for a to-be-built home is 5–7 months from contract to close, though this varies by community, current trade availability, and how early in a community's phase you are building. Buyers who need a faster close should ask about spec homes — standing inventory that is already complete or near-complete can close in 30–60 days. If you have a hard move date (corporate relocation, lease end, school year start), spec inventory is often the more reliable option than a to-be-built contract. Confirm the estimated completion window in writing at contract — builder timelines can shift due to trade schedules and permit delays, and having a documented estimate matters if you're coordinating lease breaks or movers.

What is the difference between D.R. Horton, Express Homes, and Emerald Homes?

All three are D.R. Horton brands — different marketing names for different product tiers from the same parent company. Express Homes is the entry-level line: smaller plans, more production-grade base finishes, lower base prices. It targets first-time buyers and is most common in affordability-focused suburbs like Princeton, Anna, and Aubrey. The core D.R. Horton brand is the move-up line: larger plans, more standard features, mid-range price points. Emerald Homes is the elevated line: upgraded standard finishes, more architectural detail, larger plans with more flex space — aimed at buyers upgrading from a previous home rather than buying for the first time. In DFW, you may find different D.R. Horton brands in different communities within the same suburb. When comparing, confirm which product line a specific community falls under, as the base inclusions differ meaningfully.

Should I be concerned about the DHI Mortgage class action?

The 2025 class action alleges that DHI Mortgage (D.R. Horton's in-house lender) systematically presented monthly payment estimates that used underestimated property tax figures, making homes appear more affordable on a monthly basis than they actually were after taxes were properly accounted for. No final court judgment has been issued as of this writing. The practical step for any D.R. Horton buyer using DHI Mortgage is straightforward: independently look up the county appraisal district's most recent assessed value for the specific property (not the purchase price) and calculate your expected property tax bill using the current tax rate for that county and school district. Do not rely solely on the estimate DHI Mortgage provides. Kristen can walk you through this calculation for any DFW community during your consultation.
