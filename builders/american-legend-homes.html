Who Is American Legend Homes? Score 84 A

American Legend Homes was co-founded in 2003 by Chris and Clay Bright, sons of H.R. "Bum" Bright — the late Dallas businessman and former owner of the Dallas Cowboys. The company operates under Bright Industries, a Dallas-area family conglomerate with roots in real estate development, commercial property, oil and gas, and golf operations stretching back to the 1950s. The Bright family's direct personal involvement from day one shaped American Legend's culture around responsive service and attentive warranty follow-up — a meaningful distinction from national production builders where leadership is several organizational layers removed from the job site. The first American Legend home was completed in 2004 in Jackson Ranch, a community near Lake Dallas.

Today, American Legend builds approximately 800 homes per year across the Dallas–Fort Worth market and a Colorado division. Their DFW operations are led by CEO Kevin Egan and President & COO Paul Miller. The builder concentrates its DFW footprint in the high-growth north Collin County corridor — primarily Celina and Prosper — plus communities in Midlothian, Denton, and Lewisville. Rather than spreading thin across every DFW suburb, American Legend focuses on markets where they maintain build consistency and community-level sales relationships. For families specifically targeting Celina ISD or Prosper ISD, they are one of the most active builders in those markets.

What Sets American Legend Apart

American Legend occupies a specific niche in the DFW new construction market: more personalization than national volume builders, without the price premium of fully custom construction. Four things distinguish them in practice for relocating families.

Family & Employee Ownership — Local Accountability That Shows

American Legend has been family and employee-owned since 2003, with no private equity or national conglomerate ownership. Decisions about your community, your warranty claim, and your build timeline are made by people with their names on the building who live in the same market. The Bright family's founder-to-present involvement created a company culture that national production builders — where decisions run through corporate layers in another state — cannot replicate.

Semi-Custom Structural Flexibility Before the Slab

Unlike production builders who sell a fixed plan, American Legend allows structural modifications before the foundation is poured: media rooms, extra bedrooms, bay windows, extended covered patios, and garage size options. Finish selections — countertops, flooring, cabinetry, appliances — happen later at the design center. This two-phase approach gives buyers meaningful customization control that goes beyond paint and tile choices, at move-up builder price points.

In-House Design Center with Experienced Consultants

American Legend operates its own DFW design center with in-house staff — not an outsourced vendor. Design consultants guide buyers through countertops, flooring, cabinetry, backsplashes, and appliances with the current selection and budget context, not a hard-sell on the most expensive tier. For families relocating from out of state, virtual design center appointments are available, making the process accessible without requiring a mid-build trip to Lewisville.

7-Business-Day Warranty Response Target — Published, Not Marketing Language

American Legend's published goal is to resolve all warranty service requests within 7 business days. This is a specific, measurable commitment stated on their warranty page — not a vague "we care about our customers" claim. It sets a clear service expectation and gives buyers a documented baseline when evaluating post-close support. Kristen's advice: get this in writing as part of your closing packet and submit any warranty items in writing so you have a timestamp trail.

American Legend Homes Warranty — Texas Standard 1-2-10 Coverage

Texas law (the Residential Construction Liability Act, or RCLA) requires all homebuilders to provide at minimum 1 year of workmanship coverage, 2 years for mechanical systems, and 10 years for structural defects. American Legend's published warranty meets these statutory floors — 1-year workmanship, 2-year systems, 10-year structural. This is the same coverage as most national production builders, but below regional competitors like Highland Homes, which offers 2-year workmanship coverage. The builder's stated target is to resolve all warranty service requests within 7 business days — a specific, published commitment documented at amlegendhomes.com/texas-warranty.

How American Legend Handles Warranty Repairs

American Legend handles warranty claims in-house with a stated 7-business-day resolution target. Buyers submit warranty requests through the builder's warranty portal. The in-house team — rather than a third-party outsourced service — means the people responding to your request have direct knowledge of American Legend's construction methods and subcontractor relationships. Based on recent BBB complaint records, some buyers have experienced delays beyond the 7-day target for drainage-related issues that require site evaluation before a resolution plan can be approved. Kristen recommends submitting all warranty requests in writing through the portal and following up with a timestamped email if you don't receive a response acknowledgment within 3 business days.

What the warranty doesn't cover

Standard exclusions apply: normal settling (hairline drywall cracks), cosmetic wear over time, damage caused by the homeowner or third-party contractors, and items covered under appliance manufacturer warranties rather than the builder's warranty. Landscaping and irrigation are generally not covered after the first year. Drainage issues that arise from lot grading relationships between adjacent properties are the most common source of disputes — if you're considering a lot in an active community where neighboring lots are still being developed, Kristen recommends visiting the lot after a rainstorm before signing to assess natural drainage patterns. Review the full warranty document at amlegendhomes.com/texas-warranty before closing.

American Legend Homes Incentives & Current Promotions

Incentives Change — Here's How to Find Them

American Legend Homes typically offers closing cost assistance and rate buydown programs tied to their preferred lenders — Academy Mortgage and Supreme Lending (the Rasmusson team). Specific terms change by community, lot phase, and market conditions. Deals are most aggressive at end-of-phase closeouts and during slower sales months, and can disappear quickly when a community is selling actively. No specific current promotions were listed on the builder's public promotions page at the time this page was last updated — always verify directly with the community sales team or through Kristen, who checks in regularly.

Rate Buydowns

Rate buydown programs through Academy Mortgage or Supreme Lending that reduce your interest rate temporarily (2-1 buydown) or permanently. The savings can be substantial — but only if the offered rate is genuinely competitive. Always get a quote from an independent lender before accepting a builder's rate.

Closing Cost Credits

American Legend has historically offered closing cost contributions when buyers use their preferred lender. Dollar amounts and terms vary by community and phase — never assume the same deal applies across different American Legend communities or price points.

Upgrade Packages

Design center credits or upgrade allowances are occasionally offered to move spec homes approaching completion. Less common on to-be-built contracts, more common on homes that are 60–90 days from completion without a buyer signed.

Important: Builder incentives are almost always tied to using their preferred lender. That's not inherently bad — sometimes the rate is genuinely competitive — but you should always get a competing quote from an independent lender first. A buyer's agent can help you compare the true cost of the deal across lenders.

American Legend Homes Floor Plans & Design Process

American Legend offers dozens of floor plans across their DFW communities, ranging from approximately 1,600 square feet in the Midlothian Bridgewater 40s series to 4,350+ square feet in the Prosper Star Trail 65s. Plans are organized by community "series" — 40s, 50s, 55s, 60s, 65s — indicating the minimum lot width in feet. A 40s plan is designed for a narrower lot with a more efficient footprint; a 65s plan offers more elbow room and broader plan options, including the builder's largest and most customizable designs. Beds range from 3 to 5, and bathrooms from 2 to 5 across the full portfolio.

Semi-Custom Structural Options

Before the foundation is poured, buyers select from structural options that permanently change the home's footprint: media rooms, additional bedrooms, bay windows, extended covered patios, and garage configurations (2-car vs. 3-car). Once construction begins, the plan is locked. Finish selections — countertops, flooring, cabinetry, fixtures, appliances — are made at the design center after structural options are finalized. This two-phase approach gives buyers meaningful customization early in the process, but it requires buyers to be ready to make structural decisions quickly after contract signing, often within the first few weeks. American Legend's sales teams walk buyers through the available options community by community at the model home.

Design Center

American Legend operates its own DFW design center with in-house design consultants — not an outsourced third-party vendor. Consultants guide buyers through selections that fit their aesthetic preferences and their budget, with access to the current style palette across countertops, appliances, flooring, backsplashes, and custom cabinetry. For families purchasing from out of state, virtual design center appointments are available, which makes American Legend more accessible for relocating buyers who can't make multiple trips to Lewisville during the build process.

Standard features included at base price

Standard features vary by community and plan series. Higher-series plans (60s, 65s) typically carry elevated standard specifications compared to entry 40s plans. Common inclusions across American Legend communities include hardwood-style flooring in main living areas, granite or quartz countertops in the kitchen, stainless steel appliances, covered back patio, and energy-efficient construction practices. Specific standard feature sheets are available at each community's model home — Kristen recommends requesting the full feature sheet in writing before your design center appointment so you know exactly what's included at base price vs. what costs extra.

How much do American Legend Homes homes cost in DFW?

Where does American Legend Homes build in DFW?

As of May 2026, American Legend Homes has 15 active builder communities across 5 DFW suburbs: Celina (6 communities), Prosper (5 communities), Midlothian (2 communities), Denton (1 community), and Lewisville (1 townhome community at Castle Hills Northpointe). What is American Legend Homes' warranty?

American Legend Homes offers a standard 1-2-10 Texas warranty: 1 year of workmanship coverage (visible defects in paint, trim, tile, flooring installation, window seals, and caulking), 2 years of mechanical systems coverage (HVAC ductwork, plumbing distribution lines, electrical wiring), and 10 years of structural coverage (foundation and load-bearing elements). Their stated service goal is to resolve all warranty requests within 7 business days. The full warranty document is available at amlegendhomes.com/texas-warranty. Note that the 1-year workmanship period is the Texas legal minimum — if extended workmanship coverage is a key factor, compare American Legend directly against Highland Homes (2-year workmanship) or Drees before making a decision.

Do I need a Realtor to buy an American Legend Homes new construction?

You don't need one, but you should have one — and it costs you nothing extra. American Legend pays the buyer's agent commission; the builder price is the same whether you bring a Realtor or not. The builder's sales agent works for American Legend, not for you. A buyer's agent reviews the contract, explains upgrade pricing relative to market value, advises on lot selection, recommends independent inspectors, and represents your interests at the pre-closing walkthrough. If you visit a model home without registering a Realtor on your first visit, you may permanently lose the ability to have that Realtor represent you for that community — so register before you go, not after.

How long does American Legend Homes take to build a house in DFW?

American Legend uses a 10-stage construction process and states a goal of breaking ground within 60 days of contract signing for to-be-built homes. Based on their staged process and typical regional builder timelines in the DFW market, expect approximately 6–10 months from contract to closing for a to-be-built home. If you're purchasing a spec home — one already under construction or complete — timelines are shorter, sometimes 30–90 days depending on the home's completion stage. American Legend lists available move-in-ready and quick-move-in homes on their website and through the ShowingNew portal. Timeline reliability in DFW has generally improved from the 2021–2022 supply chain period, but confirm the current estimate directly with the community sales team for your specific plan and lot.

Yes — American Legend operates model homes in most of their active DFW communities. Primary DFW communities with active model homes include Celina (North Sky, Ramble, Ten Mile Creek, Mustang Lakes Villas), Prosper (Mosaic, Star Trail), and Midlothian (Bridgewater). Hours are typically Monday–Saturday 10am–6pm and Sunday 12pm–6pm, though these vary by location — confirm directly with the community sales team before you make the drive.
