Who Is Southgate Homes? Score 98 A+

Southgate Homes is a Dallas–Fort Worth luxury homebuilder and a wholly owned subsidiary of Green Brick Partners, Inc. (NYSE: GRBK), the third-largest homebuilder in the DFW metro. Southgate operates exclusively in North Collin County's most prestigious master-planned communities — Windsong Ranch in Prosper, Painted Tree in McKinney, The Reserve at Watters in Allen, and Prosper Ridge — delivering production luxury homes in the $724K–$1.4M range with architectural detail and standard finishes that most builders price as premium upgrades. The company has earned a spot on D Home magazine's Best Builders in Dallas list eight consecutive years running, the longest active streak of any builder in the DFW luxury production segment.

Southgate is headquartered at 2805 Dallas N. Pkwy, Suite 400, Plano, TX 75093. Its parent company Green Brick Partners also owns Normandy Homes, Trophy Signature Homes, CB JENI Homes, and Centre Living Homes in Texas, giving Southgate access to shared land pipelines, preferred vendor networks, and the financial stability of a publicly traded enterprise without losing the focused attention that defines a design-led builder with roughly 39 employees. That small team relative to home volume means construction management is closer to the build — and to buyers — than you'd find at a national volume operation.

What Sets Southgate Apart

In a market where most builders offer entry-level specs and charge heavily for upgrades, Southgate's point of difference is that their base homes already arrive at a standard most families would describe as "move-in ready" without a design center spending spree.

Luxury-Level Standard Finishes

Southgate's base spec includes architectural elements other builders charge thousands for as upgrades: full-height cabinetry stacks, 8-foot interior doors, designer-level countertops, and masonry exteriors. Independent reviews consistently note that buyers needed fewer design center add-ons to reach a finished look that felt authentic to the price point — an important distinction when build budgets are already stretched at $724K and above. (Note: confirm current included features for your specific plan and community with the sales team before signing.)

Exclusive Master-Planned Community Access

Southgate doesn't build on scattered lots. Every active community — Windsong Ranch, Painted Tree, Reserve at Watters, Prosper Ridge — sits inside established master-planned developments with amenity centers, walking trails, resort pools, and access to A-rated school zones (Prosper ISD, McKinney ISD, Allen ISD). These communities have decades of infrastructure built around them and proven resale histories. Families who buy with Southgate are also buying into a specific community ecosystem, not just a home.

Green Brick Financial Transparency

As a subsidiary of Green Brick Partners (NYSE: GRBK), Southgate's parent company reports publicly traded financial data each quarter. Buyers signing 10–14 month build contracts can review GRBK's balance sheet, debt levels, and community pipeline before committing — a level of financial transparency that private regional builders can't offer. Green Brick's Texas portfolio also means land development expertise and vendor relationships that support consistent material supply across communities.

Southgate Homes Warranty — Meets Texas Minimum, Third-Party Administered

Texas law (the Residential Construction Liability Act) requires builders to provide at minimum: 1 year of workmanship coverage, 2 years of mechanical systems coverage, and 10 years of structural coverage. Southgate meets these minimums — but doesn't exceed the workmanship coverage period. What distinguishes their approach is who administers the claims: Southgate uses StrucSure Home Warranty, a third-party warranty administrator, which creates an independent record of every claim filed. That paper trail benefits buyers if a dispute ever escalates beyond the builder's internal team.

How Southgate Handles Warranty Repairs

Warranty requests are submitted through Southgate's online portal at southgatehomes.com/warranty-request/. Using a third-party administrator (StrucSure Home Warranty) means your claim goes on record outside of Southgate's internal tracking — which is an important distinction if you ever need to escalate or document recurring issues. The one BBB complaint on record in the past three years involved a structural warranty dispute, suggesting that complex claims (as opposed to routine workmanship items) can require persistence. Buyers should document every warranty submission in writing and retain copies.

What the warranty doesn't cover

Normal settlement cracks (hairline cracks in drywall or tile grout), cosmetic issues identified after closing but not noted at the walkthrough, damage caused by homeowner modifications or improper maintenance, landscaping and irrigation issues, and appliances covered under their own manufacturer warranties. Mechanical system components (HVAC equipment itself, plumbing fixtures, water heaters) are typically covered by equipment manufacturer warranties rather than the builder's systems coverage. At the $724K–$1.4M price point, note that a 1-year workmanship warranty meets — but does not exceed — Texas's minimum. Some competitors offer 2-year workmanship coverage at lower price points.

Honest Assessment — What Families Love and What to Know

Southgate Homes scores a 98 (A+) on our 100-point builder index. Here's what drives that score — and where relocating families should pay attention before signing.

Strengths

  • Top BBB standing — A+ accredited since 2013 with one complaint in the past three years (resolved). At this price point, that complaint-to-volume ratio is strong.
  • Eight consecutive D Home Best Builder awards — This is third-party recognition from a credible Dallas publication, not self-reported marketing. No other DFW luxury production builder has maintained this streak.
  • Exclusive, high-amenity communities — Windsong Ranch, Painted Tree, Reserve at Watters, and Prosper Ridge all sit inside master-planned communities with established amenity packages and top-rated school zone access. Buying with Southgate means buying into a proven community infrastructure.
  • Green Brick financial visibility — GRBK is NYSE-listed; buyers can review the parent company's financial health before signing a 10–14 month build contract. That's not possible with private regional builders.
  • Focused North Collin County expertise — Southgate's 3-suburb concentration means their teams know these communities, lot characteristics, and school zone boundaries deeply. Buyers don't get assigned to a generalist sales team covering 20 different suburbs.

Things to Know

  • 1-year workmanship warranty only — At $724K–$1.4M, this is the one area where Southgate doesn't lead. Texas law requires 1 year minimum; several mid-tier builders offer 2 years. Worth comparing directly if warranty protection is a priority.
  • Incentives are lender-tied — Closing cost credits and rate promotions require using Green Brick Mortgage and Green Brick Title. Always get an independent rate quote before assuming the incentive compensates for a less competitive rate.
  • Build timelines can extend — Multiple buyer accounts report timelines running 2–3 months beyond initial estimates, particularly for plans with structural options or premium finish packages. Southgate is primarily build-to-order; not the right choice for families who need to close within 6 months.
  • Limited vendor flexibility during design — Some buyers have noted that not all initially described options remain available by the time their design appointment comes around, and that the approved vendor list is narrower than expected. Confirm finish availability in writing at contract, not at the design center.

Southgate Homes Incentives & Current Promotions

Incentives Change — Here's How to Find Them

Southgate runs time-limited promotions that change by community, phase close, and quarter. As of research date (May 27, 2026), one incentive was confirmed publicly active: an upgraded structural sliding patio door (value up to $20,000) for contracts signed by May 31, 2026. Earlier promotions — a rate buydown at 3.99% and closing cost credits — had expiry dates that have passed. Do not assume any specific offer is still active without confirming with the Southgate sales team or Kristen directly before visiting.

Rate Buydown

Southgate periodically offers below-market mortgage rates through Green Brick Mortgage, including 2-1 buydown structures. When active, these reduce your effective interest rate for the first two years of the loan. Must use Green Brick Mortgage to qualify. Rates and qualifying communities change quarterly — confirm at contract, not from this page.

Closing Cost Credits

Closing cost savings packages are offered on select homes when using both Green Brick Mortgage and Green Brick Title. Dollar amounts and qualifying communities vary by phase. Ask specifically what closing cost incentive is currently being offered — and what rate you'd be locked into to receive it — before writing an offer.

Structural Upgrades

Southgate has offered structural upgrade incentives — including an upgraded sliding door package valued up to $20,000 — for contracts signed by specific dates. These represent genuine value when the item would otherwise be a costly structural option. Confirm current offerings with the sales team; expiry dates are firm.

Important: Southgate's most valuable incentives are consistently tied to using Green Brick Mortgage and Green Brick Title. That's not inherently a problem — sometimes the rate is genuinely competitive — but you should always get a competing quote from an independent lender first. A buyer's agent can help you compare the true all-in cost of the deal across lenders before you commit.

Southgate Homes Floor Plans & Design Process

Southgate's current DFW plans range from approximately 2,928 to 4,547 sq ft across 3–6 bedrooms and 3–6 bathrooms, priced from $723,990 to $1,445,000. Plan series are labeled by lot width — the "50-Series" sits on 50-foot lots, the "60-Series" on 60-foot lots, and the "71-Series" on 71-foot lots — with wider lots accommodating larger footprints and more expressive architectural options. Each community carries its own set of plans; what's available in Windsong Ranch differs from Painted Tree and Reserve at Watters.

Production Floor Plans with Luxury-Level Selections

Southgate is a production builder, which means the floor plan configuration is set at contract. Structural changes — relocating a staircase, adding a bedroom, modifying a roofline — are generally not available after signing. What buyers customize is the finish level: countertop material and edge profile, cabinetry color and hardware, flooring species and stain, tile patterns, and fixture packages. What makes this different from typical production customization is that Southgate's standard-included finishes already sit at a level most builders call "semi-custom." In practice, many buyers find they're making taste-based choices rather than paying to upgrade from an objectively bare-bones base spec.

Design Center

Southgate operates an in-house design center where buyers make finish selections typically within the first 30–60 days of signing their contract. For families relocating from out of state, virtual appointment options are available for many decisions — a meaningful advantage when you're managing a long-distance move. That said, some buyers have noted that not every option discussed during the sales presentation remains available by their design appointment date. Confirm the availability of any finish or structural option in writing at the time of contract, not at the design center appointment.

Standard features included at base price

Based on Southgate's marketing materials and independent buyer accounts, their base homes include luxury-tier architectural finishes as standard: full-height cabinetry, elevated countertop levels, 8-foot interior doors, masonry exteriors, and designer interior detailing including wood beams in select plans. The company specifically markets that these are standard — not upgrade — inclusions. Verify the current standard feature sheet for your specific plan and community with the sales team, as included items can shift between plan generations and phase releases.

Southgate Homes DFW — Frequently Asked Questions

Is Southgate Homes a good builder?

Southgate Homes earns a 98/A+ on our 100-point DFW builder index — among the highest scores of any active builder in the metro. With an A+ BBB accreditation held since 2013, one complaint filed in the past three years (resolved), and eight consecutive D Home Best Builder awards, Southgate has a stronger documented reputation than most builders in the DFW luxury production segment. Buyer experiences do vary: some families report exceptional responsiveness from construction teams and weekly build updates; others note timeline extensions, limited vendor flexibility, and variability in warranty response speed. At $724K–$1.4M, buyers should approach this as luxury production — not fully custom — which means some constraints on structural changes and vendor selection are baked into the model. For relocating families buying in Windsong Ranch, Painted Tree, or Reserve at Watters, Southgate consistently earns its standing as one of the strongest options in the segment.

How much do Southgate Homes cost in DFW?

As of May 2026, Southgate's active DFW communities range from approximately $723,990 (Painted Tree 50-Series in McKinney) to $1,445,000 (Windsong Ranch 61-Series in Prosper). Pricing varies significantly by lot width, elevation, structural options, and community. The figures from Kristen's ShowingNew portal reflect currently available or under-construction homes — a new-contract base price for a given plan may differ from what active inventory is listed at. Always confirm current pricing with the community sales team, and understand that lot premiums (backing to green space, on a cul-de-sac, etc.) add to the base contract price.

Where does Southgate Homes build in DFW?

As of May 2026, Southgate is actively building in three North Collin County suburbs: McKinney (Painted Tree community, 50-Series and 60-Series plans), Prosper (Windsong Ranch 61-Series and 71-Series, plus Prosper Ridge), and Allen (The Reserve at Watters). All three suburbs are served by top-ranked ISDs — McKinney ISD, Prosper ISD, and Allen ISD respectively. Southgate does not currently build in Frisco, Plano, Coppell, Southlake, Flower Mound, or other established DFW suburbs. Families targeting those areas should look at other builders for their primary search.

What is Southgate Homes' warranty?

Southgate provides the Texas minimum warranty: 1 year for workmanship and materials, 2 years for mechanical systems (HVAC, plumbing, electrical distribution), and 10 years for structural defects. Warranty administration is handled through StrucSure Home Warranty, a third-party administrator. This means your claim is tracked independently of Southgate's internal systems — which creates a paper trail that benefits buyers if a dispute ever arises. Submit warranty requests through the online portal at southgatehomes.com/warranty-request/. One note worth flagging: the 1-year workmanship coverage meets but does not exceed Texas's minimum. At $724K–$1.4M, that's a data point worth comparing directly against builders who offer extended workmanship coverage.

Do I need a Realtor to buy a Southgate Homes new construction?

You don't need one, but you should have one — and it costs you nothing extra. Southgate pays the buyer's agent commission; the builder price is the same whether you bring a Realtor or not. Southgate's sales agent works for Southgate, not for you. A buyer's agent reviews the contract, explains upgrade pricing relative to market value, advises on lot selection, recommends independent inspectors, and represents your interests at the pre-closing walkthrough. If you visit a Southgate model home without registering a Realtor's name on your first visit, you may permanently lose the ability to have that Realtor represent you for that community — this is standard builder policy. Contact Kristen before your first visit to make sure her name is registered.

How long does Southgate Homes take to build a house in DFW?

Southgate builds primarily to order (build-to-suit), so timelines run from contract signing to close. Based on buyer accounts and typical luxury production timelines in North Collin County, expect 10–14 months for a standard plan. Wider-lot plans with more structural complexity or higher-end finish packages have been reported to run 12–16 months. Southgate is not the right builder for families who need to move in within 6–8 months unless a spec (quick-move-in) home happens to be available in the right community. For families relocating with a firm start-of-school deadline, ask Southgate specifically about QMI homes at time of inquiry — and get a realistic completion estimate in writing.

Does Southgate require me to use Green Brick Mortgage?

Southgate does not require you to use Green Brick Mortgage — but their incentives are tied to it. Closing cost credits and rate buydown promotions are typically only available to buyers who use both Green Brick Mortgage and Green Brick Title. This is common practice among builder-affiliated lenders. The right approach is to get a rate quote and loan estimate from an independent lender before evaluating any incentive package. Sometimes the total value of the incentive clearly outweighs using an outside lender; sometimes it doesn't. A buyer's agent can help you run the full cost comparison before you sign. Never assume the incentive is worth it without doing the math on the rate and fees side independently.
