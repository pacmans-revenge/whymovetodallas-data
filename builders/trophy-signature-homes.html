Who Is Trophy Signature Homes? Score — pending

Trophy Signature Homes was founded in 2018 in the Dallas–Fort Worth market with a clear mandate: deliver ENERGY STAR-certified homes with substantial included features at prices accessible to first-time and move-up buyers. The company operates as a wholly owned subsidiary of Green Brick Partners, Inc. (NYSE: GRBK), which is the third-largest homebuilder in DFW by land and lot control. That relationship gives Trophy the lot pipeline and capital backing of a major publicly traded company while maintaining a focused brand identity around value and energy efficiency.

In eight years, Trophy has grown to become the 7th largest homebuilder in the Dallas–Fort Worth area, with 25 active DFW communities across 17 cities as of May 2026. The company has since expanded to Austin and Houston, but DFW remains its core market. Trophy is a BBB-accredited builder (A+ rating, accredited since 2021), a Dallas Builders Association member, and an Operation Finally Home partner — building mortgage-free homes for wounded veterans and their families. The builder has been named a Top 100 Place to Work for three consecutive years (2022, 2023, 2024) and a Best Place to Work in 2025, with five communities ranked in RCLCO's Top-Selling Master-Planned Communities of 2025.

What Sets Trophy Apart

Trophy's stated philosophy is "more is always included" — and the spec sheet backs it up at the price point. Several features that DFW's volume builders offer as paid upgrades are standard in Trophy homes.

ENERGY STAR Efficiency Package Included at Base Price

Trophy bundles a meaningful energy-efficiency package into every home's base price. This includes: a minimum 15.2 SEER2 HVAC system, double-pane Low-E insulated windows, fresh air ventilation, smart thermostat, LED lighting throughout, low-flow commodes and faucets, and ENERGY STAR-certified appliances. Spray foam insulation and tankless water heaters are available in select communities and homes — not universally standard, but more accessible than most comparable builders at this price tier. Trophy is one of the first DFW builders to include this full lineup as baseline.

Green Brick Financial Ecosystem

As a Green Brick subsidiary, Trophy buyers have access to Green Brick Mortgage (in-house lender offering rate buydown incentives and a complimentary 3-year refinancing program), Green Brick Title (title insurance and closing services), and Green Brick Insurance (homeowners insurance quotes). The bundled convenience is real, though buyers should always compare Green Brick Mortgage's rates against an independent lender before committing — the incentive may or may not outweigh the benefit of a competing quote.

8 Named Plan Series Across the Full Price Range

Trophy organizes its floor plans into eight named series — Gem, Trophy, Texas Tree, Painter, Musician, Victory, Golf Course, and River — covering 1,232 to roughly 4,400 square feet and 3 to 5 bedrooms. This naming convention isn't just marketing: each series targets a distinct price tier and lot size, so a family's budget typically points to a specific series before they even pick a floor plan. DFW price range runs from the high $230s (Gem/Painter entry series in Farmersville and Alvarado) to the low $700s (Painted Tree master-planned community in McKinney).

Operation Finally Home Partner

Trophy is an active Operation Finally Home partner — a nonprofit that builds mortgage-free homes for wounded warriors, veterans with service-connected disabilities, and widows of fallen heroes. For relocating military families and veterans who have served, this is a meaningful signal of organizational values. Trophy also offers an Everyday Heroes program ($1,000 purchase credit for teachers, nurses, firefighters, law enforcement, and military personnel — confirm current program terms on their site).

Trophy Signature Homes Warranty — Meets Texas Legal Minimums

Trophy Signature Homes offers a standard 1-2-10 new home warranty — the same structure Texas law (RCLA) requires as a floor. Texas mandates: 1 year workmanship, 2 years mechanical/electrical/plumbing systems, and 10 years structural. Trophy meets all three tiers. The 10-year structural warranty is administered by 2-10 Home Buyers Warranty, an independent third-party provider specializing in long-term structural protection for new construction. Workmanship and systems claims go through Trophy's own warranty department via an online request form, with 3-6-9-12 month scheduled check-in visits.

How Trophy Handles Warranty Repairs

Trophy uses an online warranty request form at trophysignaturehomes.com/warranty-request. The company schedules 3-, 6-, 9-, and 12-month check-in visits during the first year to review and address items. Warranty contractors are dispatched for approved items. The 10-year structural claim is administered directly through 2-10 Home Buyers Warranty (a separate entity from Trophy), so structural claims go through 2-10's own process. Homeowners should document all warranty claims in writing through the online form — verbal requests are not trackable.

What the warranty doesn't cover

Trophy's warranty manual lists specific non-warrantable items, including: cabinet damage and loose hinges, carpet shade variances or wear patterns, caulking shrinkage, driveway and walkway cracks narrower than 1/4 inch, sink and countertop discoloration, drywall nail pops and cracks narrower than 1/32 inch, fences, glass and mirror damage, paint and texture cracks narrower than 1/8 inch, subfloor squeaks and stair pops, tile chips, towel racks and toilet paper dispensers, wood flooring scratches and nicks, and damage caused by severe weather events. Review the full warranty manual at trophysignaturehomes.com/warranty-manuals before closing.

Honest Assessment — What Families Love and What to Know

Trophy Signature Homes is a strong value-play for families targeting DFW's outer growth corridors. Here's what the data shows — and where relocating families should set their expectations before signing.

Strengths

  • Substantial included features at the price point — ENERGY STAR appliances, Low-E windows, 15.2 SEER2 HVAC minimum, smart thermostat, LED lighting, low-flow fixtures, and fresh air ventilation are all base inclusions. Comparable volume builders frequently charge $10,000–$25,000 in upgrade packages for the same specs.
  • A+ BBB, accredited since 2021 — Trophy holds an A+ rating with the Better Business Bureau and has been accredited since April 2021. No TREC disciplinary actions were found in research for Trophy or its parent company Green Brick Partners in publicly available records.
  • Green Brick Parents backing — as a subsidiary of Green Brick Partners (NYSE: GRBK), DFW's third-largest homebuilder by lot control, Trophy has the land pipeline and financial stability of a publicly traded company. This materially reduces the risk of a builder going under mid-construction that smaller regional builders carry.
  • Top-selling master-planned communities — five Trophy communities were ranked in RCLCO's Top-Selling Master-Planned Communities of 2025 nationally. Communities designed to that standard tend to have stronger long-term resale performance than standalone subdivisions.
  • Rate buydown and financing programs — Trophy's "Signature Savings" program (through Green Brick Mortgage) was offering 2.99% (5.451 APR) at time of research in May 2026 — a materially below-market rate. The program also includes a complimentary 3-year refinancing option if rates drop. Confirm current terms before visiting; these programs change frequently.
  • Everyday Heroes and veteran programs — $1,000 purchase credit for teachers, nurses, police, firefighters, and military; Operation Finally Home partnership for wounded veterans. Meaningful for Kristen's veteran and first-responder clients.

Things to Know

  • Outer-corridor-only DFW footprint — Trophy does not build in Frisco, Plano, Southlake, Flower Mound, Coppell, Allen, or Colleyville. Their communities are in Forney, Lavon, Princeton, Seagoville, Alvarado, Farmersville, and similar outer suburbs — most are 30–50+ minutes from major DFW employment centers. Location is the primary tradeoff for the price advantage.
  • Post-close warranty responsiveness complaints — the most consistent pattern in BBB and third-party reviews is difficulty getting warranty work completed after closing. Multiple homeowners reported that Trophy's 3-6-9-12 month check-in process resulted in items being deferred or closed without resolution. Document every claim in writing via the online form; keep your own dated records.
  • 1-year workmanship warranty matches Texas legal minimum only — Trophy's 1-year workmanship coverage meets the RCLA floor but does not exceed it. Perry Homes, for comparison, offers a combined 2-year workmanship-plus-systems period. Families who want more post-close protection should factor this in.
  • No published aggregate TrustBuilder score — Trophy has individual community reviews on Eliant's platform (reviews.eliant.com/trophy-signature-homes) but no single aggregate TrustBuilder score is publicly available. This makes direct benchmark comparisons against other DFW builders harder to perform independently.
  • Spray foam insulation is select, not universal — though Trophy markets spray foam as a differentiator, it is available in select communities and homes, not as a universal standard across all plans. Ask specifically which communities and plans include it before choosing a lot.

Who Trophy Signature Homes is best for — and when to look elsewhere

Best fit: First-time buyers and value-driven move-up families who want a well-featured, ENERGY STAR-certified home in DFW's outer growth corridors and are comfortable with a 30–50 minute commute to central employment. Budget-conscious families who want included features (not upgrade fees) at a sub-$400K price point will find Trophy's included package hard to match in DFW's new construction market.

When another builder may fit better: If your family is set on Kristen's core target suburbs — Frisco, Flower Mound, Southlake, McKinney's established sections, Allen, Plano, Coppell, or Colleyville — Trophy has no presence there. For those locations, Highland Homes, Perry Homes, Drees Custom Homes, or David Weekley Homes are worth evaluating. If post-close warranty service responsiveness is a top priority, review the available BBB complaint summaries and compare against builders with documented faster claim resolution.

Trophy Signature Homes Floor Plans & Design Process

Trophy's DFW floor plan library spans eight named series and a range of approximately 1,232 to 4,400+ square feet across communities. In DFW specifically, plans run from 3 to 5 bedrooms and 2 to 4 bathrooms. Pricing at time of research (May 2026) ran from $238,990 in Farmersville (Lakehaven community, Gem/Painter series) to $714,490 in McKinney (Painted Tree community, larger plans). At Trails of Lavon — one of Trophy's flagship DFW communities — there are 21 floor plans available, ranging from 1,232 to 3,617 square feet.

Production Floor Plans with Series-Based Selection

Trophy is a production builder — floor plans within each series are engineered and fixed in their structural configuration, which is how the builder delivers consistent quality at volume pricing. Buyers select from available plans within the series that matches their lot and budget tier. The series naming (Gem, Trophy, Texas Tree, Painter, Musician, Victory, Golf Course, River) aligns plans to price tiers and lot widths, making it relatively straightforward to understand what you can get at a given budget. Buyers cannot request structural modifications to existing plans — this is the same model used by most DFW volume and semi-custom builders at this price tier.

Design Center

Trophy's design selections process is managed through their sales centers and design team — there is no separate standalone design center in the style of luxury builders. Selections typically include interior finishes such as flooring materials, countertop options, cabinetry colors, and hardware within Trophy's curated packages. Relocating buyers purchasing virtually or from out of state should ask the sales team specifically about the remote-selection process and whether virtual appointments are available — Trophy's team has experience working with relocation buyers from California, Illinois, and other high-traffic origin states.

Standard features included at base price

At base price, Trophy homes include: 15.2 SEER2 HVAC minimum, double-pane Low-E insulated windows, fresh air ventilation system, smart thermostat, LED lighting throughout, low-flow commodes and faucets, ENERGY STAR-certified appliances, and modern exterior elevations designed to the specific plan series. Spray foam insulation and tankless water heaters are included in select communities and plans — ask specifically which communities carry these as standard before choosing a lot. These features are confirmed inclusions on Trophy's energy savings page (trophysignaturehomes.com/high-efficiency-home).

Kristen's Agent Perspective

Kristen Carpentier DFW Family Relocation Specialist · TREC #760457 · eXp Realty
"Trophy is one of the better value propositions in DFW's outer growth corridors — their energy efficiency package is genuinely impressive at the price point, and the Green Brick backing means you're not taking a bet on a small builder's balance sheet. But the location equation is real, and so is the warranty responsiveness pattern I hear from buyers. Go in knowing both."

What I tell families considering Trophy: the "more is always included" approach is legitimate at their price tier. In a DFW new construction market where builders frequently charge $15,000–$30,000 in design center upgrades just to get to a livable spec level, Trophy's energy package at base is a real differentiator. If your family is targeting a sub-$450K home in a community with good school district access and you're willing to commute 35–45 minutes, Trophy deserves a serious look — especially in communities like Eastridge and Southridge in McKinney (McKinney ISD), Devonshire in Forney (Forney ISD), and the Lavon-area communities (Community ISD).

This isn't unique to Trophy — it's a common pattern with production builders at this price tier — but it's more pronounced here than with some alternatives. For remote buyers especially, having an agent registered and present at the pre-close walkthrough and the first warranty check-in is worth the time.

Bottom line: Trophy Signature Homes is the right choice for families who want a feature-rich, ENERGY STAR-certified home in DFW's outer growth corridors at a price that's hard to match, and who are prepared to be organized and proactive about warranty claims post-close. What is Trophy Signature Homes' warranty?

Trophy Signature Homes provides a standard 1-2-10 new home warranty: 1 year of workmanship coverage (drywall, trim, paint, tile, roofing finishes, cabinetry), 2 years of distribution systems coverage (mechanical, electrical, and plumbing systems), and 10 years of structural coverage administered by 2-10 Home Buyers Warranty (an independent third-party structural warranty company). This warranty structure exactly meets Texas's RCLA legal minimums — it does not exceed them. Claims are filed through Trophy's online warranty request form at trophysignaturehomes.com/warranty-request. Trophy schedules 3-, 6-, 9-, and 12-month check-in visits during the first year. Key non-warrantable items include fences, cabinet damage, carpet issues, subfloor squeaks, tile chips, and normal settling cracks — review the full warranty manual at trophysignaturehomes.com/warranty-manuals before closing.

Do I need a Realtor to buy a Trophy Signature Homes new construction?

You don't need one, but you should have one — and it costs you nothing extra. Trophy's sales agents work for Trophy, not for you — they are trained to represent the builder's interests. Trophy's commission program was offering up to 5% to buyer's agents at time of research — confirming that having representation costs you nothing. How long does Trophy Signature Homes take to build a house in DFW?

Trophy Signature Homes does not publish a specific build-time range on their website. For DFW production builders in this price tier, typical contract-to-close timelines generally run 6–12 months for production homes (spec or built-to-order from existing plans), depending on community, lot availability, and current supply chain conditions. Trophy also maintains inventory homes (move-in-ready or near-complete spec homes) at several DFW communities for buyers who need a faster close — these can often close in 30–90 days from contract. Does Trophy Signature Homes require you to use their lender?

No — Trophy Signature Homes does not require buyers to use Green Brick Mortgage. However, their primary rate incentive (the "Signature Savings" buydown program, which was offering 2.99% / 5.451 APR at time of research in May 2026) is typically tied to using Green Brick Mortgage. Buyers who choose an outside lender may still be eligible for other incentives (design center credits, closing cost contributions) but may not qualify for the rate buydown. Always ask the sales team which incentives are lender-tied and which are available to any buyer. Before committing to Green Brick Mortgage, get a competing rate quote from an independent lender — sometimes the buydown is the better deal, sometimes origination fees make it wash. A buyer's agent experienced in new construction can help you compare the true cost of the deal.
