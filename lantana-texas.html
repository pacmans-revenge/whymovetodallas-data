Getting Your Bearings

Where Is Lantana, Texas?

Lantana is an unincorporated master-planned community in Denton County, Texas, positioned along the FM 407 corridor between Argyle and Bartonville — about 12 miles northwest of Flower Mound and 30 miles northwest of downtown Dallas. It sits at the convergence of Highways 377 and 114, giving residents convenient access to both the I-35W and I-35E corridors into Fort Worth and Dallas respectively. The community is technically unincorporated Denton County, which means no city government — and no city property tax.

What makes Lantana's location work for families is the layering of quick access. DFW International Airport is about 18 minutes east via Highway 114 — one of the shortest airport drives you'll find anywhere in the Metroplex at this price point. The Flower Mound and Argyle commercial corridors on FM 407 handle most daily errands. And while the commute to downtown Dallas is real (35–40 minutes on a clear day), the Las Colinas and Irving employment corridors are a quick 25–30 minutes east on 114, making Lantana genuinely practical for families with one or both spouses working in mid-corridor tech, energy, or finance roles.

Education

Schools in Lantana — A Split ISD Community

Here's the honest conversation I have with every family buying in Lantana: the community sits at the intersection of multiple school districts, and the ISD your children attend depends entirely on which street your home is on. The majority of homes within the core Lantana master plan are served by Denton ISD, which operates four campuses directly within or immediately adjacent to the community. A smaller western fringe of Lantana falls within Northwest ISD. A few outlying parcels near the Argyle edge may feed Argyle ISD. Before you make an offer on any Lantana home, verify the attendance zone for your specific address — not the zip code, the actual address — with the district boundary tool. I do this on every offer I write.

For most Lantana families, Denton ISD is the day-to-day reality. The district earns a B rating from the Texas Education Agency (score ~82/100, 2023), and Niche gives it an A- overall with an A in College Prep. Three elementary campuses are located within walking distance of multiple Lantana neighborhoods — a genuine rarity in master-planned DFW communities. Students feed into Harpool Middle School and then to W.H. Guyer High School, one of the larger and better-regarded campuses in the district.

Family Life

What Life Actually Looks Like in Lantana

Lantana is deliberately designed as a community, not just a collection of houses near a highway. The 10-plus miles of paved trails that connect every village, the five HOA pool complexes, the 18-hole golf course, and the community fitness center are all built into the infrastructure — not afterthoughts. What residents consistently report, on Reddit and in Facebook relocation groups, is that it feels more like a neighborhood from 30 years ago than most new DFW developments: neighbors actually know each other, kids ride bikes between sections, and the HOA community calendar stays full year-round.

Youth Sports & Athletics

Lantana hosts active AYSO soccer and Little League programs, with registration through the HOA community portal. Most organized leagues use the community's own athletic fields and parks. Guyer High School athletic programs are well-funded and competitive at the 6A level.

Outdoor Living & Trails

The 10+ mile paved trail system is the spine of the community — it connects every village, loops around the golf course's green space, and passes multiple fishing ponds stocked for catch-and-release. Families use it for morning runs, evening bike rides, and the kind of spontaneous neighbor encounters that build real community.

Shopping & Dining

Lantana itself has limited in-community retail — a handful of businesses at the FM 407/Orchid Hill intersection. The real dining and shopping is 8–12 minutes east in Flower Mound's Lakeside area, Long Prairie Road corridor, and the Bartonville shopping strip on 407. HEB (a Texas grocery institution), Target, and most major chains are within a 10-minute drive.

Events & Community Culture

The Lantana HOA runs one of the more active community event calendars in northwest DFW — Music on the Green summer concerts, holiday lights festivals, food truck nights, Easter egg hunts, and pool parties. Residents on Facebook groups consistently cite the community programming as a reason they chose Lantana over larger, more anonymous developments.

The Golf Course Difference

The Jay Morrish-designed 18-hole golf course is more than an amenity — it's the physical framework that gives Lantana its open, park-like feel. Even non-golfers benefit from the course's green space buffer between neighborhoods and its aesthetic quality. HOA members receive resident golf rates, and the clubhouse hosts community gatherings year-round.

Community Feel & Connection

The 93% homeownership rate tells you something important about Lantana's social fabric — these are long-term committed residents, not a transient rental community. Neighbors genuinely know each other. The median household income of $200K means most families are in similar life stages with similar priorities. If you're coming from a place where that kind of neighborliness is rare, prepare to be surprised.

The honest tradeoff: Lantana's community infrastructure is exceptional, but the lifestyle is genuinely insular. There are almost no restaurants or bars within walking distance, no coffee shop to work from, no "third place" beyond the amenity centers. If you want to walk to dinner or grab an impromptu coffee with a neighbor, this isn't it. FM 407 is a 5–10 minute drive for virtually everything. Families who've made Lantana work long-term tend to embrace this — they host at home, they use the trails and pools as their social infrastructure, and they make the drive to Flower Mound when they want a night out. But it's a real adjustment if you're coming from an urban or walkable suburban environment.
Getting Outside

Recreation, Walkability & Community Amenities

Lantana is one of the more paradoxical communities in DFW for the walkability question: a Walk Score of 28 (officially car-dependent for errands) sits alongside a genuinely walkable interior trail network that connects every neighborhood to the golf course, fishing ponds, and amenity centers. The honest answer is: you need a car to leave Lantana, but once you're home, the community itself is remarkably pedestrian-friendly. There is no DART rail, no DCTA bus service, and no public transit option serving the community.

Parks, Trails & Green Space

The Lantana HOA maintains over 300 acres of open space, including the trail system, green belts, fishing ponds, and the golf course corridor. Individual parks are distributed throughout each village section, keeping most homes within a 5–10 minute walk of a playground or green space. Twin Coves Park on Lake Grapevine (in Flower Mound) adds significant outdoor recreation options about 12 minutes east.

Lantana Trail System

10+ miles of paved and natural surface trails connecting every village section. Suitable for running, walking, cycling, and scooters. The trail loops around the golf course perimeter and through multiple green belt sections.

Lantana Golf Club

Jay Morrish-designed 18-hole championship course. The course's 300+ acres of managed green space creates a park-like buffer between neighborhoods. Junior golf programs available; HOA member resident rates.

Fishing Ponds

Multiple stocked catch-and-release ponds distributed throughout the community. Popular with families for weekend activity — no fishing license required for ponds within HOA property. Kids fish these regularly after school.

Village Pocket Parks

Each neighborhood section maintains its own neighborhood park with playground equipment. Most lots in Lantana are within a 5–8 minute walk of a community playground, making after-school and weekend outdoor play genuinely accessible without driving.

Five Community Pool Complexes

Five HOA-maintained aquatic facilities distributed across the community — so no single pool gets overwhelmed in summer. Includes lap pools, leisure pools, splash areas, and one resort-style facility with a waterslide for kids.

Twin Coves Park — Lake Grapevine

About 12 minutes east in Flower Mound — a significant Denton County park on the shores of Lake Grapevine. Boat ramps, sandy beach areas, hiking trails, and picnic areas. The go-to for Lantana families wanting a lake day without a long drive.

Pros & Cons of Living in Lantana

✓ What Families Love

  • Best-in-class master-planned infrastructure — 10+ miles of trails, 5 pools, golf course, fitness center, and tennis courts, all HOA-maintained. The amenities are genuinely better than most DFW communities at this price point.
  • True neighborhood feel — 93% homeownership, $200K median income, and a community HOA that actually programs events means neighbors know each other. Residents who've lived in Plano or Allen and moved to Lantana often say it's the first place they've felt like they actually know their neighbors.
  • Elementary schools within walking distance — Three Denton ISD elementary campuses sit within or immediately adjacent to the community. For families with young kids, this is a genuine daily-life advantage.
  • No city property tax — Unincorporated Denton County means no city tax, saving $500–$2,000 per year compared to incorporated suburbs. The effective rate with MUD is still real, but you're not paying a city government you voted for in the wrong town.
  • DFW Airport in 18 minutes — One of the shortest suburb-to-airport drives in the entire Metroplex. Corporate travelers rate this highly — and so do families who visit grandparents frequently.
  • Very safe and stable community — Safety sub-score of 89/100, consistent A+ family ratings from Niche, and a community where kids genuinely still play outside unsupervised. Parents moving from dense urban environments frequently note this as the biggest lifestyle upgrade.

— What to Weigh

  • Fully car-dependent — no exceptions — Walk Score 28. You cannot walk to a grocery store, restaurant, or coffee shop. Every errand is a drive. Non-drivers are functionally stranded without a ride.
  • HOA fees and rules are real — Monthly HOA dues of $121–$150 plus an architectural review committee that takes exterior changes seriously. Fence colors, landscaping, additions — all require approval. For buyers who want autonomy over their property, this is a friction point.
  • Dallas commute is longer than it looks on a map — Downtown Dallas is 35–45 minutes on a clear day; with traffic, budget 50–60 minutes. Remote workers can make Lantana work; daily Dallas commuters should do a realistic test drive during rush hour before buying.
  • Limited in-community dining and retail — Lantana has almost nothing to eat or drink within the community boundaries. The FM 407 corridor in Flower Mound is the closest alternative. If you want to walk to dinner or pop into a wine bar on a Tuesday, this isn't the place.
  • FM 407 corridor traffic at rush hour — The main artery in and out of Lantana gets congested during morning and evening peaks, especially at the Hwy 377 intersection in Argyle. Budget 10–15 extra minutes during rush hour compared to off-peak travel times.

What school district is Lantana, TX in?

The ISD for any specific home depends on the exact address — not the zip code. Always verify with the district boundary tool before making an offer.
Is Lantana, Texas a good place to raise a family?
Yes — Lantana consistently earns an A+ from Niche for families, and the community demographics back it up: 55% of households have children, 93% are owner-occupied, and the median household income is $200K. The trail system, 5 community pools, youth sports programming, and close proximity to Denton ISD elementary schools make it unusually well-designed for family life. Residents in online relocation groups frequently describe it as the most "neighborhood-feeling" community they've lived in, in DFW.
How far is Lantana, TX from Dallas?
Lantana is approximately 30–34 miles from downtown Dallas, which translates to roughly 35–42 minutes on a clear day via I-35E South. During rush hour, plan 50–60 minutes. The Las Colinas/Irving corridor on Highway 114 is a closer 25–30 minutes, and DFW Airport is approximately 18–22 minutes east. Lantana's location makes most sense for families working in the Highway 114 corridor, Fort Worth, or with jobs near DFW Airport — the daily downtown Dallas commute is real.
What is the HOA fee in Lantana, TX?
HOA dues in Lantana typically run $121–$150 per month, depending on the specific village section. These fees cover the trail system maintenance, five community pool complexes, the fitness center, tennis courts, community events programming, and common area landscaping. Lantana's HOA also maintains an active architectural review process — exterior changes including fencing, landscaping additions, and structural changes require prior approval. Budget accordingly and review the CC&Rs before closing.
Is Lantana, TX safe?
Lantana is widely considered one of the safer communities in northwest Denton County. As an unincorporated CDP, it doesn't file separate crime statistics with the FBI, but a safety sub-score of 89/100 (top 11% of DFW communities), an A+ Niche families rating, and consistent resident feedback describe it as very safe. The 93% homeownership rate and active HOA community fabric contribute to low transient activity. The Denton County Sheriff's Department provides law enforcement services.
What are home prices in Lantana, TX?
The median single-family home price in Lantana is approximately $650K (NTREIS trailing 12-month data, 2026). Entry-level Lantana homes — smaller lots in Juniper or similar villages — start around $450K–$500K. The Larkspur and Laviana sections command $650K–$1.1M+ for larger lots, golf course proximity, or executive-style finishes. The market has remained in seller's market territory with roughly 2.9 months of supply, meaning well-priced homes typically receive offers within the first week or two.
What is the property tax rate in Lantana, TX?
The base property tax rate in Lantana (unincorporated Denton County) is approximately 1.58 per $100 assessed value, made up of Denton ISD (~$1.1569), Denton County (~$0.1859), the hospital district (~$0.0903), and miscellaneous special districts (~$0.15). There is no city property tax — Lantana is unincorporated. Many Lantana sections also carry a Municipal Utility District (MUD) assessment of an additional $0.30–$0.40 per $100, bringing the effective rate to roughly 1.88–1.95% for those homes. Always request the tax certificate for a specific address before closing.
What amenities does Lantana, TX have?
Lantana's HOA amenities include 10+ miles of connected trails, five community pool complexes (including one with a waterslide), a fitness center, tennis and pickleball courts, multiple neighborhood pocket parks, stocked fishing ponds, and a community event center. The Jay Morrish-designed 18-hole golf course is semi-private with HOA member rates. The HOA also runs an active events calendar: Music on the Green summer concerts, holiday festivals, food truck events, Easter egg hunts, and community social programming throughout the year.
What neighborhoods are in Lantana, TX?
Lantana is organized into distinct village sections: Larkspur (the original established section, $500K–$750K, mature trees and larger lots), Laviana (executive-tier, $700K–$1.1M+), Juniper (energy-efficient lower-maintenance homes, $450K–$625K), and Wimberley/Sonora (newer family-oriented phases, $500K–$700K, walkable to elementary schools). Each village shares the same HOA infrastructure and trail connectivity. Specific school zoning can vary by section, so verify the ISD for any home address before committing.
Is Lantana, TX worth it compared to Flower Mound or Argyle?
It depends on your priorities. Lantana wins on amenity infrastructure (5 pools, 10+ miles trails, golf course, fitness center) and community cohesion — you're unlikely to find that level of built-in programming at the same price in Flower Mound or Argyle. Flower Mound offers more walkable retail and dining and slightly shorter Dallas commutes. Argyle gives you Argyle ISD (consistently rated A+ and considered one of the top rural-to-suburban ISDs in DFW) with larger lot options and lower HOA overhead. If A+ schools are non-negotiable and you want a larger property with fewer HOA restrictions, Argyle is worth a serious look. If community infrastructure and neighborhood feel matter more, Lantana is hard to beat in this price range.
