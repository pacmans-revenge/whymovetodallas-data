District Overview

McKinney Independent School District serves the city of McKinney, Texas — one of the fastest-growing cities in the United States — and portions of the surrounding Collin County area. As of the 2024–25 school year, MISD enrolls approximately 23,342 students across 32 campuses: elementary, middle, and three comprehensive high schools. District administrative offices are located at 1 Duvall Street, McKinney, TX 75069.

McKinney ISD is a large, growing district serving a genuinely diverse cross-section of the community — from long-established McKinney neighborhoods to new-construction master-planned communities on the city's edges. The district's student body reflects McKinney's rapid demographic evolution, which influences both its academic profile and the breadth of programs it offers.

One critical note for relocating families: a McKinney address does not automatically mean McKinney ISD. The city of McKinney overlaps with Frisco ISD, Prosper ISD, and Lovejoy ISD boundaries. Always verify your specific home address using the TEA School District Locator or the McKinney ISD address lookup tool at mckinneyisd.net before making an offer.

Address Boundary Warning

McKinney city limits overlap with four ISDs: McKinney ISD (primary), Frisco ISD (southwest), Prosper ISD (northwest), and Lovejoy ISD (southeast). Never assume your address is in McKinney ISD without verifying. This is one of the most important steps Kristen takes with every McKinney buyer before submitting an offer.

TEA Accountability Rating

The Texas Education Agency assigns every school district an annual A–F accountability rating based on three domains. McKinney ISD holds a TEA B rating (score: 88 out of 100) for the 2024–25 school year. An 88 places the district well into the B range and meaningfully above the state average — but below the A-rated districts (Frisco, Allen, Prosper, Coppell, Carroll) that dominate Collin County headlines.

What the three domains measure:

  • Student Achievement — STAAR test scores, graduation rates, college and career readiness indicators
  • School Progress — year-over-year academic growth compared to similar campuses statewide
  • Closing the Gaps — performance equity across student demographic groups

McKinney ISD's B rating reflects genuine academic strength — a 96.3% graduation rate, a broad AP course catalog, and multiple high-performing elementary campuses — alongside the equity challenges that accompany serving a large, socioeconomically diverse city. The district's "Closing the Gaps" domain is where most large urban-adjacent ISDs face the greatest pressure.

Campus-level ratings vary significantly within MISD. Some elementary campuses earn individual A ratings from TEA while others score lower. Families should research the specific campus their home address feeds — not just the district-wide rating. Campus-level data is available at txschools.gov.

Why Niche Rates McKinney ISD an "A" While TEA Gives It a "B"

Niche.com and TEA measure fundamentally different things. TEA's B (score: 88) is driven primarily by STAAR performance data and equity metrics across all student subgroups — objective, state-standardized measures. Niche's A factors in community satisfaction surveys, teacher quality reviews, diversity metrics, extracurricular breadth, and parent ratings — inputs that tend to favor districts with strong community culture and robust program offerings, even when raw test-score metrics are more mixed. Both ratings capture something real. The Niche A reflects what parents who live in McKinney ISD say about their experience; the TEA B reflects where the district stands on the state's standardized performance scale.

For relocating families, the practical takeaway: McKinney ISD offers a solid, well-rounded education with strong extracurriculars and genuine community involvement, but families laser-focused on STAAR performance rankings may also want to compare specific campus data before finalizing a home address.

Graduation Rate

McKinney ISD's four-year graduation rate is 96.3% — notably strong for a district of its size and demographic diversity, and above the state average. This figure reflects the district's focus on credit recovery, student supports, and college and career readiness programming at the secondary level.

Advanced Placement

All three McKinney ISD high schools offer a comprehensive AP course catalog. Students across McKinney High School, McKinney Boyd, and McKinney North have access to AP courses in English, history, mathematics, sciences, world languages, and the arts. AP participation and pass rates are published annually through TEA and College Board reporting.

Dual Credit

McKinney ISD partners with Collin College for dual credit enrollment. Eligible juniors and seniors can earn college credit while still in high school, at no cost to the family, under the state's Texas College Credit Program. Dual credit is available on-campus and at Collin College locations in McKinney and surrounding areas.

College and Career Readiness

MISD tracks the TEA-required College, Career, and Military Readiness (CCMR) rate — the percentage of graduates completing at least one readiness indicator such as an AP or IB exam score, dual credit completion, or an industry certification through a CTE pathway. The district's CCMR performance is publicly reported at txschools.gov.

STAAR Performance

District-wide STAAR proficiency rates are available by campus at txschools.gov. Performance varies meaningfully across MISD's 32 campuses — some elementary schools consistently post A-level numbers while others reflect the academic challenges of serving higher-need student populations. Families should review campus-specific STAAR data for their assigned schools, not rely on district-wide averages alone.

Campus Highlights

McKinney ISD operates three comprehensive high schools, four middle schools, and 25 elementary campuses. Which high school your child attends depends on your home address within McKinney's attendance zones. Here are the key campuses relocating families most frequently ask about.

McKinney High School

Grades 9–12LionsUIL 6A

The original McKinney ISD high school — one of the oldest and most established in Collin County. Serves central and southern McKinney. Strong fine arts tradition (band, choir, theater) and competitive athletics. Deeply rooted in McKinney's community identity.

McKinney Boyd High School

Grades 9–12BroncosUIL 6A

Opened in 2005 to serve western McKinney's growth. Strong across academics and athletics, with a well-established AP program and a reputation for a tight-knit campus culture. Serves many of the master-planned communities in west McKinney.

McKinney North High School

Grades 9–12BulldogsUIL 6A

Opened to absorb growth in northern McKinney's expanding neighborhoods. Serves the newer residential developments in northern McKinney. AP program, CTE pathways, and athletics programs continue to grow with the campus's enrollment base.

Faubion Middle School

Grades 6–8Central McKinney

One of the district's established middle schools, serving central McKinney neighborhoods. Offers GT services, pre-AP coursework, fine arts, and athletics as part of the standard middle school program.

Dowell Middle School

Grades 6–8West McKinney

Serves western McKinney and is the primary feeder middle school for McKinney Boyd High School. Strong pre-AP offerings and active fine arts program. Reflects the newer community growth on McKinney's west side.

Elementary Campuses

25 CampusesK–5 or PreK–5

MISD operates 25 elementary schools across McKinney. Campus quality and demographics vary — some consistently earn A ratings from TEA while others serve higher-need populations. Always verify your specific elementary assignment and review that campus's individual TEA data before purchasing.

High school attendance zones are determined by home address. MISD maintains a campus locator and attendance zone maps at mckinneyisd.net. Contact the district enrollment office to verify your specific campus assignment before making an offer.

Special Programs

Gifted & Talented (GT)

MISD identifies and serves GT students K–12 through a multi-criteria assessment process. Elementary GT students receive pull-out enrichment and differentiated instruction. Secondary GT students have access to pre-AP and AP coursework aligned to their identified areas of strength. Newly enrolled students from out of state should bring prior GT documentation — MISD will review it as part of its identification process.

Advanced Placement (AP)

All three high schools offer comprehensive AP course menus covering English, history, mathematics, sciences, world languages, and the arts. AP participation is open to eligible students at each campus. College Board exam fees may be subsidized for income-eligible students through state and federal programs.

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

MISD's CTE pathways span Health Science, Business and Finance, Technology Applications, Architecture and Construction, and more. Students can earn industry-recognized certifications alongside their high school diploma. Pathway options vary by campus — review each high school's specific CTE offerings on the MISD website.

Project Lead The Way (PLTW)

McKinney ISD participates in Project Lead The Way, offering hands-on STEM and engineering courses at the secondary level. PLTW courses count toward CTE pathway credit and align with industry certifications in engineering, biomedical science, and computer science.

Fine Arts

Fine arts programming is a genuine strength across all three high schools — band, choir, orchestra, visual art, and theater are offered at each campus. McKinney High School in particular has a long-standing fine arts tradition. MISD fine arts programs compete at the UIL 6A level, with multiple programs advancing to regional and state competition.

Dual Credit (Collin College)

Through MISD's partnership with Collin College, eligible juniors and seniors can enroll in college courses at no cost, earning both high school and college credit simultaneously. Collin College has a McKinney campus, making on-site dual credit accessible for many MISD high school students.

Special Education & Support Services

McKinney ISD provides special education and related services to eligible students ages 3–21 under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Services are delivered across the full least-restrictive-environment continuum — from full inclusion in general education classrooms with co-teaching support, to resource room pull-out, to self-contained Life Skills programs, depending on the student's Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Related services available within MISD include speech-language therapy, occupational therapy, physical therapy, behavioral support, and ARD (Admissions, Review, and Dismissal) facilitation. Transition programs at the high school level prepare students for post-secondary education, employment, and independent living — a required component of every IEP for students age 16 and older under federal law.

Out-of-state IEP transfers: Texas law requires McKinney ISD to either adopt the student's existing IEP from another state or convene a new ARD meeting within 30 days of enrollment. Bring all prior IEP documentation, evaluation reports, eligibility determinations, and service plans to your enrollment appointment. The sooner you contact MISD's Special Education department, the smoother the transition.

For students who do not qualify for special education but require formal accommodations under the Rehabilitation Act, MISD provides Section 504 services. Contact the campus 504 Coordinator after enrollment to initiate an evaluation. MISD also provides dyslexia screening and intervention services at all campuses as required by Texas Education Code Chapter 38.

Athletics & Extracurriculars

McKinney ISD competes in the University Interscholastic League (UIL) at Class 6A — the highest UIL classification — across all three high schools. The district's three programs — the McKinney Lions, McKinney Boyd Broncos, and McKinney North Bulldogs — compete across football, basketball, baseball, softball, soccer, swimming, track, cross country, tennis, and golf.

All three programs are regionally competitive, with football and basketball programs particularly well-established. The district's athletics infrastructure includes multiple stadium facilities and practice facilities serving the three campuses. Having three 6A programs creates a meaningful campus identity and school-spirit culture across McKinney's high schools — a factor many families cite when choosing between the three attendance zones.

Beyond athletics, MISD's UIL competitive activities include Academic Decathlon, One Act Play, Debate, Science Olympiad, and a full range of student organizations aligned to CTE pathways — DECA, FBLA, HOSA, SkillsUSA, and National Honor Society chapters are active across all three high schools.

Fine arts extracurriculars — marching band, orchestra, choir, theater — compete at the UIL 6A level and are a significant part of student life at all three campuses, particularly at McKinney High School, which has one of the district's longest fine arts traditions.

School Choice Within the District

Attendance Zones

McKinney ISD assigns students to campuses based on home address attendance zones. With three high schools and four middle schools, which campus your child attends matters — campus cultures, feeder patterns, and specific program strengths differ across the district. Verify your attendance zone assignment before purchasing a home if a specific campus is a priority.

Intra-District Transfers

Intra-district transfers (requesting a campus other than your assigned attendance zone campus) are considered by MISD on a case-by-case basis, subject to capacity at the receiving campus. Transfers are not guaranteed and must typically be renewed annually. The application process and window are detailed on the MISD website.

Out-of-District Transfers

McKinney ISD has limited open enrollment for students who reside outside district boundaries. Out-of-district transfers are subject to available capacity and are not automatically approved. Families seeking to enroll from outside MISD boundaries should contact the district's enrollment office for current availability and requirements.

Boundary Overlap — Critical for McKinney Buyers

This is perhaps the most important school choice issue in McKinney: the city of McKinney overlaps with Frisco ISD (southwest), Prosper ISD (northwest), and Lovejoy ISD (southeast) in addition to McKinney ISD. A home in a McKinney zip code may be zoned to any of these four ISDs. Lovejoy ISD in particular attracts buyers specifically seeking it out for academic performance — and Lovejoy addresses often carry a McKinney mailing address despite being a different district. Always verify the specific ISD using the TEA School District Locator.

District Finances

Tax Rate

McKinney ISD's certified total tax rate is $1.13 per $100 of assessed value (Collin County Appraisal District). This comprises the Maintenance & Operations (M&O) rate and the Interest & Sinking (I&S) rate. The I&S component reflects bond debt service — MISD has passed bond packages to fund new campus construction, facility upgrades, and technology infrastructure as McKinney's rapid population growth continues to drive enrollment.

For a home assessed at $600,000, the MISD school district tax levy is approximately $6,780 per year before exemptions. Texas homeowners with a primary homestead exemption receive a $100,000 reduction in assessed value for school district taxes (increased to $100,000 by HB 3, effective 2023), meaningfully reducing the effective tax burden. On a $600,000 assessed home, the homestead exemption reduces the taxable value to $500,000, for an annual levy of approximately $5,650.

TEA FIRST Rating

McKinney ISD holds a Superior financial rating under the TEA FIRST (Financial Integrity Rating System of Texas) framework — the highest available rating. FIRST ratings evaluate school district financial management practices including fund balance levels, debt management, and administrative cost ratios. A Superior FIRST rating indicates that MISD manages taxpayer funds responsibly at the institutional level, even as its academic TEA rating sits at B.

Per-Pupil Expenditure

McKinney ISD's per-pupil expenditure is $14,116 (2024–25 data, TEA PEIMS). This reflects total instructional and operational spending per enrolled student and is publicly reported through TEA's financial transparency system. Current data is available at txschools.gov.

Property Tax Administration

Property in McKinney ISD is appraised by the Collin County Appraisal District. Homeowners should file for the standard homestead exemption with the appraisal district after closing — this is not automatic. The homestead exemption reduces the appraised value used to calculate school district taxes and caps year-over-year appraisal increases at 10% for homestead properties.

Enrollment — New Families

How to Enroll

New student enrollment in McKinney ISD is completed through the district's online enrollment portal at mckinneyisd.net. Required documentation includes:

  • Proof of residency — executed lease agreement or deed/closing disclosure showing a McKinney ISD address
  • Birth certificate — required for all first-time Texas enrollees
  • Immunization records — Texas-required vaccines (Tdap, meningococcal, and others per grade level)
  • Prior academic records — report cards, transcripts, and standardized test results from previous school
  • IEP or 504 documentation — if applicable, bring all current documents to accelerate special education intake
  • Proof of custody — if applicable, bring legal documentation

Enrollment Timing

MISD accepts enrollment year-round for mid-year transfers. Online enrollment typically opens in spring for the following school year. As McKinney continues to grow, new campuses are periodically added — verify current campus assignments and zone maps at the time of your home search, not just at closing, as zone boundaries can shift with new campus openings.

Gifted Identification for Transfer Students

Students transferring from out of state who are identified as gifted in their prior district should bring all available GT documentation — assessment results, eligibility letters, and any service records. MISD uses a multi-criteria identification process and will review prior documentation as part of its screening. Initial GT assessment for newly enrolled students typically begins within the first several weeks of enrollment.

Pre-K Options

MISD offers Pre-K programs for income-eligible and at-risk students at designated elementary campuses. Contact MISD's Early Childhood department for current eligibility criteria, campus availability, and enrollment timing for Pre-K seats.

Frequently Asked Questions

My home address says "McKinney, TX" — does that mean I'm in McKinney ISD? ▾
Not necessarily — and this is one of the most common surprises for McKinney buyers. The city of McKinney overlaps with four ISDs: McKinney ISD (the primary district), Frisco ISD (southwest McKinney), Prosper ISD (northwest McKinney), and Lovejoy ISD (southeast McKinney). A McKinney zip code or mailing address does not determine your ISD. Always verify your specific address using the TEA School District Locator before making an offer. This is a step Kristen walks every McKinney buyer through before they go under contract.
What is the difference between the three McKinney ISD high schools? ▾
All three high schools — McKinney High (Lions), McKinney Boyd (Broncos), and McKinney North (Bulldogs) — offer AP courses, UIL 6A athletics, CTE pathways, and fine arts. The differences are in campus culture, feeder neighborhood demographics, and program maturity. McKinney High is the oldest with the longest-established traditions in fine arts and athletics. Boyd serves much of west McKinney's master-planned communities and has built a strong academic and athletic culture since opening in 2005. North is newer and growing with northern McKinney's development, with programs that are still building their depth. Which high school your address feeds should factor into your home search if a specific program matters to your family.
Which neighborhoods in McKinney feed which high school? ▾
Attendance zone boundaries are managed by McKinney ISD and are subject to change as new developments open and enrollment shifts. As a general guide: McKinney High School serves central and southern McKinney; McKinney Boyd serves western McKinney (including many of the established master-planned communities west of US-75); McKinney North serves newer neighborhoods in northern McKinney. For the authoritative zone map, use the attendance zone lookup at mckinneyisd.net or contact the district enrollment office. Never rely on neighborhood marketing materials to determine your ISD or high school zone — always verify directly with the district.
Why does McKinney ISD have a TEA B rating but a Niche A grade? ▾
TEA and Niche measure different things. TEA's B (score: 88) reflects STAAR performance data and equity metrics across all student subgroups — standardized, objective measures set by the state. Niche's A incorporates community satisfaction surveys, teacher quality reviews, diversity scores, extracurricular breadth, and parent ratings — qualitative inputs that tend to favor districts with strong community culture and engaged families. McKinney ISD scores well on community experience and program breadth, which drives the Niche A. The TEA B reflects the challenges of serving a large, diverse, rapidly growing city where equity gaps are more pronounced than in smaller, more homogenous districts. Both ratings are accurate in what they measure.
Can I request a transfer to a different McKinney ISD campus than our assigned one? ▾
Intra-district transfers are possible but are granted at the district's discretion based on available capacity at the receiving campus. They are not guaranteed and must typically be renewed each year. The application process opens at a specific window, usually in spring for the following school year. If attending a specific campus is important to your family, the more reliable path is to verify that your home address falls within that campus's attendance zone before purchasing.
How does McKinney ISD handle students with IEPs transferring from out of state? ▾
Texas law requires McKinney ISD to either adopt your child's existing IEP from another state or convene an ARD (Admissions, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment. Do not wait — contact MISD's Special Education department as soon as you initiate enrollment and bring all prior IEP documents, evaluation reports, and eligibility records to your enrollment appointment. The earlier you make contact, the smoother the service transition will be. Section 504 transfers follow a similar process through the campus 504 Coordinator.
