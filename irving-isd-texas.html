Irving ISD at a Glance

45 Campuses

Irving ISD serves approximately 33,000 students across 45 campuses in the southern two-thirds of the city of Irving. The district is one of the more diverse in the Dallas–Fort Worth metro — and that diversity shows up in genuine programmatic depth. A Two-Way Dual Language pathway runs from elementary school all the way through MacArthur High School, a Fine Arts program recognized nationally by the NAMM Foundation for multiple years, and a Career and Technical Education curriculum described by the district as nationally recognized. For families relocating to Irving or the Las Colinas business corridor, Irving ISD is the most likely district — but not automatically so, which is the most important thing to understand before you start touring homes.

The honest tradeoff is the 2024 TEA C rating (score: 72). That number reflects a district still working to close academic achievement gaps across a student population with significant economic diversity — approximately 67% of students qualify for free or reduced-price lunch. The C doesn't tell the full story of any individual campus, and Irving ISD does have campuses that outperform the district average significantly. But families who are specifically prioritizing the highest aggregate academic rankings in DFW should know going in that Irving ISD isn't currently shortlisted for that — and should weigh that against the real advantages in programs, affordability, and location that it does offer.

One more thing every Irving buyer must know before making an offer: the city of Irving is served by three independent school districts. Irving ISD covers the southern two-thirds of the city. The northern third — including Valley Ranch and the western portions of Las Colinas — falls under Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD or Coppell ISD. A Las Colinas address does not automatically mean Irving ISD. Verify before you buy.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Want a K–12 Two-Way Dual Language pathway in English and Spanish
  • Prioritize Fine Arts — band, choir, visual arts, theater at a district-wide level
  • Are working in the Las Colinas business corridor and want to minimize commute
  • Value strong CTE pathways that connect high school to real-world career tracks
  • Want access to collegiate-level courses while still in high school (Singley Collegiate or South Irving Collegiate)

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • You are specifically prioritizing the highest TEA district rating in DFW — Irving ISD's 2024 C places it below the DFW average
  • You're buying in Valley Ranch or northern Las Colinas and assume those addresses feed to Irving ISD — they typically don't
  • You want a smaller, tighter-knit campus culture — with 45 campuses and large high school enrollments, the experience is more urban than suburban
  • You want to be in a district with a lower overall free/reduced-lunch rate for peer-context reasons
⚠ Boundary Verification — Critical for Las Colinas Buyers

Not every Irving address is zoned to Irving ISD. The city is served by three school districts: Irving ISD (southern two-thirds), Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD (Valley Ranch and western Las Colinas), and Coppell ISD (select Las Colinas communities). Always verify your specific address using the district's campus locator at irvingisd.net/registration or the TEA School District Locator before making an offer.

TEA Accountability Rating

Irving ISD received a C (score: 72) on the 2024 Texas Education Agency A–F Accountability ratings — the most recently available rating at the time of publication. TEA rates districts and campuses across three primary domains: Student Achievement, School Progress, and Closing the Gaps. Each domain measures a different slice of how the district is serving students.

Student Achievement measures how many students meet or exceed grade-level expectations on STAAR, graduation rates, and college/career/military readiness (CCMR). School Progress looks at how much students improved year over year relative to peers with similar starting points. Closing the Gaps focuses specifically on whether the district is narrowing performance differences across student subgroups.

For a family trying to interpret a C at the district level: the aggregate score reflects the full 33,000-student population, which includes a significant share of English learners and economically disadvantaged students — groups that add complexity to the accountability calculation. Individual campuses vary widely within any large, diverse district. A campus serving a specific neighborhood may perform substantially above or below the district average. The district rating sets context; the campus rating matters more when you're choosing a home.

What This Means for Your Home Search

Use the Irving ISD 2024 C rating as a starting point, not a verdict. Check the specific campus ratings for the elementary, middle, and high school that would serve your address on txschools.gov. You'll find real variation across Irving ISD campuses — some rate significantly higher than the district average.

Academics & College Readiness

Irving ISD's 2024 graduation rate is approximately 92.3% — above the national average of 86.5%. The district offers AP coursework at all three comprehensive high schools. AP participation rates at the high school level range from 55% (Nimitz High School) to 62% (Irving High School), indicating that a meaningful share of students are attempting college-level coursework. Irving ISD also offers dual credit and CTE pathways as alternative routes to post-secondary readiness.

The district's College, Career, and Military Readiness (CCMR) rates — which TEA uses to measure the share of graduates meeting at least one post-secondary indicator — are worth verifying on txschools.gov for the most current year, as these shift annually. Families prioritizing college-prep depth should research individual campus data: MacArthur High School hosts the district's Two-Way Dual Language honors pathway, where students in the program take honors and AP courses and can earn the Irving ISD Dual Language Letter of Completion, recognized by colleges and employers.

The practical reality for a relocating family: Irving ISD provides adequate college preparation for motivated students, particularly through its AP, dual credit, and collegiate academy programs. Academic support quality varies by campus. If your child is an advanced or highly gifted learner who needs a structured, district-wide gifted and talented environment — rather than AP access at a single campus — research which specific campus serves your address and what that campus's GT program looks like.

Notable Campuses

Irving ISD operates three comprehensive high schools and multiple specialized and choice campuses. Below are the campuses most relevant to families evaluating the district.

MacArthur High School

High School Grades 9–12 Dual Language Pathway

MacArthur hosts Irving ISD's flagship Two-Way Dual Language secondary program, where bilingual students take honors and AP coursework in both English and Spanish. It also offers Project Lead The Way (PLTW) engineering and a Gifted & Talented program. Known locally as "Mac High," it is the district's most program-rich comprehensive high school. Source: Irving ISD, U.S. News.

Nimitz High School

High School ~2,380 students Collegiate Academy

Nimitz hosts the South Irving Collegiate program, allowing high school students to pursue an associate degree while still enrolled in high school — one of the more substantive dual-enrollment models in the area. AP participation rate is approximately 55%. Source: Irving ISD, U.S. News, Texas Tribune.

Irving High School

High School Grades 9–12 AP Participation: 62%

Irving High School has the highest AP participation rate of the three comprehensive high schools at approximately 62%, with broad course offerings in CTE and Fine Arts. Source: U.S. News, Irving ISD.

Singley Collegiate Academy

High School / Collegiate College Degree in HS

Singley Collegiate is a Schools of Choice campus where qualifying students can earn a college degree concurrently with their high school diploma. This is a competitive application program — not available to all students by address. Families interested in this pathway should apply through Irving ISD's Schools of Choice process. Source: Irving ISD.

Specialty Programs

Two-Way Dual Language (K–12)

District-wide program, campus-limited at each level. Irving ISD operates one of the few DFW-area K–12 Dual Language continuum programs. The Two-Way model develops bilingualism and biliteracy in English and Spanish. Elementary campuses offering the program include Brandenburg, Farine, and T. Haley (grades 4–5). The pathway continues through Travis Middle School and culminates at MacArthur High School, where students may earn the Irving ISD Dual Language Letter of Completion. Home-search implication: Families specifically targeting the Dual Language pathway should confirm their home address feeds to a participating elementary campus — not all Irving ISD elementaries offer the program. Transportation is typically parent-provided to choice campuses. Verify at irvingisd.net.

Fine Arts — NAMM Recognized District

Districtwide program, strong at the campus level. Irving ISD has been named a Best Community for Music Education by the NAMM Foundation for multiple years and has been recognized as a District of Distinction by the Texas Art Education Association for three consecutive years. Fine arts offerings include band, choir, orchestra, visual arts, theater, and dance across multiple campuses. This is a genuine districtwide commitment, not a single-campus outlier. Families for whom Fine Arts is a priority will find Irving ISD unusually well-resourced relative to its TEA rating.

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

Districtwide at the high school level. Irving ISD's CTE program is described by the district as nationally recognized, with pathways beginning in middle school and continuing through 9th–12th grade. Pathways include engineering (via PLTW at MacArthur), health sciences, business, hospitality, and more. Hands-on learning, internships, and job shadowing are built into the model. No application required — CTE courses are available to all high school students by default. Source: Irving ISD.

Gifted & Talented (GT)

Campus-level program, testing required. Irving ISD offers GT services at the elementary and secondary levels. GT identification involves a referral and multi-criteria assessment process. At the high school level, GT is typically accessed through the honors and AP course pathway rather than a standalone GT program. Families with identified GT students should contact the district's GT coordinator to understand what services are available at the specific campus serving their address. Source: Irving ISD.

Collegiate Academies

Application-required, campus-limited. Irving ISD offers two collegiate pathways: Singley Collegiate Academy and South Irving Collegiate at Nimitz. Both allow high school students to earn an associate degree concurrently. These are competitive, application-based programs — not guaranteed by address. Families interested in a collegiate pathway should apply during the Schools of Choice enrollment window. Source: Irving ISD.

Schools of Choice (Middle & High School)

Application-required, magnet-style programs. Irving ISD's Schools of Choice portfolio gives students access to specialized campuses beyond their attendance-zone assignment. Options include performing arts, STEM, and collegiate tracks. Applications are accepted during an annual window. Enrollment in choice programs is not guaranteed and may involve waitlists. Source: Irving ISD.

Enrollment Guide

Irving ISD enrollment for new students follows this general process. Always verify current details on irvingisd.net/registration for the 2025–26 school year, as windows and requirements are updated annually.

Step 1 — Verify Your Address

Before anything else, confirm which Irving ISD campus serves your address. Use the campus locator on the Irving ISD registration page or the TEA School District Locator. Remember: not all Irving addresses fall within Irving ISD boundaries (see Boundary section below).

Step 2 — Gather Required Documents

You will need to provide proof of residency (lease, mortgage statement, or utility bill in your name with your Irving address), proof of the child's age (birth certificate or passport), immunization records (Texas requires specific vaccinations for school enrollment), and a withdrawal form or records from the previous school.

Step 3 — Complete Online Registration

Irving ISD uses an online registration portal at irvingisd.net/registration. New families create an account, complete the enrollment form, and upload required documents. The campus office will schedule an in-person appointment to finalize enrollment and assign the student to a class.

Step 4 — Intra-District Transfers

Students who wish to attend a campus other than their assigned attendance-zone school may apply for an intra-district transfer. Transfers are approved based on campus capacity, available space, and the student's disciplinary record. Transfer approval is not guaranteed and must be renewed annually. The Schools of Choice application process runs separately and has its own enrollment window. Contact the district's Student Services office for current transfer guidelines.

Step 5 — Inter-District Transfers

If you live outside Irving ISD boundaries and wish to attend Irving ISD, inter-district transfers require approval from both your home district and Irving ISD. These are typically reviewed on a space-available basis and are not guaranteed.

Step 6 — Special Education & IEP Transfers

Under Texas law, if your child has an active IEP or Section 504 plan, Irving ISD must hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment to review and accept or adapt the existing plan. Bring all prior documentation, including the current IEP and any evaluation reports. Contact Irving ISD's Special Services department early in your relocation process — before you finalize your home — to understand what services are available at the campus serving your address and whether the existing plan can be honored as written. Source: Texas Education Code §29.005.

Boundary Verification

This is the most important section on this page for families relocating to Irving. The city of Irving is divided among three independent school districts, and the split is not intuitive when you're searching for a home online.

Which Parts of Irving Fall in Irving ISD?

Irving ISD serves the southern two-thirds of the city of Irving. This includes much of the core city neighborhoods south of SH 114, the southern Las Colinas area, and areas along Loop 12. It does not include Valley Ranch, most of the area north of SH 183 near Las Colinas, or the communities along the northern edge of Irving near the Coppell and Carrollton borders.

The Northern Third — Two Other Districts

Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD (CFBISD) serves Valley Ranch and the western portions of Las Colinas, including areas along Northgate Drive and the MacArthur Boulevard corridor in the northern part of the city. CFBISD also serves parts of the Hackberry Creek area. Coppell ISD serves selected Las Colinas communities in the northeastern portion of Irving, including some developments near the Coppell border. Both CFBISD and Coppell ISD are rated higher than Irving ISD on TEA accountability, and home values in those attendance zones reflect it.

Common Edge Cases

Several communities that carry a Las Colinas address — a corporate address rather than a municipal one — can fall into any of the three districts depending on the exact street and subdivision. Neighborhoods and master-planned communities straddling the northern boundary are particularly unpredictable. A home listed as "Las Colinas" on Zillow or HAR may be in Irving ISD, CFBISD, or Coppell ISD. The listing agent's stated school district is not always correct.

New Construction

Several new construction developments exist in the northern Irving area. New communities in Las Colinas should be treated as unverified for school district assignment until you confirm with the district directly — not with the builder's sales representative, who may or may not have accurate boundary knowledge.

How to Verify

The most reliable verification methods are: (1) the Irving ISD address lookup tool on their registration page, (2) the TEA School District Locator, or (3) calling Irving ISD's Student Services department directly at the number on irvingisd.net.

Home-Search Implication

Making an offer on a Las Colinas or northern Irving home without verifying the school district can mean your child ends up in a different district — and a very different academic environment — than you planned. Boundary maps do not always match what common-sense geography suggests. If the school district matters to your decision, verify the specific address before going under contract, not after.

Taxes & Finances

Irving ISD's 2024 certified total property tax rate is approximately $1.17 per $100 of assessed value (verify the current certified rate at the Texas Comptroller's website, as rates are adopted annually in August–September). For a home assessed at $450,000, that translates to roughly $5,265 per year in ISD-portion property taxes, or approximately $439 per month — before applying the Texas homestead exemption.

Texas homestead exemption: Texas offers a $100,000 homestead exemption on school district taxes for your primary residence. Applied to a $450,000 home, your taxable value drops to $350,000 — reducing ISD taxes to roughly $4,095 per year ($341/month). This is a meaningful saving and applies automatically after you file. New homeowners should file the exemption with the Dallas Central Appraisal District (DCAD) promptly after purchase.

Irving ISD's $1.17 rate sits in the mid-range for Dallas County districts — below the DFW average for comparable urban districts. For context: Coppell ISD, which serves some overlapping Irving addresses, has historically carried a similar or slightly higher rate with a significantly higher TEA accountability score. CFBISD, which serves Valley Ranch, has also carried comparable rates. The tax rate alone should not determine school district choice, but it should be factored into your monthly payment calculations alongside the per-district academic quality you're evaluating.

Irving ISD's per-pupil expenditure is approximately $12,870 per student — about 10% below the national average of $14,347. This is a notable figure for a district serving a high proportion of economically disadvantaged students, where federal funding supplements typically increase per-pupil spend. Families evaluating district resource levels should cross-reference the Texas Comptroller's Public Finance data for the most current figures.

How Irving ISD Compares

Families evaluating homes in the Irving area most commonly cross-shop Irving ISD against Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD (CFBISD) and Coppell ISD — both of which physically overlap with Irving city limits in the northern portion of the city.

Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD ~25,000 B ~$1.13 Valley Ranch, slightly higher academic ratings, similar programs
Coppell ISD ~12,900 A ~$1.15 Highest academic ratings in the area, smaller district, premium home prices

Families who specifically buy in Valley Ranch are typically in CFBISD — a district with a B rating, solid programs, and a similar tax rate. Families in the northeast Las Colinas pockets may find themselves in Coppell ISD, which carries an A rating and the premium home prices that often accompany it. Irving ISD's competitive advantage is its Dual Language K–12 pathway and Fine Arts depth — two areas where it outperforms both CFBISD and Coppell ISD in terms of program scale and recognition. Families who don't need those specific programs may find CFBISD or Coppell ISD a better overall academic fit for the same geographic area.

Community Feel

Irving ISD families describe a district with strong pockets of parent engagement — particularly in Fine Arts booster organizations, dual language parent groups, and CTE career fairs — alongside the realistic complexity of serving a large, economically diverse, multilingual student population. The district's Fine Arts reputation is genuine and tends to attract families who prioritize the arts in ways that some of the more "academic ranking" focused DFW suburbs don't. If your family values a school culture where a kid in band, theater, or choir is celebrated at the same level as the football team, Irving ISD schools — particularly at the MacArthur and Nimitz campuses — tend to reflect that culture.

On Reddit and community forums, current Irving ISD families often acknowledge the gap between the district's state accountability rating and their individual campus experience. A common theme: families who researched individual campuses carefully and chose based on program fit (Dual Language, Fine Arts, CTE) tend to be more satisfied than those who assumed the district-level C rating told the complete story. Campus selection matters more in Irving ISD than in a uniformly higher-rated district where any campus is likely to meet your needs.

One genuinely unique aspect of Irving ISD: the Las Colinas business corridor — home to major corporate campuses including ExxonMobil, Celanese, and financial services firms — puts a meaningful share of Irving ISD families in a corporate-relocation demographic. These are often dual-income professional families who moved for a job and are navigating the school district decision under a 60-day timeline. That dynamic shapes the community in some areas: active PTAs, parent-driven enrichment programs, and a level of family engagement that doesn't always show up in aggregate district statistics.

Frequently Asked Questions

My home is in Las Colinas. Does that mean it's automatically in Irving ISD? ▾
Not necessarily. Las Colinas is a corporate district address that spans multiple school district boundaries. The northern portions of Las Colinas — including the Valley Ranch area and communities near the MacArthur Boulevard and Northgate Drive corridors — fall under Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD. Some communities in the northeastern Las Colinas area near the Coppell border are served by Coppell ISD. Irving ISD serves the southern and central Las Colinas areas. The only reliable way to confirm is to use the Irving ISD address lookup at irvingisd.net/registration or the TEA School District Locator at tea.texas.gov before making an offer.
What's the difference between the three high schools in Irving ISD? ▾
Irving ISD has three comprehensive high schools. MacArthur High School (Mac High) is the most program-dense: it hosts the Two-Way Dual Language secondary pathway, PLTW engineering, and strong Gifted & Talented offerings. Nimitz High School hosts the South Irving Collegiate program, where students can earn an associate degree while still in high school, and carries an AP participation rate of about 55%. Irving High School has the highest AP participation rate of the three at approximately 62%, with strong CTE and Fine Arts offerings. All three serve large student populations — enrollment runs from roughly 2,000 to 2,500 students per campus. Which high school serves your address depends entirely on your street location within the district; verify with Irving ISD directly.
How does Irving ISD handle special education and IEP transfers? ▾
Under Texas law, when a student with an active IEP or Section 504 plan enrolls in a new district, the receiving district must hold an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days to review the existing plan and determine how to honor or adapt it. Irving ISD follows this requirement. Bring all prior documentation — including the current IEP, most recent evaluation, and any related service records — to enrollment. Contact Irving ISD's Special Services office early in your search process, ideally before you commit to a neighborhood, to understand what services are available at the specific campus that would serve your address. Parent experience with IEP implementation varies by campus and student needs; direct conversations with the Special Services team are the most reliable way to assess fit for your child's specific situation.
Why does Irving ISD have a C from TEA but a B− from Niche? Which should I trust? ▾
The two systems measure different things. TEA's A–F rating is based on Texas-specific criteria: STAAR test scores, graduation rates, and whether the district is closing academic gaps between student subgroups. It is a standardized, state-administered accountability system. Niche's grade blends multiple factors — academic performance, teacher quality, resources, extracurriculars, and community data, including some survey-based elements — and often produces different results than TEA. Neither is wrong; they're measuring different aspects of school quality. For a family making a housing decision, the TEA rating is more useful for comparing districts on academic performance metrics. The Niche grade adds context about school experience. In Irving ISD's case, the gap may partly reflect the district's genuine strength in non-academic programs (Fine Arts, CTE) that Niche weighs but TEA does not.
Can my child access the Dual Language program if my address isn't in a participating elementary school zone? ▾
Possibly, through Irving ISD's intra-district transfer process. The Two-Way Dual Language program is offered at specific elementary campuses (including Brandenburg, Farine, and T. Haley for upper elementary grades). If your attendance-zone elementary doesn't offer the program, you may apply for an intra-district transfer to a participating campus. Transfer approval depends on available space and is not guaranteed. Transportation to choice campuses is typically parent-provided. Applications for Schools of Choice and transfers are accepted during an annual enrollment window. Contact Irving ISD's Student Services office for the current-year application timeline and space availability at the Dual Language campuses.
How does Irving ISD's $1.17 tax rate compare to neighboring districts? ▾
Irving ISD's approximately $1.17 per $100 assessed value (2024 certified rate — verify current rate at comptroller.texas.gov) is in the mid-range for the area. Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD has historically carried a rate near $1.13. Coppell ISD, which serves some Irving addresses and has a significantly higher TEA rating, has typically been in the $1.13–$1.15 range. Grand Prairie ISD, to the south, is comparable at approximately $1.18. On a $450,000 home (before the $100,000 Texas homestead exemption), Irving ISD's rate translates to roughly $4,095 per year in school taxes. Rates are set annually in August–September — always verify the current certified rate before closing on a home.
Irving ISD has won Fine Arts recognition — what does that look like in practice for a student? ▾
Irving ISD's NAMM "Best Community for Music Education" recognition and Texas Art Education Association "District of Distinction" designation reflect a genuine district-wide investment in Fine Arts, not just a single standout campus. In practice, this means band, choir, orchestra, visual arts, theater, and dance are offered with above-average resources across multiple campuses. High school marching bands compete at a serious level; theater programs regularly advance to UIL competitions; visual arts students submit nationally recognized portfolios. For a family relocating with a child who is serious about the arts — or who might become serious with the right environment — Irving ISD is worth shortlisting on this dimension even if the TEA rating gives pause. The arts culture is real and district-supported, not an accident of one exceptional director.
