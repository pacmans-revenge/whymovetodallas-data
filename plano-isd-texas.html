District Overview

Plano Independent School District is one of the most academically elite large school districts in Texas — and one of the most misunderstood. With nearly 49,000 students across 74 campuses, PISD serves a broad area that includes the vast majority of the city of Plano as well as portions of Allen, Richardson, Murphy, and far North Dallas. The district's administrative offices are located at 2700 W. 15th Street, Plano, TX 75075.

PISD's defining characteristic is its college preparation culture. Three separate high schools — Plano Senior, Plano East, and Plano West — are all authorized IB World Schools offering the IB Diploma Programme. No other DFW district comes close to offering IB at that scale. The district also hosts the Science/Engineering Magnet (SEM) at Plano Senior — one of the most selective STEM magnet programs in North Texas, open by competitive application to any PISD student.

As with all large Texas districts, a Plano city address does not automatically guarantee PISD enrollment, and homes in adjacent cities like Richardson or Murphy may fall within PISD boundaries. Always verify the specific address using the TEA School District Locator or PISD's address lookup tool before making an offer.

Key Cities Served

Plano (primary service area), portions of Allen, Richardson, Murphy, and far North Dallas. Boundaries are irregular — verify every address individually.

TEA Accountability Rating

The Texas Education Agency assigns every school district an annual A–F accountability rating across three domains. Plano ISD earned a TEA B rating (score: 82 out of 100) for the 2024–25 school year.

Context Matters

A score of 82 at a district of nearly 49,000 students — producing 43.4% AP/IB participation and more National Merit Scholars than virtually any district in Texas — tells a more nuanced story than the letter grade alone. Scale depresses aggregate TEA scores; districts with this many students face statistical headwinds that smaller, selective districts never encounter. PISD's academic output at the upper end of the achievement spectrum is extraordinary.

What the three domains measure:

  • Student Achievement — STAAR test scores, graduation rates, college/career readiness indicators
  • School Progress — year-over-year academic growth compared to similar campuses
  • Closing the Gaps — performance equity across student demographic groups

Families evaluating PISD on letter grade alone risk misreading the district's actual performance at the level that matters most for college-bound students. The district's AP/IB participation rate of 43.4% — among the highest for any large Texas district — and its National Merit Scholar production tell a story the B rating does not fully capture. Campus-level ratings for all 74 campuses are available at txschools.gov.

Graduation Rate

Plano ISD's four-year graduation rate is approximately 96% (TEA four-year longitudinal rate), consistently above state and national averages. Nearly all graduating seniors complete at least one AP or IB exam, dual credit course, or other college-readiness indicator before earning their diploma.

Advanced Placement & International Baccalaureate

Plano ISD's 43.4% AP/IB participation rate is exceptionally high for a district of its size. That figure means nearly half of all PISD students are engaged in college-level coursework while in high school — a ceiling most districts in Texas never approach. The IB Diploma Programme is offered at all three comprehensive high schools, creating access at a scale unmatched anywhere in DFW.

IB Access Unmatched in DFW

Plano ISD has IB Diploma Programmes at THREE separate high schools — Plano Senior, Plano East, and Plano West. A level of IB access unmatched in DFW. Families prioritizing the IB track have three campus options rather than one, giving far more geographic flexibility when choosing a home address.

National Merit Scholars

Plano ISD consistently produces more National Merit Scholarship semifinalists and finalists than virtually any other district in Texas. This is driven by the concentration of high-achieving students in the district, the depth of AP and IB coursework available, and the culture of academic competition across all three high schools. For families who track PSAT/NMSQT outcomes, PISD is among the top districts in the country — not just Texas.

College & Career Readiness

PISD tracks "College, Career, and Military Readiness" (CCMR) as required by TEA. Indicators include AP/IB exam scores of 3 or above, dual enrollment completions, and industry certifications through CTE programs. The district's CCMR rate is among the highest for large Texas districts. Per-pupil expenditure of $16,882 — the highest among the major DFW districts profiled on this site — reflects significant investment in instructional resources, staffing ratios, and program depth.

STAAR Proficiency

District-wide STAAR proficiency rates are available by campus at txschools.gov. PISD consistently scores above state averages across tested subjects. Campus-level variation exists — research the elementary school assigned to your specific address, not just district aggregates.

Campus Highlights

Plano ISD operates three comprehensive high schools, one alternative campus, multiple junior high schools, and more than 50 elementary campuses. All three comprehensive high schools are IB World Schools — an unusual distinction that gives the entire district a college-prep orientation regardless of which campus a student attends.

Elementary Campuses

Special Programs

IB Diploma Programme (3 Campuses)

All three PISD comprehensive high schools — Plano Senior, Plano East, and Plano West — are authorized IB World Schools. The IB Diploma Programme is available at each campus for zoned students. No other DFW district offers IB at three separate high schools simultaneously.

Science/Engineering Magnet (SEM)

Hosted at Plano Senior High School, SEM is one of the most competitive STEM magnet programs in North Texas. Open by application to any PISD student regardless of home address. Application windows typically run November–January for the following fall. Priority is given to students who apply on time.

Gifted & Talented (GT)

One of the most developed GT programs in Texas, with K–12 GT identification and services. Students are identified through a multi-criteria process. Elementary GT pull-out enrichment progresses to advanced coursework at the secondary level. Families transferring with a GT-identified child should bring all prior assessment documentation.

Dual Language Immersion

PISD offers two Dual Language Immersion tracks at the elementary level: Spanish/English and Mandarin/English. Programs run from Kindergarten through elementary, with application required. A limited number of seats are available — apply early, as demand consistently exceeds capacity.

Advanced Placement (AP)

All three high schools offer 30+ AP courses per campus. AP participation rates are among the highest in Texas for a district of this size. Dual credit partnerships allow eligible students to earn college credit simultaneously — contact PISD's high school counselors for current partnership institutions.

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

PISD's CTE pathways span technology, health science, business, engineering, and fine arts. Students can earn industry-recognized certifications alongside their high school diploma. Programs include DECA, FBLA, HOSA, and SkillsUSA chapters at all three high schools.

Special Education & Support Services

Plano ISD provides special education and related services to eligible students ages 3–21 under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Services span the full continuum from inclusion in general education classrooms to specialized campus placements, determined by each student's Individualized Education Program (IEP).

Given PISD's size — nearly 49,000 students — the district maintains specialized centers that many smaller districts cannot offer. These include dedicated programs for students with autism spectrum disorder, hearing impairment, visual impairment, and those who are significantly cognitively disabled. Families with children who require high-support or low-incidence disability services will find PISD's continuum unusually comprehensive for a suburban district.

Support services include speech-language therapy, occupational therapy, physical therapy, ARD (Admissions, Review, and Dismissal) facilitation, behavioral support, assistive technology, and transition planning for secondary students. For Section 504 accommodations, contact the campus 504 Coordinator. PISD also provides dyslexia testing and structured literacy intervention at all campuses.

Families relocating with a student who has an existing IEP from another state should contact PISD's Special Education department immediately upon enrollment. Texas law requires the district to either adopt the current IEP or convene a new ARD meeting within 30 days of enrollment. Bring all prior IEP, evaluation, and eligibility documentation to the enrollment appointment.

Athletics & Extracurriculars

Plano ISD competes in the University Interscholastic League (UIL) at Class 6A — the highest UIL classification — across all three comprehensive high schools. All three programs are nationally competitive across multiple sports, with football, basketball, soccer, swimming, track and field, and cross country regularly producing UIL state qualifiers and champions.

The three high schools have a long-established athletic culture. Plano Senior's football program is one of the most decorated in Texas history. All three campuses have dedicated athletic complexes and participate in the 6A DFW-area district classification, competing against the largest programs in the state.

Beyond athletics, PISD offers a full range of UIL competitive activities including Academic Decathlon, One Act Play, Debate, Science Olympiad, and Math Olympiad. Fine arts programming at all three high schools includes orchestra, band, choir, visual arts, and theatre — all competing at the 6A level. Multiple PISD fine arts programs regularly advance to UIL State competitions.

Each high school campus typically supports 50+ student clubs including National Honor Society chapters, specialty clubs aligned to CTE pathways (DECA, FBLA, HOSA, SkillsUSA), and student government. The depth of extracurricular options at all three campuses is comparable to what families might expect from a small college environment.

School Choice Within the District

Attendance Zones

Plano ISD is primarily attendance-zone based at the elementary, junior high, and high school levels. Your home address determines your assigned campus for all three tiers. Families who prioritize a specific high school — particularly IB at a particular campus — should verify their address assignment before purchasing or leasing a home.

Science/Engineering Magnet (SEM) — District-Wide Application

The SEM at Plano Senior High School is the district's flagship school-choice option. Any PISD student, regardless of home address, can apply. The application window typically runs November through January for the following fall semester. If attending SEM is a family priority, submit the application as early as possible — seats are limited and competition is significant. Students not accepted can remain at their address-zoned high school with full access to AP and IB coursework.

IB Programmes — Campus-Based

The IB Diploma Programme at Plano Senior, Plano East, and Plano West is available to students zoned to each respective campus. Unlike SEM, IB is not a separate application magnet — students at an IB World School campus can self-select into the IB track. If IB at a specific campus is a priority, choose a home address that feeds to that school. Given three IB campuses, most Plano addresses already guarantee access to an IB programme.

Dual Language Immersion — Elementary Application

DLI programs in Spanish/English and Mandarin/English at the elementary level require a separate application, with application windows typically opening in the fall for the following academic year. Seats are limited. Contact PISD's Curriculum & Instruction department for current program locations and application timing.

Intra-District Transfers

Intra-district transfer requests (to a different campus than the address-assigned campus, outside of SEM or DLI) are subject to campus capacity and are not guaranteed. Transfers must be renewed annually and can be revoked if enrollment caps are reached at the receiving campus.

District Finances

Tax Rate

Plano ISD's 2024 certified total tax rate is $1.04 per $100 of assessed value (Texas Comptroller). This rate is among the most moderate for high-performing DFW ISDs, reflecting the district's established tax base and relatively stable enrollment compared to rapidly growing districts that carry heavier bond debt service. Plano ISD properties are appraised by the Collin County Appraisal District.

For a home assessed at $700,000, the PISD school district tax levy is approximately $7,280 per year at this rate, before exemptions. Texas homeowners with a primary homestead exemption receive a $100,000 reduction in assessed value for school district taxes (per HB 3, effective 2023), lowering the effective taxable value to $600,000 and the annual levy to approximately $6,240. County and city taxes are assessed separately and vary by location within the district.

Per-Pupil Expenditure

Plano ISD's per-pupil expenditure of $16,882 is the highest among the major DFW school districts profiled on this site. This reflects meaningful investment in instructional resources, program depth, staffing ratios, and student services — the financial foundation behind the district's AP/IB participation rate and specialized support services. Current data is available through the TEA's PEIMS financial reporting system at txschools.gov.

TEA FIRST Rating

Plano ISD holds a Texas FIRST Superior rating from the Texas Education Agency — the highest classification under the Financial Integrity Rating System of Texas. A Superior FIRST rating indicates sound financial management, adequate reserves, and strong internal controls. This rating is independent of the academic A–F system and speaks to the district's long-term fiscal health.

Enrollment — New Families

How to Enroll

New student enrollment for Plano ISD is completed online through the district's enrollment portal at pisd.edu. Required documents include:

  • Proof of residency — lease agreement or deed/closing disclosure (two documents may be required)
  • Birth certificate — for first-time Texas enrollees
  • Immunization records — Texas-required vaccines (Tdap, meningococcal, and others per DSHS schedule)
  • Previous school records — transcript, IEP if applicable, GT documentation, standardized test scores
  • Proof of custody — if applicable

SEM and DLI Application Timing

If the Science/Engineering Magnet or Dual Language Immersion program is a priority for your family, plan around their respective application windows. SEM applications typically open in November and close in January for the following fall. DLI applications also typically open in fall for the following year. Missing these windows means waiting until the next cycle — which may affect your home-purchase timeline if SEM or DLI is a key decision driver.

Mid-Year Transfers

PISD accepts mid-year enrollments year-round. Families relocating with an offer letter but not yet a signed lease may contact PISD's enrollment department to understand timing options. Initiate enrollment within the first week of establishing Texas residency to avoid delays in campus placement.

GT Identification for Transfer Students

Families arriving with a GT-identified student should bring all prior assessment documentation. PISD's GT department will review previous records as part of its multi-criteria identification process. New assessments may be required. The process typically takes 4–6 weeks from enrollment to placement decision.

Frequently Asked Questions

My address says "Plano, TX" — does that mean I'm automatically in Plano ISD? ▾
Not necessarily. PISD's boundary does not align perfectly with the city of Plano's city limits. Some Plano addresses fall within Frisco ISD, Allen ISD, or Richardson ISD. Conversely, homes in Richardson, Murphy, and portions of far North Dallas may be zoned to Plano ISD. Always verify your specific address using the TEA School District Locator or PISD's address lookup at pisd.edu before making an offer.
Which Plano ISD high school will my child attend — and how do I know? ▾
Your assigned high school is determined by your home address. PISD has three comprehensive high schools — Plano Senior, Plano East, and Plano West — each serving different geographic areas of the district. Use PISD's campus locator at pisd.edu or contact the enrollment department with your specific address to confirm which high school and feeder junior high are assigned. If you have a strong preference for a particular campus or its specific programming, verify the feeder pattern before signing a lease or making an offer.
How does the Science/Engineering Magnet (SEM) application process work? ▾
SEM at Plano Senior High School is a district-wide magnet program open to any PISD student by competitive application — your home address does not determine eligibility. Applications typically open in November and close in January for the following fall semester. The selection process is competitive and includes academic criteria. If accepted, students attend Plano Senior regardless of their address-assigned high school. If not accepted, students attend their address-zoned campus and have full access to AP courses and the IB programme at that campus.
All three high schools offer IB — what does that actually mean for my family? ▾
It means that for most families buying a home anywhere in Plano ISD, access to an IB Diploma Programme is effectively guaranteed — regardless of which high school your address feeds to. You do not need to engineer your home purchase around a single IB campus the way families in other DFW districts sometimes must. The IB track at each campus is available to students who are enrolled at that school and choose to pursue it. It is not a separate application magnet (unlike SEM) — students opt in at the campus level.
What does the highest per-pupil expenditure actually mean for my child's education? ▾
Plano ISD's per-pupil expenditure of $16,882 is the highest among major DFW districts. In practical terms, this translates to: more instructional staff per student, deeper program offerings (particularly for GT and special education), access to resources like dedicated SEM facilities, and a broader extracurricular and arts infrastructure. Higher per-pupil spending doesn't guarantee outcomes, but it reflects the financial commitment behind the district's academic depth. Families comparing PISD to districts with lower per-pupil figures should factor this into the full cost-of-living calculation alongside tax rates.
Why does Plano ISD have a B rating if it produces so many National Merit Scholars? ▾
The TEA A–F system measures aggregate performance across all 49,000 students in all three domains: Student Achievement, School Progress, and Closing the Gaps. At a district of this scale and demographic diversity, the "Closing the Gaps" domain in particular can compress overall scores below what the top-end academic outputs suggest. The B rating reflects the full picture of a large, diverse district — not just its highest achievers. For families whose children are college-bound and likely to engage with AP, IB, or SEM, the district's performance at that tier is exceptional. The B rating is not a ceiling — it is an average across a very large and varied student population.
