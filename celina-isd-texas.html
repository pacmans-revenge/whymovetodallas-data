Celina ISD is one of the fastest-growing school districts in Texas — and that growth is both the story and the honest complication for relocating families. The district has earned a "B" (87/100) from the Texas Education Agency for four consecutive years through 2024, which is a genuine achievement for a system adding students and campuses as fast as Celina has grown. The honest tradeoff is that growing-pains are real: campus assignments shift as new schools open, and at least one campus (Martin Elementary) earned a "C" in the most recent accountability cycle. Families who want a small-town Texas identity, affordable new construction, and a district that has held its academic footing during explosive growth will find a lot to like here. Families whose top priority is maximum AP course breadth, an IB programme, or a large established high school will likely look at Prosper ISD or McKinney ISD first.

The district serves the city of Celina as well as new-construction communities in parts of southern Celina and eastern Anna. One critical note for buyers: not every home with a Celina address is zoned to Celina ISD. The 75009 ZIP code straddles both Celina ISD and Prosper ISD, and some of the most prominent new-construction communities in south Celina — including Light Farms and Sam Johnson Elementary's feeder area — are actually Prosper ISD territory. Always verify your specific address before making an offer.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Value a small-town Texas community identity over big-district name recognition
  • Want newer homes at a lower price point than Prosper or Frisco
  • Work remotely or have flexibility on commute (Celina is 35–45 min from the Legacy Corridor)
  • Are comfortable with a district actively building — campus configurations will evolve through 2027
  • Have a student who thrives in a smaller, well-known high school environment with strong athletics

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • Your top priority is an IB programme — Celina ISD does not offer one
  • You need maximum AP course variety and dual-enrollment access from multiple programs
  • You plan to commute daily to Plano, Frisco, or the Legacy Corridor and need the shortest drive
  • You are buying in south Celina new construction and have not yet verified your address in the district locator

TEA Accountability Rating

2024 overall rating: B (87/100) — Celina ISD's fourth consecutive "B" from the Texas Education Agency. All campuses received an A or B rating in 2024 except Martin Elementary, which received a C.

The TEA A–F system evaluates districts across three domains:

  • Student Achievement — measures grade-level mastery on STAAR and end-of-course assessments, graduation rates, and college/career/military readiness (CCMR). This is the most heavily weighted domain.
  • School Progress — evaluates year-over-year student growth on STAAR, rewarding districts where students are improving even if absolute scores are not yet at the top.
  • Closing the Gaps — examines performance across student groups, including economically disadvantaged students, English learners, and students with disabilities.

What an 87 means for families in Celina specifically: A "B" at 87 tells you the district is performing solidly on grade-level expectations and graduation outcomes — but not at the A-level of Prosper ISD (which scored 90+ in recent years). For a district that has been absorbing hundreds of new students per year while simultaneously building new campuses, maintaining an 87 four years running is more meaningful than the letter grade alone suggests. For most families relocating to Celina, the 87 is a reasonable academic floor. If your child's trajectory requires the highest-performing district available, compare against Prosper ISD's ratings before deciding.

Note on TEA Data Timing

TEA released both 2024 and 2025 accountability ratings in late 2025. The 2024 rating (B, 87) is the most recently confirmed published score referenced throughout this page. Always check txschools.gov for the most current published data, as ratings can lag the current school year by 12–18 months.

Academic Performance

Celina ISD's academic picture is best understood as a district that performs above its size and growth rate — not as one competing head-to-head with the largest suburban ISDs on breadth of advanced programming.

Graduation rate: Celina High School posted a 99.5% graduation rate in the 2022–23 school year (per district/TEA reporting), which exceeds the state average and outperforms many larger, more recognized DFW districts. A district this size producing a near-perfect graduation rate is a genuine data point — not just a talking point.

AP participation: The AP® participation rate at Celina High School is 47% (per GreatSchools, 2024–25 data). This means nearly half of CHS students are enrolled in at least one AP course, which is competitive for a high school of roughly 1,300 students. The AP course catalog is smaller than what you'd find at a 3,000-student high school, but motivated students have meaningful access to college-level work.

Dual credit: Celina ISD has a partnership with Collin College that allows high school students to earn college credit. Available pathways include Associate of Arts, Associate of Science, Associate of Applied Science, Teaching certifications, and a Certified Nurse Aide (CNA) track — providing real workforce and transfer options for students who want to graduate with hours already banked.

STAAR performance: Celina High School consistently outperforms state averages on STAAR End-of-Course assessments across Algebra I, Biology, English I and II Reading, and U.S. History (per district reporting). District-level STAAR proficiency data for elementary grades is updated annually by TEA and should be verified at txschools.gov for the most current school year.

What these numbers mean for your child: Celina ISD's academic profile is appropriate for the large majority of K–12 students — including those pursuing AP coursework, dual credit, and college-track graduation plans. Students targeting highly selective universities or specialized programs like IB, engineering academies, or performing arts conservatories will find a smaller menu here than at a 30,000-student district. Those factors matter for some families and are irrelevant for others; be clear about what your child needs before the comparison shopping begins.

Campus Highlights

Celina ISD currently operates 8 campuses: one early childhood school (Pre-K), four elementary schools (K–5), one middle school (Grades 6–8), one high school (Grades 9–12), and one additional campus opening for 2025–26. A second middle school, Willard Middle School, is expected to open in fall 2026. Important for buyers in 2025–26 and beyond: the district has announced a temporary grade reconfiguration beginning in 2026–27, with elementaries shifting to PK–4 and middle schools serving grades 5–6 and 7–8 for two transitional years before returning to the standard K–5 / 6–8 structure. A child starting elementary in 2026 may experience campus changes across the transition period. Verify current campus assignments at celinaisd.com/who-we-are/attendance-zones.

Specialty Programs

Celina ISD offers a focused set of academic programs appropriate for its size. The district does not have an IB programme or a dedicated STEM magnet school. What it does offer is real access to GT services, a growing CTE catalog, and a dual credit pathway that gives motivated high schoolers a meaningful head start.

Gifted & Talented (GT)

Districtwide, campus-based delivery. GT teachers are present on each campus. Identification occurs through referral windows in fall (results by November) and spring (results by April). Testing uses the CogAT and MAP assessments — students must meet 2 of 3 CogAT criteria as "very high" and 1 of 2 MAP criteria as "very high" to qualify. No application or lottery — the process starts with a teacher or parent referral. Home-search implication: GT services are available districtwide, so your campus assignment does not restrict access to identification — but the depth of GT enrichment can vary by campus staffing.

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

High school only — Celina High School. CTE pathways include Agriculture, Business, Audio/Visual Technology, Law Enforcement, Education, Health Science, and STEM. Admission is based on student interest, aptitude, age appropriateness, and class availability — no application or competitive selection. Home-search implication: CTE is centralized at the high school, so all students zoned to CHS have equal access regardless of where they live in the district.

Dual Credit — Collin College Partnership

High school only. Celina ISD has a formal dual credit agreement with Collin College. Students can earn college credit toward Associate of Arts, Associate of Science, Associate of Applied Science, an Associate of Teaching, or a Certified Nurse Aide (CNA) credential while still in high school. No transportation is typically required — courses are delivered on campus or online. Home-search implication: all students zoned to Celina High School can access this pathway; confirm current course offerings with the CHS counselor each year as the catalog evolves.

Project Lead The Way (PLTW)

High school only. Celina HS offers PLTW coursework, a nationally recognized STEM curriculum covering engineering, biomedical, and computer science pathways. PLTW is open enrollment — no application or testing required — making it accessible to students who discover an interest in STEM after arriving. Home-search implication: available to all CHS students; introductory STEM coursework is also offered at Moore Middle School to help students build familiarity before high school.

Advanced Placement (AP)

High school only — Celina High School. AP courses are offered across core subjects. The 47% AP participation rate (2024–25, per GreatSchools) is competitive for a school of roughly 1,300 students. The catalog is smaller than what a 2,000–3,000 student high school would offer — families with students targeting 10+ AP courses should confirm specific course availability with CHS directly, as offerings vary year to year based on enrollment and staffing.

Note: Celina ISD does not currently offer an International Baccalaureate (IB) programme, a dual language / bilingual immersion program, or a dedicated fine arts magnet. Families for whom any of these programs are a priority should verify availability directly with the district, as program offerings can expand over time in a fast-growing district.

How to Enroll in Celina ISD

Enrollment steps for the 2025–26 school year (verify current-year timelines at celinaisd.com/enrollment):

  1. Verify your address: Confirm your specific address is zoned to Celina ISD — not Prosper ISD — using the district's attendance zone tool at celinaisd.com/attendance-zones. Do this before enrolling; an incorrect assumption is one of the most common mistakes in south Celina new construction.
  2. Gather residency documents: Typical requirements include proof of residency (lease, mortgage, or utility bill), parent/guardian ID, the child's birth certificate, immunization records, and prior school records or transcripts. Specific document requirements are posted on the enrollment page each year.
  3. Complete online registration: Online enrollment is available at celinaisd.com/families-and-community/enrollment. Celina ISD uses a digital enrollment platform — paper packet pickup is not the standard process.
  4. Enrollment window: Open enrollment for new students typically begins in spring for the following fall school year. Check the district website each February for current-year open enrollment dates.
  5. Intra-district transfers: Families wishing to request a campus other than their attendance-zone campus submit a transfer request through the district. Approval is based on available space and is not guaranteed. Contact the district's Student Services office for current-year transfer policies.
  6. Inter-district transfers: Students from outside Celina ISD boundaries may apply for inter-district transfers subject to capacity. Approval is at the district's discretion and is not guaranteed. Current policies are maintained on the district website.
  7. IEP / 504 transfers: Texas law requires that a new district convene an ARD meeting within 30 days of enrollment to review and adjust any existing IEP. Bring all prior IEP, 504, evaluation, and ARD documentation with you to enrollment. Contact Celina ISD's Special Services department directly for guidance on the transition process and to discuss your child's specific needs before your start date.

Boundary Verification — Read This Before You Buy

Celina ISD's boundary situation is one of the most important things a relocating buyer needs to understand before making an offer on a home in the Celina area — and it is consistently underestimated by buyers who assume a city address equals a school district assignment.

The Core Issue: 75009 ZIP Code Straddles Two Districts

The 75009 ZIP code — which covers Celina, TX — includes addresses served by both Celina ISD and Prosper ISD. This is not an obscure edge case. Some of the most heavily marketed new-construction communities near Preston Road in southern Celina are zoned to Prosper ISD, not Celina ISD. Confirmed examples include:

  • Light Farms community (south Celina) — feeds to Light Farms Elementary, which is a Prosper ISD campus despite the Celina mailing address
  • Other south Celina developments near Preston Road — Sam Johnson Elementary, also a Prosper ISD campus, serves portions of 75009

If you are specifically targeting Celina ISD schools (or specifically targeting Prosper ISD schools), you cannot rely on the city name, ZIP code, or even the neighborhood name to determine your district. You must verify the specific street address.

How to Verify Your Address

  • Celina ISD attendance zone tool: celinaisd.com/who-we-are/attendance-zones
  • TEA school locator: tea.texas.gov/apps/school-directory
  • Prosper ISD boundary tool (if you're in south Celina): prosperisd.net

New Construction Communities — Verify Every Address Individually

As Celina continues to grow northward, new subdivision lots are being developed on the edges of both Celina ISD and Prosper ISD territory. Even within the same master-planned community, different phases or sections can feed to different districts as boundary lines are drawn. A builder's sales representative may not know the district assignment for a specific lot — or may inadvertently provide inaccurate information. Always pull the address through the district locator yourself.

Campus Assignment Changes Within Celina ISD

Even for addresses clearly within Celina ISD boundaries, campus assignments can change as the district opens new schools. The 5th elementary opened for 2025–26, and Willard Middle School opens in fall 2026. When new campuses open, the district redraws attendance zones — families who purchased based on an assigned campus may find their child redirected to a new school. This is normal for fast-growth districts and is not unusual for Celina. Verify current campus assignment directly with the district at the time of enrollment, not at the time of your home purchase.

Home-Search Implication

Making an offer on a home in the Celina area without verifying the specific address in the district locator can mean your child attends a different school — or a different district entirely — than you planned. Boundary maps do not always match common-sense geography, and builder community names are not a reliable proxy for district assignment. This is one of the most important pre-offer verification steps for buyers in this market.

Tax Rate & School Finance

2025 certified total tax rate: $1.2089 per $100 of assessed value. This represents a modest reduction from prior years — the Celina ISD board approved the $1.2089 rate in August 2025, described as just under a 3% reduction for the community (per Community Impact reporting).

What this means for your monthly payment: On a $500,000 home after Texas's $100,000 homestead exemption ($400,000 taxable value), the school district portion alone adds approximately $403/month ($400,000 × $1.2089 ÷ 100 ÷ 12). On a $600,000 home (taxable $500,000 after exemption), that figure is approximately $504/month. These are school district taxes only — city, county, and MUD taxes are additional and vary by community.

Comparison to neighboring districts (2025 rates): Celina ISD's $1.2089 rate is broadly comparable to peers in the north Collin County corridor. Prosper ISD operates at approximately $1.24/$100, and many surrounding districts sit between $1.20 and $1.26 (the state cap for most districts without a special voter approval). Grapevine-Colleyville ISD is the notable outlier in DFW at approximately $0.92/$100 — but it serves a very different geography. For buyers comparing Celina and Prosper specifically, the tax rate difference is small enough that it should not be the deciding factor.

Texas $100,000 homestead exemption: Texas law provides a mandatory $100,000 homestead exemption from school district taxes for owner-occupied primary residences, effective for the 2023 tax year forward. Apply through your county appraisal district after closing.

MUD districts: Many newer Celina subdivisions sit within a Municipal Utility District (MUD), which adds a separate tax on top of the school, city, and county rates — sometimes $0.30–$0.80/$100 or more depending on the MUD's bond status. Always ask about MUD tax rates for any specific subdivision you are considering. This can significantly affect total annual property tax.

Per-pupil expenditure: Specific per-pupil expenditure data for Celina ISD is updated annually by the TEA through PEIMS reporting. Verify current figures at txschools.gov — look for the Finance tab on the Celina ISD district report card.

How Celina ISD Compares

Celina ISD is most often cross-shopped against Prosper ISD (the neighboring district to the south and west) and occasionally against Anna ISD (to the east) or McKinney ISD (for buyers willing to look at more established suburbs).

District Size TEA Grade (2024) Tax Rate (2025) Best For
Celina ISD ~5,500 students B (87) $1.2089 Small-town identity, affordable new homes, community athletics
Prosper ISD ~33,000 students A (90+) ~$1.24 Families prioritizing top TEA rating, more AP/program variety, established infrastructure
Anna ISD ~5,500 students C (~75) ~$1.26 Budget-conscious buyers in far northeast Collin County; more modest price points
McKinney ISD ~26,000 students B ~$1.20 Families who want a large established district with multiple HS options and full program variety

The Celina vs. Prosper choice is the one that matters most for families in this corridor. Prosper ISD is larger, more established, and has consistently earned A-level TEA ratings — it is the choice for families who want the highest available accountability score and the widest program menu in the area. The tradeoff: homes in confirmed Prosper ISD territory (particularly those within Prosper ISD's core attendance zones) often carry a price premium over comparable homes clearly within Celina ISD. Celina ISD tends to attract families who value the small-town community feel and football culture, are comfortable with a district in an active growth phase, and want more home for their budget. Anna ISD serves families in far northeast Collin County at more accessible price points, but the C TEA rating and lower Niche scores place it behind both Celina and Prosper for families with strong academic priorities. McKinney ISD is worth considering for buyers who want a large established district with multiple high schools — though McKinney itself is further south and more developed than Celina.

Community Culture & What It Feels Like

Celina has one of the strongest small-town identities of any rapidly growing DFW suburb, and the schools are central to that identity. Friday night football is a genuine community event in Celina — the Celina Bobcats have won multiple state titles in their classification, and athletic pride permeates the district's culture. Families who want their children to grow up in a community where the school is the center of social life, where you'll see the same kids through 12 years of school together, and where parents are deeply involved in booster clubs and campus life will find exactly that in Celina ISD.

The honest complication is that rapid growth is changing the character of that tight-knit community faster than many long-time Celina residents expected. Neighborhoods that were rural and separated five years ago are now connected by development, and many new residents arrived in the last 2–3 years from other parts of Texas or from out of state. Parent forums and community discussions online reflect a mix of enthusiasm for the growth and genuine concern about whether the district can maintain its culture and quality as enrollment scales. For a family moving in today, the experience will vary depending on your subdivision, your children's campuses, and which direction the district continues to develop.

The district's community feedback — synthesized from local parent forums and real estate discussions — reflects consistent praise for the athletics program, the tight-knit feel at elementary campuses, and the staff's responsiveness. The most common criticism is around facilities and growing pains: parents in newer subdivisions sometimes describe feeling uncertain about which campus their child will ultimately attend as new schools open, and the C rating at Martin Elementary has prompted genuine parent concern about campus-level variation. Families moving in with a student entering middle or high school will generally find a more settled experience than those with elementary-age children who may experience a campus transition during the 2026–28 reconfiguration period.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does a Celina, TX address guarantee enrollment in Celina ISD? ▾
No — a Celina city address or 75009 ZIP code does not guarantee Celina ISD enrollment. The 75009 ZIP code spans both Celina ISD and Prosper ISD territory. Several prominent new-construction communities in south Celina near Preston Road — including Light Farms, where Light Farms Elementary and Sam Johnson Elementary serve students — are zoned to Prosper ISD despite having Celina mailing addresses. Always verify your specific street address using Celina ISD's attendance zone tool at celinaisd.com/who-we-are/attendance-zones before making a purchase offer. The TEA school locator (tea.texas.gov/apps/school-directory) provides a second verification point.
What is Celina ISD's current TEA rating? ▾
Celina ISD received a "B" (87 out of 100) from the Texas Education Agency in its 2024 accountability rating — the fourth consecutive year at that level. All campuses earned an A or B rating except Martin Elementary, which received a C in 2024. TEA releases new ratings annually, typically in late summer or fall. Check txschools.gov for the most current published rating, as data can lag 12–18 months behind the current school year. The 2025 ratings were released by TEA in late 2025.
Why does Niche give Celina ISD a B+ while TEA gives it an 87 (B)? What's the difference? ▾
These two systems measure different things. TEA's A–F accountability rating is a state-standardized assessment based on STAAR scores, graduation rates, and college/career/military readiness data. It is objective and uniform across all Texas districts. Niche is a third-party platform that blends standardized test scores with parent reviews, teacher ratings, administrative quality assessments, and neighborhood factors — all weighted by their own proprietary formula. A Niche B+ in the same neighborhood as a TEA B (87) is plausible because Niche may weight parent satisfaction or community factors more favorably for a small, community-focused district. Both are informative. Use TEA data as the primary objective standard, and Niche as supplementary context — especially for campus-level detail where TEA data can be sparse.
Does Celina ISD offer special education and IEP services? ▾
Yes, Celina ISD provides special education services, 504 accommodations, dyslexia support, and related services. Texas law requires that when a student with an existing IEP transfers to a new Texas district, the new district must convene an ARD (Admission, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment to review and adopt or adjust the IEP. Bring all prior IEP, 504, evaluation, and ARD documentation to enrollment — do not assume records will transfer automatically. For families with significant or complex special education needs, contacting Celina ISD's Special Services department directly before your move date is the most reliable way to understand what to expect. Parent experience with special education services varies by campus and student need; the department contact information is at celinaisd.com.
How does the Celina ISD tax rate compare to Prosper ISD and other neighbors? ▾
Celina ISD's 2025 certified total tax rate is $1.2089 per $100 of assessed value — a modest reduction from prior years. Prosper ISD operates at approximately $1.24/$100, making Celina ISD marginally lower. Anna ISD sits near the $1.26 state cap for most districts. On a $500,000 home with the $100,000 Texas homestead exemption ($400,000 taxable value), the Celina ISD portion is approximately $4,836 annually ($403/month). Note that many newer Celina subdivisions sit within Municipal Utility Districts (MUDs) that add a separate tax layer — sometimes $0.30–$0.80/$100 or more — on top of the school, city, and county rates. Always ask about MUD taxes for any specific subdivision before making an offer; they can meaningfully affect your total property tax bill.
What will happen to my child's campus assignment when new Celina ISD schools open? ▾
Campus assignments can change when new schools open in fast-growth districts, and Celina ISD is actively expanding. A 5th elementary opened for 2025–26, and Willard Middle School is expected to open in fall 2026. When Willard opens, the district has announced a temporary grade reconfiguration: for the 2026–27 and 2027–28 school years, elementary campuses will serve PK–4 (rather than K–5), Willard will serve Grades 5–6, and Moore Middle will serve Grades 7–8. The district plans to return to the standard K–5/6–8 structure after that transitional period. A child entering elementary school in 2026 may experience two campus transitions in three years during this window. The district sends communication to affected families when boundary changes are made. For the most current attendance zone assignments, use celinaisd.com/who-we-are/attendance-zones or contact the district's Student Services office directly.
Is Celina ISD a good choice compared to Prosper ISD for a family relocating to north Collin County? ▾
The answer depends on your family's priorities. Prosper ISD is larger (~33,000 students), has earned A-level TEA ratings consistently, offers more AP course variety and a broader program menu, and has more established infrastructure. Homes in Prosper ISD territory often carry a price premium. Celina ISD is smaller (~5,500 students), has maintained a "B" (87) through rapid growth, has a strong small-town community culture centered on athletics and school involvement, and generally offers more home for the dollar. Families whose top priority is the highest available TEA rating and the widest program catalog tend to prefer Prosper ISD. Families who value the small-town identity, community cohesion, and more accessible home prices — and who are comfortable with a district still adding campuses — often prefer Celina ISD. Both are reasonable choices for most families; the "better" district depends on what your family is actually optimizing for.
