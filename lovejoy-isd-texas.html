Lovejoy ISD serves Lucas and Fairview, Texas — two of Collin County’s most sought-after residential communities, situated between Allen and McKinney roughly 25–30 miles north of downtown Dallas. With approximately 4,100 students across just five campuses, the district earned a TEA score of 96 for 2024–25 — placing it among the top five school districts in all of Texas by that measure. Only a handful of districts in the state have ever reached this level.

Lovejoy ISD is named for the Lovejoy community, a historically rural area that has been enveloped by the expansion of the Collin County suburbs. The district is small by design — and families who choose Lucas or Fairview often say the district’s size is the point. Every student feeds into a single high school. Teachers know students by name from elementary through graduation. The community feels less like an institution and more like a large, academically ambitious family. That tight-knit culture, combined with performance metrics that rival the best private schools in Texas, is what draws relocating families to Lovejoy ISD specifically — and why many say they chose Lucas or Fairview over Frisco, Plano, or McKinney despite similar housing prices.

Critical Boundary Note for Buyers

Not every address in Lucas or Fairview feeds into Lovejoy ISD. Both cities sit at the junction of three school district boundaries — Lovejoy ISD, Allen ISD, and McKinney ISD. Some streets in Lucas belong to Allen ISD; some Fairview addresses fall in McKinney ISD. Always verify the specific parcel using the TEA School District Locator and the Collin County Appraisal District before purchasing. Do not rely on city name, subdivision name, or the listing agent’s representation alone.

TEA Rating & Academic Performance

Texas assigns each school district an A–F accountability rating each year through the Texas Education Agency (TEA). Lovejoy ISD earned an A rating with a score of 96 for the 2024–25 school year — an extraordinary result that places the district in rarefied air alongside Highland Park ISD and Westlake Academy among the highest-scoring districts in the state.

How the TEA Rating Is Calculated

TEA ratings are built on three domains:

  • Student Achievement — STAAR test scores, graduation rates, college/career readiness
  • School Progress — year-over-year academic growth for all students
  • Closing the Gaps — performance equity across student subgroups

Lovejoy ISD’s score of 96 is not simply “above average.” A score of 90+ earns an A; most highly regarded suburban ISDs in DFW score in the low-to-mid 90s. Reaching 96 requires exceptional performance across all three domains simultaneously — something very few districts in Texas sustain. For context, districts like Allen ISD and Frisco ISD — both considered outstanding — score in the 88–93 range. Lovejoy’s 96 is a genuine outlier.

Niche Grade: A+

Niche.com assigns Lovejoy ISD an A+ overall, with top marks in academics, college preparation, and teachers. Lovejoy ISD appears on national “best school districts in Texas” lists alongside Highland Park ISD and Carroll ISD (Southlake) — two of the most consistently cited elite suburban districts in the state. The consistency of Lovejoy’s performance across multiple independent rating sources is a meaningful signal: the data and the lived family experience point in the same direction.

Per-pupil expenditure is approximately $14,500 annually, supported by Collin County’s strong residential property base. The district carries a TEA FIRST (Financial Integrity Rating System of Texas) rating of Superior — the highest financial tier — indicating responsible management of public education funds. A higher per-pupil spend relative to larger ISDs is partly a function of the district’s size: fixed costs spread across fewer students produce higher per-pupil figures, but those dollars translate into lower class sizes and more individualized teacher attention.

Academics & College Preparation

Lovejoy ISD’s graduation rate of approximately 99% is among the highest in Texas. More telling than that headline figure is the depth of college readiness the district produces at its size. With one high school and a senior class that typically numbers around 300 students, Lovejoy High School’s AP participation rate and exam pass rates are exceptionally high on a per-student basis — outpacing districts many times its enrollment.

Advanced Placement

Lovejoy High School offers an extensive AP catalog relative to its enrollment. AP courses span the full range of subjects: English Language and Literature, Calculus AB and BC, Statistics, US History, World History, Government, Economics, Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Computer Science, Psychology, Spanish, French, and fine arts options. Participation rates are high — many Lovejoy students graduate with multiple AP exam scores of 3 or higher. The culture at Lovejoy HS is one where AP and honors enrollment is the norm, not the exception.

Dual Credit — Collin College Partnership

Eligible Lovejoy High School students can access dual credit courses through Collin College, earning credit that counts simultaneously toward the high school transcript and the college transcript. This is a meaningful cost-saving and acceleration opportunity for college-bound students, and the Collin College partnership gives Lovejoy students access to a broad course catalog beyond what a small district can staff internally.

College Admissions Outcomes

Lovejoy ISD produces a disproportionate share of National Merit Scholarship semifinalists and commended scholars relative to its enrollment. Graduates attend selective universities at rates that reflect the district’s academic culture — Texas flagship universities (UT Austin, Texas A&M), as well as selective private and out-of-state institutions. The small cohort size means that high-achieving students are well known to their teachers and counselors, which translates into strong recommendation letters and personalized college counseling that is harder to deliver at scale in larger ISDs.

No IB Programme — Why AP Delivers the Same Rigor

Lovejoy ISD does not offer the International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma Programme. This is a deliberate fit for a district of its size — IB requires significant infrastructure investment and a minimum cohort size to operate well. Lovejoy’s AP + honors + dual credit structure delivers equivalent academic rigor without the IB overhead, and the district’s TEA score of 96 and college admissions outcomes reflect that this approach works exceptionally well for its students.

Campuses

Lovejoy ISD operates five campuses: three elementary schools, one intermediate school serving grades 6–8, and one high school. All five campuses feed into Lovejoy High School, which means every student who enters Hart, Lovejoy, or Puster Elementary knows they are on a 13-year path to graduating together as a Leopard. That continuity — same peers, same community, same culture from kindergarten through senior year — is something families who have experienced it describe as irreplaceable.

Lovejoy High School

Grades 9–12 UIL 4A The Leopards

The district’s single comprehensive high school, serving all Lovejoy ISD students in grades 9–12. Extensive AP catalog, dual credit through Collin College, JROTC, strong fine arts, and UIL academic competition with state-level appearances. Cross-country and track among the best small-district programs in Texas. The small senior class (~300 students) means every student is visible and supported.

Sloan Creek Intermediate School

Grades 6–8 Middle School

Lovejoy’s sole intermediate campus, serving all district students in grades 6–8. Pre-AP coursework, GT instruction, fine arts, athletics, and UIL academic team participation. The single-campus middle school means the transition to high school is smooth — students enter Lovejoy HS already knowing virtually all of their classmates.

Hart Elementary

Grades PK–5 Elementary

One of three elementary campuses in Lovejoy ISD. GT identification and enrichment services, fine arts integration, and a small-school culture where teachers know every student. Attendance zone is determined by home address within the district.

Lovejoy Elementary

Grades PK–5 Elementary

The namesake elementary campus, honoring the district’s historic Lovejoy community roots. Same GT services, fine arts, and community-embedded culture as across the district. The small size of each elementary campus allows for genuine family-school partnerships that are difficult to sustain at larger ISDs.

Puster Elementary

Grades PK–5 Elementary

Third of Lovejoy ISD’s three elementary campuses. Offers the same academic programs, GT services, and fine arts programming as Hart and Lovejoy Elementary. Campus assignment among the three elementary schools is determined by attendance zone based on home address.

One of Everything

One high school. One intermediate school. Three elementary schools. The district’s small size means there is no “better” or “worse” campus within Lovejoy ISD — all students converge at Sloan Creek for middle school and Lovejoy High School for grades 9–12 regardless of which elementary they attend. The only question is which elementary campus your address falls in. Verify campus assignment at lovejoyisd.net.

Signature Programs & Pathways

Lovejoy ISD’s program depth is impressive for a district of its size. Where many small ISDs offer limited extracurricular breadth, Lovejoy has built strong programs in academic competition, fine arts, JROTC, and athletics — all operating within a student body of roughly 4,100. The result is that students who want to pursue a competitive pathway — whether UIL academics, marching band, or cross-country — can do so without being lost in a large institution.

Advanced Placement (AP)

Extensive AP catalog relative to enrollment, spanning STEM, humanities, social sciences, and fine arts. High participation rates and above-average pass rates. AP vertical alignment begins with pre-AP and honors coursework in middle school, so students arrive at Lovejoy HS prepared to succeed in challenging coursework from day one.

Gifted and Talented (GT)

GT identification and services at every grade level: elementary enrichment and pull-out, middle school GT/pre-AP courses, and the full honors and AP catalog at the high school. GT testing occurs within the district. Out-of-state GT documentation is reviewed by the district coordinator upon enrollment — bring all prior evaluation reports and identification records.

UIL Academic Competition

Lovejoy ISD is a consistent competitor at the state level in UIL Academic Competition, which includes events in math, science, social studies, language arts, debate, journalism, and computer science. The district’s small size actually creates an advantage in UIL academics — motivated students get more practice opportunities and coaching attention than they would in larger programs. State championship appearances are a consistent part of Lovejoy’s UIL academic history.

JROTC

Lovejoy High School operates a Junior Reserve Officers’ Training Corps (JROTC) program. JROTC develops leadership, discipline, and civic responsibility. It is a popular program among students from military and veteran families, and participation does not obligate students to any military service commitment.

Fine Arts

Band, choir, theater, and visual art programs operate at all grade levels. The fine arts culture at Lovejoy ISD is strong relative to the district’s size — competitive marching band, performing choirs, theater productions, and visual art programs that have produced regional and state recognition. Small class sizes allow for more individual instruction in every arts discipline.

Dual Credit — Collin College

Qualifying Lovejoy HS students can enroll in dual credit courses through Collin College, earning simultaneous high school and college credit. Tuition arrangements vary; check current district and Collin College guidelines for cost and eligibility. Dual credit is a meaningful acceleration tool for students who want to enter college with hours already completed.

Cross-Country & Track

Lovejoy ISD’s cross-country and track programs have been among the top small-district athletic programs in Texas, competing at the state level consistently. This is worth noting for student-athletes who run — Lovejoy competes in UIL 4A, where a talented distance runner has a realistic path to state competition that might be harder to achieve in a 6A district with deeper rosters.

College Counseling

With a graduating class of around 300 students, Lovejoy HS counselors carry lower caseloads than at large ISDs. Students receive more individualized attention in the college planning process. The district’s track record of National Merit recognition and selective university admissions reflects a counseling culture that actively supports ambitious college goals, not just graduation completion.

Special Education & Support Services

Lovejoy ISD provides a full continuum of special education services as required under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). The district operates Admission, Review, and Dismissal (ARD) committees to develop each student’s Individualized Education Program (IEP) based on evaluation results and parent input.

Small-District Advantage in Special Education

The district’s size of approximately 4,100 students means that special education case managers carry lower caseloads than their counterparts at large ISDs. Students with IEPs receive more individualized attention, and ARD teams are typically smaller and more relationship-driven. Families who have experienced special education at a large suburban ISD before moving to Lovejoy frequently note the difference in responsiveness and personalization.

Service Continuum

Lovejoy ISD offers the full IDEA service continuum, including:

  • Inclusion / co-teach — SpEd students in general education classrooms with support
  • Resource room — small-group instruction in specific skill areas
  • Self-contained settings — for students requiring more structured environments
  • Transition services — post-secondary planning beginning at age 16 (or earlier per IEP)

Related services include speech-language pathology, occupational therapy, physical therapy, assistive technology, and counseling, depending on individual student needs as determined by the ARD committee.

Out-of-State IEP Transfers

Texas law requires Lovejoy ISD to convene an ARD meeting within 30 days of your child’s enrollment to review an out-of-state IEP. The district must provide services during that review period. Texas uses different eligibility categories than some states — the ARD may result in an amended IEP or a new evaluation if the committee determines current data does not support existing services. Bring all evaluation reports, progress notes, and prior IEP documents — not just the summary page — to the enrollment appointment.

For SpEd Families

Contact Lovejoy ISD’s Special Education Department directly before enrollment if your child has an IEP, 504 plan, or a pending evaluation: lovejoyisd.net. The 30-day ARD window begins on the enrollment date, so the sooner you engage the district, the sooner services are in place. For 504 plans, contact the campus 504 coordinator at the assigned campus.

Dyslexia Services

Texas requires all public school districts to provide dyslexia identification and intervention services. Lovejoy ISD provides dyslexia screening, evaluation, and structured literacy intervention in compliance with Texas Education Code Chapter 38.003 and the Texas Dyslexia Handbook. If your child has a prior dyslexia identification from another state, bring all testing documentation to enrollment.

Lovejoy ISD Athletics

Lovejoy ISD competes at the UIL 4A level — the second-largest UIL classification in Texas. The district’s enrollment has been growing steadily, and there is ongoing discussion about whether Lovejoy will eventually reclassify to 5A as the Lucas and Fairview communities continue to develop. For now, 4A classification means that talented student-athletes compete against similarly sized programs — creating meaningful opportunities for individual recognition and team success that might be harder to achieve in a 6A district.

Cross-Country & Track

Lovejoy’s cross-country and track programs are the district’s most consistent state-level competitors. The Leopards have fielded individual and team state qualifiers regularly, and the program’s coaching and training culture is a genuine draw for student-athletes who run. Families with competitive runners should know that Lovejoy offers a path to state competition within the 4A classification that is realistic and well-supported.

Other Athletic Programs

Lovejoy ISD fields programs across the full UIL catalog for 4A schools, including:

  • Swimming and Diving — competitive at district and regional levels
  • Tennis — strong individual and team programs
  • Golf — district-level competition
  • Football — Leopards compete in District 7-4A; playoff appearances regular
  • Basketball, Baseball, Softball, Soccer, Volleyball — active programs with consistent district competition
UIL Classification & Transfer Eligibility

UIL eligibility rules apply to all Lovejoy ISD student-athletes: enrollment and academic standards must be met to compete. Students transferring from another district may face UIL transfer eligibility waiting periods depending on the circumstances of the transfer. If your student-athlete is relocating mid-season, contact the Lovejoy ISD Athletic Department early to understand eligibility timelines. Full UIL transfer rules are at uiltexas.org.

School Choice & Enrollment Boundaries

Lovejoy ISD is a residence-only district. There is no open enrollment for out-of-district students — if you do not own or rent a home within Lovejoy ISD’s attendance boundaries, your children cannot attend Lovejoy ISD schools. This is not a technicality; the district’s enrollment has grown steadily and capacity is consumed by in-boundary students. Families who want Lovejoy ISD must purchase or lease a home that is confirmed to be within the district.

Within-District Simplicity

Within the district, school choice is straightforward. There is only one high school (Lovejoy HS), one intermediate school (Sloan Creek), and three elementary schools. Elementary campus assignment is determined by attendance zone based on your home address. There are no magnet programs, no selective admissions pathways, and no within-district transfers needed — every student follows the same path from elementary through graduation.

The Lucas / Fairview Boundary Issue

This is the most important practical issue for buyers. Both Lucas and Fairview are served by multiple school districts — Lovejoy ISD, Allen ISD, and McKinney ISD. The boundary does not follow subdivision lines or even street patterns in a consistent way in all areas. A home on one side of a street may be Lovejoy ISD; the home directly across the street may be Allen ISD. This is not theoretical — buyers have discovered the boundary mismatch after closing, finding that the home they purchased specifically for Lovejoy ISD access is actually in Allen ISD.

The protocol is simple and non-negotiable: use the TEA School District Locator to enter the full street address of every home you seriously consider, and confirm the result against the Collin County Appraisal District parcel record at collincad.org. The CCAD parcel record lists all taxing jurisdictions, including the school district, for every property. These are public records. This verification step takes five minutes and eliminates the risk of one of the most expensive mistakes a relocating family can make.

Always Verify Before You Offer

Kristen runs TEA and CCAD verification on every home her clients consider in the Lucas and Fairview market. It is a standard step in the home search process, not an afterthought. If you are working with another agent who does not do this verification, ask them to. If you are searching on your own, do not rely on Zillow, Redfin, or any listing site for ISD information — those data feeds are frequently inaccurate at district boundaries. The TEA locator and CCAD are the only authoritative sources.

District Finances & Tax Rate

Understanding Lovejoy ISD’s tax rate is an important part of the home purchase decision in Lucas and Fairview. School district taxes are the largest single component of the Texas property tax bill for most homeowners.

Current Tax Rate

Lovejoy ISD’s total tax rate is approximately $1.14 per $100 of assessed value for 2024–25. For a home assessed at $750,000 in Lucas — a representative price point for the area — that means approximately $8,550 in annual school district taxes before the homestead exemption. Texas homeowners who claim a homestead exemption receive a $100,000 reduction in assessed value for school district tax purposes, bringing that figure to approximately $7,410 per year.

How Lovejoy ISD Compares to Neighboring Districts

Lucas and Fairview homes at the Lovejoy ISD boundary also have access to Allen ISD and McKinney ISD. For reference:

  • Lovejoy ISD: ~$1.14 per $100
  • Allen ISD: ~$1.13 per $100
  • McKinney ISD: ~$1.17 per $100

The tax rate difference between Lovejoy, Allen, and McKinney ISDs is negligible — families in this market are choosing between similarly priced options. The decision is about the school district experience, not a meaningful tax cost difference. All three districts are strong; Lovejoy’s TEA score of 96 is the differentiator.

TEA FIRST Rating: Superior

Lovejoy ISD holds a Superior financial integrity rating from the TEA FIRST program — the highest tier. This means the district is managing public education funds with transparency and effectiveness. Small-district finances can sometimes be strained by a narrow tax base, but Lovejoy ISD benefits from some of the highest residential property values in Collin County, providing a stable and well-funded foundation.

Collin County Appraisal

Property taxes in the Lucas and Fairview area are assessed by the Collin County Appraisal District (CCAD). The Texas homestead exemption reduces the assessed value used for school taxes by $100,000, and homeowners who are 65 or older or disabled may qualify for additional exemptions and a school tax freeze. Verify current rates and exemption details at collincad.org.

Tax Calculation Example

$800,000 home assessed value (Lucas)
Minus $100,000 homestead exemption = $700,000 taxable value
x $1.14 per $100 = $7,980 / year in Lovejoy ISD school taxes
Additional Collin County, City of Lucas, and other taxing entity rates apply on top of this figure. Always verify current rates with CCAD at collincad.org.

Enrolling in Lovejoy ISD

Lovejoy ISD uses an online enrollment portal accessible through lovejoyisd.net. Enrollment windows for the upcoming school year typically open in spring. Mid-year enrollment is accepted on a rolling basis and is the norm for relocating families arriving outside of the standard enrollment window.

Required Documents

  • Proof of residency within Lovejoy ISD boundaries — deed/mortgage statement confirming ownership, or a signed lease plus a current utility bill, both showing the Lucas or Fairview address. The address must be in the Lovejoy ISD attendance zone, not merely in the city of Lucas or Fairview.
  • Immunization records — Texas-required vaccines per the DSHS schedule. Prior-state records are accepted if immunizations are current.
  • Birth certificate — certified copy
  • Prior academic records — most recent report card or transcript, standardized test scores
  • IEP / 504 documentation — if applicable; bring all evaluation reports, not just the summary document
  • GT identification records — if your child has been identified as Gifted and Talented in another state, bring documentation for review by the district coordinator

Enrollment Timeline for Relocating Families

If you are closing on a Lucas or Fairview home between April and August, begin the enrollment process as soon as closing documents are available. Lovejoy ISD’s small enrollment means its programs fill quickly — early enrollment ensures your child’s GT placement, fine arts, and extracurricular registrations are in place before the school year begins. For mid-year arrivals, enrollment can typically be completed within a few business days once all documents are submitted.

Address Verification at Enrollment

Lovejoy ISD verifies that the enrollment address is within district boundaries at the time of enrollment. This is a formal step, not a formality — the district will not enroll a student at an address outside Lovejoy ISD boundaries. Confirm boundary status before you commit to a home purchase using the TEA locator and CCAD, not after.

Enrollment Contact

Lovejoy ISD Administration: lovejoyisd.net | 259 Country Club Road, Allen, TX 75002 (mailing address). For special education, IEP transfers, or GT testing inquiries, request the appropriate department coordinator through the district’s main enrollment contact line.

Lovejoy ISD — Frequently Asked Questions

I’m looking at homes in Lucas — are all Lucas addresses in Lovejoy ISD? ⌄
No — and this is the most important thing to understand about buying in Lucas or Fairview. Both cities sit at the junction of three school districts: Lovejoy ISD, Allen ISD, and McKinney ISD. The boundary does not follow subdivision lines or city limits. Some streets in Lucas are Allen ISD; some Fairview addresses are McKinney ISD. The only reliable way to confirm which district a specific property falls in is to enter the full street address into the TEA School District Locator and cross-check it against the Collin County Appraisal District parcel record at collincad.org. Never rely on the city name, subdivision name, or a listing agent’s representation — verify the specific parcel before making any offer contingent on Lovejoy ISD access.
What UIL classification does Lovejoy compete in, and will it change? ⌄
Lovejoy ISD currently competes at the UIL 4A level. As Lucas and Fairview continue to grow and district enrollment increases, there is ongoing discussion about whether Lovejoy will reclassify to 5A in a future UIL realignment cycle. UIL conducts enrollment-based reclassification every two years, so classification can change. For student-athletes, 4A classification today means competing against programs of similar size, which creates meaningful individual and team opportunities — particularly in cross-country, track, tennis, and swimming. If UIL classification is a specific concern for your student-athlete, verify the current classification status at uiltexas.org before your relocation timeline.
Does Lovejoy ISD have open enrollment for out-of-district families? ⌄
No. Lovejoy ISD does not offer open enrollment for students who live outside the district’s attendance boundaries. Enrollment is restricted to students whose families reside within the district. This is a hard boundary, not a waitlist situation — there is no process to apply for out-of-district admission. Families who want Lovejoy ISD must purchase or lease a home that is confirmed within district boundaries. Given the demand for the district, this is unlikely to change. If you are set on Lovejoy ISD, the path is straightforward: buy in the right place and verify the boundary before you offer.
How does Lovejoy achieve a 96 TEA score with only ~4,100 students? ⌄
Several factors work together. First, the district serves a community with high residential property values, which funds competitive per-pupil spending (~$14,500) that supports strong staffing and resources. Second, the small size creates naturally lower class sizes and higher teacher-to-student ratios, which the research consistently links to better academic outcomes. Third, the community culture is deeply invested in education — parent engagement is high, and the expectation of academic excellence starts at the elementary level. Fourth, the single high school model means that all district resources, all of the coaching talent, all of the fine arts and academic competition infrastructure, are concentrated on one campus and one student cohort rather than spread across multiple high schools. The TEA score of 96 reflects the cumulative effect of all of these factors — not one magic program.
Is there an IB programme at Lovejoy ISD? ⌄
No. Lovejoy ISD does not offer the International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma Programme. The district’s college preparation model is built on an extensive AP catalog, dual credit through Collin College, and a strong honors and pre-AP sequence beginning in middle school. This approach is effective — Lovejoy’s college readiness metrics and National Merit outcomes are strong without IB. For families whose student is mid-IB Diploma and needs an IB World School to continue, Allen ISD offers the IB Diploma Programme at Allen High School. If the IB Diploma is a requirement, Allen ISD would be the better fit; if strong college preparation through AP and dual credit is the goal, Lovejoy delivers it at an exceptional level.
What makes Lovejoy ISD different from Allen ISD and McKinney ISD? ⌄
The defining difference is scale. Allen ISD serves ~22,000 students across 26 campuses; McKinney ISD serves ~26,000 students across 40+ campuses; Lovejoy ISD serves ~4,100 students across 5 campuses. All three hold TEA A ratings, but Lovejoy’s TEA score of 96 is meaningfully higher than Allen’s ~91 and McKinney’s scores. The small-district experience is qualitatively different: every student feeds into one high school, teachers know students across years, the community is tighter, and there is no risk of being anonymous. The tradeoff is breadth — Allen ISD has 30+ AP courses and an IB Diploma Programme; McKinney ISD has multiple high schools with specialized programs. For families where the small-district culture and elite academic performance are the priorities, Lovejoy is the answer. For families who want the largest possible program catalog or the IB Diploma, Allen ISD may be the better fit.
