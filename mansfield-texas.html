Location & Commute

Where Is Mansfield? Commutes & Getting Around

Mansfield sits about 22 miles southeast of Downtown Fort Worth and 35 miles southwest of Downtown Dallas, in Tarrant County (with a small sliver in Johnson County). The city straddles US-287 and SH-360, giving commuters direct access to Fort Worth, Arlington, the mid-cities corridor, and Grand Prairie without touching I-20 or I-35W for most trips.

For families working in Fort Worth — especially Lockheed Martin, Bell Textron, or the American Airlines campus — Mansfield is a straight shot north on US-287. The Arlington entertainment district (AT&T Stadium, Globe Life Field) is 15 minutes up SH-360. Dallas-proper requires I-20 east and runs 35–50 minutes depending on traffic, which is the honest tradeoff for living on the south side of the metro.

No rail service in Mansfield. The city is not a TRE or DART member city. A personal vehicle is essential for all daily errands and commutes. US-287 and SH-360 are the primary arteries — US-287 north toward Fort Worth carries moderate peak-hour traffic, while I-20 east toward Dallas is heavier and less predictable. Mansfield residents heading to north DFW corridors (Legacy West, Frisco) should budget 50–65 minutes and factor toll road costs on SH-121 and DNT.
Education

Mansfield ISD — Schools in Mansfield

Mansfield ISD is one of the most underrated A-rated districts in DFW. With roughly 35,000 students across six high schools, the district earns consistent A ratings from both TEA and Niche — yet it flies under the radar compared to Frisco or Southlake, which helps keep home prices more accessible. Band programs, athletics, and CTE pathways are district strengths, and the multi-campus high school model means students attend schools designed around community character rather than a single mega-campus.

Family Life

What Life Actually Looks Like in Mansfield

Friday Night Lights Culture

Mansfield ISD athletics are a genuine community anchor. Six high school football programs fill stadiums on Friday nights — the culture here is real, not manufactured. If youth sports and ISD athletics matter to your family, Mansfield's depth of program across all six campuses is a meaningful lifestyle asset.

Joe Pool Lake — 20 Minutes Out

Cedar Hill State Park on Joe Pool Lake is a legitimate outdoor escape — boating, fishing, camping, and swimming just 20 minutes from Mansfield. Families get real lake access without paying lakefront prices. It's one of the most underappreciated DFW outdoor assets and practically a backyard amenity for Mansfield residents.

Pro Sports 15 Minutes Away

AT&T Stadium (Dallas Cowboys) and Globe Life Field (Texas Rangers) are both in Arlington, 15 minutes up SH-360. For families who love major league sports, Mansfield's location beats most north DFW suburbs by a significant margin — no 45-minute drive required for Cowboys or Rangers games.

Community Events & City Life

Mansfield hosts its own city festivals, a downtown Main Street district, and HOA-driven neighborhood events across the larger master-planned communities. The city size — roughly 75,000 — is large enough to support real amenities but small enough that it doesn't feel anonymous. Parents report it's easy to find your crowd through school athletics and community organizations.

Mansfield National Golf Club

A solid public-access golf facility within the city — Mansfield National Golf Club is a well-maintained 18-hole course that doesn't require a private membership. Combined with nearby municipal courses in Arlington and Cedar Hill, golfers in Mansfield have a range of options without driving to north DFW.

Established Neighborhood Character

Mansfield is not a construction zone — the city has an established character that many fast-growing north DFW suburbs have traded away for density. Mature trees, older neighborhoods with real architectural variety, and a sense that the community has been here a while all contribute to a lifestyle feel that newer suburbs simply can't replicate yet.

The honest tradeoff: Mansfield's location on the south side of the DFW metro means north DFW destinations — Frisco, Plano's Legacy West corridor, Allen — require 45–65 minutes each way. If your employer is in Plano or your family spends significant time in north Dallas, the geography works against you. The in-city dining and retail scene, while improving, doesn't yet match what you'd find in Frisco or Southlake — most upscale dining and shopping requires a trip to Arlington or Fort Worth. No rail transit exists or is planned.
Getting Outside

Recreation, Walkability & City Amenities

Mansfield is car-dependent for daily errands — no DART rail, no TRE station, no meaningful public transit. A personal vehicle is essential. What the city compensates with is meaningful: a solid parks system, the Mansfield Activities Center (a full-service recreation facility), and Joe Pool Lake and Cedar Hill State Park just 20 minutes southwest for serious outdoor recreation.

Parks, Trails & Green Space

Mansfield maintains over 30 parks and green spaces across the city, with more than 20 miles of hike-and-bike trails connecting neighborhoods. The city's parks system ranks in the top 10% of DFW suburbs for parks per resident — a genuine quality-of-life strength that most relocating families discover only after they move in.

Oak Creek Park

A large community park with athletic fields, a splash pad, walking trails, and picnic pavilions. One of the most-used parks in the city — good indicator of why Mansfield families with young kids rate the parks system highly. Free splash pad is a summer staple.

Hawaiian Falls Waterpark

A full-scale water park within Mansfield city limits — wave pools, slides, and family attractions. A rare in-city amenity that most DFW suburbs require a 30–45 minute drive to access. Summer season is a major draw for families with school-age children.

Walnut Creek Linear Park Trail

A connected trail system running through the heart of Mansfield's established neighborhoods — Walnut Creek Estates and surrounding areas are directly linked. Good for morning runs, family bike rides, and walking the dog without needing to load up a car.

Broad Park

Broad Park serves the central Mansfield area with sports fields, basketball courts, and open green space. A practical family park — not a destination experience, but a solid neighborhood resource for weekend play and organized recreation programs.

Joe Pool Lake — Cedar Hill State Park

Twenty minutes southwest, Cedar Hill State Park on Joe Pool Lake is one of the best state park experiences in the DFW metro — boating, fishing, camping, and lakefront hiking. Families in Mansfield use it like a backyard lake. Annual state park passes make it accessible year-round.

Six Flags Over Texas — 15 Minutes

Six Flags Over Texas and Hurricane Harbor are both in Arlington, just 15 minutes from Mansfield. For families with kids, this is a legitimate lifestyle amenity — season passes are affordable, and spontaneous visits are possible on a weeknight. Most DFW suburbs require 35–45 minutes to reach Six Flags.

Safety & Crime

Is Mansfield Safe?

Mansfield is considered a safe community by DFW standards. The violent crime rate runs well below the national average of approximately 4.0 per 1,000 residents, and property crime — while present, as in any city of 75,000 — is consistent with comparable suburban communities in Tarrant County. Niche rates the city's safety at A-, reflecting strong resident sentiment and relatively low crime indices.

Violent Crime Rate

~1.8 per 1,000 residents — well below the national average of ~4.0/1,000. Mansfield's violent crime rate is in the lower range for DFW suburbs of comparable size.

Property Crime

~18 per 1,000 residents — at or slightly below the Texas suburban average. Auto theft and package theft are the most common issues; home break-ins are relatively rare in established neighborhoods.

Mansfield PD

Mansfield Police Department maintains an active community policing program and solid response times across the city's neighborhoods. Niche Safety Grade: A-. Source: Niche 2025, NeighborhoodScout, FBI UCR data.
