Crandall ISD Overview

6,443 Students (2025–26)
9 Campuses
C / 75 TEA Grade (2024)
C+ Niche Grade (3rd party)
97.3% Graduation Rate (2023)
$1.26 Tax Rate / $100 (2024–25)

Crandall ISD is a fast-growing school district in southwestern Kaufman County, serving the city of Crandall and surrounding rural communities about 25 miles southeast of downtown Dallas. It is one of the fastest-growing districts in the county — enrollment has roughly doubled in the last decade — and that growth is both the district's biggest asset and its most honest tradeoff. Families who move here get newer campuses, newer housing, and far lower home prices than the established DFW suburbs to the north. The district's TEA score of 75 (a C) reflects growing pains still being worked through, but the 97.3% four-year graduation rate tells a different story: students who stay through senior year are crossing the finish line at a high rate.

For relocating families, the key question isn't whether Crandall ISD is "good enough" — it's whether the tradeoffs fit your family's priorities. If your household is drawn to Kaufman County by affordable home prices, a quieter pace, and a tight-knit community feel, and you're realistic that the district is still building out the program depth that larger ISDs offer, Crandall can be a genuinely solid choice. If AP/IB pathway access, established athletics facilities, or a B-to-A TEA rating is a priority, neighboring Rockwall ISD or the growing Forney ISD may be a better fit to evaluate alongside it.

One major structural change took effect for 2025–26: the district added a second middle school and reconfigured all elementary schools to serve Pre-K through 5th grade (previously some campuses included 6th grade). Ninth grade also moved back to the high school after previously being housed separately. For families arriving mid-restructure, this means campus assignments may differ from what older online sources show — always confirm your address zone with the district directly.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Are drawn to Kaufman County by affordability and want to stretch their housing budget further than in Rockwall or Forney
  • Value a close-knit community where kids grow up with the same classmates from elementary through high school
  • Have a student strong in athletics — the district's athletic programs are a cultural centerpiece
  • Are comfortable with a district actively building infrastructure and accept that program variety is growing over time
  • Commute toward southern Dallas, Mesquite, or work remotely — the I-30 corridor is the primary commute route

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • AP/IB or specialized magnet programs are a hard requirement — the district currently offers dual credit and CTE but not full IB or magnet pathways
  • You need a B- or higher TEA rating on the district report card before making your home-buying decision
  • Your commute is toward Plano, Frisco, or the northern DFW corridor — the geographic position adds significant drive time
  • You want the stability of a fully built-out district with locked campus assignments — boundary rezoning is ongoing as enrollment grows
Boundary Verification

Not every home with a Crandall or Kaufman County address falls within Crandall ISD boundaries. Parts of western Kaufman County fall within other districts, and the 2025–26 restructuring reassigned some addresses to new campuses. Always verify your specific address using the district's enrollment office before making an offer — do not rely solely on a real estate listing or ZIP code to determine school assignment.

New construction communities along TX-34 and surrounding growth corridors may have campus assignments different from older established neighborhoods. Confirm directly at (972) 427-6000 or via the Crandall ISD enrollment portal.

TEA Accountability Rating

Crandall ISD received a C (score: 75) on the 2024 Texas Education Agency A–F Accountability Report. TEA ratings are published by the Texas Education Agency through txschools.gov and use a 0–100 numeric score converted to a letter grade: A (90+), B (80–89), C (70–79), D (60–69), F (below 60).

The three TEA domains — and what they measure — are:

  • Student Achievement — absolute performance on STAAR tests, graduation rates, and post-secondary readiness indicators. This domain measures how many students are meeting grade-level standards today.
  • School Progress — whether students are growing academically year over year, and how the district performs relative to similarly situated peers. A district can score well here even if its absolute proficiency is lower than wealthier neighbors.
  • Closing the Gaps — whether historically underserved student groups are making progress. This is the domain where fast-growth districts with diverse incoming enrollment most often face challenges.

A C rating in the context of a fast-growing, rapidly diversifying district like Crandall is different from a C in a long-established district. The district is adding hundreds of students annually, integrating new campus configurations, and building out staffing — all of which create short-term pressure on accountability scores. What the numbers also show is a 97.3% four-year graduation rate (Class of 2023), which suggests the students who are in the system long enough to be tracked through high school are achieving at a high rate. That gap between the C TEA score and the near-perfect graduation rate is something families should dig into, not dismiss.

Important Note on Rating Year

TEA A–F ratings can lag one to two years behind the current school year. The 2024 rating applies to the 2023–24 school year. Always check txschools.gov (Crandall ISD) for the most current published rating before making decisions based on this data.

Academic Performance

Crandall ISD's most notable academic data point is its graduation rate. The Class of 2023 achieved a 97.3% four-year graduation rate — a figure that ranks well above the Texas state average and above many districts with higher TEA letter grades. This metric matters for relocating families because it reflects how well the district retains and supports students from freshman year through senior year, regardless of incoming proficiency levels.

STAAR proficiency rates in reading and math have shown variability consistent with a fast-growth district absorbing large numbers of new students annually. For current STAAR data by campus and grade level, the most reliable source is the Texas Tribune's Schools Explorer for Crandall ISD, which publishes state test scores by year.

At the high school level, Crandall ISD participates in dual credit coursework through Trinity Valley Community College (TVCC), allowing students to earn transferable college credit while still in high school. The district's College, Career, and Military Readiness (CCMR) program is an active focus — CCMR measures the percentage of graduates who leave high school ready for post-secondary education, a skilled trade, or military service.

What this means for relocating families: A student arriving at Crandall ISD from a high-performing suburban district will find a different environment than Frisco, Carroll, or Prosper ISD. The academic culture is developing alongside the community — there are engaged teachers and strong athletics, but the breadth of AP course offerings and competitive academic programs is more limited. Families who value a smaller, community-oriented setting where every student isn't competing in a highly pressurized academic environment sometimes find that a positive; families specifically targeting AP/IB pathway depth should investigate the current course catalog before committing.

Student-teacher ratio is approximately 19:1 (Niche, 2026), which is slightly above the DFW suburban average of 15–17:1 — a natural consequence of rapid enrollment growth outpacing teacher hiring timelines. Per-pupil expenditure is $8,929 per year (most recent available data).

Campuses

As of the 2025–26 school year, Crandall ISD operates 9 campuses — 6 elementary schools (Pre-K–5), 2 middle schools (grades 6–8), and 1 high school (grades 9–12). This configuration is new for 2025–26: the district added a second middle school and moved all elementary schools to a uniform K–5 structure, with 9th grade returning to the high school after previously being housed on a separate freshman campus.

Specialty Programs

Crandall ISD's current program offerings reflect a district focused on post-secondary workforce readiness more than traditional academic competition pathways. The core programs available are:

Dual Credit (TVCC)

Districtwide — available at Crandall High School. Students earn transferable college credit through Trinity Valley Community College while still in high school. Courses are taught by instructors who hold TVCC credentials or a master's degree with 18 graduate hours in the discipline. No lottery or application required — enrollment is based on qualifying coursework and grade level. For families prioritizing early college credit accumulation, this program is a genuine asset. Confirm the current course catalog with CHS counselors for specific offerings.

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

Districtwide — available at Crandall High School. CTE programs prepare students for career pathways in skilled trades, technology, healthcare, and business. CTE pathways are aligned with the district's CCMR (College, Career, and Military Readiness) goals. Specific pathway availability changes year to year based on staffing and enrollment — contact the CHS counseling office for the current pathway list. No application or lottery required; students enroll through standard course selection.

Athletics

Districtwide — all campuses. Athletics is the most culturally prominent program in Crandall ISD. Football, basketball, baseball, softball, track, cross country, and volleyball are all offered. For families with student-athletes, Crandall's athletic culture is a genuine differentiator from larger suburban districts where competition for roster spots is more intense. The smaller district size often means more students get meaningful playing time and leadership roles within their sport.

Gifted & Talented (GT)

District-identified — available at all campuses. Texas law requires all districts to identify and serve GT students. Crandall ISD screens students and provides services per TEA requirements. GT programming in smaller districts is typically delivered through differentiated instruction within the regular classroom rather than a dedicated separate GT campus or magnet program. Families seeking a dedicated GT center or pull-out enrichment program at the elementary level should verify current delivery format with the district's curriculum office.

Special Education & 504 Services

All campuses. Crandall ISD provides IEP, ARD, 504, dyslexia, and speech services per Texas law. Families transferring with an existing IEP: Texas law requires an ARD (Admissions, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment to review and either adopt or modify the prior IEP. Bring all prior documentation including the most recent evaluation, current IEP, and any related services agreements. Contact the Special Education department directly at (972) 427-6000 for campus-level service capacity questions before enrolling.

Programs Crandall ISD does not currently offer: International Baccalaureate (IB) program, dedicated STEM magnets, fine arts conservatory, or dual-language / Spanish immersion programs. Families for whom these are non-negotiable should evaluate Forney ISD or Rockwall ISD alongside Crandall, as both serve southeastern DFW and offer broader program variety.

Enrollment & Transfer Guide

Crandall ISD manages enrollment through an online portal and district enrollment office. All time-sensitive details below apply to the 2025–26 school year — verify current-year windows at crandall-isd.net/enroll.

New Student Enrollment Steps

  1. Confirm your address is in Crandall ISD boundaries — call (972) 427-6000 or use the enrollment portal before beginning paperwork.
  2. Complete online registration through the district's online enrollment system at crandall-isd.net. New students enroll online; returning students re-enroll through the same system each spring.
  3. Residency verification documents required: Two proofs of address (lease/deed, plus a utility bill, bank statement, or government document) are typically required. Confirm current document requirements with the enrollment office as requirements can change.
  4. Additional documents: Texas immunization records, birth certificate, Social Security number (optional for citizens), and prior school records / report card.
  5. Enrollment window: New student online enrollment for 2025–26 opened in spring 2025. Families arriving mid-year should contact the enrollment office directly for current intake scheduling.

Transfer Policy

Intra-district transfers (requesting a campus other than your assigned school) are handled by the district's enrollment department. Availability depends on campus capacity. Submit requests early — space at popular campuses can fill quickly as the district grows.

Inter-district transfers (enrolling from outside Crandall ISD boundaries) require district approval and are not guaranteed. Texas law allows districts to accept or decline inter-district transfers based on capacity. Contact the district office for the current inter-district transfer policy and application timeline.

IEP / 504 / Special Education Transfers

If your student has an existing IEP or 504 plan: Texas law requires Crandall ISD to convene an ARD (Admissions, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment to review the transferring IEP and either adopt it or propose modifications. Bring your child's most recent evaluation, current IEP, any related services agreements, and prior progress reports. Contact the Special Education department at (972) 427-6000 before the first day of school to schedule the ARD meeting in advance of arrival when possible.

Boundary Verification

Boundary verification is especially important in Crandall ISD right now because the district completed a significant attendance zone restructure for 2025–26. The addition of the second middle school triggered new boundary maps for all six elementary schools. Families purchasing homes in 2025 or 2026 should not rely on boundary information from real estate platforms, older blog posts, or prior-year school information packets — these sources may reflect pre-restructuring assignments.

Cities and Areas Served

Crandall ISD primarily serves the city of Crandall, portions of unincorporated Kaufman County in the southwest quadrant of the county, and the Combine community area. The district is bounded roughly by the southern edge of Mesquite/Balch Springs to the west, Forney ISD to the north and east, and Kaufman ISD to the east and south.

Key boundary edge cases for buyers:

  • Western boundary with Mesquite: Homes on the western edge of the district's territory — near the Mesquite/Kaufman County line — can sit close to Dallas ISD or Mesquite ISD jurisdictions. Verify the specific parcel before assuming Crandall ISD zoning.
  • New construction along TX-34 and I-20 corridors: Several new residential developments are being platted along these growth corridors. New construction communities may not appear in older boundary maps — the district updates campus assignments as new phases open. Always confirm with the enrollment office for a specific community or lot address.
  • Heartland-area homes: Some properties in the Heartland community (unincorporated area straddling Kaufman and Dallas counties) may fall in different ISDs depending on the exact address. Do not assume district assignment from the community name alone.

How to Verify Your Address

  • Crandall ISD Enrollment Portal: crandall-isd.net/enroll
  • District Enrollment Office: (972) 427-6000
  • TEA School Locator: tea.texas.gov/apps/school-directory

Home-search implication: Making an offer without verifying your specific address can mean your child attends a different school — or even a different district — than you planned. Kaufman County boundary maps do not always follow subdivision lines or street-level geography in ways that are intuitive. The ten-minute call to the enrollment office before making an offer is worth it.

Finances & Tax Rate

Crandall ISD's 2024–25 certified total tax rate is $1.26 per $100 of assessed value. This rate includes both the Maintenance & Operations (M&O) component — which funds day-to-day district operations — and the Interest & Sinking (I&S) component, which funds bond-financed capital construction.

Like neighboring Forney ISD, Crandall carries the maximum I&S rate of $0.50 per $100 to fund the new campuses, athletic facilities, and infrastructure required to absorb explosive population growth in Kaufman County. This is a meaningful structural reason why Crandall's tax rate is higher than more established districts: money that in an older district would go toward operations is being directed toward building the physical plant to serve a fast-growing population. Families should factor this in as a feature of the housing market they are entering, not a sign of financial mismanagement.

What the Tax Rate Means for Your Monthly Payment

On a $350,000 home (rough median for Crandall-area new construction), after the Texas $100,000 homestead exemption reduces the taxable value to $250,000:

  • Annual ISD tax: $250,000 × $1.26 / 100 = $3,150/year
  • Monthly impact: approximately $263/month added to your effective housing cost from ISD taxes alone

Remember: Kaufman County property is often priced well below comparable DFW suburban markets — so even with a higher rate, the total annual tax bill may be lower than on a $500,000 home at a lower rate. Run the math on the specific property, not the rate alone.

The Texas $100,000 homestead exemption applies to your primary residence and reduces the assessed value used to calculate the school district portion of your property tax. For seniors 65+ and disabled individuals, additional exemptions apply — verify current exemption levels at the Kaufman Central Appraisal District.

Per-pupil expenditure: $8,929 per student per year (most recently available data). This figure is within the typical range for smaller Texas growth-corridor districts and reflects the balance between a lean M&O budget and the capital investment being made in new facilities.

Families evaluating Crandall ISD most commonly cross-shop it against Forney ISD (same county, similar growth profile) and Rockwall ISD (adjacent county, more established, higher-rated). Here is a side-by-side on the metrics that matter most for relocation decisions:

Families who prioritize the B TEA rating and more established infrastructure tend to lean toward Rockwall ISD — but Rockwall home prices are meaningfully higher, and the commute geometry points more toward central and north Dallas rather than the southern corridor. Forney ISD offers a middle path: it's in the same Kaufman County growth zone as Crandall, carries a similar C rating, but with nearly three times the enrollment brings more campus variety and a slightly broader program footprint. Crandall is the right fit for families specifically drawn to the smaller community scale and the housing affordability of southwestern Kaufman County.

Community Feel

Crandall ISD has the feel of a district where people know each other — elementary classes that stay together through high school, Friday night football as a true community event, and the kind of parent involvement culture that smaller districts often cultivate more organically than large suburban ones. Families who move to Crandall from big-city suburbs sometimes describe it as the community-first environment they were looking for, without the competitive intensity that can characterize larger, more academically high-profile districts.

Athletics is the social glue here in a way that is different from the college-prep ecosystem of districts like Carroll or Frisco. Booster clubs are active, game nights draw the community out, and student-athletes in Crandall often describe being able to participate in multiple sports in ways that roster competition in larger districts wouldn't allow. For families with kids who thrive in an environment of active participation over spectatorship, this is worth weighing.

The honest community sentiment — drawn from parent forums and online discussions — also acknowledges the growth stress. The rapid influx of new families has put pressure on the district to hire quickly, build quickly, and update boundary maps frequently. Some longer-term residents describe a district in transition: more professional and better-resourced than it was a decade ago, but still working to build out the administrative infrastructure and program depth that larger, longer-established ISDs take for granted. Families arriving with realistic expectations — this is a growing district with genuine community, not a fully mature suburban ISD — tend to have positive experiences. Those expecting Frisco ISD on a Crandall budget will be disappointed.

One thing worth noting for families from high-cost-of-living origin states: the housing value in Crandall-area Kaufman County is genuinely different from most of DFW. New construction in the $280,000–$380,000 range is available. The tradeoff for that value is distance from the employment centers that concentrate in the northern DFW corridor — if your job or your spouse's is in Plano, Allen, or Frisco, the commute from Crandall is not a minor inconvenience. But for remote workers, I-30/I-20 commuters toward southern Dallas, or families prioritizing monthly housing cost above proximity to northern suburbs, the math can work well.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my Crandall address guarantee enrollment in Crandall ISD? ▾
Not automatically. The city of Crandall falls primarily within Crandall ISD, but not every Kaufman County address with a Crandall mailing address is within the district's attendance zone. Homes on the western fringe near the Dallas/Kaufman county line, or in certain unincorporated areas, may fall within a different district. Additionally, the 2025–26 boundary restructuring changed campus assignments within the district — so even if your address is in Crandall ISD, the specific campus assigned to it may differ from what older sources show. Always verify your exact address with the district enrollment office at (972) 427-6000 or through the enrollment portal at crandall-isd.net before making an offer.
Why is Crandall ISD's graduation rate so high if the TEA rating is a C? ▾
This is one of Crandall ISD's most interesting data points. The 2024 TEA C rating (score: 75) measures year-over-year test score performance and accountability domains including STAAR proficiency — metrics heavily influenced by how quickly a fast-growing district can absorb and serve new students at or above grade level. The graduation rate, by contrast, measures how many students who entered 9th grade earned a diploma within four years. Crandall's 97.3% graduation rate for the Class of 2023 reflects strong support for students once they are in the system — counselors, relationships, and a culture that keeps kids on track. The gap between the accountability score and the graduation rate is real, and it's one reason a C TEA rating alone doesn't tell the whole story for this district.
What changed in Crandall ISD for the 2025–26 school year? ▾
The district made three significant structural changes for 2025–26. First, a second middle school opened, bringing the district to two 6–8 campuses. Second, all six elementary schools were reconfigured to serve Pre-K through 5th grade uniformly — previously some campuses housed 6th graders. Third, 9th grade returned to the high school campus after a period when freshmen were housed separately. These changes triggered boundary rezoning for all elementary attendance zones. Families who purchased a home in prior years and researched school assignments at that time should re-confirm their campus assignment for 2025–26 directly with the district.
How does Crandall ISD's tax rate compare to neighboring districts? ▾
Crandall ISD's 2024–25 certified total rate is $1.26 per $100 of assessed value. Neighboring Forney ISD (same county) is at $1.29, while Rockwall ISD (Rockwall County) is lower at approximately $1.03. The higher rate in Crandall and Forney compared to Rockwall is largely structural: both Kaufman County districts carry the maximum I&S (bond/debt service) rate to fund the new campuses and facilities needed for their explosive enrollment growth. More established districts with completed capital infrastructure have lower I&S components. On a $350,000 home after the $100,000 homestead exemption, the Crandall ISD tax bill is approximately $3,150 per year ($263/month). On a $250,000 home it would be $1,890/year ($158/month).
What is the transfer policy — can I enroll my child in Crandall ISD if we live outside the boundaries? ▾
Inter-district transfers (enrolling from outside Crandall ISD's attendance zone) require district approval and are not guaranteed. Texas law gives districts the authority to accept or decline out-of-district transfers based on campus capacity, and a fast-growing district like Crandall may have limited availability at specific campuses. Intra-district transfers (requesting a campus other than your assigned school within the district) are also subject to capacity approval. Contact the enrollment office early in the school year cycle — typically spring for the following fall — to inquire about transfer availability. Do not purchase a home outside the district expecting a transfer to be approved.
How does special education transfer work when moving to Crandall ISD? ▾
If your student has an existing IEP (Individualized Education Program), Texas law requires Crandall ISD to convene an ARD (Admissions, Review, and Dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment. At this meeting, the district must either adopt the transferring IEP or propose modifications based on their evaluation. The student must receive services comparable to the prior IEP during the 30-day window. Bring complete documentation: your child's most recent evaluation, the current IEP, related services agreements, and prior progress reports. Contact the Special Education department at (972) 427-6000 before your child's first day — scheduling the ARD meeting in advance reduces delays in services beginning. For 504 plans, similar notification applies. Parent experience with SpEd quality varies by campus and student needs; direct contact with the Special Services office before enrollment is strongly recommended for fit-sensitive situations.
Does Crandall ISD have a dual language or Spanish immersion program? ▾
No. As of 2025–26, Crandall ISD does not offer a dual language or Spanish immersion program at the elementary level. The district offers English as a Second Language (ESL) services for students whose home language is not English, as required by Texas law, but this is a support program rather than a two-way immersion model. Families specifically seeking dual language elementary education should evaluate Forney ISD or other Kaufman County options that may offer those programs, or consult the DFW School Districts hub to identify districts with dual language offerings.
