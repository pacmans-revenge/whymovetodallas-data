Getting Your Bearings

Where Is Murphy, Texas?

Murphy sits in the northeastern corner of Collin County, roughly 22 miles from downtown Dallas and sandwiched between two of DFW's most in-demand suburbs: Plano to the west and Wylie to the east. FM 544 is the main east-west artery, running through the heart of Murphy and connecting directly into Plano and Allen. To the north, Murphy Road feeds into Allen and the SH-190 (George Bush Turnpike), which opens up the entire DFW metro — Frisco, Plano, Las Colinas, and eventually DFW Airport — without ever touching I-635 or I-30.

For families commuting to Plano's Legacy West corridor, Toyota's campus, or the Telecom Corridor in Richardson, Murphy is quietly ideal. You're on FM 544 or US-75 in minutes, and you're at most employer campuses in 15–20 minutes under normal conditions. The honest trade-off: Dallas proper takes 25–35 minutes depending on where you're headed, and Fort Worth is the better part of an hour. Murphy is very much a northeast-quadrant suburb. If your job is in Irving or DFW Airport's cargo corridor, Flower Mound or Coppell serves you better. If you're working anywhere between Plano and McKinney, Murphy might be the right call.

Transit reality: Murphy has no DART rail service, and none is planned. There is a local bus option that connects to DART via Plano, but it is not a practical commute solution for most residents. A personal vehicle is essential — budget for two cars if you have a two-working-adult household.
Education

Plano ISD — Schools Deep Dive

Most of Murphy is served by Plano ISD — one of the largest and best-resourced school districts in Texas. A smaller eastern portion of Murphy falls within Wylie ISD. This split matters when you're buying: the district your home is assigned to can vary by street, and sometimes by address within the same neighborhood. Before you make an offer anywhere in Murphy, verify your specific address at Plano ISD's or Wylie ISD's zoning lookup. I check this on every offer I write for clients in Murphy — it's not a step to skip.

For families landing in the Plano ISD portion — which is the majority of Murphy — the school pathway is distinctive. Murphy Middle School and Armstrong Middle School are the primary feeders, and from there, students attend McMillen High School for grades 9–10, then transfer to Plano East Senior High School for grades 11–12. That two-campus high school model is something families from out of state need to understand before they start comparing rankings — your child's "high school" experience is technically split between two campuses.

71 Campuses

Plano ISD serves approximately 52,000 students across 71 campuses. Murphy's Plano ISD students attend Murphy Middle or Armstrong Middle, then McMillen High (9–10) and Plano East Senior High (11–12).

Strong AP & College Prep

Plano ISD earns an A+ from Niche in college prep. The district's AP enrollment rates and graduation-to-college outcomes place it among the top performing large districts in Texas.

ISD Split — Verify Your Address

Some eastern Murphy addresses fall within Wylie ISD rather than Plano ISD. Always verify your specific address at pisd.edu or wylieisd.net before making an offer.

Where to Live

Best Neighborhoods in Murphy for Families

Murphy is almost entirely residential — there's no downtown core, no mixed-use district, no walkable village center. What it does have is established neighborhoods with larger-than-average lots for this distance from Plano, strong homeownership rates (90%), and a community that's been family-heavy since the early 2000s. Here are the neighborhoods I point families to first.

$450K–$650K · Established 2000–2004

Murphy Farms

Murphy Farms is the neighborhood most families picture when they think of Murphy — large homes on generous lots (typically 0.2–0.35 acres), built primarily between 2000 and 2004. Mature trees, established streetscapes, and a neighborhood culture that's been family-oriented for two decades. Most Murphy Farms homes fall in Plano ISD and feed Murphy Middle School. The price point is one of the best combinations of space and school quality you'll find this close to Plano.

Crescent Hill

Crescent Hill is Toll Brothers' boutique new construction community in Murphy — 75-foot home sites, 4–6 bedrooms, 3-car garages, and outdoor living spaces that are designed to be used. If you're coming from a California suburb where $900K gets you a smaller lot and a dated kitchen, Crescent Hill genuinely surprises people. Assigned to Plano ISD. This is Murphy's premium new-construction option for families who want square footage and don't want to compromise on school quality.

$420K–$580K · Established

Maxwell Creek

Maxwell Creek is one of Murphy's older established communities, with homes on lots ranging from 0.2 to 0.35 acres. Flexible floorplans and a range of sizes make it one of the more accessible entry points into Murphy homeownership. School assignment varies by exact address — some Maxwell Creek homes are Plano ISD, some Wylie ISD — so verification before offer is critical. The community has a settled, stable feel that appeals to families who want neighbors who've been there a while.

$480K–$680K · Family Community

The Ranch / Aviary

A cluster of related family neighborhoods in the heart of Murphy, The Ranch and Aviary share the relaxed, community-oriented character that defines most of the city. Homes here tend toward 2,500–4,000 sq ft on quarter-acre lots, built in the mid-2000s. Murphy's central location and park access make these neighborhoods convenient without feeling dense. Most fall within Plano ISD. Youth sports and neighborhood events keep the family energy consistent year-round.
