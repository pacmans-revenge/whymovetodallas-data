Cedar Hill ISD Overview

~7,500 Enrollment
11 Campuses
C (75) TEA Grade 2024
C Niche Grade (3rd party)
88% Grad Rate (Class of 2023)
$1.17 Tax Rate / $100

Cedar Hill ISD serves one of southern DFW's most distinctive communities — a city that sits squarely on the Dallas County and Johnson County line, offering views of Joe Pool Lake, affordable home prices relative to northern suburbs, and a tightly knit community culture built heavily around athletics, fine arts, and faith. For relocating families, that context matters as much as the district's ratings. The honest starting point: Cedar Hill ISD holds a 2024 TEA C rating (75/100), which means overall academic outcomes are below the Texas state average. That is the primary tradeoff families weigh when shortlisting homes here.

But Cedar Hill ISD has a specific strength that sets it apart from similarly rated districts: the Cedar Hill Collegiate Academy at Cedar Hill High School, where motivated students can earn a full associate degree — up to 60 college credit hours — tuition-free through Cedar Valley Community College, alongside their high school diploma. For a family with a college-bound student who would thrive in a supportive dual-enrollment environment rather than the hyper-competitive culture of a large A-rated district, this is worth taking seriously. The district also carries a notably strong Fine Arts tradition for its size, recognized prominently at the 2025 State of the District address.

Families making a realistic decision about Cedar Hill ISD need to think about their student's specific pathway — the Early College track, athletics, fine arts, or CTE — rather than the district average. The rating reflects broad district-level outcomes; individual students who engage with the right program can have a very different experience than the composite score suggests.

Best Fit For Families Who…

  • Have a motivated student who could benefit from earning a free associate degree alongside their high school diploma
  • Prioritize fine arts — music, theater, visual arts — as a core part of their child's education
  • Value a strong athletics culture and want their kids deeply involved in school sports and extracurriculars
  • Are drawn to Cedar Hill's affordability and Joe Pool Lake lifestyle and want to make the most of the available academic programs
  • Are comparing similar C-rated southern DFW districts and want to evaluate the Early College advantage specifically

May Not Be the Best Fit If…

  • Your primary filter is top-tier TEA or Niche ratings — Cedar Hill ISD's C in both reflects outcomes below state average
  • You need a wide range of IB, multiple AP sections, or a deeply competitive pre-college academic environment
  • You're comparing to A-rated districts in northern suburbs (Frisco, Allen, Coppell) and the rating gap is a dealbreaker
  • You need district-provided transportation to a choice or specialty program — Cedar Hill ISD does not provide it
⚠ Boundary Verification

Not every Cedar Hill address falls within Cedar Hill ISD boundaries. The city straddles the Dallas County and Johnson County line, and some homes with Cedar Hill addresses may be served by Mansfield ISD or Midlothian ISD depending on their exact location. Always verify your specific address with the district before making an offer — especially in ZIP code 75106 or near the city's western and southern edges.

Contact the CHISD Lacy Enrollment Center at family.access@chisd.net or call (972) 291-1581 to confirm which campus your specific address feeds.

TEA Accountability Rating

Cedar Hill ISD received a C rating (75/100) in the 2024 TEA A-F Accountability system. This is the most recent officially published rating as of May 2026.

The Texas A-F system measures three core domains:

  • Student Achievement — STAAR performance, graduation rate, and college/career/military readiness (CCMR). This is a snapshot of where students are performing at a given moment.
  • School Progress — How much students grew academically year over year, and how the district compares to schools with similar economic profiles.
  • Closing the Gaps — Whether outcomes are improving equitably across student groups, including by income, race/ethnicity, disability status, and English proficiency.

A C rating (70–79 range) means the district is meeting the minimum state standard but falling short of what TEA considers solid performance. For relocating families, this is a meaningful signal — it indicates that district-wide STAAR outcomes, year-over-year growth, and equity metrics are below what you would find in B- or A-rated neighbors like Mansfield ISD. It does not mean every campus or every program underperforms; it is a district average that masks significant campus-level variation.

What the C Rating Means Practically

A TEA C at the district level means that, on average across all campuses, student outcomes on STAAR and related measures are below the state average — not failing, but not strong. The practical implication for a relocating family: your child's experience will be shaped more by their specific campus and program track than by the district's composite score. The Early College Academy, for example, serves students who are actively pursuing higher academic goals within the same district that produces the C average.

Academics

Graduation rate: Cedar Hill High School's Class of 2023 graduated at an 88.2% rate (Texas Tribune / TEA data), which is competitive with similarly sized districts and above some of its C-rated neighbors. A graduation rate near 90% in a diverse, economically mixed district reflects meaningful work at the high school level even when broader STAAR proficiency numbers lag.

Early College and Dual Credit: Cedar Hill ISD's most distinctive academic offering is the Early College Academy embedded within Cedar Hill High School. Students accepted into the program can complete up to 60 hours of college coursework — enough for a full associate degree — tuition-free through Cedar Valley Community College, all while earning their high school diploma. This is an unusually generous dual-enrollment structure by DFW standards; most districts offer dual credit but cap free hours at 15–30. Acceptance is selective; families considering this pathway should contact the district during middle school to understand the application process and entrance criteria.

STAAR and academic outcomes: District-level STAAR proficiency data reflects the C rating — reading and math pass rates are below state average across elementary and middle grades. For a family evaluating STAAR data, this is an honest tradeoff. It is consistent with TEA's composite score and reflects the broad student population rather than any specific program track. Campus-level data varies; checking individual campuses on txschools.gov or GreatSchools before committing to a neighborhood is strongly recommended.

AP and advanced coursework: AP courses are available at Cedar Hill High School, though the breadth of AP sections does not match larger A-rated districts. Families seeking a wide AP catalog or IB programme should verify current offerings directly with the district; the Early College Academy is the primary pathway for students seeking structured college-level rigor.

Per-pupil expenditure: Approximately $13,021 per student (most recent available data), which is below the national average of ~$14,347. Cedar Hill ISD is a Texas recapture district — because property wealth per student exceeds the state threshold, the district sends a portion of local tax revenue back to the state for redistribution to property-poor districts. This is an unusual fiscal dynamic: the district is technically "property wealthy" on paper, yet serves a community where significant operational funding is redirected to the state.

Campuses

Cedar Hill ISD operates 11 campuses serving pre-K through 12th grade. The district's headline campus for relocating families with high school-aged students is Cedar Hill High School, home to both the general campus and the Early College Academy.

Cedar Hill High School

High School (9–12) ~2,075 students Home of the Longhorns

The district's primary high school serves the majority of Cedar Hill ISD students in grades 9–12. Offers AP courses, robust fine arts, CTE programs, and is the host campus for the Early College Academy. Strong athletics tradition across multiple sports. Verify campus assignment for your specific address before purchase, as Cedar Hill has one high school serving the full district.

Cedar Hill Collegiate Academy

Early College (9–12) ~295 students Free Associate Degree

A separate pathway embedded within Cedar Hill High School, the Collegiate Academy partners with Cedar Valley Community College to give accepted students up to 60 tuition-free college credit hours. Students earn both a high school diploma and an associate degree simultaneously. This selective program is Cedar Hill ISD's highest-profile academic differentiator. Application typically begins in 8th grade; contact the district for current eligibility criteria and timelines.

W.S. Permenter Middle School

Middle School (6–8) Cedar Hill

One of the district's middle school campuses preparing students for the high school pathway. Offers exploratory fine arts, CTE introductions, and athletics programs that feed into the high school program pipeline. Families with middle-school-age students should verify campus assignment by address via the enrollment center.

Collegiate Preparatory Elementary

Elementary (Pre-K–5) Choice Program Campus School Choice

One of three CHISD elementary campuses that offers tuition-based Pre-K programs. Also part of the district's growing school choice initiative, which includes Montessori, Fine Arts, and STEM pathway options being phased in through 2026–27. Families interested in choice programs at the elementary level should contact the district to confirm program availability for their child's grade level and enrollment year.

For the complete campus list and current enrollment by campus, visit Texas Tribune Schools Explorer — Cedar Hill ISD or GreatSchools — Cedar Hill ISD.

Specialty Programs

Cedar Hill ISD's programs are organized around four core pillars: Athletics, Fine Arts, CTE, and Early College. School Choice programs — including Montessori, Fine Arts specialty tracks, STEM, and World Languages — are being phased in through 2026–27. Important note: the district does not provide transportation for choice program students; families must arrange their own transport.

Early College Academy

Campus-specific — Cedar Hill High School only. Selective admission program (application process typically begins in 8th grade). Partners with Cedar Valley Community College to offer up to 60 tuition-free college credit hours — enough for a full associate degree alongside the high school diploma. One of the most generous dual-enrollment structures in southern DFW. Home-search implication: this program does not require living in a specific attendance zone within CHISD, but it does require being in CHISD boundaries and applying competitively. Contact the Lacy Enrollment Center for current eligibility and timelines.

Fine Arts (K–12)

Districtwide, with program depth at the high school level. Fine Arts is Cedar Hill ISD's most prominently celebrated program — featured as the lead topic at the 2025 State of the District. The district operates a dedicated Fine Arts website (finearts.chisd.net) and offers band, orchestra, choir, theater, and visual arts across grade levels. For families whose students are serious performers or artists, this is a genuine differentiator. No audition or special application required for general participation; performance ensembles at the high school level are audition-based.

Career & Technical Education (CTE)

High school level, districtwide access. Cedar Hill ISD's CTE programs integrate industry standards across multiple pathways. Students can earn industry credentials and certifications, participate in aligned career student organizations (DECA, HOSA, SkillsUSA, TAFE, BPA), and pursue work-based learning through shadowing, internships, and field experience. Transportation to work-based learning sites varies by placement; confirm with the district. CTE is a strong fit for students who prefer applied, credential-focused learning over a traditional academic track.

School Choice Programs (Expanding)

Campus-specific; transportation not provided. Cedar Hill ISD is actively building out a school choice framework. Montessori and Dual Language options exist at select campuses; STEM Technology tracks are opening in 2025–26; Fine Arts and World Languages Academy are slated for 2026–27. This is an actively evolving program portfolio — families planning ahead should contact the district to confirm what is available for their child's grade level and projected enrollment year, as timelines and campus assignments may shift.

Enrollment

All Cedar Hill ISD enrollment and registration is handled through the CHISD Lacy Enrollment Center at 504 E. Beltline Rd, Cedar Hill, TX 75104. The fastest way to reach the team with questions is family.access@chisd.net; allow 24 hours for a response.

Enrollment Steps (2025–26)

  1. Verify your address — Confirm your specific Cedar Hill address falls within CHISD boundaries before purchasing. Contact family.access@chisd.net with your address to verify. (See Boundary section below.)
  2. Gather residency documents — Proof of residency is required for all students living within CHISD boundaries. Documents must be dated within 30 days of submission and must be in the parent/guardian's name.
  3. Complete new student registration — Visit chisd.net/services/lacy-enrollment-center/k-12-new-student-registration and complete the online portal. Registration opens annually; contact the district for the current enrollment window.
  4. Submit documents in person — All residency paperwork must be completed at the Lacy Enrollment Center (not accepted by email or mail for initial enrollment).
  5. Campus assignment confirmed — Once enrollment is complete, the district will confirm which campus your child is assigned to based on your address.

Transfer Policy (2025–26)

Intra-district transfers: Available on a case-by-case basis within CHISD. Transfers are granted annually and must be renewed each school year.

Out-of-district (inter-district) transfers: Students living outside CHISD boundaries may apply for consideration in grades K–12. Eligibility requirements: no more than one discipline referral in the prior year, and no out-of-school suspensions or DAEP/JJAEP placements. No transportation is provided for any transfer student. Contact the Lacy Enrollment Center for application timeline and availability.

IEP / 504 Transfers

Under Texas law, when a student with an IEP or 504 plan transfers into Cedar Hill ISD, the district must convene an ARD (admission, review, and dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment. Bring all prior evaluation records, IEP documents, and related services information to enrollment. Contact Cedar Hill ISD's Special Services office before finalizing your move to understand current program availability and how your child's plan will transfer. Parent experience with special education services varies by campus and student need; the ARD meeting is the right venue to evaluate fit for your specific situation.

Boundary Verification

Cedar Hill ISD serves Cedar Hill, Texas — but not all Cedar Hill addresses. The city sits at the intersection of Dallas County and Johnson County, and the ISD boundaries do not perfectly align with the city limits or with your ZIP code. This creates real complexity for home buyers.

Which Areas CHISD Serves

Cedar Hill ISD primarily serves homes in the City of Cedar Hill, most of which fall in ZIP codes 75104 and 75106. The district spans portions of both Dallas County (the larger share) and Johnson County. Homes within the Cedar Hill city limits generally fall in CHISD, but this is not universal — particularly on the western and southern edges of the city, where boundaries shift toward Mansfield ISD (which also serves Johnson County communities) or Midlothian ISD.

Common Edge Cases

  • Southwest Cedar Hill / ZIP 75106 outer areas: Some addresses in ZIP 75106 near the Johnson County line may be served by Mansfield ISD rather than CHISD. The city names overlap; do not assume CHISD based on city address alone.
  • New construction near the southern boundary: Newer developments on Cedar Hill's southern fringe may sit in Midlothian ISD. Always confirm the campus assignment with the district before making an offer on new construction in this area.
  • Joe Pool Lake-adjacent neighborhoods: Neighborhoods immediately east and southeast of Joe Pool Lake generally fall in Cedar Hill ISD, but street-level verification is recommended for any property near the Grand Prairie or Mansfield city limits.

How to Verify Your Address

The most reliable method is direct contact with the CHISD Lacy Enrollment Center: email family.access@chisd.net with your specific street address and ask for campus confirmation. You can also use the TEA School Locator as a secondary check.

Home-Search Implication

Making an offer without verifying your specific address can mean your child attends a school in a different district than you planned — and boundary maps do not always match common-sense geography. This matters especially in Cedar Hill because the Dallas/Johnson County line creates real ISD boundary complexity that even long-time locals sometimes misunderstand. Verify before you sign.

Finances & Tax Rate

2025 total certified tax rate: $1.17 per $100 of assessed value (source: Texas Comptroller / CHISD financial transparency). This rate covers both the Maintenance & Operations (M&O) and Interest & Sinking (I&S) components.

What that means for your monthly payment: On a $350,000 home in Cedar Hill with the Texas $100,000 homestead exemption applied, the taxable value is $250,000. At $1.17 per $100, the annual school tax is approximately $2,925 — or about $244 per month added to your mortgage payment. On a $450,000 home ($350,000 taxable), the annual school tax is approximately $4,095, or roughly $341/month. Compare this to a district at $1.44 per $100 (like some fast-growth North Texas ISDs): the same $350,000 home would cost roughly $400/month in school taxes — about $156/month more.

Texas $100,000 homestead exemption: All Texas homeowners who occupy their home as a primary residence qualify for a $100,000 reduction in assessed value for school district taxes. File with your county appraisal district in your first year of ownership.

Recapture district note: Cedar Hill ISD operates under Texas's recapture ("Robin Hood") system, meaning property values per student exceed the state threshold. The district sends a portion of local property tax revenue back to the state for redistribution. This is an unusual dynamic for a community that many perceive as modest-income — and it can create budget constraints at the district level even when tax rates remain relatively moderate.

Per-pupil expenditure: Approximately $13,021 per student (most recent available data), which is below the national average. This reflects both the district's size and the recapture dynamic.

Recent trends: Cedar Hill ISD's total tax rate has declined each year since 2018, reaching its lowest point in a decade — a positive signal for homeowners, though future bond elections could affect the I&S component.

Community Feel

Cedar Hill has a community identity that is deeply wrapped up in its high school. Cedar Hill High School football is a consistent Texas UIL powerhouse, and the culture around Friday night games, booster clubs, and youth sports is one of the defining features of life in the district. Families who value athletic participation and a community where school sports genuinely matter — rather than function as an afterthought — will find that Cedar Hill delivers. The same spirit extends to the Fine Arts program, which has built a loyal base of parent supporters and produces public performances that draw wide community attendance.

Parent sentiment gathered from community forums and school reviews reflects a district where staff relationships with students tend to be personal and attentive, in part because the district is small enough that students aren't lost in the crowd. Teachers who go out of their way for students are frequently mentioned in reviews — a consistent theme across both positive and critical accounts. The honest tradeoffs that surface repeatedly: organizational consistency varies by campus and teacher, and families accustomed to the highly systematized communication cadence of large North Texas A-rated districts may find Cedar Hill's operational infrastructure less polished.

The broader Cedar Hill lifestyle context matters here too. Joe Pool Lake, Dogwood Canyon Audubon Center, and a network of outdoor recreation options give the community a character distinct from most DFW suburbs. The housing stock is generally more affordable than northern equivalents, attracting families who want a larger home or more land without the price premium of Frisco or Prosper. For the right family — one who values Fine Arts, athletics, the Early College pathway, and a community-first culture over top-percentile test scores — Cedar Hill ISD can be a genuinely good fit that gets overlooked because of its C rating headline.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does my Cedar Hill address guarantee enrollment in Cedar Hill ISD? ▾
No — not automatically. Cedar Hill city limits do not perfectly match Cedar Hill ISD boundaries. The city straddles the Dallas County and Johnson County line, and some Cedar Hill addresses — particularly in ZIP 75106 and near the city's southern and western edges — may be served by Mansfield ISD or Midlothian ISD instead. Always verify your specific street address before making a purchase by contacting the CHISD Lacy Enrollment Center at family.access@chisd.net or (972) 291-1581.
What is the Early College Academy and how does my child apply? ▾
The Cedar Hill Collegiate Academy is an Early College program embedded within Cedar Hill High School. Accepted students earn up to 60 hours of college credit — enough for a full associate degree — tuition-free through Cedar Valley Community College, while simultaneously completing their high school diploma. The program is selective; application typically begins during 8th grade. Families who are planning a move to Cedar Hill with a college-bound middle schooler should contact the district well before their move to understand current eligibility requirements, application timelines, and acceptance criteria. This is Cedar Hill ISD's most significant academic differentiator and one of the more generous dual-enrollment structures in southern DFW.
How does Cedar Hill ISD's TEA C rating compare to its neighbors? ▾
Cedar Hill ISD's 2024 TEA score of 75 (C) puts it in the same tier as its nearest southern DFW neighbors: DeSoto ISD (C, 73) and Duncanville ISD (C, 74) — all three are within a few points of each other at the same $1.17 tax rate. The meaningful rating jump in the region is Mansfield ISD, which earned a B (88) at a slightly lower tax rate of $1.15, but covers a very different geographic footprint. For families whose search is genuinely in Cedar Hill, DeSoto, and Duncanville, the rating differences are small enough that campus-level research and program fit matter more than the composite scores.
Why does Cedar Hill ISD have a TEA C but a Niche C — aren't they measuring the same thing? ▾
They measure similar outcomes but use different methodologies and weightings, so the grades often align but can diverge. TEA's A-F system is state-specific, measuring STAAR proficiency, student progress, and equity gap closure using Texas standards. Niche aggregates TEA data alongside U.S. News rankings, user reviews, and demographic data into its own composite. In Cedar Hill's case, both systems arrive at a C — reinforcing that the district is genuinely mid-tier on academic outcomes rather than being an artifact of one rating system's quirks. Use both as a starting signal, then drill into campus-level data on txschools.gov and GreatSchools for a more specific picture.
What is Cedar Hill ISD's transfer policy for families moving into the district? ▾
Families moving into Cedar Hill ISD with an address confirmed within the district's boundaries enroll as resident students — no transfer required. Out-of-district transfer requests (for students living outside CHISD boundaries) are considered annually for grades K–12. Eligibility requires no more than one discipline referral in the prior year and no out-of-school suspensions or DAEP/JJAEP placements. No transportation is provided for transfer students. Contact the Lacy Enrollment Center to confirm current availability and application windows for the upcoming school year.
How does Cedar Hill ISD handle IEP and 504 plan transfers? ▾
Under Texas law, when a student with an existing IEP transfers into Cedar Hill ISD, the district must convene an ARD (admission, review, and dismissal) meeting within 30 days of enrollment to review and adopt or adapt the prior plan. Bring all current evaluation reports, the most recent IEP document, and prior services information to the enrollment center. Families with a student on a 504 plan should contact Cedar Hill ISD's Special Services office before finalizing a move to understand how plans transfer and what services are available at your child's assigned campus. Parent experience with special education services varies by campus and individual student needs; the ARD meeting is the right venue to evaluate fit for your family's specific situation.
Why is Cedar Hill ISD a recapture district if it serves a modest-income community? ▾
Texas's recapture formula is based on property wealth per student — not household income. Cedar Hill has a combination of commercial properties, Joe Pool Lake-adjacent real estate, and retail development that pushes the assessed property value per enrolled student above the state threshold, even though household incomes in the district may be average or below average. As a result, Cedar Hill ISD sends a portion of local property tax revenue back to the state for redistribution to property-poor districts. This creates a counterintuitive budget situation where the district contributes to the equalization pool despite not being an affluent district in the typical sense of the word.
