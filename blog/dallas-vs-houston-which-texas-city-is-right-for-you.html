Dallas vs. Houston: Which Texas City Is Right for You?

Kristen Carpentier Licensed TX Realtor®  ·  September 2, 2023  ·  8 min read

When deciding to make a move to Texas, particularly to its two most prominent cities, Dallas and Houston, it's essential to explore various aspects to ensure that your choice aligns with your lifestyle, career goals, and preferences. In this comprehensive guide, we'll delve deeper into the differences between Dallas and Houston, touching on factors like population, size, median home prices, job opportunities, weather, cuisine, recreational activities, nightlife, transportation, and crime rates.

Population of Dallas vs. Houston

Let's begin with the fundamental aspect of population. As of recent data, the Dallas metro area boasts a population of just over 7.9 million people, making it one of the most populous metropolitan areas in the United States. On the other hand, Houston, also known for its urban sprawl, is home to approximately 7.34 million people. While both cities have substantial populations, it's essential to note that Dallas recently overtook Houston as the fourth-largest metro area in the country. This impressive growth is partly attributed to its strategic location in the heart of Texas, allowing it to expand in multiple directions.

Size of Dallas vs. Houston

Size matters, especially when it comes to navigating the urban landscape. Dallas covers an extensive area of over 9,000 square miles within its metro limits. In contrast, Houston's metro area sprawls across around 10,000 square miles, although approximately 8,900 square miles constitute land, with the rest being water bodies such as Trinity Bay and Galveston Bay. When considering landmass, Dallas is relatively more condensed, making it slightly more accessible to traverse without encountering vast water expanses.

Median Home Prices in Dallas vs. Houston

The cost of living plays a pivotal role in your decision to relocate. Dallas, with its thriving economy, reports a median home price of approximately $390,000. While this figure can vary depending on the neighborhood, it's essential to note that Dallas does offer more affordable housing options if you are willing to explore areas on the outskirts of the city.

On the other hand, Houston offers even more affordability, with a median home price of around $348,000. This lower price point may appeal to those seeking a budget-friendly option. It's worth noting that both cities provide a range of housing choices to cater to different financial preferences.

Job Market in Dallas vs. Houston

Your career prospects should factor heavily into your decision. Dallas is known for its modern job market, featuring ample opportunities for growth, particularly in IT, finance, and healthcare sectors. The city has a reputation for being a hub of innovation and entrepreneurship, attracting professionals seeking diverse career options.

Houston, on the other hand, is synonymous with the energy sector, particularly oil and gas. It's also a significant player in aerospace, biomedical, healthcare, and manufacturing industries. However, when it comes to overall job growth, Dallas is currently outpacing Houston, making it a compelling choice for individuals looking to advance their careers.

Weather in Dallas, Texas vs. Houston

Texas weather is known for its extremes, and both Dallas and Houston are no exceptions. Dallas experiences hot and humid conditions, although it's generally less humid than Houston. The latter city's humidity can create a sticky, sauna-like atmosphere during the sweltering summer months.

Houston also has more cloudy days, approximately 239 annually, compared to Dallas's 179. Despite the heat, Houston's humidity stands out, making it feel even hotter than its northern counterpart. Moreover, Houston faces the risk of hurricanes, with Hurricane Harvey in 2017 causing significant damage and over 30 inches of rain in parts of the city.

In contrast, Dallas, while closer to Tornado Alley, deals with fewer hurricanes and more infrequent snow and ice events, particularly in the winter. The cold spells typically last only a short duration, offering a change of pace from the scorching summers.

Crime in Dallas, Texas vs. Houston

Safety is a top priority when choosing where to live. Both Dallas and Houston report crime rates above the national average. In Houston, the violent crime rate stands at approximately 50.4, with property crime at 63.2. In Dallas, the numbers are comparatively lower, with a violent crime rate of 37 and a property crime rate of 50.7. To put this in perspective, the national average for violent crime is 22.7, while property crime averages around 35.4.

It's essential to research and consider specific neighborhoods within these cities, as crime rates can vary significantly. Urban areas tend to have higher crime rates compared to suburban and rural areas. Prioritizing safety when selecting your new residence is crucial, so thorough research is advised.

Transportation in Dallas, Texas vs. Houston

Getting around the city is a crucial consideration, especially if you're relocating from a major metro area. Dallas boasts a relatively extensive public transportation system, featuring the DART (Dallas Area Rapid Transit) light rail system that spans about 90 miles and serves 62 stations across 13 major cities. This robust transit system moves an average of 96,000 people on weekdays and 57,000 on Saturdays.

In contrast, Houston's public transit system is more compact, covering approximately 23 miles of tracks with 44 stations, primarily within the city limits. Houston's transit system accommodates around 63,000 riders on weekdays and 31,000 on Saturdays. While both cities have public transportation options, they are not as comprehensive as those in some other major cities.

However, it's crucial to note that both Dallas and Houston are characterized by their sprawling metropolitan areas, and having a car is often necessary for convenience and accessibility. The freeway systems in both cities reflect their sprawling nature, with Dallas' freeways forming a vast interconnected

web and Houston's freeways following a hub-and-spoke pattern. Navigating either city's freeways can be challenging, so GPS navigation is often a valuable companion.

Food in Dallas, Texas vs. Houston

Texans take their food seriously, and both Dallas and Houston offer a delectable array of cuisines. Mexican, Asian, and barbecue fare are celebrated in both cities, each with its unique twist.

Dallas offers a vibrant Tex-Mex scene, with a focus on fusion flavors. While it excels in barbecue and Asian cuisine, some may find the Mexican options to lean more towards Tex-Mex than authentic Mexican. On the other hand, Houston shines in the realm of authentic Mexican cuisine, making it a go-to destination for those craving truly traditional Mexican flavors. When it comes to barbecue and Asian dishes, Dallas and Houston each have their respective gems, ensuring a diverse culinary experience.

Things to Do in Dallas, Texas vs. Houston

Leisure and recreation are vital aspects of your quality of life, and both cities have their own charm. Dallas boasts an array of activities, from professional sports events to thrilling water parks and the iconic Six Flags amusement park. Sports enthusiasts can relish the presence of all major professional sports teams in the city, making it a paradise for sports fans.

Moreover, Dallas offers a plethora of options for enjoying lakes, be it for boating, paddle boarding, or swimming. Its diverse range of lakes caters to water enthusiasts, creating a vibrant lake culture.

Houston, while home to the Gulf Coast and its scenic beaches, is often criticized for its proximity to Galveston, which may not provide the pristine beach experience found in other coastal destinations. However, with additional travel, residents can access more picturesque beach locales such as Corpus Christi or South Padre Island. When it comes to lake life, Houston offers fewer options, primarily limited to Lake Houston and community pools.

Nightlife in Dallas, Texas vs. Houston

Your preference for nightlife can be a significant influence on your decision. Dallas offers an extensive range of nightlife options, catering to various age groups and tastes. From the bustling downtown district to the trendy Deep Ellum for music and entertainment, and the lively suburbs of Plano and Frisco, Dallas ensures a vibrant nightlife scene. Fort Worth, just a short drive away, adds another layer of entertainment with great food, concerts, and even rodeos.

In contrast, Houston boasts a renowned club scene, drawing younger individuals and those seeking an energetic nightlife experience. The city is known for its lively atmosphere, with numerous clubs and venues for dancing and socializing. While both cities offer nightlife options, the choice between the two depends largely on your age and entertainment preferences.

Conclusion: Choosing Your Texas Home

In conclusion, the decision between Dallas and Houston ultimately depends on your individual preferences and priorities. Each city offers a unique blend of opportunities and challenges, catering to a wide range of lifestyles and career goals. Take your time to weigh the factors that matter most to you, and consider visiting both cities to get a firsthand experience of what life there has to offer.

Whether you're drawn to the vibrant arts scene of Dallas, the energy sector opportunities in Houston, or the diverse culinary experiences in both, Texas has something to offer everyone.
