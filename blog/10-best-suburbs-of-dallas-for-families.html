10 Best Suburbs of Dallas for Families

Kristen Carpentier Licensed TX Realtor®  ·  Updated May 30, 2026  ·  25 min read

If your family is researching Dallas-Fort Worth, you're probably drowning in suburb options — and the problem is that most ranking websites tell you the same six things in the same order. I've lived in Flower Mound for years, raised four kids here, and helped hundreds of families work through this exact decision. What I can give you that Niche.com can't is the context behind the numbers: what each suburb actually feels like to raise a family in, where the trade-offs are honest rather than glossed over, and what your budget really gets you.

This list is built on Niche.com's 2023 rankings for the DFW metro — a composite of school quality, safety, family amenities, and cost of living. The core tier of DFW's best family suburbs has stayed remarkably consistent. Whether a family is relocating from the Bay Area, Chicago's North Shore, Austin, or across town from a Dallas apartment, I keep seeing the same suburbs rise to the top. I'll walk you through all ten, with the honest pros and cons for each.

Quick context on Texas property taxes

Texas has no state income tax — but property taxes run 1.8%–2.4% annually. On a $700K home in Frisco, that's roughly $14,700/year. For most families moving from California or New York, the income tax elimination more than offsets it. But budget for it from day one.

10. Living in Parker Texas

Parker's history dates back to the mid-19th century when settlers began to establish farms and homes in the area. Today, the city proudly preserves its historical roots through various landmarks and architectural gems. The striking Southfork Ranch, known for its role in the popular TV series "Dallas," is one such historic site that attracts visitors from far and wide. This storied location provides a glimpse into the bygone era of Texas ranching and oil wealth.

One of Parker's defining characteristics is its rural ambiance. The city covers roughly 6 square miles and is known for its spacious properties, often with at least an acre, creating an ideal setting for equestrian and agricultural pursuits. The presence of horse farms and pastures lends the city a serene, pastoral quality that is hard to find in many urban settings.

Parker's real estate market runs on the higher side compared to many suburbs below — but that's mainly because of the acreage. You're paying for land, not just square footage. Parker and Sunnyvale don't yet have city-wide Lofty saved searches; reach out directly and I can pull current active listings for you.

Parker is part of the Plano Independent School District, renowned for its high-quality education. The city's location allows residents to enjoy the best of both worlds: the peaceful life of a small town and the proximity to the numerous amenities and opportunities that the larger Dallas-Fort Worth area has to offer. Shopping centers, dining options, and cultural attractions are just a short drive away.

The city's natural beauty is a standout feature. Parker's landscapes are dotted with towering oak trees, rolling hills, and serene lakes. For those who appreciate the outdoors, nearby parks and nature reserves offer hiking and recreational activities. The beauty of Parker is further enhanced by its pristine, starry night skies – a luxury not easily found in more urban environments.

9. Living in Sunnyvale Texas

Sunnyvale, Texas, which is located a short distance from Dallas Texas, is a community that offers numerous compelling reasons to make it your home. From its charming atmosphere to its unbeatable location.

Sunnyvale is a small town with big aspirations. It offers all the charm and friendliness you'd expect from a tight-knit community, but it's also strategically located just minutes from downtown Dallas. This means you can enjoy the benefits of a tranquil, suburban lifestyle while being close to the opportunities and amenities of a major city.

Families considering Sunnyvale will appreciate the Sunnyvale Independent School District. Renowned for its excellent education programs, this school district consistently ranks among the best in the state. From caring teachers to cutting-edge facilities, Sunnyvale offers a strong educational foundation for your children.

Sunnyvale is a diverse community that welcomes people from all walks of life. Its vibrant culture reflects a mosaic of backgrounds and perspectives, fostering an inclusive environment where everyone can feel at home.
One of Sunnyvale's unique aspects is its strong sense of community. Residents frequently come together for local events, festivals, and neighborhood gatherings. Whether it's a farmer's market, an art show, or a barbecue in the park, there's always something to do and new friends to meet.

The natural beauty surrounding Sunnyvale is an irresistible draw. The town is situated near the picturesque Trinity River, and its parks and trails are ideal for outdoor enthusiasts. Hiking, biking, fishing, and picnicking are just a few of the activities you can enjoy within the town's boundaries.

Sunnyvale's strategic location has made it one of the fastest-growing communities in Texas. The town's leadership is committed to maintaining its small-town charm while expanding services and amenities to accommodate a growing population. This focus on balanced growth ensures a promising future for residents.

The real estate market in Sunnyvale, Texas shows a range of homes available. Prices start in the $300,000s and go up to multi-million dollar homes. There are homes from the 1940s as well as new construction options. Sunnyvale offers something for everyone.

8. Living in Murphy Texas

Murphy is situated in Collin County, Texas, in the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex, approximately 20 miles northeast of downtown Dallas.

Murphy had a population of around 20,000 residents, contributing to its small-town charm.

The city's history can be traced back to the late 1800s when it was originally settled by pioneers and farmers. Today, it retains a strong connection to its agricultural heritage.

Murphy is part of the Plano Independent School District, which is known for its high-quality education. Many of its schools have received accolades for their academic excellence.

Murphy features several parks, including Murphy Central Park and Maxwell Creek Park, offering a wide range of recreational activities, from sports fields to hiking trails.

The city is known for its family-friendly atmosphere, with various community events and activities designed to bring residents together. Murphy's strategic location provides easy access to nearby cities, including Plano and Dallas, where residents can enjoy a wide array of cultural, dining, and employment opportunities. Murphy actively promotes community involvement, with local government initiatives and events, such as the Murphy Maize Days festival, fostering a strong sense of togetherness among its residents.

Housing options in Murphy vary widely, from well-preserved historic homes to contemporary suburban developments, catering to a diverse spectrum of preferences and budgets. As previously mentioned in the context of Parker, Texas, Murphy also offers numerous homes with generous acreage. Over the past six months, the housing market in Murphy, Texas has shown a broad price range, spanning from the mid-$400,000s to as high as $1.5 million, particularly for those homes with larger plots of land. The age of the homes is equally diverse, with properties dating back to the 1970s and more recent constructions completed as recently as 2019.

7. Living in Colleyville Texas

Colleyville is synonymous with upscale living. The city has beautiful residences, sprawling estates, and meticulously maintained properties. Residents take great pride in their homes, contributing to the city's reputation as an affluent community. Colleyville's luxury real estate market includes a wide range of housing options, from modern mansions to classic estates, catering to various tastes and preferences. As indicated below in the Colleyville TX housing market analysis, we see the homes for sale between $460,000 to $13 Million and a median sales price of $1.2 Million on the active listings. The ages of the homes show homes as old as the 1940s to new construction custom homes. Also noted that in the past 10 years, the median sales price has doubled from $400,000 to now $928,000, so this is another indication of what a desirable city it is to live in.

Families choosing to live in Colleyville often do so because of the exceptional schools in the Grapevine-Colleyville Independent School District. These schools consistently rank among the top in the state, providing students with a high-quality education and a range of extracurricular activities. The commitment to education is a key reason many families are drawn to the city.

Colleyville offers the perfect blend of suburban living and urban convenience. While it's situated in the Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex, the city maintains a small-town atmosphere. Residents enjoy a slower pace of life while still having easy access to the employment, entertainment, and cultural opportunities of the nearby cities.

Colleyville is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. The Colleyville Nature Center, spanning 46 acres, boasts walking trails, ponds, and diverse wildlife. It's a great place for hiking, picnicking, birdwatching, and communing with nature, right in your own backyard.

Colleyville's location offers residents the convenience of easy access to major highways such as State Highway 121 and State Highway 26. These highways connect the city to the Dallas-Fort Worth International Airport, making travel a breeze for both leisure and business purposes.

Throughout the year, Colleyville hosts a variety of cultural events and festivals. The Colleyville Center is a hub for art exhibitions, live music performances, and community gatherings. The annual Celebrate Colleyville festival is a highlight, offering entertainment and fun for the whole family.

6. Living in Flower Mound Texas

Flower Mound's scenic beauty is enhanced by its numerous parks, including the eponymous Flower Mound. This 12.5-acre hill is one of the town's iconic landmarks and offers panoramic views of the surrounding area. The town is also home to Cross Timbers Park, which features trails and abundant wildlife. Flower Mound fosters a sense of community through regular events such as the Flower Mound Farmers Market, the Independence Fest on July 4th, and a vibrant arts and cultural scene, including concerts and art exhibitions.

Flower Mound's location, just 25 miles northwest of Dallas and 30 miles northeast of Fort Worth, means you can easily access major employment centers, international airports, and a wide range of cultural and entertainment options within a short drive. While Flower Mound offers a great location, it's important to note that traffic can be a factor during peak hours, especially on major highways like FM 2499 and FM 1171. The town's proximity to DFW Airport can be both an advantage and, at times, a source of increased traffic due to the airport's operations. It's important to plan your routes and consider alternatives during rush hours.

The town enjoys a robust local economy with a mix of small businesses and corporate offices. Medical facilities like Texas Health Presbyterian Hospital Flower Mound provide quality healthcare services, and the Shops at Highland Village offer shopping and dining options.

The town offers a variety of housing options, from spacious single-family homes in master-planned communities like Bridlewood and Wellington to apartments and townhouses for those looking for more affordable options. There is also a burst of new construction communities going in from builders like Toll Brothers, some of which are not being advertised, so reach out if you are interested. They also have a few golf course communities in the city including the Bridlewood Country Club and the guarded gated Tour 18 Golf Community which features holes that are designed to mimic some of the most famous gold holes in the world.

5. Living in Trophy Club Texas

Trophy Club is strategically located within the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex, providing residents with easy access to major employment centers, cultural attractions, and entertainment venues in both Dallas and Fort Worth. The town's proximity to DFW International Airport is a significant convenience for travelers.

Trophy Club is a haven for golf enthusiasts with two outstanding golf courses: the Trophy Club Country Club and Whitworth Ranch Golf Course. These courses are meticulously maintained and provide stunning views. In addition to golf, the town offers numerous parks, sports facilities, and hiking and biking trails, making it an ideal place for active lifestyles.

Throughout the year, Trophy Club hosts a range of community events, such as the annual Christmas Tree Lighting, 4th of July Fireworks, National Night Out, and outdoor concerts in the town's amphitheater. These events help create a strong sense of community and provide opportunities for neighbors to come together.

Trophy Club is surrounded by picturesque landscapes, with Lake Grapevine and the Bob Jones Nature Center located nearby. These natural areas offer opportunities for hiking, fishing, boating, and picnicking. The town itself boasts well-manicured parks and tree-lined streets, adding to its overall aesthetic appeal.

The town offers a range of housing options, including single-family homes, townhouses, and apartments. Housing varies from traditional suburban residences to luxury estates, catering to various lifestyles and budgets. This diversity makes it possible for people with different housing preferences to find their ideal home.

4. Living in University Park TX

University Park's strategic location ensures easy access to major employment centers in Dallas, including the Central Business District, Dallas Medical District, and various corporate headquarters. The town's proximity to Love Field Airport and DFW International Airport makes travel convenient for both business and leisure.

Southern Methodist University contributes to the cultural and intellectual fabric of the town. The campus hosts various events, including lectures, art exhibitions, and athletic competitions, which are open to both students and the community. The George W. Bush Presidential Center is also located on the SMU campus, offering access to unique educational opportunities and exhibits.

Highland Park Village, a historic and architecturally significant shopping center, houses a collection of high-end retailers, such as Chanel, Hermes, and Ralph Lauren. It's a popular destination for luxury shopping, fine dining, and special events, contributing to the upscale lifestyle of University Park residents.

University Park's Police Department is known for its efficient and community-oriented policing. The town consistently ranks as one of the safest places to live in Texas, thanks to its proactive law enforcement and active community watch programs.

University Park's real estate market consistently ranks among the most expensive in Texas. The demand for housing in this community is driven by its reputation for quality of life, strong school system, and its desirability as a residential area for affluent families.

3. Living in Frisco Texas

Frisco's location, about 30 miles north of downtown Dallas, provides easy access to the amenities and job opportunities in the larger city. Commuting to Dallas is feasible, making it an ideal choice for those who want the benefits of city living while enjoying a suburban lifestyle.

The Frisco Texas population growth has been extraordinary. In 1990, the population was just over 6,000, and by 2021, it had reached around 200,000 residents. This remarkable growth is driven by the city's development as a business and residential hub, its thriving job market, and a reputation for a high quality of life.

Frisco's economic success is partly due to its location in the Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex, one of the largest and most dynamic economic regions in the United States. Several major corporations have chosen Frisco as their home, including the Dallas Cowboys, which built The Star, a massive mixed-use complex, as well as Toyota North America headquarters.

The city's sports culture is strong. The Dallas Cowboys' headquarters at The Star in Frisco includes Ford Center, an indoor stadium used for practices and events. Toyota Stadium is home to Major League Soccer's FC Dallas, and Dr Pepper Ballpark hosts minor league baseball games, ensuring a year-round sports scene for residents. That’s not even accounting for the youth sports programs in the area as well.

The real estate market in Frisco is characterized by steady appreciation in property values through 2020, then as the pandemic boom began, Frisco TX median sale price shot to the moon as you can see below in the Frisco 10-year median sales price chart. A variety of housing options cater to different preferences and budgets, from suburban single-family homes to urban-style apartments. This thriving market is fueled by the city's desirability and growth.

From our housing market analysis on Frisco Texas, we noted that the range in median sales prices as of the Fall of 2023 is from $111,000 for a tear-down home to as high as $6.75 million, with the median sales price of $708,000 as of September 2023 according to the 10-year history chart.

Frisco's educational reputation is anchored by the Frisco Independent School District (FISD), one of the largest and most acclaimed school districts in Texas. FISD consistently ranks among the top districts in the state, offering a wealth of educational opportunities. Students in the district consistently perform exceptionally well on state and national assessments, while the curriculum includes advanced placement (AP) courses and specialized magnet programs. FISD is equally known for its excellence beyond the classroom, with students achieving state championships in sports and garnering national recognition in fine arts and clubs. Parents and the local community play an active role, supporting the district through parent-teacher associations, booster clubs, and volunteering. FISD also emphasizes college and career readiness, providing students with guidance and vocational training programs. The district's commitment to innovative education is bolstered by award-winning teachers and modern, state-of-the-art facilities, ensuring a well-rounded and top-quality educational experience for students in Frisco.

Frisco might be your suburb if...
  • School ratings are your #1 priority — Frisco ISD is one of Texas's top-ranked districts

2. Living in Coppell Texas

Coppell's strategic location within the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex offers residents the best of both worlds. It's just a short drive away from downtown Dallas, where you can enjoy cultural attractions, shopping, and dining. Additionally, its proximity to DFW International Airport is a significant convenience for both business and leisure travelers. Coppell's commitment to safety is evident in its consistently high rankings as one of the safest cities in Texas. It has a well-funded and proactive police department, which helps maintain a low crime rate. The city also encourages community involvement through Neighborhood Watch programs and citizen engagement, fostering a sense of security and trust among residents. Coppell's family-friendly atmosphere is further enhanced by its numerous parks, recreational facilities, and family events, making it an ideal place to raise children.

Coppell is well-known for its strong sense of community. The Farmers Market, held on Saturdays, is a gathering spot where residents can purchase fresh, locally-sourced produce and handmade goods while socializing with neighbors. Events like the annual Red, White & Boom celebration bring the community together for music, food, and a spectacular fireworks display. The city also supports numerous sports leagues and recreational programs that cater to all age groups.

Coppell's thriving economy is due to its business-friendly environment. Many companies have chosen to establish their headquarters here, which results in job opportunities for residents. For example, CiCi's Pizza and Mohr Partners are based in Coppell, providing employment in various sectors, from food service to commercial real estate.

Coppell's commitment to green spaces is evident in its numerous parks and trails. Wagon Wheel Park, for instance, offers not only picturesque jogging and biking trails but also a playground, picnic areas, and sports courts. Beyond its city limits, residents have easy access to Grapevine Lake, a popular destination for boating, fishing, and water sports.

The housing market in Coppell is characterized by a wide array of options, from spacious single-family homes to modern apartments and condominiums. The city's reputation for safety, excellent schools, proximity to Dallas and the DFW Airport, and a strong sense of community make it a sought-after place to live, which can contribute to relatively higher property values.

AS we conducted the housing market analysis in Coppell Texas we noted that the median sales prices range from $295,000 to $2.9 Million with a median sales price of $656,000 of 220 homes sold in the past 6 months. The age of the homes ranges from as old as 1900 to new construction custom homes.

1. Living in Southlake Texas

Southlake's strong community spirit is not just a buzzword; it's deeply ingrained in the city's culture. Numerous events and gatherings foster this sense of togetherness. The annual Southlake Stars and Stripes celebration is a prime example. This event, held on Independence Day, draws thousands of residents to Southlake Town Square. It features live music, a patriotic parade, and an awe-inspiring fireworks show. This gathering encapsulates the community's deep-rooted love for celebrating national holidays and fostering connections among its residents. Southlake's Oktoberfest is another highlight, celebrating German heritage with traditional food, music, and fun activities, bringing diverse groups of people together. The city's commitment to the arts is evident in events like Art in the Square, which showcases local and regional artists. These gatherings underscore the city's support for cultural engagement and community involvement.

Southlake offers residents a high quality of life on several fronts. One of the most significant factors contributing to this is the city's relentless focus on safety. Southlake consistently ranks as one of the safest cities in Texas, with low crime rates and a proactive police force. Residents can enjoy peace of mind, knowing that their safety is a top priority. In addition to safety, Southlake boasts an extensive network of well-maintained parks and recreational facilities. These green spaces provide opportunities for outdoor activities, including sports, hiking, and picnicking. Bob Jones Park, with its 758 acres, is a prime example. It features picturesque hiking trails, sports fields, a dog park, and a serene lake where residents can engage in fishing or kayaking. This emphasis on green spaces and recreation contributes to the overall quality of life in Southlake.

Southlake's proximity to the Dallas-Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) is a significant advantage. DFW Airport is one of the largest and busiest airports in the world, offering direct flights to numerous domestic and international destinations. For residents, this means effortless travel and accessibility to a multitude of destinations. Whether for business or leisure, the convenience of living near such a major transportation hub cannot be overstated. Residents can travel globally with ease, making Southlake an ideal location for frequent travelers and those with global business connections.

Southlake Town Square stands as a testament to the city's vibrant retail and dining scene. This upscale lifestyle center is not just a shopping destination but also a hub for social gatherings. It boasts a diverse array of high-end boutiques, popular retail chains, and an eclectic mix of restaurants. The dining options range from casual to fine dining, catering to a wide spectrum of tastes. Beyond shopping and dining, Southlake Town Square frequently hosts community events, further enhancing its appeal. Whether you're looking for a family-friendly atmosphere, a place to meet friends or a spot for a romantic date night, Southlake Town Square has it all.

The Southlake real estate market is synonymous with luxury. The city offers a wide range of upscale neighborhoods, each with its unique charm and architectural styles. The homes in Southlake are known for their size, quality, and meticulous attention to detail. Many properties feature spacious, well-appointed interiors and beautifully landscaped yards. The luxury housing market encompasses gated communities, golf course properties, and custom-built homes, catering to various tastes and preferences. The demand for Southlake's real estate is consistently high, thanks to the city's location, top-tier schools, and commitment to preserving property values. This makes Southlake an attractive destination for homeowners seeking both luxury and long-term investment potential.

CISD consistently ranks among the best school districts in Texas, earning exemplary academic performance and accountability ratings. The district is known for offering a well-rounded education that goes beyond academics. Students have access to a wide array of extracurricular activities, ranging from athletics to the arts.
