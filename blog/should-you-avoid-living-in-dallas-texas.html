Should You AVOID Living in Dallas Texas?

Kristen Carpentier Licensed TX Realtor®  ·  June 18, 2023  ·  3 min read

Are you considering moving to Dallas, Texas? If so, you've come to the right place! In this blog post, we'll provide you with an overview of what living in Dallas is truly like. Our insights are based on our almost 12 years of experience residing in the Dallas Fort Worth Metroplex. From weather patterns and outdoor activities to housing styles and neighborhood options, we'll cover it all. So, let's dive in and discover the real Dallas!

Weather: Tornadoes, Thunderstorms, and Humidity

One aspect that often piques curiosity is Dallas' ever-changing weather. While tornadoes and hailstorms are a concern for many, the reality is that major tornadoes rarely impact the Dallas Metro area. However, thunderstorms can be intense and lightning displays are awe-inspiring. Springtime brings beautiful blooms, green landscapes, and unpredictable weather. Summers in Dallas can be scorching hot and humid, with high humidity levels and soaring temperatures. Mosquitoes and other bugs are also quite prevalent during this time, so bug protection is a must.

Winter Season: Mild or Unpredictable

Dallas winters can be mild or surprising, with temperatures ranging from single digits to the 80s. While snow and ice are not frequent, occasional ice storms can lead to unique experiences like skating on icy streets. The absence of slopes for skiing means finding creative ways to enjoy the rare snowfall, such as sledding in parks or finding hills in neighborhoods.

Outdoor Activities: Lakes, Trails, and Wildlife

Despite the weather, Dallas offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts. The city boasts numerous lakes and pools, making it an ideal destination for water-based activities like fishing, boating, and swimming. Biking and hiking trails are plentiful, though the terrain may not be as mountainous as in other states. However, you'll find serene trails along the lakes' edges and through beautiful neighborhoods. Wildlife sightings are not uncommon, with occasional visits from skunks, coyotes, raccoons, and even deer.

Choosing the Right Neighborhood: Size, Lifestyle, and Amenities

Dallas is a massive metropolitan area, spanning over 9,000 square miles. It's crucial to understand the different neighborhoods and their amenities, commute times, and lifestyles. The Dallas area offers various living options, including urban living near downtown, suburban areas with beautiful neighborhoods and schools, and country living with more land. Determining your priorities, such as proximity to schools, safety, outdoor activities, and budget, will help guide you towards the perfect neighborhood for your needs.

Housing Styles and Utilities

Dallas homes often feature stone or brick exteriors, given the South's architectural style. Shingle roofs are common due to the occasional hailstorms. Fences surrounding properties are typically wooden, and their condition and appearance may vary depending on neighbors' maintenance efforts. Utility bills, especially during hot summers, can be high, but energy-efficient new construction homes can help mitigate costs. The presence of trees in more established neighborhoods provides shade and character, enhancing the overall appeal.

Politics and Diversity

Contrary to stereotypes, Dallas is a diverse city with a range of political viewpoints. While the urban areas and certain suburbs lean towards the Democratic side, the region as a whole showcases political diversity. In the last election, in my own neighborhood, one neighbor had Beto O'Rourke yard signs and the other is string conservative. Other than some of the rural area that can be very conservative politically, many areas the ratio is barely Republican with them taking home just over 50% of the vote. So its not as a Conservative as you might think.

Living in Dallas, Texas, offers a unique experience filled with beautiful landscapes, diverse neighborhoods, and a wide array of outdoor activities. From the mesmerizing thunderstorms of spring to the scorching summers and occasionally unpredictable winters, Dallas weather keeps you on your toes. With the right neighborhood and housing choice, you can find your perfect haven within the vast Dallas Fort Worth Metroplex.
