Luxury Living: Navigating the Richest Cities in Dallas Fort Worth Metroplex

Every week I get some version of the same question from families researching a move to DFW: "We want the best — where do people with real money actually live?" It's a fair question, and the honest answer is more nuanced than any Forbes list can capture.

The Dallas–Fort Worth Metroplex has more than 7 million people spread across dozens of cities and suburbs. Several of them have median household incomes over $200,000 and median home prices that start at a million dollars and climb from there. But what separates them isn't just the price — it's the school district, the community feel, the land, and how close they are to where you actually need to go.

I've worked with families from California, Illinois, New York, and across Texas who are making exactly this decision. What they find, every time, is that "wealthy" means something different in each of these communities. Here's my breakdown of the six wealthiest cities in the Dallas–Fort Worth area, what they actually offer, and what families need to know before they buy.

Parker & Lucas, Texas

~$1.0–1.2M Median Home Price
~13,000 Population
Lovejoy ISD School District
~$165–175K Median HH Income

Parker and Lucas sit in eastern Collin County, roughly 30 miles northeast of Dallas, and they tend to get overlooked in conversations about DFW's wealthiest cities because they don't have the name recognition of Southlake or Highland Park. That's actually part of their appeal.

Median home prices in Parker run around $999,000–$1.0M, while Lucas homes are tracking closer to $1.1–1.2M — and both offer something rare in North Texas: genuine land. These are not quarter-acre suburban lots. Properties here commonly feature half-acre to full-acre lots, horse-appropriate acreage in some areas, and a rural atmosphere that you simply can't find at Southlake prices.

The schools are the main draw. Lovejoy ISD consistently ranks among the top 5 school districts in Texas on Niche (A+ rating) and posts some of the highest SAT scores and college-going rates in the state. For families for whom the school district is the non-negotiable, and who want more land per dollar than Southlake offers, Parker and Lucas are a serious option.

One note: a portion of the area feeds into the "Inspiration" master-planned community in Wylie, which falls under Wylie ISD rather than Lovejoy. Confirm the school zone before you make an offer — in this area, a single street can be the difference between two very different ISDs.

Southlake, Texas

~$1.3–1.4M Median Home Price
~30,000 Population
Carroll ISD School District
~$240K+ Median HH Income

Southlake is the name that comes up first when people ask about the wealthiest, most desirable suburbs near Dallas. That reputation is earned. Centrally located between Dallas and Fort Worth, it sits just minutes from DFW International Airport and offers one of the most complete lifestyle packages in the entire Metroplex.

The median home price has stabilized around $1.3–1.4M after a sharp run-up during the pandemic years. The Zillow average as of 2026 is approximately $1.31M — a meaningful increase from a few years ago, but one that buyers from California and New York often find less alarming than the sticker shock of their home market. The currency exchange from a Bay Area or NYC price point still works in Southlake's favor.

The engine behind Southlake's premium is Carroll ISD. It has been ranked the #1 school district in Texas on multiple occasions, earns an A+ on Niche, and maintains a culture of both academic achievement and athletics that parents who care about the full high school experience respond to immediately. That's not marketing copy — I hear it directly from families I work with. They move to Southlake because of Carroll, full stop.

Beyond schools: Southlake Town Square is one of the best mixed-use retail and dining destinations in DFW, Lake Grapevine is minutes away for weekend outdoor life, and the community has an active event calendar that makes new residents feel at home quickly. If you're relocating a family and you want the whole package — schools, amenities, community — Southlake is the suburb to beat.

The Park Cities: Highland Park & University Park

~$3.0M / $2.4M Median Price HP / UP
~31,700 Combined Population
HP ISD School District
6 miles From Downtown Dallas

Highland Park and University Park — collectively called "the Park Cities" — are a different kind of wealthy than anything else on this list. They're not suburban. They're urban enclaves, tucked inside Dallas, with mature tree canopies, walkable neighborhoods, and an old-money character that the outer suburbs simply can't replicate.

Highland Park is the pricier of the two. Median home prices are currently around $3.0M, consistent with prior years but up approximately 9% year-over-year as of late 2025. Entry-level homes here — if "entry-level" applies — start well above $1.5M, and estate properties frequently exceed $5–8M. The neighborhood is anchored by Highland Park Village, the oldest shopping center in the United States still operating in its original form, and is known for its strict architectural standards and quiet residential streets.

University Park surrounds the SMU campus and has a slightly more accessible price point, with a median around $2.4M — up 27% year-over-year as of late 2025 according to Redfin. It has more turnover than Highland Park, which means more opportunity for buyers who want to be in the Park Cities ecosystem without paying Highland Park's premium.

Both cities fall within Highland Park ISD, which earns an A+ on Niche and ranks consistently in the top tier of Texas school districts. Location is the other story: you're six miles from downtown Dallas, close to Love Field, and inside the loop — commute patterns here are completely different from the northern suburbs.

Worth knowing: The Park Cities are a separate municipality from the City of Dallas — they have their own police, their own ISD, and their own property tax structure. This matters for your tax calculation. Work with a local agent (and a Texas-familiar CPA) before making assumptions about total effective tax rate.

Westover Hills, Texas

~$3.1M Median List Price
~329 Households
~4.5 mi From Downtown Fort Worth
~$245K+ Median HH Income

Westover Hills is the entry on this list that most people — including longtime DFW residents — have never heard of. That's by design. With just 329 households, it's less a suburb and more a private enclave: its own city infrastructure, its own police department, and a median list price that has climbed to approximately $3.1M as of 2025.

Located about 4.5 miles west of downtown Fort Worth, Westover Hills offers proximity to the cultural amenities of Fort Worth — the Kimbell Art Museum, Sundance Square, TCU — while maintaining a level of residential privacy and exclusivity that's difficult to achieve in most of DFW. Properties here tend toward estate homes on larger lots, some with mature landscaping that took decades to establish.

The one honest tradeoff: school zoning. Westover Hills falls within Fort Worth ISD, which means many residents choose private school options rather than public ones. If public school district is a top-three factor for your family, Westover Hills requires a private school budget to be fully comfortable. Factor that into your total cost of living calculation.

Westlake, Texas

$3.8M–4.7M+ Average Home Price
~1,400 Population
Carroll ISD School District
~$250K+ Median HH Income

Westlake is the answer to "what's the absolute top of the market in DFW?" It sits west of Southlake along the Highway 114 corridor, has a population of approximately 1,400 people, and is one of the most deliberately exclusive small cities in North Texas.

Home prices here have risen sharply. As of 2026, the average home price in Westlake is $4.75M — up from a range of $1.9–3.6M just a few years ago. This reflects both the premium the market assigns to the community and the limited inventory that characterizes an enclave with fewer than 450 households. When supply is this constrained and demand from high-income buyers remains strong, prices compress upward.

Westlake offers two things no other DFW suburb quite replicates at this level: the Vaquero Country Club — a members-only private golf and social club with a long waitlist — and genuine acreage within a gated community environment. Properties here are typically on half-acre to multi-acre lots, designed with privacy as the primary value.

For school-age children, Westlake is zoned to Carroll ISD (the same A+ district as Southlake), and residents also have access to Westlake Academy, a charter school offering the International Baccalaureate curriculum from K–12. It's an unusual combination: top-tier public ISD options and a nationally recognized charter alternative, in the same zip code.

I'd be direct about the tradeoffs: Westlake has virtually no commercial development by design. There are no coffee shops, no grocery stores within the city limits. You drive to Southlake for everything. For buyers who want maximum privacy and are comfortable with that exchange, Westlake is unmatched in DFW. For buyers who also want walkability and convenience, it isn't the right fit.

Honorable Mentions

Three communities that regularly come up in conversations about DFW's wealthiest suburbs but didn't make the top-six list — worth knowing about:

Prosper, Texas

Prosper has made multiple Forbes-list appearances as one of the fastest-growing and wealthiest cities in the country. It's a newer-money story than Southlake or Highland Park — multi-million-dollar homes alongside well-executed master-planned communities, an active new construction market, and the appeal of Prosper ISD (A+). Families who want more square footage and land for their dollar than Southlake allows, in a community that's growing into its reputation rather than resting on it, tend to find Prosper compelling.

Bartonville, Texas

Bartonville is Denton County's best-kept luxury secret. Historically ranch and farmland, it still has the character — large lots, equestrian properties, a rural feel — but with home values that reflect how desirable that acreage has become. Median household income runs around $120,000, lower than the others on this list, but individual property values can run $1M–$3M+ for the right acreage. If your family wants horses or genuine land within 30 minutes of DFW Airport, Bartonville belongs on the shortlist.

Colleyville, Texas

Colleyville sits between Grapevine and Southlake and is often overlooked precisely because it's surrounded by more famous neighbors. Grapevine-Colleyville ISD earns a B+ to A- on Niche, median home prices run $700K–$1M+, and the community has a quieter, more established character than Southlake's high-visibility lifestyle. For buyers who want proximity to DFW Airport, a mature neighborhood, and slightly lower price points than Southlake, Colleyville is a strong alternative worth serious consideration.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the wealthiest city in the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex?

Westlake, Texas — with an average home price now exceeding $4 million and a population of just over 1,400 residents. Westlake is home to the exclusive Vaquero Country Club and is zoned to Carroll ISD, one of the top-ranked school districts in Texas.

Is Southlake the richest suburb near Dallas?

Southlake is one of the most desirable and well-known wealthy suburbs near Dallas, with a median household income over $240,000 and median home prices around $1.3–1.4M. However, Highland Park, University Park, Westover Hills, and Westlake all have higher median home prices than Southlake.

What is the most expensive neighborhood in Dallas?

Highland Park — technically its own municipality bordering Dallas — leads with a median home price around $3 million. Within the city of Dallas proper, Preston Hollow and Turtle Creek command the highest prices, with estate properties frequently exceeding $5–10M.

Which wealthy DFW suburb is best for families?

It depends on your budget and priorities. Southlake gives you Carroll ISD schools, a vibrant Town Square, and lake access at a $1.3–1.4M median — the best all-around package for most families. Parker and Lucas give you Lovejoy ISD schools with more land per dollar at around $1M. Westlake gives you maximum exclusivity and privacy at a $3M+ entry point. My job is helping you figure out which version of "best" fits your specific situation.

Do the wealthiest DFW suburbs have good school districts?

Yes — the most desirable wealthy communities in DFW are almost entirely defined by their school districts. Southlake and Westlake feed into Carroll ISD (A+ Niche rating). Parker and Lucas feed into Lovejoy ISD (A+). Highland Park and University Park are within Highland Park ISD (A+). The exception is Westover Hills, which falls under Fort Worth ISD — many residents there choose private schools instead.

Is Westlake, Texas worth the price?

For the right buyer, absolutely. Westlake offers gated estate communities, the exclusive Vaquero Country Club, Carroll ISD schools, and a residential environment with virtually no commercial density. The tradeoffs are clear: very limited inventory, an average home price over $4M, and zero walkable amenities within city limits. If privacy and prestige are the priority, there's nothing else quite like it in DFW.
